Shopify WE Pay (WE Cash / Telecom Egypt) Not Working — Wallet Not Appearing, OTP Not Received & Order Stuck Pending

Updated June 2026 · 7 failure modes · Egypt merchants · Telecom Egypt (WE) · CBE regulated · EGP only · 015 prefix

WE Cash (operated by Telecom Egypt under the WE brand, 015 prefix) is Egypt's fourth mobile carrier wallet and the least widely integrated on Egyptian payment gateways. When WE Pay stops working on Shopify, the failure is one of seven distinct problems — limited gateway support being the most common, followed by OTP delivery on a newer network, asynchronous callback failures, and checkouts that only accept Vodafone Cash 010 numbers and reject 015 entries. Identifying which of the seven failures you have is the only path to fixing it.

How WE Cash Works — and Why It Fails Differently From Vodafone Cash

WE Cash is a mobile wallet tied to the customer's Telecom Egypt (WE) SIM card (015 prefix), not a card scheme and not interchangeable with Vodafone Cash, e& Cash, or Orange Cash. Customers with a WE SIM register their national ID via the WE Cash app or at a Telecom Egypt store to create a wallet linked to their 015 number. Funds are loaded via WE dealers, ATMs, or bank transfers. At checkout, the customer selects WE Cash, enters their 015 mobile number, receives an OTP confirmation, and the purchase amount is deducted from the wallet balance.

WE Cash has the narrowest gateway support of all four Egyptian carrier wallets. Telecom Egypt launched its mobile brand WE in 2017 as Egypt's fourth carrier, significantly later than Vodafone Egypt, Orange Egypt, and e& Egypt. As a result, WE Cash has had less time to achieve payment gateway integration and has a smaller registered wallet user base. Merchants who successfully enabled Vodafone Cash, e& Cash, or Orange Cash cannot assume WE Cash is also available — it requires a separate check with the gateway provider (Paymob or similar) and is not universally included on all plan tiers. This is the most important WE-specific consideration: before troubleshooting anything else, confirm that WE Cash is actually available through your gateway on your specific plan.

WE Cash is an asynchronous mobile wallet payment with the same callback dependency as all other Egyptian mobile wallets. The customer approves via OTP, the wallet is debited, and the gateway sends a confirmation to Shopify via a webhook callback. If the callback URL is misconfigured, the order stays stuck in pending even though the customer's wallet has been charged. This is the most dangerous failure mode for all Egyptian mobile wallets — resulting in a debited customer with an unfulfilled order and no automated reconciliation path.

WE Cash is EGP only and requires a compatible gateway — Stripe and PayPal do not support it. Merchants must use an Egypt-focused gateway with explicit Telecom Egypt mobile wallet API integration. Confirm with Paymob whether your plan tier includes WE Cash and enable it as a distinct payment method separate from Vodafone Cash, e& Cash, and card payments.

7 Failure Modes Covered

  1. WE Cash option not appearing at checkout — gateway does not support or has not enabled 015 wallet
  2. Customer has WE SIM but has not registered for a WE Cash wallet
  3. OTP not received or times out before customer enters it
  4. Insufficient wallet balance — wallet pre-loading required
  5. CBE daily or monthly transaction limit exceeded
  6. Gateway callback not configured — order stuck in pending after customer pays
  7. 015 number rejected — checkout or gateway configured only for Vodafone Cash 010 numbers
1

WE Cash option not appearing at checkout — gateway does not support or has not enabled 015 wallet

Merchant Issue

The most common reason Egyptian WE customers do not see a WE Cash payment option at Shopify checkout is that either the merchant is using a payment gateway that does not support WE Cash at all, or they are using a gateway that supports WE Cash on some plans but have not confirmed availability or enabled it. WE Cash requires an explicit API integration with Telecom Egypt's mobile wallet system — this is a separate integration from Vodafone Cash, e& Cash, and Orange Cash.

Stripe and PayPal do not support WE Cash. International gateways without Egyptian mobile wallet integrations cannot offer it. For Egyptian-focused gateways, Paymob is the primary provider to check — but WE Cash availability depends on the merchant's Paymob plan and requires explicit activation. A merchant who enabled Vodafone Cash and e& Cash on Paymob cannot assume WE Cash is also live. WE Cash is an independent toggle and may not be available on all Paymob plan tiers due to WE's smaller market share and more recent integration.

The most common error pattern: a merchant integrates Paymob for Egyptian customers, enables Vodafone Cash and e& Cash, and considers the mobile wallet setup complete for all Egyptian carriers. WE customers (015 prefix) attempt to pay, see no wallet option, and abandon checkout. The merchant, testing from a Vodafone or e& number, sees checkout working correctly and never identifies that WE Cash is simply not enabled. Only checking from a WE SIM or reviewing gateway settings reveals the gap.

A secondary issue affects merchants who integrated Paymob before WE Cash was added to the available payment methods list. Legacy integrations may not include WE Cash even if the merchant's plan now technically supports it — the merchant must log in to the gateway dashboard and actively enable it rather than expecting it to appear automatically in an existing integration.

How to fix

  1. Contact your gateway to confirm WE Cash availability on your plan: Log in to your Paymob (or gateway) dashboard and look for WE Cash as a distinct payment method option, separate from Vodafone Cash and e& Cash. If it does not appear, contact gateway support to confirm whether WE Cash (Telecom Egypt, 015 prefix) is available on your plan tier and what activation steps are required. WE Cash may require a plan upgrade or separate commercial agreement.
  2. Enable WE Cash in the gateway dashboard if available: If WE Cash is listed as an available payment method, navigate to payment method settings and toggle WE Cash to enabled. Save the configuration. The payment widget at Shopify checkout should update to include a WE Cash option alongside other Egyptian payment methods.
  3. Test from a WE SIM explicitly: After enabling, verify the checkout from a device with a Telecom Egypt WE SIM (015 number) or ask a colleague with a WE SIM to test. A test with a Vodafone or e& number confirms only those wallets are visible — it does not confirm WE Cash is visible. Only a genuine WE Egypt payment attempt shows whether the 015 wallet option appears at checkout.
  4. Consider offering Fawry or bank transfer as a fallback for WE customers: While resolving WE Cash gateway availability, ensure WE Egypt customers have a non-wallet payment path. Fawry is accessible across all Egyptian carriers and does not require a registered mobile wallet. Bank transfer is carrier-agnostic. Both prevent checkout abandonment while WE Cash setup is in progress.
  5. Accept that WE Cash may not be available on your current gateway plan: WE Cash has the smallest user base and newest gateway integration among Egypt's four carrier wallets. If your gateway does not offer WE Cash on your current plan, evaluate whether the additional transaction volume from WE subscribers justifies a plan upgrade. For merchants targeting a broad Egyptian audience, covering Vodafone Cash (010), e& Cash (011), and Orange Cash (012) first — the three larger carrier wallets — may be more cost-effective than prioritizing WE Cash.
2

Customer has WE SIM but has not registered for a WE Cash wallet

Customer Issue

Owning a Telecom Egypt WE SIM card and having a 015 mobile number does not mean the customer has a WE Cash wallet. WE Cash is a separate financial service requiring explicit registration — the customer must create a wallet account linked to their 015 number before any payment can be made. A WE subscriber who has never registered for WE Cash will receive a "wallet not found" or "account not registered" error when they enter their number at checkout, even if the merchant's gateway is correctly configured and WE Cash is shown as a payment option.

When the gateway sends a wallet lookup for an unregistered 015 number to Telecom Egypt's payment system, it receives a "wallet not found" response. This is typically displayed to the customer as a generic payment error — "payment could not be completed," "account not recognized," or an error code without explanation. Customers frequently assume the merchant's checkout has a technical problem rather than recognizing that they need to register for a separate wallet service tied to their carrier.

WE Cash wallet registration can be completed through: the WE Cash app (available for Android and iOS), at Telecom Egypt WE stores or authorized dealers with a valid national ID, or via the WE app. Telecom Egypt has stores in major Egyptian cities; dealers are less widespread than Vodafone or e& Egypt due to WE's more recent market entry. Registration typically completes immediately upon national ID verification.

WE subscribers who use the carrier for home internet (ADSL, fiber) and have only recently acquired a WE mobile SIM are a common affected group. Many WE mobile subscribers are also TE Data broadband subscribers who migrated to WE mobile but never set up the financial wallet layer. Merchants enabling WE Cash should prepare customer-facing guidance for first-time registration.

How to fix

  1. Add wallet registration guidance near the WE Cash option at checkout: Display a brief note: "First time using WE Cash? Register your wallet via the WE Cash app or at any Telecom Egypt / WE store (bring national ID) before selecting this payment option." This guides unregistered customers to the correct action instead of leaving them with a confusing payment error.
  2. Include WE Cash registration guidance in cart abandonment follow-up: If a customer abandons checkout after attempting WE Cash, send a follow-up email or SMS explaining that WE Cash requires a one-time wallet registration via the WE app or at a WE store, and that they can return to complete their order once registered.
  3. Offer alternative payment methods for unregistered customers: Customers who want to pay with WE Cash but have not yet registered should be able to complete their purchase immediately. Display Fawry offline payment, Meeza card, or bank transfer as clear alternatives so that a WE Cash registration issue does not result in order abandonment.
  4. Distinguish registration errors from other failure codes in gateway logs: Your gateway returns specific error codes for "wallet not registered" versus "OTP failed" versus "insufficient balance." Identify the error code for unregistered wallets in your gateway documentation. This ensures that customer service correctly routes unregistered-wallet inquiries to registration guidance rather than payment retry instructions.
Merchant note: WE's dealer network is smaller than Vodafone or e& Egypt's, which may mean some WE subscribers in smaller cities have fewer registration touchpoints available. Point customers to the WE Cash app as the most accessible registration channel — it does not require visiting a physical store if national ID verification can be completed digitally.
3

OTP not received or times out before customer enters it

Merchant + Customer

WE Cash payments require OTP confirmation — after the customer enters their 015 number at checkout, Telecom Egypt's payment system sends a one-time password to the registered number. The customer must enter this OTP within the payment session window to complete the transaction. If the OTP does not arrive or expires before the customer enters it, the payment fails and the order is not placed.

WE network coverage is less extensive than Vodafone or Orange Egypt, particularly in rural areas. Customers in areas with limited WE network signal may experience SMS delivery delays that cause the payment session to expire before the OTP arrives. If the gateway's session timeout is set to the default 60–90 seconds, this is particularly likely on WE's network. Extending the session timeout to 180 seconds significantly reduces this failure mode for WE Cash payments specifically.

Other OTP delivery failures include: the customer has a dual-SIM phone where the WE SIM is not the active SIM at the time of the OTP; the customer's 015 number registered to the WE Cash wallet differs from the number entered at checkout (particularly for customers who recently changed their WE number); the customer's WE SIM is a data-only SIM without SMS service enabled; or the WE network is congested during peak hours. Some customers also confuse receiving the OTP via the WE Cash app notification versus by SMS — the delivery channel depends on the customer's wallet configuration.

On the merchant side, the OTP session timeout for WE Cash is typically configurable in the payment gateway's integration settings. Increasing this timeout from the default to 3–5 minutes is the single most effective action a merchant can take to reduce OTP-related WE Cash failures.

How to fix

  1. Increase the payment session timeout in gateway settings: Log in to your gateway dashboard and find the session expiry or OTP timeout setting for the WE Cash integration. Increase it from the default (60–90 seconds) to at least 3 minutes (180 seconds). This is especially important for WE Cash given WE's network is newer and coverage is less dense than Vodafone or Orange in some areas.
  2. Advise customers to have their WE phone ready with signal before checkout: Add a note to the WE Cash payment option: "You will receive an OTP on your registered WE number — make sure your WE SIM has signal and is the active SIM on dual-SIM phones before selecting this option." Customers who understand the OTP flow are less likely to be caught off guard.
  3. Remind customers to check the WE Cash app for OTP notifications: In some configurations, the WE Cash confirmation arrives as an in-app notification rather than by SMS. If a customer reports not receiving an SMS OTP, advise them to check the WE Cash app on their phone for a payment authorization prompt.
  4. Distinguish OTP timeout from OTP non-delivery in gateway logs: Session expired and OTP not delivered are different error codes in the gateway response. Timeout errors point to a session duration fix. Non-delivery errors point to customer-side issues (network signal, wrong SIM active, number mismatch). Knowing which occurred guides the correct resolution path.
  5. Provide a fallback payment option after OTP failure: If the OTP step fails, the customer should be able to switch to another payment method — Fawry, Meeza, or bank transfer — without restarting the entire order. Display alternatives clearly on the payment error screen.
4

Insufficient wallet balance — wallet pre-loading required

Customer Issue

WE Cash is a pre-funded wallet — the customer must load funds into their wallet in advance before it can be used for purchases. If the WE Cash wallet balance is less than the order total, the payment is declined at the Telecom Egypt payment system level before the OTP step completes. The customer receives an "insufficient funds" or "payment declined" error that is often mistaken for a technical checkout failure rather than a balance shortfall.

The pre-funded nature of WE Cash is fundamentally different from a debit card or direct bank transfer. Customers who are accustomed to debit card payments may not realize that their WE Cash wallet is a separate account requiring explicit top-up that is independent of their bank account balance. A customer with sufficient funds in their bank may be confused by an "insufficient funds" WE Cash error because they assume the wallet draws from their bank — it does not unless the customer has set up a bank-to-wallet auto-load.

Customers can load their WE Cash wallet through: the WE Cash app using a bank card or bank transfer; at Telecom Egypt WE stores or authorized dealers (cash deposit); and at ATMs of compatible Egyptian banks. WE's dealer network is smaller than Vodafone or Orange, so in-person loading may be less convenient for WE customers in areas without a nearby WE store. This makes the WE Cash app the most accessible loading channel for most customers.

For merchants, insufficient balance declines appear in the gateway logs as a balance-related decline code. There is no technical action the merchant can take to resolve a specific customer's balance issue — the customer must load funds and retry. Merchants can reduce this friction by displaying the order amount clearly before the customer enters their WE number.

How to fix

  1. Display the order total prominently before the WE Cash OTP step: Show the required amount and a "this will be charged to your WE Cash wallet" message before the customer enters their 015 number. This allows customers to confirm they have sufficient balance before initiating the OTP sequence, reducing wasted payment attempts that fail due to insufficient funds.
  2. Provide wallet top-up guidance on the payment failure page: If WE Cash is declined with an insufficient balance error, show: "To top up your WE Cash wallet: use the WE Cash app, visit a Telecom Egypt / WE store, or transfer from your bank app. Then return and retry your order." Link to the WE Cash app download where possible.
  3. Offer alternative payment methods for high-value orders: If your average order value is high relative to typical mobile wallet balances, ensure bank transfer and Fawry are prominently available at checkout. These options do not require pre-funding and can accommodate any order value.
  4. Add balance-awareness guidance for products with higher prices: For items priced above EGP 1,000–2,000, add a note near the buy button: "Paying with WE Cash? Make sure your wallet is topped up to the full order amount before checkout." This sets expectations early and prevents balance failures at the final payment step.
5

CBE daily or monthly transaction limit exceeded

Customer Issue

The Central Bank of Egypt sets regulatory transaction limits for all mobile wallets operating in Egypt, including WE Cash. These limits apply to all WE Cash users regardless of their wallet balance and are enforced at the Telecom Egypt payment system level. When a customer's daily or monthly transaction limit has been reached, their payment is declined even if their wallet has sufficient funds.

The CBE's tiered limit structure is based on the customer's verification level. Unverified wallets (registered with basic national ID) have lower daily and monthly transaction limits and a lower maximum balance ceiling (around EGP 10,000). Fully verified wallets require extended KYC — typically face verification or in-person confirmation — and have significantly higher limits. A WE Cash customer with an unverified wallet attempting a high-value purchase may find the order total exceeds their daily limit even if it does not exceed their wallet balance.

From the merchant's perspective, a CBE limit exceeded error and a balance error look similar in the gateway response — both are declined at the Telecom Egypt system level before the transaction completes. The distinction matters for customer guidance: a balance error requires topping up the wallet, while a limit error requires either waiting for the limit to reset (daily limits typically reset at midnight) or completing wallet verification to access a higher tier.

Merchants selling higher-value items — electronics, furniture, premium software bundles — will encounter CBE limit errors frequently among customers with unverified WE Cash wallets. The practical solution for high-value orders is to offer bank transfer as an alternative, since Egyptian bank transfers do not carry CBE mobile wallet tier limits.

How to fix

  1. Advise customers on wallet tier upgrades: Customers who encounter CBE limit errors should be advised to upgrade their WE Cash wallet to the verified tier. Verification can typically be completed through the WE Cash app or at a Telecom Egypt store with a national ID. A verified wallet has substantially higher daily and monthly transaction limits.
  2. Display limit guidance for high-value carts: If your store has products priced above EGP 1,500–2,000, add a note near the WE Cash payment option for high-value carts: "Note: WE Cash transaction limits apply based on your wallet verification tier. For higher-value orders, choose bank transfer or complete WE Cash wallet verification first." This sets expectations before the payment attempt fails.
  3. Offer bank transfer as the primary alternative for large orders: Egyptian bank transfers carry no CBE mobile wallet limits and can accommodate any order size. For order values where CBE limit errors are frequent with unverified mobile wallets, make bank transfer prominently available with clear instructions.
  4. Distinguish limit errors from balance errors in customer service: When a customer contacts you after a declined WE Cash payment, the gateway error code tells you whether it is a balance issue or a regulatory limit issue. A balance error requires topping up. A limit error requires waiting for the daily reset or upgrading verification tier. Providing the correct guidance prevents customers from topping up when the real problem is a regulatory limit.
Merchant note: CBE mobile wallet limits are reviewed periodically. The tier structure described here reflects 2026 CBE guidelines for Egyptian mobile wallets. Monitor CBE announcements annually — limits may increase as Egypt's digital payments adoption expands.
6

Gateway callback not configured — order stuck in pending after customer pays

Merchant Issue

A Shopify order stuck in pending status after a WE Cash payment is almost always caused by a missing or broken webhook callback URL. WE Cash is an asynchronous payment method — the payment flow is: (1) customer selects WE Cash and enters their 015 number; (2) Telecom Egypt sends the customer an OTP; (3) customer approves the payment via OTP; (4) Telecom Egypt's payment system notifies the payment gateway that the payment was approved; (5) the gateway notifies Shopify via a webhook callback; (6) Shopify marks the order as paid. If step 5 fails because the callback URL is misconfigured or unreachable, the payment never reaches Shopify, and the order stays in pending indefinitely.

This is the most dangerous WE Cash failure mode because it results in the customer having paid — their 015 wallet is debited and they have received a payment confirmation from WE — while the merchant's Shopify system shows no completed payment. Without a process to cross-check pending orders against gateway transaction records, the merchant may never fulfill the order, leading to customer disputes and potential chargebacks.

The most common cause of callback failures is a localhost or development URL left in the production gateway configuration. During development and testing, merchants configure callback URLs pointing to local addresses (localhost, 127.0.0.1, ngrok tunnels). When the merchant goes live, if the callback URL is not updated to the production store address, all payment confirmations from Telecom Egypt's system are sent to an unreachable address and silently fail. No error appears in the Shopify admin — only the gateway's logs show repeated failed callback attempts.

A related issue occurs when the Shopify store domain changes after the initial gateway setup: a custom domain is added, HTTPS is enforced, or the store migrates to a new URL. If the callback URL in the gateway dashboard still points to the old domain or HTTP address, callbacks fail silently and all WE Cash payments result in stuck pending orders.

How to fix

  1. Check and update the callback URL in your gateway dashboard immediately: Log in to your Paymob (or gateway) dashboard and find the webhook or callback URL configuration for the WE Cash integration. The callback URL must be the production Shopify payment confirmation endpoint — a publicly accessible HTTPS URL matching your current live store domain. It must not be a localhost address, development tunnel, or HTTP URL.
  2. Verify the callback URL is reachable from the public internet: Use curl or a webhook testing service to confirm that a POST request to the configured callback URL returns a 200 response. If the URL returns 404, a redirect, or times out, update it and retest. A non-200 response means payment confirmations cannot reach Shopify.
  3. Manually reconcile currently stuck pending orders: Log in to your payment gateway's merchant dashboard and review completed WE Cash transactions for the past 7 days. Match them against pending Shopify orders. For each matched order, manually mark it as paid in Shopify admin and fulfill it. Contact your gateway support if a settlement reconciliation is needed for any order where payment was debited but not confirmed.
  4. Set up a monitoring alert for orders stuck in pending: Configure a Shopify alert (via a Shopify app or scheduled script) to notify you when any order has been in pending status for more than 30 minutes. Pending orders that do not resolve quickly indicate a callback failure requiring immediate attention before they become customer disputes.
  5. Test the complete payment and callback flow after configuration: After updating the callback URL, complete a real WE Cash payment for a small test amount through your live Shopify checkout. Confirm that: the OTP is received, the payment is approved, the order in Shopify updates from pending to paid within 60 seconds, and the transaction appears as settled in gateway logs. Only a successful real-money end-to-end test confirms the callback is working correctly.
Critical: Do not ignore Shopify orders stuck in pending after WE Cash payments. These represent real money deducted from the customer's wallet with no fulfillment. Check your gateway transaction logs daily and cross-reference against pending Shopify orders. Every unmatched WE Cash payment is a potential dispute requiring manual reconciliation.
7

015 number rejected — checkout or gateway configured only for Vodafone Cash 010 numbers

Merchant Issue

A common but subtle failure occurs when the merchant's checkout or gateway is configured to accept Egyptian mobile wallet payments but only recognizes Vodafone Cash 010 numbers. When a WE Egypt customer (015 prefix) enters their number, the payment widget rejects it with an "invalid mobile number," "unsupported carrier," or "account not found" error — even if the customer's WE Cash wallet is registered, funded, and fully active.

This failure occurs in two ways. First, the gateway may have only Vodafone Cash enabled — the payment option at checkout is labeled for Vodafone Cash, the WE customer attempts to enter their 015 number, and the gateway rejects the non-Vodafone number at the API lookup stage. Second, some custom checkout implementations include client-side validation that checks for a 010 prefix and flags all other numbers as invalid before the payment even reaches the gateway. A merchant who built or customized the mobile wallet input may have hard-coded a 010 prefix check without accounting for 011, 012, or 015 prefixes.

Egyptian carrier prefixes: Vodafone Egypt uses 010, e& Egypt (formerly Etisalat) uses 011, Orange Egypt uses 012, and Telecom Egypt (WE) uses 015. A checkout that validates "must start with 010" inadvertently locks out all non-Vodafone mobile wallet users. Given that WE is Egypt's fourth carrier, 015 customers are the most frequently excluded group when a checkout is Vodafone-only configured — WE subscribers have fewer available payment gateway options than the other three carriers.

The resolution requires either enabling WE Cash as a separate payment method in the gateway dashboard (which creates a checkout input specifically for 015 numbers), or updating client-side validation to accept 015 entries and route them through the WE Cash API. Both require a gateway that actually supports WE Cash — if the gateway only supports Vodafone Cash, 015 numbers cannot be accepted for wallet payments regardless of validation changes.

How to fix

  1. Enable WE Cash as a separate payment method in your gateway dashboard: Do not attempt to route WE Cash through the Vodafone Cash integration — they use different carrier APIs. Log in to your gateway dashboard and enable WE Cash as a distinct payment option with its own 015 number input, separate from Vodafone Cash (010), e& Cash (011), and Orange Cash (012).
  2. Remove or update client-side validation that enforces 010-only prefix: If your checkout form has JavaScript validation accepting only numbers starting with 010, update it to also accept 011 (e&), 012 (Orange), and 015 (WE) numbers, routing each prefix to its respective wallet API. A carrier-agnostic number input with server-side routing is cleaner than four separate labeled inputs.
  3. Test the checkout from a 015 number explicitly: After enabling WE Cash and updating any validation, test by entering a 015 number in the mobile wallet field. Confirm it is accepted (not flagged as invalid), that an OTP is received, and that a payment completes. Testing with only a 010 number confirms Vodafone Cash works — it does not confirm WE Cash works.
  4. Add carrier-specific helper text to mobile wallet inputs: If you offer multiple Egyptian carrier wallets as separate checkout options, label each clearly: "Vodafone Cash (010)," "e& Cash / Etisalat (011)," "Orange Cash (012)," and "WE Cash / Telecom Egypt (015)." Clear labeling prevents customers from entering their 015 number in the Vodafone Cash field and receiving a confusing rejection.
  5. Confirm your gateway plan covers WE Cash API access before troubleshooting validation: If you cannot enable WE Cash in the dashboard despite following the steps above, contact your gateway provider to confirm whether WE Cash API access is included in your plan. WE Cash may require a plan upgrade or separate activation agreement due to WE's smaller market share and more recent integration timeline.
Merchant note: The 015 prefix rejection is the most common WE Cash complaint because it is invisible to the merchant — they never see the WE customer's failed attempt unless they monitor gateway error logs. WE (Telecom Egypt) has several million subscribers in Egypt, particularly among broadband customers who also took a WE mobile SIM. For stores targeting a broad Egyptian audience, explicitly confirming that 015 entries are accepted is a required checkout audit step.

WE Cash Shopify Integration Diagnostic Checklist

Gateway confirmed to support WE Cash on your plan — verified with gateway provider (Paymob) that WE Cash (Telecom Egypt, 015 prefix) is available on your plan tier and has been enabled as a distinct payment method.

WE Cash enabled in gateway dashboard — WE Cash is toggled on as an active payment method in the merchant's gateway settings, separate from Vodafone Cash, e& Cash, and card payment activations.

Production callback/webhook URL configured and reachable — the callback URL in the gateway dashboard is the production Shopify store URL (HTTPS, current domain), not a localhost or development address, and returns a 200 response to a test POST request.

Checkout currency is EGP — store is priced in EGP, or Shopify Markets is configured to present EGP to Egyptian customers. WE Cash processes EGP only.

Wallet registration guidance available to customers — a help note or FAQ near the WE Cash option explains that a separate wallet registration is required (WE Cash app or WE / Telecom Egypt store with national ID) for first-time users.

End-to-end test with a real WE Cash wallet completed — a test payment was made through the live checkout with a real registered WE Cash 015 wallet, OTP was received and entered, and the Shopify order updated from pending to paid within 60 seconds.

Fallback payment methods available — customers who cannot use WE Cash (wallet not registered, OTP failure, insufficient balance, gateway not supporting 015) have clear alternatives: Fawry offline code, Meeza card, or bank transfer.
