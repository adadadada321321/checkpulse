Shopify Shipping Not Showing at Checkout — 8 Failure Modes Explained

No shipping options at Shopify checkout? Shipping section completely blank? Carrier rates disappeared after an app install? Rates showing for some products but not others? Here are all 8 root causes for Shopify shipping not appearing at checkout — with exact diagnostic steps and fixes for each.

Updated June 2026 8 failure modes Shipping Zones · Carrier Rates · Plan Limits · Weight Conditions · Shipping Apps · Digital Products · Shipping Profiles

When Shopify checkout shows no shipping options, the buyer cannot proceed past the shipping step — checkout is broken. Unlike payment failures, which often produce an error message, a missing shipping section shows the buyer either a blank area where rates should appear or a persistent loading spinner. There is no error code, no alert in Shopify Admin, and no notification sent to the merchant.

The critical detail: Shopify calculates shipping rates at the moment the buyer enters their delivery address. If zero rates match — from any combination of zone, conditions, plan restrictions, or app failures — the "Continue to payment" button stays greyed out and the buyer is stuck. Because each buyer's address is different, a shipping failure may affect buyers in one country but not another, or buyers with low-value carts but not high-value carts, making it nearly impossible to detect without monitoring.

How Shopify Selects Which Shipping Rates to Show
Shopify evaluates rates in this order: (1) Find all shipping profiles that contain the products in the cart. (2) For each profile, find zones that include the buyer's delivery country and state/province. (3) For each matching zone, filter rates by their conditions (minimum/maximum weight, minimum/maximum order value). (4) If any rates use carrier-calculated pricing, fetch real-time quotes from carriers. (5) Show all rates that pass every filter.

A failure at any step produces zero results and a blank shipping section. Most merchants only discover the failure when a customer reports it — by which time revenue has already been lost.

Failure Mode 1: No Shipping Zone Covers the Buyer's Address

The most common reason Shopify shows no shipping options is that no shipping zone has been configured for the buyer's country — or the buyer's country is in a zone but the specific state or province is not covered. Shopify does not automatically create zones; every country you ship to must be explicitly added to at least one zone in at least one shipping profile.

How to check your zone coverage

  1. Admin → Settings → Shipping and delivery
  2. Under "Shipping" → click "Manage rates" (next to your General shipping profile)
  3. Review every zone listed — verify the buyer's country appears in at least one zone
  4. Click on a zone to expand it — check that it has at least one rate with no unfulfillable condition
  5. If the buyer's country is missing: click "Create zone" → add the country → add at least one rate to the new zone

Each Shipping Profile Is Independent
If your store has custom shipping profiles (for oversized items, hazardous materials, or specific brands), those profiles maintain their own zone lists independently of the General profile. A product assigned to a custom profile must have zones configured in that custom profile — the General profile zones do not apply. To check: Admin → Settings → Shipping and delivery → scroll down to see all profiles. Each product can be in only one profile at a time.

Failure Mode 2: Carrier-Calculated Shipping Not Available on Current Plan

Real-time carrier-calculated rates (live quotes from UPS, FedEx, USPS, DHL, Canada Post, etc.) are a premium Shopify feature that is not included on all plans. If you configured carrier-calculated rates in your shipping settings but your plan does not support them, those rates appear in your Admin but are never shown to buyers — resulting in a blank shipping section.

Plan availability for carrier-calculated rates

Shopify Plan Carrier-Calculated Rates Notes
Basic (monthly billing) Not included Requires paid add-on ($20/month)
Basic (annual billing) Included Annual billing unlocks carrier rates on Basic
Shopify plan Included Both monthly and annual
Advanced and Plus Included All billing cycles
Starter plan Not available Starter plan does not support custom shipping

How to confirm carrier-calculated rates are working

  1. Admin → Settings → Shipping and delivery → Manage rates
  2. Click into a zone that should show carrier rates
  3. Look for rates listed as "UPS", "FedEx", "USPS", or your carrier name
  4. If you see a yellow warning banner saying "Upgrade your plan to use carrier-calculated rates" — your plan does not support them
  5. Fix option A: Upgrade to Shopify plan or switch to annual billing
  6. Fix option B: Delete the carrier-calculated rates and add manual flat rates as a temporary workaround
Silent Failure — No Error Shown to Buyer
When carrier-calculated rates are configured but unavailable on your plan, Shopify does not show buyers an error. The shipping section either stays blank or shows a spinning loader that never resolves. The buyer cannot proceed to payment. This is one of the most common causes of complete shipping section absence on newly created stores that configured carrier rates before verifying plan eligibility.

Failure Mode 3: Weight or Price Conditions the Cart Does Not Satisfy

Every shipping rate in Shopify can have optional conditions: a minimum order weight, maximum order weight, minimum order value, or maximum order value. If every rate in a zone has at least one condition the buyer's cart does not meet, zero rates appear — even though the zone is configured correctly and the buyer's address is covered.

Common condition mismatches

  • Free shipping rate with minimum order value: You set up "Free Standard Shipping (orders over $75)" but no paid shipping rate. Buyers with a $60 cart see no shipping options — not even a paid rate — because the only rate available requires $75+.
  • All rates have minimum weight and products have no weight set: Rates configured with minimum weight conditions (e.g., "Economy Shipping — minimum 0.5 kg") will not appear for products where the weight field is blank (defaults to 0). Shopify treats weight = 0 as a cart weight of 0, which fails the minimum weight condition.
  • Weight-based tiers with gap: You have "Standard (0–5 kg)" and "Freight (over 20 kg)" but no rate covering 5–20 kg. A 10 kg cart sees no options because it falls in the gap.
  • Maximum order value that excludes high-value orders: A rate limited to "orders under $500" combined with no other rate means buyers with a $600 order see no shipping.

How to audit rate conditions

  1. Admin → Settings → Shipping and delivery → Manage rates
  2. Click on a zone → click on each rate to see its conditions
  3. Note any minimum/maximum weight or price conditions
  4. Test your checkout with a cart that does NOT meet these conditions — confirm no rates appear
  5. Add an unconditional fallback rate (e.g., "Standard Shipping — $X, no conditions") to ensure every cart has at least one option

The Fallback Rate Rule
Best practice: every shipping zone should have at least one rate with no conditions attached. This fallback rate appears for any cart in that zone regardless of weight or value. If you want to incentivize higher-value orders with free shipping, keep your conditional "Free Shipping over $X" rate AND a paid fallback rate. Remove the fallback only when you are certain every possible cart will qualify for another rate.

Failure Mode 4: Product Not Marked as a Physical Product

Shopify only calculates shipping for products marked as physical products requiring shipping. If the "This is a physical product" checkbox is unchecked on a product — which is the default for digital products and services — Shopify treats it as a non-shippable item. A cart containing only non-shippable products shows no shipping section at checkout at all.

When this happens

  • Bulk import toggled the flag off: A CSV product import that didn't include the Requires Shipping column defaults to "no shipping required" for all imported products.
  • App created the product without the flag: Some product creation apps, dropshipping apps, or print-on-demand apps create products without setting the requires-shipping flag.
  • Digital-physical hybrid cart: A cart with one digital product and one physical product only shows shipping for the physical product's weight and shipping profile. If the only product in the cart is digital, no shipping section appears.
  • Product duplicated from a digital product: Duplicating a digital product in Shopify copies the product type and the requires-shipping = false setting.

How to check and fix

  1. Admin → Products → click the product in question
  2. Scroll to the "Shipping" section on the product page
  3. Check the "This is a physical product" checkbox — if unchecked, shipping is suppressed
  4. Check it on and save the product
  5. Also verify the weight field is filled in — a physical product with weight = 0 may still fail weight-based rate conditions
Bulk Check for Non-Shippable Products
To audit all products at once: Admin → Products → Export → All products as CSV. Open the CSV and filter the Variant Requires Shipping column for "FALSE". Any product variant showing FALSE will not trigger shipping at checkout. Update those rows to "TRUE" and re-import, or fix them individually in the Admin.

Failure Mode 5: Third-Party Shipping App Failing to Return Rates

Shipping apps that provide carrier-calculated rates (EasyPost, ShipStation, Shippo, ShipBob, Australia Post, etc.) connect to Shopify as "carrier service" integrations. When a buyer enters their address, Shopify makes a real-time API call to the app asking for available rates. If the app's API is down, times out, or returns an error, Shopify receives zero rates from the app — and if no manual backup rates exist, the shipping section is blank.

Root causes of shipping app rate fetch failures

  • App API key expired or revoked: Many shipping apps use API keys tied to the merchant's account in the carrier platform. If the API key is rotated, deleted, or the carrier account subscription lapses, the app's rate fetch returns authentication errors and Shopify gets zero rates.
  • Product has no weight or dimensions set: Carrier-calculated rates require product weight (and sometimes dimensions) to compute rates. If any product in the cart has a blank weight field, the API request to the carrier may fail validation and return no rates, or return an error that Shopify interprets as zero available rates.
  • App was uninstalled without removing carrier service: If a shipping app is uninstalled from Shopify, it should remove its carrier service registration. In some cases the registration persists as a ghost — Shopify still tries to fetch rates from the uninstalled app, the request fails, and no rates appear. Fix: Admin → Settings → Shipping → check for carrier services from apps that are no longer installed.
  • Rate fetch timeout: Shopify waits up to 10 seconds for carrier rate responses. If the app's server takes longer, Shopify times out and shows zero rates. This typically affects stores during peak traffic periods when carrier API servers are under load.
  • Origin address not configured in shipping app: Many shipping apps require you to set your warehouse/origin address in their own settings (separate from Shopify's store address). Without an origin address, the distance-based rate calculation fails.

Always Configure Manual Fallback Rates Alongside App Rates
Shipping apps have outages, rate limits, and maintenance windows. If your store's shipping depends entirely on an app to provide rates, any app disruption makes your entire checkout inaccessible. Add manual flat-rate or free-shipping rates in your zones as fallbacks. These appear when the app fails to return rates, keeping checkout functional during app downtime.

Failure Mode 6: Local Delivery or Local Pickup Replacing Shipping

Shopify has three fulfillment types: shipping, local delivery, and local pickup. When local delivery or pickup is configured for a location, Shopify may show those options instead of — or in addition to — standard shipping rates. In some configurations, a buyer who enters an address near your store location sees only local delivery options, with no standard shipping options visible even though standard shipping is available.

How local delivery suppresses shipping options

  • Shopify determines the buyer's proximity based on their delivery address postal code. If the postal code falls within your local delivery radius, Shopify presents local delivery rates and may hide standard shipping depending on your settings.
  • Local pickup shows "Pick up at [location]" as the only option when the store has only one location configured as a pickup location and the buyer's product is assigned to that location's inventory.
  • The issue becomes a failure when local delivery is configured but not actually offered — for example, a store that activated local delivery accidentally or as a test and never set up delivery rates. The buyer in the local radius sees "Local delivery" as their only option but no standard shipping rates, and local delivery rates may also be unconfigured.

How to check local delivery configuration

  1. Admin → Settings → Shipping and delivery → scroll to "Local delivery"
  2. Check which locations have local delivery enabled
  3. For each location with local delivery: verify the delivery radius and rates are intentional
  4. If local delivery was enabled accidentally: toggle it off to restore standard shipping visibility for buyers in that radius
  5. If you want both options: local delivery and standard shipping can coexist — but the local delivery location must have rates configured, and the shipping profile must have a zone covering the buyer's region
Local Pickup Requires Inventory at the Pickup Location
Local pickup only appears as an option if the products in the buyer's cart have inventory at the pickup location. If you moved inventory or adjusted location assignments and local pickup stopped showing (or started incorrectly suppressing shipping), check: Admin → Products → [product] → Inventory → confirm which locations hold stock for this product.

Failure Mode 7: Conflicting Shipping Profiles Producing Zero Matches

When a buyer's cart contains products from multiple shipping profiles, Shopify calculates shipping by taking the intersection — it only shows rates that are available across all profiles for the buyer's zone. If two profiles have no overlapping rates for the buyer's zone, the result is zero shipping options displayed at checkout. This is a particularly tricky failure because each individual profile works fine when tested with products from that profile alone.

How to reproduce and diagnose

  1. Identify which products belong to which shipping profile: Admin → Settings → Shipping and delivery → scroll down — each profile lists its assigned products
  2. Test checkout with only products from Profile A → verify shipping shows correctly
  3. Test checkout with only products from Profile B → verify shipping shows correctly
  4. Test checkout with products from both Profile A and Profile B together → if shipping disappears, you have a profile conflict
  5. Fix: ensure both profiles have at least one overlapping rate for every zone where buyers might have multi-profile carts — or add the same fallback rate to both profiles
Profile Conflict Scenario Result at Checkout Fix
Profile A has "Domestic US" zone, Profile B has "US + Canada" zone Works for US-only carts; blank for Canada if Profile A missing Canada zone Add Canada zone to Profile A or consolidate profiles
Profile A has free shipping, Profile B has weight-based rates only Free shipping disappears when Profile B product added to cart Add an unconditional rate to Profile B
Custom profile product added to cart by app without profile assignment New product defaults to General profile — may differ from cart's custom profiles Explicitly assign app-created products to correct shipping profile
Dropshipping profile has overseas origin, General profile has domestic rates only Mixed cart fails for all international zones Add international rates to General profile or separate the profiles more carefully
Mixed-Profile Carts Are the Hardest Failures to Detect
Profile conflict failures are invisible during normal testing because most merchants test with one product at a time. The failure only appears when a buyer adds products from two different profiles in the same cart — which is a normal buying behavior. Monitoring tools that simulate full checkout flows are the only reliable way to catch profile conflicts in production.

Failure Mode 8: Shopify Markets Shipping Configuration Mismatch

Shopify Markets (for international selling) allows you to configure different pricing, currencies, and — critically — different shipping profiles per market. A shipping failure specific to buyers in one country or currency zone is often caused by a market that has no shipping profile assigned, or a market assigned to a profile that has no rates covering that market's countries.

How Markets affect shipping rate display

  • Each market can override shipping settings: In Admin → Settings → Markets → [market] → Shipping, you can assign a specific shipping profile to buyers in that market. If the assigned profile has no zone for that country, buyers in that market see no rates.
  • New markets default to the General profile: When you create a new market (for example, "Southeast Asia"), it inherits the General shipping profile. If your General profile only has zones for US, UK, and EU, all Southeast Asian buyers see no shipping options until you add zones or assign a different profile.
  • Currency-specific markets with different minimum order thresholds: Free shipping conditions set in USD may not translate correctly to markets priced in other currencies, causing conditional rates to fail for buyers in those markets.
  1. Admin → Settings → Markets → click the market where buyers report no shipping
  2. Scroll to "Shipping" within the market settings
  3. Verify which shipping profile is assigned to this market
  4. Open that profile → verify there is a zone covering the market's countries with at least one active rate
  5. If the market uses a different currency: test checkout in that currency to verify price-based conditions (free shipping thresholds) work correctly after currency conversion
Test Each Market Independently
When diagnosing international shipping failures, always simulate checkout from the buyer's country using the correct currency. Shopify's checkout preview tool (Admin → Online store → Themes → Preview) combined with a VPN to the buyer's country is the most reliable diagnostic method. A shipping rate that shows correctly in USD on US checkout may not appear for a buyer in Japan using JPY if JPY is not in your carrier's accepted currency list.
