CAUSE 01 / 08
Most Common

Gift Card Balance Depleted

A Shopify gift card is a prepaid payment instrument — once the balance is spent, the card is empty. The most common scenario: a customer uses part of a gift card balance on a previous order, then returns and enters the same code expecting the full original value. Shopify accurately reports the remaining balance, but customers frequently don't track partial usage.

This is also triggered when merchants issue gift cards and the recipient has already redeemed the full value but contacts support claiming it "doesn't work" on a new purchase.

FIX: Admin → Products → Gift cards → search the gift card code → check "Remaining balance." If the balance is $0, the card is fully spent. If there is a partial remaining balance, the card will apply only that amount — the customer must cover the rest with another payment method. To issue a replacement: create a new gift card for the appropriate amount and send it to the customer. You cannot add balance to an existing Shopify gift card after it's been issued.
CAUSE 02 / 08
Configuration

Gift Card Deactivated

Merchants can manually disable a gift card in Admin — for example, when a gift card was issued in error, for a fraudulent order, or as part of a refund reversal. A deactivated card shows a Status of "Disabled" and cannot be redeemed regardless of remaining balance.

Deactivation also happens automatically in some third-party gift card apps when they detect suspicious usage patterns or when a linked order is refunded or chargebacked.

FIX: Admin → Products → Gift cards → find the card → check Status. If it shows "Disabled," click "Enable" to reactivate it — take effect immediately. Before reactivating, verify the card was disabled for a legitimate reason (fraud prevention, refund) to avoid re-enabling a card that was intentionally stopped. If the card was disabled by a third-party app, check that app's admin panel for the disable reason before reactivating.
CAUSE 03 / 08
Common in Multi-Store

Wrong Store — Gift Card From a Different Shopify Store

Shopify gift cards are store-specific. A gift card issued by one Shopify store cannot be redeemed at a different Shopify store — even if both stores are owned by the same merchant and share branding. This is one of the most confusing failures for customers who receive a gift card from one storefront and try to use it at another.

Common in multi-store setups: merchants running separate storefronts for different countries (mystore.com vs. mystore.co.uk), different product lines, or a separate B2B store. The card code may look valid, but since it was issued by a different Shopify store instance, Shopify rejects it as "not valid."

FIX: There is no native Shopify way to transfer a gift card balance between stores. Options: (1) direct the customer to use the card at the store that issued it (search Admin → Products → Gift cards at each store to identify which one issued it); (2) manually issue a new gift card at the correct store for the same value and void the original; (3) if this is a recurring issue across your storefronts, consider a third-party gift card app that supports cross-store redemption (Rise.ai, for example, can be configured this way).
CAUSE 04 / 08
Configuration

Gift Card Code Format Error

Shopify gift card codes are typically 16-character alphanumeric strings, often displayed in groups of 4 (e.g., XXXX-XXXX-XXXX-XXXX). Customers who type codes manually are prone to errors: extra spaces, misread characters (0 vs. O, 1 vs. I, 8 vs. B), missing a group, or adding a hyphen where none exists.

Gift card delivery emails sometimes use stylized fonts that make specific characters ambiguous. Even a single wrong character results in "Enter a valid gift card code" — because no matching card exists in the system.

FIX: Ask the customer to copy-paste the code directly from their gift card email rather than typing it manually. In Admin → Products → Gift cards, search for the partial code to find the correct card and verify the exact format. If using a third-party gift card app, check whether that app generates codes in a different format than Shopify native — the customer may be trying to redeem an app-issued code through the native Shopify field, which won't work if the app uses a separate redemption flow.
CAUSE 05 / 08
Configuration

Currency Mismatch (Multi-Currency Stores)

Shopify native gift cards are issued in the store's primary currency. If you have enabled Shopify Markets or a currency conversion app that lets customers check out in their local currency, gift card redemption can break or apply the wrong amount — because the gift card value is in one currency and the checkout total is calculated in another.

Symptoms vary: the card may be rejected outright, apply $0, apply the nominal value in the wrong currency (a $50 USD card applying as £50), or cause a checkout error. This is more common with third-party currency apps that don't fully integrate with Shopify's native gift card system.

FIX: Confirm which currency the gift card was issued in (check the card's details in Admin → Products → Gift cards — the balance shows in the store's primary currency). If the customer is checking out in a different currency, either: (1) advise them to switch the checkout to the store's primary currency if their local currency checkout option allows it; (2) if using a third-party currency app, check that app's documentation for gift card compatibility — some apps require specific settings to allow cross-currency gift card redemption; (3) use a third-party gift card app that explicitly supports multi-currency redemption.
CAUSE 06 / 08
App-Enforced

App-Enforced Gift Card Expiration

Shopify native gift cards do not expire — their balance remains valid indefinitely. However, third-party gift card apps (Rise.ai, GiftUp, Govalo, and others) can set expiration dates on gift cards. If your store uses one of these apps, customers may receive gift cards that expire after 1, 2, or 3 years, or on a custom date set by the merchant.

When an app-managed gift card expires, the error message depends on the app — it may show the app's own "expired" message, or it may simply show Shopify's generic "not valid" message if the app deactivated the card in the background. Customers with no awareness of an expiration date are especially likely to report this as a bug.

FIX: Check the gift card app's admin panel for the specific card's expiration date and status. If expired: (1) extend the expiration date from within the app if the app allows it; (2) issue a new gift card for the same value and void the expired one; (3) consider whether your store's gift card expiration policy is adequately communicated at point of purchase — many customer disputes arise because customers didn't know the card could expire.
CAUSE 07 / 08
Common Confusion

Partial Balance — Remaining Amount Required

When a gift card's remaining balance is less than the order total, Shopify applies the gift card as partial payment and asks the customer to enter a second payment method for the remainder. Many customers expect the gift card to cover the entire order and are confused when Shopify continues to the payment entry step — they interpret the request for a credit card as the gift card "not working."

This is not a technical failure — the gift card is working correctly. But it generates a high volume of support tickets because the Shopify checkout UI doesn't always make clear that the gift card was successfully applied and only a partial balance remains to be covered.

FIX (for individual customers): Explain that the gift card was successfully applied — it covered $X of the order — and that a credit card is needed only for the remaining $Y. Show them the order summary line that reflects the gift card credit. FIX (systemic): Improve the checkout experience by customizing the order summary messaging to explicitly confirm "Gift card applied — $X remaining balance used. Please enter payment for the remaining $Y." Some themes do this well by default; others don't — check your theme's checkout templates or use a checkout customization app.
CAUSE 08 / 08
Silent Failure

Third-Party Gift Card App Failure

Third-party gift card apps (Rise.ai, GiftUp, Govalo, custom Shopify Functions) inject into the checkout pipeline to handle their own gift card issuance, tracking, and redemption. When such an app experiences an API outage, credential expiration, a rate limit, or a conflict with a Shopify platform update, it can fail in two distinct ways:

Soft failure: only the gift card redemption is blocked — customers see "not valid" for cards that have a balance, but can still complete checkout without the gift card applied. Checkout itself works; only the gift card pipeline is broken.

Hard failure: the app crash corrupts the checkout session — customers cannot complete orders at all, whether or not they're entering a gift card. The checkout URL still returns HTTP 200, so standard uptime monitoring shows all-green while every order placement is failing silently.

FIX (soft): Admin → Apps → find the gift card app → check its dashboard for API errors or outage notices. Try disabling the app temporarily to confirm whether native Shopify gift cards start working again. Contact the app developer with the specific gift card code and error message. FIX (hard): If checkout itself is broken, disable the gift card app entirely from Admin → Apps. Run a test checkout without the app to confirm the session is valid. Re-enable only after the app's support team confirms the issue is resolved. Use CheckPulse to be alerted the moment a session-level failure like this occurs — before customers report order failures.
Troubleshooting Flow

5-Step Diagnosis When a Gift Card Is Reported Broken

When a customer reports that a gift card doesn't work, go through these steps in order. You will find the cause in under 5 minutes in most cases.

1

Find the card in Admin

Go to Admin → Products → Gift cards → search for the code the customer reported. Confirm it exists. If it doesn't appear, either the code is from a different store, the code has a typo, or the card was issued via a third-party app (check that app's dashboard separately).

2

Check Remaining balance and Status

Is the balance $0? Is the Status showing "Disabled"? Zero balance is the most common cause and takes 10 seconds to confirm. A disabled card was intentionally deactivated — confirm whether that was intentional before re-enabling.

3

Confirm the store origin

If the card exists but shows valid balance and active status, verify it was issued by this specific Shopify store. If you run multiple storefronts, the card may have been issued by a different store — check each store's gift card admin if you're unsure.

4

Reproduce the failure yourself

Enter the same gift card code at your own checkout. If it works for you but not the customer, the issue may be a code format error (customer mistyped it) or a currency mismatch based on their locale. If it fails for you too, the issue is store-level.

5

Check for gift card app conflicts

If steps 1–4 show nothing wrong, go to Admin → Apps and look for any installed gift card apps. Temporarily disable them and reproduce the test. If the native Shopify gift card starts working after disabling an app, that app is the conflict.

The Monitoring Gap

Why Gift Card App Failures Are Invisible Until Customers Complain

Causes 1–7 are configuration or user-error issues — visible in Admin, fixable in minutes, and preventable with better issuance processes. They don't require monitoring.

Cause 8 — third-party gift card app failure — is different. When the app fails, here is what your standard monitoring stack sees:

What monitoring tools see during a gift card app failure
🔴
Ping monitor: the checkout URL returns HTTP 200. The page loads. The app failure is application-layer — completely invisible to TCP/HTTP checks.
🔴
Uptime Robot / Pingdom: same limitation — they verify the HTTP handshake, not whether the checkout session is valid or the gift card redemption pipeline is intact.
🟡
Shopify Analytics: conversion rate drops are visible after the fact — you see the damage in your dashboard, not before customers abandon or lose their store credit.
🟡
Manual testing: only catches the failure if you happen to test with a gift card during the outage window — intermittent app failures may resolve before you test.
🟢
End-to-end checkout simulation: verifies the full checkout session is valid — including that app-layer failures haven't corrupted the pipeline — on an automated schedule, before any customer is affected.

FAQ

Shopify Gift Card Not Working — Frequently Asked Questions

Why is my Shopify gift card not working at checkout?
The most common reasons: the card has no remaining balance (check Admin → Products → Gift cards → Remaining balance), the card has been deactivated (check Status field), or it was issued by a different Shopify store (gift cards are store-specific). Go to Admin → Products → Gift cards, search the code, and check Status and Remaining balance — the cause is visible in 90% of cases within 2 minutes.
Why does my gift card say "not valid" when the code looks correct?
"This gift card is not valid" in Shopify covers two separate causes: (1) the card was deactivated — check Admin → Products → Gift cards → Status; (2) the card was issued by a different Shopify store — gift cards are store-specific and can't be transferred between stores. If the card exists in Admin with Active status and a positive balance, a third-party gift card app may be overriding native redemption with its own validity logic — try disabling any installed gift card apps and retesting.
Why was my gift card applied but the order total didn't change?
If the card shows as applied but the total is unchanged, the most likely cause is zero remaining balance — the card applied $0 because it was fully spent. Check Admin → Products → Gift cards → Remaining balance on the specific card. If the balance appears positive but the total still didn't change, disable any installed gift card apps and retest — some apps maintain their own balance that differs from what Shopify admin shows, and an app-layer issue may be preventing the credit from being applied correctly.
Can a Shopify gift card be used at multiple stores?
No. Shopify gift cards are tied to the specific store that issued them. Even if you own multiple Shopify stores with shared branding, a gift card from Store A cannot be redeemed at Store B. There is no native Shopify way to transfer gift card balances between stores. If a customer received a gift card from one of your stores and is trying to use it at another, you will need to manually issue a new gift card at the correct store for the same value.
My gift card shows a balance but still fails at checkout — why?
If the card has a positive Remaining balance in Admin but fails at checkout, the most likely causes are: (1) a third-party gift card app is overriding native redemption — the app may have its own balance tracking that differs, or the app may be experiencing an outage; (2) the checkout is in a different currency than the card was issued in — try checking out in the store's primary currency; (3) the code the customer is entering has a typo — ask them to copy-paste directly from the gift card email. Try disabling any installed gift card apps and retesting first — that eliminates the most likely non-obvious cause.
How can a gift card failure break the entire checkout — not just the gift card?
Third-party gift card apps inject into the checkout pipeline. When such an app crashes, it can corrupt the checkout session — not just block gift card redemption, but prevent orders from being placed entirely, with or without a gift card. The checkout URL still returns HTTP 200, so standard uptime monitoring won't catch it. End-to-end checkout simulation — which actually tests whether a checkout session is valid and completeable — is the only automated way to catch this class of failure before customers report order failures and lost store credit.
