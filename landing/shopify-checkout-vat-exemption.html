Shopify B2B Tax & EU Checkout Troubleshooting

Shopify VAT Exemption Not Working — EU B2B VAT Number Entered But Tax Still Charged, VIES Validation, UK Reverse Charge

EU and UK B2B buyers frequently enter their VAT registration number at Shopify checkout and find that tax is still being charged. The VAT number field is not connected to any exemption logic unless a specific 4-layer configuration stack is in place — and none of the layers issue any error when they are missing.

Updated June 2026 Affects: EU/UK B2B stores, cross-border merchants, wholesale buyers with VAT registration
The VAT number field at Shopify checkout is not automatic. Entering a VAT number does not remove VAT unless Shopify Tax is enabled, VIES validation is turned on, and (for authenticated B2B buyers) the company account is marked tax-exempt. UK VAT numbers are not covered by EU VIES — UK reverse charge requires separate configuration or a third-party tax app.
Architecture

How Shopify B2B VAT Exemption Works — The Four-Layer Configuration Stack

Shopify VAT exemption for B2B buyers is not a single toggle. It requires four distinct layers to all be correctly configured. Any single layer missing silently prevents tax removal — with no error in Shopify Admin or at checkout.

Layer 1 — Tax Engine
Shopify Tax Must Be Active
The legacy Shopify tax system does not support VIES validation or automatic VAT exemption. You must be on Shopify Tax (the newer engine). Check: Admin → Settings → Taxes and duties. If you see a 'Migrate to Shopify Tax' option, the migration has not been completed.
Layer 2 — EU VAT Collection
EU VAT Number Collection Enabled
Admin → Settings → Taxes and duties → European Union → enable 'Collect EU VAT numbers.' This adds the VAT number input field to checkout for EU buyers. Without this, no VAT number field appears and no validation is possible.
Layer 3 — VIES Validation
VIES Verification Switched On
In the same EU Taxes section, enable 'Verify EU VAT numbers (VIES).' This connects the entered number to the EU VIES database in real time. A valid result triggers zero-rate VAT. Without VIES enabled, the field is cosmetic — it collects the number but takes no action on tax.
Layer 4 — Company Account
B2B Company Marked Tax-Exempt
For authenticated B2B buyers using Shopify Plus company accounts, the company must be explicitly set to tax-exempt: Admin → Customers → Companies → [Company] → Tax settings. Company-level tax settings override store-level VIES logic for authenticated sessions.
UK Edge Case
UK VAT Numbers — Not Covered by VIES
Post-Brexit, UK VAT numbers (GB prefix) are not in the EU VIES database. Shopify VIES validation returns 'invalid' for any UK number. UK B2B reverse charge must be handled via company-level tax exemption or a third-party tax app.
Common Gap
Legacy Tax = No Exemption
Stores that set up EU taxes before Shopify Tax launched are typically on the legacy system. The VAT number field may appear (from a prior manual configuration) but is not connected to any exemption engine. The field looks functional but does nothing.

Diagnosis Checklist

5-Step EU VAT Exemption Diagnosis

  • 1
    Confirm Shopify Tax (not legacy) is active Go to Admin → Settings → Taxes and duties. Look for a banner or prompt about Shopify Tax. If you see 'Switch to Shopify Tax' or 'Migrate tax service,' the migration has not been completed and you are running the legacy engine. Legacy Shopify tax does not support VIES validation or automated VAT exemption. Complete the Shopify Tax migration before configuring EU VAT exemption. After migration, all your existing tax settings are preserved but the exemption features become available.
  • 2
    Enable 'Collect EU VAT numbers' and 'Verify EU VAT numbers (VIES)' In Admin → Settings → Taxes and duties → scroll to the European Union section. Enable 'Collect EU VAT numbers' — this adds the VAT number input field at checkout for buyers with an EU shipping address. Then enable 'Verify EU VAT numbers (VIES)' — this connects the entered number to the EU VIES database and triggers zero-rate VAT on successful validation. Both must be enabled. Enabling collection without VIES means numbers are collected but not acted upon.
  • 3
    For B2B company account buyers: set company as tax-exempt If your B2B buyers use Shopify Plus company accounts (authenticated sessions), go to Admin → Customers → Companies → [Company name] → scroll to Tax settings. Set 'Collect tax' to disabled (exempt) for this company. Company-level tax settings take precedence over store-level VIES configuration for authenticated buyers. If the company is set to 'collect tax' (taxable), VIES validation will not override it — the buyer will still be charged VAT even if their VIES number validates.
  • 4
    For UK buyers: use company/customer tax-exempt flag or a third-party tax app UK VAT numbers (GB-prefix) are not in the EU VIES database and will fail VIES validation. For UK B2B buyers, VIES-based automatic exemption is not an option. Use one of two approaches: (1) For Shopify Plus company account buyers, mark their company as tax-exempt (same as step 3 above). (2) For non-company-account UK B2B buyers, tag their customer account (Admin → Customers → [Customer]) and create a tax override in Admin → Settings → Taxes and duties → Tax overrides applying 0% to the tag. Alternatively, use a third-party EU/UK tax app (Avalara, TaxJar, Taxamo) that natively handles UK VAT reverse charge rules.
  • 5
    Test the full flow from the buyer's perspective Open your store in an incognito window with a shipping address in Germany (or another EU country where your buyer is located). Add a product to cart, proceed to checkout, and verify: (a) the VAT number field appears at the payment step; (b) entering a valid German USt-IdNr (e.g., DE followed by 9 digits) clears VAT from the order total; (c) an invalid number returns an error and does not remove VAT. For B2B company account buyers, log in as a company buyer and verify the company's tax-exempt setting causes VAT to be absent from checkout entirely — without needing to enter a VAT number at all.
Deep Dive

Three VAT Exemption Failure Modes in Detail

Deep Dive A

The Legacy Tax Trap — Why Your VIES Settings Appear Correct But Do Nothing

This is the most common and confusing VAT exemption failure: merchants who set up EU taxes before Shopify Tax launched have VIES-looking settings in their Admin, but those settings are running on the legacy tax engine and have no effect. The legacy engine does not communicate with VIES — it accepts the number entered by the buyer, stores it in the order metadata, and then applies standard VAT rates regardless.

The confusion comes from the Admin UI, which looks nearly identical whether you are on Shopify Tax or the legacy engine. There is no persistent banner or status indicator on the Taxes and duties page showing which engine is active once you've passed through the initial migration prompt. Merchants who completed the setup years ago and assume they are on Shopify Tax may be on the legacy engine without knowing.

How to check which tax engine is active: Admin → Settings → Taxes and duties Look for: "Powered by Shopify Tax" label at top of page Or: "Upgrade to Shopify Tax" / "Migrate" prompt = still on legacy After confirming Shopify Tax: look for "European Union" section with VIES toggle Legacy engine: EU section exists but VIES has no exemption effect

The fix is completing the Shopify Tax migration. Shopify Tax uses the same tax rates as the legacy engine for standard transactions, so migrating typically does not change your existing tax rates — it adds the VIES-connected exemption capability on top. Always test a German or French shipping address in a separate browser after migration to confirm the VAT number field now removes tax.

Deep Dive B

UK Reverse Charge — Why Shopify Cannot Handle It Automatically

Since January 2021, the UK operates its own VAT system entirely outside EU rules. UK VAT registration numbers (format: GB followed by 9 digits) are maintained by HMRC, not the EU VIES system. When a UK B2B buyer (registered for VAT in the UK) purchases from an EU seller, the VAT reverse charge mechanism should apply: the buyer accounts for VAT on their own return rather than paying it to the seller. Shopify has no built-in support for this UK-specific reverse charge rule.

The practical result: UK buyers entering their VAT number at a Shopify checkout get a VIES 'invalid' response (because VIES doesn't know GB numbers exist) and are either shown an error or charged standard VAT. From the buyer's perspective, the checkout appears broken. From the compliance perspective, the seller is collecting VAT they may not be required to collect for a UK B2B transaction.

UK VAT number format: GB + 9 digits (e.g. GB123456789) VIES lookup result: always "invalid" (HMRC registry, not EU VIES) Shopify automatic exemption: not available for UK numbers Manual workarounds: 1. Shopify Plus company account → mark company tax-exempt manually 2. Customer tag + tax override → Admin → Settings → Taxes → Tax overrides 3. Third-party tax app (Avalara, TaxJar) → native UK reverse charge support

For merchants with significant UK B2B volume, a third-party tax app that integrates with HMRC's VAT API and understands UK reverse charge rules is the production-grade solution. Manual company-level exemptions work for a small, stable list of known UK buyers but do not scale and require the merchant to vet each VAT number independently.

Deep Dive C

VIES Reliability — Why Valid VAT Numbers Sometimes Don't Remove Tax

The EU VIES system is a federation of national VAT registries operated by each member state's tax authority. Shopify's VIES integration queries this federation in real time during checkout. When VIES is fully operational, a valid EU VAT number from a business registered in another EU country triggers zero-rate VAT on the Shopify order within seconds.

The problem: individual national VIES nodes have well-documented reliability issues. Germany's node (BZSt) has scheduled maintenance windows. Italy's node (Agenzia delle Entrate) has historically had availability around 95-97%, meaning roughly 1 in 30 lookups fails. Greece's node is frequently cited by EU tax professionals for its reliability gaps. When a national VIES node is down, all VAT numbers from that country return an error or time out.

VIES lookup result states: "valid" → number confirmed; VAT removed from order "invalid" → number not found; standard VAT applies "unavailable" → national node down; Shopify applies standard VAT (safe fallback) "error" / timeout → same safe fallback; VAT applied Countries with known VIES reliability issues: Germany (DE) → BZSt maintenance windows Italy (IT) → ~97% uptime; failures during peak periods Greece (GR) → documented extended outages Spain (ES) → occasional AEAT system maintenance Buyer workaround for VIES unavailability: Try again in 30-60 minutes If urgent: merchant marks order tax-exempt post-purchase via order edit

When a high-value B2B buyer is blocked by a VIES timeout, the correct short-term solution is to process the order with VAT applied and then correct it post-purchase: go to the order in Admin, use 'Edit order' to adjust the price, or issue a refund for the VAT amount separately. Document the buyer's VAT number in the order notes for your own compliance records. The buyer should receive a corrected invoice showing zero VAT for their own VAT accounting.

FAQ

Shopify VAT Exemption — Common Questions

Why is Shopify still charging VAT after a customer enters their VAT number?
Entering a VAT number does not automatically remove VAT. The field requires all four layers of configuration to be active: (1) Shopify Tax engine (not legacy), (2) 'Collect EU VAT numbers' enabled in Admin → Settings → Taxes → EU section, (3) 'Verify EU VAT numbers (VIES)' enabled, (4) for B2B company account buyers — company marked tax-exempt in company settings. If any one of these is missing, VAT is applied regardless of the number entered. The most common gap is being on the legacy tax engine — check Admin → Settings → Taxes and duties for a Shopify Tax migration prompt.
How do I enable EU VAT exemption for B2B buyers in Shopify?
Step 1: ensure Shopify Tax is active (Admin → Settings → Taxes — no migration prompt should appear). Step 2: enable 'Collect EU VAT numbers' in the European Union section. Step 3: enable 'Verify EU VAT numbers (VIES).' Step 4: for B2B company account buyers, go to Admin → Customers → Companies → [Company] → Tax settings → set to tax-exempt. Test with a real EU shipping address and a valid VAT number from that country (e.g., a German DE + 9 digits number).
Why is Shopify VAT exemption not working for UK B2B buyers?
UK VAT numbers (GB prefix) are not in the EU VIES database. Since Brexit (January 2021), the UK operates its own VAT system through HMRC. Shopify's EU VIES integration returns 'invalid' for all UK numbers — this is expected behavior, not a bug. For UK B2B buyers, use company-level tax exemption (Admin → Customers → Companies → [Company] → Tax settings → exempt) or customer-tag-based tax overrides. Merchants with significant UK B2B volume should use a third-party tax app that natively handles UK reverse charge.
What is VIES validation in Shopify and why does it sometimes fail to remove VAT?
VIES (VAT Information Exchange System) is the EU's federated VAT number database. When Shopify's VIES validation is on, it queries this database in real time. 'Valid' means the number exists — it triggers zero-rate VAT. 'Unavailable' means the national VIES node is temporarily down — Shopify falls back to standard VAT for compliance safety. Known reliability issues: Germany (BZSt maintenance windows), Italy (~97% uptime), Greece (extended outages). A buyer who can't get VAT removed should retry 30-60 minutes later. As a short-term fix, merchants can manually mark the order tax-exempt post-purchase and issue a corrected invoice.
How do I mark a Shopify B2B company as tax-exempt?
Admin → Customers → Companies → select the company → Tax settings → set 'Collect tax' to disabled (exempt). This applies to all buyers authenticated under that company account — they will have VAT removed from checkout without needing to enter a VAT number. For individual customers without a company account, tax exemption is set per-customer: Admin → Customers → [Customer] → Tax settings → exempt. Company-level exemption requires Shopify Plus with B2B company accounts enabled.
