Shopify Afterpay Not Working — Button Not Showing at Checkout (2026)

Afterpay button not appearing at checkout, missing for some customers but not others, or disappeared after working before — here are all 8 failure modes for Afterpay breaking on Shopify, with exact diagnostic steps and fixes for each.

Updated June 2026 8 failure modes Not Enabled · Order Limits · Restricted Products · Customer Account · Unsupported Country · App Conflict · Merchant Review · Clearpay Naming

Afterpay (Clearpay in the UK and Europe) at Shopify checkout is a "dynamic payment method" — Shopify's checkout engine evaluates multiple conditions before deciding whether to display the Afterpay button. If any single condition fails — the order value, the product category, the customer's country, the merchant's account status, or the gateway configuration — Afterpay silently disappears from the payment options without any error message to the customer or alert in your admin.

Unlike a payment processing error, which happens after a customer attempts to pay, Afterpay's absence is a pre-render suppression. The checkout page loads, evaluates eligibility, and simply omits the button if something is wrong. Customers assume you don't offer Afterpay; merchants assume customers aren't using it. The actual problem is invisible unless you know what to check.

Quick Check: Is Afterpay Enabled in Shopify Payments?
Before diagnosing anything else: go to Admin → Settings → Payments → Shopify Payments → Manage and scroll to the "Buy now, pay later" or "Installment payments" section. Afterpay/Clearpay must be toggled on. This setting is separate from enabling Shopify Payments itself — many merchants enable Shopify Payments but miss the secondary toggle that activates Afterpay within it.

Failure Mode 1: Afterpay Not Enabled in Shopify Payments Settings

The most common reason Afterpay doesn't appear at Shopify checkout is that it was never enabled. Afterpay's integration with Shopify works through Shopify Payments as a "buy now, pay later" option, but it has its own activation toggle that is separate from enabling Shopify Payments itself. Merchants who set up Shopify Payments often overlook this secondary step.

Additionally, Afterpay requires you to agree to Afterpay's merchant terms directly within Shopify's admin — enabling the toggle is not sufficient if you haven't accepted the terms. Shopify will prompt you to review and accept Afterpay's merchant agreement the first time you attempt to enable it.

How to enable Afterpay in Shopify Payments

  1. Go to Shopify Admin → Settings → Payments
  2. Under "Shopify Payments", click Manage
  3. Scroll down to find "Buy now, pay later" or "Installment payment providers"
  4. Toggle on Afterpay (labeled as Afterpay in US/AU/NZ/CA, Clearpay in UK/EU)
  5. If prompted, review and accept Afterpay's merchant terms
  6. Save changes — Afterpay will appear at checkout for eligible orders immediately
Afterpay Requires Its Own Merchant Account Behind the Scenes
When you enable Afterpay through Shopify Payments, Shopify creates an Afterpay merchant account on your behalf using your business information. If there are issues with this account creation (name mismatch, business type not eligible, country mismatch), the toggle may appear active but Afterpay won't display. Check for any email from Afterpay to your Shopify account email address confirming merchant account setup.

Failure Mode 2: Order Value Outside Afterpay's Min/Max Thresholds

Afterpay only appears when the order total falls within its supported range. The thresholds vary by country and can change over time, but the general principle applies universally: orders that are too small or too large will not trigger the Afterpay button. This is evaluated in real time — the button may appear as a customer adds items, then disappear if they remove items and the total drops below the minimum.

Typical Afterpay order value thresholds by region

Region Minimum Maximum Notes
United States ~$35 ~$2,000 Exact limits depend on customer's Afterpay account standing and merchant category
Australia ~$35 AUD ~$2,000 AUD Afterpay's home market; limits may be higher for established accounts
United Kingdom (Clearpay) ~£0 ~£1,000 UK minimum is effectively £0 but practical minimum is ~£6 due to installment math
Canada ~$35 CAD ~$2,000 CAD Similar to US thresholds
New Zealand ~$35 NZD ~$2,000 NZD Similar to AU thresholds

These thresholds are approximate and Afterpay updates them periodically. More importantly, individual customers may have different maximum limits based on their Afterpay account age, payment history, and credit assessment. A first-time Afterpay customer often has a lower maximum than an established customer with a strong payment record.

How to verify this is the issue

  1. Add a product to your cart that creates a total clearly within range (e.g., $100 in the US)
  2. Proceed to checkout — Afterpay should appear in the express payment section
  3. If Afterpay appears at $100 but not at $25 or $3,000, the order value thresholds are the cause
  4. Consider adding a message on product pages or in cart view: "Afterpay available for orders $35–$2,000" — this sets expectations and reduces support questions
Afterpay Messaging on Product Pages
Afterpay provides official JavaScript widgets that display installment messaging on product pages ("4 interest-free payments of $X"). These widgets are available through the Afterpay merchant portal and can be added to Shopify via custom theme code or through the Afterpay Shopify app. This messaging proactively communicates eligibility to customers before they reach checkout.

Failure Mode 3: Cart Contains Ineligible Product Categories

Afterpay explicitly prohibits certain product categories from being purchased via its service. This is a contractual restriction baked into Afterpay's merchant agreement. When your cart contains even one item from a restricted category, Afterpay suppresses its button for the entire checkout session — not just for the restricted item. The rest of the cart is clean, but the presence of a single restricted product blocks Afterpay for everything.

Product categories restricted by Afterpay

  • Gift cards and store credit: Physical and digital gift cards cannot be purchased with Afterpay. If a gift card is in the cart alongside regular products, Afterpay will not appear for the entire order. This is one of the most commonly missed restrictions — many merchants sell gift cards alongside physical products and don't realize the cart restriction applies to the whole transaction.
  • Cryptocurrency and financial instruments: Any product that constitutes a financial instrument, digital currency, or investment vehicle is prohibited.
  • Firearms, weapons, and ammunition: Prohibited across all Afterpay markets.
  • Alcohol and tobacco: Restricted in certain markets and merchant categories; some licensed merchants may be exempt.
  • Healthcare and pharmaceutical products: Prescription medications and certain medical devices are restricted. The restrictions on over-the-counter products vary by country.
  • Gambling-related products: Casino chips, lottery tickets, betting credits, and similar items are prohibited.
  • Hazardous materials: Any regulated hazardous substances.
  • Adult content and services: Prohibited across all markets.

Diagnosing a product category restriction

  1. Test checkout with a cart that contains only products from clearly eligible categories (clothing, electronics, home goods)
  2. If Afterpay appears in that test, identify which product category is causing the issue
  3. Add suspected restricted products one at a time to identify the culprit
  4. If gift cards are in your store, consider using a separate checkout flow or making gift cards ineligible for Afterpay at the product level using Shopify's payment method restrictions (available on Shopify Plus with Checkout Functions)
Gift Cards Are the #1 Hidden Afterpay Blocker
The most common product-level reason Afterpay disappears unexpectedly: a gift card is in the cart. Many merchants don't realize customers add gift cards to carts alongside other products. If you see customers complaining that Afterpay isn't available, ask whether they have a gift card in their cart. Shopify Plus merchants can use Checkout Functions to hide gift cards from the cart when Afterpay is selected, or to hide Afterpay when gift cards are present.

Failure Mode 4: Customer's Afterpay Account Is Suspended or Limit Reached

Afterpay's checkout integration evaluates the individual customer's eligibility in real time. If the customer has an Afterpay account that is suspended (due to missed payments, a dispute, or a policy violation), or if they have reached their available spending limit, the Afterpay button will not appear for that customer — even if everything is correctly configured on your store. This is one of the hardest failure modes to diagnose as a merchant because it is entirely on the customer's side.

Afterpay uses a real-time eligibility check during checkout loading. The result is binary — button shows or it doesn't. Customers are not told the reason; they simply don't see Afterpay as an option. This leads to customer complaints like "Afterpay isn't working on your site" when the actual issue is their Afterpay account.

What causes customer-side Afterpay failures

  • Missed payments: A customer with overdue Afterpay installments on another purchase may have their account temporarily suspended until the overdue amount is paid.
  • Spending limit reached: Each customer has a rolling spending limit. New customers have lower limits; the limit increases with successful payment history. If a customer's outstanding Afterpay balance is near or at their limit, new orders above the remaining limit won't show the button.
  • Account age: Brand-new Afterpay accounts have stricter limits. First-time Afterpay users may find the button appears only for lower-value orders.
  • Verification incomplete: In some markets, Afterpay requires ID verification for higher-value purchases. If verification is incomplete, limits are lower.
  • Geographic mismatch: A customer's Afterpay account is region-locked. An Australian customer using an AU Afterpay account cannot use it on a US merchant's store via Shopify Payments.

What to tell customers experiencing this

  1. Ask them to log into their Afterpay account directly at afterpay.com (or clearpay.co.uk) to check their account status and available limit
  2. If they have outstanding installments, they need to pay those first to restore their spending limit
  3. If their account is suspended, they need to contact Afterpay customer support directly — merchants have no visibility into or control over customer Afterpay account status
  4. Suggest alternative payment methods if Afterpay is unavailable for them

Failure Mode 5: Store or Customer Country Not Supported by Afterpay

Afterpay operates in a specific set of countries, and both the merchant's store location and the customer's billing country must be in supported regions. The supported countries are: United States, Australia, New Zealand, Canada, United Kingdom, France, Italy, and Spain. If your store is based in a country outside this list, Afterpay is not available through Shopify Payments regardless of any other configuration.

Even within supported countries, there is a country-matching requirement: a US Afterpay merchant can only process Afterpay for US customers. Shopify's implementation of Afterpay does not support cross-border Afterpay transactions — an Australian customer on a US merchant's store cannot use their Australian Afterpay account.

Shopify Markets and Afterpay Visibility
If you use Shopify Markets to serve customers in multiple regions, Afterpay will only appear for customers in supported markets. A customer assigned to a "Rest of World" market or a market in an unsupported country will not see Afterpay. You can configure which payment methods are available per market under Admin → Settings → Markets → select a market → Payment methods. If Afterpay is not listed as an option for a specific market, it is not available for customers in that region.

Failure Mode 6: Checkout App or Theme Conflict Hiding Afterpay

Checkout customization apps — upsell tools, post-purchase offers, loyalty integrations, checkout UI extensions — can interfere with how payment methods are displayed. When these apps update or introduce new checkout modifications, they can inadvertently hide or conflict with the section where Afterpay appears (the express payment row), causing Afterpay to disappear without any obvious connection to the app change.

This failure mode often presents as "Afterpay was working, then stopped" — and the timing frequently coincides with an app update or a new app being installed, even if you didn't explicitly change payment settings.

How to diagnose an app conflict

  1. Go to Admin → Apps and note which checkout-related apps are installed (look for apps with "checkout", "upsell", "post-purchase", "loyalty" in their names)
  2. Temporarily disable each checkout app one at a time, then test the checkout to see if Afterpay reappears
  3. When Afterpay reappears after disabling a specific app, that app is the culprit
  4. Contact the app developer with the specific conflict — they can often release a fix that makes their app compatible with Afterpay's checkout section
  5. If you're on Shopify Plus using Checkout Extensibility, review your checkout UI extensions in Admin → Settings → Checkout → Customizations for any that may be hiding payment methods
Theme Updates Can Affect BNPL Display
A Shopify theme update, particularly to a theme that modifies the checkout page layout or the express payment section, can affect how Afterpay renders. If Afterpay disappeared after a theme update, try reverting to a previous theme version to confirm the theme is the cause. In Shopify's current checkout architecture, merchants have limited control over checkout page layout without Shopify Plus, but theme code that injects custom CSS or JavaScript into the checkout can still interfere.

Failure Mode 7: Merchant's Afterpay Account Under Review or Suspended

When you enable Afterpay through Shopify Payments, Afterpay creates a merchant account behind the scenes. This account goes through an initial review, and it can also be placed under review later if Afterpay detects unusual transaction patterns, high dispute rates, or compliance concerns. During any merchant account review or suspension, Afterpay will not appear at checkout — even if the toggle is still enabled in your Shopify Payments settings.

This failure mode is difficult to detect because Shopify does not surface Afterpay's merchant account status in the Shopify admin. The only way to see it is by checking the Afterpay merchant portal directly.

How to check your Afterpay merchant account status

  1. Log in to the Afterpay Merchant Portal at portal.afterpay.com (US/AU/NZ/CA) or portal.clearpay.co.uk (UK)
  2. Check the account dashboard for any suspension notices, review flags, or required document submissions
  3. Check the email address associated with your Shopify account for any messages from Afterpay about your merchant account
  4. If you see a review notice, provide any requested documentation promptly — account reviews that wait for merchant response can leave Afterpay disabled for extended periods
  5. If you cannot locate your Afterpay merchant portal login, contact Afterpay merchant support — your account may have been created by Shopify on your behalf and you may need to do a password reset
High Dispute Rates Can Trigger Automatic Suspension
Afterpay monitors merchant dispute rates. If too many customers dispute charges or claim non-delivery on Afterpay orders, Afterpay can automatically pause the merchant's ability to accept new Afterpay payments while the dispute rate is reviewed. If you've recently had a surge in customer disputes or chargebacks, check your Afterpay merchant portal first — this is likely the cause.

Failure Mode 8: Afterpay vs Clearpay Naming Confusion and Market Configuration

Afterpay operates as "Clearpay" in the United Kingdom, France, Italy, and Spain. This branding split creates a configuration gap for merchants who sell in multiple markets. A merchant based in the US may enable "Afterpay" in their Shopify Payments settings, but UK customers checking out see "Clearpay" — except in some configurations where the market assignment doesn't correctly map the US-based merchant's Afterpay account to the UK market.

Conversely, UK-based merchants sometimes search for "Afterpay not working" when they should be looking for "Clearpay" in their Shopify Payments settings. The product is identical — only the label changes — but the settings page uses both names depending on your store's primary country.

Market-specific Afterpay/Clearpay configuration

  • US-based merchants: Your Shopify Payments settings show "Afterpay". UK customers will see "Clearpay" automatically if cross-border BNPL is supported for your merchant account, but this requires Afterpay to have enabled cross-border capability for your account.
  • UK-based merchants: Your Shopify Payments settings show "Clearpay" — look for this label when enabling. "Afterpay" won't appear in UK settings.
  • Multi-market merchants: Use Shopify Markets to create separate market configurations. Afterpay availability per market can be set under each market's payment method settings. Each market where you sell may require separate Afterpay approval.
  • Shopify Plus merchants with multiple stores: Each store requires its own Afterpay merchant account linked through Shopify Payments. Afterpay accounts are not shared across stores.
Testing Afterpay in Shopify Development or Sandbox Mode
Afterpay cannot be tested using Shopify's standard test payment methods. You need to use Afterpay's own sandbox environment, which requires creating a separate Afterpay developer/sandbox account at developers.afterpay.com. In Shopify, switch your checkout to test mode (Admin → Settings → Payments → Enable test mode) and use Afterpay's sandbox credentials. This lets you simulate Afterpay purchases without real transactions. Without this setup, you cannot confirm Afterpay is working end-to-end from the checkout UX through to payment processing.

Afterpay Diagnostic Checklist

Work through this checklist in order — the failures at the top account for the majority of "Afterpay not working" reports:

  1. Check the toggle: Admin → Settings → Payments → Shopify Payments → Manage → confirm Afterpay/Clearpay is on and terms are accepted
  2. Check Shopify Payments status: Confirm Shopify Payments is Active (not Suspended or Under Review) — all BNPL options disappear during any SP suspension
  3. Test order value: Test with a $50–$200 order to confirm the threshold isn't the issue
  4. Test cart contents: Remove gift cards and any potentially restricted products from the test cart
  5. Check your store country: Confirm your store's billing country is in Afterpay's supported list
  6. Disable checkout apps: Temporarily disable each checkout/upsell/loyalty app one at a time and retest
  7. Check Afterpay merchant portal: Log in to the Afterpay merchant portal to check for any account-level issues
  8. Test with a new Afterpay account: If possible, test checkout with a fresh Afterpay customer account to rule out customer-account-specific issues
