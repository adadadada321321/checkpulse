Shopify Charity Donation Not Working — Round-Up Not Showing, Donation Not Captured, Recurring Gift Stopped

Shopify has no built-in donation feature. Round-up widgets on the cart page are bypassed by every express checkout button. Donation amounts are silently erased by cart-modifying apps. Recurring gifts stop when cards expire. Tax receipts fail when the order webhook is auto-disabled. Here are all 8 failure modes.

📅 Updated June 2026 ⏱ 9 min read 💡 Applies to: All Shopify plans
Shopify Has No Native Charity or Donation Feature
There is no donation toggle in Shopify Settings, no round-up mechanism in the checkout flow, and no tax receipt automation built into the platform. Every donation implementation on Shopify requires a third-party app or custom development. The most common mistake is expecting a setting that does not exist — and the second most common is building a donation flow that works for standard checkout but breaks silently for every customer who uses Buy Now, Shop Pay, Apple Pay, or Google Pay.

How Donations Work in Shopify (And What Breaks)

Merchants implement charitable donations in Shopify using four main approaches, each with significant limitations:

Implementation Where It Appears Works with Express Checkout? Plan Required
Round-up app (cart page widget) Cart page (/cart) No — bypassed All plans
Donation product ($0 or variable price) Product page / cart Partial — customer must add manually All plans
Cart attribute / order note Cart page No — bypassed All plans
Checkout UI Extension (Plus only) Inside checkout flow Yes — survives express checkout Shopify Plus only

The practical consequence: on non-Plus Shopify stores, any customer who uses a Buy Now, Shop Pay, Apple Pay, Google Pay, or PayPal Express button will never see a donation option — regardless of how it is implemented — because all these flows skip the cart page entirely.

Failure Mode 1 — No Native Shopify Donation Feature

When merchants look for a way to add a charity donation at checkout, they search in Settings → Checkout, Online Store → Preferences, and the theme customizer. None of these locations contains a donation option. Unlike some competing e-commerce platforms that offer built-in charity integrations, Shopify does not provide any native donation mechanism.

Third-party apps that fill this gap (as of 2026):

  • Pledge — round-up and fixed-amount donations with direct charity disbursement, impact reporting dashboard
  • Give & Grow (Goodr) — charity selection widget, impact metrics, tax receipt via the partner charity
  • Round Up for Charity — cart page round-up widget, merchant selects charity beneficiary
  • Shop for Good — percentage-of-sale donation model, charity catalog
  • Bold Subscriptions / Recharge — used for recurring monthly donation flows
Shopify Payments Cannot Pass Through Donations
Shopify Payments and Stripe (as configured through Shopify) do not support pass-through donation processing. Donation funds collected through your store flow into your merchant account. You must manually disburse to the charity on whatever schedule you have agreed upon. If you are not a registered charitable organization, customers cannot claim a tax deduction for any donation made through your store — the receipt must come from the charity itself.

Failure Mode 2 — Round-Up Widget Not Showing at Checkout

Round-up donation widgets work by injecting a JavaScript-powered UI into the cart page (/cart). This approach works for customers who use the standard "Check Out" button. The widget never appears for customers who use express checkout buttons, but it also fails to render for standard-checkout customers in several other scenarios:

App Embed not enabled in the theme

Apps that use Shopify's App Embed Block system require the merchant to manually toggle the embed on after installation. Go to Online Store → Themes → Customize → App Embeds (in the left sidebar) and confirm the donation app embed is toggled ON. This toggle is reset when merchants switch themes or reinstall a theme — it is one of the most common reasons a donation widget stops working after a theme update.

Cookie consent manager blocking the app script

Apps like OneTrust, Cookiebot, Axeptio, Consentmo, and Complianz can block third-party scripts that haven't been explicitly categorized. The donation app's JavaScript never loads, but there is no visible error — the customer simply sees no donation option. Estimated 25–40% of EU-based visitors have cookie consent managers active. Test by opening your cart page in an Incognito window without browser extensions to verify the widget appears without consent manager interference.

Ad blockers removing donation app scripts

uBlock Origin, AdBlock Plus, and Privacy Badger can block scripts loaded from donation app CDNs, particularly if the app name or domain appears on a filter list. Approximately 20–35% of desktop users have an active ad blocker. The round-up widget silently fails to render for these users.

Theme update removing the app snippet

Older donation apps that inject directly into theme Liquid files (rather than using App Embed Blocks) may have their snippet removed when the merchant updates the theme. After a theme update, check Admin → Online Store → Themes → Edit code and search for the app's snippet name.

Failure Mode 3 — Buy Now and Express Checkout Bypass Donation Widget

This is the most significant and most commonly missed failure mode. Round-up apps, donation widgets, and cart-page charity flows are completely invisible to customers who checkout via:

  • Buy Now button (dynamic checkout button on product pages)
  • Shop Pay (accelerated checkout)
  • Apple Pay
  • Google Pay
  • PayPal Express
  • Amazon Pay
  • Meta Pay

All of these flows take the customer directly from the product page into the payment confirmation step, bypassing the cart page entirely. The donation widget never loads, the round-up never fires, and no charitable contribution is associated with the order. There is no error shown to the customer or the merchant — the order simply completes without a donation.

Express Checkout May Be Your Highest-Volume Path
On many Shopify stores, Shop Pay and Apple Pay account for 30–60% of mobile conversions. If your donation widget only appears on the cart page, the majority of your mobile orders may never see the donation option. This is a structural problem that cannot be solved without either (a) Shopify Plus and a Checkout UI Extension, or (b) disabling express checkout buttons entirely.

Mitigations for the express checkout bypass

  • Disable Buy Now buttons on key product pages — wrap the {% render 'dynamic-checkout-buttons' %} in a conditional that hides it on products where donation is expected. Trade-off: reduces one-click conversion rate for those products.
  • Add messaging near all express buttons — "To contribute to [Charity Name] with this purchase, use Add to Cart instead of Buy Now." Not ideal UX but sets expectations.
  • Shopify Plus: use a Checkout UI Extension — the only approach that shows a donation option inside the checkout flow itself, surviving all express checkout paths. Requires development effort (~8–20 hours).
  • Post-purchase donation prompt — on the order status / thank-you page, use the additional_scripts injection to show a donation CTA with a separate link. The customer has already paid; this is a secondary ask. Some donation apps support this flow natively.

Failure Mode 4 — Donation Amount Silently Erased by Cart App

Even when a donation is correctly added to the cart — as a line item, a cart attribute, or an order note — it can be silently removed by any other app that modifies the cart without re-sending the full cart state:

  • A discount app calls POST /cart/update.js with {"discount": "GIVE10"} — the API call that omits the existing note and attributes clears both silently
  • A upsell popup calls the Storefront API cartAttributesUpdate mutation with a partial object, overwriting the donation attribute with blank
  • A cart drawer app re-renders the cart after a quantity change using its own cart state snapshot — if the snapshot was taken before the donation was added, the note field shows empty after re-render
  • If the donation is a $0 line item product, apps that call /cart/update.js with an explicit updates object that maps only paid items will set the donation product quantity to 0 — removing it silently

How to identify which app is erasing the donation

  1. Add a product to cart and trigger the donation widget (round-up or checkbox). Confirm the donation appears.
  2. Visit /cart.json in your browser to see the raw cart state: check the note, attributes, and items arrays. Confirm the donation is present.
  3. Trigger each other app interaction one at a time: apply a discount code, interact with the upsell popup, change the quantity in the cart drawer.
  4. After each interaction, re-check /cart.json. The interaction that clears the donation data is the culprit app.
  5. Check the culprit app's settings for a "preserve cart attributes" or "maintain cart note" option. If absent, contact the app developer or remove the app from the checkout flow.

Failure Mode 5 — Recurring Donation Stopped Charging

Monthly or recurring charitable donations implemented via subscription apps (Recharge, Bold Subscriptions, Shopify native subscriptions via selling plans) fail to renew for the same reasons as any subscription product:

Card expiry and failed dunning

The most common cause: the donor's payment card expired or was reissued with a new number. If the subscription app's dunning email (payment update reminder) is not configured, not reaching the customer, or going to spam, the subscription lapses silently. Key checks:

  • In Recharge: Admin → Subscriptions → Failed → check the failure reason (card declined, card expired, etc.)
  • Check that "Payment failed" dunning emails are enabled in the subscription app's notification settings
  • Verify the dunning email sender domain has SPF/DKIM authentication — unauthenticated emails from subscription apps frequently land in spam
  • Check whether the app's retry schedule is active (typical: retry on day 3, 7, 14 after failure)

Customer doesn't recognize the charge

Recurring charitable donations made through a Shopify store charge the customer's card with the merchant's store name (not the charity name) in the bank statement. Customers who don't remember setting up a recurring donation may dispute it as an unknown charge. This appears in your subscription app as a "chargeback" or "dispute" rather than a failed renewal.

Label Recurring Donations Clearly
Configure your subscription app's billing descriptor to include a reference to the charitable cause (e.g., "StoreName - [Charity] Monthly Gift"). Some subscription apps allow a custom statement descriptor. Also send a pre-charge reminder email 3–5 days before each renewal billing date — this significantly reduces chargebacks on recurring donations.

Subscription app migration or plan change

Migrating between subscription apps (e.g., from Bold to Recharge, or from Recharge to Shopify native subscriptions) can silently break active donation subscriptions if payment credentials are not transferred correctly. Always test at least 3 active subscriptions through a full billing cycle after any subscription app migration before declaring the migration complete.

Failure Mode 6 — Tax Receipt Not Sent After Donation

Tax receipts for charitable donations are legally required for donors to claim tax deductions. Failure to send them is both a compliance issue and a trust issue. Technical causes:

Shopify webhook auto-disabled after repeated failures

Donation apps receive notification of completed orders via Shopify's orders/paid webhook. Shopify requires webhook endpoints to respond within 5 seconds with a 2xx HTTP status. If the app's server is slow or temporarily unavailable, the delivery fails. After 19 consecutive failures, Shopify automatically disables the webhook subscription.

When the webhook is disabled:

  • The donation app never learns that orders completed
  • No tax receipts are generated or sent
  • No impact reports are updated
  • No disbursements to the charity are triggered

To check: Admin → Settings → Notifications → Webhooks. Any webhook showing "Failed" status needs to be re-enabled. Also notify your donation app's support team — they may need to re-register the webhook from their end.

Tax receipts require a registered charity

Only registered charitable organizations (501(c)(3) in the US, registered charities in Canada/UK/Australia, etc.) can issue tax-deductible donation receipts. If your Shopify store is not itself a registered charity, you cannot issue valid tax receipts — the receipt must come from the beneficiary charity. Donation apps that partner directly with charities (Pledge, Give & Grow) handle this correctly; custom-built donation flows often do not.

Receipt email authentication

Tax receipt emails sent from donation app platforms (rather than your Shopify store's domain) may fail SPF/DKIM validation if the sending domain is not properly configured. Configure custom email authentication for the sending domain or use a transactional email service with proper DKIM setup (Postmark, Mailgun, Resend) rather than relying on the app's default email infrastructure.

Failure Mode 7 — Donation Widget Blocked by Cookie Consent / Checkout Extension Conflict (Plus)

On non-Plus Shopify stores, the most common cause of a donation widget appearing intermittently is a cookie consent manager that loads the donation app script only after the customer accepts cookies. This means:

  • Customers who decline non-essential cookies never see the donation option
  • Customers who haven't responded to the consent banner yet see no donation option until they interact with the banner
  • The consent manager must be configured to categorize the donation app as "functional" or "necessary" if you want it to load before consent

On Shopify Plus stores using Checkout UI Extensions, a different conflict occurs: multiple checkout extensions competing for the same placement slot (e.g., two apps both targeting purchase.checkout.payment-method-list.render-before) result in one extension being pushed off-screen or not rendering. To debug:

  1. Go to Admin → Settings → Checkout → Customize (Checkout Editor)
  2. Check the list of active app blocks in the checkout customizer
  3. Look for multiple extensions assigned to the same section or slot
  4. Disable non-essential extensions one at a time to identify the conflict
  5. Reposition the donation extension to a less congested slot (e.g., purchase.checkout.block.render with a specific placement)

Failure Mode 8 — Stripe / Payment Processor Holding Donation Funds

Merchants who collect charitable donations through their Shopify store and intend to pass the funds to a charity may encounter payment processor restrictions:

Scenario Risk Resolution
Non-nonprofit collecting donations on behalf of a charity High — Stripe ToS review Use a donation app that processes funds directly to the charity's registered Stripe account, not through yours
High donation volume spikes (fundraising campaigns) Medium — unusual pattern flag Pre-notify Stripe/Shopify Payments before campaigns; provide charity registration docs
Variable-price "donation" product with customer-entered amounts Medium — chargeback risk Set minimum and maximum donation amounts; use a recognized donation app rather than a custom variable-price product
Recurring donations processed as subscription products Low — normal subscription billing Standard subscription billing applies; ensure statement descriptor is clear
International donations with currency conversion Medium — conversion disputes Display donation amounts in local currency; communicate that conversion applies at transaction time
Stripe's Terms of Service on Charitable Donations
Stripe requires that merchants collecting charitable donations on behalf of a third-party charity disclose this clearly and have appropriate legal arrangements with the charity. If Stripe identifies that your business model involves pass-through charitable payments without appropriate disclosure or business verification, your account may be placed under review or have payouts held. Contact Stripe support before launching large charity campaigns to ensure your account is appropriately configured.
