Shopify InstaPay Not Working — 7 Root Causes & Exact Fixes

Updated June 2026 · Egypt merchants · CBE inter-bank transfer · EGP only

InstaPay not appearing at Shopify checkout, order stuck pending after a customer transferred funds, or payment sent to the wrong number? InstaPay (Egypt's CBE inter-bank real-time transfer system, launched January 2022) behaves fundamentally differently from mobile wallets — it has no gateway API and requires manual Shopify configuration. Critical Distinction: InstaPay Is Not a Mobile Wallet

InstaPay is fundamentally different from every other Egyptian payment option on this site. Vodafone Cash, Fawry, Ahly Pay, and Bee Wallet are pre-funded mobile wallets — customers load money into a wallet, then spend from that wallet balance. InstaPay is a CBE-mandated inter-bank real-time transfer system — money moves directly from the customer's bank account to the merchant's bank account in seconds, with no wallet involved at all.

Because InstaPay is a push-transfer system (the customer initiates from their banking app), it cannot be integrated via a payment gateway API the same way wallets are. There is no InstaPay "button" that appears automatically at Shopify checkout. Merchants must configure it as a Manual Payment Method and handle order confirmation manually. This architectural difference is the source of most InstaPay failures on Shopify.

Any Egyptian with a bank account at a participating bank can use InstaPay immediately — through their existing banking app, with no additional registration, no wallet to fund, and no app to download. This makes it the broadest-reach instant payment option in Egypt, but also the one that requires the most merchant-side setup to work correctly on Shopify.

7 Failure Modes Covered

  1. Option not appearing — InstaPay not configured as Manual Payment Method
  2. Merchant didn't provide recipient alias at checkout
  3. Order stays pending after transfer — no automatic Shopify update
  4. Transfer sent to wrong alias or misspelled number
  5. CBE per-transaction or daily transfer limit exceeded
  6. Customer's bank has not activated InstaPay in its app
  7. No mechanism for customer to submit payment proof
Failure Mode Analysis
1

InstaPay option not appearing at checkout

Merchant Configuration

When InstaPay does not appear at Shopify checkout at all, the cause is always that the merchant has not configured it. Unlike Vodafone Cash, Fawry, or Paymob wallet integrations — which appear at checkout because they are integrated via a payment gateway API — InstaPay has no Shopify payment gateway integration. There is no InstaPay SDK, no Paymob InstaPay button, and no automatic checkout widget. InstaPay is a push-transfer system: the customer initiates the payment from their own banking app after seeing the merchant's transfer details.

To offer InstaPay on Shopify, merchants must manually configure it as a Manual Payment Method. This is done in Shopify Admin → Settings → Payments → Manual Payment Methods → Add Manual Payment Method. The merchant creates a payment option named "InstaPay" (or "Pay via InstaPay") and provides the recipient information — their registered mobile number or alias — in the payment instructions field. Shopify then displays this at checkout as an option the customer can select, after which they see the transfer instructions and are expected to initiate the payment from their own banking app.

Steps to Fix

  1. Go to Shopify Admin → Settings → Payments: Scroll to the "Manual Payment Methods" section at the bottom of the page.
  2. Click "Add Manual Payment Method" → select "Create Custom Payment Method": Name it something clear: "Pay via InstaPay" or "InstaPay Bank Transfer".
  3. Add complete payment instructions: Include your registered InstaPay alias (mobile number, email alias, or bank-assigned alias), the bank name, and account holder name. Example: "Send via InstaPay to: 01XXXXXXXXX (Vodafone/any carrier) — Name: [Your Store Name]. After transfer, take a screenshot and send it to [email/WhatsApp]."
  4. Set the "Additional Details" field: Use this to tell customers what to do after transferring — where to submit the proof and how long order confirmation takes.
  5. Save and test on a real device: Complete a checkout on your live store and confirm the InstaPay option appears with correct instructions.
Note: Shopify's Manual Payment Method does not send an automatic order confirmation email when a customer selects it. The order will stay in "Pending" status until the merchant manually marks it as paid. Configure your fulfillment workflow before going live — see Failure #3 for details.
2

Merchant didn't provide recipient alias at checkout

Merchant Configuration

Even when the InstaPay option is visible at checkout, a frequent second failure is that the customer selects it but cannot complete the payment because the merchant did not provide enough information to receive the transfer. InstaPay requires the sender to know the recipient's registered alias before initiating a transfer from their banking app. An alias can be the merchant's registered mobile number, email address, or a bank-assigned alias — and it must exactly match what is registered with the merchant's bank for InstaPay.

Merchants who set up the Manual Payment Method with a vague instruction like "transfer via InstaPay to our account" or who provided a bank account number instead of an alias leave customers without the information needed to complete the payment. In Egypt's banking apps, InstaPay transfers are initiated by entering the recipient's alias — entering a traditional bank account number typically does not work for InstaPay transfers. Customers may attempt the transfer, fail to find the recipient, and abandon the order entirely rather than contacting support.

Steps to Fix

  1. Confirm your InstaPay alias with your bank: Log into your banking app, navigate to the InstaPay section, and find your registered alias (this is typically your mobile number if you set up InstaPay via mobile, or an email/alias you registered). Confirm the exact format your bank displays it in.
  2. Provide the alias explicitly at checkout: In your Manual Payment Method instructions, display the full alias exactly as it must be entered. If it is a mobile number, display all digits: "01XXXXXXXXX". If it is an email, display the full address. Do not abbreviate or describe — show the exact string the customer must enter in their banking app.
  3. Include your bank name and account name: Some banking apps require the customer to confirm the recipient name before the transfer completes. Providing "Account Name: [Name as registered]" prevents mismatches that could block the transfer.
  4. Add a note about InstaPay alias vs bank account number: "Enter this number as an InstaPay alias — do not use it as a regular bank account transfer destination." This prevents customers from attempting a SWIFT/regular bank transfer instead of InstaPay.
3

Order stays pending after transfer — no automatic Shopify update

Merchant Configuration

This is the most structurally important failure for merchants accepting InstaPay on Shopify: when a customer completes an InstaPay transfer, nothing in Shopify updates automatically. The order remains in "Pending" status. There is no webhook from InstaPay to Shopify, no automatic payment confirmation, and no automatic order status change. The customer has already paid — their banking app shows the transfer as successful — but from Shopify's perspective the order is unpaid.

Merchants who set up InstaPay without establishing a manual fulfillment workflow will accumulate a growing queue of paid orders that are not being fulfilled, while customers grow frustrated waiting for confirmation. In contrast to gateway-integrated wallets (where the gateway sends a callback and the order updates from pending to paid within seconds), InstaPay requires the merchant to perform this reconciliation step manually for every order. This is not a bug — it is a structural characteristic of all Shopify Manual Payment Methods.

Steps to Fix

  1. Establish a daily reconciliation routine: Set a fixed time each day (or multiple times per day for high-volume stores) to check your bank account or banking app for incoming InstaPay transfers, match each transfer against the corresponding Shopify order by amount and customer name, and mark verified orders as paid in Shopify Admin.
  2. Mark orders as paid in Shopify Admin: Navigate to the order → click the three dots (⋯) or "More actions" → "Mark as Paid" (or "Capture Payment" depending on your Shopify version). The order will transition from Pending to Paid and fulfillment can begin.
  3. Set customer expectations in the checkout instructions: Add a line to your InstaPay payment instructions: "After transfer, please allow [X hours] for order confirmation. You will receive an email confirmation once we verify your payment." This prevents customers from contacting support before your reconciliation window closes.
  4. Collect a transfer reference number or screenshot: Ask customers to submit their InstaPay transfer screenshot or reference number via WhatsApp/email. This accelerates matching and provides proof of payment for disputed orders.
  5. Consider Shopify notes for incoming payments: When you identify a new InstaPay transfer, immediately add a note to the matching Shopify order ("InstaPay transfer confirmed, amount EGP XXX") before marking as paid. This creates an audit trail.
Important: Do not fulfill orders before verifying the InstaPay transfer. A customer selecting "Pay via InstaPay" and checking out does not mean they actually sent the transfer. Always confirm the transfer arrived in your account before shipping.
4

Transfer sent to wrong alias or misspelled number

Both Sides

InstaPay transfers are near-instantaneous and, once confirmed, are extremely difficult to reverse. If a customer mistypes the merchant's alias — transposing a digit in the mobile number, entering a similar email address, or copying an incorrect alias — the funds are transferred to the wrong recipient immediately. The customer's banking app shows the transfer as successful (because it was — just to the wrong account), the merchant never receives the funds, and the customer believes they have paid.

This failure is significantly more likely when the merchant's alias is a long email address, an unfamiliar alias format, or when checkout instructions are unclear and customers must manually type the alias rather than copy it. It is also more likely on mobile checkouts where digit transposition is easier. Because the transfer is to a real account (even if unintended), recalling the funds requires cooperation from the unintended recipient and typically bank intervention — a process that can take weeks.

Steps to Fix

  1. Display your alias in a copy-friendly format: Where your e-commerce platform allows, format the alias so customers can tap to copy it on mobile. If you cannot make it tappable, display it prominently in a monospace format and explicitly instruct "Copy this exactly."
  2. Use the simplest alias available: If you are registered for InstaPay via mobile number and also have an email alias, use the mobile number at checkout — it is shorter, easier to type, and customers recognize the format. Reduce the chance of typos by minimizing alias length.
  3. Add a confirmation note near the alias: "Double-check every digit before confirming the transfer — InstaPay transfers cannot be reversed." This primes customers to verify before submitting.
  4. Ask customers to confirm transfer details before marking as paid: When a customer submits their payment proof, verify that the alias shown in their screenshot matches your alias exactly. Do not mark an order as paid if the proof shows a different alias — investigate immediately.
  5. Respond quickly to "I sent the transfer but you didn't receive it": This almost always means a wrong-alias transfer. Ask the customer for their transfer reference number so you can investigate. Prepare a clear process for these cases before they happen.
5

CBE per-transaction or daily transfer limit exceeded

Customer-Side Issue

InstaPay operates under Central Bank of Egypt (CBE) transaction limits. As of 2026, the per-transaction limit for retail InstaPay transfers is EGP 30,000 and the daily cumulative limit across all InstaPay transfers is EGP 50,000. These limits apply at the customer's bank level and cannot be overridden by the merchant. Customers who attempt to pay for a Shopify order exceeding EGP 30,000 in a single InstaPay transfer will have the transfer rejected by their banking app — even if they have sufficient bank account balance.

Individual banks may apply additional restrictions. Some banks set lower per-transaction limits than the CBE ceiling, particularly for newer customers or accounts that have not completed full KYC verification. Customers who have already made multiple InstaPay transfers earlier in the day may hit the EGP 50,000 daily ceiling before their final transfer reaches your store. From the customer's perspective, the failure appears as a bank-side error message in their banking app, not as a Shopify problem — they may contact the merchant confused about why their "bank is not working."

Steps to Fix

  1. Display the EGP 30,000 per-transaction limit in your InstaPay checkout instructions: "InstaPay transfers are limited to EGP 30,000 per transaction by CBE regulations. For orders above EGP 30,000, please use bank transfer or contact us." This prevents customer confusion when high-value transfers are rejected.
  2. Offer split-transfer guidance for orders above EGP 30,000: If you want to accept large orders via InstaPay, instruct customers to split into multiple transfers and notify you via WhatsApp with the total reference numbers. This is operationally more complex but feasible.
  3. Add alternative payment methods for high-value orders: For average order values approaching or exceeding EGP 30,000, configure bank transfer (traditional wire) as a parallel option. Bank transfers have higher limits and may better suit large purchases. valU installments are another option for high-value purchases without hitting CBE transfer ceilings.
  4. Advise customers with daily limit issues: If a customer reports that their transfer was rejected after earlier successful transfers today, it is likely the EGP 50,000 daily limit. Advise them to attempt the transfer the following day or use an alternative payment method.
6

Customer's bank has not activated InstaPay in its app

Customer-Side Issue

InstaPay launched in January 2022 and has since been activated at most major Egyptian banks. However, not all banks have implemented it at the same pace, and some customers at smaller or newer banks may find that their banking app does not offer an InstaPay transfer option even if the bank is technically a participant. Additionally, some banks have phased in InstaPay behind app updates — customers on older app versions may not see the InstaPay feature until they update their banking app.

This affects primarily customers at smaller banks or cooperative banks. Customers at Egypt's major retail banks (National Bank of Egypt, Banque Misr, CIB, HSBC Egypt, Banque du Caire, QNB Al Ahli, Arab African International Bank, and others) are very likely to have InstaPay active. The failure manifests as the customer not finding an "InstaPay" option in their banking app at all, even after extensive searching.

Steps to Fix

  1. Advise customers to update their banking app first: The most common cause is an outdated app version. Instruct customers to update their bank's app from the App Store or Google Play before concluding InstaPay is unavailable at their bank.
  2. Check if the bank participates: CBE publishes the list of InstaPay participant banks. If a customer's bank is not on the list, InstaPay is genuinely unavailable to them — point them to alternative payment methods immediately rather than having them troubleshoot further.
  3. Keep fallback payment methods visible: Customers whose banks do not support InstaPay should have clear alternatives: traditional bank transfer, Vodafone Cash (if they have a 010 number), Fawry offline code, or other available methods. Do not make InstaPay the only payment option.
  4. Do not attempt to troubleshoot bank-side app issues: If a customer's bank app genuinely does not show InstaPay, the merchant cannot resolve this. Acknowledge the limitation and redirect to alternatives quickly to preserve the sale.
7

No mechanism for customer to submit payment proof

Merchant Configuration

Because InstaPay has no automatic Shopify integration, the only way a merchant can match an incoming transfer to a Shopify order is through manual reconciliation — which requires either the customer's name exactly matching the order name, the transfer amount matching the order total uniquely, or the customer proactively submitting a payment screenshot or transfer reference number. Many merchants who configure InstaPay as a Manual Payment Method forget to include a clear instruction for where and how customers should submit proof of payment.

Customers who complete an InstaPay transfer but receive no acknowledgment and have no clear next step often submit multiple transfers (thinking the first failed), contact support on multiple channels simultaneously, or simply wait indefinitely for an order confirmation that never comes. Without a defined proof-submission channel, merchants cannot efficiently match payments to orders, orders are delayed, and customer satisfaction drops sharply on what should be a simple payment method.

Steps to Fix

  1. Include a proof submission instruction in checkout payment notes: "After completing your InstaPay transfer, WhatsApp your transfer screenshot to [number] with your order number. Include your order number in the message so we can match your payment." Make this the single, clear call to action after the transfer is done.
  2. Choose one proof submission channel and stick to it: Mixing email, WhatsApp, and contact forms creates confusion. Pick one (WhatsApp is dominant in Egypt for this workflow) and direct all customers there. Train yourself to process only that channel for InstaPay confirmations.
  3. Use Shopify's order note functionality: Ask customers to include their Shopify order number in the transfer description or in the WhatsApp message. This links the transfer to the right order immediately and eliminates matching ambiguity when amounts are similar across orders.
  4. Send a pending payment email immediately after checkout: Shopify sends an order confirmation when a Manual Payment Method order is placed, but it may not include clear next-step instructions. Consider adding a follow-up message via Shopify Flow or your email tool that says: "Your order is waiting for payment confirmation. Send your InstaPay screenshot to [channel] with order #[number]."
  5. Set a realistic processing window: Tell customers upfront: "Orders are confirmed within [X hours] during business hours." This prevents customers from thinking their order is lost and contacting multiple channels before you have had a chance to process their payment.

InstaPay Shopify Integration Diagnostic Checklist

InstaPay configured as Manual Payment Method in Shopify Admin — Settings → Payments → Manual Payment Methods → "Pay via InstaPay" or equivalent name is active and visible at checkout.

Recipient alias displayed exactly and completely — the full registered alias (mobile number, email, or bank alias) is shown at checkout with no abbreviations, and matches the alias registered at your bank for InstaPay.

Bank name and account holder name included — customers can confirm they are sending to the right recipient in their banking app before the transfer is submitted.

Proof submission channel specified at checkout — customers know exactly where to send their payment screenshot and order number after completing the InstaPay transfer (WhatsApp number, email, or contact form link).

EGP 30,000 per-transaction limit disclosed — a note warns customers that single InstaPay transfers are limited to EGP 30,000 and provides an alternative payment method for higher-value orders.

Order confirmation timeline stated — customers are told how long manual verification takes and when to expect their order confirmation email.

Manual reconciliation process in place — a defined daily or per-shift routine exists for checking incoming InstaPay transfers, matching to Shopify orders, and marking verified orders as paid before fulfillment.

Fallback payment methods available — customers whose banks do not support InstaPay, or who exceed CBE limits, have clear alternatives: Vodafone Cash, Fawry, bank transfer, or card payment.

Tested with a real InstaPay transfer from a participant bank — a test order was placed, a real InstaPay transfer sent, the transfer was matched to the order, and the order was marked as paid and fulfilled before going live.

Checkout currency is EGP — store is priced in EGP or Shopify Markets is configured to present EGP to Egyptian customers. InstaPay processes EGP only and cannot be used for non-EGP orders.
