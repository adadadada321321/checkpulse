Shopify Checkout Address Not Saving — Saved Addresses Not Showing, New Customer Accounts, Prefill Broken

Shopify uses two separate address systems that don't always sync. Here's why saved addresses disappear at checkout and how to fix each scenario.

Three Root Causes — Different Fixes

Most Common

Guest Checkout — No Address Is Ever Saved

Guest checkouts never save addresses to the customer profile. If the customer wasn't logged in, no address storage occurred regardless of any other setting.

Post-Migration

Legacy → New Customer Accounts Break Prefill

Switching to New Customer Accounts changes the checkout auth flow. Customers with existing address books may not see prefill until they re-authenticate in the new system.

Common Misconception

Billing Address Is Not Stored in the Address Book

Shopify only saves the shipping address to the address book. The billing address is used for payment processing only and is never added to the saved addresses list.

How Shopify Address Saving Actually Works

Shopify's address saving involves two separate systems that people often confuse:

System 1

Customer Account Address Book

Stored on the Customer object. Visible at /account/addresses. Each customer can have up to 25 addresses. One is marked "default." Managed via Admin API at /customers/{id}/addresses.

System 2

Checkout Address Prefill

At checkout, Shopify looks up the authenticated customer's default address and prefills the shipping fields. This only works if the customer is logged in AND has a default address saved.

Key fact: These two systems are connected but have a gap — the customer must be actively authenticated at checkout (not just having an account) for prefill to trigger. A returning customer who doesn't click "Log in" at checkout starts as a guest, even if they've shopped with you before.

When Does Shopify Save a New Address?

On Legacy Customer Accounts: after a logged-in customer completes checkout with a shipping address not already in their address book, Shopify automatically adds it. The customer doesn't need to do anything.

On New Customer Accounts (2023+): the behavior is more explicit — customers are presented with an option to save the address after their first order with that address. It's not automatically added without the customer's action.

Legacy vs New Customer Accounts — What Changed

Shopify introduced New Customer Accounts in 2023. The two systems have fundamentally different authentication flows, and migrating between them can break address prefill for existing customers.

Feature Legacy Customer Accounts New Customer Accounts
Login method Email + password Email + one-time code (passwordless)
Auth at checkout Cookie-based session; login persists across visits Separate auth flow; customer must re-authenticate per device
Address prefill Automatic if logged in with default address Prompts customer to confirm; may require explicit opt-in per session
Address saving after order Automatic (new address added silently) Prompted (customer sees "Save this address?" option)
Where to switch Admin → Settings → Customer accounts
Migration caveat: When you switch from Legacy to New Customer Accounts, existing customers' address books are preserved in the Shopify admin — but customers must log in using the new passwordless flow before checkout prefill will work again. Customers who have your store open in a tab with an old session may not be recognized until they re-authenticate.

How to Check Which System Your Store Uses

Go to Admin → Settings → Customer accounts. You'll see either "Legacy" or "New" customer accounts selected. If you see two options and neither is selected, or if you've recently changed this, the setting takes effect immediately but customer sessions follow the old system until they re-authenticate.

Saved Addresses Not Showing at Checkout

Customers report having saved addresses but seeing an empty form at checkout. Four causes explain this, each with a specific fix.

No Default Address Set

Shopify prefills from the default address only. If a customer has three saved addresses and none is marked default, checkout prefill does nothing. Fix: go to Admin → Customers → [customer] → Addresses → click "Set as default" on the correct address. Customers can also set their default at /account/addresses.

Customer Not Actually Logged In at Checkout Start

Prefill only fires when the checkout session is created by an authenticated customer. If the customer adds to cart as a guest and then tries to log in mid-checkout, their session was already created as a guest — prefill won't retroactively apply. The customer must be logged in before clicking "Checkout."

Email Mismatch — Addresses Under a Different Account

A customer who has shopped with you using two email addresses has two separate customer records. The address book in one record doesn't carry over to the other. Check Admin → Customers and search by both emails to see if there are duplicate customer records. Shopify doesn't automatically merge them — use a merge app or manually copy addresses.

Checkout Extension Overriding the Address Field

Checkout Extensions (apps installed via the Checkout Editor) can add custom UI components to the shipping address section. Some address validation apps (Loqate, AddressNow, Google Places for Shopify) replace or wrap the native address field — if the app is misconfigured, it can render a blank custom field over the Shopify prefill. Disable checkout apps one by one to identify the conflict.

How to verify the address book from admin: Go to Admin → Customers → search for the customer → scroll to the Addresses section. If you see addresses listed, the data is there. If the section is empty, no addresses were ever saved for this account.

Billing Address Not Saving — Why It's By Design

This is the most common misconception about Shopify's address system. Shopify's address book stores shipping addresses only — not billing addresses.

Saved to Address Book

Shipping Address

The name, address, city, country, postal code entered in the shipping step is saved and prefilled at future checkouts.

NOT Saved to Address Book

Billing Address

The billing address is used for payment processing and fraud analysis only. Even if different from shipping, it is never stored in the address book.

The billing address field at checkout defaults to "Same as shipping address." When a customer unchecks this and enters a separate billing address, that information is attached to the order's payment record but not persisted to the customer profile.

Merchants who need to store billing addresses (common in B2B/net-terms scenarios) must use a custom Checkout Extension or a metafield approach — there is no native Shopify feature that saves billing addresses to the customer profile for prefill at future checkouts.

B2B note: Shopify Plus B2B companies often have customers who need to enter their corporate billing address on every order. This is a known limitation — Shopify's B2B tier doesn't yet provide billing address prefill from a separate vault. Workarounds include draft order templates or custom checkout apps that store and retrieve billing addresses via customer metafields.

Shop Pay Addresses vs Store Customer Account Addresses

This is a frequent source of confusion when customers report "wrong addresses at checkout" or "an old address keeps appearing."

Your Store's System

Customer Account Address Book

Addresses stored in your store's Customer object. Specific to your store. Managed at /account/addresses.

Shopify's Global Network

Shop Pay Vault

Addresses stored in Shop's consumer identity layer. Shared across ALL merchants using Shop Pay. Managed by the customer at shop.app/profile.

When a customer uses the Shop Pay accelerated checkout, Shopify pulls from the Shop Pay vault — not from your store's customer account address book. This means:

  • A customer might see an address at checkout that they've never entered on your store (it came from a different merchant via Shop Pay)
  • An old address a customer thought they deleted from your store may still appear (it's still in their Shop Pay vault)
  • Addresses saved in your store's customer account won't appear in the Shop Pay accelerated checkout flow — they're different vaults

To remove an old address from Shop Pay: the customer must go to shop.app → Profile → Addresses → delete the address there. Merchants cannot remove Shop Pay addresses for customers.

7-Step Diagnostic for Address Saving Issues

Was the customer logged in during checkout?

Check Admin → Orders → [the order] → Customer section. If it shows "Guest" or no customer account linked, the order was placed as a guest. Guest checkouts never save addresses. No further investigation needed — nothing went wrong, this is expected behavior.

Check the customer's address book in admin

Admin → Customers → find the customer → scroll to Addresses. Are addresses listed? Is one marked "Default"? If the section is empty, no addresses have ever been saved for this account. If addresses are listed but not prefilling at checkout, move to step 3.

Verify Legacy vs New Customer Accounts

Admin → Settings → Customer accounts. If you recently switched from Legacy to New, customers need to re-authenticate using the new passwordless email code. Their address book is intact but the checkout prefill won't trigger until they log in via the new system at least once on that device.

Check for duplicate customer records

Search Admin → Customers for all email addresses the customer might use. If there are two records (e.g., john@gmail.com and john.smith@gmail.com) they have separate address books. Addresses saved under one won't appear when the customer logs in with the other.

Test without checkout apps

If addresses exist in the customer record but still don't prefill, temporarily disable any checkout extensions or address validation apps via Admin → Apps → look for apps with checkout permissions. Do a test checkout with the customer logged in. If prefill works after disabling an app, that app is the conflict.

Distinguish Shop Pay addresses from account addresses

If the customer reports an unexpected address appearing, ask if they use Shop Pay. The address may be from their Shop Pay vault, not your store's customer account. Have them check shop.app/profile to manage their Shop Pay addresses.

Check if the issue is billing vs shipping

If the customer says their billing address isn't being saved, confirm they understand Shopify only saves shipping addresses. The billing address at checkout is for payment processing only. If they need billing address prefill, this requires a custom checkout extension solution.

Address Issues as a Checkout Conversion Problem

Address prefill failure is a friction point that shows up in your conversion funnel as "checkout started but didn't complete." When returning customers discover their saved address isn't there, some abandon checkout rather than re-enter their details — especially on mobile.

The specific failure mode is hard to see in Shopify analytics because abandoned checkouts don't indicate why the customer left. A customer who abandons at the information step because they had to type their address again looks identical to a customer who abandoned because they changed their mind.

Metric to watch: Compare checkout completion rates for returning customers (customers with an existing account who are logged in) vs new customers. If returning customers complete checkout at a lower rate than new customers, that's a strong signal of prefill or address book friction — returning customers have higher intent and should convert at higher rates.
