Industry Benchmarks

What Is a Good Shopify Checkout Conversion Rate?

Checkout conversion rate (also called checkout completion rate) measures what percentage of customers who begin checkout actually place an order. It's a different metric from store conversion rate — the checkout funnel starts the moment someone clicks "Checkout" from the cart.

These numbers are checkout-stage conversion rates — not overall store conversion. A store converting 3% of all visitors is well within normal range even if checkout completion is 55%: most visitor-to-purchase drop-off happens before checkout begins (homepage, product page, cart abandonment).

How to find your checkout conversion rate in Shopify

Admin → Analytics → Reports → Sales → Checkout funnel. The funnel shows drop-off at each step: reached checkout → information page → payment page → order placed. Your checkout conversion rate = (orders placed ÷ reached checkout) × 100. Below 40%? This guide covers the most likely causes.

CRO Playbook

7 Shopify Checkout CRO Levers (Ranked by Impact)

Not all checkout CRO has equal payoff. These 7 levers are ranked by the conversion lift available on standard Shopify plans, highest first.

1
Enable Shop Pay and Wallet Payments Highest lift

Shop Pay pre-fills shipping, email, and payment details for any customer who has shopped on any Shopify store before. For returning buyers, it reduces a 15-field checkout to a 1-tap confirmation. Shopify's data shows a 1.72× conversion lift. Also enable Apple Pay and Google Pay — these eliminate card entry on mobile entirely.

How to enable: Admin → Settings → Payments → Shopify Payments → enable Shop Pay, Apple Pay, Google Pay. Requires Shopify Payments as your gateway.

2
Show All Costs Before Checkout Begins High lift

The single biggest reason for checkout abandonment is "unexpected costs" revealed at the payment step: shipping fees, taxes, duties, or handling charges the customer didn't know about. Eliminating this surprise is the highest-ROI CRO change available to most merchants.

How to fix: Add estimated shipping on the cart page (use a free shipping bar or Shopify's native cart customization). Display tax-inclusive pricing where your market expects it. Show free shipping threshold prominently. If you charge duties on international orders, display this before checkout — customers who learn about duties at checkout almost always abandon.

3
Enable Guest Checkout High lift for first-time buyers

Requiring account creation before purchase loses approximately 30% of first-time buyers. Guest checkout is the standard expectation. Account creation can be offered after the purchase is complete — at that point, it's a one-click step (the password is the only thing the customer needs to set; all other details are already saved).

How to check: Admin → Settings → Checkout → Customer accounts. Set to "Accounts are optional" or "Accounts are disabled." Never use "Accounts are required" unless you have a strong members-only business model.

4
Optimize Mobile Checkout High lift — 60%+ of traffic

Most Shopify stores now see 60–70% of traffic from mobile devices, but mobile checkout conversion is consistently 20–30% lower than desktop. The primary causes: small form fields, keyboard covering the active field, required fields with no autofill hint, non-numeric keyboard appearing for numeric fields, and address entry without autocomplete.

How to improve: Shopify's default checkout is mobile-optimized, but verify it on an actual device. Common issues: (1) A checkout app or extension injecting a non-mobile-friendly element. (2) Trust badges or upsell offers that push the CTA button below the fold on mobile. (3) Your theme's cart-to-checkout flow adding a modal or drawer that performs poorly on small screens. Always test checkout in a real mobile browser, not just a browser developer tools mobile simulation.

5
Add Trust Signals at the Payment Step Medium lift

Payment-step anxiety is real: customers hand over card details and want reassurance. The trust signals that move conversion at this stage are different from homepage trust signals.

What works at checkout: Payment security badge ("Secure checkout" + SSL padlock reference), money-back guarantee or return policy summary, review count / star rating (not full reviews — just a summary), brand recognizable payment logos (Visa, Mastercard, PayPal, Klarna). What doesn't work: Generic "Trusted by 10,000 customers" badges with no specifics, unrecognized third-party trust seal logos, large banner ads for other products.

How to add on standard Shopify: Use checkout extensibility apps (App Store → Checkout UI extensions) to add trust content in approved checkout extension points. Shopify Plus merchants can modify checkout.liquid directly.

6
Reduce Checkout Load Time Medium lift, high floor

Each additional second of checkout load time increases abandonment. For Shopify-hosted checkouts, the main load time levers are: third-party tracking scripts injected by apps, heavy checkout extensibility blocks, and slow payment gateway initialization. Shopify's own checkout infrastructure is fast — the latency merchants introduce is usually through apps.

How to audit: Open a checkout session in Chrome DevTools with Network throttling set to "Fast 3G." Look for third-party scripts loading during checkout initialization. Review Admin → Apps for any apps that inject scripts into checkout. Disable checkout-injecting apps one at a time and measure load time improvement.

For more detail: which apps slow down Shopify checkout.

7
Monitor for Silent Technical Failures Prevents 100% conversion loss during outages

This is the CRO lever most guides skip, but it has the highest possible impact: if your checkout is technically broken, no optimization improves conversion. The dangerous category is silent failures — checkout errors that don't show a clear error page. The customer reaches step 2 of 4, clicks "Continue," and nothing happens, or they see a spinner that never resolves. To them it looks like abandonment. To you it looks like low conversion.

Silent failures happen after payment gateway updates, app changes, theme updates, and Shopify platform changes. Standard uptime tools don't catch them — they check that a URL responds, not that the checkout flow works. The only way to detect them is to run the checkout flow itself as a test.

The Hidden Category

Silent Checkout Failures: The Conversion Killer No CRO Test Can Prevent

CRO optimization assumes the checkout is working. But there is a category of conversion loss where the checkout is technically broken — and standard analytics can't distinguish it from normal abandonment. These are silent failures: checkout errors that register as abandonment in Shopify Analytics but are actually technical failures.

Silent Failure Type What Customer Sees What Analytics Records Actual Cause
Payment methods don't load Checkout shows, payment section blank Abandoned at payment step Payment gateway API failure
Checkout session initialization fails Spinner that never resolves Abandoned at checkout start Shopify API / app conflict
Checkout URL returns 500 Generic error page or redirect Abandoned immediately Theme liquid error, app break
Address autocomplete breaks Address field doesn't save Abandoned at information step Google Maps API key expired
Shipping rates don't calculate Checkout stuck at $0 shipping Abandoned at shipping step Carrier rate API timeout

These failures are intermittent — they often appear during high-traffic periods, after app updates, or after payment gateway changes. Because they look identical to normal abandonment in analytics, a merchant running a CRO test during a silent failure period will see anomalous data and draw wrong conclusions about what optimization is working.

How long do merchants typically run with a broken checkout before discovering it?

Without proactive monitoring, the average discovery time for a silent checkout failure is 4–11 hours. The discovery mechanism is almost always a customer complaint or a sharp drop in orders noticed hours later. During that window, every customer who reached checkout experienced a 0% conversion rate — no optimization recovers that revenue.

What to Monitor

Effective checkout monitoring needs to simulate the full checkout flow, not just check that the checkout URL responds with a 200 status. The steps that matter:

  • Cart creation — can a cart be created with a real product?
  • Checkout session initialization — does the checkout session open and return a valid session ID?
  • Payment methods fetch — do configured payment gateways return their available methods?
  • Checkout URL verification — is the final checkout URL valid and reachable?

Measurement

How to Measure Checkout Conversion Rate Accurately

Shopify's Built-in Checkout Funnel

Admin → Analytics → Reports → Sales → Checkout funnel. This shows step-by-step drop-off for any date range. Limitation: Shopify counts a session as "reached checkout" when the customer clicks the Checkout button — even if the checkout page immediately errors. If you have a checkout failure during a period you're analyzing, the checkout funnel will show inflated abandonment at step 1 that isn't real abandonment — it's technical failure.

Segment by Device

Always view checkout conversion segmented by device type. Mobile checkout conversion is typically 20–30% lower than desktop. If you see an unusual mobile-vs-desktop gap, the cause is usually one of: tap targets too small, keyboard obscuring form fields, a checkout app element that doesn't render on mobile, or address autocomplete not working on iOS Safari.

Segment by Traffic Source

Paid traffic often converts at checkout at a lower rate than organic or direct traffic — not because the checkout is worse for those visitors, but because paid traffic includes more price-comparison shoppers and impulse browsers. Don't conflate traffic-mix changes with checkout CRO effectiveness.

Isolate Technical Failure Periods

Before drawing conclusions from checkout conversion data, verify that your checkout was working correctly during the analysis period. A checkout failure — even a 2-hour outage — will appear as a conversion rate drop in the data. If you run a CRO test during an unknown checkout failure, the test results are invalid. Checkout monitoring with timestamped incident logs lets you exclude failure periods from conversion analysis.

FAQ

Shopify Checkout Conversion FAQ

What is a good Shopify checkout conversion rate?
A healthy Shopify checkout conversion rate (the percentage of customers who start checkout and place an order) is 50–70%. Optimized stores with Shop Pay enabled often reach 60–75%. Below 40% indicates significant friction — usually unexpected costs revealed at checkout, required account creation, limited payment options, or mobile layout problems. Use Admin → Analytics → Reports → Sales → Checkout funnel to find your rate.
How do I check my Shopify checkout conversion rate?
Admin → Analytics → Reports → Sales → Checkout funnel. This shows the percentage of customers who reached checkout and completed a purchase, broken down by each checkout step. Your checkout conversion rate = (completed orders ÷ sessions that reached checkout) × 100. Segment by device (mobile vs. desktop) and by date range to identify patterns. Note: if your checkout had a technical failure during the period, abandonment will look higher than it actually is — the analytics can't distinguish a customer who chose to abandon from one who encountered a broken checkout.
What are the biggest reasons Shopify checkout conversion is low?
In order of frequency: (1) Unexpected costs — shipping, taxes, or fees the customer didn't know about, revealed at the payment step. (2) Required account creation. (3) Limited payment options — missing Shop Pay, Apple Pay, or PayPal. (4) Mobile checkout friction — forms that don't autofill, keyboards covering input fields, small tap targets. (5) Slow checkout load. (6) Missing trust signals at the payment step. (7) Silent technical failures — checkout errors that don't show a visible error page but prevent completion, which look like abandonment in analytics.
Does Shop Pay actually improve Shopify checkout conversion?
Yes. Shopify's own data shows Shop Pay converts at 1.72× the rate of standard guest checkout. The reason: Shop Pay pre-fills the customer's shipping address, email, and payment method from any previous purchase on any Shopify store. A returning buyer goes from a 15-field form to a 1-tap confirmation. To enable: Admin → Settings → Payments → Shopify Payments → Shop Pay. Requires Shopify Payments. Also enable Apple Pay and Google Pay — these perform similarly well for mobile buyers who have a wallet configured.
What checkout CRO changes are possible on Shopify without Shopify Plus?
Standard Shopify plans support: Shop Pay, Apple Pay, Google Pay, guest checkout, checkout branding (logo, colors), trust badges via checkout extensibility apps, post-purchase upsell apps, abandoned checkout email recovery, shipping rate optimization, and checkout monitoring. What requires Plus: full checkout.liquid customization, custom checkout fields, checkout funnel A/B testing at the template level. In practice, enabling Shop Pay + guest checkout + showing shipping costs before checkout delivers 70–80% of available conversion gain on any plan.
