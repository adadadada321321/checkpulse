Shopify Checkout Address Book Not Working — 8 Causes Explained

Saved addresses not pre-filling at checkout? Address book empty after switching to New Customer Accounts? B2B customers only seeing company addresses? Here are all 8 root causes for Shopify address book failures — for merchants and customers.

Updated June 2026 8 failure modes Saved Addresses · NCA Migration · Default Address · B2B Location · Autocomplete · Billing Address · Headless API

Shopify's saved address pre-fill at checkout sounds simple: customer logs in, their saved address appears, they click "Continue." In practice, several conditions must all be true simultaneously for this to work — and each condition that fails produces a different symptom. The most common: addresses that "disappeared" after a store switched to New Customer Accounts, or addresses that exist in the account but never appear in the checkout form.

There are eight distinct root causes. Most are not bugs — they are behaviors that changed with New Customer Accounts or that depend on conditions merchants don't know to configure.

Three Things That Must Be True for Address Pre-Fill to Work
1. Customer must be logged in BEFORE starting checkout — logging in mid-checkout (at the login step) does not reliably trigger address pre-fill.

2. Customer must have a default address set — Shopify only pre-fills the explicitly marked default address. No default = blank checkout form even with 10 saved addresses.

3. The store must be on a plan and account system that exposes saved addresses at checkout — New Customer Accounts (NCA) moves address management to the Shopify-hosted portal; the classic /account/addresses page is not used for address pre-fill on NCA stores.

Failure Mode 1: New Customer Accounts (NCA) Moves Address Management Off the Storefront

The single biggest source of "address book suddenly empty" reports is a store switching from Classic Accounts to New Customer Accounts. Merchants and customers both see the same symptom: the saved addresses that were visible on /account/addresses are gone. Third-party address management apps also stop working.

What actually happened to the saved addresses

Nothing. The addresses still exist in Shopify's database attached to the customer record. What changed is the interface used to access them. Classic Accounts exposes addresses via a Liquid-rendered storefront page at /account/addresses. New Customer Accounts uses a Shopify-hosted account portal (accessible at /a/account or via links in order confirmation emails) that is separate from the storefront theme.

Account System Where Addresses Are Managed Storefront /account/addresses Third-Party Address Apps
Classic Accounts Storefront page (/account/addresses) Works — Liquid renders full address book Work — use Classic Customer API
New Customer Accounts (NCA) Shopify-hosted portal (/a/account) Broken / blank — NCA doesn't populate this Liquid page Break — Classic Customer API not used by NCA

How customers access their addresses on NCA stores

  1. Customer clicks their account link in the storefront header — on NCA stores this redirects to the Shopify-hosted account portal, not a theme page
  2. In the account portal, customer navigates to "Addresses" to add, edit, or set a default shipping address
  3. Alternatively: customers can access addresses from the link in any Shopify order confirmation email — the "View your order" button on NCA stores links to the account portal
  4. The portal URL format is typically https://shopify.com/authentication/{shop-id}/account or the store's own /a/account redirect
Third-Party Address Apps Built on Classic Accounts Will Break on NCA
Apps that let customers manage "ship to multiple addresses," add address nicknames, or auto-fill from company address books typically use the Classic Accounts customer API to read and write address records. After switching to NCA, these apps either show a blank list, throw JavaScript errors, or fail silently. Check the app's documentation for NCA compatibility before switching. Most address-focused apps had not released NCA-compatible versions as of early 2026.

Failure Mode 2: Customer Must Log In Before Starting Checkout — Not During It

This is the most common reason a customer's saved address doesn't pre-fill even though they logged in successfully: they logged in at the login step inside checkout rather than from the storefront before adding to cart. The two login moments produce different checkout sessions.

How checkout session initialization works

When a customer clicks "Check out" or "Buy Now," Shopify creates a checkout session. If the customer was already logged in at that moment, the session is created with customer context — Shopify knows which customer this is, reads their default address, and pre-fills the shipping address form. If the customer was a guest when checkout started, the session is created without customer context. Logging in at the account login step (for required-accounts stores) authenticates the customer but does not always retroactively inject their saved addresses into the already-initialized checkout session.

Login Timing Address Pre-Fill? Notes
Logged in before starting checkout Yes — default address pre-fills Checkout session created with full customer context; default address injected into shipping form
Started as guest, logged in at checkout login step Sometimes — behavior varies Session may or may not inherit saved addresses depending on checkout version and account system; not guaranteed
Started as guest, remained as guest throughout Never No customer context = no address lookup possible
Shop Pay (logged-in Shop account) Yes — via Shop Pay's own address vault Shop Pay maintains its own address book separate from the store's customer address book; pre-fills from the Shop account

How to communicate this to customers

This is primarily a UX communication problem. On storefront pages, prompt logged-out customers to log in before checkout rather than after. A simple "Log in to use your saved addresses" message near the cart or product page, with a link to the account login, sets the expectation. On account-optional stores, this must be explicit — the storefront default behavior puts the login prompt inside checkout where it's too late to improve address pre-fill.

Shop Pay as a Workaround
For returning customers who consistently forget to log in before checkout: Shop Pay maintains its own shipping address vault (separate from the Shopify store address book) and pre-fills addresses regardless of whether the customer is logged into the store account. Returning customers who use Shop Pay get address pre-fill even without being logged into the store. Encourage returning customers to use the Shop Pay button as their default checkout method.

Failure Mode 3: No Default Address Set — Shopify Only Pre-Fills the Default

Shopify's address book supports multiple saved addresses. At checkout, Shopify pre-fills only the one address marked as the customer's default. If no default is set — which is common for customers who saved addresses through import, manual merchant entry, or old app migrations — checkout starts with a completely blank shipping form even if 10 addresses are saved.

How addresses get created without a default

  • Merchant bulk-imports customer addresses via the Admin API or CSV import — imported addresses are added but none is marked default
  • Customer saves multiple addresses and removes the one that was default, without setting a new default — Shopify doesn't auto-promote another address to default when the current default is deleted
  • Third-party app creates addresses programmatically (subscription apps adding delivery addresses, B2B company address sync apps) without setting the default flag on any record
  • Classic-to-NCA migration: some edge cases in the migration process result in default flags not being preserved correctly

How to diagnose and fix

  1. Customer logs into their account and checks the address book — the default address is typically marked with a "Default" label
  2. If no address shows the default label, or if all addresses look the same, the customer should click "Set as default" on their primary shipping address
  3. Merchant who needs to set defaults at scale: use the Admin API customerDefaultAddressUpdate mutation to programmatically set default addresses for multiple customers at once
  4. After setting a default, test by logging in as the customer (use Admin → Customers → [customer] → "View in storefront" or impersonate flow if available) and confirm the address pre-fills at checkout
// Admin API: set a customer's default address
// Requires Admin API access with customer write permissions

mutation customerDefaultAddressUpdate($customerId: ID!, $addressId: ID!) {
  customerDefaultAddressUpdate(
    customerId: $customerId
    addressId: $addressId
  ) {
    customer {
      id
      defaultAddress {
        id
        address1
        city
        country
      }
    }
    userErrors {
      field
      message
    }
  }
}
How to Check If a Customer Has a Default Address
In Admin → Customers → [customer name]: the address book section shows all saved addresses. The one labeled "Default address" is what will pre-fill at checkout. If you see addresses but no "Default address" label, none is set. Click the three-dot menu on the desired address and select "Set as default."

Failure Mode 4: B2B Company Location Restricts Address Options at Checkout

Shopify B2B (Shopify Plus) assigns company locations to B2B customers. A company location includes a physical shipping address, an assigned price list, and payment terms. When a B2B customer authenticates via the B2B portal URL, their checkout session is scoped to the company's locations — not their personal account addresses.

What B2B customers see vs what they expect

Checkout Context Address Options Shown Personal Addresses Available?
B2B portal session (via invitation link) Only company-assigned location addresses No — session is scoped to company context
Standard storefront session (via /account/login) Personal saved addresses Yes — but retail pricing, no net terms, no B2B price list
B2B with multiple company locations All locations assigned to that company No personal addresses — only company locations appear in dropdown

Adding a one-off address for a B2B customer

If a B2B customer needs to ship to an address outside their assigned company locations (a trade show, a temporary site, an individual recipient within their company), the merchant has two options:

  1. Add a new company location: Admin → Customers → Companies → [company] → Locations → Add location — add the new delivery address as an additional location and assign it a price list and payment terms. The customer can then select it at B2B checkout.
  2. Create a draft order: The merchant creates a draft order in Admin → Orders → Create order, selects the B2B customer and their price list, and manually enters the one-off shipping address. Sends the draft order invoice link to the customer for payment. This bypasses the B2B portal checkout entirely.
B2B is Shopify Plus Only
Native B2B company accounts, company locations, and net terms are available only on Shopify Plus. Non-Plus stores using third-party wholesale apps (Wholesale Club, BSS B2B, etc.) have different address restriction behavior determined by each app's own logic. Check your app's documentation for how it handles address book access for wholesale customers.

Failure Mode 5: Address Autocomplete Overwrites the Pre-Filled Saved Address

Shopify's checkout uses Google Places API for address autocomplete. The autocomplete fires whenever a customer begins typing in any address input field. If a saved address was pre-filled and the customer starts editing a single character (to fix a typo, change an apartment number, or add a company name to the address), Google Places can fire and replace all address fields with its top suggestion — which may be completely different from the saved address.

The exact sequence that causes the problem

  • Customer's default address pre-fills correctly: "123 Oak Street, Springfield, IL 62701, US"
  • Customer clicks on the address line to add "Apt 4B" — types "123 Oak S"
  • Google Places autocomplete fires after the first few keystrokes
  • Top suggestion is "123 Oak Street, Springfield, OH 45501" (different state, different ZIP)
  • If the customer accidentally clicks the suggestion or presses Enter, all fields are replaced
  • Customer sees a completely different address and assumes their saved address "changed"

The customer's saved address was not modified

Autocomplete only modifies the checkout form fields — it does not update the saved address in the customer's account. The customer's account address book still has the original saved address. Only the in-progress checkout form was overwritten.

What Was Changed What Was NOT Changed Recovery
Checkout form fields (in-session only) Customer's saved address in account (untouched) Customer reloads checkout (fresh session) — saved address pre-fills again
Autocomplete suggestion filled state, city, ZIP All other saved addresses in account (untouched) Or: customer manually re-enters the correct address; the wrong data is only in the form

Reducing autocomplete interference (Plus only)

On Shopify Plus, a Checkout UI Extension can suppress or modify the autocomplete behavior by overlaying custom input handling. For non-Plus stores, there is no merchant-side way to disable Google Places autocomplete in Shopify-hosted checkout. The best customer guidance is: if editing a pre-filled address, be careful not to trigger autocomplete by typing slowly and watching for suggestions, clicking outside the field before pressing Enter, or waiting until all edits are complete before any keystroke suggestions appear.

Failure Mode 6: Billing Address Is Not Saved to the Address Book

A customer asks why their billing address never pre-fills at checkout even though they've entered it dozens of times. The reason: Shopify's customer address book stores shipping addresses only. Billing addresses entered at checkout are not persisted anywhere unless they are identical to the shipping address.

How Shopify handles billing address storage

Address Type Saved to Account Address Book? Pre-Fills at Next Checkout?
Shipping address Yes — if customer was logged in when order was placed Yes — if marked as default
Billing address (when "same as shipping" is checked) Indirectly — the shipping address is saved; billing is inferred as same Yes — inferred from saved shipping address
Billing address (when "use different billing address" is checked) No — the different billing address is NOT saved Never — must be re-entered every checkout
Shop Pay — saved payment method billing address Shop Pay vault only — not in the store's address book Yes — via Shop Pay's own vault, not the store's address book

This is a common friction point for customers who have a permanent billing address (home office, corporate headquarters, billing department) different from every shipping address. There is no native Shopify solution for saving a separate billing address that auto-fills. Shop Pay partially solves this by saving the card's billing address in the payment vault, but this is only available when the customer uses Shop Pay as their payment method.

Communicating This to Customers
In your order confirmation email or account settings page, add a note: "Your billing address is not saved separately. If you use the same billing address every time, add it as your default shipping address — it will pre-fill as the billing address option you can confirm at checkout. If your billing and shipping addresses always differ, you'll need to enter your billing address at each checkout."

Failure Mode 7: International Address Fields Don't Map to the Saved Address Format

Shopify's checkout address form changes its field structure based on the selected country. A customer who saved a UK address (postcode, no "state/province" field) and then checks out on a store configured for US addresses encounters mismatched fields — their saved address pre-fills incompletely, with some fields empty or validation errors.

Field mapping differences by country

Country Region Field Label Postal Code Format Common Mismatch
United States State 5 or 9 digits (ZIP / ZIP+4) UK postcodes rejected; Canadian postal codes fail ZIP validation
United Kingdom County (optional) Alphanumeric (e.g., SW1A 1AA) Saved as "county" — maps to "state" field on US form; US validation rejects alphanumeric
Canada Province A1A 1A1 (letter-digit alternating) Saved postal code pre-fills into ZIP field on US checkout; validation rejects format
Japan Prefecture 7 digits (e.g., 100-0001) Prefecture field may not have an equivalent in non-Japanese checkout form; field left blank
Australia State / Territory 4 digits Generally maps well to US form; rare mismatches on territory codes

Why this happens at a technical level

Shopify stores address fields as generic strings: address1, address2, city, province, zip, country. When the checkout form renders, it uses the selected country to determine what labels and validation rules apply to these fields. A UK postcode stored in zip will pre-fill into whatever field the checkout form calls the postal input — but if the form is set to US mode and validates ZIP codes as 5-digit numbers, the UK postcode fails validation and the field shows an error even though the data is technically present.

Diagnosing international address pre-fill failures

  1. Check which country the checkout form defaulted to — Shopify uses the store's country setting and the customer's saved country to determine the initial form layout
  2. If the country dropdown shows the wrong country, the field labels and validation rules will mismatch the saved address; instruct the customer to first select their correct country, then the other fields will re-render and may populate correctly
  3. For stores selling internationally: ensure your shipping zones include the customer's country, otherwise the country may not appear in the checkout country dropdown and the form defaults to the store's primary country with wrong field format
  4. Merchants on Shopify Markets: assign the correct country to each market and ensure the checkout locale matches the market; Shopify Markets can auto-detect country from IP and set the correct checkout form layout

Failure Mode 8: Headless Storefront — Address Book Not Fetched or Rendered

Headless Shopify storefronts (Hydrogen, Next.js, custom builds using the Storefront API) have zero automatic address pre-fill. There is no built-in address book component. Every step of address management — fetching saved addresses, displaying them for selection, updating the cart with the selected address, and passing the address into checkout — must be implemented by the developer.

What headless storefronts must implement for address book support

Feature API / Mutation Needed Common Mistake
Fetch customer's saved addresses Customer Account API: customer { addresses { nodes { ... } } } Using the Storefront API instead of Customer Account API — Storefront API doesn't return private customer address data
Display address selection UI Custom React/UI component — not provided by Shopify No UI built — customer sees blank form; developer assumed Shopify handles the display layer
Set selected address on cart Storefront API: cartBuyerIdentityUpdate with deliveryAddressPreferences Address selected in UI but not sent to Shopify cart — cart has no address context; checkout starts blank
Add new address to account Customer Account API: customerAddressCreate mutation Address saved only in component state — lost on refresh; never persisted to Shopify customer record
Set default address Admin API: customerDefaultAddressUpdate — requires server-side Admin API call Attempting to set default via Storefront API — not possible from client-side; requires server-side Admin API

Minimal headless address book implementation

// Customer Account API: fetch saved addresses
// (Must use OAuth 2.0 PKCE access token — see Login Not Working guide)

const CUSTOMER_ADDRESSES_QUERY = `
  query CustomerAddresses {
    customer {
      defaultAddress {
        id
        address1
        address2
        city
        province
        zip
        country
        firstName
        lastName
      }
      addresses(first: 10) {
        nodes {
          id
          address1
          address2
          city
          province
          zip
          country
          firstName
          lastName
        }
      }
    }
  }
`;

// After customer selects an address, update the cart:
const CART_BUYER_IDENTITY_UPDATE = `
  mutation cartBuyerIdentityUpdate($cartId: ID!, $buyerIdentity: CartBuyerIdentityInput!) {
    cartBuyerIdentityUpdate(cartId: $cartId, buyerIdentity: $buyerIdentity) {
      cart {
        id
        buyerIdentity {
          deliveryAddressPreferences {
            ... on MailingAddress {
              address1
              city
              country
            }
          }
        }
      }
      userErrors {
        field
        message
      }
    }
  }
`;

// Pass the selected address as deliveryAddressPreferences in buyerIdentity
const response = await fetch('https://{shop}.myshopify.com/api/2024-01/graphql.json', {
  method: 'POST',
  headers: {
    'Content-Type': 'application/json',
    'X-Shopify-Storefront-Access-Token': storefrontAccessToken,
  },
  body: JSON.stringify({
    query: CART_BUYER_IDENTITY_UPDATE,
    variables: {
      cartId: cartId,
      buyerIdentity: {
        customerAccessToken: customerAccountApiAccessToken,
        deliveryAddressPreferences: [{
          deliveryAddress: {
            address1: selectedAddress.address1,
            address2: selectedAddress.address2,
            city: selectedAddress.city,
            province: selectedAddress.province,
            zip: selectedAddress.zip,
            country: selectedAddress.country,
            firstName: selectedAddress.firstName,
            lastName: selectedAddress.lastName,
          }
        }]
      }
    }
  })
});
Customer Account API Access Token Is Different from Storefront API Token
The Customer Account API uses OAuth 2.0 PKCE — the access token is a short-lived JWT obtained after the customer completes the OTP login flow. The Storefront API uses a public API key that does not change. Many headless implementations mistakenly pass the Storefront API key to Customer Account API endpoints and get "unauthorized" errors. The two APIs have separate endpoints, separate authentication methods, and separate scopes. See the Login Not Working guide for the full Customer Account API auth flow.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can a customer have saved addresses but see "no addresses found" at checkout?

Yes — this happens in three scenarios. First, if no address is marked as the default, checkout doesn't know which address to pre-fill and starts blank (though a saved address dropdown may appear after clicking the address field, depending on checkout version). Second, if the customer is logged in via a Shop Pay identity that doesn't match the customer record with saved addresses — the Shop account email and the purchase account email are different. Third, on NCA stores where the storefront's /account/addresses Liquid page is still being used — the page renders blank because NCA doesn't populate it, giving the impression the address book is empty when addresses actually exist in the Shopify-hosted portal.

Does Shopify save the shipping address from a completed order automatically?

Yes — if the customer was logged in when the order was placed, Shopify adds the shipping address from the order to their address book. This is the primary way address books get populated: from past orders. Guest checkout orders do not save addresses to any account. If a logged-in customer's order address is different from their existing saved addresses, the new address is added to the account but not automatically set as the default — the existing default remains unchanged. This is why a customer who moved to a new address still sees their old address pre-fill until they go to the account portal and update their default.

Why does the address pre-fill sometimes show the billing address instead of the shipping address at the shipping step?

Shopify's checkout uses the customer's default saved address for the shipping address field. The billing address is handled separately at the payment step. If the checkout appears to pre-fill with a billing address at the shipping step, it's likely that the customer's saved "shipping" address in their account is actually their billing address — they saved it that way on a prior order where billing and shipping were the same. The customer can go to their account portal and add a separate default shipping address to fix this.

Can I pre-fill checkout with an address without the customer having to log in?

Yes, in two ways. First: cart permalink URLs support pre-filling checkout fields using URL parameters — you can encode address fields into a /cart URL and Shopify will populate them in the checkout form without requiring a customer login. This is useful for pre-filling addresses from CRM data or form submissions. Second: for headless storefronts, cartBuyerIdentityUpdate accepts a deliveryAddressPreferences parameter that can be set without a customer access token — passing a one-time-use address this way pre-fills the checkout address form for the current cart session without requiring account login.
