Shopify Abandoned Checkout Recovery Not Working — Emails Not Sending, Klaviyo Not Firing

Shopify's abandoned checkout recovery has a mandatory 1-hour delay, only fires if the customer entered their email first, and silently skips EU customers who didn't give marketing consent. Here is the complete diagnostic for every failure mode.

📅 Updated June 2026 ⏱ 10 min read 💡 Applies to: All Shopify plans
The Most Misdiagnosed Issue in Shopify
Merchants frequently conclude that abandoned checkout recovery "isn't working" immediately after a test abandonment. It's almost always the mandatory 1-hour delay — not a broken system. Read Failure Mode 1 first before investigating further.

How Shopify's Abandoned Checkout Recovery Works

Shopify's native abandoned checkout recovery is a single automated email sent exactly 1 hour after a customer abandons their checkout. It is available on all paid Shopify plans and is enabled by default.

The system has several requirements that must all be true for a recovery email to fire:

  • The customer must have entered an email address (or phone number) in the Contact step of checkout
  • The checkout must still be in an abandoned state after 1 hour — if the customer returns and purchases within the window, the sequence is cancelled
  • For EU stores: the customer must have given marketing consent at checkout
  • The store's abandoned checkout email must be enabled in Shopify Admin → Settings → Notifications

If any one of these conditions fails, no email is sent — and Shopify provides no error or log entry to explain why.

Failure Mode 1 — The 1-Hour Delay Is Mandatory (Most Common Cause)

On Basic, Shopify, and Advanced plans, the recovery email fires exactly 1 hour after the checkout is abandoned. This delay cannot be changed. There is no option in Shopify Admin to adjust the timing.

This is the most common reason merchants think recovery is broken: they abandon a test checkout, check their email 5 minutes later, see nothing, and assume the system is down. The system is working — the email just hasn't fired yet.

How to Test Correctly
To verify the system is working: abandon a checkout with a real email address, wait the full 60 minutes, then check your inbox. Also check Shopify Admin → Marketing → Automations or Orders → Abandoned checkouts to see the recovery email status per checkout record.

Where to check abandoned checkout records

  1. Go to Shopify Admin → Orders → Abandoned checkouts
  2. Find the checkout record for your test — each abandoned checkout shows its status
  3. Click into the record to see whether a recovery email was sent, is scheduled, or was not sent (with a reason)
  4. If the status shows "Recovery email scheduled", the 1-hour timer is still running
  5. If status shows "Recovery email sent", the email fired — check your inbox and spam folder

Customizing timing (Shopify Plus only)

Shopify Plus merchants can disable native abandoned checkout emails entirely and replace them with a custom Shopify Flow automation or a third-party ESP sequence (Klaviyo, Omnisend, Drip), which allows configuring custom timing. The standard approach is a 3-email sequence: 1 hour, 24 hours, 72 hours. Non-Plus merchants who need custom timing must use a third-party marketing app.

Failure Mode 2 — Customer Abandoned Before Entering Email

Shopify captures the customer's email address as soon as they type it in the Contact step and move forward — it does not wait for them to reach the payment step. But if the customer leaves the checkout page before entering any email address, Shopify has no contact information and cannot send a recovery email.

Customer Abandonment Point Recovery Email Why
Cart page (before clicking "Check out") Not possible No checkout record created; no contact info captured
Contact step — email entered, then left Sent Email captured as soon as field is filled; checkout record exists
Contact step — closed tab before entering email Not possible No email address to send to
Shipping step — abandoned Sent Email was captured at Contact step
Payment step — abandoned Sent Email was captured at Contact step
Completed order within 1 hour Cancelled Customer converted; Shopify cancels the recovery sequence

A large share of "abandoned" carts never actually start checkout — customers browse, add items, and leave from the cart page without clicking "Check out." These cart abandonments are not recoverable through Shopify's built-in system. To reach these customers, you need a separate "browse abandonment" or "cart abandonment" email strategy via a marketing app that sets a cookie and captures the email earlier (e.g., via a newsletter popup before checkout).

Failure Mode 3 — GDPR Marketing Consent Not Given (EU Stores)

For stores selling to EU/EEA customers, Shopify's abandoned checkout recovery emails are treated as marketing communications under GDPR. Shopify displays a marketing consent checkbox at checkout for these customers.

If the customer did not check the marketing consent box, Shopify will not send the abandoned checkout recovery email. This is intentional compliance behavior. The Admin → Abandoned checkouts record will show the checkout as abandoned but no recovery email scheduled.

GDPR Cannot Be Worked Around
You cannot pre-check the marketing consent checkbox or make it mandatory — this would violate GDPR's requirement for freely given, explicit consent. You also cannot send recovery emails to opted-out EU customers and frame them as "transactional" — this is legally contested and regulators have taken a clear position that abandonment recovery emails are marketing.

Legitimate ways to improve EU recovery rates

  • Make the marketing consent checkbox more prominent with a clear benefit statement ("Check this to receive help finishing your order")
  • Collect marketing consent earlier via a newsletter popup, before the customer starts checkout — this creates a profile that ESP tools can use to trigger recovery flows
  • Use retargeting ads (Facebook/Google) which have a different legal basis (legitimate interest in some jurisdictions) to reach abandoning EU customers
  • For logged-in customers who previously consented to marketing, Shopify may still send the recovery email — their existing consent record applies

Failure Mode 4 — Shopify Native Emails Disabled

Merchants who switch to a third-party ESP (Klaviyo, Omnisend, etc.) sometimes disable Shopify's native emails without configuring the ESP flow, or configure both systems simultaneously — resulting in either duplicate emails or none at all.

How to verify native email status

  1. Go to Shopify Admin → Settings → Notifications
  2. Scroll to the Email marketing section
  3. Find "Abandoned checkout" — verify it shows as Active
  4. If it shows as disabled, click to re-enable — or confirm your ESP flow is correctly configured before disabling
  5. If using Klaviyo or another ESP, you should disable the Shopify native email to avoid duplicates; leave only the ESP flow active
Duplicate Email Problem
Running both Shopify native abandoned checkout emails AND a Klaviyo/Omnisend flow simultaneously means customers receive two recovery emails for the same abandoned checkout. This creates a confusing experience and can increase unsubscribes. Disable the Shopify native email when using a third-party ESP flow.

Failure Mode 5 — Klaviyo "Started Checkout" Not Firing

Klaviyo's abandoned checkout recovery depends on the Started Checkout event, which is tracked by Klaviyo's JavaScript snippet on your storefront. This is separate from Shopify's native system. It has its own set of failure modes.

Why the Started Checkout event doesn't fire

Cause Result Fix
Cookie consent manager blocking Klaviyo JS before opt-in No event fired; JS not loaded Configure CMP to allow Klaviyo as "functional" or load after consent on an opt-in basis; or use server-side tracking
Ad blocker (uBlock, Brave) blocking Klaviyo JS No event for ~25–35% of desktop traffic Use server-side Klaviyo tracking; consider first-party data capture
Content Security Policy blocking klaviyo.com JS errors in console; no events Add static.klaviyo.com and a.klaviyo.com to CSP allowlist
Customer email not in Klaviyo profile system Event fires but no profile to send to Klaviyo creates a profile on first event if email is identified; ensure email is passed during checkout
Customer is on Klaviyo suppression list Flow skips the profile silently Check Klaviyo → Profiles → Suppression list; manually unsuppress if customer requested re-activation
Klaviyo flow filter "Placed order zero times since starting" not met Flow filtered out Review flow filters; ensure the time window logic matches your intended targeting

How to verify Klaviyo is receiving Started Checkout events

  1. Go to Klaviyo → Analytics → Metrics
  2. Find the Started Checkout metric and click into it
  3. Look for recent activity — if no events appear in the last 24 hours despite known checkout activity, the tracking is broken
  4. Open your store in a browser with no ad blockers, add an item to cart, begin checkout, enter your email address
  5. Check Klaviyo → Profiles → [your test email] — the Started Checkout event should appear within seconds
  6. If not, open the browser console on your store's checkout page and look for Klaviyo JS errors

Failure Mode 6 — Shopify Webhook Auto-Disabled

Third-party marketing apps and ESP integrations receive abandoned checkout data via Shopify's checkouts/create and checkouts/update webhooks. Shopify auto-disables webhook subscriptions when the receiving endpoint fails repeatedly.

A webhook subscription is auto-disabled after 19 consecutive delivery failures (non-2xx response from the endpoint, or no response within the 5-second timeout). When this happens, all new checkout events are silently dropped — no errors are shown in Shopify Admin, and no recovery emails fire from the ESP.

How to check webhook status

  1. Go to Shopify Admin → Settings → Notifications → Webhooks
  2. Look for checkouts/create and checkouts/update webhook subscriptions
  3. Check the "Last failed" column — if there are recent failures and the subscription is marked as paused or failed, this is the cause
  4. Alternatively, query the Admin API: GET /admin/api/2024-01/webhooks.json — look for "failures": n on the checkout webhooks
  5. Delete and re-create the webhook subscription from within your ESP's Shopify integration settings (Klaviyo → Integrations → Shopify → Reconnect or Sync settings)
The 5-Second Webhook Timeout
Shopify requires your ESP's webhook endpoint to respond within 5 seconds. If your ESP was having performance issues (high latency, deploy, incident), Shopify marks those deliveries as failures. After enough failures, the subscription is auto-disabled. Reconnecting the ESP integration typically recreates the webhook subscriptions fresh.

Failure Mode 7 — Recovery Emails Going to Spam

The recovery email is sending, but customers aren't seeing it because it's landing in spam. This is more common than merchants realize — they check admin, see "sent", and assume the customer ignored it. The email may be in spam.

Diagnosing email deliverability

  1. Go to Shopify Admin → Settings → Notifications → Sender email
  2. Check whether your sender email is a custom domain address (e.g., hello@yourstore.com) or a generic address
  3. If it's custom, verify SPF and DKIM records are correctly configured by clicking "Verify email" — Shopify shows whether authentication is passing
  4. Send a test recovery email to a Gmail account and check if it lands in Primary, Promotions, or Spam
  5. If landing in Spam: SPF/DKIM authentication failure or your domain has low sender reputation — consider warming up a dedicated sending domain
  6. Check whether any customer has previously marked your emails as spam — this creates a reputation signal that affects deliverability for all recipients

Failure Mode 8 — Headless Storefront / Hydrogen (No Native Recovery)

Shopify's abandoned checkout recovery system only applies to the native Shopify checkout. Headless storefronts using the Storefront API or Hydrogen do not automatically produce the abandoned checkout records that trigger Shopify's recovery emails.

When a Hydrogen or headless storefront creates a cart via cartCreate and then begins checkout, the native Shopify abandoned checkout recovery system does not track this flow in the same way as the standard storefront checkout.

Implementing abandoned cart recovery in headless/Hydrogen

  1. Capture the customer's email early — when they subscribe to a newsletter popup, log in, or enter their email in a cart field before checkout
  2. When the cart is created or updated, send the cart contents and customer email to your ESP via API (e.g., Klaviyo's Track API with a custom Started Checkout event, including cart URL)
  3. Subscribe to Shopify's checkouts/create webhook at the server level and forward the payload to your ESP
  4. Configure an abandoned checkout flow in your ESP triggered by the custom event, with your own timing and email content
  5. Generate a cart recovery URL that restores cart state — either a Shopify cart permalink or a custom URL that calls cartCreate with the saved line items
  6. The Hydrogen starter template does not include this functionality — it must be built as a custom integration
Cart Permalink Limitation in Headless
The standard Shopify cart permalink (/cart/[variantId]:[quantity]) restores to the native Shopify checkout, not to your headless storefront. For headless recovery, you need a custom recovery URL that initializes your storefront with the saved cart items via the Storefront API.

When Recovery Emails Are Sending But Not Converting

If the diagnostic above confirms emails are sending correctly but recovery rates are poor, the issue is content and timing rather than a technical failure. Common content problems:

  • Generic subject line — "You left something behind" is easy to ignore; personalized subject lines with the product name convert 2–3× better
  • No product image — the recovery email template should show the product the customer was looking at; Shopify's default template includes this if configured correctly
  • No urgency or social proof — a single email with no scarcity signal or review quote gives customers no reason to act now vs later
  • Single email only — Shopify's native system sends one email; a 3-email sequence (1h, 24h, 72h) via Klaviyo or Omnisend typically recovers 2–4× more revenue
  • No discount incentive — some merchants offer a small discount in the third email of the sequence to convert fence-sitters; this increases recovery rates but reduces margin
