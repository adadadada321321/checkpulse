Shopify Ahly Pay (National Bank of Egypt) Not Working — 7 Root Causes & Exact Fixes

Updated June 2026 · Egypt merchants · NBE wallet · EGP only

Ahly Pay not appearing at Shopify checkout, OTP not received, or order stuck pending after approval? Ahly Pay (operated by National Bank of Egypt, Egypt's largest state bank) fails in 7 distinct ways — and the most common one is a confusion between the Ahly Mobile banking app and the Ahly Pay wallet. They are separate NBE products. This guide covers every failure mode with exact steps to resolve each.

Critical Distinction: Ahly Mobile ≠ Ahly Pay

National Bank of Egypt operates two separate digital products that merchants and customers regularly confuse. Ahly Mobile is the banking app — customers use it to check their NBE account balance, make transfers, and manage their bank products. Ahly Pay is a separate mobile wallet — it requires independent registration, carries its own pre-funded balance, and is the product used for merchant payments.

A customer who has banked with NBE for 20 years and uses Ahly Mobile daily does NOT have Ahly Pay automatically. They must download the Ahly Pay app separately and complete wallet registration. This single misconception causes the majority of Ahly Pay failures at Shopify checkout in Egypt.

NBE is Egypt's largest and oldest bank (founded 1898) — a very large portion of Egyptian shoppers have NBE bank accounts, which means this confusion affects more potential customers than any other Egyptian wallet product.

7 Failure Modes Covered

  1. Option not appearing — gateway does not support Ahly Pay
  2. Customer has Ahly Mobile banking app but no Ahly Pay wallet
  3. OTP not received or payment session expired
  4. Insufficient Ahly Pay wallet balance (bank account is separate)
  5. CBE daily or monthly transaction limit exceeded
  6. Gateway callback not configured — order stuck pending after approval
  7. Mobile number mismatch between Ahly Pay registration and checkout
Failure Mode Analysis
1

Ahly Pay option not appearing at checkout

Merchant Configuration

When the Ahly Pay option is completely absent from the Shopify checkout payment methods, the root cause is almost always that the merchant's payment gateway either does not support Ahly Pay at all or has not enabled it explicitly. Ahly Pay is a bank-issued mobile wallet operated by National Bank of Egypt — it is not a carrier wallet and it is not a card scheme. Supporting NBE debit or credit cards does not enable Ahly Pay.

Paymob is the primary Egyptian payment gateway with Ahly Pay integration. However, Ahly Pay availability on Paymob is plan-dependent — it is not available on all merchant plan tiers. Merchants who enabled Vodafone Cash or Fawry on Paymob cannot assume Ahly Pay was also enabled; each wallet requires a separate activation request. Other Egyptian gateways (Kashier, PayTabs Egypt) may offer Ahly Pay on select configurations — confirm directly with your gateway provider.

International gateways — Stripe, PayPal, Square — do not support Ahly Pay. They process card payments (Visa, Mastercard, Amex) and international wallets but have no integration with the Egyptian CBE mobile wallet framework. Merchants using only international gateways cannot accept Ahly Pay regardless of any other configuration change.

Steps to Fix

  1. Confirm your gateway supports Ahly Pay on your plan: Contact Paymob (or your gateway provider) and ask explicitly whether Ahly Pay (NBE mobile wallet) is supported on your current plan tier. Do not assume based on having other Egyptian wallets enabled.
  2. Request Ahly Pay activation: If supported, submit an activation request to your gateway. There may be NBE merchant verification steps required before Ahly Pay can be enabled for your account.
  3. Enable Ahly Pay as a separate payment method: In your gateway dashboard, locate the payment methods section and enable Ahly Pay explicitly — it will be listed separately from Vodafone Cash, Fawry, and card payments.
  4. Test with a real Ahly Pay wallet: After enabling, conduct an end-to-end test using a real registered Ahly Pay wallet to confirm the option appears and the payment flow completes correctly before opening it to customers.
2

Customer has Ahly Mobile banking app but no Ahly Pay wallet

Customer-Side Issue

This is the single most common Ahly Pay failure for Egyptian Shopify merchants, and it stems from a product naming confusion that NBE has created by operating two separate apps with the word "Ahly" in the name. Ahly Mobile (also referred to as "CIB Mobile" by some and "Ahly Mobile" by NBE customers broadly) is the banking app that lets customers check their account balance, initiate transfers, pay bills, and manage their National Bank of Egypt accounts. Ahly Pay is a completely separate product — a CBE-regulated mobile wallet that requires its own registration process.

A customer who has been using National Bank of Egypt for years and has the Ahly Mobile app installed on their phone is NOT an Ahly Pay wallet holder unless they separately downloaded the Ahly Pay app and completed wallet registration. When such a customer attempts to enter their mobile number at the Ahly Pay checkout step, they will receive errors like "wallet not found," "account not registered," or "mobile number not recognized" — because no Ahly Pay wallet exists for that number, even though the customer's NBE bank account is fully active.

Given that NBE is Egypt's largest bank with tens of millions of customers, this confusion affects a very large share of potential Ahly Pay users. Many will abandon checkout believing there is a technical problem with the store, when in fact they simply need to register for Ahly Pay separately.

Steps to Fix

  1. Add clarification near the Ahly Pay payment option: Display a note explaining that Ahly Pay is a separate product from Ahly Mobile. Example: "Ahly Pay requires separate registration via the Ahly Pay app — your NBE bank account alone is not enough to pay with Ahly Pay."
  2. Provide wallet registration guidance: Link to NBE's Ahly Pay registration page or include brief instructions: download the Ahly Pay app → register with your Egyptian mobile number → fund the wallet → return to complete purchase.
  3. Keep fallback payment options visible: Customers who discover they need to register for Ahly Pay will not complete the registration mid-checkout. Ensure alternative payment methods (Vodafone Cash, Fawry, Meeza card, bank transfer) are clearly visible and usable without Ahly Pay.
  4. Consider post-abandonment email for Ahly Pay failures: If your email capture allows it, sending a short registration guide to customers who abandoned at the Ahly Pay step can recover sales that would otherwise be permanently lost.
Note for merchants: This failure cannot be resolved from the merchant side — it requires the customer to take action before returning to the store. What merchants can control is preventing confusion, preserving the customer's cart, and making fallback payment options obvious so the immediate sale is not lost.
3

OTP not received or payment session expired

Both Sides

Ahly Pay uses a one-time password (OTP) sent via SMS to authorize each payment. OTP failures are the third most common Ahly Pay issue on Shopify and can originate from several distinct causes: the OTP SMS is delayed by the carrier network and arrives after the session has already timed out; the customer entered their phone number incorrectly; the customer's mobile number is on a carrier with poor SMS delivery reliability at that moment; or the payment session timeout configured in the gateway is too short to accommodate realistic SMS delivery delays.

When the OTP session expires before the customer enters a valid code, the payment fails and the customer must restart the checkout from the payment method selection step. If the gateway session timeout is set very low (under 90 seconds), a significant share of legitimate Ahly Pay payments will fail for customers on networks with any SMS latency — even if the customer's wallet is fully funded and registered.

Steps to Fix

  1. Extend the OTP session timeout: In your gateway settings for Ahly Pay, set the OTP session expiry to at least 180 seconds (3 minutes). This accommodates SMS delivery delays without meaningfully increasing fraud risk.
  2. Add checkout guidance about the OTP step: Inform customers before they enter their number: "You will receive an SMS with a code. Enter it within 3 minutes to confirm the payment." This reduces confusion when the OTP arrives after a brief delay.
  3. Instruct customers to check for OTP resend: If the OTP does not arrive, most Ahly Pay flows offer a "Resend OTP" option. Advise customers to use this rather than refreshing the page, which will end the session.
  4. Verify the customer's registered number: If a customer reports consistently not receiving an OTP, confirm that the mobile number they are entering at checkout matches the number registered in their Ahly Pay wallet — see Failure #7 for number mismatch details.
4

Insufficient Ahly Pay wallet balance (bank account is a separate balance)

Customer-Side Issue

Ahly Pay is a pre-funded mobile wallet — its balance is completely separate from the customer's National Bank of Egypt bank account balance. A customer who has EGP 50,000 in their NBE savings account but EGP 0 in their Ahly Pay wallet cannot pay at Shopify checkout using Ahly Pay, regardless of how much money is in their bank. This is a very common confusion because many customers assume that Ahly Pay draws directly from their NBE bank account at the point of purchase.

The wallet must be funded in advance — via the Ahly Pay app (bank-to-wallet transfer), at an NBE branch or ATM, or through an authorized top-up channel. Unlike a debit card transaction (which draws directly from the linked bank account at the point of sale), Ahly Pay requires the customer to pre-load funds into the wallet before attempting a purchase. When the Ahly Pay wallet balance is insufficient, the payment will be rejected at the point of OTP authorization.

Steps to Fix

  1. Communicate the pre-funding requirement at checkout: Add a visible note near the Ahly Pay payment option: "Ahly Pay uses your wallet balance, not your NBE bank account directly. Top up your Ahly Pay wallet before checkout via the Ahly Pay app."
  2. Display the order total prominently before payment selection: Customers should see the exact EGP amount before selecting Ahly Pay so they can verify their wallet has sufficient balance. Shopify's default checkout should do this, but confirm it is visible.
  3. Provide top-up instructions: Brief guidance near the payment option: "To top up: open the Ahly Pay app → Add Money → Transfer from your NBE account or visit any NBE branch or ATM."
  4. Offer fallback payment options: Customers who discover insufficient Ahly Pay balance mid-checkout should have clearly visible alternatives to complete the purchase rather than abandoning.
5

CBE daily or monthly transaction limit exceeded

Customer-Side Issue

Ahly Pay, like all CBE-regulated Egyptian mobile wallets, operates under the Central Bank of Egypt's tiered transaction and balance limits. These limits apply per wallet tier: unverified wallets carry lower transaction caps (approximately EGP 5,000 balance limit and lower daily transaction ceilings), while fully verified wallets are eligible for higher limits under CBE guidelines. Customers who have made several payments during the day or month may hit these limits and have an Ahly Pay payment rejected even when their wallet has sufficient balance.

From the merchant's perspective, this failure looks identical to an insufficient balance failure — the OTP step fails or the payment is rejected without a detailed explanation of why. The customer typically knows they have wallet balance but does not connect the failure to CBE transaction limits. High-value orders from customers who are also using their Ahly Pay wallet for everyday purchases are at elevated risk of hitting daily limits.

Steps to Fix

  1. Advise customers to verify their Ahly Pay tier: Customers should check their wallet tier in the Ahly Pay app. Full KYC verification (national ID verification at an NBE branch or in-app) unlocks higher limits. Unverified wallets have the tightest caps.
  2. Add error guidance when payment is rejected: If your gateway provides a limit-exceeded error code, display a customer-facing message: "Ahly Pay limit reached — verify your identity in the Ahly Pay app to increase transaction limits, or use an alternative payment method."
  3. Consider order split guidance for high-value merchants: For merchants whose average order value is close to or above CBE wallet limits, proactively inform customers that high-value purchases may require a verified Ahly Pay wallet.
  4. Ensure alternative payment methods are available: Customers who hit CBE limits should have clear alternatives visible — Meeza card, bank transfer, or installment products like valU for high-value purchases.
6

Gateway callback URL misconfigured — order stuck pending after OTP approval

Merchant Configuration

This is the most dangerous Ahly Pay failure mode for merchants because it creates a real financial risk: the customer's Ahly Pay wallet is debited and the payment is confirmed on the gateway side, but the Shopify order never transitions from pending to paid. This happens when the callback (webhook) URL configured in the gateway is not correctly pointing to the live Shopify store. The gateway sends the payment confirmation to an unreachable or stale URL, receives no acknowledgment, and the order remains in a perpetually pending state.

Common causes include: the gateway was originally configured during development with a localhost URL or a development tunnel (like ngrok); the store domain changed after the gateway was set up; the store migrated from one Shopify plan to another and the payment settings were not updated; or the callback URL was never configured at all when Ahly Pay was activated. In all of these cases, the customer receives a payment confirmation from their Ahly Pay app, but the merchant's Shopify dashboard shows no paid order — creating a dispute risk if products are shipped or fulfillment is initiated without confirming payment.

Steps to Fix

  1. Immediately verify the callback URL in your gateway dashboard: Navigate to your payment gateway settings and locate the Ahly Pay (or general mobile wallet) webhook/callback URL. Confirm it matches your live Shopify store URL exactly — HTTPS, current domain, correct path.
  2. Test the callback URL is reachable: Send a test POST request to the callback URL and verify it returns a 200 response. A localhost or expired tunnel URL will return connection refused.
  3. Reconcile pending Shopify orders with gateway transactions: Pull a report of Shopify orders in "pending payment" status from the past 30 days and compare against confirmed Ahly Pay transactions in your gateway dashboard. Any matches represent real payments that were received but not recorded in Shopify.
  4. Process confirmed payments manually if found: For orders where payment is confirmed in the gateway but pending in Shopify, contact Shopify support about manually marking the order as paid, and do not ship until the payment is verified on both sides.
  5. Update the callback URL and run an end-to-end test: After updating the URL, complete a real Ahly Pay test purchase and confirm the Shopify order transitions from pending to paid within 60 seconds of OTP approval.
Financial risk warning: A misconfigured callback URL means you may be fulfilling orders without confirmed payment. Before shipping any order that was placed via Ahly Pay and shows as pending, verify payment status in your gateway dashboard directly — do not rely solely on the Shopify order status.
7

Mobile number mismatch between Ahly Pay registration and checkout entry

Customer-Side Issue

Ahly Pay wallets are registered to a specific Egyptian mobile number. When a customer attempts a payment at Shopify checkout, the number they enter must exactly match the number registered in their Ahly Pay wallet. If there is a mismatch — the customer registered their wallet with one number but enters a different number at checkout, or they have multiple SIM cards and are currently active on a different number — the gateway will return a "wallet not found" or "invalid account" error.

This happens more often than merchants expect because: Egyptian customers frequently use multiple SIM cards (Vodafone, Orange, We, Etisalat); the customer may have registered their Ahly Pay wallet years ago with a number they no longer use as their primary; or the customer may have transferred their Ahly Pay registration to a new number at some point and forgotten the original number. Since Ahly Pay is carrier-agnostic (it works with any Egyptian carrier's SIM), customers may have registered on any of the four major carriers.

Steps to Fix

  1. Add guidance near the Ahly Pay number entry field: A note like "Enter the mobile number registered in your Ahly Pay app — this may be different from your current active SIM" helps customers identify the correct number before attempting payment.
  2. Instruct customers to check their registered number in the Ahly Pay app: The Ahly Pay app displays the registered mobile number in the account profile section. Customers can verify their registered number before entering it at checkout.
  3. Clarify that Ahly Pay is carrier-agnostic: Unlike Vodafone Cash (010 only) or Orange Cash (012 only), Ahly Pay works with any Egyptian mobile number. The constraint is not carrier but wallet registration — any prefix (010, 011, 012, 015) is valid if it was used to register the wallet.
  4. Handle "wallet not found" errors with actionable guidance: When this error occurs, display a message: "No Ahly Pay wallet found for this number. Check the registered number in your Ahly Pay app, or use an alternative payment method." Avoid generic error messages that leave customers confused.

Ahly Pay Shopify Integration Diagnostic Checklist

Gateway confirmed to support Ahly Pay on your plan — verified with Paymob (or your gateway provider) that Ahly Pay (NBE mobile wallet) is available on your plan tier and activated as a distinct payment method, separate from card payments and carrier wallets.

Ahly Pay enabled in gateway dashboard — the Ahly Pay option is toggled on as an active payment method and verified to appear at the live checkout for real customers.

Production callback/webhook URL configured and reachable — the callback URL in the gateway is the live HTTPS Shopify store URL with the correct domain, returns 200 on a test POST, and is not a localhost or development tunnel address.

OTP session timeout set to at least 3 minutes — Ahly Pay payment session expiry is configured to 180 seconds or more to accommodate SMS delivery delays and customer response time.

Checkout clarifies that Ahly Mobile ≠ Ahly Pay wallet — a visible note near the Ahly Pay option explains that NBE bank account and Ahly Mobile banking app users need separate Ahly Pay wallet registration before using this payment method.

Pre-funding requirement communicated at checkout — customers are informed that Ahly Pay wallet balance is separate from any NBE bank account and must be topped up via the Ahly Pay app, at an NBE branch, or ATM before purchase.

Number registration guidance available — a help note explains that customers must enter the mobile number registered in their Ahly Pay wallet, which may differ from their current active SIM, and that Ahly Pay works with any Egyptian carrier prefix.

Checkout currency is EGP — store is priced in EGP, or Shopify Markets is configured to present EGP to Egyptian customers. Ahly Pay processes EGP only and cannot be used for non-EGP orders.

End-to-end test with a real Ahly Pay wallet completed — a test payment was made through the live Shopify checkout with a real registered Ahly Pay wallet, OTP was received and entered within the session window, and the order updated from pending to paid within 60 seconds.

Fallback payment methods available — customers who cannot use Ahly Pay have clear alternatives: Vodafone Cash, Bee Wallet, Fawry offline code, Meeza card, or bank transfer — to prevent checkout abandonment when Ahly Pay fails for any reason.
