Shopify Sympl Not Working — Not Showing, Card Balance, National ID & OTP Issues

Updated June 2026 · 7 failure modes · Egypt merchants (EGP) · Save now, pay later

Sympl is an Egyptian "save now, pay later" fintech backed by Beco Capital — and it works fundamentally differently from valU and Shahry. Sympl is not a credit product. It does not extend credit or score customers. Instead, it requires the customer's Egyptian bank card to have the full purchase amount available, then schedules installment repayments from that card. A flat upfront fee of 100 EGP applies to every transaction. This creates a unique set of Shopify failure modes — most critically, customers with sufficient intent but low card balance will be declined even though they would pass any credit check. This guide covers all seven Sympl failure modes for Shopify merchants.

How Sympl Differs From valU and Shahry on Shopify in Egypt

Sympl is not a credit product. This is the most important fact for troubleshooting Sympl failures. valU (EFG Hermes-backed) and Shahry (VC-backed, ML credit scoring) both extend credit to customers — they lend money based on creditworthiness, and the customer repays the loan in installments. Sympl does neither. Sympl requires the customer's Egyptian bank card to hold the full purchase amount at checkout. Sympl then charges the full amount to the card, and separately schedules repayments back to the customer in installments. This means a customer declined by Sympl is not "credit rejected" — they simply do not have the cash available. A customer with a 2,000 EGP cart needs 2,000 EGP on their card, regardless of their creditworthiness.

Sympl charges a flat 100 EGP upfront fee on every transaction. This fee is fixed regardless of purchase amount — whether the cart is 300 EGP or 3,000 EGP. The 100 EGP is Sympl's service fee for splitting the purchase into installments. The remaining installments are zero percent interest. This fee is charged at checkout and can cause abandonment if customers are not informed beforehand. Merchants should display the Sympl fee prominently on product pages alongside any Sympl badge.

Sympl requires no pre-registration, no app download, and no credit application. Unlike valU (requires the valU app and a pre-approved credit limit) and Shahry (requires the Shahry app and ML credit approval), any Egyptian bank cardholder can attempt Sympl at checkout for the first time. The checkout flow asks for card details, mobile number, and Egyptian National ID — then confirms or declines in real time. This simplicity is Sympl's advantage for merchants, but it means declined customers often don't understand why, since they expected instant approval.

Sympl's installment plans run 3 to 5 installments at monthly, bi-weekly, or weekly frequency — all at zero percent interest. Sympl operates only in Egypt in EGP. Launched in 2020/2021 with $6M seed funding led by Beco Capital (UAE), Sympl has grown to over 800 merchants and 85,000 active users, reporting strong profitability in 2024 and targeting African expansion.

Sympl Not Showing at Checkout

Merchant-Side

Sympl not appearing at checkout is the most common merchant complaint. Unlike payment failures that surface during a transaction, this failure is silent — Egyptian customers never see Sympl as an option and either abandon or pay with a method they didn't prefer.

The primary cause is a missed activation step. Merchants install the Sympl app from the Shopify App Store — which creates the integration — but do not complete the mandatory second step: enabling Sympl as an alternative payment provider in Shopify Admin → Settings → Payments → Alternative payment providers. Installing the app is not the same as activating it at checkout. This two-step requirement applies to every BNPL provider on Shopify (Tabby, valU, Shahry, Tamara, Postpay) and is the most commonly skipped step during onboarding.

The second most common cause is currency. Sympl is EGP-only — it will not render at checkout for any currency other than Egyptian Pounds. Shopify stores serving multiple markets must verify that Egyptian customers see EGP, not USD, EUR, or another currency. Sympl also requires a shipping address in Egypt — it will not appear for customers shipping outside Egypt even if they hold an Egyptian card.

A third cause is sandbox mode. Sympl's Shopify integration may be installed with test/sandbox API credentials from the merchant onboarding process. Sandbox credentials will not display the option at live checkout. The Sympl merchant portal must show Live or Production status for checkout to be active.

How to Fix

  1. Go to Shopify Admin → Settings → Payments
  2. Scroll to Alternative payment providers → Confirm Sympl is listed and set to Active (not just installed)
  3. Verify your store currency for Egyptian customers is EGP (Egyptian Pound) — check Settings → General → Store currency and Shopify Markets for Egypt
  4. In your Sympl merchant portal, confirm your account is Live / Active — not Pending or Test
  5. Confirm Sympl is using live production API credentials, not sandbox keys
  6. Test checkout using a shipping address in Egypt — Sympl will not appear for non-Egypt addresses
Note: Sympl's Shopify app may have a separate activation prompt inside the Sympl merchant portal after installation. Check for any "complete setup" or "activate store" steps in the Sympl dashboard before assuming the Shopify-side configuration is the problem.
2

Card Balance Insufficient — Full Purchase Amount Required

Customer-Side

This is Sympl's most misunderstood failure mode — and the one that most surprises merchants and customers alike. Unlike Shahry and valU, which extend credit (lending money up to a credit limit), Sympl is not a credit product. Sympl requires the customer's Egyptian bank card to have the full cart amount available at the time of purchase.

Sympl's mechanism is closer to a debit-based installment scheduler than traditional BNPL. When a customer chooses Sympl at checkout, Sympl charges the full purchase amount to their card immediately. Sympl then manages the repayment schedule — refunding the customer in installments according to their chosen plan (3-5 installments, monthly/bi-weekly/weekly). The customer effectively gets their money back in installments while having paid upfront. This means a customer with a 1,500 EGP cart and only 800 EGP on their card will be declined by Sympl — not because of creditworthiness, but because of insufficient balance.

This is the key practical divergence from Shahry and valU: a customer with strong credit history but temporary low cash balance can use Shahry or valU (credit products) but cannot use Sympl. Conversely, a customer with no credit history but a fully funded debit card can use Sympl but may not qualify for valU or Shahry.

The 100 EGP Sympl service fee is also deducted in addition to the cart amount. A customer with exactly 1,000 EGP on their card attempting a 1,000 EGP purchase will be declined because the card needs 1,000 EGP (cart) plus 100 EGP (Sympl fee) available.

What to Tell Your Customer

  1. Explain that Sympl requires the full cart amount on the card at the time of purchase — it is not a credit product
  2. The card also needs an additional 100 EGP for Sympl's service fee — so a 900 EGP cart requires 1,000 EGP available on the card
  3. The customer should check their card balance before retrying — ensure the full purchase amount plus 100 EGP is available
  4. If the customer has the funds but in a different account or card, they should use that card
  5. If the customer wants BNPL without needing full balance available, suggest Tabby, valU, or Shahry — these extend credit and do not require the full amount on card
Merchant note: Many Egypt merchants assume all BNPL providers work the same way. Sympl's debit-based model means its declined population is different from Shahry and valU declines. A customer declined by Sympl for balance reasons may approve instantly on Shahry or valU. Offer multiple Egypt BNPL options at checkout to maximize conversion.

Non-Egyptian Bank Card Rejected

Customer-Side

Sympl only accepts cards issued by Egyptian banks. Even if the card network is Visa or Mastercard, a card issued by a bank outside Egypt — UAE, Saudi Arabia, UK, US, or any other country — will be rejected. This is not a Visa or Mastercard network restriction; it is a Sympl policy restriction tied to Sympl's Egypt-only regulatory structure and its requirement to match the card with an Egyptian National ID and Egyptian mobile number.

This affects several common customer groups: Egyptian expatriates living abroad who use non-Egyptian bank accounts, non-Egyptian residents in Egypt (tourists, GCC nationals, expatriates) who do not hold Egyptian bank accounts, and customers who have a mix of Egyptian and international cards. Even a dual-national Egyptian with both an Egyptian and a foreign bank account must use their Egyptian bank card — not the foreign one — for Sympl to proceed.

Merchants serving Egypt but also GCC markets should note that Tabby and Postpay support non-Egyptian cards for customers in their respective markets and may be better alternatives for customers who cannot use Sympl due to card origin restrictions.

How to Address This

  1. Confirm the customer's card is issued by an Egyptian bank — if in doubt, the card's issuing bank is usually printed on the back
  2. For Egyptian customers with both Egyptian and foreign cards, advise them to use their Egyptian bank-issued card
  3. For non-Egyptian customers, Sympl is not available — offer standard payment methods (Visa/Mastercard, PayPal) or alternatives like Tabby for GCC/Egypt customers
  4. Ensure your checkout page clearly indicates that Sympl is available for Egyptian bank cards only — this prevents non-Egyptian customers from attempting and abandoning
Egypt target audience: Sympl's 50M+ addressable market is Egypt's debit and credit cardholders with Egyptian bank accounts. Its 800+ merchants and 85,000 active customers represent the early penetration of this market — non-Egyptian cardholders are intentionally out of scope.

Egyptian National ID Verification Failure

Customer-Side

Sympl requires customers to provide their Egyptian National ID number at checkout. This is a standard requirement across all Egypt-based BNPL and fintech providers (valU, Shahry, and Sympl all require National ID). Sympl uses the National ID to verify customer identity and match it against the card details provided. If the verification fails, the transaction is declined.

Common causes of National ID failure include: the customer entering an incorrect digit in their 14-digit National ID number; a recently issued or renewed National ID that has not yet propagated through Egypt's civil registry (new IDs can take weeks to appear in third-party verification databases); an expired National ID (the ID expiry date must not be passed); and — unlike Shahry which has a 21+ age minimum — Sympl's age requirement is not publicly specified, though customers must be adults holding a valid Egyptian National ID.

Unlike Shahry, which requires a separate identity verification step through its app during onboarding, Sympl performs National ID verification at checkout in real time with no pre-registration. This means the first time a customer attempts Sympl, any National ID mismatch causes an immediate checkout decline with no second chance to correct the input without restarting the checkout.

How to Address This

  1. Ask the customer to double-check their 14-digit Egyptian National ID number for typos — a single incorrect digit causes rejection
  2. Confirm the customer's National ID is not expired — the expiry date is printed on the ID card
  3. If the ID was recently renewed, the customer should wait 2-4 weeks for the civil registry to update, then retry
  4. For non-Egyptian nationals or expatriates who do not hold an Egyptian National ID, Sympl is not available — redirect to standard checkout or alternative providers
  5. If ID verification fails repeatedly for a valid ID, direct the customer to contact Sympl support: contact@sympl.ai or call 15987
Sympl contact: Sympl's official support channels are contact@sympl.ai and the Egyptian hotline 15987. For National ID verification issues that cannot be resolved by the customer, Sympl's support team can manually investigate.
5

OTP / Card Verification Failure

Customer-Side

Sympl's checkout flow sends a one-time password (OTP) to the customer's registered mobile number as part of the identity and card verification process. Multiple users have reported OTP delivery failures as a persistent issue — particularly cases where the OTP code simply does not arrive, causing the checkout session to time out.

In addition to OTP non-delivery, users report a related checkout loop: being asked to re-enter card details multiple times as if no details were provided, even when the card information was correctly entered. A 1 EGP authorization charge is deducted from the card for verification but the transaction never completes. These repeated 1 EGP deductions with no completed purchase indicate a verification loop in Sympl's checkout system, not a card problem on the customer's side.

Common causes for OTP failure: the mobile number provided does not match the number registered with the customer's Egyptian bank card; the number is a non-Egyptian SIM (Sympl expects an Egyptian mobile number, +20 prefix); the customer's mobile carrier is experiencing a temporary SMS delay; or Sympl's SMS gateway has a momentary outage.

How to Address This

  1. Confirm the customer is using an Egyptian mobile number (+20 prefix) — Sympl requires an Egyptian SIM for OTP delivery
  2. Verify the mobile number matches the number registered with their Egyptian bank card — some banks register a different number
  3. If OTP does not arrive within 60 seconds, ask the customer to request a resend and check for SMS delivery
  4. If stuck in a card re-entry loop with 1 EGP deductions, advise the customer to stop and contact Sympl support at 15987 — do not retry repeatedly, as multiple authorization attempts can temporarily block the card
  5. Try the checkout again from a fresh browser session or a different device before contacting support
  6. If the issue persists, contact Sympl support at contact@sympl.ai or 15987 — this is likely a backend verification issue on Sympl's side
Known issue: OTP delivery failures and card re-entry loops have been reported by multiple Sympl users. These are typically transient platform issues rather than customer-specific problems. If multiple customers report the same issue simultaneously, check Sympl's status page or contact Sympl merchant support — a platform-side issue may be affecting checkout broadly.

100 EGP Fee Causing Checkout Abandonment

Merchant + Customer

Sympl charges a flat service fee of 100 Egyptian Pounds on every transaction. This fee is fixed regardless of the cart amount — a 300 EGP purchase and a 3,000 EGP purchase both carry the same 100 EGP Sympl fee. The fee is presented at checkout and charged upfront as part of the first payment. Unlike interest (which grows with the loan amount), Sympl's flat fee is more significant as a percentage of smaller purchases.

This fee is the second most significant abandonment driver after card balance issues. Customers who reach the Sympl checkout step and see a 100 EGP fee they were not expecting often abandon — not because they cannot pay it, but because they were not informed upfront that Sympl would cost extra. Abandonment due to unexpected fees is the most predictable and avoidable loss for merchants using Sympl.

For a 300 EGP cart, the 100 EGP fee represents a 33% surcharge — making Sympl economically unattractive versus paying the full 300 EGP with a card. At 1,000 EGP+ purchases, the 100 EGP fee (10% or less of cart value) becomes more palatable, and the benefit of spreading payments into installments outweighs the fee for most customers. Sympl is therefore most economically compelling for larger Egyptian purchases.

How to Reduce Fee-Related Abandonment

  1. Display the Sympl 100 EGP fee prominently on product pages alongside the Sympl badge — "Pay in installments with Sympl — 100 EGP service fee applies"
  2. Consider adding a pre-checkout tooltip or modal that explains Sympl's fee structure before the customer selects Sympl at checkout
  3. Position Sympl primarily for higher-ticket items (1,000 EGP+) where the fee represents a smaller fraction of the cart and installment benefit is more compelling
  4. For lower-ticket items where the 100 EGP fee is proportionally large, consider also offering Tabby or valU (which use interest or merchant fee models rather than a fixed customer fee)
  5. Review your Sympl merchant agreement — some merchant arrangements allow Sympl's fee to be merchant-subsidized, removing the customer-facing 100 EGP charge entirely
Merchant-subsidized fee option: Depending on the merchant's agreement with Sympl, it may be possible to structure the fee as merchant-paid rather than customer-paid. This removes the 100 EGP customer-facing charge and eliminates this abandonment driver entirely. Contact Sympl at contact@sympl.ai to discuss merchant pricing options.

Merchant Onboarding Not Complete

Merchant-Side

Sympl requires merchants to complete a formal onboarding and approval process before checkout goes live. This involves merchant verification, commercial registration review, and Sympl's internal merchant risk assessment. Until this approval is complete, the Sympl option at checkout will either not appear at all or will be visible in test/sandbox mode but not process real transactions.

Common signs of incomplete merchant onboarding: Sympl appears at checkout but shows an error when the customer tries to proceed; Sympl does not appear at all despite the app being installed and configured; or the Sympl merchant portal shows a "Pending" or "Under Review" account status. Merchants who rush to deploy Sympl immediately after installing the Shopify app often encounter this — the integration may be technically set up, but the merchant account is not yet active.

Additionally, existing approved merchants can have their account placed on hold for periodic compliance reviews or if there is a dispute or chargeback issue. A previously working Sympl integration that suddenly stops working is often the result of an account hold rather than a technical problem.

How to Fix

  1. Log into the Sympl merchant portal and check your account status — it must show Live / Active for checkout to work
  2. If status shows Pending, contact Sympl merchant support at contact@sympl.ai or 15987 to inquire about approval timeline
  3. Ensure you have submitted all required merchant documents: commercial registration, Tax Card, and any financial statements Sympl requires
  4. For a previously working integration that stopped: check the merchant portal for any compliance hold, account flag, or update required notice
  5. If Shopify shows Sympl as an active alternative payment provider but it does not display at checkout, try reinstalling the Sympl Shopify app and re-enabling it in payments settings — this can clear stale configuration states
Sympl merchant support: contact@sympl.ai and Egyptian hotline 15987. Merchant onboarding issues typically require email to ensure you have a paper trail and can reference case numbers for follow-up.

Frequently Asked

Common Questions About Sympl on Shopify

Can I use Sympl, valU, and Shahry together on the same Shopify store?

Yes — Shopify allows multiple alternative payment providers to be active simultaneously. Egypt merchants can offer all three (Sympl, valU, and Shahry) alongside Tabby for maximum conversion. Each serves a different customer segment: Sympl for customers with funds available who want zero-credit installments; valU for customers who want long-term credit financing; Shahry for underbanked customers with thin credit history. A customer declined by one provider may approve on another. Displaying all relevant options increases Egypt checkout conversion significantly.

Why did Sympl stop working after it was working before?

A previously working Sympl integration can stop functioning for several reasons: the merchant's Sympl account was placed on a compliance hold; Sympl pushed an API or app update requiring reinstallation; the Shopify payments alternative provider toggle was accidentally disabled; or the merchant's Shopify store URL or settings changed in a way that invalidated Sympl's integration. Check the Sympl merchant portal first for account status. If Active, try reinstalling the Sympl Shopify app and re-enabling it as an alternative payment provider. Contact Sympl at contact@sympl.ai or 15987 if the issue persists after reinstallation.

Is Sympl expanding outside Egypt in 2026?

Sympl reported strong profitability in 2024 and announced plans for African expansion. As of mid-2026, Sympl remains Egypt-only for its Shopify integration. Merchants who need BNPL coverage for non-Egyptian markets (UAE, KSA, Kuwait, Qatar, Bahrain) should use Tabby (covers all GCC markets plus Egypt), Tamara (KSA, UAE, Kuwait, Bahrain), Postpay (UAE, KSA, Egypt), or Cashew (UAE, KSA, Kuwait). Do not assume Sympl will appear for non-Egypt shipping addresses, even as the company expands — Shopify merchant integrations are country-specific and require separate activation for each market.
