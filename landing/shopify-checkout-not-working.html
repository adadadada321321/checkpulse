Shopify Checkout Not Working? 7 Common Causes & How to Fix Them

By CheckPulse  ·  10 min read  ·  Related: Shopify Checkout Monitoring Guide

Your Shopify store looks fine. Products load. The homepage is fast. But somewhere between "Add to Cart" and "Order Confirmed," customers are dropping off — or worse, they're hitting an error page and leaving for good.

This guide covers the seven most common reasons Shopify checkout stops working, how to diagnose each one quickly, and what to do to fix it. At the end, we cover how to make sure you find out before a customer does.

1. Shopify Payments Suspended or Flagged

CAUSE 01 Shopify Payments account suspended, on hold, or flagged for review

This is the most dangerous checkout failure because it's completely invisible from the outside. Your store looks healthy. Customers reach the payment step. But Shopify Payments refuses to load — returning a 403, an empty payment methods array, or a timeout.

Shopify suspends or flags Payments accounts for several reasons:

  • Sudden spike in chargeback rate (above ~0.5%)
  • Selling in a restricted product category without prior approval
  • Incomplete identity verification (KYC)
  • Suspicious transaction patterns triggering Stripe's fraud models (Shopify Payments runs on Stripe)
  • Account not updated after a business structure change
How to fix

Log into Shopify Admin → Settings → Payments. Look for a yellow or red banner near Shopify Payments. If you see "Account on hold" or "Verification needed," follow the link immediately. Response times from Shopify vary from hours to several business days. While you wait, enable a backup payment gateway (PayPal is the fastest to activate) to keep revenue flowing.

Why you won't see this in your monitoring tool

Uptime monitors ping your homepage URL and check for a 200 status. Shopify Payments suspension doesn't affect your homepage at all — it only breaks the payment step, which most monitoring tools never reach.

2. Payment Gateway Misconfiguration

CAUSE 02 Third-party payment gateway credentials expired or incorrectly configured

If you're using PayPal, Stripe, Braintree, Klarna, or another third-party gateway alongside or instead of Shopify Payments, your API keys, OAuth tokens, and webhook secrets can expire or become invalid — especially after:

  • A gateway-side account password change
  • Rolling API keys during a security audit
  • The gateway migrating to a new API version
  • Shopify renewing its OAuth connection with the gateway
How to fix

Go to Shopify Admin → Settings → Payments → Manage for the affected gateway. Re-enter or refresh your API credentials. For PayPal, disconnect and reconnect the account. For custom gateways, verify your API key hasn't been rotated in the provider's dashboard.

3. 3DS / SCA Redirect Loop

CAUSE 03 3D Secure or Strong Customer Authentication redirect fails or loops

Since PSD2 regulations in Europe and expanded 3DS requirements globally, SCA (Strong Customer Authentication) redirects have become one of the most common silent checkout killers. Customers are redirected to their bank for verification, but the redirect back to Shopify fails — leaving them on a blank page or bounced back to cart.

Common triggers:

  • Shopify store URL changed (subdomain, custom domain) without updating gateway return URLs
  • CDN or proxy stripping 3DS callback parameters
  • Browser blocking third-party cookies required by the 3DS iframe
  • Gateway-side 3DS configuration pointing to old Shopify checkout URLs
How to fix

Test a real purchase on a mobile device in an incognito window (simulates the most common customer environment). If 3DS fires and the redirect fails, log into your payment gateway's developer console and verify the return URL matches your current Shopify checkout domain exactly. Contact the gateway's support with the specific transaction ID — they can trace the redirect handshake.

4. Checkout Session Expiry or Token Errors

CAUSE 04 Checkout tokens expire, return 404, or throw "checkout unavailable"

Shopify generates a unique checkout token for each session. This token expires after a period of inactivity or can become invalid due to:

  • Cart modifications after checkout was initiated
  • Storefront API version mismatches (if you use a custom storefront)
  • A Shopify platform update changing the checkout token format
  • Multi-currency checkout session inconsistencies
How to fix

If you're on a custom storefront or headless setup, check your Storefront API version in the Shopify Partner Dashboard and ensure you're using a non-deprecated version. For standard Shopify themes, a "checkout unavailable" error often resolves itself within minutes — but if it persists, contact Shopify Support and provide your store URL plus the specific error string customers are seeing.

5. Third-Party App Conflict

CAUSE 05 A recently installed or updated app breaks the checkout flow

Shopify apps that inject JavaScript into checkout, modify cart behavior, or add checkout UI extensions are the most common cause of sudden checkout breakage after a previously working store. This includes:

  • Upsell / cross-sell apps modifying the cart before checkout
  • Loyalty and rewards apps adding checkout fields
  • Custom discount apps interfering with Shopify Functions
  • Analytics apps injecting scripts that conflict with checkout's CSP
How to fix

Check your Shopify Admin for apps installed or updated in the 24-48 hours before the issue started. Disable the most recently changed app and test checkout immediately. If checkout recovers, you've found the culprit. Contact the app developer with the specific timing and symptoms — most will respond within hours for critical issues.

6. Inventory or Variant Issue

CAUSE 06 Product inventory tracking, variant availability, or "sold out" logic blocks checkout

This cause is more product-specific than store-wide, but it's easy to miss if it happens to a bestseller. Common scenarios:

  • Inventory tracking enabled and stock accidentally set to 0 for a popular SKU
  • A variant deleted from a product but still appearing in carts from cached sessions
  • Multi-location inventory misconfiguration after adding a new fulfillment center
  • Shopify sync with an external inventory system (ERP, 3PL) sending incorrect stock levels
How to fix

Check the specific products reported by customers. In Shopify Admin → Products, verify inventory levels and that all variants are active and available. If you sync inventory from an external system, check the sync logs for recent errors or mismatches.

7. Custom Checkout Modifications

CAUSE 07 Custom checkout UI extensions, Liquid customizations, or theme edits break after a Shopify update

Shopify periodically updates the checkout and checkout.liquid (for eligible plans). Custom modifications — whether through Checkout UI Extensions, checkout.liquid edits, or scripts injected by apps — can break when Shopify updates the underlying checkout infrastructure.

Highest-risk customizations:

  • Custom fields added to checkout (gift messages, order notes, B2B fields)
  • Custom discount logic via Shopify Scripts or Functions
  • Theme overrides to the checkout thank you page
  • Custom tracking pixels injected into checkout
How to fix

Review recent Shopify changelog entries for any checkout-related updates. Temporarily disable custom checkout scripts and extensions to test if stock checkout works. Re-enable them one by one to isolate the conflict.

How to Diagnose a Checkout Failure Fast

When a customer reports they can't complete checkout, here's the fastest diagnostic sequence:

  1. Open an incognito window and attempt a real purchase with a test product. Don't rely on customer descriptions — reproduce it yourself first.
  2. Check Shopify Admin → Orders for any orders in "pending payment" status from the reported timeframe. This tells you if the issue is before or after payment submission.
  3. Go to Settings → Payments and look for any banners, warnings, or alerts about Shopify Payments status.
  4. Review the store's activity log (Settings → Plan → View activity log) for changes made in the last 48 hours — app installs, theme changes, payment gateway edits.
  5. Open browser developer tools → Network tab during a checkout attempt. Look for red (failed) requests, especially calls to checkout.json, payment_methods, or sessions.
  6. Test on multiple devices and browsers. A failure only on mobile, or only on Safari, points to a browser-specific issue (3DS cookie blocking is common on Safari).

How to Prevent Silent Checkout Failures

Fixing a checkout failure after it happens is the reactive path. The real cost isn't the hour it takes to fix — it's the 4, 8, or 11 hours before anyone knew it was broken.

The fundamental problem: standard uptime monitoring was designed for servers, not for checkout flows. It pings your homepage URL and checks for HTTP 200. Your Shopify store's homepage will return 200 even when your checkout is completely broken. These two things are unrelated in Shopify's architecture.

Checkout-specific monitoring works differently. Instead of pinging a URL, it simulates what a real customer does:

  1. Fetch products from the store
  2. Add an item to cart
  3. Initiate a checkout session
  4. Verify payment methods are available and the checkout URL is reachable

If any of these steps fail — and only these steps will catch a Shopify Payments suspension, a gateway credential expiry, or a 3DS redirect break — you get an alert with the exact step and HTTP status code, not a vague "something went wrong."

Checkout monitoring checklist

  • Monitor runs on an interval (every 30 min or less)
  • Simulates cart creation, not just homepage ping
  • Checks payment methods availability specifically
  • Alerts include exact failure step and HTTP status
  • Sends email to team, not just a dashboard (most failures happen at night)
  • Works without any code changes to the store

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if my Shopify checkout is working right now?
Open an incognito browser window, go to your store, add a product to cart, and proceed through checkout until the payment step. If payment methods load and the checkout URL looks like checkout.shopify.com/..., the flow is healthy. For automated ongoing verification, CheckPulse runs this simulation every 30 minutes and alerts you the moment it fails.
Can Shopify checkout break without the homepage being affected?
Yes — and this is the most common scenario. Shopify's checkout flow runs on separate infrastructure from your store's frontend. A Shopify Payments suspension, gateway credential expiry, or 3DS configuration issue will not affect your homepage, product pages, or any standard uptime monitor. The failure is only visible at the payment step.
Will Shopify alert me if my checkout breaks?
Shopify will send an email if your Shopify Payments account is suspended, but these notifications can be delayed and are sometimes filtered as spam. Shopify does not proactively monitor your checkout flow for other failure types (gateway misconfiguration, 3DS issues, app conflicts). You need to monitor this yourself.
How long do Shopify checkout failures typically last before being caught?
Based on common reports from Shopify merchants, the average silent checkout failure runs for 4–12 hours before being discovered — usually by a customer complaint, not proactive monitoring. At the Shopify industry average of $100/order, a 6-hour failure during business hours costs roughly $600–$2,400 depending on store traffic.
