Shopify Humm Not Working — Not Showing, Payment Declined, Installment Failed

Updated June 2026 · 7 failure modes · AU, NZ, IE, and UK merchants & customers

Humm (formerly Oxipay) is one of the most misunderstood BNPL integrations on Shopify — it runs in four markets under two distinct product lines with four different currencies, and merchants who set it up once often don't realize when it silently breaks. From the Oxipay-to-Humm migration leaving deprecated plugins in place, to Humm Small limits blocking high-AOV stores, to fortnightly direct debit failures weeks after fulfilment — Humm has failure modes that no other BNPL creates. This guide covers all 7 with exact diagnostic steps.

Why Humm Fails Differently From Other BNPL

Humm runs two separate products, not one. Humm Small covers purchases up to approximately AU$2,000 or NZ$2,000, repaid in 5 fortnightly interest-free payments. Humm Big covers purchases up to AU$30,000 or NZ$20,000, repaid over 6 to 60 months — with interest charges on longer terms. These are separately approved merchant products. A store that only has Humm Small enabled will have customers declined at checkout when their cart exceeds the Small limit, even though Humm Big could cover the order. High-AOV stores must apply for and enable both products.

The Oxipay rebrand created a legacy plugin problem. FlexiGroup rebranded Oxipay to Humm in 2020. The Shopify App Store integration changed accordingly, but merchants who installed the old Oxipay app and never updated may be running a deprecated plugin that no longer connects to Humm's live payment infrastructure. The checkout may appear to show Humm but fail silently at the payment processing step.

Repayment method varies by market. In Australia and New Zealand, Humm primarily uses fortnightly direct debit from a bank account — not a card charge. This creates different failure modes: bank account closed, insufficient funds at debit time, bank account details changed since signup. In Ireland and the UK, card-based repayment is more common. The debit/card distinction determines which fix applies when a post-checkout payment fails.

Humm Not Showing at Shopify Checkout (Including Oxipay Legacy Plugin)

Merchant-side

Humm is a third-party alternative payment provider — it is not built into Shopify Payments and can silently disappear from checkout for configuration, currency, or plugin reasons that built-in payment methods never encounter.

The most common and least obvious reason: the merchant is still running the old Oxipay Shopify app. Oxipay rebranded to Humm in 2020, and FlexiGroup migrated the payment infrastructure. Merchants who installed the Oxipay app years ago and never updated may see Humm listed in their Shopify alternative payment providers but find it failing at the processing step — the Oxipay app no longer connects to the live Humm payment API. The fix is to uninstall the Oxipay app and install the current Humm app from the Shopify App Store.

Currency is the second major cause. Humm only operates in four markets with four distinct currencies: AUD for Australia, NZD for New Zealand, EUR for Ireland, and GBP for the United Kingdom. A store selling in USD, or a multi-currency store that presents these currencies as conversions rather than fixed checkout currencies, will not show Humm. Shopify Markets setups must have each market configured with the correct local currency as the mandatory checkout currency.

Other causes: the merchant's Humm account has not completed the onboarding and approval process; sandbox or test API credentials are still active; the Humm app is installed but not enabled in Shopify Admin → Settings → Payments → Alternative payment providers; or the order total is below Humm's minimum eligible purchase amount.

How to Diagnose

  1. Check which Humm app is installed: Shopify Admin → Apps — look for "Humm" (the current app). If you see "Oxipay," uninstall it and install the Humm app from the Shopify App Store instead
  2. Check store currency per market: Shopify Admin → Settings → Markets → each market → confirm AUD (AU), NZD (NZ), EUR (IE), or GBP (UK) is set as the fixed checkout currency — not a conversion from a USD base
  3. Check merchant account status: Log into the Humm merchant portal and confirm your account is approved and live — accounts pending review cannot process transactions
  4. Check API credentials mode: In the Humm app settings, confirm Live API keys are configured — test/sandbox credentials suppress Humm at live checkout
  5. Check Shopify payment settings: Shopify Admin → Settings → Payments → Alternative payment providers — confirm Humm is listed and active
  6. Test with regional address: In checkout, enter an Australian, New Zealand, Irish, or UK shipping address and verify Humm appears for that market's currency
  7. Check order minimum: Confirm the cart total meets Humm's minimum eligible order amount (typically AU$20 / NZ$20; check Humm merchant docs for current thresholds)
  8. Test in incognito: Open a private browser window and complete checkout — rules out browser extension or session cache conflicts
Oxipay migration note: If your Humm integration was set up before 2021, verify you are using the current Humm app — not the deprecated Oxipay plugin. The checkout may appear to work during the payment selection step but fail at processing because the Oxipay API endpoints are no longer active.

Eligibility Assessment Declined

Customer-side

Before approving a Humm payment plan, Humm performs a real-time eligibility assessment. For Humm Small purchases, this is a lighter soft check that does not affect the customer's credit score. For Humm Big purchases — larger amounts over longer repayment terms — the assessment is more thorough and may involve a credit inquiry.

Common reasons for a Humm eligibility decline: the customer is under 18 years old (Humm requires all customers to be 18 or older); the customer's identity details (name, address, date of birth) could not be verified against credit bureau records; the customer has a thin credit file with insufficient history; there are adverse entries on the credit file such as payment defaults or collections; or the customer already has an overdue Humm account balance. Humm does not disclose the specific reason for the decline to customers or merchants.

For Humm Big specifically — if the order exceeds the Humm Small limit and the customer is applying for a Humm Big plan — the eligibility bar is higher. Customers with limited credit history who were approved for Humm Small on smaller purchases may be declined for a Humm Big application on the same visit. The product split between Small and Big means a customer can be declined for the Big plan while technically being eligible for Small — but Small cannot cover the order amount.

Fix Steps for Customers

  1. Verify age: Humm requires customers to be 18 or older — younger customers cannot use any Humm product
  2. Check address accuracy: Ensure the billing address at checkout exactly matches your credit file and bank statement address
  3. Wait before reapplying: If declined, avoid reapplying immediately — wait at least 30 days and build credit history with smaller purchases
  4. Check for existing overdue balance: Log into the Humm app and confirm there is no outstanding overdue amount that is blocking new applications
  5. Use alternative payment: Afterpay, Zip, or a credit card as an alternative while building Humm eligibility
  6. Contact Humm support: Humm customer support can clarify account-level issues — they cannot reverse the risk model's decision but can identify account flags that are blocking approval
Merchant note: Eligibility declines are entirely between the customer and Humm's risk model. You cannot appeal on a customer's behalf. For high-AOV stores, consider offering Afterpay and Zip alongside Humm so customers who cannot qualify for Humm Big have BNPL alternatives.
3

Humm Small vs Humm Big Plan Limit Confusion

Merchant-side

This is Humm's most distinctive failure mode — one that does not exist with any other BNPL. Humm operates two completely separate products that must each be independently approved and enabled for a merchant:

Humm Small: Covers purchases up to approximately AU$2,000 or NZ$2,000. Split into 5 equal fortnightly payments, all interest-free. No credit check beyond the standard eligibility assessment. Most commonly used for everyday retail purchases.

Humm Big: Covers purchases from the Small limit up to AU$30,000 or NZ$20,000. Repaid over 6 to 60 months. Interest-free options exist for shorter terms (typically up to 6 or 12 months, depending on merchant agreement), but longer repayment periods may carry interest charges. Requires a separate merchant agreement and approval from Humm's commercial team. Requires a more thorough customer credit assessment at checkout.

The failure scenario: a Shopify merchant in Australia set up Humm Small when they launched and primarily sold lower-value products. They later expanded into furniture, appliances, or electronics with carts above AU$2,000. Customers see Humm at checkout, select it, and are declined with an order limit error — not because Humm doesn't support the amount, but because Humm Big was never applied for or enabled. The customer sees a confusing decline that appears to be a credit rejection but is actually a merchant configuration gap.

Fix Steps for Merchants

  1. Identify your AOV range: Check your Shopify analytics for average and maximum cart values — if any significant order volume is above AU$2,000 or NZ$2,000, you need Humm Big
  2. Apply for Humm Big: Contact Humm's merchant team directly through the Humm merchant portal or humm.com merchant sign-up page — Humm Big requires a separate commercial agreement
  3. Enable Humm Big once approved: After approval, Humm Big must be separately enabled in your Humm merchant portal settings and may require additional configuration in Shopify's payment settings
  4. Add cart messaging for range clarity: Add checkout messaging indicating Humm is available for purchases up to AU$2,000 (Small) or up to AU$30,000 (Big) — set customer expectations before the decline screen
  5. Test across order value tiers: Test Humm checkout with a cart at AU$500, AU$1,500, AU$2,500, and AU$10,000 to confirm both Small and Big plans activate at the appropriate thresholds
  6. Review interest disclosure for Big: If you offer Humm Big with longer repayment terms, ensure your checkout and product pages correctly disclose that interest may apply — required by consumer credit regulations in AU and NZ

Installment or Direct Debit Payment Failed Post-Checkout

Customer-side

Humm's post-checkout payment failures are unlike card-based BNPL failures because in Australia and New Zealand, Humm primarily collects repayments via fortnightly direct debit from a bank account — not a card charge. This creates a distinct set of failure causes that customers don't anticipate: the order completed at checkout using a card payment, but the ongoing repayments debit from a linked bank account.

Insufficient funds on debit day is the most common cause. Humm processes fortnightly debits on a fixed schedule. If the nominated bank account has insufficient funds on that date, the debit fails. Unlike a card transaction that can be retried against a different card, a direct debit failure requires the customer to either transfer funds into the nominated account before the retry, or update to a different bank account in the Humm app.

Bank account closed or changed is the second major cause. Customers who switch banks, close savings accounts, or change their primary transaction account since signing up with Humm will have all future direct debits fail — the account number no longer exists or belongs to someone else. The customer must update their bank account details in the Humm app before the next scheduled debit.

For markets where Humm uses card repayment (Ireland and UK), the failure modes are more familiar: card expired between checkout and a later installment; card replaced due to loss or theft; bank blocked the recurring charge from Humm; or insufficient credit limit on a credit card being used for repayment. In all cases, the fix requires updating payment details in the Humm app before the overdue amount accumulates and triggers account suspension.

Fix Steps for Customers

  1. Check the Humm app immediately: Failed payments appear prominently on the Humm app home screen with a "Pay now" or "Update details" prompt
  2. For direct debit failures (AU/NZ): verify bank account details: Go to Humm app → Account → Payment Methods → confirm your nominated bank BSB and account number are current — if you changed banks, update to the new account
  3. Ensure sufficient funds before debit day: Humm processes debits in the early morning — ensure funds are in the nominated account the night before the scheduled payment date
  4. For card failures (IE/UK): update your card: Add a new card in Humm → Account → Payment Methods and set it as the default repayment card
  5. Call your bank if debits are being blocked: Some banks block unfamiliar direct debits by default — contact your bank to whitelist Humm's direct debit authority
  6. Pay the overdue amount manually: If a payment failed, Humm allows manual payment of the overdue installment in the app — doing this before the next scheduled retry prevents late fees and account suspension
Merchant note: Post-checkout direct debit and installment failures are between the customer and Humm. As with all BNPL products, Humm pays the merchant upfront at checkout — your revenue is not affected by whether the customer successfully repays their installments. However, customers experiencing payment failures may contact your store first. Direct them to the Humm app or Humm customer support.

Customer Account Suspended — Overdue Balance

Customer-side

When a customer's scheduled Humm repayment fails and all retry attempts are exhausted without resolution, Humm suspends the account. A suspended Humm account cannot be used for new purchases at any Humm-accepting merchant — including your Shopify store — until the total overdue balance is paid in full. This is the most common reason a returning Humm customer who has used Humm before suddenly cannot complete a new checkout.

The suspension lifecycle: Humm attempts the scheduled debit or card charge → payment fails → Humm sends email and SMS notification → Humm retries over several days → all retries fail → Humm issues overdue notices → account is suspended. Customers who miss the initial failure notifications may not realize they are suspended until they next attempt a Humm purchase.

Late payment fees may apply once a repayment is overdue — the total amount shown in the Humm app on suspension will include the original missed payment plus any fees. Customers must pay the full overdue amount (not just one missed installment) to have their account reinstated. For persistent non-payment, Humm may refer the debt to a collections agency, which can result in adverse entries on the customer's credit file in Australia or New Zealand.

Fix Steps for Customers

  1. Open the Humm app: The home screen shows overdue balances and account suspensions with a "Pay now" button
  2. Pay the total overdue balance: Use a different bank account or card than the one that failed — add it in Humm → Account → Payment Methods, then select it for the overdue payment
  3. Check for late fees: The overdue amount in the Humm app includes all fees — pay the total shown, not just the original installment amount
  4. Wait for reinstatement: After payment confirms, Humm typically reinstates accounts within a few hours — if it does not clear within 24 hours, contact Humm customer support with the payment confirmation
  5. Update payment details after reinstatement: Once reinstated, update your primary bank account or card to prevent future failures on the same payment method that caused the suspension
  6. Set up payment reminders: Humm sends fortnightly debit confirmation emails — add each debit date to your calendar and ensure funds are available the night before
Merchant note: Account suspension appears as a generic Humm decline at checkout — you will not see the reason. If a customer says Humm worked previously but is now failing, an overdue balance suspension is the most likely explanation. Direct them to check the Humm app. Their previous purchases are not affected and your store's revenue from prior orders is secure.

Unsupported Country or Currency (AUD / NZD / EUR / GBP)

Merchant-side

Humm will not appear at checkout unless two conditions are simultaneously true: the active checkout currency is one of the four supported currencies (AUD, NZD, EUR, or GBP), and the customer's shipping address is in a supported country (Australia, New Zealand, Ireland, or the United Kingdom). Both must be true — an AUD store with a US shipping address will suppress Humm, and an Australian shipping address with a USD currency will also suppress it.

The four-market, four-currency nature of Humm creates configuration complexity that simpler BNPL products don't have. An Irish merchant who sets up Shopify in EUR and enables Humm will serve Irish customers correctly, but if that merchant later enables Shopify Markets for international customers, Humm must be explicitly scoped to the Ireland market only — not applied globally. Similarly, an Australian merchant adding a New Zealand market must configure and enable Humm separately for NZD currency, as Humm Small and Big may have different limits between the AU and NZ markets.

Multi-currency stores that use currency conversion (presenting USD as the base currency and converting to AUD/NZD on display) present a specific risk: Humm's currency detection operates on the checkout currency, not the displayed currency. If the actual checkout transaction processes in USD and Shopify converts to AUD for display, Humm will not trigger. The store must present the actual regional currency as the checkout currency, not a conversion of it.

Fix for Merchants

  1. Set per-market fixed currencies: Shopify Admin → Markets → Australia → ensure AUD is the fixed checkout currency (not converted from USD); repeat for NZ (NZD), Ireland (EUR), UK (GBP)
  2. Scope Humm per market: In Humm app settings and Shopify payment settings, confirm Humm is enabled per market — not as a global alternative payment method
  3. Test each market end-to-end: Add a cart, enter an AU address with AUD currency → confirm Humm appears; repeat for NZ/NZD, IE/EUR, UK/GBP independently
  4. Audit currency conversion settings: Confirm that checkout currencies for Humm markets are fixed to regional currencies, not runtime conversions from another base currency
  5. Add regional payment messaging: Note on product or checkout pages that Humm is available for AU, NZ, IE, and UK orders — reduces confusion for international customers who see Humm in payment options but are shipping outside supported countries
  6. Verify Shopify Markets currency locking: Shopify Admin → Markets → each Humm market → Currencies → confirm "customers can only pay in [currency]" is enabled rather than allowing multi-currency selection

Refund Timing Issues During Active Repayment Plan

Merchant + Customer

Refunds for Humm orders are more complex than standard card refunds because the customer's repayment plan may be partially active at the time the merchant issues the refund. Humm must cancel or adjust future installments, determine what has already been collected, and calculate whether a cash refund to the customer is needed or whether cancelling remaining scheduled payments is sufficient.

Full refund: When a merchant issues a full refund through Shopify Admin, Humm cancels all remaining scheduled repayments in the customer's plan and refunds any repayments already collected back to the customer's original bank account (for direct debit) or card. Bank account refunds typically take 3–5 business days; card refunds take 5–7 business days. Customers expecting an immediate refund are frequently disappointed — the bank processing timeline is not controllable by Humm or the merchant.

Partial refund: Humm adjusts the remaining repayment schedule proportionally to the partial refund amount. If the partial refund is less than payments already collected, Humm refunds the overpayment difference to the customer. If future installments cover the refund amount without any cash back being required, the customer receives schedule adjustment rather than a cash refund — which can be confusing if they expected money returned to their account.

Webhook failures between Shopify and Humm can cause refund processing delays or failures. If the Shopify refund event webhook does not successfully reach the Humm endpoint, Humm's payment plan is not cancelled or adjusted. The customer may continue to have fortnightly direct debits collected by Humm for an order the merchant already refunded in Shopify. Always verify refund processing in the Humm merchant portal — not just in Shopify Admin.

How to Handle Refund Issues

  1. Always initiate refunds through Shopify Admin: Use Shopify's refund flow — not the Humm merchant portal directly — to ensure the webhook triggers and records stay synchronized
  2. Confirm in Humm merchant portal: After issuing the refund in Shopify, log into the Humm merchant portal and confirm the order shows as refunded and the customer's payment plan is cancelled or adjusted
  3. Explain bank processing time to customers: Direct debit refunds take 3–5 business days; card refunds take 5–7 business days — communicate this timeline proactively to prevent repeat support contacts
  4. For partial refunds, set clear expectations: Explain that Humm's partial refund process adjusts future scheduled payments — the customer may receive no immediate cash back if the refund amount is covered by cancelling remaining installments
  5. Monitor for continued debits after full refund: If a customer reports ongoing Humm direct debits after a full refund was issued, check the Shopify webhook delivery log (Admin → Settings → Notifications → Webhooks) for failed Humm endpoint calls and escalate to Humm merchant support with the order ID
  6. Escalate webhook failures promptly: Systematic refund webhook failures between Shopify and Humm may indicate an endpoint URL change — contact Humm merchant support to verify the current webhook endpoint URL and re-register it in Shopify
Customer tip: If you returned an item and expected a Humm refund, check your bank account (for AU/NZ) or card statement (for IE/UK) within 5–7 business days. If Humm direct debits continue after a full return, contact the merchant to confirm the Shopify refund was processed and then escalate directly to Humm support with your order number and return confirmation.
