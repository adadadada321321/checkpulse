Shopify Scalapay Not Working — Not Showing, Payment Declined, Installment Failed

Updated June 2026 · 7 failure modes · Italy, Germany, France, Spain, Portugal, and other EU merchants & customers

Scalapay splits purchases into 3 equal installments — the first at checkout, the remaining 2 charged automatically at approximately 30-day intervals. That ongoing payment schedule creates failure modes that one-time card transactions never face: missed second or third installments, account suspension from overdue payments, and refund complexity mid-plan. Add strict EUR-only currency requirements across a 10+ country European footprint, and Scalapay has more ways to silently break than many Shopify merchants realize. This guide covers all 7 failure modes with exact fixes.

Why Scalapay Fails Differently From Other BNPL

Scalapay is Europe's pay-in-3 BNPL. Unlike Klarna (which has pay-later, pay-in-3, and financing products) or Afterpay (fortnightly installments), Scalapay's core model is strictly 3 equal installments: one at checkout, one at 30 days, one at 60 days. That simplicity is a feature — but it also means 2 future automatic card charges after the customer walks away with their purchase.

Post-checkout failures are common: a customer who checks out successfully can have their 30-day or 60-day installment declined due to insufficient funds, a card replacement, or a bank block on recurring charges. Scalapay retries and notifies the customer — but if retries exhaust, the account is suspended across all merchants until the overdue balance is cleared.

EUR and EU geography are absolute requirements: Scalapay operates in Italy, Germany, France, Spain, Portugal, Austria, Switzerland, Finland, Belgium, and the Netherlands. The store must present EUR as the checkout currency and the customer's shipping address must be in a supported country — both conditions must be true simultaneously. Multi-currency stores using Shopify Markets must configure Scalapay per market, not globally.

1

Scalapay Not Showing at Shopify Checkout

Merchant-side

Scalapay is a third-party payment app — it is not built into Shopify Payments. Like any third-party payment method, it can silently disappear from checkout for configuration or eligibility reasons that built-in methods never experience.

The most common reason Scalapay vanishes: the store currency is not EUR. Scalapay is a eurozone BNPL and will not appear at checkout for any other currency. Shopify stores selling in USD, GBP, CHF, or any non-EUR currency cannot offer Scalapay to any customer regardless of location. Multi-currency stores using Shopify Markets must ensure each supported EU market storefront has EUR set as the mandatory checkout currency — not a currency conversion from a base currency.

Other common causes: the Scalapay app was not installed from the Shopify App Store, or was removed during a theme, platform, or headless migration; the Scalapay app is installed but the merchant's Scalapay account has not completed approval (Scalapay requires a business verification process before going live); the app is still using sandbox or test credentials which suppress Scalapay at live checkout; the order amount falls below Scalapay's minimum eligible purchase threshold (typically €5–€10 depending on market); or Scalapay is not enabled in Shopify's alternative payment providers settings after installation.

How to Diagnose

  1. Check store currency: Shopify Admin → Settings → Store details → Currency — must be EUR for Scalapay to display; USD, GBP, and all other currencies suppress Scalapay
  2. Check app installation: Shopify Admin → Apps → confirm Scalapay is installed and active, not paused or removed after a theme or platform migration
  3. Check merchant account status: Log into the Scalapay merchant dashboard and confirm your account is approved and live — pending or under-review accounts cannot process live orders
  4. Check API credentials mode: In the Scalapay app settings, confirm Live credentials are configured — test or sandbox credentials prevent Scalapay from appearing to real customers
  5. Check payment settings: Shopify Admin → Settings → Payments → Alternative payment providers — confirm Scalapay is listed and enabled for the active market
  6. Test with EU address: In checkout, enter an Italian, German, French, Spanish, or Portuguese shipping address and confirm Scalapay appears — rules out country filtering
  7. Check order minimum: Ensure the cart total meets Scalapay's minimum eligible amount for the region (typically €5–€10 — verify in Scalapay's merchant documentation for current thresholds)
  8. Test in incognito: Open a private browser window and complete checkout — rules out browser extension or session cache conflicts
2

Eligibility Check Declined at First Use

Customer-side

Before approving a customer's first Scalapay plan, Scalapay performs a real-time eligibility assessment — a background check that evaluates creditworthiness, account standing, and identity verification against Scalapay's risk model. If the assessment returns a decline, the customer cannot use Scalapay for that transaction and sees a decline message at checkout.

Common reasons for a first-use eligibility decline: the customer is under 18 years old (Scalapay requires all customers to be at least 18); a thin credit file with insufficient credit history for the risk model to assess; previous defaults or delinquent payments visible in Scalapay's risk data; the customer's shipping or billing address could not be verified; or the customer already has existing Scalapay plans with outstanding or overdue installments on their account.

Returning Scalapay customers with a good payment history rarely face eligibility declines — the assessment is most stringent for first-time Scalapay users. Customers who are declined should wait at least 30 days before attempting again, as repeated failed applications within a short window can create an unfavorable signal. During this time, alternative payment methods — credit card, PayPal, or Klarna — should be prominently offered at checkout.

Fix Steps for Customers

  1. Verify age requirement: Scalapay requires customers to be 18 or older — customers under 18 cannot use Scalapay
  2. Check billing address accuracy: Ensure the billing address entered at checkout exactly matches your bank or credit card statement address — mismatches can trigger identity verification failures
  3. Check for existing overdue plans: Log into the Scalapay app and verify you have no overdue installments from previous purchases — existing overdue plans block new approvals
  4. Wait 30 days before retrying: Avoid reapplying immediately after a decline — wait at least 30 days to avoid signaling repeated failed applications to the risk model
  5. Use an alternative payment method: Credit card, PayPal, or Klarna as a checkout alternative while resolving any underlying issues
  6. Contact Scalapay customer support: Scalapay's support can provide limited information about account-level blocks — they cannot reverse the risk model's decision but can clarify if there is a specific account issue
Merchant note: Eligibility declines are entirely between the customer and Scalapay's risk model. You cannot override or appeal a decline on a customer's behalf. Ensure your checkout offers multiple BNPL and payment options so customers who cannot use Scalapay have alternatives rather than abandoning the cart.
3

Second or Third Installment Payment Failed

Customer-side

Scalapay's most distinctive failure mode — and the one most likely to generate customer service contacts — is a failed installment payment that occurs after the customer has already received the goods. The checkout payment (installment 1 of 3) succeeds, the order is fulfilled, and then the 30-day or 60-day charge fails.

The most common cause: insufficient funds on the scheduled payment date. Scalapay charges each installment automatically on the same card used at checkout. If the card balance is insufficient on the scheduled day, the charge fails. Scalapay immediately notifies the customer by email and retries the charge over the following days.

A frequent second cause is card replacement. Customers whose card was lost, stolen, renewed, or cancelled between checkout and a later installment will have that payment fail because the card number changed. Customers must update their payment card in the Scalapay app before the retry window closes — many do not realize their Scalapay plan is still linked to the old card number until a payment fails.

Bank-side blocks are another common cause: some European banks, particularly in Italy and Germany, treat Scalapay's recurring charges as unusual recurring transactions and decline them after the first authorization. The customer may need to call their bank and explicitly authorize Scalapay as a recurring payment source, or switch to a different card in the Scalapay app.

Fix Steps for Customers

  1. Check the Scalapay app immediately: Open the Scalapay app — failed payments appear on the home screen with a prompt to update your payment method or pay the overdue amount
  2. Update card if replaced: If your card was renewed, lost, or cancelled since checkout, add the new card in Scalapay → Account → Payment Methods before the next retry attempt
  3. Ensure sufficient funds on the payment date: Scalapay charges installments on the scheduled date — check your available balance the day before and ensure it covers the installment amount
  4. Contact your bank: If Scalapay charges are being blocked, call your bank's customer service and authorize Scalapay as a trusted recurring payment source — specifically ask them to whitelist Scalapay transactions
  5. Switch to an alternative card: Add a different debit or credit card in the Scalapay app and set it as the payment method for the current plan to avoid further failures
  6. Pay the overdue installment manually: If a retry has already failed, the Scalapay app allows manual payment of the overdue amount — doing this promptly prevents account suspension
Merchant note: Once an order is fulfilled and the first installment cleared, payment failures on later installments are between the customer and Scalapay. Scalapay pays the merchant upfront at the time of order and absorbs the collection risk — your revenue is not affected by whether the customer pays their subsequent installments. However, customers may contact you about Scalapay payment failures, not realizing the issue is post-checkout. Direct them to the Scalapay app or Scalapay customer support.
4

Customer Account Suspended — Overdue Installments

Customer-side

When a customer's installment payment fails and all retry attempts are exhausted, Scalapay suspends the customer's account. A suspended account cannot be used for new Scalapay purchases at any merchant — including Shopify stores, any brand's direct site, or Scalapay's in-app marketplace — until the overdue balance is paid in full.

The suspension lifecycle: Scalapay charges the scheduled installment → charge fails → Scalapay retries over several days → all retries fail → Scalapay sends overdue notices by email and push notification → account is suspended. Customers receive multiple notifications before suspension, but if they miss these (especially if the email address on their Scalapay account is outdated), the suspension comes as a surprise at their next checkout.

To clear a suspension, the customer must open the Scalapay app, pay the total overdue balance using a different payment method than the one that failed — typically adding a new debit or credit card and paying immediately. After payment processes, Scalapay typically reinstates the account within a few hours. For customers with persistent non-payment, Scalapay may report the debt to credit bureaus in applicable markets (particularly Italy), which can affect the customer's credit file.

Fix Steps for Customers

  1. Open the Scalapay app: Check the dashboard — overdue balances and account suspensions are prominently displayed with a "Pay now" call to action
  2. Add a different payment card: Go to Scalapay → Account → Payment Methods and add a card that is different from the one that failed — then pay the outstanding overdue balance
  3. Pay the full overdue amount: Scalapay requires the entire overdue balance to be settled before reinstating the account — partial payments do not lift the suspension
  4. Wait for reinstatement: After payment confirms, Scalapay typically reinstates account access within a few hours — if the account is not reinstated within 24 hours, contact Scalapay customer support directly
  5. Update primary payment card: After clearing the overdue amount, update your default payment card in the Scalapay app to a card with sufficient credit limit to prevent future installment failures
  6. Enable Scalapay email notifications: Ensure the email address on your Scalapay account is current — overdue notices are sent by email and missing them leads to unexpected suspensions
Merchant note: A suspended Scalapay account appears as a generic payment decline at checkout — you will not see the specific reason. If a customer says Scalapay worked before but is now declining at checkout despite correct currency and country, an overdue installment suspension is the most likely cause. Advise them to check the Scalapay app. You cannot clear account suspensions on behalf of customers, and your revenue from previous fulfilled orders is not affected.
5

Order Below Minimum or Above Customer Limit

Merchant + Customer

Scalapay enforces both a minimum order amount and a per-customer maximum. Orders outside these bounds either will not show Scalapay as a payment option at all, or will fail at the eligibility step when the customer selects it.

Minimum order: Scalapay has a minimum eligible purchase amount that varies slightly by market — typically €5–€10. Carts below this threshold will not show Scalapay at checkout at all, or may show it greyed out as unavailable. This silently excludes low-value transactions and can confuse merchants who see Scalapay working for some orders but not others with no obvious pattern.

Per-customer maximum: Each Scalapay customer has a maximum order value set by Scalapay's risk assessment. New customers receive a conservative initial limit — often €200–€500 depending on their credit profile — which increases over time as they build a positive installment payment history. The per-customer maximum is not displayed anywhere before checkout, so customers only discover they have exceeded it when Scalapay declines the order at the eligibility step. This creates a poor experience for new customers placing a high-value first order: they select Scalapay, proceed through the Scalapay flow, and receive a decline without explanation.

Unlike credit card limits, there is no way to request a temporary Scalapay limit increase for a specific purchase. The limit is updated periodically by Scalapay's algorithm as the customer builds a repayment history.

How to Diagnose and Fix

  1. Check order minimum: Verify the cart value meets Scalapay's minimum for the market — typically €5–€10; check Scalapay's merchant documentation for current per-market thresholds
  2. Add checkout messaging: Use Shopify's checkout customization or an app to add a note that Scalapay is available for orders above the minimum — this prevents customer confusion on low-value purchases
  3. For maximum exceeded: If a customer is declined for exceeding their Scalapay limit, offer alternative payment methods (credit card, PayPal, Klarna financing) and suggest building Scalapay history with smaller purchases first
  4. New customer guidance for high-AOV stores: Add checkout or product page messaging that new Scalapay users may face lower initial limits — this sets expectations before the decline screen appears
  5. Guide customers to build history: Customers who want higher Scalapay limits should complete smaller Scalapay purchases and pay all installments on time — limits increase automatically as the repayment track record grows
6

Unsupported Country or Currency

Merchant-side

Scalapay will not display at checkout unless two conditions are simultaneously met: the active checkout currency is EUR, and the customer's shipping address is in a Scalapay-supported country (Italy, Germany, France, Spain, Portugal, Austria, Switzerland, Finland, Belgium, or the Netherlands). Both conditions must be true — an EUR store with a US shipping address will not show Scalapay, and an Italian shipping address with a non-EUR currency will also suppress it.

This creates issues for international Shopify stores. A store that sells globally and uses Shopify Payments' automatic currency conversion presents EUR to customers in the eurozone — but if currency detection misfires (a VPN user appearing as EU-based while shipping elsewhere, or a customer who manually overrides the currency selector), Scalapay will silently disappear. The inverse also happens: a customer with a legitimate Italian shipping address but a CHF payment preference will not see Scalapay even though Switzerland is a supported market.

For Shopify Markets setups, Scalapay must be configured and enabled per market — not as a global alternative payment method. Enabling Scalapay globally on a multi-currency store causes inconsistent behavior: working correctly for EUR-market customers but failing silently for all other markets. Additionally, some Shopify themes display all payment method logos on product pages as a static preview — this can show Scalapay to non-EU visitors who then arrive at checkout expecting it to be available.

Fix for Merchants

  1. Set EUR markets to fixed EUR currency: Shopify Admin → Markets → select each supported EU country market → ensure EUR is set as the mandatory checkout currency, not a conversion from a base currency like USD
  2. Enable Scalapay per market: In Scalapay app settings and Shopify payment settings, confirm Scalapay is enabled specifically for each supported EU market — Italy, Germany, France, Spain, Portugal, Austria, Switzerland, Finland, Belgium, Netherlands
  3. Test end-to-end per country: Add a product to cart, enter an Italian shipping address with EUR currency → confirm Scalapay appears; repeat for German, French, and Spanish addresses
  4. Handle Switzerland carefully: Switzerland uses CHF, not EUR — if you sell in Switzerland, Scalapay requires a Switzerland-specific market configured with CHF or EUR depending on how your store is set up; verify with Scalapay merchant support for Switzerland-specific configuration
  5. Add geographic messaging: State on product pages or at checkout that Scalapay is available for EU orders in supported countries — this prevents non-EU customers from selecting Scalapay and receiving a confusing decline
  6. Audit theme payment badges: If your theme shows payment method logos on product pages, verify the badge display logic respects geolocation so Scalapay only appears for eligible visitors
Switzerland note: Switzerland is in Scalapay's supported market list but uses CHF rather than EUR. Scalapay's Switzerland availability may require specific CHF configuration in your Shopify Markets setup. Verify directly with Scalapay merchant support for the current Switzerland configuration requirements.

Refund Timing Issues During Active Installment Plan

Merchant + Customer

Refunds for Scalapay orders are more complex than single-payment card refunds because the customer's installment plan may still be active at the time the merchant issues the refund. Scalapay must cancel or adjust future installments, calculate what has already been collected, and determine whether a refund payment needs to be issued to the customer or whether cancelling the remaining installments is sufficient.

Full refund: When a merchant issues a full refund in Shopify Admin, Scalapay cancels all remaining installments in the customer's plan and refunds any installments already collected back to the customer's original card. If only the first installment was collected (the checkout payment), only that amount is refunded; if two installments were collected, both are refunded. The refund timeline is typically 3–7 business days. Customers expecting an immediate refund are often frustrated — the bank processing time is not controllable by the merchant or Scalapay.

Partial refund: Partial refunds are more complex. Scalapay adjusts the remaining installment schedule proportionally — reducing future installment amounts or cancelling remaining installments if the refund amount covers them. If the partial refund amount exceeds what has already been collected, the customer receives a card refund for the difference. Customers often don't understand why they receive a smaller-than-expected refund, especially if the partial refund was less than the installments already paid.

Webhook synchronization failures between Shopify and Scalapay can cause refund delays or silent failures. If the Shopify refund event webhook does not reach Scalapay's endpoint, the installment plan is not cancelled — the customer continues to be charged for an order the merchant already refunded. Always verify refund processing in the Scalapay merchant dashboard after issuing a Shopify refund.

How to Handle Refund Issues

  1. Always initiate refunds through Shopify Admin: Use Shopify's refund flow — not the Scalapay merchant dashboard directly — to ensure the refund webhook is triggered and records stay synchronized between Shopify and Scalapay
  2. Confirm in the Scalapay merchant dashboard: After issuing the refund in Shopify, log into the Scalapay merchant dashboard and confirm the order shows as refunded and the customer's installment plan is cancelled or adjusted accordingly
  3. Explain the bank timeline to customers: Scalapay refunds take 3–7 business days to appear on the customer's card — this is standard bank processing time, not a delay by the merchant
  4. For partial refunds, set expectations early: Explain to customers that Scalapay's partial refund process adjusts or cancels remaining installments — they may not receive a direct cash refund if the future installments cover the refund amount
  5. Monitor for post-refund installment charges: If a customer reports being charged a Scalapay installment after a refund was issued, check the Scalapay merchant dashboard for webhook delivery failures and escalate to Scalapay merchant support with the order ID and Shopify refund transaction ID
  6. Check webhook delivery logs: Shopify Admin → Settings → Notifications → Webhooks — look for failed delivery attempts to Scalapay's webhook endpoint; systematic failures indicate a webhook configuration issue that needs Scalapay support involvement
Customer tip: If you returned an item and expected a Scalapay refund, check your card statement for a refund from Scalapay within 5–7 business days. If your 30-day or 60-day installment is still being charged after a full return, contact the merchant to confirm the Shopify refund was processed, then escalate to Scalapay customer support directly with your order number and proof of return.

Run through this before contacting Scalapay merchant support

Scalapay app installed and active: Shopify Admin → Apps → confirm Scalapay is installed and shows as active, not paused or removed after a theme migration, Shopify plan upgrade, or headless commerce implementation

Webhook endpoint registered and delivering: Shopify Admin → Settings → Notifications → Webhooks — confirm Scalapay's webhook URL is registered and receiving delivery confirmations without errors; webhook failures block refund synchronization and can cause customers to be charged for refunded orders
