Shopify POS & Online Checkout Sync Troubleshooting

Shopify POS and Online Checkout Are Not the Same System

POS (in-store) and online checkout in Shopify share the same product catalog and inventory, but they run on separate checkout engines with different payment method availability, fulfillment logic, session handling, and error behavior. A customer paying in-store with tap-to-pay cannot use that same method online. An abandoned POS cart can hold inventory that prevents an online buyer from completing purchase. A refund processed at POS and a refund processed at online checkout update inventory differently. This guide covers every way POS and online checkout diverge, and the exact Admin configuration to minimize sync failures.

Updated June 2026 Affects: omnichannel stores, multi-location inventory, POS + online sellers
Shopify POS and online checkout share inventory counts in real time, but they do NOT share payment methods, checkout sessions, or fulfillment workflows. Configuration changes made for online checkout (extensions, discounts, shipping rates) do NOT automatically apply to POS. This is by design — but it silently breaks stores that assume they are the same.
Architecture

Four Divergence Patterns Between Shopify POS and Online Checkout

Shopify POS and online checkout share a single inventory layer but diverge in four distinct ways. Each pattern causes different failure modes and requires different Admin configuration to manage. Understanding which pattern applies to your store is the first diagnostic step.

Pattern 1 — Payment Methods
Payment Method Divergence
Tap to Pay, hardware card readers, gift cards from POS, and split tenders (cash + card) are POS-only. Apple Pay, Shop Pay, BNPL (Afterpay, Klarna), and Buy Now Pay Later are online-only. Neither set appears in the other channel.
Pattern 2 — Inventory
Inventory Hold Divergence
POS creates a session-level inventory hold when a customer is actively in checkout. If the POS session is abandoned (power cut, app crash, staff closes without voiding), the hold can persist for minutes to hours, blocking online buyers from purchasing the same item.
Pattern 3 — Fulfillment
Fulfillment Flow Divergence
POS orders default to "fulfilled at POS location" immediately on payment. Online orders require explicit fulfillment with tracking. In a multi-location setup, POS fulfills from the POS location's inventory — not the location you'd pick for shipping — causing inventory count mismatches.
Pattern 4 — Discounts
Discount and Extension Divergence
Checkout extensions (custom scripts, checkout UI extensions, automatic discounts with checkout conditions) do NOT run on POS checkout. A discount that requires a checkout condition may trigger online but not in store. Shopify Scripts and Checkout Extensibility are online-only.

Deep Dive

Three POS Sync Failure Patterns in Detail

Deep Dive A

POS Session Holds and Online Inventory Blocking

When a customer's POS checkout is started (items scanned, payment screen open), Shopify places a temporary inventory hold on those line items. This hold prevents overselling — another POS terminal at the same location can't sell the last unit. However, the same hold also blocks the online checkout from allowing purchase of the same item.

If a POS session is abandoned — the app crashes, the terminal loses connection, or a staff member closes the cart without voiding it — the hold can persist. The hold timeout varies: Shopify's documentation states it releases "automatically" but does not specify a consistent timer. In practice, holds from abandoned POS sessions have been observed to persist from 10 minutes to over an hour depending on store configuration and Shopify's server-side session expiry.

To release a hold manually: open Shopify POS app → tap the menu → Orders → look for "Abandoned checkouts" or "Open carts" — void or complete any open sessions. If you can't access the POS terminal, you can sometimes find and cancel the hold from Shopify Admin → Orders → Drafts (POS drafts show here as draft orders during an active session).

POS hold release path: Shopify POS app → Menu → Orders → Abandoned Checkouts / Open Carts → Void If POS unavailable: Admin → Orders → Drafts → find open POS draft → Cancel draft Hold timeout (automatic): 10 min typical; up to ~60 min in some configurations
Deep Dive B

Why Tap to Pay at POS Doesn't Work for Phone or Online Orders

Tap to Pay on iPhone (and Android NFC equivalents through Shopify POS) is a hardware-layer feature — it uses the physical device's NFC reader at the point of sale. This payment method literally cannot exist in an online checkout flow because there is no physical card to tap. However, stores regularly encounter this as a confusion point when: (1) staff try to create a draft order for a phone order and want to charge via the POS tap-to-pay flow — this doesn't work; the draft order invoice goes to the customer's email for online payment, (2) merchants migrating from in-person-only to omnichannel assume tap-to-pay is "just another payment method" and expect it to appear online, (3) integration tools (e.g., buy-online-pick-up-in-store flows) that send customers to a "checkout" page confuse the physical POS checkout with the online checkout.

For phone orders: use Admin → Orders → Create Order → send invoice. The customer pays via the online checkout link in the email, using Apple Pay, card, Shop Pay, or any enabled online payment method. For BOPIS (buy online, pick up in store): the online checkout handles payment; POS handles the pickup fulfillment — they never share the payment step.

Phone order workflow (POS payment methods NOT available): Admin → Orders → Create order → Add products → Send invoice Customer pays via email link → online checkout (card, Apple Pay, Shop Pay) BOPIS workflow: Customer pays online → POS fulfills pickup → no shared payment step
Deep Dive C

Multi-Location Inventory Sync and POS Fulfillment Routing

If your store has multiple locations (e.g., a warehouse for online orders, and a retail store running POS), Shopify routes POS orders to fulfill from the POS location's inventory. Online orders fulfill based on your shipping and fulfillment priority settings. This creates a common inventory divergence pattern: (1) Online checkout shows "in stock" because the warehouse location has 5 units. (2) Customer visits retail store, staff sells the last unit there via POS. (3) POS correctly deducts from the retail location — but if the retail location isn't configured as a priority for online orders, the online "in stock" count doesn't visually update until the next sync cycle. (4) A second online buyer purchases the same item, expecting warehouse fulfillment — but the warehouse has 4 units, not 0, so fulfillment succeeds from the warehouse.

This is actually correct behavior, but causes merchant confusion when "we sold it at the store" and the online order still ships. Fix: Admin → Settings → Locations → set your fulfillment priority per location. For multi-location stores where retail and warehouse carry the same SKU, use location-specific stock counts and explicitly configure which location fulfills online orders. Third-party inventory management tools (Stocky, Linnworks, ShipHero) provide more granular location-level sync visibility.

Multi-location fulfillment config: Admin → Settings → Locations → set fulfillment priority Per-location inventory: Admin → Products → [Product] → Inventory → view all locations Check: each location has "Track quantity" enabled Third-party sync tools for granular visibility: Stocky (Shopify-native), Linnworks, ShipHero, Skubana

FAQ

Shopify POS vs Online Checkout — Frequently Asked Questions

Why is an item showing as out of stock online even though we have it on the shelf?
This is almost always a POS session hold. When a customer (or staff member) starts a POS checkout — items are scanned, the payment screen is open — Shopify places a temporary hold on those inventory units to prevent overselling. If that POS session is abandoned (app crash, terminal closed without voiding the cart, network drop), the hold can persist. To release it: open Shopify POS → menu → Orders → look for Abandoned Checkouts or Open Carts — void any open sessions. If you can't find it in POS, check Admin → Orders → Drafts — abandoned POS sessions sometimes appear there. If neither shows anything, wait up to 30 minutes for Shopify's server-side session expiry to release the hold automatically.
Can a customer use tap-to-pay at Shopify POS for an online order?
No. Tap to Pay on iPhone and the Shopify POS card reader are hardware-level payment methods that only work at a physical point-of-sale device. There is no way to use tap-to-pay for an online checkout because there is no physical card to present. For phone orders or orders that need to be placed by staff on behalf of a customer, use Admin → Orders → Create order (draft order workflow) and send the customer an invoice. The customer pays through the invoice link using online payment methods (card, Apple Pay, Shop Pay) — these are separate from what POS supports.
Why did a discount work online but not at POS checkout?
Most likely the discount is implemented using Checkout Extensions (Checkout UI Extensions) or Checkout Scripts, neither of which runs on Shopify POS. POS checkout uses a separate engine. Discounts that run as Checkout Extensibility customizations, post-purchase upsell scripts, or checkout condition scripts are online-only. Basic discount codes and basic automatic discounts (set in Admin → Discounts) do work on both POS and online — but any discount that requires a checkout-level condition evaluated by a script or extension will only fire online. Solution: recreate the discount logic using standard Shopify discount rules (percentage, fixed amount, BOGO, minimum purchase threshold) without checkout extensions — these apply to both channels. For POS-specific discounts, use POS App Extensions.
Why does the inventory count differ between what POS shows and what the online store shows?
If your store has multiple locations, POS and online checkout pull inventory from different location pools. POS shows inventory at the active POS location. The online store shows inventory based on which locations are enabled for online selling (Admin → Settings → Locations → enable each location for online sales). If the retail store location isn't enabled for online selling, online buyers see only warehouse inventory — and POS sales at the retail location don't visually reduce the online count until the next sync. Additionally, there's up to a 30-second sync delay for inventory updates between POS and online. For high-velocity items, this can cause apparent stock discrepancies during peak traffic. Fix: Admin → Settings → Locations → ensure all relevant locations are enabled for online selling, and set fulfillment priority clearly.
How do I prevent POS inventory holds from blocking online buyers?
There's no Admin toggle to disable POS inventory holds — they're a core Shopify anti-oversell mechanism. Your best options are: (1) Train staff to always void abandoned POS carts rather than closing the app or terminal — this immediately releases the hold. (2) Keep POS session duration short — don't start a POS checkout until the customer is ready to pay. (3) For high-demand items, consider a small online inventory buffer using a third-party inventory tool (Stocky, Skubana, Linnworks) that reserves a portion of stock for each channel separately. (4) Monitor for abandoned POS sessions daily — Admin → Orders → Drafts will show POS draft orders that were never completed. Regularly canceling these releases any associated holds. (5) If you're on Shopify Plus, checkout extensibility can include inventory reservation logic, but this applies to online checkout only — POS holds are handled at the POS engine layer.
