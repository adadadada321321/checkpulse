Shopify Free Shipping Not Applying at Checkout — Threshold Not Triggering, Automatic Discount Blocked

Shopify has two separate mechanisms for free shipping — a shipping rate and an automatic discount — and they fail in completely different ways. Here's what's actually blocking it.

Five Root Causes — Different Fixes

Most Common

Competing Automatic Discount Wins

Shopify allows only one automatic discount per order on non-Plus plans. If another automatic discount is active, free shipping is silently dropped even if the cart meets the threshold.

Shipping Rate Setup

Free Shipping Rate Missing from Zone

A free shipping rate configured under Shipping & Delivery only shows if the customer's address falls within a zone where that rate exists. International customers often miss it.

Threshold Timing

Checkout Evaluates at Load, Not Live

Automatic discounts are evaluated when checkout loads. Adding items mid-checkout or using a cart permalink may not trigger re-evaluation until the checkout page is refreshed.

Market-Specific Shipping Profile Gap

Shopify Markets uses per-market shipping profiles. Free shipping set in your default profile may not carry over to market overrides covering specific countries.

Product Exclusions

When a free shipping automatic discount is restricted to specific products or collections, the threshold is calculated on eligible items only — not the full cart total.

How Shopify Free Shipping Actually Works: Two Separate Systems

Most merchants don't realize Shopify has two completely independent mechanisms for offering free shipping. They live in different parts of the admin and fail in different ways.

System 1 — Shipping Rate

Settings → Shipping and Delivery → Manage Rates

A "Free shipping" rate is added directly to a shipping zone. It appears as a shipping option at checkout for customers whose address matches that zone. Can have a minimum order value or weight condition. Does not interact with the Discounts system at all.

System 2 — Discount

Discounts → Create discount → Free shipping

A free shipping discount (code or automatic) zeroes out the shipping cost at checkout when eligibility conditions are met. It competes with other automatic discounts. Can be restricted to specific products, collections, or customer groups.

Key distinction: A shipping rate must exist in the shipping zone for the customer's address. A free shipping discount applies on top of whatever shipping rates exist — it zeros the cost but the rate itself still has to be there. If the only shipping rate for a zone is a paid rate and a free shipping discount applies, the discount zeroes it. If there's no shipping rate for the zone at all, neither system shows free shipping.

Which System Are You Using?

Go to Settings → Shipping and delivery. Look at your shipping zones. If you see "Free shipping" listed as a rate option under a zone, you're using System 1. Go to Discounts. If you see an active free shipping discount, you're using System 2. Many stores have both active simultaneously — which can cause unexpected interactions.

Failure Mode 1: Competing Automatic Discount Blocks Free Shipping

This is the most common cause and the hardest to notice — the cart clearly shows "You've unlocked free shipping!" but at checkout, a paid shipping option is charged.

Shopify rule: On all non-Plus plans, only one automatic discount applies per order. If you have two active automatic discounts — for example, "Free shipping over $75" and "10% off summer sale" — only one will apply at checkout. Shopify applies the discount with the higher customer value. Free shipping is calculated against shipping cost, which may be $8–15. A 10% discount on a $100 cart is $10, so the percentage discount may win, and free shipping is silently dropped.

How to Confirm This Is Happening

Check all active automatic discounts

Go to Discounts in your Shopify admin. Filter by "Automatic." Look at the Status column — any discount with "Active" status is competing with your free shipping discount.

Test in incognito with a cart above threshold

Add items that exceed the free shipping threshold to a fresh cart. Proceed to checkout. On the payment page, look at the shipping line. If you see a shipping cost AND the order has another discount applied, that's the conflict.

Pause the competing discount temporarily

In Discounts, find the other automatic discount and click "Deactivate." Re-test checkout. If free shipping now appears, the conflict is confirmed.

Fixes

Option A — Combine into one automatic discount: Create a single automatic discount that gives free shipping over $75 regardless of other conditions. Remove the competing discount. This limits flexibility.

Option B — Convert free shipping to a shipping rate: Under Settings → Shipping and delivery, add a "Free shipping" rate with a minimum order value. Shipping rates don't compete with automatic discounts — the rate appears at checkout independently. The free shipping discount can then be removed.

Option C — Use a discount code instead of automatic: Make the free shipping a code ("FREESHIP") rather than automatic. Shopify allows one automatic discount plus one discount code per order, so the code doesn't compete with your sitewide automatic discount.

Option D — Upgrade to Shopify Plus: Plus merchants can use discount combinations natively via the Discounts API and checkout extensibility, allowing multiple automatic discounts to stack.

Failure Mode 2: Free Shipping Rate Not Showing for Customer's Zone

If you're using System 1 (a free shipping rate under Shipping and Delivery), the rate only appears if the customer's shipping address matches a zone where that rate is configured.

Common Scenario

Domestic Rate, International Customer

Free shipping configured in "United States" zone. Customer ships to Canada. Canada is in a different zone with no free shipping rate. Customer sees only paid rates. Working as configured, but often unintentional.

Also Common

Rate Has Minimum That Isn't Met

Free shipping rate is set to "$0 with minimum order of $75." Cart is $74.99. The rate exists in the zone but doesn't show because the minimum isn't met. The cart bar may say "Add $0.01 for free shipping" but this is often not visible in all themes.

How to Add Free Shipping Rate to All Zones

Open your shipping profiles

Go to Settings → Shipping and delivery → Shipping profiles. Click on "Manage rates" for the profile that covers the products in question. If you have multiple profiles (e.g., one for heavy items), check each one.

Check each zone

For every zone listed (Domestic, International, Rest of world, etc.), expand the rates. Look for "Free shipping." If it's missing from a zone where you want it, click "Add rate" and create it with the appropriate minimum.

Verify carrier-calculated rate interference

If you use carrier-calculated shipping (UPS, FedEx, USPS live rates), confirm the free shipping rate is listed above or alongside the carrier rates. Carrier rates don't block free shipping, but the customer must select "Free shipping" — it won't auto-apply.

Note on "Rest of World" zone: Shopify's "Rest of World" is a catch-all zone. If you want all international customers to have free shipping over a certain amount, you need to add the free shipping rate to the Rest of World zone as well. Many stores add it to Domestic only and are surprised when international customers don't see it.

Failure Mode 3: Threshold Not Evaluated After Cart Changes

Automatic discount eligibility is evaluated when the checkout session is created or the page is loaded — not in real-time as the cart changes. This creates a specific failure pattern.

The Cart Permalink / Buy Now Problem

Cart permalinks (URLs like /cart?items=...&discount=FREESHIP) or Buy Now buttons create a checkout session with a fixed cart snapshot. If the customer modifies the cart after clicking Buy Now — adding or removing items — the discount eligibility isn't automatically re-evaluated against the new cart. The threshold bar in the storefront (usually a liquid snippet) updates in real time because it reads the cart, but the checkout discount evaluation happens at session creation.

Practical impact: A customer who qualifies for free shipping from the cart page but edits their cart after entering checkout (removing an item that brings them under threshold) may still see free shipping applied — because eligibility was checked when checkout loaded. Conversely, a customer who was below threshold when checkout loaded but then received a gift card credit adjustment may not get free shipping even if their net total now qualifies. Eligibility is checked once per checkout session load.

When to Expect Re-Evaluation

Trigger Discount Re-Evaluated? Notes
Customer navigates back to cart and re-enters checkout Yes New checkout session created — discount eligibility re-checked from scratch
Customer refreshes the checkout page Yes (Shopify re-evaluates on page reload) Eligibility re-checked against current cart state

Failure Mode 4: Shopify Markets Shipping Profile Gaps

Shopify Markets allows you to create market-specific shipping overrides. When a market has a custom shipping profile, it replaces — not extends — your default shipping profile for customers in that market.

How Market Shipping Overrides Work

Go to Settings → Markets → select a market → Shipping and delivery. If this market has a custom shipping override (you'll see rates listed here), those rates are what the customer sees — not the rates in your main Shipping and delivery settings. If you added a free shipping rate in your main profile but the market override doesn't include it, customers in that market won't see free shipping.

Failure Mode 5: Product or Collection Exclusions in Discount Setup

When creating a free shipping automatic discount in Shopify, the "Applies to" section lets you restrict which products qualify. This restriction applies to both the discount application and, on some Shopify versions, the threshold calculation.

How Partial Eligibility Works

Suppose you create a free shipping discount that applies only to the "Electronics" collection with a minimum order of $100. A customer has $80 of electronics and $40 of clothing in their cart. The cart total is $120, which seems to meet the threshold. But the eligible subtotal — Electronics only — is $80, which is below the $100 minimum. The discount doesn't apply, even though the full cart total exceeds the threshold.

Confusing behavior: The storefront cart bar typically calculates the threshold based on the full cart total, not the eligible subtotal. So the bar may say "You've unlocked free shipping!" when the customer hasn't actually met the discount's conditions. The cart bar is a theme snippet that reads the cart — it doesn't know about your discount eligibility criteria. The mismatch between the cart bar promise and the checkout reality is a significant source of customer confusion and abandoned carts.

Checking Your Discount Eligibility Setup

Open the discount in Shopify admin

Go to Discounts → click on your free shipping discount. Scroll to the "Applies to" section.

Check "Minimum purchase requirements"

Look at whether the minimum is set to "Minimum purchase amount" and whether it's applied to "All products" or "Specific collections/products." If it says specific collections, the threshold applies only to those items.

Match your cart bar logic to the actual conditions

If you must restrict free shipping to specific collections, update your cart bar theme snippet to calculate the threshold only using cart items in those collections (requires liquid or JavaScript filtering on cart.items filtered by product type, vendor, or collection handle).

8-Step Free Shipping Diagnostic

Run through this checklist in order. Most issues are resolved by step 4.

Identify which system you're using

Check Settings → Shipping and delivery for a free shipping rate AND Discounts for a free shipping discount. Note which are active.

Count active automatic discounts

Go to Discounts → filter by Automatic → Status: Active. If you have more than one active automatic discount, the conflict rule applies. Identify which two are competing.

Check the customer's shipping address zone

Where is the customer shipping to? Is that country/region in a zone that has either a free shipping rate or has shipping rates that a free shipping discount can zero? If the zone has no rates at all, nothing applies.

Verify the threshold is actually met at checkout — not just in the cart

The cart total and checkout subtotal can differ if: a discount code was already applied before checkout (reducing the subtotal below threshold), or if taxes are excluded from the threshold calculation in your discount settings.

Test in incognito, fresh cart, no browser extensions

Browser extensions (ad blockers, currency converters, coupon finders like Honey) can interfere with checkout discount application. Open a private window with extensions disabled, build the cart, and test the full checkout flow.

Check if a third-party app is managing shipping

Apps like ShipStation, Shipify, or carrier-calculated shipping apps sometimes override Shopify's native shipping rate logic. Check your installed apps and their permissions in the Shopify Partners API — look for apps with "write_shipping" scope.

Verify Shopify Markets override

If the customer is in a country covered by a Shopify Markets market with a custom shipping override, check that market's shipping settings specifically.

Review discount eligibility restrictions

Open the free shipping discount. Check "Applies to" (specific products vs all), "Minimum purchase requirements" scope, and "Customer eligibility" (all customers, specific segments, or specific customers).
