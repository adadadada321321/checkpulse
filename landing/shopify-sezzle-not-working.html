Shopify Sezzle Not Working — Not Showing, Declined, Order Not Created, Payment Failed (2026)

Sezzle on Shopify has 8 distinct failure categories: not appearing at checkout, checkout declined, orders lost after approval, biweekly payment failures, frozen accounts, and refund issues — each requires a different fix.

Updated June 2026 8 failure modes Sezzle App · US & Canada Only · Spending Limits · Biweekly Payments · Webhook Failures · Account Freeze · Refunds

Sezzle is a buy-now-pay-later provider that splits purchases into 4 interest-free payments every 2 weeks. Unlike Affirm (which is embedded as Shopify Installments), Sezzle is installed as a third-party app from the Shopify App Store. This means Sezzle has its own integration layer — and its own failure points — separate from Shopify Payments. When merchants or customers say "Sezzle not working on Shopify," they typically mean one of eight distinct problems that each require a completely different approach to diagnose.

Failure Mode 1: Sezzle Not Showing at Shopify Checkout

This is the most common Sezzle issue for merchants. Sezzle is invisible at checkout when any one of several conditions isn't met.

Sezzle app status

Because Sezzle is a Shopify app — not a built-in payment method — it must be actively installed and configured. Go to Shopify Admin → Apps → Sezzle. If Sezzle isn't in your app list, it needs to be installed. If it's installed, check that:

  • Your Sezzle merchant account is approved (approval email from Sezzle required)
  • API keys are entered in the Sezzle app settings (public key and private key from your Sezzle merchant dashboard)
  • The app is set to Live Mode — many merchants forget to switch from Sandbox/Test mode after setup
  • The payment method is enabled in Shopify Admin → Settings → Payments → Alternative payment providers

Country and regional availability

Sezzle is only available to merchants and customers in the United States and Canada. If your store is based elsewhere, Sezzle won't work. If your store is US/Canada-based but international customers try to check out, Sezzle will be hidden for them automatically based on their billing address or IP.

Cart value limits

Sezzle has both minimum and maximum order thresholds. The default minimum is typically $35. If your cart is below the minimum, Sezzle won't appear. Individual merchants can also set custom maximum order limits in the Sezzle merchant portal — orders above that maximum will not show Sezzle as an option.

Theme conflicts

Some Shopify themes, especially heavily customized ones, override the default payment method display in ways that break third-party BNPL widgets. If Sezzle shows up in a fresh/default theme but not your live theme, a theme conflict is likely. Test with a default Shopify theme to confirm, then work with your theme developer to resolve the conflict.

Common merchant mistake

The Sezzle app can show as "installed" in your app list while the payment gateway is deactivated. Always verify Sezzle is enabled under Settings → Payments → Alternative payment providers separately from checking the app is installed.

Failure Mode 2: Sezzle Checkout Declined by Sezzle

Sezzle has its own risk and credit evaluation system that operates independently from your Shopify store. When a customer is declined by Sezzle at checkout, it is a Sezzle decision — not a Shopify payment failure.

Why Sezzle declines customers

Reason Who it affects Customer action
Spending limit too low for order total New Sezzle accounts Build Sezzle history or use Sezzle Up
Existing past-due payment on another Sezzle order Customers with active Sezzle plans Pay overdue balance in Sezzle app
Account frozen due to previous missed payments Customers with history of missed payments Contact Sezzle support to unfreeze
Ineligible country/region International customers Use a different payment method
Sezzle risk system flagged the checkout High-value orders or unusual patterns Contact Sezzle support

New account limits

New Sezzle accounts start with a low spending limit — often $150–$300. A first-time Sezzle customer trying to use Sezzle for a $400 order will almost always be declined. This is by design: Sezzle's limit increases automatically as customers build a positive repayment history. Customers can also enroll in Sezzle Up (Sezzle's credit-building feature) to accelerate limit growth by reporting their payment history to credit bureaus.

What to do as a merchant

You cannot override Sezzle's decline decisions. The best merchant practice is to offer multiple BNPL and payment options so customers declined by Sezzle can complete their purchase with Afterpay, Klarna, a credit card, or PayPal. Consider messaging near the Sezzle option: "Don't qualify for Sezzle? Try another installment option below."

Failure Mode 3: Order Not Created in Shopify After Sezzle Approval

This is the highest-stakes Sezzle failure: the customer completes the Sezzle checkout flow, sees a Sezzle confirmation, but returns to the store and finds no order in their confirmation email or in Shopify admin.

What causes the disconnect

After the customer approves the Sezzle checkout, Sezzle must send a webhook notification to your Shopify store to trigger order creation. If this webhook fails — due to a timeout, a changed webhook URL, a Shopify API rate limit, or an SSL certificate issue on the merchant side — the order is never created in Shopify even though Sezzle recorded the transaction.

Do not ask the customer to retry immediately

If a customer reports completing Sezzle checkout but getting no Shopify order, do not ask them to try again without first confirming with Sezzle whether the original transaction was recorded. A second checkout attempt while the first is still active in Sezzle can create two separate Sezzle installment plans for the same order — which is very difficult to unwind.

How to investigate

  1. Ask the customer for their Sezzle order confirmation number or email
  2. Log in to your Sezzle merchant dashboard and search for the transaction by amount and date
  3. Check Shopify Admin → Settings → Notifications → Webhooks for delivery failures
  4. Contact Sezzle merchant support with the transaction ID to request them to resend the webhook or cancel the transaction
  5. Once confirmed the original Sezzle transaction was voided, ask the customer to retry

Failure Mode 4: Sezzle Biweekly Installment Payment Failed

Sezzle's installment structure is 4 equal payments made every 2 weeks. The first payment (25%) is charged at checkout. The remaining three payments are automatically charged to the customer's linked payment method on the schedule Sezzle set at time of purchase.

Why installment payments fail

  • Insufficient funds — the customer's bank account or debit card ran low before the scheduled debit
  • Card expired — the card linked to the customer's Sezzle account expired between the purchase and the scheduled payment
  • Card replaced by bank — the bank issued a new card number (often after a fraud event) and the Sezzle-linked card is now invalid
  • Bank blocked the charge — some banks block recurring charges from BNPL services, especially on debit cards
  • Account closed — the customer closed the bank account linked to Sezzle

Who resolves this — and how

Failed installment payments are between Sezzle and the customer. As a merchant, you are not affected — Sezzle paid you in full at the time of the original order (minus their merchant fee). The collections risk sits entirely with Sezzle. Sezzle sends the customer payment failure notifications and gives them a grace period to update their payment method or reschedule the installment. Customers can reschedule up to once per installment via the Sezzle app.

Sezzle's reschedule feature

Sezzle allows customers to reschedule a failing payment by up to 2 weeks — once per installment. This prevents account freeze for customers experiencing a temporary cash shortage. Customers manage this in the Sezzle app under "My Orders → Reschedule Payment."

Failure Mode 5: Sezzle Account Frozen or Past Due

When a customer has multiple missed installment payments across their Sezzle orders, Sezzle freezes their account. A frozen account cannot be used for new purchases on any Shopify store or elsewhere.

Signs of a frozen Sezzle account

  • Customer says "Sezzle keeps declining me on every store, not just yours"
  • Sezzle's error message at checkout references a past-due balance or account issue
  • Customer reports receiving Sezzle collection emails

Resolution path

Only Sezzle can unfreeze an account. The customer must: (1) pay all overdue balances in the Sezzle app; (2) contact Sezzle support to request account reinstatement; (3) wait for Sezzle's review. Merchants have no visibility into or control over individual customer account status with Sezzle.

Impact on merchants

Customers with frozen Sezzle accounts are loyal customers you want to keep. When Sezzle declines them, offer alternatives: "Sezzle isn't available right now — try Afterpay, Klarna, or pay with a credit card." Do not phrase it as a store issue.

Failure Mode 6: Sezzle Popup or Checkout Window Blocked

Sezzle's checkout flow opens as a popup or overlay window in the customer's browser. If the browser blocks this popup, the customer can't complete the Sezzle checkout at all — they click "Pay with Sezzle" and nothing happens.

Why the popup gets blocked

  • Browser popup blocker enabled (common in Chrome and Safari when set to "Block all popups")
  • Ad blocker or privacy extension blocking the Sezzle domain
  • Strict browser privacy settings (common in Firefox Focus or hardened setups)
  • Third-party cookie restrictions preventing Sezzle's session from loading

Customer fix

Tell customers to: allow popups for your store's domain in their browser settings; temporarily disable their ad blocker or whitelist your store; try a different browser (Chrome tends to be the most permissive with BNPL checkout flows). The fix is always on the customer's browser — you cannot change Sezzle's architecture to avoid popup usage.

Failure Mode 7: Sezzle Refund Not Processing Correctly

Sezzle refunds work differently from card refunds because the customer has only paid 25% of the order at the time of checkout — the remaining installments are owed but not yet collected.

How Sezzle refunds work

  • Full refund before all payments collected: Sezzle cancels all remaining installments and refunds any payments already made to the customer's original payment method. Processing time: 3–10 business days.
  • Full refund after all 4 payments collected: Sezzle refunds the full amount to the customer's original payment method. The merchant is debited for the refund amount minus Sezzle's merchant fee (which is typically non-refundable).
  • Partial refunds: Supported. Sezzle adjusts the remaining installments proportionally. Always initiate through Shopify Admin, not the Sezzle merchant portal, to keep the two systems in sync.

Common refund problems

Problem Cause Fix
Refund initiated in Shopify but no Sezzle refund issued Webhook to Sezzle failed or credentials expired Check Sezzle app API credentials; contact Sezzle support with order ID
Customer says refund not received after 10 days Bank processing delay or original card closed Confirm Sezzle issued the refund; customer contacts their bank

Failure Mode 8: Sezzle Merchant Account Not Approved or API Misconfigured

Because Sezzle requires merchant approval (unlike some BNPL providers that activate automatically), some stores have the Sezzle app installed but never completed the merchant account approval process — so it appears configured but never actually works.

Approval and configuration checklist

  1. Confirm you received an approval email from Sezzle after submitting your merchant application — without this, the account is not active
  2. Log in to sezzle.com/merchant and verify your account status shows as Active, not Pending or Suspended
  3. In the Sezzle merchant portal, go to Settings → API Keys and copy the Live Public Key and Live Private Key
  4. In Shopify Admin, open the Sezzle app and paste both keys — confirm the mode is set to "Live" not "Sandbox"
  5. In Shopify Admin → Settings → Payments → Alternative payment providers, confirm Sezzle is activated and not just listed
  6. Do a test purchase (low value) using a real Sezzle account to confirm end-to-end integration works
Sandbox mode is the #1 Sezzle configuration mistake

Sezzle's sandbox mode looks identical to live mode from the merchant's side — Sezzle appears at checkout, the checkout flow opens, but no real transactions go through. Always confirm you switched to Live API keys after completing test setup.

8-Step Sezzle Diagnostic Checklist

  1. Merchant account approved? — log in to sezzle.com/merchant and confirm Active status
  2. Live API keys configured? — not Sandbox keys, not test mode
  3. Sezzle enabled under Shopify Payments settings? — Settings → Payments → Alternative payment providers
  4. Cart value within Sezzle's limits? — above minimum ($35 default), below any merchant-set maximum
  5. Store and customer in US or Canada? — Sezzle is not available elsewhere
  6. Customer's Sezzle account in good standing? — no past-due balance, not frozen
  7. Webhooks delivering to Shopify? — check Settings → Notifications → Webhooks for failures
  8. Theme not blocking Sezzle widget? — test with a default Shopify theme to isolate theme conflicts

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is Sezzle not showing at Shopify checkout?

Sezzle not showing at Shopify checkout is almost always caused by one of four things: (1) the Sezzle app is not installed or is installed but deactivated — Sezzle is a third-party Shopify app, not a native payment method; (2) your store or the customer is in an ineligible country — Sezzle is only available in the US and Canada; (3) Sezzle's minimum or maximum cart thresholds are not met; (4) the app integration is misconfigured — merchant ID not set, test mode still active in production, or a theme conflict hiding the payment widget. Check Shopify Admin → Apps → Sezzle → Configuration to verify the integration is active and correctly configured.

Why did Sezzle decline my customer at Shopify checkout?

Sezzle declines are Sezzle's own risk decisions, not Shopify's. Common reasons: the customer's Sezzle spending limit is lower than the order total (new accounts start with low limits that increase over time); the customer has an existing past-due Sezzle payment on another order; Sezzle's risk system flagged the customer or order; the customer is in an unsupported country; or the customer's Sezzle account is frozen due to missed payments. Merchants cannot override Sezzle's decline decisions.

Why did my Sezzle payment fail weeks after the Shopify order?

Sezzle splits purchases into 4 equal payments every 2 weeks. If a scheduled biweekly payment fails, the card linked to Sezzle was declined (expired, insufficient funds, replaced by the bank, or blocked by the customer's bank). As a merchant, you were paid in full at the time of the original order — the collections issue is between Sezzle and the customer. Sezzle sends the customer a notification and gives them time to reschedule or update their payment method.

The customer completed Sezzle checkout but no Shopify order was created — what happened?

This is a webhook or redirect failure. After the customer approves the Sezzle checkout, Sezzle sends a webhook to create the Shopify order. If this webhook fails, no order is created even though Sezzle recorded the transaction. Do not ask the customer to retry without confirming the original Sezzle transaction is voided first. Contact Sezzle merchant support with the transaction timestamp and customer details to investigate and resolve.

How do refunds work for Sezzle orders on Shopify?

Sezzle refunds initiated from Shopify Admin cancel any remaining installments and refund payments already collected. Processing time is 3–10 business days. Always initiate refunds through Shopify Admin, not the Sezzle portal, to keep both systems synchronized. Sezzle's merchant processing fee is not refunded on returns — this is standard across all BNPL providers.

Can international customers use Sezzle on Shopify?

No. Sezzle is only available to merchants and customers in the United States and Canada. International customers from other regions cannot use Sezzle regardless of store configuration. For stores serving international customers who want BNPL, enable Klarna (available in EU, UK, AU) or Afterpay (available in AU, UK, US, Canada, NZ) as alternatives.
