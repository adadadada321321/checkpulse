Shopify e& Cash (Etisalat) Not Working — Wallet Not Appearing, OTP Not Received & Order Stuck Pending

Updated June 2026 · 7 failure modes · Egypt merchants · e& Egypt (formerly Etisalat) · CBE regulated · EGP only · 011 prefix

e& Cash (formerly Etisalat Cash, operated by e& Egypt) is Egypt's third mobile wallet tied to the 011 carrier network. When e& Cash stops working on Shopify, the failure is one of seven separate problems — narrower gateway support than Vodafone Cash, OTP delivery tied to the e& network, asynchronous callback failures that leave orders pending after payment, or the checkout being configured only for Vodafone Cash 010 numbers and rejecting 011 entries. Identifying which of the seven failures you have is the only way to fix it.

How e& Cash Works — and Why It Fails Differently From Vodafone Cash

e& Cash is a mobile wallet tied to the customer's e& Egypt SIM card (011 prefix), not a card scheme and not interchangeable with Vodafone Cash or Orange Cash. Customers with an e& SIM register their national ID via the e& Cash app, at an e& Egypt store, or via USSD to create a wallet linked to their 011 number. Funds are loaded via e& dealers, ATMs, bank transfers, or the e& Cash app. At checkout, the customer selects e& Cash, enters their 011 mobile number, receives an OTP to confirm the payment, and the purchase amount is deducted from the wallet balance.

Gateway support for e& Cash is narrower than for Vodafone Cash. Vodafone Cash, backed by Egypt's largest mobile carrier, has broader and earlier integration across Egyptian payment gateways. e& Cash is supported by Paymob but may not be available on all Paymob plans, and availability varies across other Egyptian gateways. Merchants who successfully enabled Vodafone Cash cannot assume e& Cash is automatically enabled — it requires a separate check and a separate toggle in the gateway dashboard. This is the single most important difference between e& Cash and Vodafone Cash troubleshooting on Shopify.

e& Cash is an asynchronous mobile wallet payment with the same callback dependency as Vodafone Cash. The customer approves via OTP, the wallet is debited, and the gateway sends a confirmation to Shopify via a webhook callback. If the callback URL is misconfigured, the order stays stuck in pending even though the customer's wallet has been charged. This is the most dangerous failure mode because it results in a debited customer with an unfulfilled order and no automated reconciliation.

e& Cash requires a compatible gateway — Stripe and PayPal do not support it. Merchants must use an Egypt-focused gateway with explicit e& Egypt mobile wallet API integration. Paymob is the primary option; confirm with your gateway whether e& Cash (011) is enabled as a separate method from Vodafone Cash (010) in your account.

7 Failure Modes Covered

  1. e& Cash option not appearing at checkout — gateway does not support or has not enabled 011 wallet
  2. Customer has e& SIM but has not registered for an e& Cash wallet
  3. OTP not received or times out before customer enters it
  4. Insufficient wallet balance — wallet pre-loading required
  5. CBE daily or monthly transaction limit exceeded
  6. Gateway callback not configured — order stuck in pending after customer pays
  7. 011 number rejected — checkout or gateway configured only for Vodafone Cash 010 numbers
1

e& Cash option not appearing at checkout — gateway does not support or has not enabled 011 wallet

Merchant Issue

The most common reason Egyptian e& customers do not see an e& Cash payment option at Shopify checkout is that either the merchant is using a payment gateway that does not support e& Cash, or they are using a compatible gateway but have not enabled e& Cash as a separate payment method — distinct from Vodafone Cash. e& Cash requires explicit API integration with e& Egypt's mobile wallet system, which is a separate integration from Vodafone Cash.

Stripe and PayPal do not support e& Cash. International gateways without Egyptian mobile wallet integrations cannot offer it. For Egyptian-focused gateways, Paymob is the primary provider with e& Cash support — but e& Cash availability on Paymob depends on the merchant's plan and on explicit activation in the gateway dashboard. A merchant who enabled Vodafone Cash on Paymob and saw it working cannot assume e& Cash is also live — the two are independent toggles.

The critical error pattern is: a merchant integrates Paymob for Egyptian customers, enables Vodafone Cash, and considers the mobile wallet setup complete. e& Egypt customers (011 prefix) attempt to pay, see no wallet option, and abandon the checkout. The merchant, checking from a Vodafone Egypt number, sees the checkout working correctly and may not identify that e& Cash is simply not enabled. Only checking from an e& SIM or reviewing gateway settings reveals the gap.

A secondary issue affects merchants who integrated Paymob before e& Cash was added to Paymob's payment method list. Legacy integrations may not include e& Cash in the enabled methods even if the merchant's gateway plan now supports it — the merchant must log in to the dashboard and actively enable it in the payment methods configuration rather than expecting it to appear automatically in an existing integration.

How to fix

  1. Confirm your gateway supports e& Cash separately from Vodafone Cash: Log in to your Paymob (or gateway) dashboard and look for e& Cash as a distinct payment method option, separate from Vodafone Cash. If it does not appear in the available payment methods list, contact your gateway's support to confirm whether your plan includes e& Cash and what activation steps are required.
  2. Enable e& Cash in the gateway dashboard: If e& Cash is available on your plan, navigate to payment methods and toggle e& Cash to enabled. Save the configuration. The payment widget at Shopify checkout should update within minutes to include an e& Cash option alongside Vodafone Cash and card payments.
  3. Test from an e& Egypt SIM: After enabling, verify the checkout from a device with an e& Egypt SIM (011 number) or ask a colleague with an e& SIM to test the checkout. A Vodafone Egypt test only confirms Vodafone Cash is visible — it does not confirm e& Cash is visible. Only a genuine e& Egypt payment attempt shows whether the 011 wallet option appears.
  4. Check whether your Paymob plan tier includes e& Cash: Paymob offers different service tiers. e& Cash API access may not be included on all plans. If you cannot find e& Cash in the available payment methods after checking, ask Paymob support which plan tier enables e& Cash and whether an upgrade is required.
  5. Consider offering Fawry or bank transfer as a fallback for e& customers: While resolving e& Cash gateway setup, ensure e& Egypt customers have a non-wallet payment path. Fawry is widely accessible in Egypt across all carriers, and bank transfer is carrier-agnostic. This prevents checkout abandonment while e& Cash is being enabled.
2

Customer has e& SIM but has not registered for an e& Cash wallet

Customer Issue

Owning an e& Egypt SIM card and using an 011 mobile number does not mean the customer has an e& Cash wallet. e& Cash is a separate financial service requiring explicit registration — the customer must create a wallet account linked to their 011 number before any payment can be made. An e& subscriber who has never registered for e& Cash will receive a "wallet not found" or "account not registered" error when they enter their number at checkout, even if the gateway is correctly configured and the checkout shows e& Cash as an option.

When the gateway sends a wallet lookup for an unregistered 011 number to the e& Egypt payment system, it receives a "wallet not found" response. This is typically displayed to the customer as a generic payment error — "payment could not be completed," "account not recognized," or an error code without explanation. Customers frequently assume the merchant's checkout has a technical problem rather than recognizing that they need to register for a separate wallet service.

e& Cash wallet registration can be completed through: the e& Cash app (available on Android and iOS), at any e& Egypt store or authorized dealer with a valid national ID, or via a USSD code on the e& number. Registration is typically processed immediately, and the wallet is active within minutes of completing the national ID verification step. e& Egypt has a network of stores and dealers across Egypt where registration can be completed in person.

New e& SIM holders and prepaid e& customers are the most affected group. Many e& subscribers use their number for calls and data but have never set up the financial wallet layer. Merchants enabling e& Cash for the first time should anticipate a wave of registration-related failures and prepare customer-facing guidance accordingly.

How to fix

  1. Add wallet registration guidance near the e& Cash option at checkout: Display a brief note: "First time using e& Cash? Register your wallet at any e& Egypt store (bring national ID) or via the e& Cash app before selecting this payment option." This guides unregistered customers to the right action instead of leaving them with a confusing payment error.
  2. Include e& Cash registration guidance in cart abandonment follow-up: If a customer abandons checkout after attempting e& Cash, follow up with an email or SMS explaining that e& Cash requires a one-time wallet registration and that they can register quickly via the e& Cash app or at any e& store before completing their order.
  3. Offer alternative payment methods for unregistered customers: Customers who want to pay with e& Cash but have not registered should be able to complete their purchase immediately using another method. Display Fawry offline payment, Meeza card, or bank transfer as clear alternatives at checkout so an e& Cash registration issue does not result in order abandonment.
  4. Distinguish registration errors from other failures in gateway logs: Your gateway returns specific error codes for "wallet not registered" versus "OTP failed" versus "insufficient balance." Identify which error codes correspond to unregistered accounts in your gateway documentation or support. This lets you route customer service inquiries correctly — a registration error gets registration guidance, not a payment retry.
Merchant note: The registration gap is common when merchants first enable e& Cash. Existing customers who have had e& SIMs for years may never have registered for the wallet. Plan for registration-related support inquiries in the first weeks and prepare a clear one-step guide: "Download the e& Cash app → verify national ID → wallet is active."
3

OTP not received or times out before customer enters it

Merchant + Customer

e& Cash payments require OTP confirmation — after the customer enters their 011 mobile number at checkout, e& Egypt's payment system sends a one-time password to the registered number or via the e& Cash app. The customer must enter this OTP within the payment session window to complete the transaction. If the OTP does not arrive or expires before the customer enters it, the payment fails and the order is not placed.

The most common OTP delivery failure is network delay during high-traffic periods. If the gateway's session timeout is short (60–90 seconds is common for mobile wallet payment sessions), an OTP delayed by network congestion may arrive after the session has already expired. The customer sees a "payment session expired" or "OTP timed out" error and must restart checkout — at which point some customers abandon rather than retry. Extending the session timeout to 180 seconds or more significantly reduces this failure mode.

Other OTP delivery failures include: the customer is out of e& Egypt network coverage (traveling, in a weak signal area, or with an inactive SIM); the phone number entered at checkout does not exactly match the number registered to the e& Cash wallet; the customer's SIM has been replaced and the old number is still associated with the wallet; or the customer has a dual-SIM phone and the e& SIM is not the active SIM at the time of the OTP. In some cases, the OTP arrives in the e& Cash app notification rather than by SMS — customers who are not familiar with the app may miss it.

On the merchant side, the session timeout for e& Cash OTP can typically be configured in the payment gateway's integration settings. Increasing this from the default to 3–5 minutes gives customers time to receive and enter the OTP without the session expiring, particularly for customers in areas with slower SMS delivery.

How to fix

  1. Increase the payment session timeout in gateway settings: Log in to your gateway dashboard and find the session expiry or OTP timeout setting for the e& Cash integration. Increase it from the default (60–90 seconds) to at least 3 minutes (180 seconds). This reduces OTP timeout failures without adding any security risk — incomplete sessions expire normally regardless.
  2. Advise customers to have their phone ready before entering checkout: Add a note to the e& Cash payment option: "You will receive an OTP on your registered e& number — make sure your phone is nearby with e& network coverage before selecting this option." Customers who understand the flow are less likely to be caught off guard by the OTP step.
  3. Remind customers to check the e& Cash app: Some e& Cash OTPs arrive as in-app notifications rather than SMS. If a customer reports not receiving an SMS OTP, advise them to check the e& Cash app on their phone for a payment confirmation notification. The delivery channel depends on how the customer's wallet is configured and the gateway's integration.
  4. Distinguish OTP timeout from OTP not delivered in gateway logs: Your gateway returns different error codes for an expired session versus an OTP that was never sent (unregistered number, network delivery failure). OTP timeout errors indicate you need to increase the session timeout. OTP not delivered errors indicate a customer-side issue that requires guidance on number verification and signal.
  5. Provide a fallback payment option after OTP failure: If the OTP step fails, the customer should be able to switch to another payment method immediately — Fawry, Meeza, or bank transfer — without restarting their entire order. Display alternatives clearly on the payment error screen.
4

Insufficient wallet balance — wallet pre-loading required

Customer Issue

e& Cash is a pre-funded wallet — the customer must load funds into their wallet in advance before it can be used for purchases. If the e& Cash wallet balance is less than the order total, the payment is declined at the e& Egypt system level before the OTP step completes. The customer receives an "insufficient funds" or "payment declined" error that is often mistaken for a technical checkout failure rather than a balance shortfall.

The pre-funded nature of e& Cash is fundamentally different from a debit card or bank transfer, where funds are drawn directly from a bank account. Customers who are accustomed to debit card payments may not understand that their e& Cash wallet is a separate account requiring explicit top-up. A customer with ample bank funds may be confused by an "insufficient funds" error on e& Cash because they assume the wallet draws from their bank. It does not — unless the customer has set up a bank-to-wallet auto-load, the wallet balance is independent of the bank account.

Customers can load their e& Cash wallet through multiple channels: at any e& Egypt store or authorized dealer (cash deposit, immediate credit to wallet); via ATMs of compatible Egyptian banks; through mobile banking apps of banks that support wallet top-up; or through the e& Cash app using a bank transfer or card. Loading is typically processed immediately at e& stores and via banking apps; ATM loading may have a short processing delay.

For merchants, insufficient balance declines appear in the gateway logs as a balance-related decline code. There is no technical action the merchant can take to resolve a balance issue for a specific transaction — the customer must load funds and retry. Merchants can reduce this friction by displaying the required amount clearly before the customer enters their e& number and by providing top-up guidance on the payment failure page.

How to fix

  1. Display the order total prominently before the e& Cash OTP step: Show the order amount and a "this will be charged to your e& Cash wallet" message before the customer enters their number. Customers should confirm they have sufficient balance before initiating the OTP sequence, avoiding wasted payment attempts that fail due to insufficient funds.
  2. Provide wallet top-up guidance on the payment failure page: If e& Cash is declined with an insufficient balance error, show: "To top up your e& Cash wallet: visit any e& Egypt store, use the e& Cash app, or transfer from your bank app. Then return and retry your order." Link to the e& Cash app download if possible.
  3. Offer alternative payment methods for high-value orders: If your average order value is high relative to typical e& Cash wallet balances, ensure bank transfer and Fawry are prominently available at checkout. These options are not subject to the pre-funding requirement and can accommodate any order value.
  4. Add balance-awareness guidance in product pages for high-value items: For items above EGP 1,000–2,000, add a note near the buy button or in the product description: "Paying with e& Cash? Make sure your wallet is topped up before checkout." This sets expectations early and prevents balance failures at the final payment step.
5

CBE daily or monthly transaction limit exceeded

Customer Issue

The Central Bank of Egypt sets regulatory transaction limits for all mobile wallets operating in Egypt, including e& Cash. These limits apply to all e& Cash users regardless of their wallet balance and are enforced at the e& Egypt payment system level. When a customer's daily or monthly transaction limit has been reached, their payment is declined even if their wallet has sufficient funds for the purchase.

The CBE's tiered limit structure for mobile wallets is based on the customer's verification level. Unverified wallets (registered with basic national ID) have lower daily and monthly transaction limits and a lower maximum balance ceiling (EGP 10,000). Fully verified wallets — requiring extended KYC including face verification or in-person bank verification — have significantly higher limits and a higher maximum balance. An e& Cash customer with an unverified wallet attempting a high-value purchase may find the order total exceeds their daily limit even if it does not exceed their wallet balance.

From the merchant's perspective, a CBE limit exceeded error looks similar to a balance error in the gateway response — both are declined at the e& Egypt system level. The distinction matters for customer guidance: a balance error requires topping up the wallet, while a limit error requires either waiting for the limit to reset (daily limits typically reset at midnight) or upgrading the wallet verification tier to increase the limit ceiling.

Merchants selling high-value items — electronics, furniture, bundled software packages, or high-end products priced above the typical CBE mobile wallet daily limit — will encounter this failure frequently among customers with unverified e& Cash wallets. The practical fix is to offer bank transfer as an alternative for high-value orders, since Egyptian bank transfers do not carry CBE mobile wallet tier limits.

How to fix

  1. Advise customers on wallet tier upgrades: Customers who encounter CBE limit errors should be advised to upgrade their e& Cash wallet to the verified tier. Verification can be completed at any e& Egypt store with national ID and may also be available through the e& Cash app. A verified wallet has substantially higher daily and monthly transaction limits.
  2. Display limit guidance for high-value orders: If your store has products priced above EGP 1,500–2,000, add a note near the e& Cash option for high-value carts: "Note: e& Cash transaction limits apply based on your wallet tier. For higher-value orders, you may need a verified e& Cash wallet or choose bank transfer." This sets expectations before the payment attempt fails.
  3. Offer bank transfer as the primary alternative for large orders: Egyptian bank transfers carry no CBE mobile wallet tier limits and can accommodate any order size. For orders where CBE limit errors are frequent, make bank transfer prominently available at checkout with clear payment instructions.
  4. Distinguish limit errors from balance errors in customer service: When a customer contacts you after a declined e& Cash payment, the gateway error code tells you whether it is a balance issue or a limit issue. A balance error requires topping up the wallet. A limit error requires either waiting until the daily limit resets (midnight) or upgrading wallet verification tier. Providing the correct guidance prevents customers from topping up when the issue is a regulatory limit, not a balance shortfall.
Merchant note: CBE mobile wallet limits are updated periodically as Egypt's digital payments regulations evolve. The limits described here are based on 2026 CBE guidelines. Check the CBE's published mobile wallet framework annually — limits may increase as Egypt's financial inclusion targets advance.
6

Gateway callback not configured — order stuck in pending after customer pays

Merchant Issue

A Shopify order stuck in pending status after an e& Cash payment is almost always caused by a missing or broken webhook callback URL. e& Cash is an asynchronous payment method — the payment flow is: (1) customer selects e& Cash and enters their 011 number; (2) e& Egypt sends the customer an OTP; (3) customer approves the payment in the app or via OTP; (4) e& Egypt's system notifies the payment gateway that payment was approved; (5) the gateway notifies Shopify via a webhook callback; (6) Shopify marks the order as paid. If step 5 fails because the callback URL is misconfigured or unreachable, the payment never reaches Shopify, and the order stays in pending indefinitely.

This is the most dangerous e& Cash failure mode because it results in the customer having paid — their wallet is debited and they have a payment confirmation from e& — while the merchant's Shopify system shows no completed payment. Without a process to cross-check pending orders against gateway transaction records, the merchant may never fulfill the order, leaving the customer to dispute the charge.

The most common cause of callback failures is a localhost or development URL left in the production gateway configuration. During development and testing, merchants configure callback URLs pointing to local addresses (localhost, 127.0.0.1, ngrok tunnels). When deploying to production, if the callback URL is not updated, all payment confirmations from e& Egypt are sent to an unreachable address and silently fail. No error is visible to the merchant — only the gateway's logs show failed callback attempts.

A related issue occurs when the Shopify store's domain changes after the initial gateway setup: a custom domain is added, the store migrates to a new URL, or HTTPS is enforced on a store that previously used HTTP. If the callback URL in the gateway dashboard still points to the old domain or HTTP address, callbacks fail silently and all e& Cash payments result in stuck pending orders.

How to fix

  1. Check and update the callback URL in your gateway dashboard immediately: Log in to your Paymob (or gateway) dashboard and find the webhook or callback URL configuration for the e& Cash integration. The callback URL must be the production Shopify payment confirmation endpoint — a publicly accessible HTTPS URL matching your current live store domain. It must not be a localhost, development tunnel, or HTTP address.
  2. Verify the callback URL is reachable from the public internet: Use curl or a webhook testing service to confirm that a POST request to the configured callback URL returns a 200 response. If the URL returns 404, redirect, or timeout, update it and retest. A non-200 response means payment confirmations cannot reach Shopify.
  3. Manually reconcile currently stuck pending orders: Log in to your payment gateway's merchant dashboard and review completed e& Cash transactions for the past 7 days. Match them against pending Shopify orders. For each matched order, manually mark it as paid in Shopify admin (manual payment capture) and fulfill it. Contact your gateway support if a refund or settlement reconciliation is needed.
  4. Set up a monitoring alert for orders stuck in pending: Configure a Shopify alert (via a Shopify app or a scheduled script) to notify you when any order has been in pending status for more than 30 minutes. Pending orders that do not resolve quickly indicate a callback failure and require immediate attention before they become customer disputes.
  5. Test the complete payment and callback flow after configuration: After updating the callback URL, complete a real e& Cash payment for a small amount through your live Shopify checkout. Confirm that: the OTP is received, the payment is approved, the order in Shopify updates from pending to paid within 60 seconds, and the transaction appears as settled in your gateway logs. Only a successful real-money end-to-end test confirms the callback is working correctly.
Critical: Do not ignore Shopify orders stuck in pending after e& Cash payments. These represent real money deducted from the customer's wallet with no fulfillment. Check your gateway transaction logs daily and cross-reference against pending Shopify orders. Every unmatched e& Cash payment is a pending dispute or chargeback risk that requires manual reconciliation.

011 number rejected — checkout or gateway configured only for Vodafone Cash 010 numbers

Merchant Issue

A subtle but common failure occurs when the merchant's checkout or gateway is configured to accept Egyptian mobile wallet payments but only recognizes or accepts Vodafone Cash 010 numbers. When an e& Egypt customer (011 prefix) enters their number, the payment widget rejects it with an "invalid mobile number," "unsupported carrier," or "account not found" error — even if the customer's e& Cash wallet is registered, funded, and fully active.

This happens in two ways. First, the gateway may have Vodafone Cash enabled but e& Cash disabled — the payment method selection correctly shows a Vodafone Cash option, the customer attempts to use it with their 011 number, and the gateway rejects the non-Vodafone number at the API lookup stage. Second, some custom checkout implementations include client-side validation that checks for a 010 prefix and flags 011 entries as invalid before the payment even reaches the gateway. A merchant who built their own checkout mobile wallet input may have hard-coded a 010 prefix check without accounting for other carrier prefixes.

Egyptian carrier prefixes: Vodafone Egypt uses 010, Orange Egypt uses 012, e& Egypt uses 011, and WE (Telecom Egypt) uses 015. A checkout that validates "must start with 010" to mean "must be a valid Egyptian Vodafone number" inadvertently locks out all non-Vodafone mobile wallet users. If you have a single mobile wallet payment input that is labeled "Vodafone Cash" and the customer enters an 011 number, the rejection is expected. The fix is to add a separate e& Cash payment option with its own 011 number input.

The practical resolution requires either: enabling e& Cash as a separate payment method in the gateway dashboard (which creates a distinct checkout input for 011 numbers), or updating client-side validation to accept 011 entries and route them through the e& Cash API rather than the Vodafone Cash API. Both require a gateway that supports e& Cash — if the gateway only supports Vodafone Cash, 011 numbers cannot be accepted for wallet payments regardless of validation changes.

How to fix

  1. Enable e& Cash as a separate payment method in your gateway dashboard: Do not attempt to route e& Cash through the Vodafone Cash integration — they use different carrier APIs. Log in to your gateway dashboard and enable e& Cash as its own payment option. This creates a distinct 011 input at checkout labeled for e& Egypt customers, separate from the Vodafone Cash 010 input.
  2. Remove or update client-side validation that enforces 010-only prefix: If your checkout form has JavaScript validation that only accepts numbers starting with 010 (Vodafone), update it to also accept 011 (e&), 012 (Orange), and 015 (WE) numbers — routing each prefix to its respective wallet API. A carrier-agnostic input with server-side routing is better than multiple labeled inputs if your gateway supports unified handling.
  3. Test the checkout from an 011 number explicitly: After enabling e& Cash and updating any validation, test the checkout by entering an 011 number in the mobile wallet field. Confirm it is accepted (not flagged as invalid), receives an OTP, and completes a payment. A test with a 010 number only confirms Vodafone Cash works — it does not confirm e& Cash works.
  4. Add carrier-specific helper text to mobile wallet inputs: If you offer both Vodafone Cash and e& Cash as separate checkout options, label each clearly: "Vodafone Cash (010 numbers)" and "e& Cash — Etisalat (011 numbers)." This prevents customers from entering an 011 number in the Vodafone Cash field and receiving a confusing rejection.
  5. Confirm your gateway plan covers e& Cash API access: If you cannot enable e& Cash in the dashboard despite following the steps above, contact your gateway provider to confirm whether e& Cash API access is included in your current plan. Some gateways require plan upgrades or separate commercial agreements to include e& Cash in addition to Vodafone Cash.
Merchant note: The 011 prefix rejection is most common among merchants who set up mobile wallet payments specifically for Vodafone Cash and never considered the other Egyptian carriers. With roughly 13–15% of Egyptian mobile subscriptions on the e& network (formerly Etisalat Egypt), locking out 011 numbers excludes a meaningful segment of Egyptian customers from mobile wallet checkout.

e& Cash Shopify Integration Diagnostic Checklist

Gateway supports e& Cash separately from Vodafone Cash — confirmed with gateway provider (Paymob) that your plan includes e& Cash API access and it is enabled as a distinct payment method, not bundled into the Vodafone Cash toggle.

e& Cash enabled in gateway dashboard — e& Cash is toggled on as an active payment method in the merchant's gateway settings, separate from Vodafone Cash and card payment activations.

Production callback/webhook URL configured and reachable — the callback URL in the gateway dashboard is the production Shopify store URL (HTTPS, current domain), not a localhost or development address, and returns a 200 response to a test POST request.

OTP session timeout set to at least 3 minutes — payment session expiry for e& Cash OTP is configured to 180 seconds or more to accommodate SMS delivery delays on the e& network.

011 prefix accepted by checkout form — the mobile number input field at checkout does not have client-side validation that restricts entries to 010 (Vodafone) numbers only; 011 entries are accepted and routed to the e& Cash API.

Checkout currency is EGP — store is priced in EGP, or Shopify Markets is configured to present EGP to Egyptian customers. e& Cash processes EGP only.

Wallet registration guidance available to customers — a help note or FAQ near the e& Cash option explains that a separate wallet registration is required (e& Egypt store with national ID, e& Cash app, or USSD) for first-time users.

End-to-end test with a real e& Cash wallet completed — a test payment was made through the live checkout with a real registered e& Cash 011 wallet, OTP was received and entered, and the Shopify order updated from pending to paid within 60 seconds.

Fallback payment methods available — customers who cannot use e& Cash (wallet not registered, OTP failure, insufficient balance, carrier not supported) have clear alternatives: Fawry offline code, Meeza card, or bank transfer.
