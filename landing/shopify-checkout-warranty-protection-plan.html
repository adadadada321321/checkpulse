Shopify Warranty Not Working at Checkout — Protection Plan Not Adding, Extend / Clyde / Asurion Error

Shopify has no native warranty or protection plan system. Every extended warranty, accidental damage plan, and product protection offer on a Shopify store is delivered by a third-party app that injects a custom product variant into the cart. When that variant is stale, mispriced, or conflicted with another app, checkout breaks.

Updated June 2026 Covers Extend · Clyde · Asurion · Mulberry · InsureShield
The Core Insight: A "warranty not applying at checkout" error is almost never a Shopify problem. The warranty app creates a special Protection Plan product variant in your Shopify catalog. If that variant is deactivated, archived, or mispriced — Shopify silently drops it during cart validation. The customer reaches checkout with no warranty line item and no explanation. The investigation always starts in the warranty app dashboard, not in Shopify Admin.
How It Works

The Three-Layer Warranty Architecture: Widget → Cart Variant → Checkout

Every Shopify warranty integration follows the same three-layer architecture. Failures can happen at any layer — and the layer where the failure occurs determines the fix.

Layer 1 — Widget
Product Page / Cart Offer Widget
The warranty app injects an offer widget on the product page, cart drawer, or cart page. The widget fetches pricing from the app's API based on the product price and category. If the widget doesn't load, the app's JavaScript failed to inject (script missing from theme, app not installed, or AdBlocker).
Layer 2 — Cart Variant
Protection Plan Product Variant in Cart
When the customer clicks "Add Protection," the app adds a special Protection Plan SKU to the cart — a custom product variant created in your Shopify catalog by the app. Shopify treats this as a normal line item. If the variant is inactive or mispriced, Shopify's cart validation removes it before checkout renders.
Layer 3 — Checkout
Checkout Processing & Post-Purchase
At checkout, Shopify processes the Protection Plan line item as a standard product purchase. The warranty app's backend receives the order webhook and registers the coverage. If you use post-purchase upsell flows, the warranty may also be offered as a one-click add-on after the initial order — using a different API path entirely.

The Stale Variant Problem — Why Protection Plans Silently Disappear

The most common warranty checkout failure has nothing to do with checkout logic. It's a stale product variant in your Shopify catalog. Here's exactly how it happens.

How the Stale Variant Failure Happens

The Warranty App Creates Real Products in Your Shopify Catalog

When you install Extend, Clyde, Mulberry, or any similar warranty app, the app creates one or more products in your Shopify catalog. These products have names like "Extend Protection Plan," "Clyde Contract," or "Mulberry Coverage." Each product has variants representing different coverage tiers (1-year, 2-year, 3-year) at different price points.

These are real Shopify product variants — they appear in your product list, they have inventory records, and they go through cart validation like any other product. The app manages their prices by syncing them against the prices of the products they protect.

What breaks this sync:

1. Product price change: You update your main product from $299 to $349. The warranty app needs to recalculate and update its Protection Plan variant prices (since warranty cost is usually a % of product price). If the sync job doesn't run immediately, there's a window where the Protection Plan variant price is stale.

2. Variant archival: An admin accidentally archives or deletes the Protection Plan product. Most warranty apps automatically recreate it, but there's a window where the cart add-on throws an error because the variant ID no longer exists.

3. App reinstall: If you uninstall and reinstall the warranty app (even temporarily during troubleshooting), the app creates new Protection Plan product variants with new Shopify variant IDs. Any cached cart or stored "add to cart" button that references the old variant ID will fail silently.

How to Find and Fix It

Check the Protection Plan Product Status in Shopify Admin

Navigate to Shopify Admin → Products. In the search bar, type the name of your warranty app's product: "Extend Protection," "Clyde Contract," "Mulberry," or "InsureShield." If you see the product, click it and check:

Status: Active ✓ (not Draft or Archived) Variants: all variants showing with prices > $0.00 Inventory: not tracked (warranty plans are virtual, no inventory)

If any variant shows $0.00 price, Draft status, or Archived status — this is the cause of the silent cart drop. Go to your warranty app dashboard and trigger a full re-sync. In Extend: Settings → Products → Sync. In Clyde: Settings → Catalog → Force Sync. After sync, refresh the product in Shopify Admin and verify all variants are active with non-zero prices.

The Subscription App Conflict — Why Recharge + Extend Breaks Checkout

The second most common catastrophic failure: subscription apps and warranty apps conflict at the cart transformation layer.

Why the Conflict Happens

Both Apps Fight Over the Same Cart Line Item

Subscription apps like Recharge, Skio, and Bold Subscriptions work by attaching a "selling plan" to a cart line item. A selling plan tells Shopify's checkout: this is a recurring purchase, not a one-time order. The checkout handles billing, dunning, and renewal accordingly.

Warranty apps work by adding a second line item to the cart and linking it to the original product line item with a cart attribute or metafield. The warranty is logically attached to the product.

The collision: When Recharge transforms the cart for subscription checkout, it validates that each line item is a valid subscription product. The Protection Plan variant does not have a selling plan — it's a one-time purchase. Recharge's cart validation rejects it. Meanwhile, Extend expects its cart attribute (linking the warranty to the product) to survive the cart transformation. It doesn't — Recharge's transformation overwrites custom cart attributes.

Result: checkout either fails with a generic error, or completes but with the warranty dropped from the order. Neither scenario gives the customer a clear error message.

Resolution path: Check your warranty app's documentation for "subscription compatibility." Extend maintains a list of subscription apps with verified integration support. If your subscription app is on that list, follow the explicit integration guide. If it's not on the list, you cannot reliably run both apps simultaneously — you must choose which takes precedence, or separate the products (sell warranty-only and subscription-only products through different pages without cross-app cart logic).

Diagnosis

5-Step Warranty Checkout Diagnosis

Start at Step 1. Each step eliminates one layer of the warranty architecture. Stop as soon as you find the failure.

  • 1
    Confirm which layer is failing — widget, cart, or checkout Test in an incognito window: (a) Does the warranty widget appear on the product page? If not → Layer 1 failure (JavaScript not loading). (b) Click "Add Protection" — does a Protection Plan line item appear in the cart? If not → Layer 2 failure (variant add failed). (c) Proceed to checkout with the protection plan in cart — does it still appear at the checkout page? If it disappears → Layer 2 failure (Shopify cart validation dropped the variant). If it appears but checkout throws an error → Layer 3 failure (checkout processing error).
  • 2
    Check the Protection Plan product in Shopify Admin → Products Search for your warranty app's product name. Verify: Status = Active, all variants have a price greater than $0.00, variants are not archived or in Draft mode. If anything looks wrong, go to your warranty app dashboard and trigger a full catalog re-sync before testing again.
  • 3
    Test without any other apps in the checkout flow Temporarily disable other checkout-modifying apps (subscription apps, upsell apps, loyalty apps). Test the warranty add-on again. If it works with other apps disabled, you have a conflict. Re-enable apps one at a time to identify which one causes the conflict. The most common culprit is a subscription app (Recharge, Skio).
  • 4
    Check Checkout Extensibility compatibility If your theme recently migrated to Checkout Extensibility (the upgrade from checkout.liquid), your warranty app may need to install a new Checkout UI Extension. Go to Shopify Admin → Settings → Checkout → Checkout UI extensions and verify your warranty app is listed and active. If it's missing, go to your warranty app's setup guide and install the checkout extension.
  • 5
    Run a full end-to-end test and verify in the warranty app dashboard Complete a test order with the protection plan using Shopify's bogus payment gateway (Admin → Settings → Payments → bogus gateway). After the order completes, open your warranty app dashboard and verify: (a) the order appears, (b) the protection plan is attributed to the correct product, (c) coverage is registered. If the order appears in Shopify but not in the warranty app dashboard, the order webhook failed — check your warranty app's webhook settings.

FAQ

Shopify Warranty and Protection Plan — Frequently Asked Questions

Why is the Extend warranty not showing at Shopify checkout?
When the Extend widget appears on the product page but no protection plan shows at checkout, the protection plan line item was silently removed during Shopify's cart validation. This almost always means the Protection Plan product variant in your Shopify catalog is either deactivated, archived, or priced at $0.00. Go to Shopify Admin → Products, search "Extend Protection Plan," and check that all variants are Active with non-zero prices. Then go to your Extend dashboard → Settings → Products and trigger a full re-sync. After syncing, test the checkout again in an incognito window.
Why does the Clyde protection plan disappear at Shopify checkout?
Clyde's most common cause of disappearing plans is the Checkout Extensibility migration. If your theme recently upgraded from checkout.liquid to Checkout Extensibility, you need to manually install Clyde's Checkout UI Extension. Go to Shopify Admin → Settings → Checkout → Checkout UI Extensions and verify Clyde is listed and enabled. If it's not listed, follow Clyde's checkout extension installation guide from their support docs. The product page widget works independently, but checkout rendering requires the extension. Also verify the Clyde Contract product in your Shopify catalog is not archived.
Why does adding a warranty plan conflict with my Shopify subscription app?
Subscription apps (Recharge, Skio) and warranty apps both modify the cart's line items at checkout. Recharge transforms cart items by attaching selling plan metadata. Warranty apps add a separate Protection Plan line item linked to the original product. These transformations collide: Recharge's validation rejects the non-subscription Protection Plan line item, or the warranty app's cart attributes are overwritten during Recharge's transformation. To fix: check your warranty app's documentation for explicit subscription app compatibility. Extend has a documented Recharge integration. If your warranty app doesn't list your subscription app as compatible, you cannot reliably run both simultaneously and must choose one.
Can a customer use a discount code AND add a warranty plan to their Shopify checkout?
Yes — discount codes and warranty protection plans coexist at checkout. The protection plan is a separate line item, and warranty apps typically configure their Protection Plan product to be excluded from discount eligibility so discount codes don't accidentally reduce the warranty price. Where this can go wrong: if your discount code is set to "All products" without any exclusions, and your warranty app hasn't configured the Protection Plan product type as discount-ineligible, the code may incorrectly reduce the warranty price. To prevent this, go to Shopify Admin → Discounts → edit your discount codes → add a product exclusion for your warranty app's product type or collection.
How do I test my Shopify warranty or protection plan end-to-end before a product launch?
Run four tests: (1) Widget test — open product page in incognito, verify warranty widget appears. (2) Cart add test — click "Add Protection," verify Protection Plan line item appears in cart at correct price. (3) Checkout completion test — complete a test order with the bogus payment gateway, then verify the order appears in your warranty app's dashboard with coverage registered. (4) Conflict test — if you use a subscription app, add both a subscription product and a warranty to the same cart and attempt checkout; if it fails or drops either item, you have a confirmed conflict. Run these tests monthly and after any app update, theme update, or Shopify plan change.
