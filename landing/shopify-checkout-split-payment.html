Shopify BNPL & Split Payment Troubleshooting

Shopify Split Payment Not Working — Shop Pay Installments Not Available, Afterpay/Klarna/Sezzle Missing, BNPL Not Showing at Checkout

Shopify has no native split payment or installment feature. Shop Pay Installments (US/CA only) is the closest built-in option but requires merchant eligibility approval. Every other BNPL method — Afterpay, Klarna Pay Later, Sezzle, Zip, and Paidy — requires Shopify Payments active, a supported country market, and a manual toggle. None are automatically enabled.

Updated June 2026 Affects: US/CA stores (Installments), global stores with BNPL, B2B deposit workflows
Shopify does not have a built-in split payment system. Shop Pay Installments requires US/CA location and merchant eligibility. All other BNPL methods (Afterpay, Klarna, Sezzle, Zip) require Shopify Payments as the active gateway, a country match, and a separate activation toggle. Third-party gateways (Stripe, Adyen) block all native BNPL options. Manual deposit + balance for custom/B2B orders requires a dedicated app.
Architecture

Four Paths to Split Payment on Shopify — and When Each One Applies

Shopify splits the "buy now, pay later" problem into four distinct architectures. Each requires different setup, has different geographic availability, and fails silently in different ways. Understanding which path applies to your store is the first diagnostic step.

Path 1 — US/CA Only
Shop Pay Installments
The only built-in Shopify installment product. Powered by Affirm. US and Canada stores only. Requires Shopify Payments + Shop Pay enabled. Merchant eligibility assessment required — not all stores qualify. Orders $50–$30,000 only. No EU/UK/APAC availability.
Path 2 — Shopify Payments BNPL
Afterpay, Klarna, Sezzle, Zip via Shopify Payments
Available in supported markets when Shopify Payments is the active gateway. Each method is a separate toggle (off by default). Country and currency must match per method. Third-party gateways block all of these — they are Shopify Payments-exclusive integrations.
Path 3 — Third-Party BNPL App
Direct BNPL App Integration
For stores not on Shopify Payments (or in countries where Shopify Payments BNPL is unavailable), install the BNPL provider's official Shopify app (Afterpay, Klarna, Sezzle). These create a parallel checkout overlay — the buyer is redirected to the provider's site. More friction than native integration.
Path 4 — Custom / B2B
Deposit + Balance Apps or Net Terms
For manual split payment (50% deposit at checkout, 50% on delivery) or B2B deferred payment, use a deposit app (Partial.ly, Omega Deposit) or Shopify B2B Net Terms (Plus only). No native Shopify support — always requires an app or custom development.
Common Blocker
Third-Party Gateway = No Native BNPL
If your active gateway is Stripe, PayPal, Braintree, Adyen, or any non-Shopify processor, all Shopify Payments BNPL options (Afterpay, Klarna, Sezzle, Shop Pay Installments) are unavailable. You must use Path 3 (dedicated BNPL app) or switch to Shopify Payments.
B2B Edge Case
BNPL Does Not Apply to B2B Sessions
Consumer BNPL methods (Afterpay, Klarna, Shop Pay Installments) do not appear for Shopify Plus B2B company account checkouts. B2B split payment uses Net Terms (net 30/60/90) or deposit apps — these are entirely separate architectures from consumer BNPL.

Diagnosis Checklist

5-Step BNPL & Split Payment Diagnosis

  • 1
    Confirm Shopify Payments is your active gateway Go to Admin → Settings → Payments. Check that Shopify Payments is shown as the primary payment provider. If you see Stripe, PayPal, Braintree, Adyen, or any other provider listed as the main gateway, all Shopify Payments-native BNPL options (Shop Pay Installments, Afterpay, Klarna, Sezzle, Zip) are unavailable. Your only option for BNPL in that case is installing the BNPL provider's dedicated Shopify app (Path 3). If you want to use native BNPL integrations, you must switch your primary gateway to Shopify Payments — which may affect your existing payment flows and requires re-testing checkout end-to-end.
  • 2
    Individually toggle each BNPL method in Shopify Payments → Manage In Admin → Settings → Payments → Shopify Payments → click Manage → scroll to the 'Additional payment methods' or 'Buy now, pay later' section. Each BNPL method has its own toggle: Afterpay, Klarna (multiple products), Sezzle, Zip/Quadpay, Paidy (Japan). None are enabled by default. Enabling one does not enable the others. Check each one you want active. Save changes. Then open checkout in incognito from a supported country to verify the method appears — Admin toggles being on does not guarantee appearance if country or currency conditions are not met.
  • 3
    Verify the buyer's country and checkout currency match the BNPL method's requirements Each BNPL method is tied to specific countries and requires the checkout to present in the correct currency. Afterpay requires: US (USD), CA (CAD), AU (AUD), NZ (NZD), UK as Clearpay (GBP). Klarna Pay Later: widely available in DE, SE, NL, AT, FI, NO, DK (EUR or local currency). Sezzle: US and CA only. Zip: AU, NZ, US, UK, ZA. If the buyer's billing or shipping address is outside a supported country, or if Shopify Markets is displaying the price in a non-matching currency, the BNPL option will not appear at checkout — with no error message to the buyer or merchant.
  • 4
    For Shop Pay Installments: verify merchant eligibility and order amount range If you are in the US or CA and have Shopify Payments active but do not see an Installments option in Admin → Settings → Payments → Shop Pay, your store may not yet be eligible. Eligibility is assessed by Affirm based on business type, industry, and payment history. Ineligible industries include gambling, adult content, firearms, tobacco, and certain financial services. Additionally, Shop Pay Installments only appears at checkout for orders between approximately $50 and $30,000 USD — orders outside this range do not show the installment option even if your store is fully configured. Contact Shopify Support through Admin to request eligibility review if the option is absent entirely.
  • 5
    For deposit/split payment: use a dedicated app and monitor balance charge webhooks If you need manual split payment (deposit at checkout + balance later), Shopify does not have a native solution. Install a deposit app such as Partial.ly, Deposit & Partial Payment by Omega, or a similar tool from the Shopify App Store. After installation: (a) configure the deposit percentage or fixed amount per product or product type; (b) set the balance charge date (days after purchase, or event-based like "delivery"); (c) critically — verify the app has webhook monitoring and sends you a notification if the balance charge fails (card expired, authorization lapsed). Balance charge failures are the most common production issue with deposit apps and they are silent by default if the app does not send failure alerts.
Deep Dive

Three BNPL Failure Patterns in Detail

Deep Dive A

Shop Pay Installments Eligibility — Why It's Invisible to Most Stores Outside the US

Shop Pay Installments is frequently searched by merchants who assume it is a global feature — it is not. It launched in the US in 2021 and expanded to Canada in 2023. As of 2026, it remains exclusively a US/CA product. The underlying financing is provided by Affirm (US) and Paybright (CA), both of which perform real-time credit assessment on the buyer at the time of purchase. Shopify does not have equivalent BNPL financing partners for EU, UK, or APAC markets through this specific program.

Even within the US, the merchant must pass Affirm's eligibility criteria. Shopify processes the eligibility check automatically when you enable Shop Pay, but the result is not explicitly communicated — stores that are not eligible simply do not see the Installments section in Admin. This leads to merchants assuming the feature is hidden or requires activation, when in fact they have been rejected silently. If you are based in the US, have Shopify Payments active, have Shop Pay enabled, but see no Installments option under Admin → Settings → Payments → Shop Pay, contact Shopify Support and specifically ask about installment eligibility status. They can tell you whether your store is under review, approved, or ineligible and the reason.

Eligibility check path: Admin → Settings → Payments → Shop Pay → look for "Installments" section If absent: contact Support → ask about eligibility status Ineligible categories: adult, gambling, firearms, tobacco, financial services Order range: $50 minimum, ~$30,000 maximum

For stores outside US/CA that need installment payment, the options are third-party BNPL apps (Klarna app, Afterpay app, Sezzle app installed directly) or regional BNPL providers that have their own Shopify integrations — for example, Scalapay in Italy/France/Spain, Alma in France, or Paidy in Japan.

Deep Dive B

Klarna vs Afterpay vs Sezzle on Shopify — Configuration Differences and Why They Fail Differently

All three are enabled through the same Shopify Payments → Manage interface, but they have meaningfully different configuration requirements and fail in different ways. Understanding the differences prevents treating them as interchangeable.

Afterpay is the most broadly available through Shopify Payments: US, CA, AU, NZ, and UK (as Clearpay). Afterpay performs its own buyer eligibility check in real time. If an Afterpay-eligible order is between approximately $1 and $2,000, the option appears. The most common failure: merchants in the EU enable Afterpay expecting it to show for German or French customers — it will not. Afterpay's European market presence runs through a separate Clearpay product that requires the dedicated Clearpay app, not the Shopify Payments toggle.

Klarna has multiple products in Shopify Payments: Pay Now (immediate bank transfer), Pay Later (invoice in 14-30 days), Pay in 3 (split into 3 installments), and Financing (longer-term). These are separate toggles. Nordic and DACH merchants commonly enable Klarna Financing but need Pay in 3 — the wrong product is active and the expected option does not appear. Additionally, Klarna's availability in the US is limited compared to Sweden, Germany, or the UK — US merchants expecting Klarna Pay Later to behave the same as in Europe are often surprised by inconsistent appearance.

Sezzle is US and Canada only, focused on younger demographics with limited credit history. It has a lower average order value range than Afterpay or Klarna. Sezzle's main failure mode is merchants in Canada enabling it and finding it absent because the buyer's billing address is in a non-supported province or because the cart total is outside Sezzle's range ($35–$2,500 typically). Sezzle is the least available globally and has the narrowest eligibility window.

BNPL method by region (Shopify Payments native integration): US: Shop Pay Installments, Afterpay, Sezzle, Klarna (limited), Zip CA: Shop Pay Installments, Afterpay, Sezzle AU: Afterpay, Zip/Quadpay NZ: Afterpay, Zip UK: Clearpay (via Afterpay toggle), Klarna Pay Later/Pay in 3 EU: Klarna (DE/SE/NL/AT/FI/NO/DK/CH), Scalapay (IT/FR/ES via app) JP: Paidy (via Shopify Payments Paidy toggle)
Deep Dive C

Manual Deposit + Balance for Custom Orders — The Only Real Split Payment on Shopify

The phrase "split payment" on Shopify most commonly means consumer BNPL (Afterpay, Klarna), but for merchants selling custom products, made-to-order furniture, event services, or high-value B2B orders, it means something different: collecting a deposit at order placement and charging the remaining balance at delivery or completion. Shopify has no native mechanism for this workflow.

The most functional solution is the app Partial.ly. It integrates with Shopify checkout and allows the merchant to define: percentage or fixed deposit amount per product (e.g. 30% deposit), the number of installments for the remaining balance, the dates or triggers for balance charges, and automatic retry logic when a payment fails. The buyer completes the deposit at checkout through normal Shopify payment flow; Partial.ly handles subsequent charges using stored payment authorization. The main risk: stored payment authorizations expire. If a buyer placed a custom order with a 90-day lead time and your deposit app tries to charge the balance at day 90, the authorization may have lapsed. Configure the app to re-request payment authorization before the charge date when the lead time is long.

For B2B wholesale specifically, Shopify Plus B2B Net Terms is a cleaner solution than a deposit app — it removes the payment complexity entirely at checkout and generates an invoice with a due date (Net 30, 60, or 90). The buyer does not pay at checkout at all. This requires: Shopify Plus plan, B2B company accounts enabled, and Net Terms configured per company. It does not involve card storage or authorization — it is a pure invoice workflow.

Deposit app setup checklist (Partial.ly or equivalent): 1. Define deposit % per product or product type 2. Set balance charge trigger (date, event, or manual) 3. Configure failed payment notification to merchant (not just buyer) 4. Set authorization re-request window (for lead times >30 days) 5. Test full flow: place order → confirm deposit charged → verify balance charge fires correctly 6. Monitor order notes in Shopify for balance payment status

FAQ

Split Payment & BNPL — Frequently Asked Questions

Why is Shop Pay Installments not available on my Shopify store?
Shop Pay Installments is US and Canada only, requires Shopify Payments active, and requires merchant eligibility approval from Affirm (the financing partner). If you do not see an Installments section under Admin → Settings → Payments → Shop Pay, your store may be ineligible (wrong country, ineligible product category, or pending review). Contact Shopify Support to check eligibility status. Order amounts outside $50–$30,000 also do not show Installments at checkout even on eligible stores.
How do I add Afterpay to Shopify checkout?
Afterpay is available through Shopify Payments in the US, CA, AU, NZ, and UK (as Clearpay). Go to Admin → Settings → Payments → Shopify Payments → Manage → find Afterpay in the additional payment methods section and toggle it on. Shopify Payments must be your active gateway. If you use a third-party gateway, you must install the official Afterpay app from the Shopify App Store instead, which creates a separate checkout overlay.
Can Shopify do split payment or deposit plus balance for custom orders?
Shopify has no native deposit-plus-balance split payment. For custom orders, use a deposit app like Partial.ly or Deposit & Partial Payment by Omega (Shopify App Store). These apps collect a deposit at checkout and schedule the balance charge separately. For B2B wholesale, use Shopify Plus B2B Net Terms (Net 30/60/90) instead — this skips payment at checkout entirely and issues an invoice with a due date.
Why is Klarna Pay Later not showing at my Shopify checkout?
Four causes: (1) Shopify Payments not active as your gateway, (2) Klarna not individually toggled on in Payments → Manage (Klarna has multiple products — Pay Now, Pay Later, Pay in 3, Financing — each is a separate toggle), (3) checkout currency doesn't match Klarna's market requirements, (4) the order amount is outside Klarna's range for your market. Enable Klarna products one by one and test from the correct country with the correct currency.
Does Shopify support BNPL for B2B wholesale orders?
Consumer BNPL (Afterpay, Klarna, Shop Pay Installments) does not appear for Shopify Plus B2B company account checkout sessions. It requires individual consumer credit assessment which is incompatible with company accounts. For B2B deferred payment, use Shopify B2B Net Terms (Shopify Plus required) — this lets approved companies place orders on Net 30/60/90 day payment terms without paying at checkout.
