What Actually Happens to Your Checkout

This is the part most guides skip. When Shopify suspends your Payments account, the failure is invisible from the outside. Here's exactly what breaks:

# Shopify checkout API — payment methods endpoint
GET /checkouts/{token}/payment_url
→ Step 1: Products fetch ............. 200 OK
→ Step 2: Cart creation .............. 200 OK
→ Step 3: Checkout session init ...... 200 OK
→ Step 4: Payment methods fetch ...... 200 OK (empty)
payment_gateways: [] ← NO PAYMENT OPTIONS

The critical detail: the API returns 200 OK. Your store is not "down" in any detectable way. The response is valid JSON. The status is success. But the payment gateways array is empty — which means your checkout renders with zero payment options.

What customers see varies by theme:

  • A blank payment section with no cards, PayPal, or other options
  • A grayed-out "Place order" button that can't be clicked
  • A generic "No payment methods available" message (some themes)
  • A checkout that appears to load but never presents payment options

In every case: no customer can complete an order. Most will leave without telling you.

Why Shopify Suspends Payment Accounts

Shopify sends an email when your account is suspended, and the email usually states the reason. The most common causes:

Most Common

High chargeback rate

Shopify Payments requires chargebacks below 0.5%. Exceeding this triggers automatic review and possible suspension, especially if the rate climbs quickly.

Common

Identity verification required

Shopify periodically requires stores to re-verify the account holder's identity. Failing to respond within the deadline suspends payouts and checkout.

Common

Restricted product category

Shopify Payments does not support certain product types (supplements, certain electronics, high-risk goods). Selling these can result in account suspension.

Less Common

Suspicious transaction velocity

A sudden spike in order volume — such as from a viral post or influencer campaign — can trigger fraud detection and a temporary hold on payments.

Less Common

Policy violation

Shopify's Acceptable Use Policy prohibits certain business models. If Shopify's compliance team flags your store, payments may be suspended during investigation.

Rare

Bank or card network requirement

Visa/Mastercard compliance requirements can require Shopify to pause a merchant's processing while documentation is collected.

How Long Does a Suspension Last?

Duration depends entirely on the reason and how quickly you respond to Shopify's request:

  • Identity verification: Usually resolved within 24 hours of submitting documents
  • Chargeback review: 3–10 business days; may require submitting dispute evidence
  • Restricted products: Requires category change or migration to a third-party gateway; timeline varies
  • Policy investigation: 5–15 business days; outcome not guaranteed

The fastest path to resolution in every case: respond to Shopify's email immediately and completely. Delays in responding extend the suspension window by days.

Immediate Steps When You Discover a Suspension

01

Check your email immediately

Shopify sends a suspension notice to the account email. Search for "Shopify Payments" and "account" in your inbox. The email states the reason and usually requests specific documentation or action.

02

Log in to Shopify Admin → Settings → Payments

Check for any banners or action items on the Payments page. Shopify often surfaces the suspension reason here with a direct link to the resolution flow.

03

Activate a backup payment method immediately

Don't wait for Shopify to resolve the suspension before recovering your checkout. Add PayPal or another third-party gateway as an alternative so customers can buy now. See the next section.

04

Contact Shopify Support with your case number

Use the in-admin chat or email. Reference the suspension email's case or ticket number. Ask for the specific documentation needed and the expected timeline. Don't submit a new request — respond to the original.

05

Submit required documentation immediately and completely

Partial submissions restart the review clock. Submit everything Shopify asks for in one response: government ID, business registration, bank statements, or dispute evidence — whatever applies to your case.

Accept Payments While Suspended

You do not need to wait for Shopify Payments to be reinstated. You can activate a third-party payment provider in minutes:

  1. Go to Shopify Admin → Settings → Payments
  2. Click Add payment method in the "Supported payment methods" section
  3. Select PayPal, Stripe, or another available gateway
  4. Complete the provider's onboarding (usually 5–10 minutes)
  5. Test a checkout to confirm payment options appear

Transaction fees: Most Shopify plans charge a 0.5–2% fee on third-party payments. On a $10,000/day store, that's $50–200/day in extra fees. That's far less painful than zero revenue from a broken checkout. Enable the backup gateway immediately.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why did Shopify suspend my payments?

Shopify suspends payment accounts for several reasons: high chargeback rates (above 0.5%), selling in a restricted product category, identity verification failure, suspicious transaction velocity, or suspected policy violations. You'll receive an email from Shopify with the reason. The most common cause is chargebacks exceeding Shopify Payments' risk threshold.

What do customers see when Shopify Payments is suspended?

Customers see either a blank payment section with no options, a grayed-out checkout button, or a generic error. The experience depends on your theme, but in all cases customers cannot complete a purchase. Most will leave without contacting you — making checkout monitoring essential to know how long the failure has been occurring.

How long does a Shopify Payments suspension last?

Most suspensions are resolved within 1–5 business days once you respond to Shopify's verification request. Identity verification suspensions are often lifted within 24 hours. Chargeback-related suspensions may require a review period of 3–10 business days. Responding immediately to Shopify's email is the fastest way to shorten the suspension window.

Can I still accept payments while Shopify Payments is suspended?

Yes. You can activate a third-party payment provider (PayPal, Stripe, or another gateway) as an alternative while Shopify Payments is suspended. Go to Settings → Payments → Add payment method. Shopify charges a 0.5–2% transaction fee on third-party payments on most plans, but this is far less than the revenue loss from a completely broken checkout.

How do I know when Shopify Payments is restored?

Shopify sends a reinstatement email, but timing is unpredictable. The most reliable method is automated checkout monitoring — a tool that simulates the checkout flow and detects when payment methods return. CheckPulse checks every 5 minutes and sends an alert the moment payments are restored.

Does a Shopify Payments suspension affect my existing orders or payouts?

Suspended accounts typically have payouts paused in addition to new payment processing. Existing orders that were already paid should still fulfill normally. Pending payouts may be held during the suspension period and released when the account is reinstated. Check the Payments section in your Shopify admin for the payout status.
