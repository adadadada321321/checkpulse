Shopify Markets Checkout Not Working — International Checkout Errors

When Shopify Markets is enabled, you gain a distinct category of checkout failures that don't appear in single-market stores. Here are the 6 most common, how to diagnose each, and how to fix them.

Important distinction: Shopify Markets checkout failures are caused by market configuration — not payment gateway settings or checkout apps. They require a different diagnosis path than standard checkout errors.
Context

Why Shopify Markets Creates a New Category of Checkout Failures

When you enable Shopify Markets, your checkout gains a routing layer: before serving the checkout page, Shopify determines which market the customer belongs to — based on IP geolocation, domain, or URL parameter — and then loads the configuration for that market: currency, price list, language, available payment methods, and tax rules. Every step in that routing chain is a potential failure point.

Standard Checkout Errors

Single-market failures

  • Payment methods not loading (API error)
  • Payment error at submission
  • 3D Secure redirect loop
  • Checkout slow to load
  • Session expired mid-checkout
  • App conflict breaking submit button

These affect all customers globally. Monitored by standard checkout health checks.

Shopify Markets Failures

Market-routing failures

  • Customer assigned to wrong market
  • Local payment methods missing for market
  • Price list / currency mismatch at payment
  • Checkout domain not routing to market
  • B2B market blocking retail customers
  • Currency conversion fallback failing

These affect specific countries or regions only. May show green on US-based uptime monitors while international customers fail.

Root Causes

The 6 Most Common Shopify Markets Checkout Failures

1
Customer Assigned to Wrong Market
Symptom: international customer sees wrong currency or wrong language at checkout

This is the most common Shopify Markets checkout failure. Shopify assigns customers to markets based on their IP geolocation at the time of first visit. If a country isn't explicitly assigned to an international market, it defaults to your primary market — which is typically your domestic market with domestic currency and payment methods.

A German customer routed to your US primary market sees USD prices at checkout. If you haven't enabled Shopify Payments in their actual market (Germany), they may see no local payment methods (no SOFORT, no Giropay). They can't complete checkout — but your checkout health monitor shows green because it's testing from a US IP.

The second cause of wrong-market assignment: market preference cookies. When you restructure your markets (add countries to a new market, remove them from the primary), customers who already have a market preference cookie may still be routed to the old assignment until the cookie expires or is cleared.

Fix Steps
  1. Audit your market assignments: Shopify Admin → Settings → Markets. Expand each market and verify that the correct countries are listed. Every country you sell to should be in a specific market — if it's not listed anywhere, it's defaulting to primary.
  2. Test market routing: Use a VPN to simulate a customer from the target country. Open your store, proceed to checkout, verify the currency and available payment methods match the market configuration.
  3. Clear the test cookie: Use incognito mode each time you test — market preference cookies persist across sessions and will show you the cached assignment, not the current one.
  4. Check for country overlap: A country can only be in one market. If you accidentally added a country to two markets (by moving it), Shopify silently drops it from the older market — verify each country appears in exactly one market.
2
Local Payment Methods Missing for Market
Symptom: iDEAL, Klarna, SOFORT, Bancontact, or other local methods not showing at checkout in specific countries

Local payment methods in Shopify must be explicitly enabled per market. Enabling Shopify Payments globally does not automatically activate all regional methods in every market. iDEAL for the Netherlands, SOFORT for Germany, Bancontact for Belgium, and Klarna across Europe each require separate configuration inside the market's payment settings.

Additionally, every local payment method has currency requirements. iDEAL requires EUR — if your Netherlands market is configured with a non-EUR currency (or no currency), iDEAL won't appear. BACS Direct Debit requires GBP. These currency-method mismatches are invisible in the Shopify admin but surface as missing payment options at checkout.

Common misconfiguration: a merchant enables Shopify Payments for their EU market but configures the market currency as USD (for unified reporting) — all EUR-required local methods silently disappear from checkout for that market.

Fix Steps
  1. Navigate to payment settings by market: Shopify Admin → Settings → Payments → Shopify Payments → Manage → Local payment methods. The list shows which methods are enabled; expand each to verify market-level configuration.
  2. Verify currency matches method requirement: For each local method, check Shopify's documentation for the required currency. Then confirm your market's pricing currency matches. Admin → Settings → Markets → [Market] → Pricing shows the configured currency.
  3. Test with a local customer profile: VPN to the target country, clear cookies, proceed through checkout, and verify the expected local methods appear at the payment step.
  4. Check Shopify's eligibility list: Not all local methods are available in all Shopify plan tiers or all countries. Some require your Shopify account's billing country to match specific criteria.
3
Price List or Currency Mismatch at Payment Submission
Symptom: checkout shows correct price, but payment fails with a currency or amount error

When you configure market-specific pricing in Shopify Markets, prices can be set in two ways: automatic conversion (Shopify converts from your base currency at a live exchange rate) or fixed price lists (you manually set prices per currency). Both can cause payment failures at submission if there's a mismatch between what the customer sees and what Shopify sends to the payment gateway.

The most common cause: exchange rate lag. Shopify caches exchange rates and updates them periodically. If a customer begins checkout during a rate update, the price displayed may differ by a fraction from the price submitted to the payment gateway. Some payment gateways reject this as a mismatch. The customer sees no clear error — just a failed payment.

For fixed price lists, the failure is different: if a product's price list entry is missing for a market, Shopify falls back to automatic conversion. If automatic conversion is also disabled for that market, the checkout can't determine a price in the market currency — resulting in a checkout error that prevents the customer from reaching the payment step.

Fix Steps
  1. Audit your price list coverage: Admin → Settings → Markets → [Market] → Pricing. If you're using fixed prices, verify every product has a price entry in the price list — gaps cause silent conversion fallbacks or errors.
  2. Enable rate rounding for automatic conversion: Admin → Settings → Markets → [Market] → Pricing → scroll to rounding rules. Rounding prices to clean amounts (e.g., €29.99 instead of €29.87) reduces gateway mismatch rejections.
  3. Test the specific failing market: Add a product to cart, proceed to checkout from the market's country, and submit a test payment. Note the currency and exact amount at the submission step — compare with what the gateway receives in your payment gateway dashboard.
  4. Check gateway currency support: Not all payment gateways support all currencies. If your market uses a currency the gateway doesn't support, Shopify should warn you — but verify anyway in the gateway's documentation.
4
Checkout Domain Not Routing to Correct Market
Symptom: customers on international subdomains land in the wrong market at checkout

Shopify Markets supports domain-based market routing: you can assign a custom domain or subdomain to each market (e.g., de.yourstore.com for Germany, fr.yourstore.com for France). When a customer visits via that domain, Shopify should route them into the corresponding market automatically.

The failure happens when the checkout subdomain doesn't follow this routing. Shopify checkout uses a separate domain (checkout.shopify.com or your custom checkout domain). If your DNS or Shopify Markets domain configuration has a mismatch, customers who enter your store via de.yourstore.com may be redirected to a checkout URL that drops the market context — landing them in your primary (US) market with USD pricing.

This is especially common after adding a new market domain, after DNS propagation delays, or after upgrading from a single-market setup to multi-market.

Fix Steps
  1. Verify domain assignments: Admin → Settings → Domains. Each international domain should be assigned to a market. Click each domain and confirm the market assignment is correct.
  2. Check DNS propagation: If you recently added a market domain, DNS changes take 24–72 hours to fully propagate. Use a DNS checker tool to verify the domain resolves correctly from multiple global locations.
  3. Test the checkout URL directly: Navigate to https://[market-domain]/cart, add a product, and proceed to checkout. Verify the checkout URL and the displayed currency/language match the expected market.
  4. For Hydrogen / headless: Market context must be passed explicitly in your storefront API calls. Verify your Hydrogen app is passing the country and language parameters correctly in the cart and checkout queries.
5
B2B Market Settings Blocking Retail Checkout
Symptom: specific customers can't reach checkout — "checkout unavailable" or redirect to account login

Shopify Markets supports B2B markets — markets restricted to approved company accounts. This is useful for wholesale or trade pricing. However, B2B market misconfiguration is a common source of checkout failures for regular retail customers who accidentally land in a B2B market context.

The most common trigger: a merchant creates a B2B market for a country (e.g., "EU Wholesale") and assigns specific countries to it for business customers. If those same countries are no longer in the retail EU market (because Shopify only allows each country in one market), retail customers from those countries now hit the B2B market — which requires a company account login before checkout is accessible.

From the retail customer's perspective, this looks like: they add items to cart, click checkout, and are either blocked with a "not available" message or redirected to a login page with no clear path forward.

Fix Steps
  1. Audit B2B market country assignments: Admin → Settings → Markets → identify any market with B2B enabled. Check which countries are assigned. Verify those countries are not countries where you also sell to retail customers.
  2. Separate B2B and retail markets cleanly: If you sell to both B2B and retail in the same country, you cannot use market-level restrictions — B2B must be handled at the customer account level (Shopify B2B on Plus) or via a separate Shopify store.
  3. Test from affected country: VPN to a country assigned to your B2B market. Add a product to cart without logging in and attempt checkout. Confirm whether retail access is blocked.
6
Currency Conversion Fallback Failing Silently
Symptom: customers in markets with unsupported currencies see checkout errors instead of fallback pricing

Not all Shopify payment methods support every currency. When a customer's market is configured with a currency that the active payment gateway doesn't support, Shopify is supposed to fall back to your base currency (typically USD or GBP) for the checkout. If this fallback isn't configured, or if it's been disabled, the customer hits an error at checkout instead.

This most commonly affects markets in regions with less common currencies: Southeast Asia, Eastern Europe, Latin America, or Africa. A merchant creates a market for Indonesia, configures IDR as the display currency for product pages, but their payment gateway doesn't support IDR transactions. The checkout either fails at the payment step or shows an error preventing the customer from selecting a payment method.

The failure is silent in your admin — no alerts, no logs. Revenue from that market drops to zero, and you only find out when a customer from Indonesia emails you saying "I can't check out."

Fix Steps
  1. Check your gateway's supported currencies: Every payment gateway has a published list. Shopify Payments supports ~130 currencies; third-party gateways vary. Compare your market currencies against what your gateway supports.
  2. Configure a currency fallback: Admin → Settings → Markets → [Market] → Pricing. If the market currency isn't supported by your gateway, either switch the market currency to one the gateway supports, or enable "Automatically convert prices" to your base currency as a fallback.
  3. Test checkout from the affected market: VPN to the country, complete a full checkout simulation through payment. If the payment step shows an error or no payment methods, the currency is not being handled correctly.
  4. Consider a third-party payment gateway: If your primary gateway doesn't support a key market's currency, adding a gateway that does (e.g., Adyen, Stripe) for that market may be the cleanest fix.
Diagnosis

Quick Diagnosis Checklist for International Checkout Problems

When a specific country or region can't complete checkout, run this sequence before digging deeper.

Step 1
Identify the affected market
Is the failure country-specific or region-wide? Which customers are reporting it? This determines whether it's a market-routing, payment method, or currency issue.
Step 2
Verify market assignment
Admin → Settings → Markets. Confirm the affected country is in the expected market — not in the primary market or a B2B market by accident.
Step 3
VPN test from affected country
Use a VPN (or actual device in that country). Open the store in incognito mode. Check the market indicator — does the store show the correct currency and language?
Step 4
Proceed to checkout
Add a product and go to checkout. Does the checkout domain match the expected market? Does the currency in checkout match the market's configured currency?
Step 5
Check payment methods step
Reach the payment step. Are the expected local payment methods present? If iDEAL is missing for Netherlands, or SOFORT for Germany, that's a local payment method configuration issue.
Step 6
Attempt test payment
Submit a test payment (use Shopify test card mode). Does it succeed, or fail with a currency/amount error? Currency errors at submission point to a price list or gateway currency support issue.

FAQ

Shopify International Checkout — Common Questions

Why is Shopify checkout not working for international customers?
International Shopify checkout failures usually fall into one of six categories: wrong market assignment (customer routed to the primary market instead of their regional market), local payment methods missing (iDEAL, Klarna, Sofort not enabled per market), price list or currency mismatch at payment submission, checkout domain not routing to the correct market, B2B market settings blocking retail customers, or currency conversion fallback failing for unsupported currencies. Each has a distinct diagnosis path — start by identifying which country is affected, then use a VPN to test checkout from that country in incognito mode.
What is Shopify Markets and what checkout problems does it cause?
Shopify Markets is Shopify's built-in international commerce feature, available on Basic plan and above. It lets you configure separate pricing, currency, language, domains, and payment methods per country or region. When enabled, your checkout gains a routing layer that assigns customers to markets based on geolocation, domain, or URL parameters — and loads the market's configuration. Each routing step is a potential failure point, creating a distinct category of international checkout errors that don't appear in single-market stores and are invisible to US-based uptime monitors.
How do I fix Shopify checkout showing wrong currency for international customers?
If international customers see the wrong currency at checkout, the three most likely causes are: (1) the customer isn't being routed to the correct market — go to Admin → Settings → Markets and verify their country is assigned to the right market, not defaulting to primary; (2) the market has a price list with manual prices set in the wrong currency; (3) a stale market preference cookie from an old assignment is overriding the new configuration. Test in incognito mode from the target country using a VPN, and verify currency at the checkout domain — not just on product pages.
Why are local payment methods (iDEAL, Klarna, Sofort) not showing at Shopify checkout?
Local payment methods must be enabled per market in Shopify — enabling Shopify Payments globally does not activate all regional methods automatically. Go to Admin → Settings → Payments → Shopify Payments → Manage → Local payment methods to configure per-market. Additionally, each local method has currency requirements: iDEAL requires EUR, BACS requires GBP. If your market's pricing currency doesn't match the method's requirement, the method won't appear at checkout. Verify both the method configuration and the market's currency setting.
How do I monitor Shopify checkout for international markets automatically?
Standard uptime monitors test from fixed US-based IPs — your checkout can fail for European or Asian customers while your monitor shows green. The most reliable approach is running checkout simulations with market-specific parameters, verifying payment methods endpoints return the correct local methods per market, and testing from the market's actual country.
