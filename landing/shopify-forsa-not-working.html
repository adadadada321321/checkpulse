Shopify Forsa Not Working — CIB BNPL Not Showing, Application Declined & Installment Failures

Updated June 2026 · 7 failure modes · Egypt merchants · CIB Forsa installment financing

Forsa is CIB's (Commercial International Bank) Buy Now Pay Later program — Egypt's largest private bank backing a BNPL service with installment plans of 3, 6, 12, 24, and 36 months. Unlike fintech-native BNPL services such as valU or Shahry, Forsa is directly operated by a traditional bank, which means it uses stricter credit criteria, requires CIB's merchant onboarding approval, and has product category restrictions. When Forsa stops working on Shopify, the failure mode matters: not showing at checkout, an application declined mid-flow, and an OTP failure are three entirely different problems with three different fixes.

How Forsa Works — and Why It's Different From Other Egypt BNPL

Forsa is bank-backed, not fintech-native. CIB (Commercial International Bank) operates Forsa directly as an installment lending product. This makes Forsa more like a bank loan than a typical BNPL product — the credit assessment uses Egypt's official credit bureau (i-Score), requires Egyptian National ID, and follows banking-grade KYC. Customers with strong credit bureau profiles and existing banking relationships are most likely to be approved; customers with no credit history or negative bureau entries will be declined.

Forsa requires merchant onboarding by CIB. Unlike Tabby or Postpay — where a merchant can self-serve and go live within hours — Forsa requires merchants to apply to CIB's Forsa program, submit business documents, and receive approval before the payment option can appear at checkout. There is no self-serve onboarding portal. The process involves direct engagement with a CIB relationship manager or Forsa business development contact.

Forsa is Egypt-only and EGP-only. Forsa financing is available exclusively for purchases by Egyptian customers on Egyptian merchant stores. The payment option processes in Egyptian Pounds only — orders priced in other currencies require the store to operate in EGP or display EGP pricing alongside the foreign currency. Customers outside Egypt cannot apply for Forsa at checkout.

Forsa's installment terms are merchant-specific. CIB negotiates installment plan availability (3, 6, 12, 24, 36 months) and interest/administrative fee rates individually with each merchant. A merchant approved for Forsa may only offer 6-month plans while another offers 3 to 36 months. If customers see fewer plan options than expected, the merchant's Forsa contract determines what is available — not a configuration error.

Forsa Not Appearing at Checkout — Merchant Not Onboarded or App Misconfigured

Merchant Configuration

The most common Forsa issue merchants report is that the payment option never appears at checkout — even after installing the Forsa Shopify app. This happens for two distinct reasons: the merchant has not completed CIB Forsa's merchant onboarding process, or the Forsa Shopify app is installed but not configured with valid merchant credentials.

Forsa requires every merchant to apply to CIB's Forsa business program and receive explicit approval before the payment option can be enabled. This is not a self-serve process. The merchant must contact CIB or a Forsa business development representative, submit their business registration documents, and sign a merchant agreement with CIB. Until this agreement is in place and CIB activates the merchant account in Forsa's system, the checkout widget cannot appear — even with the app installed and credentials entered.

If the merchant is onboarded but Forsa still doesn't show, the issue is typically credential configuration: the Forsa Shopify app requires a merchant ID and API key issued by CIB Forsa. If these credentials are entered incorrectly (wrong merchant ID, wrong environment, or API key from a test account), the app's authentication fails silently and hides the payment option from customers.

Onboarding takes time: CIB Forsa merchant onboarding is not instant. The process typically takes 7–21 business days from initial contact to activation, involving document review and signing a merchant financing agreement. Do not install the Shopify app and expect immediate functionality without first completing the onboarding with CIB directly.

How to Fix — Forsa Not Appearing at Checkout

  1. Confirm your merchant onboarding status — contact the CIB Forsa business team or your CIB relationship manager to verify your merchant account is active in the Forsa system. If you have not started onboarding, initiate the application with CIB's merchant services.
  2. Verify your Forsa merchant credentials in the Shopify app settings — confirm the merchant ID and API key match exactly what CIB Forsa issued you. These are case-sensitive.
  3. Confirm you are using production credentials, not test/sandbox credentials — CIB Forsa may issue separate test credentials during onboarding. Production credentials are required for the payment option to display on a live store.
  4. Check the Forsa app's connection status in the Shopify admin — some Forsa integrations include a "connection test" or status indicator that shows whether the app is successfully authenticated with CIB Forsa's API.
  5. Verify the app is enabled as a payment method in Shopify → Settings → Payments → Third-party providers — the Forsa app must be listed and enabled, not just installed.

Customer Application Declined — Credit Eligibility or No i-Score Data

Customer Eligibility

When a customer selects Forsa at checkout, they go through a real-time credit eligibility check. CIB Forsa queries Egypt's credit bureau (i-Score, operated by I-Score Egypt) and applies its own creditworthiness criteria. If the customer does not meet these criteria, the application is declined at checkout and the customer cannot proceed with Forsa financing.

The most common decline reasons: the customer has existing overdue loans, credit card debt, or a negative credit bureau history that reduces their i-Score below CIB Forsa's approval threshold; the customer has never taken a loan or credit card in Egypt, so there is no i-Score data to evaluate (thin credit file); the customer's declared income is below the minimum required by CIB for Forsa financing; or the customer is employed informally without payslips, making income verification difficult in CIB's assessment model.

Unlike Shahry (which uses ML scoring with alternative data) or Postpay (which uses behavioral signals from the merchant's platform), Forsa's credit decision is rooted in traditional banking credit assessment — customers without formal employment or without a positive credit bureau history are significantly less likely to be approved. This is the fundamental difference between a bank-backed BNPL (Forsa) and a fintech BNPL (Shahry, Postpay).

What to tell declined customers: A Forsa decline is not a technical error — it is a credit decision by CIB. Declined customers should be directed to alternative payment methods at checkout. Consider offering valU (broader scoring model) or Shahry (ML-based, targets underbanked customers) alongside Forsa to capture buyers that Forsa's model does not approve.

How to Handle — Customer Application Declined

  1. Display alternative payment options clearly — when Forsa declines a customer, ensure other payment methods (card, Paymob wallets, valU, Shahry) are immediately visible so the customer can complete the purchase through a different method.
  2. Do not retry the Forsa application immediately — repeated application attempts in a short window may be flagged. Advise customers to retry after addressing any bureau issues (e.g., clearing overdue debt) and waiting the required cooling-off period.
  3. Recommend that declined customers check their i-Score — customers can request their credit report from i-Score Egypt (iscore.com.eg) to understand if there are errors or overdue entries that are causing the decline.
  4. Offer Forsa for future purchases — customers who build a positive credit history (on-time card payments, cleared loans) may qualify for Forsa on future purchases. First-time declines are not permanent disqualifications.

Credit Limit Below Order Amount — Forsa Cannot Finance the Full Cart

Customer Limit

A customer may be approved for Forsa financing but have an approved credit limit that is lower than their cart total. In this case, Forsa cannot finance the purchase and the checkout fails at the amount validation step — even though the customer is a valid Forsa holder. This is different from a declined application: the customer is eligible, but the specific purchase amount exceeds what CIB has approved for them.

Forsa credit limits are assigned individually based on the customer's credit assessment and CIB's internal models. A customer with a 5,000 EGP Forsa limit cannot use Forsa to buy a product priced at 8,000 EGP without a limit increase. Unlike a credit card where the customer knows their limit upfront, many Forsa users do not know their exact approved limit until a transaction is declined at the amount stage.

This failure is particularly frustrating for merchants selling higher-value items (furniture, electronics, appliances) where the cart total frequently exceeds individual Forsa limits. Merchants in these categories often see high Forsa "availability" (the option appears) but high abandonment at the amount confirmation step because customer limits cluster below the typical cart value.

Partial financing is not available: Forsa does not support splitting a cart between Forsa financing and another payment method (e.g., pay 3,000 EGP via Forsa and 2,000 EGP via card). The order total must be within the customer's Forsa limit. Customers in this situation must either reduce their cart value or choose an alternative payment method for the full amount.

How to Handle — Credit Limit Below Order Amount

  1. Display an approximate Forsa limit range near the Forsa option at checkout — messaging like "Available for orders up to approximately 10,000 EGP depending on your approved limit" sets expectations and reduces abandoned carts from limit failures.
  2. Offer split-basket options — allow customers to remove items and check out with a reduced cart that fits within their Forsa limit, completing the remainder of their purchase separately.
  3. Inform customers about the Forsa limit increase process — customers can request a credit limit increase through the CIB mobile banking app or by contacting CIB customer service. Increases are not immediate but may allow future purchases of higher-value items.
  4. Position valU or Shahry as alternatives for higher-value carts — some BNPL services have different (sometimes higher) per-transaction limits that may better suit high-ticket purchases in your product category.

OTP Not Received — Mobile Number Mismatch With CIB Records

Customer Experience

Forsa's checkout flow requires OTP (one-time password) verification to authenticate the customer and authorize the installment agreement. The OTP is sent to the mobile number registered in the customer's CIB records. If the customer is using a different mobile number than the one on file with CIB — because they changed SIMs, ported their number, or have multiple phone numbers — the OTP is delivered to the CIB-registered number, not the one the customer is actively using.

This failure is common among Egyptian customers who frequently change mobile numbers (a recognized behavior in Egypt's prepaid mobile market), use secondary SIMs for online shopping, or have not updated their CIB contact details after changing networks. The customer sees an OTP request at checkout, waits for an SMS that never arrives on their current phone, and eventually abandons the purchase.

A secondary OTP failure mode occurs when the CIB-registered mobile number is an older number that the customer no longer has access to — the SIM is deactivated, the number was transferred to another user by the carrier after 90 days of inactivity, or the customer no longer owns the device. In these cases, the OTP is delivered but unrecoverable, and the checkout cannot be completed until the customer updates their CIB mobile number.

How to Handle — OTP Not Received

  1. Advise customers to check their CIB-registered number — the OTP is always sent to the number on file at CIB, which may differ from the number they are currently using. Ask them to check if the OTP arrived on a different SIM or device.
  2. Direct customers to update their CIB mobile number before retrying — customers can update their registered mobile number via the CIB mobile banking app (CIB Egypt), online banking portal, or by visiting a CIB branch. The update takes effect immediately in most cases.
  3. Check if OTPs have a short expiry window — Forsa OTPs may expire in 60–120 seconds. If the customer's SMS is delayed (network congestion, roaming), the OTP may arrive after expiry. Advise the customer to initiate the Forsa flow again and watch for the SMS immediately.
  4. Do not offer to "resend" the OTP repeatedly — multiple OTP requests in quick succession may trigger CIB's anti-fraud controls, locking the customer out of the Forsa flow for a cooling-off period. Suggest waiting 5–10 minutes before retrying.

National ID Not Accepted or Minimum Age Not Met

Customer Eligibility

Forsa financing requires a valid Egyptian National ID (بطاقة الرقم القومي) for identity verification. Non-Egyptian nationals, customers without a registered National ID, or customers whose National ID has expired cannot use Forsa at checkout. This is a hard requirement from CIB as a licensed bank — Forsa is a credit product, and credit products in Egypt require the borrower to be an Egyptian citizen or permanent resident with a valid national identification.

The minimum age requirement for Forsa is typically 21 years old. Customers under 21 attempting to use Forsa will have their application declined regardless of creditworthiness. The National ID number encodes the customer's date of birth, so age verification is automatic — there is no way to circumvent this requirement by entering a different date of birth.

A less common but notable failure: customers whose National ID has been renewed (new number assigned after expiry) but who still have the old number registered in their CIB profile. The new National ID number will not match CIB's records, causing verification to fail even though the customer is a valid Egyptian national with an active ID. These customers need to update their National ID with CIB before Forsa can be used.

For merchants with international customers: Forsa is not available to non-Egyptian customers. If your Shopify store serves a mix of Egyptian and non-Egyptian buyers, configure Forsa to appear only for EGP orders or Egyptian billing addresses — or display a note explaining that Forsa is available to Egyptian National ID holders only. Showing Forsa to international customers who then experience a verification failure creates unnecessary abandonment.

How to Handle — National ID or Age Issues

  1. Add eligibility messaging near the Forsa option at checkout — "Available to Egyptian National ID holders aged 21 and above" — so ineligible customers know immediately without starting the application flow.
  2. Direct under-21 customers to alternative payment methods — cards, Fawry, cash on delivery, or bank transfer are available to all ages. Shahry also has a minimum age, so check the requirements of each BNPL provider you offer.
  3. Advise customers with updated National IDs to update their CIB profile — if a customer has a new National ID number (e.g., after renewal), they need to update this at a CIB branch or via CIB online banking before using Forsa.
  4. Non-Egyptian residents should be directed to card or wallet payment — Paymob accepts international cards, making it the best fallback for non-Egyptian buyers in your Egypt-focused store.

Below Minimum Order Amount for Forsa Financing

Merchant Configuration

Forsa has a minimum eligible order amount below which installment financing is not offered. Orders below this threshold will either not display the Forsa option at checkout, or display it but fail when the customer attempts to proceed. This minimum exists because the administrative cost of processing a multi-installment loan is economically justified only above a certain transaction size.

The minimum order amount is set by CIB Forsa and may be configured differently per merchant category — a merchant selling consumer electronics may have a different minimum than one selling fashion. The typical range as of 2026 is approximately 500 to 1,500 Egyptian Pounds, but merchants should confirm their specific minimum in their CIB Forsa merchant agreement or by contacting their CIB Forsa relationship manager.

A common merchant error is not displaying the Forsa minimum clearly on product pages or in the checkout. Customers who add a 400 EGP item to their cart, see Forsa listed as a payment option, begin the Forsa application flow, and then receive a "minimum amount not met" error have a poor experience — and the merchant loses a conversion. The minimum should be disclosed before the customer invests time in the Forsa application.

How to Fix — Below Minimum Order Amount

  1. Confirm your Forsa minimum order amount in your CIB Forsa merchant agreement — the specific minimum for your merchant category and contract terms.
  2. Display the minimum amount on product pages near the Forsa badge — "Forsa installments available on orders over X EGP" — so customers know before reaching checkout.
  3. Hide or disable the Forsa option at checkout for orders below the minimum if your Forsa Shopify integration supports dynamic minimum enforcement — preventing the application flow from starting for carts that will not be approved.
  4. Consider bundle pricing or product packaging that brings carts above the Forsa minimum — customers who want Forsa financing may be willing to add additional items to qualify.
  5. Contact CIB Forsa to discuss your minimum — if your average order value frequently sits just below the minimum, your CIB relationship manager may be able to negotiate a lower minimum for your merchant category.

Product Category Restricted From Forsa Installments

Merchant + CIB Policy

CIB Forsa does not finance all product categories. As a bank-issued installment credit product, Forsa is subject to banking regulations and CIB's internal credit risk policies, which restrict financing of certain goods and services. Merchants whose primary product categories fall into restricted categories may find that Forsa processes for some orders but not others — or that CIB declines to onboard the merchant entirely.

Commonly restricted or limited categories include: financial products and investments (Forsa cannot finance the purchase of other financial instruments); digital goods and non-physical products in some configurations (since repossession is not possible); certain consumable products (where the goods may be consumed before installments are paid); and products that may conflict with CIB's ethical or compliance guidelines. The full restricted category list is defined in the CIB Forsa merchant agreement.

For multi-category merchants, this creates an asymmetric experience: a customer buying a TV (eligible) has Forsa available, while a customer buying a software subscription (may be restricted) does not. If the Forsa Shopify app does not filter by category, the option may appear for restricted items and then fail at the Forsa API level when the merchant category code is checked.

Category mismatch at onboarding: Some merchants who sell both eligible and restricted products onboard with Forsa under their primary eligible category but then use Forsa for all products including restricted ones. Forsa's API checks the merchant category code (MCC) at the transaction level — transactions that do not match the merchant's onboarded category may be declined even if the customer is approved and within their limit.

How to Handle — Category Restrictions

  1. Review the restricted categories in your CIB Forsa merchant agreement — the agreement specifies which product types are eligible for Forsa financing under your merchant contract.
  2. Configure Forsa to appear only for eligible product categories in your Shopify store — use Shopify's payment method visibility rules or custom logic to show Forsa only when the cart contains eligible products.
  3. Contact your CIB Forsa relationship manager if a product category you want to offer with Forsa is currently restricted — some categories can be added to the merchant agreement with CIB's approval.
  4. Display clear messaging for products where Forsa is unavailable — "Forsa installments not available for this product category" prevents customers from starting an application that will fail.
  5. Use other BNPL options for restricted categories — valU and Shahry may have different category restrictions that cover what Forsa cannot, allowing you to offer installments on a broader product range by combining providers.

Common Questions About Forsa on Shopify

Should I offer Forsa and valU together on the same Shopify store?

Yes — offering both Forsa and valU maximizes your installment financing coverage in Egypt. Forsa targets the banked customer segment with formal credit history; valU serves a broader base including customers without traditional banking relationships. The two services do not compete for the same customer at checkout — Forsa will approve some customers that valU might not, and valU will approve customers that Forsa's conservative criteria would decline. Combined, they give the majority of Egyptian online shoppers access to some form of BNPL installments on your store. Adding Shahry as a third option further extends coverage to underbanked customers. The main operational downside is managing three separate merchant relationships and three sets of settlement cycles, but the conversion uplift typically justifies this for stores with a significant Egyptian customer base.

How long does Forsa take to settle funds to merchants in Egypt?

Forsa settlement to merchants is handled by CIB and typically occurs within a defined settlement cycle agreed in the merchant contract — commonly 1 to 5 business days after the transaction is completed. Unlike BNPL services where the provider pays the merchant upfront and collects from the customer over time, CIB Forsa pays merchants the full order amount (minus any agreed administrative fee) and then manages the installment collection from the customer directly. This means merchants receive their money relatively quickly, similar to a normal card transaction settlement, without waiting for the customer to complete all their installment payments. Confirm your specific settlement timeline in your CIB Forsa merchant agreement, as terms vary by merchant category and relationship.

Can customers use Forsa on Shopify if they are not CIB bank customers?

Yes — Forsa is not limited to existing CIB customers, although having a CIB relationship improves approval odds. Non-CIB customers can apply for Forsa financing at checkout through a first-time credit assessment process. The Forsa application flow captures the customer's National ID, mobile number, and other required information, then runs a real-time credit assessment against Egypt's credit bureau (i-Score). Non-CIB customers who have a positive credit bureau history — from credit cards with other banks, car loans, or other formal credit — can be approved for Forsa. Customers with no bureau history (no formal credit products in Egypt) are the most likely to be declined, regardless of whether they are CIB customers or not. CIB customers with an existing account simply have a faster verification path since CIB already has their KYC data.
