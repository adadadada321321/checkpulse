Shopify Checkout Email Troubleshooting

Shopify Checkout Email Not Working — Invalid Email, Apple Hide My Email Blocked, Confirmation Email Not Received

Shopify checkout email problems come in two varieties: the email field refusing to accept a valid address, and the checkout succeeding but the confirmation email never arriving. The causes are different for each. Invalid email errors at checkout most often come from mobile autocorrect typos, anti-fraud apps blocking privacy relay addresses like Apple Hide My Email, or disposable email domain blocklists catching legitimate services. Missing confirmation emails come from spam filtering, a typo in the address, or Apple's relay becoming inactive. Shop Pay adds a third layer — it pre-fills the email from the customer's account profile, not whatever they typed. This guide covers every failure mode and the exact path to fix each one.

Updated June 2026 Affects: Apple Hide My Email, privacy relay services, anti-fraud apps, transactional email, Shop Pay
Shopify's native email validation accepts almost any RFC 5322-compliant address — including Apple Hide My Email relay addresses. Most email rejections at Shopify checkout come from third-party anti-fraud apps, not Shopify itself. Missing confirmation emails are a deliverability problem, not a checkout problem — the order completed successfully.
Root Causes

Five Reasons Shopify Checkout Rejects or Loses an Email Address

Email failures in Shopify checkout fall into two groups: the field actively rejecting the address (preventing checkout), and the checkout completing but the email not reaching the customer (silent failure). The five root causes span both groups.

Cause 1 — Format Error
Invalid Email Format at Input
Mobile autocorrect is the most common cause — it inserts a space before the @, replaces @ with a comma or period, or auto-capitalizes the domain (gmail.Com). Double dots (..) in the local part (john..doe@gmail.com) and missing TLD (user@company) also fail RFC 5322 validation. These errors are invisible to the customer who typed confidently on their phone.
Cause 2 — Anti-Fraud App
Disposable or Privacy Relay Email Blocked
Third-party anti-fraud apps (FraudLabs Pro, NoFraud, Signifyd) maintain blocklists of disposable email domains. Apple Hide My Email (@privaterelay.appleid.com), SimpleLogin, Firefox Relay, and Fastmail aliases all appear on some of these lists despite being legitimate privacy services used by real customers.
Cause 3 — Shop Pay Pre-fill
Wrong Email Auto-filled by Shop Pay
When a customer signs into Shop Pay at checkout, the email field is set to their Shop Pay account email — overriding whatever they had typed. If their Shop Pay account was created with an old address, a work email, or an email they no longer check, order confirmations go there instead of their intended inbox. Merchants cannot override this behavior.
Cause 4 — Inactive Relay
Apple Hide My Email Relay Turned Off
Apple Hide My Email addresses only work while the relay is active. If the customer disabled "Hide My Email" for a specific app in their Apple ID settings, or if their iCloud subscription lapsed (Hide My Email requires iCloud+), the relay stops forwarding. Checkout succeeds but every subsequent email — confirmation, shipping, marketing — silently disappears.
Cause 5 — Deliverability
Confirmation Email Blocked or Filtered
Shopify sends transactional email from shared infrastructure. Without a custom sender domain with SPF and DKIM configured, some mail servers — particularly corporate Microsoft 365 and Google Workspace deployments with strict policies — reject or quarantine emails from Shopify's sending IPs. The checkout succeeded; the email is the problem.

Deep Dive A

Email Format Validation — What Shopify Accepts and Why Mobile Causes Silent Typos

Shopify validates email format using RFC 5322 syntax. The rules are simple — but mobile keyboards make them surprisingly easy to violate without the customer noticing.

Deep Dive A — Valid vs. Invalid Formats

What Shopify Accepts and What It Rejects

Shopify's email validation checks for a valid local part (before the @), exactly one @ symbol, and a valid domain with at least one dot. These are the common failures:

Valid (Shopify accepts): john.doe@gmail.com ✓ john+tag@company.co.uk ✓ (plus addressing) xyz.abc@privaterelay.appleid.com ✓ (Apple Hide My Email) user@simplelogin.io ✓ (privacy relay) JOHN@GMAIL.COM ✓ (case-insensitive) Invalid (Shopify rejects): john .doe@gmail.com ✗ (space before @) john.doe @gmail.com ✗ (space after local part) john.doe@gmail,com ✗ (comma instead of dot — autocorrect) john..doe@gmail.com ✗ (double dot in local part) johndoe@gmail ✗ (missing TLD) @gmail.com ✗ (missing local part)

The space typo is the most common mobile autocorrect failure. On iOS, the spacebar is immediately adjacent to the @ key — a fat-finger tap inserts a space. On Android, autocorrect can replace the @ symbol with a period or comma if the keyboard "corrects" what it interprets as a punctuation mistake.

To reduce mobile typos: use type="email" and autocomplete="email" on the email input (Shopify does this natively). Consider adding inputmode="email" as well — it suppresses autocorrect on most mobile keyboards. Shopify Plus merchants can enforce this via Checkout UI Extension on the email field.

Deep Dive A — Common Typo Patterns

Email Domain Typos That Reach Real Customers

Customers who submit a typo'd email address complete checkout successfully — Shopify has no way to know the domain is misspelled. You see a completed order; the customer sees nothing because the confirmation email bounced. The patterns below account for over 80% of domain typos in e-commerce:

gmail.com → gmial.com, gmail.co, gmal.com, gmail.con, gmai.com hotmail.com → hotmal.com, hotmaill.com, hotmail.co yahoo.com → yaho.com, yahooo.com, yahoo.co outlook.com → outllook.com, outlok.com, outloo.com icloud.com → iclod.com, icoud.com

Proactive fix: run a weekly query of Admin → Orders, export customer emails, and filter for known typo patterns. For recent orders with typo domains, contact customers via their phone number (if collected) or social media to get a corrected email and resend the order confirmation. This reduces chargebacks from customers who never received their confirmation and thought the order failed.

Deep Dive B

Apple Hide My Email and Privacy Relay Services — What Works and What Breaks

Apple Hide My Email is an iCloud+ feature that generates a unique random email address for each app or website. The relay address forwards to the user's real Apple ID email. These addresses are valid and technically functional — but they create problems at several layers of the Shopify ecosystem.

Deep Dive B — How the Relay Works

Apple Hide My Email Technical Flow

When a customer uses "Sign in with Apple" or selects "Hide My Email" during checkout, Apple generates an address like xyz123.abc456@privaterelay.appleid.com. Apple's relay server receives all email sent to that address and forwards it to the customer's real Apple ID email. The relay is active as long as: (1) the customer has an active iCloud+ subscription ($0.99/mo or higher), (2) the relay for that specific address is enabled in Apple ID settings, and (3) Apple's relay infrastructure is operational.

Apple Hide My Email flow: Shopify → sends to xyz123.abc456@privaterelay.appleid.com → Apple relay server receives → [relay active?] Yes → forwards to customer's real email ✓ → [relay active?] No → email silently dropped ✗ Common relay deactivation triggers: - iCloud+ subscription expired - Customer manually disabled relay in Apple ID → iCloud → Hide My Email - Customer used "Sign in with Apple" to create account then later deleted it

From Shopify's perspective, every email to a privaterelay.appleid.com address succeeds — there is no bounce. The relay server accepts the email. You will never see a hard bounce or delivery failure in Shopify's notification logs. The only way to know the relay is inactive is if the customer contacts you.

Deep Dive C

Transactional Email Deliverability — Why Confirmation Emails Go Missing

Shopify order confirmation emails are sent from Shopify's shared infrastructure. Without a custom sender domain, you're competing with the reputation of thousands of other Shopify merchants sharing the same sending IPs. Here's how to fix deliverability at the root.

Deep Dive C — Custom Sender Domain

Configuring a Custom Sender Email with SPF and DKIM

By default, Shopify sends notifications from a @shopify-notifications.com subdomain. Setting up a custom sender email routes notifications through your own domain, which you can authenticate with SPF and DKIM records — telling recipient mail servers that you authorized Shopify to send on your behalf.

Step 1: Admin → Settings → Notifications → Sender email → Enter your custom email: hello@yourdomain.com → Shopify will prompt you to verify domain ownership Step 2: Add DNS records at your domain registrar SPF: v=spf1 include:shops.shopify.com ~all DKIM: TXT record with value Shopify provides (auto-generated per store) Step 3: Verify in Admin → Notifications → Domain status shows "Authenticated" Result: Emails arrive as "From: hello@yourdomain.com" not "shopify-notifications.com" → Higher deliverability with corporate mail servers → Builds domain reputation over time → Reduces spam filter false positives

DMARC is optional but recommended for domains with significant email volume: v=DMARC1; p=none; rua=mailto:dmarc@yourdomain.com. Start with p=none to monitor without breaking delivery, then advance to p=quarantine after a few weeks of clean reports.

Deep Dive C — Resending and Manual Recovery

How to Resend Order Confirmations and Recover Missing Emails

If a customer didn't receive their confirmation, the fastest fix is a manual resend — no need to create a new order or issue a refund. Shopify keeps the email address on record for every order regardless of whether the confirmation was delivered.

Manual resend: Admin → Orders → [select order] → More actions → Resend email → Choose: Order confirmation / Shipping confirmation / etc. Check email on file: Admin → Orders → [select order] → Customer section → Email address → Compare to what customer says they used → If typo: cannot change email on existing order; create a note and manually email the customer from your support address Bulk audit for typo domains (export CSV): Admin → Orders → Export → All orders → Email column → Filter for @gmail.con, @hotmail.co, etc. in spreadsheet

For ongoing deliverability monitoring: check Admin → Settings → Notifications periodically for any domain authentication warnings. Shopify will flag if SPF or DKIM records become misconfigured after a domain transfer or DNS change.

Diagnosis Checklist

Email Fix Checklist — Start Here

  • 1
    Verify the email on the order Admin → Orders → select order → check the email address in the Customer section. Common findings: typo in domain (gmail.con), old email from Shop Pay account, Apple relay address (@privaterelay.appleid.com). This tells you which failure mode you're dealing with before spending time on the wrong fix.
  • 2
    Resend the confirmation email Admin → Orders → [order] → More actions → Resend email → Order confirmation. Ask the customer to check spam folder. If the address is clearly a typo, contact the customer via phone or their alternate contact method (if available) and ask for their correct email to send manually from your support address.
  • 3
    If Apple Hide My Email or relay address: whitelist in anti-fraud app If customers are being blocked at checkout: open your anti-fraud app settings (FraudLabs Pro, NoFraud, Signifyd) and add @privaterelay.appleid.com to the allowed domain list. Also add @simplelogin.io, @mozmail.com, @relay.firefox.com. These are real customers using legitimate privacy tools, not fraudsters.
  • 4
    Set up custom sender email with SPF and DKIM Admin → Settings → Notifications → Sender email → enter your domain email → follow DNS setup steps. This is the single highest-impact action for improving confirmation email deliverability. Reduces corporate mail server rejections and builds domain sending reputation over time.
  • 5
    Audit ESP suppression list for wrongly-bounced relay addresses If you use Klaviyo, Omnisend, or another ESP: check the suppression/unsubscribe list for @privaterelay.appleid.com addresses. Hard bounces that occurred because a relay was temporarily inactive (not because the address was invalid) should be removed so post-purchase flows can reach these customers again.
  • 6
    For Shop Pay email conflicts: inform customer to update Shop Pay account If the order went to a customer's old Shop Pay email: advise them to log into shop.app to update their account email address. Alternatively, they can check out as a guest in future orders by not using the "Sign in with Shop" button — guest checkout bypasses Shop Pay auto-fill entirely.
FAQ

Shopify Checkout Email — Frequently Asked Questions

Why does Shopify checkout say my email is invalid even though it's a real email address?
Shopify's native email validation accepts virtually any correctly formatted address, including Apple Hide My Email relay addresses. If a valid email is being rejected at checkout, a third-party anti-fraud app installed on the store is most likely blocking it — particularly if the address ends in @privaterelay.appleid.com or another privacy relay domain. The app's domain blocklist treats relay services as disposable email. Ask the merchant to whitelist privacy relay domains, or try entering a different email address to proceed.
Why is Apple Hide My Email not working at Shopify checkout?
Apple Hide My Email addresses (ending in @privaterelay.appleid.com) are technically valid and pass Shopify's native validation. The two most common failure modes: (1) the merchant has an anti-fraud app that blocks @privaterelay.appleid.com as a disposable email domain — fix is to whitelist the domain in the anti-fraud app settings; (2) the relay is inactive because the customer's iCloud+ subscription lapsed or they disabled it in Apple ID settings — fix is for the customer to re-enable the relay or use their real email address instead.
Why is my Shopify order confirmation email not arriving?
Most commonly: the email went to spam (check spam/junk folder), there's a typo in the email address entered at checkout, or the store doesn't have a custom sender domain configured (making Shopify's emails look suspicious to corporate mail servers). Merchants can resend the confirmation via Admin → Orders → More actions → Resend email. For ongoing deliverability, set up a custom sender email with SPF and DKIM records in Admin → Settings → Notifications.
Why does Shop Pay use the wrong email at checkout?
Shop Pay auto-fills the email from the customer's Shop Pay account profile, overriding whatever was typed. If the Shop Pay account email is old, order confirmations go there. Customers should update their Shop Pay account email at shop.app, or check out as a guest by not signing in via Shop Pay to use a different email for a specific order. Merchants cannot override or modify which email Shop Pay uses.
Why are some customers not receiving marketing emails after checkout?
Three common causes: (1) The customer used Apple Hide My Email and the relay is no longer active — all emails are silently dropped by Apple's relay server. (2) The email address is on your ESP's suppression list from a previous hard bounce when the relay was inactive. (3) The customer subscribed at checkout but your ESP requires double opt-in — the confirmation email went to spam and they never clicked confirm. For (1) and (2): audit your suppression list for relay addresses. For (3): consider disabling double opt-in for Shopify checkout subscribers, or ensure the opt-in confirmation email has strong deliverability.
