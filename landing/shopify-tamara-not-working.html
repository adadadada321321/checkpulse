Shopify Tamara Not Working — Not Showing, Declined, Absher Verification Failed

Updated June 2026 · 7 failure modes · Saudi Arabia, UAE, Kuwait & Bahrain merchants

Tamara is the leading BNPL platform in Saudi Arabia and a strong competitor to Tabby across the GCC — but it fails in ways that are specific to its Pay-in-3 model, its Absher-based identity verification for Saudi nationals, and its four-market coverage that excludes Egypt. Unlike Tabby, Tamara shows customers their available credit in the app — but this transparency doesn't prevent declines when limits are consumed. Its Absher integration creates a unique failure mode with no equivalent in any Western BNPL product. This guide covers all 7 Tamara failure modes with exact diagnostic steps for Saudi Arabia, UAE, Kuwait, and Bahrain merchants on Shopify.

How Tamara Differs From Tabby and Western BNPL

Tamara is Saudi-first and uses Absher as its primary identity layer. Absher is the Saudi government's digital identity platform — the same system Saudi nationals use to renew passports, verify identity for government services, and manage residency permits. When a Saudi national creates or verifies a Tamara account, they authenticate through Absher. This creates a deeply integrated verification path that works seamlessly for Saudi citizens with active Absher accounts — but fails for anyone whose Absher data is stale, whose mobile number changed since Absher was configured, or who has not activated Absher. No Western BNPL product has an equivalent integration.

Tamara shows customers their available credit — unlike Tabby. Tabby deliberately keeps spending limits opaque; customers learn the limit only through a decline. Tamara takes the opposite approach: the Tamara app shows each customer their available Tamara credit limit. This means customers who hit their Tamara limit generally understand why — "I've used my limit" rather than "Tamara is broken." However, this transparency doesn't prevent declines; it just makes the reason clearer. Merchants still receive support tickets from customers confused about why their Tamara limit is what it is and how to increase it.

Tamara uses a Pay-in-3 structure, not Pay-in-4. The Tamara default is three equal installments: one-third at checkout, one-third 30 days later, one-third 60 days later. Tamara also offers Pay Later (30-day deferred payment). This is structurally different from Tabby's Pay-in-4 monthly model — the installment amounts, payment schedule, and total cost display are different. Merchants configuring both Tabby and Tamara must manage two distinct product widgets with different installment messaging on product pages.

Tamara covers Bahrain but not Egypt. Tamara operates in Saudi Arabia (SAR), UAE (AED), Kuwait (KWD), and Bahrain (BHD). Tabby operates in UAE, Saudi Arabia, Egypt, Kuwait, and Qatar. Merchants targeting Egypt exclusively cannot use Tamara — they need Tabby. Merchants selling across the full GCC plus Egypt typically run both platforms: Tamara for KSA/UAE/Kuwait/Bahrain, Tabby for Egypt (and Qatar).

1

Tamara Not Showing at Shopify Checkout

Merchant-side

Tamara is a third-party alternative payment provider — it requires a two-step activation that many Shopify merchants miss. Installing the Tamara app from the Shopify App Store is necessary but not sufficient. Tamara must also be manually enabled as an alternative payment provider in Shopify Admin → Settings → Payments → Alternative payment providers. The two steps are frequently confused: merchants see the Tamara app installed and assume checkout activation happens automatically. It does not.

Currency is the primary suppressor for multi-market stores. Tamara only appears at checkout when the transaction currency is SAR (Saudi Arabia), AED (UAE), KWD (Kuwait), or BHD (Bahrain). Stores with USD, EUR, or any non-Tamara-market base currency will not display Tamara regardless of how the app is configured. Multi-currency stores that display SAR or AED as a runtime conversion from USD — rather than setting these as the fixed checkout currency per Shopify Market — will suppress Tamara for all Saudi and UAE customers. This is the most common cause of Tamara "disappearing" after a store expands internationally.

Additional causes: the merchant's Tamara merchant account onboarding is incomplete (Tamara requires business verification, commercial registration documents, and approval before going live — this typically takes 3–7 business days in Saudi Arabia and 2–5 in UAE); test/sandbox API credentials are configured instead of live production keys; or Tamara is enabled for the wrong market in the Tamara merchant portal. Saudi Arabia and UAE are configured separately in the Tamara merchant dashboard — enabling Tamara for UAE without enabling it for KSA suppresses it for Saudi customers even on a store that ships to Saudi Arabia.

Product category restrictions also suppress Tamara. Tamara does not support digital goods, gift cards, subscription products, or financial services products. Stores whose entire catalog falls into these categories will not see Tamara regardless of payment configuration. If your store mixes physical and digital products, Tamara will appear for physical-goods-only carts but may be suppressed for mixed carts in some configurations.

How to Diagnose

  1. Check the two-step activation: Shopify Admin → Apps → confirm the Tamara app is installed; then Shopify Admin → Settings → Payments → Alternative payment providers → confirm Tamara is listed and enabled. Installed ≠ enabled at checkout.
  2. Check store currency per market: Shopify Admin → Settings → Markets → Saudi Arabia → confirm SAR is the fixed checkout currency, not a runtime conversion from USD; repeat for UAE (AED), Kuwait (KWD), Bahrain (BHD)
  3. Check Tamara merchant account status per market: Log into the Tamara merchant portal and confirm your account is fully approved and live for each market you sell in — Saudi Arabia and UAE are separately activated; accounts pending document review cannot process transactions
  4. Check API credential mode: In the Tamara app settings, confirm Live (production) API public and secret keys are configured — test/sandbox credentials suppress Tamara at live checkout and are a common oversight after initial integration
  5. Test cart value above minimum: Add physical products totalling above the market minimum (100 SAR / 100 AED / 5 KWD / approximately 10 BHD) and test checkout with a supported shipping address — Tamara should appear for valid cart values above the minimum
  6. Check product category: If your store sells only digital products, gift cards, or subscriptions, Tamara will not appear — add a physical product to the cart and retest
  7. Test in incognito with a supported shipping address: Open a private browser window, add physical products above the minimum, enter a Saudi Arabia or UAE shipping address, and proceed to checkout — rules out browser cache and session conflicts
Market activation tip: Tamara's merchant portal has separate activation toggles for Saudi Arabia, UAE, Kuwait, and Bahrain. If Tamara appears for UAE customers but not Saudi customers (or vice versa), check the per-market activation status in your Tamara merchant dashboard — market-specific activation is a common gap after initial setup.
2

Per-Transaction Risk Assessment Declined — "Not Available for This Order"

Customer-side

Like Tabby, Tamara evaluates every purchase independently through a real-time risk assessment — not just at account creation. Each checkout triggers a decision against Tamara's risk model, and that model can decline a customer who has successfully completed Tamara purchases at the same merchant before. Tamara's risk model considers the customer's Tamara payment history, number and value of currently open Tamara plans, available credit relative to the cart value, phone trust score, and the specific purchase pattern.

Key difference from Tabby: Tamara shows customers their available credit. When a Tamara customer is declined because their available credit is insufficient, the Tamara app communicates this — customers can see their remaining Tamara credit and understand that they need to pay down existing plans before taking on new ones. This is structurally more transparent than Tabby's opaque limit model. However, it does not prevent declines; it just means declined customers are more likely to understand the reason without needing to contact the merchant.

New Tamara customers face the highest decline risk. Tamara's risk model starts conservatively for customers with no Tamara purchase history. First-time purchases are evaluated against a limited data set, which sometimes results in decline or approval for a smaller amount than the customer expected. Customers who have used Tamara consistently for 6–12 months with no late payments generally receive higher available credit and lower decline rates.

Merchants cannot access the reason for a Tamara decline or appeal it on behalf of customers. The decline is a decision between Tamara and the customer. Merchants should present alternative payment methods (Tabby, credit card, Apple Pay) as visible fallback options whenever Tamara is offered, to reduce abandonment when Tamara declines.

Fix Steps for Customers

  1. Check your available Tamara credit in the app: Open the Tamara app → My Account → check your available credit limit — if it is less than your cart value, you cannot proceed with this purchase until credit is freed up
  2. Pay down open Tamara plans: Tamara app → My Plans → review open installment plans → make early payments to reduce your outstanding balance and free up available credit
  3. Try a lower cart value: If your available Tamara credit is partially consumed, reducing the cart value below your remaining available amount may allow the transaction to proceed
  4. Wait for open plans to close: As existing Tamara plans complete and balances clear, your available credit increases — retry the purchase once a plan has finished paying off
  5. Contact Tamara customer support: Tamara support can provide more detail on why a specific transaction was declined and whether there is an action you can take — their in-app chat is typically faster than email support
  6. Use Tabby or a credit card as an alternative: If your Tamara credit is temporarily insufficient, Tabby (if available) offers a parallel MENA BNPL option — or complete with a credit card and use Tamara again once your limit refreshes
Merchant note: "Tamara not available" messages at checkout are almost always customer-level risk or credit decisions, not a problem with your Tamara integration. If some customers see this error and others can check out with Tamara successfully, your integration is working correctly. If all customers see the error, check your merchant account status, per-market activation, and API credentials.
3

Customer Credit Limit Reached

Customer-side

Tamara assigns each customer a credit limit — the maximum total outstanding balance they can carry across all active Tamara plans simultaneously. Unlike Tabby, Tamara makes this limit visible to customers in the app. When a new purchase would cause their total outstanding balance to exceed their credit limit, the transaction is declined. The customer can see their current outstanding balance and available credit in the Tamara app, which means they can diagnose the situation themselves without contacting the merchant.

Credit limits start conservatively and grow with payment history. New Tamara customers with no payment history receive a starting credit limit calibrated to their risk profile — typically lower than what an established Tamara user with 12+ months of on-time payments would receive. The limit grows as customers complete plans on time. Tamara communicates limit increases to customers via the app when they occur, and customers can request a limit review through the app after establishing a payment history track record.

Seasonal shopping events create predictable limit exhaustion. During Ramadan, Saudi National Day sales, and White Friday (Black Friday), GCC consumers shop heavily across multiple merchants simultaneously. By mid-season, many customers have open Tamara plans from three or four stores — and their available credit is substantially consumed. A new high-value purchase then gets declined not because of risk but simply because the credit math doesn't work. Unlike Tabby (where this is a hidden surprise), Tamara customers can see this happening in the app — but they still contact merchants asking why Tamara "isn't working."

Tamara's credit limit is shared across all markets. A customer using Tamara in both Saudi Arabia and UAE is drawing from the same credit pool regardless of which merchant or currency they are shopping with. Cross-border shopping between KSA and UAE (common for GCC residents who work in one country and shop in another) can consume the shared limit faster than single-market customers expect.

Fix Steps for Customers

  1. Check your Tamara credit limit and usage: Tamara app → Home or My Account → view your current available credit and total credit limit — this shows exactly how much you have left for new purchases
  2. Pay down the plan with the largest remaining balance: Tamara app → My Plans → select the plan with the highest outstanding balance → Pay Now — this frees the most credit relative to other plans
  3. Make early payments on multiple plans: You do not need to close a plan entirely to free credit — making an early payment on any plan reduces your outstanding balance and increases your available credit immediately
  4. Retry the purchase after paying down plans: After reducing your outstanding balance, the Tamara credit check at checkout will reflect the updated available amount — retry if your available credit now exceeds the cart value
  5. Request a credit limit review: Tamara app → My Account → request a credit limit increase — customers with 6+ months of on-time payment history are more likely to receive a limit increase review. Approval is not guaranteed and may take a few business days
  6. Split the purchase across two orders: If the cart is close to your available credit, consider splitting into two smaller orders across payment cycles — one now and one after a plan payment reduces your outstanding balance
Merchant note: Tamara's limit transparency means customers generally know when they have hit their credit ceiling. If a customer contacts you saying "Tamara says my limit is reached," direct them to the Tamara app to pay down existing plans and retry. Unlike Tabby limit issues (where customers are confused because the limit was never disclosed), Tamara customers can self-diagnose this failure mode with the app. Offering Tabby as a parallel MENA BNPL at checkout provides an alternative when Tamara credit is exhausted.
4

Absher or Identity Verification Failure

Customer-side

Tamara's identity verification for Saudi nationals is built on top of Absher — the Saudi government's national digital identity platform. When a Saudi citizen creates a Tamara account or completes KYC verification, the process validates their identity against Absher records using their National ID number (Huwiyya), full name, and registered mobile number. This integration is more authoritative than the manual document checks used by Western BNPL products — and it also creates a more specific failure mode with no equivalent in Afterpay, Klarna, or Zip.

Absher verification fails in four primary scenarios. First, the customer's Absher account is not active or was never fully set up — Absher requires registration and phone number linking that not all Saudi citizens have completed, particularly older customers or those who have been residing abroad. Second, the mobile number registered in Absher differs from the phone number the customer provides to Tamara — customers who changed phone numbers without updating Absher will see a verification mismatch. Third, the customer's National ID has recently been renewed and the updated data has not yet propagated to the Absher system — this self-resolves within 24–72 hours. Fourth, a name discrepancy between the National ID and what the customer enters at Tamara registration (Arabic name with different transliteration, or a name shortened by a syllable) causes the identity check to fail.

Expatriate residents in Saudi Arabia use Iqama verification instead of Absher. Non-Saudi residents verify identity using their Iqama (residency permit) number. Iqama verification fails when the Iqama is expired, when the name on the Iqama differs from what the customer enters (transliteration issues are frequent — "Mohammed" vs "Muhammad" vs "Mohamed" are all valid transliterations of the same Arabic name), or when the Iqama data has not propagated after a recent renewal. Expatriates who recently changed sponsors (common in Saudi Arabia's new sponsorship-flexibility system) may have a brief window where their Iqama data is in transition and verification fails.

UAE customers verify with Emirates ID. UAE nationals and residents verify through their Emirates ID number. Customers whose Emirates ID has expired cannot complete Tamara verification. Customers who recently received their Emirates ID after moving to the UAE (new residents) may find that the ID data takes 24–72 hours to be fully queryable by Tamara's verification partner. Customers with a valid passport but no Emirates ID yet cannot complete full Tamara verification in UAE — they may be limited to smaller purchase amounts or declined entirely until their Emirates ID is issued.

Fix Steps for Customers

  1. Activate and update your Absher account (Saudi nationals): Download the Absher app (available on iOS and Android, published by Saudi Arabia's Ministry of Interior); ensure your registered mobile number in Absher matches the number you use with Tamara; update your Absher profile if your phone number has changed
  2. Use your legal name exactly as on your Huwiyya or Iqama: Enter your full legal name precisely as it appears on your Saudi National ID or Iqama, including middle name if it appears there — no nicknames, abbreviated names, or alternate transliterations
  3. Check your Iqama expiry date (Saudi expatriates): An expired Iqama will fail Tamara verification — renew your Iqama before attempting to use Tamara; Tamara identity verification requires a currently valid Iqama
  4. Wait 72 hours after document renewals: If you recently renewed your National ID, Iqama, or Emirates ID, wait 2–3 business days for the updated data to propagate to Tamara's verification systems before retrying — data propagation delays are common after document renewals
  5. Ensure your Emirates ID is active (UAE customers): Check your Emirates ID expiry date — expired Emirates IDs fail Tamara identity verification; renew through GDRFA or an Amer service center before retrying Tamara checkout
  6. Contact Tamara support for manual verification: Tamara offers a manual document review pathway for customers who cannot pass automated identity verification — contact Tamara support via the app chat with your identity document and request manual review; manual verification typically takes 1–3 business days
  7. Use a credit card or Apple Pay while awaiting verification: Complete your purchase now using an alternative payment method, then resolve the Tamara identity verification through the app before your next purchase
Merchant note: Absher and Iqama verification failures are entirely between the customer and Tamara. You cannot verify customers on your behalf or access their identity documents. If new Saudi customers consistently report Tamara account creation failures, consider adding a note in your checkout directing first-time Tamara users to create and verify their account at tamara.co before checkout — completing the KYC step outside of the checkout flow reduces mid-checkout friction for first-time Tamara users who encounter an identity verification step they were not expecting.
5

Phone Number Verification / OTP Failure

Customer-side

Like Tabby, Tamara uses mobile phone number as the primary customer identifier and sends verification codes (OTP) via SMS. Tamara's OTP system is calibrated for local SIM cards in its operating markets: Saudi Arabia (+966, mobile numbers starting 05x), UAE (+971, numbers starting 05x), Kuwait (+965, numbers starting 05x or 06x), and Bahrain (+973, numbers starting 03x or 06x). Customers providing a phone number outside these formats encounter friction or outright OTP failure.

Expatriates with foreign SIM cards are the most affected group. The GCC has an exceptionally high proportion of expatriate residents — in UAE, expatriates represent approximately 88% of the total population; in Saudi Arabia, around 38%. Many long-term GCC residents still use a home-country mobile number (Indian +91, Pakistani +92, UK +44, Filipino +63, etc.) as their primary phone. Tamara cannot reliably deliver OTP to these numbers through its local Saudi/UAE/Kuwait/Bahrain SMS partnerships. These customers are systematically unable to complete new Tamara account creation or may face OTP delivery failures even with existing Tamara accounts.

Newly activated SIM cards may face elevated friction. Tamara's carrier data partnerships allow it to assess the trust level of a phone number — including how recently it was activated and its usage pattern. A SIM card activated within the last 30–60 days may have insufficient history for Tamara to approve full OTP-based verification without additional friction. Customers who recently bought a local SIM specifically to use Tamara may encounter higher verification requirements during this initial period.

Additional OTP failure causes: SMS delivery delays or failure during peak shopping periods when Tamara's SMS gateway is under load; the customer's carrier blocking international SMS (some GCC carrier settings block SMS from non-local originating numbers); the OTP expiring before the customer enters it (Tamara OTPs are short-lived — typically 2–5 minutes); or the customer using a VoIP, virtual, or WhatsApp Business number rather than a registered personal mobile SIM.

Fix Steps for Customers

  1. Use a local GCC mobile number: Tamara requires a Saudi (+966), UAE (+971), Kuwaiti (+965), or Bahraini (+973) mobile number for the respective market — a local SIM card is required for OTP delivery in each market
  2. Verify your number format is correct: Saudi Arabia: +966 5x xxx xxxx; UAE: +971 5x xxx xxxx; Kuwait: +965 xxxx xxxx; Bahrain: +973 xxxx xxxx — include the country code and confirm no typos
  3. Check that your number is not a VoIP or virtual number: Tamara does not accept WhatsApp Business numbers, VoIP numbers, or business landlines for OTP — use a registered personal mobile SIM
  4. Request a new OTP immediately if the first expires: Tamara OTPs expire quickly — if you did not receive the code within 60 seconds or it expired, tap "Resend OTP" before the verification session times out
  5. Check carrier SMS filtering: Some carriers in the GCC filter international SMS senders — ask your carrier or check SMS settings on your device for any spam or sender filtering that might block Tamara's OTP messages
  6. Contact Tamara customer support: If a valid local number consistently fails OTP delivery, Tamara support may be able to assist with an alternative verification pathway or investigate a delivery issue with your carrier
  7. Use Tabby or a credit card if OTP cannot be resolved: Tabby covers Saudi Arabia, UAE, Kuwait, and Qatar — if Tamara OTP cannot be resolved at checkout, Tabby is the most direct alternative MENA BNPL
Expat customer note: Tamara OTP is designed for locally-registered SIM cards in each GCC market. Expatriates using home-country phone numbers will typically be unable to create a Tamara account or may face persistent OTP issues. Acquiring a local Saudi, UAE, Kuwaiti, or Bahraini SIM card is the reliable fix — this is a structural constraint of Tamara's phone-first identity model, not a temporary error.
6

Order Value Below Tamara Minimum

Merchant + Customer

Tamara enforces minimum purchase thresholds in each market. If the cart value falls below the minimum, Tamara does not appear at checkout — with no error message explaining why. The minimum exists because splitting a very small amount into three equal Pay-in-3 installments would result in installment amounts so small as to be impractical for both Tamara and the customer.

Market-specific minimums: Saudi Arabia (SAR 100), UAE (AED 100), Kuwait (KWD 5), Bahrain (BHD varies — approximately BHD 10). These thresholds apply to the cart subtotal before shipping. Shipping costs are not typically counted toward the minimum in standard configurations. Merchants should note that Tamara's minimum for Saudi Arabia and UAE (100 SAR / 100 AED) is the same level as Tabby's minimum — this is not coincidental; both companies calibrated minimums for the GCC market context.

Merchants with product catalogs including lower-priced accessories, add-ons, beauty consumables, or small starter kits frequently encounter the minimum suppression issue. Tamara appears for their main product checkout but disappears for accessory or refill purchases. The customer, who successfully used Tamara for their main purchase, expects Tamara to be available for all future purchases and is confused when it is absent for a small cart.

Unlike some Western BNPL platforms (such as Afterpay's AU$10 minimum, which is rarely reached in retail contexts), Tamara's 100 SAR / 100 AED minimum is regularly reached by GCC stores that sell beauty, personal care, or lifestyle accessories with typical prices in the SAR 30–80 range. Merchants should review their product catalog against the Tamara minimum and proactively remove Tamara promotional messaging from product pages where standalone product prices cannot meet the threshold.

Fix Steps for Merchants

  1. Test checkout with cart values above the minimum: If Tamara disappears for small orders, test with cart values above SAR 100 (KSA), AED 100 (UAE), KWD 5 (Kuwait), or BHD 10 (Bahrain) — confirm Tamara reappears above the threshold and disappears below it
  2. Remove Tamara widgets from product pages below the minimum: Do not show "Pay in 3 with Tamara" widgets on product pages where the standalone product price is below the market minimum — customers who see the widget expect Tamara to work at checkout
  3. Add cart value guidance near the Tamara widget: Consider a message such as "Add SAR X more to your cart to pay in 3 with Tamara" — this converts minimum-adjacent carts by encouraging small add-on purchases rather than losing the sale to abandonment
  4. Configure the Tamara widget's minimum display amount: The Tamara Shopify widget has configuration options to set a minimum price below which the widget is hidden on product pages — configure this to match the market minimum to prevent false Tamara availability signals for low-price products
  5. Bundle low-price items to cross the threshold: For products priced at SAR 70–99 or AED 70–99, consider bundle pricing, gift wrapping, or extended warranty add-ons that bring the cart above the Tamara minimum while adding genuine value
Shopify widget tip: Both Tamara and Tabby offer Shopify product page widgets that can display installment amounts. Configure the minimum display price on both widgets to match the respective market minimum (100 SAR / 100 AED for both). This ensures customers only see BNPL promotional messaging on products that will actually qualify at checkout, preventing the most common source of "Tamara was advertised but didn't appear" support tickets.
7

Store Currency or Shopify Markets Configuration Mismatch

Merchant-side

Tamara operates exclusively in SAR (Saudi Arabia), AED (UAE), KWD (Kuwait), and BHD (Bahrain). It will not appear at checkout unless the active transaction currency is one of these four, and the customer's shipping address is in the corresponding country. Both conditions must be true simultaneously — a Saudi Arabia shipping address with a USD transaction currency will suppress Tamara, and a SAR-denominated store that ships globally will suppress Tamara for non-Saudi addresses.

Shopify Markets currency configuration is the most common source of this failure. Many GCC-based Shopify merchants started as single-country stores (SAR or AED) and later expanded internationally using Shopify Markets. During this expansion, merchants commonly set USD as the global base currency with local currencies presented as runtime conversions for display. This satisfies international customers but suppresses Tamara for existing local customers — Tamara requires the actual transaction to settle in SAR, AED, KWD, or BHD, not a USD transaction displayed as local currency.

The Shopify Markets fix is to configure per-market fixed checkout currencies: Saudi Arabia market → fixed SAR, UAE market → fixed AED, Kuwait market → fixed KWD, Bahrain market → fixed BHD. A "fixed" checkout currency means Shopify processes the transaction in that local currency. A "currency conversion" or "display only" setting processes the transaction in the store base currency (often USD) and only converts for display — which Tamara does not accept.

Merchants with both MENA and global markets must manage separate currency configurations. The same Shopify Markets setting that enables Tamara for Saudi customers (fixed SAR) will mean international customers pay in SAR — which is usually not desired. The solution is to configure international markets with USD (or their relevant currency) while configuring MENA markets with fixed local currencies. Each market has independent currency settings in Shopify Admin → Settings → Markets.

Fix for Merchants

  1. Set per-market fixed currencies in Shopify Markets: Shopify Admin → Settings → Markets → Saudi Arabia → set SAR as the fixed checkout currency (not a conversion from USD); repeat for UAE (AED), Kuwait (KWD), Bahrain (BHD)
  2. Confirm "fixed" vs "auto-convert" setting: In each MENA market's currency settings, confirm the currency type is "fixed" — not "auto-convert" or "display only" — Tamara is suppressed by auto-convert even when the displayed currency is SAR or AED
  3. Enable Tamara per market in the Tamara merchant portal: In your Tamara merchant dashboard, confirm Tamara is active for each market (KSA, UAE, Kuwait, Bahrain) separately — market-specific activation is required and independent
  4. Test each market independently: Add a cart, enter a Saudi Arabia shipping address with SAR fixed currency → confirm Tamara appears; repeat for UAE (AED), Kuwait (KWD), Bahrain (BHD); test each market separately to confirm market-specific currency configurations are working
  5. Re-test after any Shopify Markets changes: Whenever you add a new market, change the store base currency, or update Shopify Markets currency settings, re-test Tamara for all four markets — currency configuration changes can suppress MENA alternative payment methods without a visible error in Shopify Admin
  6. Check Tamara app market assignments: In the Tamara Shopify app settings, confirm the app is scoped to the correct markets — some versions of the Tamara app require explicit market assignment; an app configured only for the primary market may not activate for secondary Shopify Markets
USD-priced stores: Shopify stores that price globally in USD cannot offer Tamara. Tamara's merchant agreement and payment infrastructure requires SAR, AED, KWD, or BHD transaction settlement. If your store sells in USD for all markets, Tamara will not be available for your MENA customers regardless of how the app is configured. Offer credit card, Apple Pay, or Tabby instead — and consider whether configuring fixed local currencies for MENA markets is worth the additional payment infrastructure complexity given your MENA sales volume.
