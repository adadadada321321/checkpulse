B2B & Wholesale Checkout Errors

Shopify B2B Checkout Not Working — Wholesale, Company Accounts & Net Terms Errors

Shopify B2B checkout has more failure points than consumer checkout. Company accounts, custom price lists, net payment terms, draft order links, and tax exemptions each add a layer — and each can break silently without any error appearing on your dashboard.

Root Cause

Why Shopify B2B Checkout Breaks Differently

Shopify's native B2B functionality (available on Shopify Plus since 2022) runs on a completely different checkout architecture than consumer checkout. A B2B checkout session must resolve six layers in sequence before a buyer can complete their purchase — and a failure in any one layer presents as a confusing error or silent block to the customer, not a clear "checkout is down" signal.

Layer 1
Company Account Authentication
B2B buyers must be logged in with their company account — not a personal customer account. If the company account login fails, session expires, or the customer accidentally logs in with a personal account, they see retail pricing and lose access to B2B features.
Layer 2
Price List Resolution
Shopify must resolve which price list applies to this company account at checkout. If no price list is assigned, the product's retail price is used. If the price list doesn't cover all products in the cart, mixed pricing occurs.
Layer 3
Payment Terms Eligibility
Net payment terms (Net 30/60/90) require Shopify Plus, Shopify Payments, and explicit net terms enabled on the company account — all three simultaneously. If any condition is unmet, net terms silently disappear from checkout.
Layer 4
Tax Exemption Application
B2B company accounts can be configured with tax exemptions. Shopify must apply these at checkout based on the shipping address jurisdiction. A misconfigured exemption or a recent address change can cause the wrong tax to be charged.
Layer 5
Quantity Rules & Minimums
B2B price lists can enforce minimum order quantities, quantity increments, and case pack rules. When a buyer's cart doesn't meet these rules, checkout is blocked — but the error message is often unclear about why.
Layer 6
Payment Method & Gateway
For immediate payment, Shopify Payments handles B2B transactions. For net terms orders, Shopify generates an invoice for offline payment. Gateway configuration errors surface here — especially if Shopify Payments isn't fully verified.
Why standard monitoring misses B2B checkout failures

Standard uptime tools ping your storefront URL or even simulate a consumer checkout. They cannot log in as a B2B company account buyer, resolve price list pricing, verify net terms availability, or detect tax exemption failures. B2B checkout failures require B2B-specific simulation — they're invisible to any tool that doesn't authenticate as a company account.

Failure Modes

8 Common Shopify B2B Checkout Failures

Failure 1 Company account not recognized — buyer sees retail pricing

The B2B buyer reaches checkout and sees full retail pricing instead of their negotiated rates. This happens when they're not logged in with their company account, when their company account contact lacks the Ordering permission, or when the company account was recently deactivated or modified. It can also occur when a buyer creates a personal customer account instead of using their company account invitation link.

Fix: Go to Admin → Customers → Companies → [Company Name] → Contacts and verify: (1) the buyer's email is listed as a contact, (2) the contact has the "Ordering" location permission enabled, (3) the company account is not paused. Send the buyer a new login invitation via the company account page if their session was lost. If the account was recently migrated from a third-party wholesale app, company account permissions may need to be re-granted manually.
Failure 2 Custom B2B pricing not applying at checkout

The buyer is logged in but sees retail prices at checkout. Price list assignments can fail silently when: a price list was edited and not re-saved correctly, a product was added to the catalog after the price list was created (so it's not covered), the price list currency doesn't match the store's active market currency, or the price list was accidentally unassigned from the company during an admin bulk edit.

Fix: Admin → Customers → Companies → [Company] → Price Lists — verify a price list is attached. Open the price list and check: (1) the product in question is included (not filtered out by collection or product tag), (2) the pricing rule applies (percentage discount, fixed price, or volume tier), (3) the currency matches. After any price list change, test with a fresh browser session as the B2B buyer — cached checkout sessions can hold stale pricing.
Failure 3 Net payment terms missing from checkout

The buyer reaches the payment step and net terms (Net 30, Net 60, Net 90) don't appear — only immediate payment options are shown. Net terms require four conditions to be met simultaneously: Shopify Plus plan, Shopify Payments as the active gateway, net terms explicitly enabled on the company account, and no outstanding overdue invoices on the account. If any one condition fails, net terms silently disappear with no error shown.

Fix: Check each condition in order. (1) Confirm Shopify Plus: Admin → Settings → Plan. (2) Confirm Shopify Payments: Admin → Settings → Payments → Shopify Payments must be active, not a third-party gateway. (3) Enable net terms on the company: Admin → Customers → Companies → [Company] → Payment Terms → assign Net 30/60/90. (4) Check outstanding invoices: Admin → Customers → Companies → [Company] → Orders — filter by "Payment pending." Clear any overdue amounts before net terms will re-appear.
Failure 4 Draft order checkout link expired or invalid

The buyer clicks the checkout link from a draft order invoice email and gets an error ("This link has expired," "Order not found," or a blank page). Draft order checkout links expire after approximately 72 hours by default. Any edit to the draft order after the link was sent — adding a product, changing quantity, applying a discount — also invalidates the previous link. A buyer who starts checkout from the link but doesn't complete it can sometimes lock the draft temporarily.

Fix: In Admin → Orders → Drafts, locate the draft and click "Send invoice" to generate and email a fresh checkout link. If the draft was edited, always resend the invoice after edits — never ask the buyer to use an old link. For high-value or time-sensitive orders, note the 72-hour window in the invoice email. If you need longer-lived draft orders, set a custom expiration in Shopify Admin → Settings → Checkout → Draft orders.
Failure 5 Tax exemption not applied — wrong tax charged

A B2B buyer who is tax-exempt (e.g., a registered reseller or nonprofit) is charged sales tax at checkout. Shopify B2B tax exemptions are configured per-company and applied based on the shipping address jurisdiction. Common failure modes: the exemption wasn't configured on the company account, the exemption is set for one state/province but the order ships to a different jurisdiction, or a recent Shopify Markets configuration change affected tax overrides.

Fix: Admin → Customers → Companies → [Company] → Tax Exemptions. Verify the correct exemption type is applied (e.g., "Reseller" for B2B resellers) and that it covers the correct jurisdictions. If the buyer ships to multiple states, confirm the exemption covers all shipping destinations. Note: Shopify B2B tax exemptions apply at the company level, not per-order — a buyer cannot override tax status at checkout themselves.
Failure 6 Quantity rules blocking checkout silently

The buyer adds items to cart and reaches checkout, but can't proceed or sees an error about quantities. B2B price lists support quantity rules: minimum order quantities (MOQ), quantity increments (e.g., must buy in multiples of 6), and case pack enforcement. When a buyer's cart doesn't meet these rules, Shopify blocks checkout — but the error message is often a generic "There's a problem with your order" without specifying which product or rule is the issue.

Fix: Admin → Products → [Product] → B2B Price Lists — check if quantity rules are configured. Communicate MOQ requirements clearly on product pages and in the cart. If buyers are hitting quantity errors without understanding why, add a cart validation app or use Shopify's theme customization to display B2B quantity requirements on product pages. For the immediate error: identify which product in the cart has a quantity rule and have the buyer adjust their order quantity to meet the minimum or increment.
Failure 7 B2B store login loop — customer can't authenticate

The buyer tries to log in to access B2B pricing but gets stuck in a login loop, receives "Account not found," or is redirected to a retail-facing login page instead of the B2B portal. This happens when: the store is using a password-protected B2B-only storefront that's improperly configured, the Shopify B2B customer accounts setting redirects to the wrong URL, or the buyer tries to log in through a bookmark that points to the consumer login instead of the B2B company account login.

Fix: Verify the B2B login URL is correct — the Shopify B2B company account login is at /a/login or your custom B2B store domain, not the standard Shopify customer login at /account/login. In Admin → Settings → Customer Accounts, confirm "New customer accounts" is enabled for B2B (required for company accounts). Send the buyer a direct login link generated from Admin → Customers → Companies → [Company] → [Contact] → "Send login link" to bypass the login form entirely.
Failure 8 B2B features unavailable — store on wrong Shopify plan

After a Shopify plan change or trial expiration, B2B features silently stop working. Shopify's native B2B (company accounts, price lists, net terms) requires Shopify Plus. If a store downgrades from Plus to a lower plan — even temporarily — company accounts become inaccessible, custom pricing stops applying, and net terms disappear. This failure is particularly insidious because the storefront still works for consumer orders, making the problem easy to miss.

Fix: Admin → Settings → Plan — confirm the store is on Shopify Plus. If a plan downgrade occurred, upgrading back to Plus restores B2B functionality, but may require re-verifying company account settings. After any plan change, run a full B2B checkout test immediately: log in as a test company account, add a product, verify pricing applies, and attempt checkout through to the payment step.

Testing Protocol

B2B Checkout Test Checklist

Run this after any theme update, app change, plan change, or Shopify platform update. Use a dedicated test company account with a low-value product.

  • Log in as the test B2B company account (not a personal customer account)
  • Verify the product page shows company-specific pricing, not retail price
  • Add to cart — verify cart reflects B2B price and any quantity rules are shown
  • Proceed to checkout — verify company name and billing address pre-populate
  • At payment step: verify net terms appear (if configured) alongside immediate payment
  • Verify tax exemption is applied (no tax line, or correct exempt amount)
  • Complete checkout — verify confirmation email arrives within 2 minutes
  • Log in to the B2B portal — verify the order appears under the company account
  • Test with a draft order invoice: create draft → send invoice → complete checkout from link
  • Verify draft link works within 1 hour of sending (test expiry by waiting 73h separately)

FAQ

Shopify B2B Checkout — Common Questions

Why is Shopify B2B checkout not working for my wholesale customers?
Shopify B2B checkout requires several layers to work correctly simultaneously — and each is a distinct failure point. The most common causes are: (1) The customer isn't logged into their Shopify company account — B2B pricing, net terms, and tax exemptions are all tied to authenticated company account sessions. If the login fails or the session expires, customers see retail pricing or get blocked at checkout. Check Admin → Customers → Companies and verify the customer's company account is active and their contact has the Ordering permission. (2) The price list assigned to the company isn't applying — this happens when price list rules don't cover all products, or when a recent update didn't propagate. (3) Net payment terms aren't appearing — Shopify B2B net terms only appear when Shopify Payments is the active gateway AND the customer's company account has net terms enabled. (4) The B2B store is on a Shopify plan that doesn't include B2B features — Shopify B2B requires Shopify Plus.
Why is Shopify B2B custom pricing not showing at checkout?
When B2B custom pricing isn't appearing at checkout, the most common causes are: (1) The customer isn't logged in with their company account — B2B prices are session-scoped. (2) The price list isn't assigned to the company — verify in Admin → Customers → Companies → [Company] → Price Lists. (3) The price list exists but doesn't include the specific product being purchased — price lists can be scoped to specific products or collections. (4) A price list update was made and the customer is viewing a cached version — have them clear cookies and log in fresh. (5) The store has multiple currencies and the price list currency doesn't match the buyer's selected currency.
Why are net payment terms not appearing at Shopify B2B checkout?
Shopify B2B net payment terms have strict requirements that must all be met simultaneously: (1) Your store must be on Shopify Plus. (2) Shopify Payments must be your active payment gateway — third-party gateways do not support Shopify net terms. (3) The customer must be logged in with a company account that has net terms enabled — configure in Admin → Customers → Companies → [Company] → Payment Terms. (4) The customer's company account must not have any outstanding overdue invoices. If all conditions are met and net terms still don't appear, contact Shopify Plus support — this is sometimes a Shopify Payments account verification issue.
Why is the Shopify draft order checkout link not working?
Draft order checkout links expire and can fail in several ways: (1) The checkout link expired — draft links typically expire after 72 hours. Resend the invoice from Admin → Orders → Drafts to generate a fresh link. (2) The draft was edited after the link was sent — any edit to a draft order invalidates the existing checkout link. Always resend the invoice after edits. (3) The buyer's B2B login session doesn't match the company account the draft was created under. (4) A product in the draft became unavailable (inventory hit zero, product unpublished). Check each line item is still purchasable.
How do I monitor Shopify B2B checkout automatically?
Shopify B2B checkout is harder to monitor than consumer checkout because it requires an authenticated company account session. Standard uptime tools that ping your homepage won't detect B2B-specific failures — they can't log in as a B2B buyer or verify company pricing. The most practical approach: (1) CheckPulse for automated checkout flow monitoring — it simulates the full checkout process every 30 minutes and alerts you when any step fails, catching payment method failures and checkout URL issues that affect all customers including B2B; (2) A weekly manual B2B checkout test using a test company account; (3) Monitor your average B2B order volume — a sudden drop often indicates a B2B checkout failure before support tickets arrive.
