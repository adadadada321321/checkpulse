Shopify Klarna Not Working — Not Showing at Checkout, Payment Declined (2026)

Klarna not appearing at checkout, buyers getting declined silently, Klarna showing for some orders but not others, or two Klarna integrations conflicting — here are all 8 failure modes for Klarna breaking on Shopify, with exact diagnostic steps and fixes for each.

Updated June 2026 8 failure modes App Not Configured · Country Unavailable · Order Amount Limits · Buyer Declined · Dual Integration Conflict · Account Suspended · Theme Block Missing · Currency Mismatch

Klarna is one of the most popular buy now, pay later (BNPL) options on Shopify — offering buyers the ability to split purchases into installments or pay later without interest. With over 150 million active Klarna users globally, it can materially lift conversion for merchants in supported markets, particularly for higher-ticket items where buyers prefer not to commit the full amount upfront.

When Klarna stops working, the failure is almost always invisible to the merchant. Klarna simply doesn't appear at checkout, or buyers silently receive a decline from Klarna's real-time risk assessment with no explanation. There is no admin notification, no alert email, and the buyer sees only a vague "not available" message. Revenue disappears without a trace.

Klarna failures on Shopify fall into eight distinct categories, each requiring a different diagnosis and fix. Critically, Klarna on Shopify can be configured through two completely separate paths — native Shopify Payments or a standalone Klarna app — and confusion between these two integrations is one of the most common sources of silent failure.

Quick Diagnostic: Which Klarna Integration Are You Using?
Before diagnosing any Klarna failure, determine which integration path you have: (1) Shopify Payments native — go to Admin → Settings → Payments → Shopify Payments → Manage. If you see "Installments" or "Klarna" listed here, this is your integration. (2) Standalone Klarna app — go to Admin → Apps and search for "Klarna." If you have the Klarna On-Site Messaging or Klarna Payments app installed, this is your integration. (3) Both simultaneously — if both are active, you have a conflict that must be resolved. Only one integration should be active at a time.

Failure Mode 1: Klarna App Not Installed or App Block Not Added to Checkout

Merchants using the standalone Klarna app (rather than native Shopify Payments) face a two-step setup that is commonly left incomplete. Installing the Klarna app from the Shopify App Store does not automatically make Klarna appear at checkout — a checkout extension app block must also be added to your store's checkout theme. Without this block, Klarna is installed but invisible.

The two steps merchants commonly leave incomplete

  • Step 1 — Install the Klarna app and connect credentials: Search for "Klarna" in the Shopify App Store and install the Klarna Payments or Klarna On-Site Messaging app. During setup, you will be asked to enter your Klarna Merchant ID and API credentials from your Klarna Merchant Portal. Without valid credentials, the integration cannot activate.
  • Step 2 — Add the Klarna app block to your checkout theme: After installing and connecting the app, go to Shopify Admin → Settings → Checkout → Customize. In the checkout editor, find the "Payment" section and add the Klarna app block. This block is what renders Klarna in the payment step. Without it, the app is installed and connected but Klarna never appears to buyers.

How to verify the checkout block is active

  1. Go to Shopify Admin → Settings → Checkout → Customize (opens the checkout editor)
  2. Navigate to the Payment step in the checkout editor
  3. Check whether the Klarna app block is present in the payment section — it should be listed under "App blocks" in the left panel
  4. If the block is not present, click the "+" icon in the payment section and search for "Klarna" to add it
  5. Save and publish the checkout customization
  6. Run a test checkout with an order above $50 in an incognito window — Klarna should appear in the payment options
Theme Updates Can Remove App Blocks
When you update or switch your Shopify checkout theme, previously added app blocks — including Klarna — are not automatically carried over. After any checkout theme update or switch, re-verify that the Klarna app block is still present in the payment section. This is the most common reason Klarna "suddenly disappeared" after a theme change.

Failure Mode 2: Klarna Not Available in the Merchant's Country or Buyer's Region

Klarna operates in a specific set of countries and will not appear for buyers or merchants outside those markets. Geographic availability applies to both the merchant (your store's registered country) and the buyer (the shipping address on the order). If either falls outside Klarna's supported regions, Klarna will not be offered as a payment option.

Countries where Klarna is available for merchants

Region Countries Klarna Available?
North America United States, Canada Yes
Europe (Western) UK, Germany, Sweden, Norway, Denmark, Finland, Netherlands, Belgium, Switzerland, Austria, France, Italy, Spain, Ireland, Portugal Yes
Europe (Eastern) Poland, Czech Republic Yes (limited products)
Asia Pacific Australia, New Zealand Yes
Middle East & Africa All countries No
Latin America All countries No
South & Southeast Asia All countries (including India) No

Note that Klarna availability also depends on the buyer's shipping country, not just the merchant's store country. A US merchant selling internationally will not show Klarna to buyers shipping to unsupported markets, regardless of how the store is configured.

Klarna Has Separate Merchant Accounts Per Region
Unlike some payment providers with a single global merchant account, Klarna requires separate merchant accounts for different regional markets. A Klarna merchant account registered in the US cannot process Klarna payments for buyers in Germany. Merchants who sell cross-border in supported regions need Klarna merchant accounts in each target market. This is a common source of "Klarna works for US buyers but not European buyers" complaints.

Diagnosing geographic availability issues

  1. Confirm your Klarna merchant account is registered in the same country as your primary buyer market
  2. Test checkout using a VPN set to the buyer's country — if Klarna appears with the VPN on, geographic restriction is not the issue; if it disappears, the buyer's location is the cause
  3. In the Klarna Merchant Portal, verify which markets your Klarna account is approved for and whether those match your store's shipping destinations
  4. For merchants selling to buyers in multiple supported Klarna countries, you may need separate Klarna merchant accounts for each region

Failure Mode 3: Order Amount Outside Klarna's Minimum or Maximum Limits

Klarna does not appear as a payment option for orders that fall outside its purchase amount thresholds. Every Klarna market has a minimum order amount below which Klarna will not be offered, and an effective maximum based on the buyer's account credit limit. These limits are enforced silently — Klarna simply doesn't render for qualifying orders without any explanation shown to the buyer.

Typical Klarna order amount limits by market

Market Minimum Order Maximum Typical Limit
United States ~$35 $10,000 (buyer-dependent)
United Kingdom ~£25 £1,000–£5,000 (buyer-dependent)
Germany ~€20 €5,000 (buyer-dependent)
Australia ~A$35 A$2,000 (buyer-dependent)
Sweden ~kr200 kr50,000 (buyer-dependent)

The maximum limit is not a fixed threshold — it depends on Klarna's real-time credit assessment of the individual buyer. A buyer who can use Klarna for a $200 order may not be approved for the same amount on a different day, or may have a higher limit on a smaller order.

Klarna Disappears for Low-Value Orders Without Warning
If your store sells products below Klarna's minimum purchase threshold, Klarna will not appear at all for those orders. There is no message explaining why — buyers simply don't see Klarna as an option. If you notice Klarna is absent only on certain orders, check whether those orders are below the minimum threshold for your market (~$35 in the US). This is not an error; it is expected behavior.

What to do about order amount limits

  1. Log into the Klarna Merchant Portal → Settings → Ordering rules — confirm the minimum order amount configured for your account
  2. If you sell low-value products, consider whether Klarna is a meaningful payment option for your catalog at all; alternative BNPL providers may have lower minimums
  3. For high-value orders where Klarna is disappearing, the buyer's individual credit limit with Klarna may be the constraint — this is not something the merchant can override or investigate directly
  4. Ensure your checkout shows alternative payment options prominently so buyers are not stranded when Klarna is unavailable for their specific order

Failure Mode 4: Buyer Declined by Klarna's Real-Time Credit Assessment

Klarna performs a real-time credit and risk assessment on every transaction attempt. This assessment is invisible to merchants and returns only a binary outcome — approved or declined — with no reason code shared with the merchant. Buyers who are declined see only a generic "Klarna is not available for this purchase" message. From the merchant's view, it looks like Klarna disappeared or failed, when in reality Klarna is working correctly and has declined this specific buyer for this specific transaction.

What triggers a Klarna buyer decline

  • Existing unpaid Klarna balance: Buyers with overdue payments on previous Klarna orders — regardless of which merchant they purchased from — will be declined for new Klarna transactions until the overdue balance is resolved. This is the single most common reason buyers report "Klarna stopped working."
  • Klarna's risk scoring: Klarna runs a proprietary risk model on each transaction using factors including purchase history, order value, product category, shipping address, email, and device signals. Buyers who trigger risk flags are declined without explanation. A buyer who has used Klarna successfully before can be declined on a new purchase if risk signals change.
  • New buyer with no Klarna history: First-time Klarna users start with a limited credit profile. Some transaction types (particularly higher-value pay-in-full-later options) may not be available until a buyer establishes a positive Klarna payment history.
  • Shipping to a non-billing-address country: Some Klarna products require the shipping address to match the billing country. Buyers shipping internationally from a Klarna-supported country to a non-supported country may be declined.
  • Product category restrictions: Certain product categories — including some regulated goods, digital downloads only, and subscription products — may be restricted by Klarna based on your merchant account configuration or Klarna's risk policies for that category.
Merchant Cannot Override Klarna Buyer Declines
When Klarna declines a buyer, there is nothing the merchant can do to approve the transaction. Klarna's credit decision is final for that attempt. The buyer must resolve any outstanding Klarna balance, try again later, or use a different payment method. Contacting Klarna merchant support about a specific buyer decline will not result in an override. Ensure your checkout always has a visible alternative payment option for buyers who cannot use Klarna.

What merchants can do

  1. Ensure your checkout has a clear, visible alternative payment option immediately after the Klarna option — buyers who are declined need a fast path to completing the purchase another way
  2. If a buyer contacts you saying "Klarna isn't working," advise them to check their Klarna account for any overdue balances at klarna.com or in the Klarna app
  3. Do not try to re-process the same Klarna transaction repeatedly — multiple declined attempts can further reduce approval probability for that buyer
  4. Consider whether your product catalog and price points are well-suited for Klarna's risk model — very high-value items or categories with high return rates tend to have lower Klarna approval rates

Failure Mode 5: Conflict Between Native Shopify Payments Klarna and the Standalone Klarna App

Klarna on Shopify can be added through two completely separate integration paths: natively through Shopify Payments (where Klarna is built in as an installment option) or through a standalone Klarna app installed from the Shopify App Store. Running both integrations simultaneously creates a conflict that causes unpredictable behavior — Klarna may appear twice, not appear at all, or appear and fail at different rates depending on which integration is processing a given transaction.

The two Klarna integration paths on Shopify

Property Klarna via Shopify Payments Standalone Klarna App
Where to configure Admin → Settings → Payments → Shopify Payments → Manage → Installments Shopify App Store → install Klarna app → Klarna Merchant Portal credentials
Requires Shopify Payments Yes — only available with Shopify Payments as primary gateway No — works with any payment gateway
Requires separate Klarna account No — uses Shopify's Klarna agreement Yes — requires your own Klarna merchant account
Checkout block required No — appears automatically when enabled Yes — must add Klarna app block to checkout theme
Who to contact for support Shopify Support Klarna Merchant Support
Having Both Active Creates Silent Conflicts
If you have Klarna enabled through Shopify Payments AND have the Klarna app installed with credentials connected, both integrations will attempt to render at checkout. You may see Klarna appearing twice, or Klarna appearing then disappearing, or checkout errors on Klarna payment attempts. To resolve: choose one integration path and disable the other. For most merchants on Shopify Payments, the native integration is simpler — disable the Klarna app or disconnect its credentials. For merchants not on Shopify Payments, use only the Klarna app.

How to identify and resolve the conflict

  1. Go to Admin → Settings → Payments — check whether Klarna or "Installments" appears under Shopify Payments
  2. Go to Admin → Apps — check whether a Klarna app is installed
  3. If both are active, decide which integration to keep based on your payment gateway setup
  4. If you are on Shopify Payments: disable the Klarna app by disconnecting its credentials or uninstalling it; keep the native integration enabled in Shopify Payments settings
  5. If you are on a third-party gateway: the native Shopify Payments Klarna integration is not available to you; use only the standalone Klarna app
  6. After disabling one integration, clear checkout theme app blocks and re-add only the block for the integration you are keeping, then test with a live order above the minimum threshold

Failure Mode 6: Klarna Merchant Account Suspended or Under Review

Klarna reviews merchant accounts on an ongoing basis and can suspend Klarna payment processing if the account raises compliance flags, has an elevated dispute rate, or fails to respond to documentation requests. When a merchant account is suspended, Klarna stops appearing for all buyers — but no notification is sent to the Shopify store. The merchant discovers this only when buyers start reporting that Klarna is unavailable.

What triggers a Klarna merchant account review

  • High dispute or return rate: If buyers frequently dispute Klarna transactions or request refunds through Klarna's buyer protection, Klarna's merchant risk team may restrict the account while investigating. Klarna tracks dispute rates by merchant and flags accounts that exceed acceptable thresholds.
  • Compliance document request: Klarna periodically requests business verification documents — business registration, bank account details, tax identification. If these are not submitted within the required timeframe, the account may be restricted.
  • Product category policy violation: If your store sells products that Klarna has restricted — certain regulated goods, prohibited categories, or products that violate Klarna's merchant terms — the account may be suspended.
  • Bank account or payout issue: If Klarna cannot successfully deposit payouts to the connected bank account (account closed, routing error, bank rejection), the account may be placed on hold.
  • Delayed shipping or fulfillment complaints: Klarna receives buyer complaints about merchants who take payment but fail to ship. Accounts with a pattern of delayed fulfillment may be reviewed and restricted.

How to check Klarna merchant account status

  1. Log into the Klarna Merchant Portal at merchants.klarna.com
  2. Check the dashboard for any alerts, restriction notices, or action required items
  3. Go to Settings → Account and check whether the account status shows as "Active" or any restricted state
  4. Check the email address registered with your Klarna merchant account for any messages from Klarna — compliance requests often arrive by email and are easy to miss
  5. Check your Klarna payout reports for any held or failed payouts — payment holds often precede account restrictions
  6. If restricted, respond to any open Klarna requests as quickly as possible — compliance reviews do not resolve automatically and extended delays lead to permanent account suspension

Failure Mode 7: Theme or Checkout Extension Blocking Klarna From Rendering

The standalone Klarna app renders through a checkout extension that must coexist with other checkout customizations. If another checkout extension, a theme customization, or a Content Security Policy (CSP) header interferes with the Klarna extension, the Klarna option will not appear at checkout. This failure is distinct from the app block not being added — it occurs when the block is present but something else prevents it from rendering.

Common sources of Klarna rendering failures

  • Another checkout extension taking priority: Shopify's checkout extensibility allows multiple extensions in the payment section. If extension ordering or priority rules prevent the Klarna extension from rendering in its expected position, it may be suppressed. This is more common in stores with many active checkout extensions.
  • Content Security Policy blocking Klarna scripts: Klarna's checkout extension loads JavaScript from Klarna's CDN. If a security app or theme configuration has set a CSP that does not whitelist Klarna's script domains, the browser will block the Klarna script and the payment option will not render. Look for CSP errors in browser DevTools → Console mentioning "klarna.com" or "klarnaservices.com".
  • Theme JavaScript error preventing payment step from rendering: A JavaScript error in your theme's checkout customization code that runs before or during payment step rendering can prevent all checkout extensions from initializing, including Klarna.
  • Custom checkout script hiding payment options conditionally: Some merchants add custom JavaScript to checkout to conditionally show or hide payment options based on cart contents or customer attributes. If this logic incorrectly targets or excludes Klarna, it can suppress Klarna's rendering.

How to isolate a rendering conflict

  1. Open browser DevTools → Console on the checkout payment step — look for errors mentioning "klarna", "CSP", "Content Security Policy", or "Refused to load script"
  2. Open DevTools → Network tab → filter by "klarna" — confirm that Klarna's JavaScript is being fetched and returns 200 status, not blocked or 404
  3. Test checkout in a fresh incognito window with all browser extensions disabled — if Klarna appears, a browser extension is blocking it in your normal testing environment
  4. Go to Admin → Settings → Checkout → Customize → temporarily disable other checkout extensions in the payment section — if Klarna reappears, another extension is causing a conflict
  5. Temporarily switch to Shopify's default Dawn checkout theme — if Klarna appears, the conflict is in your custom theme's JavaScript
  6. If CSP is the issue, update your CSP header to include: script-src *.klarna.com *.klarnaservices.com and connect-src *.klarna.com *.klarnaservices.com
Extension Ordering Matters in Shopify Checkout
In Shopify's extensibility editor, the order of app blocks in the payment section affects how they render. If the Klarna app block is positioned after another extension that fails to load, it may not render either. Try reordering the payment section blocks so Klarna is first, and test whether it appears. Extension ordering is often overlooked during initial setup.

Failure Mode 8: Currency or Market Mismatch Between Store and Klarna Configuration

Klarna requires the checkout currency to match the currency associated with the buyer's Klarna market. If your Shopify store displays prices in a currency that doesn't match the Klarna market your account is configured for, Klarna will not be offered as a payment option — even if the buyer's country is supported by Klarna. This is particularly common for stores using multi-currency checkout or Shopify Markets.

How currency mismatch causes Klarna to disappear

  • Store displays USD for European buyers: A merchant with a Klarna EU account who has their store set to display prices in USD for all buyers — rather than converting to local currencies — will find Klarna unavailable for European buyers. Klarna EU expects EUR, GBP, SEK, NOK, or DKK depending on the buyer's country, not USD.
  • Shopify Markets currency override: Merchants using Shopify Markets who have configured currency overrides that don't align with Klarna's market requirements will cause Klarna to fail silently for buyers in those markets.
  • Currency conversion app rounding to unsupported currency: Third-party currency conversion apps that convert prices to a currency not supported by Klarna for that market will suppress Klarna as a payment option.
  • Wrong Klarna market credentials in app settings: If the standalone Klarna app is configured with credentials from a Klarna account registered in country A, but the buyer is in country B (even if both are supported by Klarna), the currency mismatch between the credentials and the buyer's currency will cause Klarna to fail.

How to resolve currency and market mismatches

  1. Log into the Klarna Merchant Portal and confirm which markets (countries) and currencies your Klarna account is approved for
  2. Go to Shopify Admin → Settings → Markets — confirm that your market currency settings align with Klarna's approved currencies for each market
  3. For buyers in the EU, your checkout should display the local currency (EUR, GBP, SEK, etc.) — not USD — for Klarna to appear
  4. If you use a third-party currency conversion app, confirm it converts to Klarna-supported currencies for each buyer's market, not a fixed currency
  5. If you are using the standalone Klarna app, confirm the API credentials in the app settings correspond to the Klarna merchant account for the correct regional market (US credentials for US buyers, EU credentials for EU buyers, etc.)
  6. Test checkout from different geographic IP addresses (using a VPN or Shopify's geographic checkout preview) to confirm Klarna appears for buyers in each of your target markets
Shopify Markets and Klarna Require Careful Alignment
Merchants using Shopify Markets to sell across multiple countries need to ensure each market's currency setting aligns with the Klarna merchant account configured for that region. Shopify Markets can display different currencies per market, but Klarna will only appear when the displayed currency matches Klarna's expected currency for the buyer's country. Mismatches between Shopify Markets configuration and Klarna merchant account settings are a growing source of silent Klarna failures as more merchants expand internationally.

Klarna vs. Other BNPL Options on Shopify

Shopify supports multiple BNPL providers in addition to Klarna, including Shop Pay Installments (powered by Affirm), Afterpay/Clearpay, Affirm directly, and Sezzle. Merchants who are experiencing persistent Klarna failures — particularly in markets where Klarna has limited availability or high decline rates — should consider whether an alternative BNPL option better fits their buyer demographic and market.

BNPL Provider Key Markets Integration Min. Order
Klarna US, UK, EU, AU, CA Shopify Payments native or standalone app ~$35 (US)
Shop Pay Installments US only Shopify Payments only $50
Afterpay / Clearpay US, UK, AU, CA, NZ, FR, IT, ES Standalone app ~$35 (US)
Affirm US, CA Standalone app $50
Multiple BNPL Providers Can Coexist — With Care
Unlike the dual-integration conflict that exists between the two Klarna integration paths, different BNPL providers (Klarna + Afterpay, for example) can coexist on the same Shopify checkout without conflicting. Each will appear in the payment section for buyers who are eligible. Offering multiple BNPL options increases the likelihood that at least one will be available when a buyer is declined by Klarna's credit assessment — improving checkout completion rate for buyers who want to split payments.
