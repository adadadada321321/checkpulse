Shopify Store Credit Not Working at Checkout — The Two-Architecture Problem

Shopify has two completely different store credit systems. Native store credit is account-bound — guest checkout customers can never see it. Gift-card-based store credit works everywhere. Most merchants don't know which one their returns app uses until customers start complaining that their store credit disappeared.

Silent Failures

Three Failures That Generate the Most Support Tickets

Silent Failure #1 — Guest Checkout Invisibility

The store credit that vanished before checkout started

A customer contacts support: "I was issued $20 store credit for my return and now it's gone." You check Admin → Customers and the $20 is right there. The credit was never removed. The customer simply checked out as a guest without logging in. Native Shopify store credit requires the customer to be authenticated against the account that holds the credit. Without login, Shopify doesn't look for a credit — the option doesn't appear in the checkout UI at all. The customer saw no store credit field, assumed it was gone, and either paid full price or abandoned the cart.

Fix

Your store credit issuance email must include: (1) the exact email address the customer should log in with, (2) explicit instructions to log in before going to checkout, and (3) an alternative — if the customer insists on guest checkout, offer to convert the store credit to a gift card code. Gift card codes work without login. Some returns apps (Rise.ai) issue credit as gift card codes by default — this eliminates the problem at the source.

Silent Failure #2 — Returns App Ledger Isolation

The store credit that only exists inside the app

A customer received store credit from your returns portal (Loop Returns, AfterShip Returns, ReturnGO, or similar). They go to your Shopify checkout to use it. There is no store credit field. They contact support. You check your returns app and the credit is there. You check Shopify Admin → Customers and the store credit is zero. The returns app issued credit to its own internal ledger — a database that belongs to the returns app, not to Shopify. The credit only exists inside the app. The customer can only apply it by using the app's own checkout widget, which the merchant may not have installed or made visible.

Fix

Check your returns app settings for how store credit is issued: look for "Credit type" or "Refund method" — it should say "Shopify Gift Card" or "Shopify Store Credit" rather than "Portal Credit" or "App Credit." If it uses the app's own ledger, enable the Shopify gift card option if available, or ensure the returns app's checkout widget is installed on your theme and the customer is informed to use the returns portal to redeem credit.

Silent Failure #3 — Currency Mismatch at Scale

The store credit that works for domestic customers but not international ones

International customers who were issued store credit in your store's primary currency (USD) are checking out in their local currency (GBP, EUR, CAD). The store credit balance converted at the current exchange rate may be significantly different from the amount the customer was told. If the converted amount is below the checkout minimum order threshold, or if Shopify's currency conversion logic doesn't correctly apply the credit at checkout, the balance appears as zero or an unexpected amount. The customer's $20 USD store credit shows as £15.20 GBP — but the checkout order minimum is £20 — so the credit is present but cannot be applied to the order.

Fix

For multi-currency stores, issue store credit in the currency the customer used to pay, not your store's primary currency. Check your returns app settings for currency options. If issuing native Shopify store credit manually, Admin → Customers → Store credit now allows currency selection — match the customer's checkout currency. For Rise.ai gift card codes, the code is denominated in the currency it was issued in — verify this matches the customer's market currency before sending.

Diagnosis

6-Step Store Credit Diagnosis Checklist

Work through these steps in order when a customer reports store credit not working. Each step rules out one failure category.

  • 1
    Was the customer logged in? Ask the customer which email they used at checkout. Check Admin → Customers for that email. If the checkout email differs from the account email holding the credit — or if they checked out as a guest — this is the cause. Resolve: ask the customer to log in and re-attempt checkout, or convert the credit to a gift card code.
  • 2
    Does the credit exist in Shopify Admin? Check Admin → Customers → [customer] → Store credit. If balance is zero, the credit was never issued to native Shopify. It may exist in your returns app's own ledger. Check the returns app dashboard for the customer's credit balance separately.
  • 3
    What type of credit did your returns app issue? Check your returns app settings for credit/refund method. If it says "Portal Credit," "App Credit," or anything other than "Shopify Gift Card" or "Shopify Store Credit" — the credit lives in the app's own database and cannot be applied at standard Shopify checkout.
  • 4
    Does the credit currency match the checkout currency? For multi-currency stores: check the currency the credit was issued in (Admin → Customers → Store credit) and compare to the currency the customer's checkout was using. Currency mismatch causes either conversion surprises or credit invisibility depending on checkout configuration.
  • 5
    For gift-card-based credit: check the code status For Rise.ai or AfterShip: look up the specific gift card code in Admin → Products → Gift cards or in the app dashboard. Verify the remaining balance, expiry date, and whether the code was already used (partially or fully). A $0 remaining balance means it was already spent.
  • 6
    Run a test checkout end-to-end Issue yourself a $5 store credit (or test gift card code) and complete a test checkout from the exact same path the customer would take: same device type, same login status (or guest), same currency. If it fails for you too, you have a systematic issue. If it works for you, the customer's specific session has something unusual — document and escalate to the app's support.

FAQ

Shopify Store Credit — Frequently Asked Questions

Why is my Shopify store credit not showing at checkout?
The most common reason is that the customer is checking out as a guest. Native Shopify store credit is account-bound — it requires the customer to be logged in to the account that holds the balance. When shopping as a guest, the store credit option is completely absent from the checkout UI, not just greyed out. Ask the customer to log in using the exact email address their store account is registered under. If your returns app issued the credit to its own proprietary ledger rather than Shopify's native system, the credit will also be invisible at standard Shopify checkout regardless of login status — the customer must use the app's own checkout widget to apply it.
What is the difference between Shopify store credit and a gift card?
Native Shopify store credit is an account balance tied to a specific customer record. No code, no forwarding, requires login. Shopify gift cards are payment instruments tied to a unique alphanumeric code — anyone with the code can use it without logging in. Rise.ai and AfterShip issue store credit by generating Shopify gift card codes behind the scenes. This means Rise.ai store credit works in guest checkout, while native Shopify store credit does not. If your store uses Rise.ai, your customers get a code they can apply anywhere, which significantly reduces the login-at-checkout friction that plagues native store credit.
Why is Rise.ai store credit not working at Shopify checkout?
Rise.ai issues store credit as a Shopify gift card code sent via email. If the Rise.ai credit isn't working, check: (1) Did the customer receive the email? Search the Rise.ai dashboard for the customer and verify the send status. (2) Is the code still valid? Look up the code in Admin → Products → Gift cards to see the remaining balance and expiry date — Rise.ai codes have a configurable expiration. (3) Is there a currency mismatch? The gift card code is denominated in the currency your store used when issuing it. If the customer's checkout session is in a different currency, the code may not apply as expected. (4) Did the customer already use part of the code? The remaining balance after partial use may be insufficient for the current order total.
Can Shopify store credit be used with a gift card or discount code?
Native Shopify store credit applies at the payment step, similar to gift cards. It does not interact with discount codes and does not count against the discount code limit. A customer can use native store credit plus a promotional discount code in the same checkout. Gift-card-based store credit (Rise.ai) is also a payment instrument — same rules apply. The stacking problem that trips up merchants: loyalty points applied as a discount code plus a promo code plus store credit. The store credit is fine. The conflict is between the two discount codes (loyalty code and promo code). On standard Shopify plans, only one discount code is allowed per checkout, so the loyalty code gets silently dropped. See the Gift Card + Loyalty Stacking guide for the full diagnosis.
How do I test Shopify store credit at checkout?
Run four scenarios: (1) Issue $10 native store credit to a test account, log in as that account, go to checkout, verify the credit appears and can be applied. (2) Use the same test account but check out as a guest — the credit should be invisible. Document this as expected behavior for your customer service team. (3) If you use Rise.ai or AfterShip, issue a test credit, find the gift card code in the email or app dashboard, and apply it at checkout without logging in — it should work. (4) For multi-currency: issue store credit in USD, then switch your browser's language/region settings to simulate a GBP checkout, and verify the balance displays correctly in the checkout currency. Run these tests monthly and after any returns app update, Shopify plan change, or theme edit.
