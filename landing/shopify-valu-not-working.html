Shopify valU Not Working — Not Showing, Declined, National ID & Installment Issues

Updated June 2026 · 7 failure modes · Egypt merchants (EGP)

valU is Egypt's largest BNPL and consumer financing platform, backed by EFG Hermes — one of Egypt's most established financial institutions. It offers installment plans from 3 to 36 months in Egyptian Pounds, making it unique among all Shopify BNPL providers in both its Egypt-only focus and its exceptionally long repayment terms. Its Shopify integration fails in ways tied to its EGP-only currency gate, EFG Hermes' formal credit underwriting model, and Egyptian National ID identity requirements. This guide covers all 7 valU failure modes with exact diagnostic steps for Egypt merchants.

How valU Differs From Tabby and Postpay on Shopify in Egypt

valU is Egypt-exclusive — it operates only in Egyptian Pounds (EGP) and only for customers in Egypt. Tabby is the primary alternative that also covers Egypt (alongside UAE, KSA, Kuwait, Qatar), and Postpay covers Egypt alongside UAE and Saudi Arabia. Merchants with a Pan-MENA customer base typically need valU for Egyptian customers who want longer installment plans, plus Tabby or Postpay for shorter-term BNPL across the full region.

valU offers installment plans of 3, 6, 10, 12, 16, 24, and 36 months — the longest repayment horizon of any BNPL provider in the Shopify ecosystem. This makes it not just a BNPL tool but a consumer financing product for high-ticket items: electronics, furniture, large appliances, and fashion at higher price points. No other Shopify BNPL provider (Tabby, Tamara, Cashew, Postpay, Splitit) offers 36-month terms. This longer horizon means valU's approval process is more similar to consumer finance than the instant BNPL scoring used by Tabby or Cashew.

valU is backed by EFG Hermes, Egypt's largest investment bank and financial services group. This institutional backing means valU's credit assessment is more structured and conservative than fintech-native BNPL providers. New customers may face stricter initial credit limits. Customers with Egyptian bank account history, a mobile wallet, or a record in Egypt's credit bureau (I-Score) typically receive more favorable limits. Unlike Tabby's opaque limit, valU shows customers their available credit limit in the app.

Longer plans on valU carry a financing fee. Short 3-payment plans are sometimes offered at 0% for qualifying merchant promotions. Plans of 6 months and above typically carry an annual financing fee (APR). This distinguishes valU from pure-BNPL providers like Tabby and Cashew that only offer 0% installment plans. Merchants advertising "buy now pay later" in Egypt using valU should be aware of this distinction to avoid customer confusion when longer-term plans show a financing fee at checkout.

valU Not Showing at Shopify Checkout

Merchant-side

valU is a third-party alternative payment provider on Shopify, which requires two separate actions to appear at checkout. Installing the valU app from the Shopify App Store is the first step — but valU will not appear at checkout until it is also manually enabled as an alternative payment provider in Shopify Admin → Settings → Payments → Alternative payment providers. Merchants frequently install the app and consider configuration complete. It is not. The payment provider toggle in Shopify Admin must be activated separately, and this two-step requirement is the single most common suppression cause across all BNPL providers on Shopify.

Currency is the hard gate for valU. valU is Egypt-exclusive and operates only in Egyptian Pounds (EGP). If your store's checkout currency is USD, EUR, or any non-EGP currency — even if EGP is displayed as a runtime conversion from USD — valU will not appear. Multi-currency stores using Shopify Markets with USD as the base currency and runtime conversion to EGP will suppress valU for all Egyptian customers regardless of all other configuration being correct. The fix is to set EGP as the fixed checkout currency for the Egypt market in Shopify Admin → Settings → Markets → Egypt.

Geographic scope is absolute. valU appears only for checkouts shipping to Egypt. A customer in the UAE with an Egyptian background, or an Egyptian expat in Saudi Arabia, will not see valU — not because of a configuration error, but because valU does not operate outside Egypt. For non-Egypt MENA customers, Tabby (UAE, KSA, Egypt, Kuwait, Qatar) is the most comprehensive alternative.

Additional suppression causes: test or sandbox API credentials configured instead of live production keys; the cart contains only digital products or services (valU focuses on physical goods); the merchant's EFG Hermes account onboarding is pending (see failure mode 7); or the valU Shopify app version is outdated and requires an update from the Shopify App Store.

How to Diagnose

  1. Check two-step activation: Shopify Admin → Apps → confirm the valU app is listed as installed; then Shopify Admin → Settings → Payments → Alternative payment providers → confirm valU is listed and the toggle is enabled. Both must be true simultaneously.
  2. Check checkout currency for Egypt: Shopify Admin → Settings → Markets → Egypt → confirm EGP is set as the fixed checkout currency, not a runtime conversion from USD. Currency conversion suppresses valU for all Egyptian customers even when valU is correctly installed and activated.
  3. Verify the customer's shipping destination is Egypt: valU only appears when the checkout shipping address is in Egypt. Customers shipping outside Egypt will not see valU — this is expected behavior.
  4. Check API credential mode: In the valU app settings in Shopify, confirm live production API credentials are configured — not sandbox or test credentials. Test credentials suppress valU for real customers.
  5. Verify merchant onboarding status: Log into the valU merchant portal (or contact EFG Hermes merchant support) to confirm your account shows active live status. Pending onboarding blocks all valU transactions silently (see failure mode 7).
  6. Test with a physical product above minimum: Add physical products totalling above 500 EGP to cart and check out with an Egypt shipping address. valU should appear as a payment option for a valid cart.
Egypt-only scope note: valU appears only for EGP checkouts shipping to Egypt. Egyptian customers outside Egypt, or non-Egyptian customers within Egypt without Egyptian National IDs, will not be able to complete a valU transaction. For broader MENA coverage including Egypt, consider adding Tabby (supports EGP alongside AED, SAR, KWD, QAR) as a complementary BNPL option.
2

Credit Assessment Declined — "Financing Not Available"

Customer-side

Unlike fintech-native BNPL providers that use lightweight per-transaction risk scoring, valU's credit assessment is backed by EFG Hermes' financial underwriting criteria. Each valU application is evaluated against the customer's repayment history within the valU platform, their outstanding balance across open plans, their Egyptian National ID verification status, and their broader financial profile visible through Egypt's I-Score credit bureau where applicable. This more rigorous assessment means valU approval rates are generally lower than Tabby or Cashew for first-time users, and that a prior approval does not guarantee future approval for a larger plan amount.

First-time valU customers face conservative initial limits. New customers with no repayment history on the platform receive an initial credit limit that may be insufficient for high-ticket items. The limit grows over time as the customer demonstrates consistent on-time repayments across multiple plans. Merchants selling electronics, furniture, or other high-value products in Egypt should set customer expectations that valU approval for large carts may require an established account history.

Longer plan terms carry higher decline risk. Applying for a 36-month valU plan on a high-value cart is assessed more conservatively than a 3-month plan on a lower-value cart. The underwriting criteria tighten with plan duration because EFG Hermes assumes a longer credit exposure. Customers who are declined on a 24-month plan may be approved for the same purchase on a 6-month plan — the financing fee is higher on the shorter plan, but the approval probability increases significantly. The valU app may offer alternative plan durations at checkout when the primary selected plan is declined.

Merchants cannot override valU credit decisions or access the decline reason. Unlike Tabby's opaque decline where customers have no visibility into their limit, valU shows customers their available credit limit in the app — meaning customers who are declined can usually check their available balance before attributing the issue to the merchant's integration.

Fix Steps for Customers

  1. Check your available valU credit limit in the app: Open the valU app → Home or Account → confirm your available credit balance. If the cart value exceeds your available limit, the decline is a limit issue, not a configuration problem.
  2. Try a shorter installment plan: At valU checkout, select a 3-month or 6-month plan instead of a longer duration. Shorter plans are assessed more favorably and have higher approval rates. The financing fee may be higher, but approval probability increases.
  3. Reduce the cart value: If your available valU credit is partially consumed by open plans, a smaller cart value may fit within your remaining limit. Consider splitting a large purchase into two separate orders.
  4. Wait for existing plans to progress: Open valU plans reduce your available credit capacity. Making an early payment on an existing plan frees credit capacity for new purchases. After the payment processes, retry the declined purchase.
  5. Ensure your National ID is fully verified: Open the valU app → Profile → verify your Egyptian National ID is confirmed. An unverified or expired ID is a silent decline trigger for all new valU plans.
  6. Contact valU customer support: valU's in-app and phone support can provide context on a specific decline. For customers with a strong repayment history, a credit limit review request may be appropriate.
Merchant note: valU declines affecting specific customers are credit decisions, not integration errors. If all customers are simultaneously unable to complete a valU transaction, that indicates a merchant account or configuration issue (check failure mode 7). If only some customers are declined, your integration is working correctly — the issue is at the individual customer credit assessment level.
3

Credit Limit Reached or Insufficient for Cart Value

Customer-side

valU assigns each customer a credit limit — the maximum outstanding balance they can carry across all open valU plans simultaneously. Unlike Tabby, Cashew, and Spotii — which do not show customers their credit limit — valU makes the available credit limit visible in the app. This is a meaningful usability advantage, but it means customers approaching their limit are aware of the constraint before it triggers a decline at checkout.

Long plan durations create extended credit holds. A valU plan opened for 24 or 36 months holds a portion of the customer's available credit limit for the entire duration of the plan. A customer who used valU for a large purchase 18 months ago on a 36-month plan still has a portion of their credit limit held by that plan for the remaining 18 months. Customers who take out multiple long-duration valU plans in a short period — common during Egypt's major sale seasons and promotional events — can exhaust their available credit capacity even if their monthly payment obligations are low relative to their income.

Egypt's inflation context creates a specific valU limit pressure. EGP purchasing power has fluctuated significantly in recent years. A valU credit limit set in EGP terms in 2023 may represent significantly different real purchasing power in 2025 and 2026. Customers whose valU limits have not been increased to keep pace with EGP price levels may find their nominal limit is no longer sufficient for purchases that were comfortably within limit in prior years. Credit limit review requests submitted to valU support — backed by a clean repayment history — are the mechanism for addressing this.

Unlike BNPL providers whose limits are binary (approved or declined, no transparency), valU's visible limit means this failure mode can be self-diagnosed by the customer before checkout. Merchants who explain in their Egypt-facing FAQ that valU limits are visible in-app, and that the fix is to manage open plans or request a limit review, can reduce support tickets significantly.

Fix Steps for Customers

  1. Check your valU credit limit in the app: valU app → Home or Wallet → view your available credit balance. If the cart value exceeds the available balance, you need to either free up credit or select a smaller plan amount.
  2. Make an early payment on an open plan: valU app → My Plans → select any active plan → make a payment. Early payment on any plan reduces your outstanding balance and increases available credit capacity for new purchases.
  3. Select a lower plan amount or split the purchase: If the full cart value is above your available limit, consider splitting the purchase into two separate orders, or reducing the cart to a value that fits within your remaining credit capacity.
  4. Request a credit limit increase: Customers with a 6+ month history of on-time valU payments can contact valU customer support or use the in-app limit review request feature. Increased limit requests are evaluated based on repayment track record.
  5. Try a shorter plan duration: Shorter-term plans (3 or 6 months) sometimes unlock higher approval amounts for the same cart value compared to longer plans, because the per-month commitment is higher but the risk assessment criteria differ.
Merchant note: Because valU shows customers their available credit, valU limit-related declines generate a different type of merchant support ticket than Tabby or Cashew declines. Customers reaching out to merchants about valU limits already know their limit is the issue — they are seeking advice on how to increase it, not reporting a broken checkout. Point customers to the valU app's limit management features and contact information.

Egyptian National ID Verification Failure

Customer-side

valU requires every customer to verify their identity using their Egyptian National ID (البطاقة القومية — al-bitaqa al-qawmiyya). This is the only accepted identity document — unlike Tabby or Postpay, which accept a range of documents including passports and residence permits for non-Egyptian residents. The Egyptian National ID is issued to Egyptian nationals only, which effectively means valU is available only to Egyptian citizens — not to foreign nationals residing in Egypt, not to Egyptians with expired national IDs, and not to customers whose National ID details do not precisely match their valU account registration.

Recently issued or renewed National IDs are a common verification friction point. Egypt's Civil Registration Authority (Mصلحة الأحوال المدنية) issues or renews National IDs on a rolling basis. A recently issued or renewed National ID may not yet be propagated through Egypt's digital identity verification systems. Customers who renewed their National ID in the past 7–14 days may experience verification failures not because of an error, but because the national database has not yet reflected the renewed document. Waiting and retrying is typically the resolution in these cases.

Digital ID photo quality is a frequent automated rejection cause. valU's identity verification includes an automated document scan where the customer photographs both sides of their National ID. Common failure triggers: the ID is photographed on a non-flat surface (curved or held); glare from light sources obscures the holographic elements or barcode; the photo is blurry; the ID's corners are cropped out of frame; or the ID's surface is scratched or worn to the point where automated OCR cannot parse the document number. These automated rejections can often be resolved by resubmitting with a better quality photograph.

Name and date-of-birth consistency is required. The name and date of birth on the Egyptian National ID must exactly match the details entered in the valU account. Transliteration differences between the Arabic name on the National ID and the Latin-script entry used during registration are a frequent mismatch. Customers who registered valU with an informal version of their name (a nickname or shortened name) rather than the full legal name on their National ID will face verification failure until the account name is updated to match.

Fix Steps for Customers

  1. Check verification status in the valU app: valU app → Profile or Account → check if your Egyptian National ID shows as verified. A pending or rejected status blocks all new plans until resolved.
  2. Resubmit with a high-quality photo: If automated verification failed, resubmit your National ID scan. Use a flat, well-lit surface with no glare. Ensure all four corners are visible, the ID is not blurry, and the holographic elements are not covered. Use the rear camera of your phone, not the front camera.
  3. Wait if your ID was recently renewed: If your National ID was issued or renewed in the past two weeks, wait 7–14 days before retrying valU verification. Egypt's civil registration database propagation can take this long to reflect newly issued documents.
  4. Verify name and date of birth consistency: Ensure the name entered in your valU account exactly matches the name on your National ID (in Latin script as transliterated). If they differ, update your valU account profile before resubmitting verification.
  5. Contact valU support for manual review: If automated verification has failed twice or more, request manual review by contacting valU customer support. Manual review by the compliance team can process cases that the automated system rejects, including worn or low-contrast National IDs.
  6. Ensure your National ID is not expired: Egypt's National ID has an expiry date. An expired National ID is not accepted for valU verification even if all other details are correct. Renew your ID at a civil registration office before attempting to use valU.
Non-Egyptian residents note: valU's Egyptian National ID requirement means it is unavailable to non-Egyptian nationals residing in Egypt, regardless of their residency status. Foreign nationals — including expats in Egypt on work permits — cannot use valU. For merchants with a mixed Egyptian-national and expat customer base in Egypt, Tabby accepts a broader range of identity documents and is a more inclusive option for non-Egyptian customers.

Phone Number Verification / OTP Failure

Customer-side

valU uses SMS OTP (one-time password) verification during account setup and at checkout confirmation. OTPs are delivered to the Egyptian mobile number (+20) registered on the customer's valU account. OTP delivery fails in several scenarios that are common in Egypt due to regional SIM patterns and telecom infrastructure characteristics.

Non-Egyptian SIM cards are the primary OTP failure cause. valU's OTP delivery is optimized for Egyptian mobile numbers with country code +20 (Vodafone Egypt, Orange Egypt, Etisalat Egypt, WE). Customers who are Egyptian nationals but currently residing or travelling outside Egypt may not receive valU OTPs reliably due to roaming restrictions on SMS delivery from Egyptian fintech gateways. Egyptian expats using Gulf SIM cards (+971, +966, +965) or any non-Egyptian number as their registered valU phone number will face persistent OTP delivery failures until they update their registered number to an active Egyptian SIM.

SMS delivery reliability across Egyptian carriers varies. Egypt's telecom infrastructure has variable SMS delivery performance for fintech short-code messages. Messages from financial platform gateways are sometimes filtered by carrier spam detection systems, particularly on prepaid SIM cards. Customers who consistently report missing OTPs should check their SMS spam or blocked messages folder before assuming a valU system problem. Switching between WiFi and mobile data can also affect OTP SMS delivery timing in areas with mixed signal quality.

OTP expiry is a frequent source of repeat failures. valU's OTPs expire within a short window (typically 3–5 minutes). Customers who are slow to enter the OTP — perhaps because they switched apps or were looking at the SMS notification — receive an expired code error and need to request a new OTP. Each OTP request generates a new code, invalidating all prior codes. Multiple rapid OTP requests can trigger a temporary rate limit on OTP delivery to a specific number.

Fix Steps for Customers

  1. Confirm your registered phone number: valU app → Profile or Account → Phone Number — verify this is your current, active Egyptian mobile number (+20). If you are using a non-Egyptian SIM as your primary number, OTP delivery will be unreliable until you update it.
  2. Check SMS spam or filtered folder: Some Egyptian carriers and SMS apps route fintech short-code SMS to spam or promotional folders. Check your messages app's spam or filtered folder if the OTP did not arrive in your main inbox.
  3. Request a fresh OTP if it expired: OTPs expire after 3–5 minutes. If you took too long entering the code, request a new OTP. Do not retry an expired code — it will not work. Wait 30 seconds between OTP requests to avoid rate limiting.
  4. Switch to mobile data if on WiFi: In areas where WiFi calling or messaging apps may intercept SMS, switching to a mobile data connection can help ensure the OTP arrives via the standard SMS channel.
  5. Update your phone number if you changed it: valU app → Account → Update Phone Number → enter your new active Egyptian (+20) number and complete re-verification before attempting another checkout.
  6. Contact valU support if OTP never arrives: If multiple OTP requests go undelivered to a confirmed Egyptian number, contact valU customer support to investigate whether there is a carrier-level block or an account-level issue with your registered number.

Order Value Below valU Minimum

Merchant + Customer

valU has a minimum order value requirement — typically around 500 EGP for standard transactions. Carts below this threshold will not display valU as a payment option at checkout, regardless of the customer's account status, credit limit, or the merchant's configuration. This minimum is applied before valU's credit assessment — so a below-minimum cart never reaches the approval stage, and no error is displayed to the customer. valU simply does not appear.

The minimum is applied to the post-discount transaction value. Discount codes, automatic promotions, or order-level offers that reduce the checkout total below 500 EGP will suppress valU — even if the original cart items were individually priced above the minimum. A customer who applies a large discount code bringing the order total to 400 EGP will no longer see valU even if it was visible earlier in the checkout flow. This is a common cause of "valU disappeared after I entered a promo code" support tickets.

valU's minimum compared to other Egypt BNPL options. Tabby's minimum order value in Egypt is very low — near-zero in practice — making it viable for low-value EGP carts. Postpay's Egypt minimum is also lower than valU's. valU's 500 EGP minimum reflects its positioning as a consumer financing product for mid-to-high value purchases rather than everyday low-value BNPL. Merchants whose average order values in Egypt are below 500 EGP — fast fashion, accessories, small home goods — will see valU suppressed on a majority of orders and should consider Tabby as the primary BNPL option for those products.

EGP inflation note. The 500 EGP minimum, stated in nominal terms, represents a lower real purchasing power threshold over time as EGP inflation progresses. A minimum that once screened out truly low-value carts now passes more of what would historically have been considered mid-range purchases. Merchants should periodically verify the current valU minimum from the valU merchant portal, as thresholds may be adjusted.

Fix Steps

  1. Verify cart value after all discounts: Calculate the total cart value after all promo codes and promotions are applied. If the total is below 500 EGP, valU not appearing is expected behavior — not a bug or configuration error.
  2. Check whether a discount code is pushing the cart below minimum: Test the checkout with and without the discount code to confirm the promotion is bringing the order total below the valU threshold.
  3. Consider Tabby for low-value EGP orders: If below-minimum cart suppression affects a significant portion of your Egyptian orders, Tabby's lower minimum makes it a better BNPL fit for that product category.
  4. Consider bundle pricing or threshold incentives: If valU is the BNPL that resonates best with your Egyptian customer base, pricing strategies that naturally push cart values above 500 EGP — free shipping threshold, bundle discounts for multi-item orders — can both increase valU eligibility and improve average order value.
  5. Verify the current minimum with valU: The 500 EGP figure is the standard threshold, but valU may adjust minimums by merchant category or promote specific minimums during campaign periods. Log into the valU merchant portal to confirm the current threshold for your merchant account.

Merchant Onboarding or EFG Hermes Account Pending

Merchant-side

valU merchant onboarding is managed through EFG Hermes' merchant services team and involves a formal business verification and commercial agreement process. Unlike fintech-native BNPL providers with automated onboarding, valU's EFG Hermes backing means the merchant onboarding process follows financial institution procedures: commercial registration review, tax registration verification (Egyptian Tax Authority), bank account validation, and a contract execution step. Merchants who complete app installation and Shopify payment activation but have not completed the full EFG Hermes onboarding process will see valU configured in Shopify but no customers will be able to transact — valU blocks all live transactions until the merchant account is fully activated.

Merchant onboarding is a silent failure mode on Shopify. A merchant can have valU fully enabled in Shopify Admin with correct EGP currency configuration and live API credentials, and still have zero successful valU transactions because the EFG Hermes merchant account is pending final activation. Shopify's checkout shows no error to customers — valU simply does not appear or declines silently. There is no automatic Shopify-side notification that the blockage is at the EFG Hermes account level. Checking the valU merchant portal or contacting EFG Hermes merchant support is the only way to confirm activation status.

Egyptian commercial registration requirements add specificity to onboarding. valU's merchant onboarding requires the merchant's Egyptian Commercial Registration (السجل التجاري), Egyptian Tax Card (البطاقة الضريبية), and in some cases the commercial activity license relevant to the merchant's product category. Merchants operating in Egypt as foreign-invested entities, or Egyptian branches of international companies, may face additional documentation steps compared to locally registered Egyptian businesses. Incomplete document submissions are the most common reason for extended onboarding review periods.

API credential transition after activation is a critical step. During the onboarding review period, EFG Hermes provides test/sandbox API credentials to configure the Shopify integration and run test checkouts. These test credentials allow checkout testing in a sandbox — but they suppress valU for all real customers. A common merchant error is completing EFG Hermes onboarding approval but not updating the Shopify integration from test to the live production API credentials issued after activation. valU will appear to work in test mode but will be blocked for real Egyptian customers until live production keys are applied.

How to Diagnose and Fix

  1. Check your valU merchant portal for activation status: Log into the valU merchant portal or contact EFG Hermes merchant services to confirm your account shows "Active" or "Live" status. If the portal shows "Pending," "Under Review," or "Documents Required," your account is not yet activated and all customer transactions will be blocked.
  2. Submit any missing onboarding documents: If EFG Hermes has indicated documents are missing or need updating, resubmit promptly. Required documents typically include: Egyptian Commercial Registration (السجل التجاري); Egyptian Tax Card (البطاقة الضريبية); bank account verification or voided cheque; authorized signatory National ID. The specific requirements are outlined in the EFG Hermes merchant onboarding checklist.
  3. Switch from test to live API credentials after activation: After EFG Hermes confirms account activation, log into the valU merchant portal to retrieve your live production API credentials. In Shopify Admin → Apps → valU → Configuration, replace all test/sandbox keys with the live production keys. Test credentials suppress valU for real customers even after full merchant activation.
  4. Run a live validation transaction: After switching to live credentials, complete a small real purchase end-to-end using an actual customer account (not a test account). Confirm the order appears in both Shopify and the valU merchant portal. This confirms the full integration is live and processing correctly.
  5. Escalate stalled reviews to EFG Hermes merchant support: If your onboarding submission has been pending for more than 10 business days without a status update, contact EFG Hermes merchant services directly with your application reference. Manual escalation typically resolves document processing delays. EFG Hermes follows Egyptian banking business hours — allow for public holiday gaps.
  6. Check for annual re-verification: Egyptian commercial registrations and tax cards require periodic renewal. If valU was working previously and stopped without any merchant-side changes, check whether any registered business documents have recently expired. Expired commercial registration is a common trigger for valU account suspension that does not generate a Shopify-visible error.
Critical diagnostic question: Is valU configured in Shopify but not appearing for any Egyptian customer? If yes and you recently completed valU integration, check your EFG Hermes merchant account activation status before debugging currency, app settings, or API configuration. Incomplete merchant onboarding is the most common reason for valU showing as configured in Shopify but generating zero customer transactions during the first weeks after installation.
