Shopify Checkout Error — Shipping

Shopify Checkout Shipping Rate Not Updating

Carrier-calculated rates disappear, "No shipping methods available" appears after address entry, UPS/FedEx/USPS rates missing, shipping calculator stuck loading. Carrier API timeout is the most common cause — Shopify silently drops timed-out rates with no error message shown to the customer.

Updated June 2026 6 root causes 7 silent failure modes
The core issue: Shopify fetches shipping rates dynamically at two points — when the customer enters their shipping address, and when the cart changes. Flat-rate and free-shipping options are always instant. Carrier-calculated rates (UPS, FedEx, USPS, DHL, plus rates from shipping apps) require a real-time API call. When those calls time out, Shopify silently removes the carrier options with no error message. Customers see either only flat-rate options, or "No shipping methods available" if flat rates don't cover that zone.
Architecture

How Shopify Fetches Shipping Rates

Two independent rate systems run in parallel. One always works. The other can fail silently.

System 1 — Always Available
Static Rates
Flat-rate, free shipping, and price-based rates are stored in Shopify. They load instantly — no external API call required. Never affected by timeouts or carrier outages.
System 2 — Can Fail Silently
Carrier-Calculated Rates
Real-time rates from UPS, FedEx, USPS, DHL, Australia Post, and third-party shipping apps. Shopify makes an external API call for each provider. If any call times out, those rates are silently dropped — no error shown to the customer.
When Rates Are Fetched
Two Trigger Points
Trigger 1: customer enters or changes their shipping address. Trigger 2: cart changes (quantity update, add/remove item, discount applied). Each trigger fires a fresh rate calculation for carrier providers.
Timeout Windows
10–15 Second Limit
Shopify allows 10–15 seconds for carrier API responses (varies by provider integration). Custom Carrier Service API endpoints (merchant-hosted) must respond within exactly 10 seconds. Exceeding the timeout = rates silently dropped.

Deep Dives

Three Technical Explanations

Deep Dive A

Carrier-Calculated Shipping Mechanics — Timeout, Plan Requirements, and Fallback Behavior

When a customer enters their shipping address at Shopify checkout, Shopify simultaneously calls each carrier rate provider configured in your shipping settings. Each call is made in parallel (not sequentially), so multiple carrier APIs are queried at the same time. The total rate-fetch time is approximately the time of the slowest individual provider that responds within the timeout window.

Timeout behavior: Shopify waits up to 10–15 seconds for each carrier-calculated provider (the exact limit depends on the integration type). Custom Carrier Service API endpoints — where the merchant hosts their own rate-calculation endpoint — must respond within exactly 10 seconds. If any provider exceeds the timeout, Shopify silently excludes its rates from the checkout display. The remaining providers' rates are still shown. If all providers time out and there are no flat-rate options for the customer's zone, the result is "No shipping methods available."

Plan requirement for third-party calculated rates: Real-time carrier rates (UPS, FedEx, USPS, DHL via their APIs) are not available on all Shopify plans. On the Basic and Shopify (Standard) monthly billing plans, you need to switch to annual billing to unlock "Third-party calculated shipping rates." On Shopify Advanced and Shopify Plus, the feature is always included. If you don't see real-time carrier rates in Admin → Settings → Shipping, this is the most likely reason.

Shopify's own carrier rates (via Shopify Shipping): If you use Shopify Shipping to access USPS/UPS/DHL/Canada Post at discounted rates, these are connected differently — through Shopify's own carrier account integration. These are more reliable than third-party carrier APIs but can still time out during carrier system outages.

Recommended fallback strategy: Always configure at least one flat-rate or price-based rate for every shipping zone as a fallback. When carrier APIs are unavailable (carrier outage, peak traffic, timeout), flat rates ensure customers can still complete checkout. Losing a sale because of a UPS API outage is avoidable.

Deep Dive B

Shipping Zone Configuration — Why "No Shipping Methods Available" Appears for Valid Addresses

Every Shopify store defines shipping zones — geographic regions where the store will ship to, with specific rates configured for each zone. If a customer's shipping address falls outside all configured zones, Shopify shows "No shipping methods available" with no explanation.

Common zone configuration gaps:

  • No "Rest of World" zone: Stores that ship internationally but haven't added a catch-all zone leave all unlisted countries with no rates. Customers from those countries see the error even if you'd happily ship to them.
  • US-only store, customers in Puerto Rico or Guam: US territories are not automatically included in "United States" zones — they require separate zone entries.
  • Postal code-based exclusions: Some carriers have rate rules restricted by ZIP code range. An address outside that range returns no rates from that carrier even if it's in the country zone.
  • State/province-level exclusions: Some stores configure rates by state (e.g., excluding Hawaii and Alaska from standard rates). Customers in those states see no rates if no alternative zone is configured.
  • Custom shipping profiles: Products on a custom shipping profile (not the general profile) must have their own zone and rate configuration. A product added to a custom profile but not assigned rates can block checkout for orders containing that product.

Diagnosing zone coverage: In Shopify Admin → Settings → Shipping and Delivery, click on each shipping profile and review the zone map. Look for addresses your customers report as failing and verify whether a rate exists for that zone. For international stores, ensure a "Rest of World" zone exists with at least one rate option.

Deep Dive C

Third-Party Shipping App Diagnostics — ShipStation, Shippo, EasyPost, ShipBob, and Custom Carrier Services

Apps that provide shipping rates to Shopify use the Carrier Service API. When a customer enters their shipping address, Shopify sends a rate request to each registered carrier service endpoint — both Shopify's own carriers and any app endpoints. The apps must respond within 10 seconds with a JSON list of available rates.

Why app-provided rates disappear:

  • App endpoint timeout (>10 seconds): If the app's rate calculation takes longer than 10 seconds, Shopify drops the response. Causes include high API load on the app's side, slow carrier API calls from within the app, or complex rate-rule evaluation.
  • Carrier account credentials expired: Apps connect to carrier APIs (UPS, FedEx) using credentials you provided during setup. When those credentials expire (annual key rotations, OAuth token expiry), the app can no longer fetch rates and returns an empty or error response.
  • Shipment constraints not met: Your negotiated carrier rates may not support certain destinations, package types, or weight classes. The app returns no rates for those combinations.
  • App disconnected from Shopify: If the Shopify-to-app OAuth connection was revoked (password reset, app update, user-initiated disconnect), the app's carrier service endpoint is no longer called.

Diagnostic steps by app:

  • ShipStation: Settings → Shipping → Rates → click "Test rates" for your connected carrier. Also check Settings → Stores → your Shopify store connection status.
  • Shippo: In the Shippo dashboard, look for "Carrier Connections" and verify each carrier shows "Connected." Test a rate quote manually with a sample address and weight to confirm rates are being returned.
  • EasyPost: Check the EasyPost dashboard for API errors in the Logs section. Verify carrier account status and that credentials haven't expired.
  • ShipBob: Contact ShipBob support — rate availability from ShipBob depends on fulfillment center inventory location and zone coverage, which isn't visible in the merchant dashboard.

Verifying a carrier service is still registered: In Shopify Admin → Settings → Shipping and Delivery → Carrier accounts — look for your app listed as a carrier. If it's missing, the app needs to be reinstalled or the carrier service re-registered. Some apps do this automatically on reconnect; others require manual re-setup.

Diagnostic

Customer Diagnostic (5 Steps)

  • 1
    Refresh the checkout page. Carrier API timeouts are often transient. Refreshing forces a new rate calculation request. If carrier rates appear after a refresh, the original request timed out — a persistent flat-rate backup option would have prevented the failed experience.
  • 2
    Check that your shipping address country matches your actual location. If the checkout form shows the wrong country (common with VPNs or when traveling), shipping zones and carrier rates for the wrong country may return no options. Update the country field manually.
  • 3
    Verify your address is complete. Some carrier APIs require a full address including apartment/suite number, city, state, and postal code to return rates. A partially-entered address can cause carrier rate calculation to fail.
  • 4
    Try clearing your browser cache and starting a new checkout session. Stale session data can cause cached rates from a previous address to persist. A fresh session forces a clean rate calculation.
  • 5
    Contact the store directly. If rates are consistently missing to your address, the store may not have shipping configured for your region. The store can manually create a draft order with a custom rate or enable shipping to your area.

Merchant Diagnostic (6 Steps)

  • 1
    Test the checkout with the failing address immediately. Use an incognito browser window to simulate a fresh customer session. Note exactly which rates appear and which are missing. Compare this to your Shopify Shipping settings to identify which provider is absent.
  • 2
    Check Shopify Admin → Settings → Shipping and Delivery. Verify: (a) the customer's address falls within a configured shipping zone; (b) rates are configured for that zone; (c) any carrier-calculated rate providers are still listed as active.
  • 3
    Verify product weights are configured. Go to Admin → Products → select affected products → check that Weight is set. Carrier rate APIs require accurate weight. Weight = 0 or blank often causes carrier APIs to return no results or an error, which Shopify silently drops.
  • 4
    Check your Shopify plan for carrier rate access. In Admin → Settings → Shipping, look for a prompt near carrier accounts. On Basic/Shopify monthly billing, third-party calculated rates require switching to annual billing. If you recently downgraded, this feature may have been removed.
  • 5
    Check your shipping app's connection status. Open each shipping app (ShipStation, Shippo, EasyPost, etc.) and verify: (a) the Shopify connection is active; (b) carrier credentials are valid; (c) a test rate calculation succeeds using a sample address and weight.
  • 6
    Add a flat-rate fallback for every zone. Regardless of the root cause, adding a flat-rate backup ("Standard Shipping — $7.99") ensures customers can always complete checkout even when carrier APIs are unavailable. This immediately stops the "no shipping methods" conversion loss while you debug the carrier API issue.
