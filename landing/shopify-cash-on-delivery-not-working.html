Shopify Cash on Delivery Not Working — COD Not Showing at Checkout (2026)

COD not appearing at checkout, not available for your shipping zone, disappearing for certain orders, or missing entirely after a recent change — here are all 8 failure modes for Cash on Delivery breaking on Shopify, with exact diagnostic steps and fixes for each.

Updated June 2026 8 failure modes Not Enabled · Zone Restriction · Order Amount Limit · Digital Products · Product Exclusions · Carrier Limitation · Markets Misconfiguration · App Conflict

Cash on Delivery (COD) is one of the most important payment methods for merchants selling in South Asia, the Middle East and North Africa, Southeast Asia, and Eastern Europe — markets where buyers strongly prefer to pay in cash when goods arrive, and where card penetration and digital wallet adoption remain lower than in Western markets. For many merchants in these regions, COD accounts for 40–80% of total orders.

When COD stops working on Shopify, the impact is immediate and severe. Buyers in high-COD markets who cannot see the COD option at checkout will not complete their purchase — they have no alternative payment method they trust or want to use. Unlike card payment failures, which buyers may retry with a different card, COD failures cause clean abandonment with no fallback.

COD failures on Shopify fall into eight distinct categories. Critically, COD is not enabled by default on Shopify — it must be manually activated — and most of the common failures stem from configuration gaps that are easy to miss during initial setup or after platform updates.

Quick Check: Is COD Actually Enabled?
Before diagnosing any COD failure, confirm the method is active: go to Admin → Settings → Payments → scroll to "Manual payment methods." If "Cash on delivery (COD)" is not listed there, it is not enabled and will not appear at checkout for any buyer. Adding it takes under 30 seconds. If it is listed, proceed to the more specific failure modes below.

Failure Mode 1: COD Manual Payment Method Not Enabled in Settings

Cash on Delivery is a manual payment method on Shopify — it does not come pre-activated and requires explicit setup. Unlike Shopify Payments (which is enabled when you verify your store) or PayPal (which appears automatically for stores in supported countries), COD must be deliberately added under Manual Payment Methods. This is the most common reason COD does not appear: merchants assume it will appear automatically, or they set up their store without realizing the activation step was required.

How to enable Cash on Delivery on Shopify

  1. Go to Shopify Admin → Settings → Payments
  2. Scroll past the primary payment provider (Shopify Payments, Stripe, etc.) to the section labeled "Manual payment methods"
  3. Click "Add manual payment method"
  4. Select "Cash on delivery (COD)" from the dropdown list
  5. Optionally enter payment instructions — this text is shown to buyers at checkout and in the order confirmation email (e.g., "Pay the courier in cash when your order arrives. Please have exact change ready.")
  6. Click "Activate"
  7. Run a test checkout immediately — COD should now appear in the payment options step
COD Requires Manual Fulfillment Review
Orders placed with COD are not automatically fulfilled. Shopify treats COD as a manual payment — the order is created with payment status "Pending." You must either manually mark it as paid after delivery, or use an app or fulfillment automation that handles COD order status updates. Without this, COD orders will stay in "Pending" indefinitely and your financials will be inaccurate.

Failure Mode 2: COD Not Available for the Buyer's Shipping Zone or Country

Shopify's native manual payment methods — including COD — do not have built-in geographic restrictions. By default, once COD is enabled, it will appear for all buyers regardless of their shipping address. This means that if you've installed a COD restriction app (to limit COD to specific countries or zones) or used Shopify Checkout Functions to apply zone-based rules, those restrictions may be incorrectly excluding the buyer's location.

Conversely, for merchants who want to offer COD only in specific regions but haven't configured restrictions, COD may appear for buyers in countries where the merchant's fulfillment partner cannot physically collect payment on delivery — causing operational problems rather than a checkout failure.

Countries and regions with highest COD demand

Region High-COD Countries COD Share of Orders (typical)
South Asia India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka, Nepal 40–80%
Middle East & North Africa Egypt, Saudi Arabia, UAE, Kuwait, Jordan, Lebanon, Morocco, Algeria 50–75%
Southeast Asia Philippines, Vietnam, Indonesia, Thailand, Malaysia 25–60%

How to diagnose a zone restriction issue

  1. Check your installed apps — search for any app with "COD", "payment rules", "checkout rules", or "cash on delivery" in the name that might apply geographic restrictions
  2. If using Shopify Checkout Functions, review the function logic to confirm the buyer's country is included in the COD-allowed list
  3. Test checkout using a VPN set to the buyer's country — if COD appears with the VPN, the buyer's IP-based geolocation is being used to restrict the method
  4. Check Shopify Admin → Settings → Markets — if you have COD configured per market, verify the buyer's country is assigned to a market where COD is enabled
  5. If no restriction logic is active and COD still doesn't appear for a specific country, confirm the buyer is reaching the shipping step successfully before the payment step (COD requires a valid shipping address and method to be selected first)
COD Requires a Shipping Method to Be Selected First
COD only appears at the payment step after the buyer has selected a shipping method. If a buyer's address has no available shipping rates configured, they cannot reach the payment step at all — and COD appears to be missing. Always test COD with a shipping address that has at least one active shipping rate. If buyers in certain regions see no shipping options, that is a shipping zone configuration issue, not a COD issue.

Failure Mode 3: Order Amount Outside the COD Minimum or Maximum Threshold

Many merchants who offer COD configure it with order value limits — a minimum to ensure the economics of COD collection make sense (COD collection fees from couriers are fixed costs that become expensive relative to very small orders) and a maximum to limit financial exposure on high-value orders where a buyer refuses delivery and the goods must be returned. These thresholds are enforced by checkout apps or Checkout Functions and, when misconfigured, cause COD to silently disappear for orders outside the allowed range.

Typical COD order value configurations

Configuration Typical Range Business Reason
Minimum COD order $10–$30 / ₹500–₹1,500 COD collection fee is fixed; uneconomical for tiny orders
Maximum COD order $200–$500 / ₹5,000–₹15,000 High-value COD returns are expensive; risk of non-delivery payment
No maximum — Merchant absorbs all COD return risk
COD Silently Disappears for Out-of-Range Orders
When an order amount falls outside the configured COD range, the payment method simply does not appear in the checkout payment list. There is no message to the buyer explaining why. Buyers who expect COD to always be available — especially repeat customers in high-COD markets — will see it missing without explanation and are likely to abandon. If your order value threshold is aggressive, consider displaying a note in your store policy explaining COD limits.

How to review and adjust COD order amount restrictions

  1. Open the app or Checkout Function that controls your COD threshold — check the configured minimum and maximum values
  2. Test checkout with an order that falls within the expected range — COD should appear; test with an order above the maximum and below the minimum to confirm the restriction is working as intended
  3. If the threshold appears incorrect (e.g., COD not showing for orders you expect it to cover), re-check the currency — some apps store thresholds in USD but apply them to INR or AED orders without conversion, causing mismatches
  4. Consider whether the current thresholds are still appropriate for your product catalog — review your COD return rate data before tightening limits further

Failure Mode 4: Cart Contains Digital or Non-Physical Products

Cash on Delivery is fundamentally a physical delivery payment method — payment is collected when a physical package is handed to the buyer. Shopify's COD option is not designed for digital products, downloadable files, virtual goods, or services that do not involve physical delivery. If a buyer's cart contains only digital items (digital downloads, gift cards, subscriptions, virtual products), COD is logically inapplicable and — if correctly configured — should not appear.

However, this also creates silent failures in mixed-cart scenarios: a buyer with one physical product and one digital product in their cart may see COD suppressed for the entire cart even though one item is physical. The exact behavior depends on how COD restrictions are implemented.

How Shopify handles COD for digital products

  • Default behavior (no restriction app): Shopify does not automatically suppress COD for digital products. COD will appear at checkout regardless of product type. If the buyer places an order, you will receive a COD order with no physical delivery to occur — a problematic scenario that you must handle manually.
  • With a COD restriction app: Most COD restriction apps can be configured to suppress COD when the cart contains products tagged as "digital" or assigned to a specific collection. This requires you to correctly tag all digital products in your store.
  • With Checkout Functions (Shopify Plus): You can write a function that checks whether any cart line item is a digital product (by product type, tag, or fulfillment service) and hides COD when only digital items are present, or for the entire cart if any digital item is present.
COD on Digital-Only Carts Creates Operational Problems
If COD is not restricted for digital products and a buyer places a COD order for a digital download, your store will show the order as "Pending" payment indefinitely — no delivery occurs, so no cash is collected, and the order never gets marked paid. If you fulfill digital products automatically upon order creation (as most do), you have already sent the digital product before receiving payment. Always exclude digital-only carts from COD, either via a restriction app or Checkout Functions.

Failure Mode 5: Product-Level or Collection-Level COD Exclusions

Some merchants restrict COD for specific products or collections — typically for high-value items (where COD non-delivery risk is too high), perishable goods (which cannot be returned if rejected), or items with very high return rates that make COD uneconomical. These exclusions are configured via apps or Checkout Functions and can cause COD to disappear for specific cart configurations even when it works correctly for other products.

Common scenarios where product-level COD exclusions cause silent failures

  • Product tag mismatch: A product that should be excluded from COD is missing the "no-cod" or equivalent tag. A product that should be eligible for COD was accidentally tagged with the exclusion tag. Both cause incorrect behavior — COD available when it shouldn't be, or unavailable when it should be.
  • Collection membership change: A product was moved to a COD-excluded collection (e.g., "High Value Items"), but the merchant intended to exclude only certain SKUs, not the whole collection. All products in that collection now lose COD.
  • Variant-level exclusion not supported: Most COD restriction apps operate at the product level. If you want to exclude only a specific variant (e.g., a 10-pack bundle) from COD but allow the single-unit variant, you may need Checkout Functions to implement variant-level logic.
  • Mixed cart partial exclusion: If one item in a cart is tagged for COD exclusion, some apps suppress COD for the entire cart while others suppress it only when the excluded item is the only item. Review your app's documentation on mixed-cart behavior.

How to audit product-level COD restrictions

  1. Identify the specific product or collection that triggers the COD disappearance by testing checkout with different combinations of items
  2. Check whether the product has any tags related to payment restrictions — look for tags like "no-cod", "cod-excluded", "digital", or custom tags your COD app uses
  3. Check your COD restriction app settings for the list of excluded collections or product types
  4. If the exclusion is correct, review whether the exclusion logic applies to the entire cart or only when that item is present — adjust the app setting to match your intended behavior
  5. Document your COD eligibility rules in a store policy or product description note so buyers are not surprised

Failure Mode 6: Carrier or Fulfillment Partner Does Not Support COD for the Selected Shipping Method

COD is not just a Shopify checkout configuration — it requires the physical carrier or fulfillment partner to actually collect payment at the buyer's door. If the shipping method selected by the buyer is fulfilled by a carrier that does not support COD collection in the delivery zone, offering COD at checkout creates a downstream operational failure: you ship the order as COD, the carrier delivers it and collects cash, but the integration between your fulfillment partner and your Shopify store may not reflect the payment status correctly.

Some merchants configure their COD app to suppress COD when specific shipping methods are selected (e.g., "Standard Shipping via X Carrier" — no COD, "Local Delivery" — COD allowed). When this logic breaks due to app updates, shipping zone changes, or carrier API changes, COD may disappear for shipping methods it should support or appear for methods where the carrier cannot collect.

Carriers and COD support — what to verify

Carrier Type COD Support Key Consideration
Local delivery (own staff) Full support You control collection directly — simplest COD setup
Regional courier (e.g., Aramex, DTDC, J&T) Widely supported COD collection fees apply; confirm fee structure and remittance schedule
International carriers (DHL, FedEx, UPS) Partial — specific routes only COD available in select countries; premium fees; not all service levels
Standard domestic post (USPS, Royal Mail, etc.) Generally not supported Standard postal services typically do not offer COD collection
3PL (third-party logistics) Depends on partner Verify COD capability explicitly with your 3PL; not universal

What to do if carrier COD support is the issue

  1. Contact your carrier or 3PL and confirm explicitly which shipping methods support COD collection and in which countries
  2. Configure your COD restriction app to show COD only when a COD-eligible shipping method is selected — hide COD when a carrier that cannot collect is selected
  3. For local delivery orders, consider a separate "Local Delivery — Pay on Delivery" manual payment method to distinguish local COD from carrier-collected COD
  4. Update your shipping zone configuration to ensure COD-eligible zones are served only by COD-capable carriers

Failure Mode 7: Shopify Markets Misconfiguration Suppressing COD for a Specific Market

Merchants using Shopify Markets to manage multiple geographic markets may find COD appearing in some markets but not others due to per-market payment method configuration. Shopify Markets allows different settings per market, and if COD is not explicitly enabled for a specific market — or if a market is configured without access to manual payment methods — COD will be invisible to buyers assigned to that market.

How Shopify Markets affects COD availability

  • Market without manual payment methods enabled: If a market is configured to use only specific payment providers (e.g., Shopify Payments only), manual payment methods including COD may not appear for buyers in that market, even if COD is globally enabled.
  • Buyer assigned to wrong market: Shopify automatically assigns buyers to markets based on their IP address or shipping address. If a buyer from Pakistan is assigned to the "International" market rather than a South Asia market where COD is configured, they will see the payment options for the International market (which may not include COD).
  • Market currency preventing COD activation: Some COD restriction apps or Checkout Functions check the checkout currency to determine COD eligibility. If the market currency for a buyer's region is not in the app's allowed currency list, COD is suppressed regardless of country settings.
  • New market added without COD configuration: When a new Shopify Market is created (e.g., a new Gulf region market), the default payment settings may not include COD. Always check payment method configuration when creating a new market.

How to verify and fix Markets-related COD issues

  1. Go to Shopify Admin → Settings → Markets → select the market the affected buyer belongs to
  2. Check the payment methods listed for that market — confirm COD (Manual payment methods) is included
  3. If COD is missing from the market's payment method list, add it through the market settings
  4. Test checkout using Shopify's geographic checkout preview (Admin → Online Store → Themes → Customize → Preview as customer in [country]) to simulate the buyer's market assignment
  5. If the buyer is being assigned to the wrong market, check whether their IP-based assignment matches their actual shipping country — consider explicitly binding COD to the correct market based on shipping address rather than IP
Markets Configuration is Hidden From Most Merchants
Many Shopify merchants set up Shopify Markets early in their store setup and then forget about it. When COD issues arise, they check payment settings and find COD enabled — but the issue is that the buyer's market has a separate payment configuration that overrides the global setting. Always check market-level payment settings in addition to global payment settings when diagnosing COD failures.

Failure Mode 8: Checkout App or Extension Conflict Suppressing COD

Shopify's checkout extensibility layer allows apps to modify which payment methods are shown or hidden based on cart conditions. If a checkout extension, script, or app is incorrectly targeting COD — either through a misconfigured rule, a bug, or unintended interaction with another extension — COD can be suppressed for reasons entirely unrelated to the COD configuration itself.

Common app conflicts that affect COD visibility

  • A upsell or checkout customization app hiding all manual payment methods: Some checkout apps that add upsells, order bumps, or custom flows inadvertently affect which payment methods are visible. If the app modifies the payment step rendering, manual methods like COD may be de-listed or visually hidden by the app's CSS.
  • Two COD apps active simultaneously: If you have two COD-related apps installed (e.g., one for COD availability rules and one for COD verification via OTP), they may conflict in how they apply payment method visibility logic. One app's allow-list may contradict the other's block-list.
  • A loyalty or rewards app conditionally modifying payment options: Apps that apply store credit or reward points to checkout may modify the payment step in ways that affect COD visibility, particularly if the app is designed to prioritize certain payment methods.
  • Outdated app using deprecated Shopify Scripts: Shopify Scripts (the predecessor to Checkout Functions) are deprecated and may not work correctly on all Shopify plan types or after certain platform updates. An outdated script that was hiding COD for certain conditions may start hiding COD universally when the script execution fails silently.

How to isolate a checkout app conflict

  1. Go to Shopify Admin → Apps → and identify all apps that modify or interact with the checkout payment step
  2. Temporarily disable each payment-related app one at a time (starting with the most recently installed or updated) and test checkout after each disable — when COD returns, the most recently disabled app is the cause
  3. Check the disabled app's settings for any rules that might be targeting manual payment methods or COD specifically
  4. If using Shopify Checkout Functions, go to Admin → Settings → Checkout → Checkout Functions and review any active functions — look for functions that call hidePaymentMethod and audit the conditions
  5. Contact the app developer with the specific conditions under which COD disappears — provide the cart contents, buyer location, and order amount so they can reproduce the issue
App Updates Can Break COD Without Warning
Checkout apps update frequently, and updates can change how payment method visibility rules are applied. If COD was working and then suddenly stopped without any changes on your end, check whether any of your checkout apps were recently updated. App changelog notifications are easy to miss. Subscribe to update notifications for checkout-critical apps so you can correlate COD failures with app changes quickly.

COD Operational Best Practices for Shopify Merchants

Beyond the technical setup, COD creates operational considerations that card-based payment methods do not. Merchants who rely heavily on COD should have processes in place for each of the following:

  • Order status management: COD orders start as "Payment Pending" in Shopify. You need a process for marking orders as paid after the courier confirms cash collection — either through manual updates, a COD management app, or courier API integration.
  • Non-delivery and return handling: COD return rates (orders where the buyer refuses delivery) typically run 5–30% in high-COD markets, compared to under 5% for prepaid orders. Plan your shipping cost model and return logistics accordingly.
  • COD verification (OTP or phone confirmation): Many merchants in high-COD markets use apps that require buyers to verify their order via OTP (one-time password) to their phone number before the order is confirmed. This reduces fake COD orders and return rates. Consider whether a COD verification step fits your market.
  • COD fee pass-through: Courier COD collection fees (typically 1–3% of order value) can be passed to buyers as a COD surcharge. Some COD restriction apps support adding a COD fee to the order total. Decide whether to absorb the fee or pass it through, and configure your checkout accordingly.
  • COD analytics: Track your COD conversion rate, non-delivery rate, and COD order average order value separately from prepaid orders. COD customers often have different behavior patterns — understanding this helps optimize inventory, fulfillment, and marketing spend.
Monitor COD Availability Proactively
COD failures are invisible from the Shopify admin dashboard. You will not receive any alert when COD stops appearing at checkout due to an app update, a Markets configuration drift, or a zone rule conflict. Merchants relying on COD for a significant portion of orders should test checkout end-to-end from affected buyer geographies at least weekly — or use a checkout monitoring tool to catch failures before they affect revenue.
