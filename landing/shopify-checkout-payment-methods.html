Shopify Checkout Guide

Shopify Checkout Payment Methods

Apple Pay, BNPL, international methods, and why payment options silently disappear — costing sales you never see.

Updated June 2026 12 min read Applies to all Shopify plans
Why This Matters

Missing Payment Methods Are Silent Revenue Leaks

Most Shopify merchants check their payment methods once — when they first set up the store. After that, they assume nothing changes. That assumption costs money.

Payment methods disappear for reasons that have nothing to do with your settings: a Shopify Payments account status change, an app update that breaks the checkout UI, a domain change that invalidates Apple Pay's merchant registration, or a plan downgrade that silently removes BNPL options.

The problem is visibility. Shopify's admin shows your payment configuration — the methods you have enabled. It does not show you what customers actually see when they reach checkout. A payment method can be correctly configured in admin and silently absent at checkout for a specific device, browser, or cart size.

The Revenue Cost

Apple Pay on mobile. On iPhone, Apple Pay removes the need to type 16-digit card numbers, expiry dates, and CVVs on a small screen. Stores that add Apple Pay consistently report mobile conversion lifts of 10–30%, because the barrier to completing a purchase drops from 30+ form field taps to a single Face ID authentication.

BNPL (Afterpay, Klarna, Shop Pay Installments). Buy now, pay later options increase average order value by 15–50% in apparel, home goods, electronics, and beauty — categories where cart size often determines whether the customer completes the purchase or saves it for later. The mechanism: breaking a $180 purchase into four $45 payments removes the psychological barrier of a large single charge.

PayPal for trust. For first-time visitors who don't trust an unfamiliar store with their card details, PayPal is the trust bridge. Removing it (or letting it stop working) blocks a meaningful segment of new customers who would otherwise convert.

Key Methods

The Five Payment Methods That Move Conversion

Not all payment methods have equal impact on conversion. These five are the highest-leverage options for most Shopify merchants.

01 — APPLE PAY

Apple Pay — The Mobile Conversion Multiplier

Apple Pay is the single biggest mobile checkout conversion lever for most Shopify stores. On iPhone, it reduces checkout from a 5-step form to a single Face ID or Touch ID tap. The customer never types a card number, expiry, or billing address — all of it is pre-filled from their Apple Wallet.

Requirement: Shopify Payments. When enabled, Apple Pay appears automatically on Safari on iPhone, iPad, and Mac. It does not appear on Chrome or Firefox even on Apple devices — it is Safari-only.

Common failure mode: Apple Pay disappears after a domain change. When you move from a development URL to a custom domain (or change your custom domain), Shopify must re-verify the Apple Pay merchant registration on the new domain. This usually takes minutes but can take up to 24 hours and will cause Apple Pay to silently not render for that period.

How to enable: Settings → Payments → Shopify Payments → Manage → Wallets → Apple Pay (enabled by default with Shopify Payments)
02 — SHOP PAY

Shop Pay — Shopify's Accelerated Checkout

Shop Pay remembers customer details across all Shopify stores — email, shipping address, and payment info. Returning Shopify customers can check out in two taps: enter email to receive a verification code, confirm, and the order is placed. For returning customers, Shop Pay consistently shows the highest conversion rate of any payment method on Shopify.

Requirement: Shopify Payments. Available on all plans. Shop Pay also adds Shop Pay Installments — the BNPL option that lets customers split purchases into installments.

Common failure mode: Shop Pay may not appear when the customer's browser has third-party cookies blocked, which prevents the recognition check. It also will not appear for customers using privacy browsers with aggressive tracking protection.

How to enable: Settings → Payments → Shopify Payments → Manage → Accelerated checkouts → Shop Pay
03 — BNPL

Buy Now, Pay Later — AOV Lift for Mid-to-High AOV Stores

Buy now, pay later options — Shop Pay Installments (US, CA, UK, AU), Afterpay (US, CA, AU, NZ, UK), and Klarna (EU, US) — allow customers to split purchases into installments with no interest for short-term plans. The merchant receives the full amount upfront; the BNPL provider handles the installment collection and the risk.

Impact threshold: BNPL options only show at checkout when the cart total falls within the eligible range. Shop Pay Installments in the US appears for orders from $50 to $30,000. Afterpay has similar thresholds. If the cart is below the minimum, the option simply does not appear — this is expected but often misread as a configuration problem.

Common failure mode: BNPL options stop appearing after a Shopify Payments account review or when the merchant's account is flagged. They can also fail to render on older mobile browsers that do not support the required JavaScript APIs.

How to enable: Settings → Payments → Shopify Payments → Manage → Buy now, pay later → toggle on available providers for your region
04 — PAYPAL

PayPal Express — The Trust Bridge for New Customers

PayPal serves a specific segment of customers who are unwilling to enter their card details on an unfamiliar store. These customers will not convert without a "pay with PayPal" option, because PayPal's buyer protection and the familiar interface function as a trust layer that the store itself cannot provide for first-time visitors.

Requirement: No plan restriction. Available as a separate payment method on all plans. Connects via Shopify's PayPal Express integration or through third-party gateways.

Common failure mode: PayPal popups blocked by browser popup blockers. Some customers use browsers that block all popups by default, causing the PayPal login window to not open. This looks like a broken checkout to the customer but is a browser-side configuration issue.

How to enable: Settings → Payments → Add payment method → PayPal Express → connect your PayPal business account
05 — LOCAL METHODS

International Payment Methods — Essential for Markets Merchants

If you sell internationally, the payment methods you offer must match what customers in each country actually use. iDEAL is the dominant payment method in the Netherlands (used for over 60% of online payments). Bancontact dominates Belgium. SEPA Direct Debit is essential for EU B2B purchases. Ignoring local payment preferences in key markets is equivalent to not offering a checkout at all for a meaningful share of those visitors.

Requirement: Shopify Payments must be set up for each market's currency and country, or a gateway that supports those local methods must be used. Shopify Markets automates much of this configuration, but payment method availability still depends on your gateway's support for each local method.

Common failure mode: When enabling Shopify Markets, local payment methods may not automatically appear even if your gateway supports them. They must be explicitly enabled per market in Settings → Payments. Merchants frequently set up markets and assume local payment methods are active — they are not by default.

How to enable: Settings → Markets → [market] → Preferences → Payment methods → review which methods are active per market
Silent Failures

Why Payment Methods Silently Stop Showing

The most dangerous payment method failures are not errors — they are absences. A payment method that was working yesterday simply stops appearing today, with no error message, no admin notification, and no obvious signal until customers start abandoning checkout or contacting support.

Silent Failure Risk

Shopify's admin shows your payment method configuration — the methods you have enabled. It does not show what customers see in a live checkout, on a specific device, at a specific cart size. A method can be correctly configured and silently absent at checkout. The only way to know is to run a real checkout test.

Cause 1 — Shopify Payments Account Status Change

Shopify monitors merchant accounts for risk signals: high chargeback rates, product category flags, identity verification requirements, or manual review triggers. When an account enters a review state, Apple Pay, Google Pay, Shop Pay, and BNPL options may stop appearing — even if the underlying credit card processing continues to work. You may receive an email about the review, or you may not. The payment methods section in admin may continue to show these options as "enabled."

Cause 2 — Domain Change Without Re-verification

Apple Pay requires merchant domain verification — Shopify registers your domain with Apple as an authorized Apple Pay merchant. When you change your store's domain (or add a new custom domain), Shopify initiates a re-verification. Until this completes, Apple Pay will not appear on the new domain. This is one of the most common causes of Apple Pay disappearing after a domain migration and can take up to 24 hours to resolve automatically.

Cause 3 — App Conflict in Checkout

Checkout apps — theme scripts, upsell widgets, analytics injections, chat widgets — run JavaScript in the checkout page. A JavaScript error from any of these apps can interfere with the payment section rendering. The symptom: the payment section loads but payment methods are missing, appear with broken styling, or the "Place order" button is unresponsive. This most commonly happens after an app update, a theme change, or adding a new app.

Cause 4 — BNPL Cart Amount Thresholds

This is expected behavior, not a bug, but it causes significant merchant confusion. BNPL providers (Afterpay, Shop Pay Installments, Klarna) only appear when the cart total falls within their eligible range. For most US providers, the minimum is $35–$50 and the maximum is $2,000–$30,000. A cart below the minimum threshold shows no BNPL option. Merchants set up BNPL, test it with a $12 item, and conclude it is not working — but it is working correctly for eligible cart sizes.

Cause 5 — Browser and Device Restrictions

Apple Pay only appears in Safari on Apple devices. Google Pay only appears in Chrome. Meta Pay requires Facebook login state in the browser. Privacy browsers (Brave, Firefox with strict mode, DuckDuckGo) may block the recognition checks that Shop Pay and Apple Pay rely on, causing them to not render even for customers who use them regularly on other browsers.

Setup Guide

How to Set Up Shopify Payment Methods Correctly

Follow this sequence to maximize checkout conversion through payment method coverage.

01
Enable Shopify Payments first. If you are in a supported country (US, CA, UK, AU, IE, NZ, and others), switch to Shopify Payments as your primary gateway. This unlocks Apple Pay, Google Pay, Shop Pay, Meta Pay, and BNPL in a single step — and eliminates transaction fees on top of your plan subscription. Go to Settings → Payments → Shopify Payments and complete the account setup including identity verification.
02
Enable all wallet methods under Shopify Payments. After Shopify Payments is active, go to Settings → Payments → Shopify Payments → Manage → Accelerated checkouts. Enable Shop Pay, Apple Pay, Google Pay, and Meta Pay. All four are off by default in some configurations. Enabling all four costs nothing and adds meaningful conversion lift.
03
Enable BNPL for your eligible markets. Still inside Shopify Payments → Manage, find the Buy now, pay later section. Enable Shop Pay Installments if you are in the US (requires minimum AOV eligibility) and Afterpay/Clearpay if you are in US, CA, AU, NZ, or UK. For EU stores, check if Klarna is available for your country. BNPL setup may require agreeing to additional terms with each provider.
04
Add PayPal Express as a secondary method. Go to Settings → Payments → Add payment method → search for PayPal Express → connect your PayPal business account. PayPal handles its own login and credentialing. Once connected, it appears as a separate payment option at checkout alongside your Shopify Payments methods.
05
Configure international methods per market. If you use Shopify Markets, go to Settings → Markets → [each active market] → Preferences. Review the payment methods available for that market. Enable local methods (iDEAL for Netherlands, Bancontact for Belgium, SEPA for EU B2B) where your gateway supports them. Do not assume they activate automatically when you enable a market.
06
Run a checkout test from an iPhone in Safari. This is the single most important verification step. Open your store on an iPhone in Safari, add an item to cart, and proceed to checkout. Confirm Apple Pay appears as a payment option. Add a low-value item and a high-value item to verify BNPL thresholds. Test the same checkout in Chrome on Android to verify Google Pay appears. Record what you see — not what the admin shows.
07
Set up automated checkout monitoring. Manual testing catches the current state. Automated monitoring catches changes — app updates, Shopify Payments status changes, or domain issues — before they cost meaningful revenue. A checkout monitor that runs hourly will detect a missing payment method the same hour it disappears rather than days later when a customer emails asking why Apple Pay is gone.

FAQ

Shopify Payment Methods — Common Questions

Why is Apple Pay not showing at Shopify checkout?

Apple Pay not appearing is usually caused by one of these: (1) the customer is using Chrome or Firefox — Apple Pay only renders in Safari on Apple devices; (2) no card is saved in the customer's Apple Wallet; (3) your store is not on Shopify Payments — Apple Pay is not available via third-party gateways; (4) your domain recently changed and the Apple Pay merchant registration is re-verifying; (5) your Shopify Payments account is under review; (6) Apple Pay was manually disabled under Settings → Payments → Shopify Payments → Manage → Wallets. Verify all six, then test in Safari on iPhone with a card saved in Apple Wallet.

How do I add Afterpay or Klarna to Shopify checkout?

Via Shopify Payments: go to Settings → Payments → Shopify Payments → Manage → Buy now, pay later. Enable Afterpay/Clearpay (available in US, CA, AU, NZ, UK) or Klarna (select EU and US markets). These will appear at checkout for eligible cart amounts. Without Shopify Payments: add Afterpay or Klarna as a separate payment method under Settings → Payments → Add payment method. You need an active merchant account with that BNPL provider. Always test after enabling — BNPL methods only appear above their minimum cart threshold (typically $35–$50).

Why did payment methods disappear from my Shopify checkout?

The most common causes: (1) Shopify Payments account entered a review or restricted state; (2) a checkout app JavaScript error is preventing the payment section from rendering correctly; (3) your domain changed and Apple Pay's merchant registration is reverifying; (4) a plan downgrade removed payment options; (5) the customer's cart is below a BNPL provider's minimum threshold. Check your Shopify Payments status in admin first, then run a test checkout to see what is actually appearing. If admin shows methods as enabled but they don't appear at checkout, a checkout app conflict is the most likely culprit.

Does Shopify charge extra fees for payment methods?

Shopify Payments methods (Apple Pay, Google Pay, Shop Pay, BNPL) carry no additional transaction fees beyond Shopify Payments' standard card processing rates. Third-party gateways (PayPal, Amazon Pay, Klarna via direct integration) incur Shopify's additional transaction fee: 2% on Basic, 1% on Shopify, 0.5% on Advanced, 0.15%–0.20% on Plus (negotiated). BNPL providers charge the merchant a small fee per transaction (typically 4–6% + fixed fee) — this is separate from Shopify's fees and is charged by the BNPL provider. For high-volume stores, the BNPL economics depend on the AOV lift they generate vs the per-transaction cost.

Do more payment methods slow down Shopify checkout?

Shopify Payments methods (Apple Pay, Google Pay, Shop Pay, BNPL via Shopify) are hosted and rendered by Shopify — they do not add meaningful load time because they load from Shopify's infrastructure alongside the rest of the checkout page. Third-party gateways (PayPal, Klarna via direct integration, Amazon Pay) load their own JavaScript and may add a small overhead, but typically 100–300ms which is within acceptable thresholds. The performance risk comes not from payment methods themselves but from apps that inject JavaScript into the checkout payment section — upsell apps, custom checkout scripts, and analytics trackers can add 500ms–2s+ to the payment section load time if poorly optimized. Payment method diversity alone is almost always worth the negligible load overhead.
