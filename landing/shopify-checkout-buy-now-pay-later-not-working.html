Shopify Buy Now Pay Later Not Working — Klarna, Afterpay, Affirm Not Showing (2026)

Klarna not showing at checkout, Afterpay unavailable, Affirm not rendering, Shop Pay Instalments missing — here are all 8 failure modes for BNPL breaking at Shopify checkout, with exact diagnostic steps and fixes for Klarna, Afterpay, Affirm, Zip, Sezzle, and Shop Pay Instalments.

Updated June 2026 8 failure modes Geo Restriction · Order Range · Not Enabled · Category Excluded · Buyer Eligibility · Gateway Conflict · Extension Conflict · Provider Outage

Buy now pay later (BNPL) payment options — Klarna, Afterpay, Affirm, Zip, Sezzle, and Shop Pay Instalments — have become an expected part of Shopify checkout for many buyer segments, particularly on higher-ticket purchases. When BNPL doesn't appear, buyers who were counting on splitting their purchase may reduce their order size, delay the purchase, or abandon entirely.

BNPL failures at Shopify checkout are rarely caused by a single obvious error. They involve an intersection of merchant settings, buyer eligibility, geographic availability, gateway configuration, and real-time underwriting decisions made by the BNPL provider. Many failures are silent — the option simply doesn't render, with no error message for the buyer or the merchant.

First: Identify Which BNPL Integration Path You're Using
Shopify offers BNPL in two ways: (1) Native via Shopify Payments — Affirm (as Shop Pay Instalments), Afterpay/Clearpay, and Klarna are available as part of the Shopify Payments package in supported regions. (2) Standalone app integrations — each BNPL provider also has a standalone Shopify app that works independently of Shopify Payments. The failure mode diagnosis differs significantly between these two paths. Before troubleshooting, go to Admin → Settings → Payments and identify whether your BNPL option appears under Shopify Payments or as a separate payment provider.

Failure Mode 1: BNPL Provider Not Available in the Buyer's Country

The most common reason BNPL options don't appear at Shopify checkout is geographic availability. Every BNPL provider operates in specific markets and uses real-time geolocation to determine whether a buyer is eligible to see the option. The option is hidden server-side before it ever renders — merchants see no error, buyers see no option, and there is no indication that anything went wrong.

Geographic availability by provider

  • Klarna: Available in the US, UK, Canada, Australia, Germany, Austria, Netherlands, Belgium, France, Spain, Italy, Sweden, Norway, Denmark, Finland, Poland, and a growing list of markets. Buyers in unsupported countries will not see Klarna even if the merchant has it enabled. Klarna's availability also varies by product type within countries.
  • Afterpay / Clearpay: Available in Australia, New Zealand, the US, Canada, and the UK (where it operates as Clearpay). Afterpay is not available in continental Europe, Asia, or Latin America. Buyers outside these markets will not see the option.
  • Affirm: Available in the US and Canada. Non-US/Canada buyers will not see Affirm regardless of merchant configuration. Shop Pay Instalments (powered by Affirm) has the same geographic restriction.
  • Zip (formerly Quadpay): Available in the US, Australia, UK, and a handful of other markets. Zip's availability within markets also varies by merchant category.
  • Sezzle: Available primarily in the US and Canada.

Diagnosing geographic availability issues

  1. Test checkout from the same country as your primary buyer base — use a VPN set to the buyer's expected country if testing remotely
  2. Check the BNPL provider's official documentation for the current list of supported markets — availability changes as providers expand
  3. If you're seeing BNPL in your own test but buyers in specific countries report not seeing it, the issue is geographic restriction, not a configuration error
  4. For international stores, consider offering multiple BNPL providers to cover different market geographies — Klarna for Europe, Afterpay for AU/NZ/US, Affirm for US/CA
Merchant Location vs. Buyer Location
BNPL availability is determined by the buyer's geolocated country, not the merchant's store country. A US merchant with Afterpay enabled will not show Afterpay to a buyer located in Germany. Conversely, a German merchant with Klarna enabled will show Klarna to a US buyer if Klarna is also active for US buyers in that merchant's Klarna configuration. Always test from the buyer's geography, not your own.

Failure Mode 2: Order Total Outside the Eligible Range

Every BNPL provider has minimum and maximum order value thresholds. Orders below the minimum are not eligible for instalment payments — the option simply doesn't render. Orders above the maximum may be blocked by the provider's underwriting engine. These thresholds vary by provider, by country, and sometimes by individual buyer account history with the provider.

Order value thresholds by provider

  • Klarna: Minimum varies by country (often $10–$35 USD equivalent). Maximum varies by buyer's Klarna credit limit (determined at runtime by Klarna's underwriting). The merchant cannot see why a specific buyer was declined.
  • Afterpay: Minimum of $1 in most markets. Maximum order limit is $2,000 in the US; $2,000 AUD in Australia. Individual buyer limits may be lower than the provider maximum based on the buyer's Afterpay account history.
  • Affirm / Shop Pay Instalments: Minimum of $50 in the US. Maximum underwriting limit is $17,500 (Affirm's programmatic limit). Orders below $50 will not display Affirm as an option even if all other conditions are met.
  • Zip: Minimum typically $35. Maximum $1,500 in the US; $2,000 AUD in Australia.
  • Sezzle: No hard minimum published; typical orders start at $35+. Maximum is determined by buyer's Sezzle spending limit.
The $50 Minimum Is the Most Common Miss for Affirm / Shop Pay Instalments
The Affirm / Shop Pay Instalments $50 minimum order threshold catches many merchants off guard. If you sell products below $50, those products will never show Shop Pay Instalments — even if the merchant has it enabled and the buyer is in the US. This threshold cannot be configured or overridden by the merchant. If your average order value is below $50, Affirm-based BNPL is not a viable option for your store and you should look at Klarna or Afterpay, which have lower minimums.

What to do about order range exclusions

  1. Check your average order value against the minimum thresholds for each BNPL provider you have enabled — if AOV is below the minimum, the BNPL option will rarely if ever appear
  2. Consider offering order-level discounts or bundle incentives that push cart values above the BNPL threshold for high-margin products
  3. For high-ticket stores where the maximum limit is the constraint, Klarna's higher-credit underwriting or a direct Affirm integration (not Shop Pay Instalments) may offer higher approval rates for premium buyers
  4. If buyers report that BNPL appeared for them previously but not now, check whether their cart total changed (e.g., a coupon brought it below the minimum)

Failure Mode 3: BNPL Not Enabled in Payment Settings

BNPL options at Shopify checkout require explicit activation — they are not on by default. Both the native Shopify Payments BNPL options and standalone app integrations require a setup step in the merchant's Admin. Merchants who assumed BNPL was included automatically after installing an app or enabling Shopify Payments are the most common source of this failure.

Where to enable BNPL in Shopify Admin

  • Shop Pay Instalments (Affirm): Admin → Settings → Payments → Shopify Payments → Manage → scroll to "Accelerated checkouts" or "Shop Pay Instalments" → toggle on. This is separate from enabling Shop Pay (the accelerated checkout). Enabling Shop Pay does NOT automatically enable Shop Pay Instalments.
  • Afterpay / Clearpay via Shopify Payments: Admin → Settings → Payments → Shopify Payments → Manage → "Buy now, pay later" section → enable Afterpay/Clearpay. Available only in supported markets.
  • Klarna via Shopify Payments: Admin → Settings → Payments → Shopify Payments → Manage → enable Klarna. Not available in all Shopify Payments markets.
  • Standalone BNPL apps (Klarna app, Afterpay app, Zip app, etc.): Install the app from the Shopify App Store → follow the app's activation flow → the provider will appear as a separate payment option under Admin → Settings → Payments → "Alternative payment methods."

Afterpay / Clearpay Native or Standalone app Shopify Payments → Manage → BNPL section

Always Do a Post-Setup Test in Incognito
After enabling any BNPL option, always run a test checkout in an incognito window as a buyer to confirm the option appears. Admin-side toggles do not always reflect immediately in the buyer-facing checkout — there can be a cache delay of a few minutes. Use a real (non-test-mode) checkout flow to confirm the BNPL option renders in the payment step.

Failure Mode 4: Product Category Excluded by BNPL Provider

Every BNPL provider maintains a list of excluded product categories that are not eligible for instalment payments. These exclusions are enforced at the session level — if any item in the cart belongs to an excluded category, the BNPL option will not render for that entire checkout session. This is one of the most confusing failure modes because the exclusion can affect otherwise eligible orders.

Commonly excluded product categories

  • Gift cards and store credit: All major BNPL providers (Klarna, Afterpay, Affirm, Zip) exclude gift cards and store credit purchases. If a buyer's cart contains a gift card product, BNPL will not render for that entire cart — even if other non-gift-card items are in the cart.
  • Firearms, weapons, and ammunition: Excluded by all major BNPL providers under their terms of service. If your store sells firearms or related products, BNPL options will not be available.
  • Adult content and services: Excluded by most BNPL providers. Merchants in this category cannot offer BNPL regardless of configuration.
  • Gambling and cryptocurrency: Excluded by all major BNPL providers.
  • Tobacco and vaping products: Excluded by most BNPL providers; some providers (particularly Klarna) may allow this in certain markets where tobacco retail is standard.
  • Services without a physical delivery: Some BNPL providers restrict instalment payments to tangible goods; digital services, consulting, or subscription-only products may be excluded depending on the provider.
  • Prepaid products and top-ups: Excluded by most providers for the same reason as gift cards.
A Single Gift Card in the Cart Blocks BNPL for the Entire Checkout
The most surprising category exclusion for Shopify merchants: if a buyer adds one gift card product to a cart that also contains $400 worth of eligible products, BNPL will not render for that entire checkout session. The gift card exclusion applies at the cart level, not the product level. Merchants selling mixed carts (products + gift cards) should consider whether to remove gift cards from their cart implementation or warn buyers that adding a gift card will remove BNPL payment options.

Diagnosing category exclusions

  1. Test BNPL with a cart containing only a single, clearly eligible physical product — if BNPL shows up, the issue is a specific product type in your typical cart
  2. Test with a cart that mirrors your typical buyer's cart composition — note whether BNPL disappears when certain product types are added
  3. Review each BNPL provider's terms of service for the specific categories they exclude in your market — exclusion lists vary between providers
  4. If gift cards are causing the exclusion, consider separating gift card purchases into a dedicated checkout flow that does not offer BNPL, and clearly label it as such

Failure Mode 5: Buyer Eligibility Declined by Real-Time Underwriting

BNPL providers perform a real-time soft credit check or eligibility assessment every time a buyer reaches checkout. This assessment is invisible to merchants — there is no webhook, no admin notification, and no error log when a buyer is declined. The BNPL option simply doesn't render for that specific buyer in that specific checkout session, even if everything else is configured correctly.

What triggers buyer-level BNPL ineligibility

  • Buyer has exceeded their BNPL spending limit: All BNPL providers assign buyers a credit limit. Buyers who have multiple open BNPL installment plans or who have reached their provider-assigned limit will not see the option. This is the most common buyer-side exclusion and is entirely outside the merchant's control.
  • Buyer has a delinquent BNPL payment: Buyers with missed payments on existing Klarna, Afterpay, or Affirm plans are typically blocked from initiating new BNPL purchases until their outstanding balance is resolved. The buyer sees no explanation — just the absence of the BNPL option.
  • Buyer is a new BNPL account with low initial limit: New Afterpay and Klarna accounts start with a lower initial spending limit and build over time. A new buyer trying to use BNPL for a $500 order may find the option unavailable because their initial credit limit is $150.
  • Buyer's age is below the provider's minimum: BNPL requires buyers to be 18+ in most markets. Providers attempt to verify this at sign-up but may also enforce it at the session level in certain markets.
  • Buyer's billing address doesn't match their BNPL account: Some providers cross-check the billing address entered at checkout against the buyer's registered BNPL account address. A mismatch can trigger an eligibility check failure.
Merchants Cannot See Why a Buyer Was Declined
BNPL providers do not expose underwriting decisions to merchants — this is a deliberate privacy protection for buyers. When a merchant asks "why isn't Klarna showing for my customer?", the answer may be that the buyer has exceeded their limit or has a delinquent payment. Merchants cannot override these eligibility checks. The most useful thing you can do is ensure your checkout has clear alternative payment methods so buyers who can't use BNPL are not stranded.

Failure Mode 6: Third-Party Gateway Conflict

Shopify's native BNPL integrations — Shop Pay Instalments (Affirm), Afterpay via Shopify Payments, and Klarna via Shopify Payments — are only available when Shopify Payments is the active primary gateway. When merchants switch to a third-party gateway (Stripe, Braintree, Authorize.net, PayPal as primary, Square, or any other processor), native BNPL options are removed from the checkout without a warning or notification.

How the gateway conflict works

  • Native BNPL is bundled with Shopify Payments: Shopify's native BNPL options are built on top of Shopify Payments' payment processing infrastructure. They cannot be offered independently — they require Shopify Payments to be the primary gateway to function.
  • Switching gateways silently removes BNPL: When you change your primary gateway from Shopify Payments to a third-party provider, Shopify removes all native BNPL options from checkout. There is no confirmation step warning you that BNPL will be lost. Merchants often discover this only when buyers report that BNPL is gone.
  • Third-party gateway + standalone BNPL app = the workaround: If you need a third-party gateway (for specific pricing, features, or regional requirements), you can restore BNPL by installing the BNPL provider's standalone Shopify app. Klarna, Afterpay, Affirm, Zip, and Sezzle all have standalone apps that function independently of Shopify Payments and appear as separate payment options under "Alternative payment methods."
  • Standalone app BNPL has different UX: BNPL through a standalone app appears as a separate payment button rather than integrated into the Shopify Payments payment block. The buyer selects it as a distinct option. This is a minor UX difference but worth noting for checkout design.
Gateway Configuration Native BNPL Available? Standalone BNPL App Available?
Shopify Payments as primary gateway Yes Yes (both can coexist)
Third-party gateway (Stripe, Braintree, etc.) No Yes — requires standalone app install
PayPal as primary gateway No Yes — requires standalone app install
BNPL Loss Is a Common Hidden Cost of Switching Gateways
When merchants evaluate switching from Shopify Payments to a third-party gateway for lower transaction fees or specific features, they often don't factor in the cost of losing native BNPL. If BNPL is driving a meaningful percentage of your higher-ticket orders, losing it can offset the fee savings from the gateway switch. Before switching gateways, calculate your BNPL attach rate (what % of orders use BNPL) and estimate the conversion impact of losing it.

Failure Mode 7: Checkout Extension Conflict Hiding BNPL

Third-party Checkout UI Extensions installed via the Shopify App Store can interfere with BNPL rendering. Extensions that modify the payment section, redirect the checkout flow, or override the payment method display can inadvertently suppress BNPL options — or conflict with the specific JavaScript that BNPL providers inject into the checkout.

How extensions break BNPL rendering

  • Extension modifying the payment methods section: Extensions that add custom content to or around the payment step can interfere with how Shopify renders alternative payment options including BNPL. If the extension hooks into the payment method section and the implementation is not compatible with BNPL provider widgets, the BNPL option may be hidden or fail to initialize.
  • Extension applying conditional logic that filters payment options: Some loyalty, gifting, or B2B extensions apply conditional visibility rules to payment methods. If a rule inadvertently targets BNPL payment method IDs (by matching on method name or type), it can hide BNPL for buyers who match the condition.
  • Post-purchase extension conflict: Extensions that run after payment but before the confirmation page can conflict with BNPL providers' confirmation flows — particularly Klarna and Afterpay, which redirect buyers through the provider's site and back to Shopify's confirmation page. An extension that doesn't handle these return redirects correctly may break the BNPL confirmation flow.
  • Extension introducing a JavaScript error that cascades: If an extension throws an unhandled JavaScript exception in the checkout page's runtime, it can prevent the payment method initialization script from executing — causing all alternative payment options, including BNPL, to fail silently.

How to isolate an extension conflict

  1. Go to Admin → Settings → Checkout → Customize → toggle off all active extensions → run a test checkout in incognito to see if BNPL appears
  2. If BNPL appears with all extensions off, re-enable extensions one at a time, testing after each re-enable, until you identify the conflicting extension
  3. Open browser DevTools → Console during checkout with extensions enabled — look for uncaught JavaScript errors that appear when the payment step loads
  4. Contact the app developer for the conflicting extension with your console error log — ask them to test specifically against the BNPL options you use
  5. As a short-term fix, use the Shopify checkout editor to configure the conflicting extension to be hidden on the payment step while keeping it active on other checkout steps
BNPL Extension Conflicts Have No Visible Error for the Buyer
When an extension conflict hides BNPL, buyers see a normal checkout — just without the BNPL option they were expecting. They don't receive an error message. They don't know the option should be there. They simply don't see it and either proceed without BNPL or abandon. This means BNPL extension conflicts are entirely invisible in your standard analytics. Only checkout monitoring that tracks payment option render rates will surface this class of failure.

Failure Mode 8: BNPL Provider API Outage or Timeout

BNPL options at Shopify checkout load via a real-time API call to the BNPL provider. Shopify's checkout page requests the list of available payment options at page load — if the BNPL provider's API is down, degraded, or timing out, the option simply does not render. The buyer sees a checkout without BNPL; Shopify's admin shows no errors; and the issue resolves itself when the provider restores service.

How BNPL API outages present

  • BNPL disappears for all buyers simultaneously: Unlike buyer eligibility failures (which affect individual buyers) or category exclusions (which affect specific products), a provider API outage affects all buyers at the same time. If BNPL disappears across your entire store and multiple buyers report it simultaneously, this is the most likely cause.
  • BNPL intermittently appears and disappears: API degradation (high latency, partial outages) can cause BNPL to render for some checkout sessions but not others, depending on whether the API call times out before checkout renders. This is harder to diagnose because it's not a consistent failure.
  • Other Shopify merchants see the same issue: Provider outages affect all merchants using that BNPL option — not just your store. If your BNPL stops working, check the provider's status page and community forums for reports from other merchants.

How to respond to a BNPL provider outage

  1. Check the BNPL provider's official status page: status.klarna.com, status.afterpay.com, Affirm's status page, or the provider's equivalent
  2. Check community forums (Shopify Community, Reddit r/shopify) for reports from other merchants experiencing the same issue at the same time
  3. Do not attempt to fix a provider outage by modifying your settings — changes made during an outage can create a second issue that persists after the outage resolves
  4. If the outage is prolonged (more than 30 minutes), consider posting a brief notice at checkout: "Instalment payment options temporarily unavailable — we apologize for the inconvenience" so buyers who expected BNPL aren't confused
  5. File a support ticket with the BNPL provider after the outage resolves if you need a timeline or incident report for your records
Outage Pattern Most Likely Cause Action
BNPL gone for all buyers, all products, started suddenly Provider API outage Check provider status page; wait for resolution
BNPL gone for one buyer, works for others Buyer eligibility declined Inform buyer to check their BNPL account
BNPL intermittent (sometimes shows, sometimes doesn't) API degradation or timeout Monitor provider status; log timestamps of failures
BNPL gone after a settings change Configuration change removed BNPL Review recent changes; re-enable or revert
Provider Outages Are More Common Than Merchants Expect
Major BNPL providers have multiple outages per year that affect checkout rendering. Klarna, Afterpay, and Affirm all maintain public status pages but don't always update them in real time. If your BNPL disappeared suddenly and you've ruled out configuration changes, check the provider's status page first — you may be dealing with an incident that will resolve without any action on your part. Without checkout monitoring, merchants often spend 30-60 minutes troubleshooting their own settings before realizing it's a provider-side issue.
