Shopify Payment Failed?
7 Causes & How to Fix Each

Updated June 2026  ·  9 min read  ·  By CheckPulse Team

A failed payment is money that never arrives. Unlike a slow page or a broken image, a payment failure means a customer tried to give you money — and couldn't. On a busy store, even one hour of silent payment failures can erase hundreds or thousands of dollars in revenue before anyone notices.

This guide covers the seven most common reasons Shopify payments fail, how to tell them apart from the error messages and order data, and exactly what to do to fix each one.

⚠ The silent failure problem

Most payment failures produce no Shopify admin notification. Customers abandon. You see a dip in orders hours later — or not until the next day. By then, the damage is done. Automated checkout monitoring is the only way to catch this in real time.

CAUSE 01 Payment Gateway Timeout or Connection Error

Your payment processor (Stripe, Braintree, PayPal, Adyen, etc.) is reachable but taking too long to respond, or the connection between Shopify's servers and the gateway is dropping. This is the most common type of payment failure on high-traffic days — flash sales, Black Friday, product launches.

Symptoms:

  • Customers see "We're sorry — there was a problem processing your payment" or similar
  • The order does not appear in Shopify admin
  • Gateway dashboard shows no corresponding transaction attempt
  • Issue starts suddenly and affects multiple customers at the same time
▶ How to fix
  • Check your payment gateway's status page immediately (Stripe: status.stripe.com, PayPal: status.paypal.com)
  • If gateway is degraded: enable a backup payment method temporarily (PayPal as a secondary option, or manual bank transfer for high-value orders)
  • Check Shopify Status (shopifystatus.com) — sometimes the issue is on Shopify's side, not the gateway
  • If persistent and gateway shows green: contact gateway support with timestamps of failures
  • Review gateway API credentials in Shopify Admin → Settings → Payments — re-entering credentials sometimes resolves stale token issues
CAUSE 02 3D Secure (3DS) Authentication Failure

3D Secure is the bank-triggered authentication step (the popup or redirect where customers verify their identity with their bank). Since PSD2 regulations in Europe and increasing SCA requirements globally, 3DS failures have become one of the fastest-growing causes of abandoned payments. The customer's card is fine — but the authentication step fails or times out.

Symptoms:

  • Customer sees a bank popup or redirect that either errors out or closes unexpectedly
  • Gateway shows the charge as "authentication failed" or "3DS challenge failed"
  • Affects European customers disproportionately (SCA requirement)
  • Issue may occur in specific browsers (Safari cookie restrictions can break 3DS popups)
// Gateway error code (Stripe example)
payment_intent.payment_failed
decline_code: "authentication_required"
message: "Your card was declined. This transaction requires authentication."
▶ How to fix
  • Verify your gateway supports 3DS2 (the newer, less disruptive version) — most modern gateways do, but confirm in gateway settings
  • Enable "Smart 3DS" or "Adaptive authentication" in your gateway dashboard to only challenge high-risk transactions
  • Test the checkout flow specifically in Safari and Firefox — Safari's ITP (Intelligent Tracking Prevention) can break some 3DS popups
  • If using Shopify Payments: ensure your store's fraud protection settings aren't triggering manual review for otherwise low-risk orders
  • Add a note to the checkout page informing customers that their bank may request verification — it reduces abandonment when the popup appears
CAUSE 03 Shopify Payments Account Hold or Suspension

Shopify Payments (powered by Stripe) can place your payouts on hold or suspend payment processing entirely due to risk reviews, high chargeback rates, suspicious activity patterns, or policy violations. When this happens, payments at checkout fail silently — the customer gets a generic error and you may not receive a clear notification.

Symptoms:

  • All Shopify Payments transactions fail; third-party gateways (PayPal) still work
  • Shopify admin shows a banner in the Payments section or Home
  • You received an email from Shopify risk team (check spam)
  • Payout schedule shows "On hold" or "Under review"
▶ How to fix
  • Immediately check Shopify Admin → Settings → Payments → Shopify Payments for any notifications or required actions
  • Check the email address associated with your Shopify account, including spam — Shopify risk team sends notices here
  • Enable PayPal Express Checkout as a backup while the hold is under review so you don't lose sales completely
  • Respond to the risk team's request promptly — holds are often resolved within 1-3 business days with proper documentation
  • Common resolution requests: business registration documents, supplier invoices, refund policy, website review
Prevention

Shopify Payments monitors chargeback rates. Rates above 1% of transactions trigger automatic review. Proactively manage chargebacks: respond to disputes, use clear billing descriptors, and send proactive order confirmation emails.

CAUSE 04 Card Decline (Bank-Side, Not a Gateway Issue)

Sometimes the payment fails not because of anything on your store — the customer's bank is declining the charge. This can happen due to insufficient funds, suspicious activity flags on the customer's account, international transaction blocks, or daily spending limits. This is the least actionable cause from the merchant's side, but it's important to identify it correctly so you don't waste time debugging your checkout.

Symptoms:

  • Individual customer reports failure; other customers checkout fine simultaneously
  • Gateway shows "do_not_honor", "insufficient_funds", "card_velocity_exceeded", or "restricted_card"
  • Customer reports their card works on other sites
▶ How to fix
  • Show a helpful error message: "Your bank declined this charge. Please try a different card or contact your bank." — don't show raw error codes to customers
  • Offer alternative payment methods: PayPal, Apple Pay, Google Pay, Buy Now Pay Later (Shop Pay Installments, Klarna, Afterpay)
  • If a high-value B2B customer is affected: offer invoice payment or bank transfer as a manual alternative
  • Review your average decline rate in gateway dashboard monthly — a sudden spike indicates a different underlying issue
CAUSE 05 Currency or Region Mismatch

If your store uses Shopify Markets or a currency conversion app, a mismatch between the displayed currency and the currency your gateway is configured to settle in can cause payment failures. This is especially common after adding new markets, changing payment gateways, or installing currency conversion apps.

Symptoms:

  • Payments fail for customers in specific countries or currency regions
  • Gateway shows "currency_not_supported" or "invalid_currency" errors
  • Domestics customers checkout fine; international customers fail
  • Issue started after a Shopify Markets configuration change or app install
▶ How to fix
  • In Shopify Admin → Settings → Payments, verify the currencies enabled match what your gateway actually supports
  • Check your gateway's supported currency list in its own dashboard — not all currencies are supported by all gateways
  • If using a currency converter app, check for conflicts with Shopify Markets — some apps are incompatible with the built-in Markets currency handling
  • Test checkout as a customer in each major target market using a VPN — this is the only reliable way to catch region-specific failures
  • If using Shopify Payments: multi-currency is only supported in certain countries. Check supported countries in Shopify Payments documentation
CAUSE 06 Fraud Filter Blocking Legitimate Orders

Shopify's built-in fraud analysis and third-party fraud prevention apps (NoFraud, Signifyd, Kount) can block legitimate orders when configured too aggressively. The customer's payment method is valid, but the order is flagged and declined before it's processed. This is sometimes called "false positive fraud" and is more common on stores that sell high-value, digital, or international orders.

Symptoms:

  • Orders appear in Shopify admin as "high risk" or are cancelled automatically
  • Gateway shows no error (payment was never attempted) or "blocked by fraud rule"
  • Customers report being declined despite a valid card
  • Issue concentrated on specific customer segments: new customers, international, high-value orders
▶ How to fix
  • Review Shopify Admin → Orders → filter by "High risk" — look for patterns (are these legitimate order types for your store?)
  • In fraud prevention app settings, review which rules are triggering most often and adjust thresholds — most apps show a "reason" for each blocked order
  • For Shopify Payments: the fraud filter can be adjusted under Settings → Payments → Fraud prevention — disable automatic order cancellation and switch to manual review
  • Add address verification (AVS) and CVV requirements to reduce fraud while maintaining legitimate conversion rate
  • For flagged customers you know are legitimate: create a draft order in Shopify admin and send them a payment link directly
CAUSE 07 Checkout App Conflict

Apps that modify the checkout — upsell apps, post-purchase apps, checkout customization apps, loyalty apps — can break payment processing when they conflict with each other or with a Shopify update. This is one of the hardest causes to diagnose because the app may have worked fine for months before a platform update breaks the integration.

Symptoms:

  • Payment failures started after installing a new app or a Shopify platform update
  • Checkout works fine without specific app features enabled
  • Console errors in browser developer tools during payment step
  • Gateway receives malformed or incomplete payment data
▶ How to fix
  • Use a staged rollback: disable checkout-modifying apps one at a time and test after each — identify which app broke payment
  • Check for Shopify announcements in the Partner Dashboard about deprecated checkout APIs — Shopify's checkout extensibility migration broke many older apps
  • Review app changelog and support forum for reports of payment issues after recent updates
  • If you recently migrated to Shopify Checkout Extensibility: ensure all apps have updated to the new checkout UI extensions (old ScriptTag-based apps are no longer supported)
  • Contact app support with your store URL and specific failure timestamp — most can pull server-side logs to identify the conflict

Quick Diagnostic Checklist

  • Is the failure affecting all customers or specific customers?
  • Does the gateway dashboard show any failed transaction attempts?
  • Is the gateway status page showing any incidents?
  • Is Shopify Payments showing any holds or alerts in admin?
  • Did the issue start after a new app install or Shopify update?
  • Do European customers fail more than domestic customers? (3DS)
  • Does checkout work with test card but not real cards?

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does Shopify say "payment declined" but my bank shows no charge attempt?

This usually means the payment was blocked before reaching your bank — either by Shopify's fraud prevention, a checkout app conflict, or a gateway configuration issue. Check Shopify admin for risk flags on the order and check your gateway dashboard for the transaction attempt.

How do I know if it's a Shopify problem vs. my payment gateway's problem?

Try placing a test order using a different payment method (e.g., PayPal if your main gateway is Stripe). If PayPal works, the issue is with your primary gateway. Also check both shopifystatus.com and your gateway's status page simultaneously to isolate where the failure originates.

Can I accept payments if Shopify Payments is on hold?

Yes. Add PayPal Express Checkout or another third-party gateway (Stripe directly, Square, etc.) in Shopify Admin → Settings → Payments → Third-party providers. Note that when using third-party gateways, Shopify charges a transaction fee of 0.5%–2% depending on your plan.

How long do Shopify Payments holds typically last?

Routine risk reviews are usually resolved within 1-5 business days. If Shopify requests documentation (business registration, invoices), providing complete, accurate documents quickly shortens the timeline. Severe holds (high chargebacks, policy violations) can take longer and may require escalation through Shopify Support.

Why do my Shopify payments fail only on mobile?

Mobile payment failures are often related to 3DS authentication popup blocking (especially Safari on iOS), Apple Pay or Google Pay configuration issues, or checkout app scripts that don't render correctly on mobile viewports. Test checkout specifically on Safari iOS and Chrome Android with a real card to reproduce the issue.

How many payment failures are "normal" for a Shopify store?

A healthy payment failure rate (due to legitimate card declines) is 2-5% of checkout attempts. If you're seeing failures above 10%, something is wrong on the gateway or checkout configuration side. Gateway dashboards show your decline rate — compare it against previous weeks to spot sudden changes.
