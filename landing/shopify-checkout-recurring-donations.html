Shopify Recurring Donation Not Working — Charity Billing, Tax Receipt, and Donation Widget Failures

Shopify has no native donation product type. Nonprofits and cause-based merchants implement recurring giving through Recharge, Bold Subscriptions, or dedicated apps like Donation Bear. When these fail, donation widgets vanish silently, monthly gifts stop renewing, and tax receipts are never delivered — with no alert anywhere in Shopify.

Updated June 2026 Affects: Nonprofit Shopify stores, recurring giving programs, charity campaigns
Shopify has no native donation product type. Every recurring donation on Shopify is built using a general-purpose subscription billing app (Recharge, Bold Subscriptions) or a donation-specific app (Donation Bear, Simple Donate, Tip Jar). These apps each have their own billing, webhook, and email systems — all independent from Shopify. When any of them fail, Shopify Admin shows everything as normal.
Architecture

How Recurring Donations Work on Shopify

A typical nonprofit Shopify store stacks 2–4 apps to collect and manage recurring gifts. Each layer has independent billing and can fail silently.

Layer 1 — Widget
Donation App (Donation Bear, Simple Donate, Tip Jar)
Injects a donation widget into the cart page or product page. Donor selects amount and frequency. Adds a donation line item to the Shopify cart. Fails silently when app billing lapses or theme update overwrites the snippet.
Layer 2 — Recurring Billing
Recharge or Bold Subscriptions
Manages the subscription record: billing cycle, dunning, card updates, cancellations. Creates a Shopify order on each successful charge. Donor must have a customer account — guest checkout breaks recurring gift setup.
Layer 3 — Receipt
Tax Receipt Email Template
Separate from Shopify's standard order confirmation. Must be configured in the donation or billing app with charity registration number and the required statement that no goods/services were exchanged. Commonly skipped or misconfigured.
Layer 4 — Donor Records
CRM / Donor Management
Most nonprofits sync Shopify orders to an external CRM (Salesforce Nonprofit, Bloomerang, Little Green Light) via a webhook or Zapier integration. Year-end giving summaries are generated from CRM data, not from Shopify.

Diagnosis Checklist

5-Step Recurring Donation Diagnosis

  • 1
    Check the donation app's billing status in Shopify Go to Shopify Admin → Apps → find your donation app (Donation Bear, Simple Donate, Tip Jar, etc.) and verify it shows Active. If the app's own Shopify subscription has lapsed, its code stops running immediately and the donation widget silently disappears from your storefront. Also check your billing app (Recharge or Bold Subscriptions) in the same list — a lapse there stops all recurring charges.
  • 2
    Check the theme for the donation app's snippet Go to Shopify Admin → Online Store → Themes → Actions → Edit code. Search for the donation app's snippet reference (typically in cart.liquid, product.liquid, or a section file). If the snippet reference is missing, a recent theme update overwrote it. Re-install the donation app and follow the app's setup guide to re-add the snippet — this is the most common cause of a donation widget disappearing after a theme change.
  • 3
    Check the donor's subscription record in Recharge or Bold In Recharge: go to the Recharge dashboard → Customers → search for the donor's email → Subscriptions tab. In Bold: go to Bold Subscriptions → Customers → search by email. Verify the subscription status (Active, Cancelled, Failed), the last billing date, and the next billing date. If the subscription was cancelled, check the cancellation reason — it will show whether it was customer-initiated, dunning-exhausted, or triggered by a refund.
  • 4
    Verify the tax receipt email template is configured In your donation app's settings, find the email or receipt template section. Verify that the template includes: (1) your organization's legal name and charitable registration number; (2) the statement "No goods or services were provided in exchange for this contribution" (required for US IRS purposes); (3) the donation date and amount. Send a test receipt to yourself. If the template is not configured, donors are receiving only Shopify's standard order confirmation, which is not a valid charitable receipt.
  • 5
    Test the full donation flow in an incognito window as a new donor Open an incognito browser window and go to your store as a first-time visitor. Add a donation using the donation widget, proceed to checkout, and complete a test transaction using a Shopify test payment. Verify: (1) the donation widget appears and the amount submits correctly; (2) a Shopify order is created; (3) a tax receipt email arrives (check spam); (4) for recurring donations via Recharge, a subscription record is created in the Recharge dashboard. This end-to-end test catches widget, billing, and email failures in one pass.
Deep Dive

Three Failure Modes in Detail

Deep Dive 1

The Donation Widget Disappears — App Billing and Theme Conflicts

Donation widgets on Shopify are rendered by a Shopify app. The app injects a liquid snippet into your theme and runs JavaScript that renders the donation UI. Two things commonly cause the widget to vanish silently: the app's own Shopify billing lapses, or a theme update overwrites the snippet.

When a Shopify app's billing lapses — because the merchant's credit card on file expired or the charge failed — Shopify immediately suspends the app. The app's JavaScript stops loading, and any storefront widget it rendered stops appearing. There is no storefront error message and no notification in Shopify Admin to the merchant. A donor visiting the site simply sees no donation option.

Shopify Admin → Apps → [donation app name] Check: Status = Active, Billing = Current If suspended: Update billing → reactivate app → verify widget reappears

Theme updates are the second common cause. When you update your Shopify theme (or switch to a new theme), the new theme's files overwrite the customizations the donation app made to your old theme's liquid files. The app's snippet reference is gone, but the app itself is still active and billing — so there is no app-level alert, and nothing in Shopify Admin indicates a problem. The fix: re-run the donation app's setup wizard or manually re-insert the app's snippet into the new theme's relevant liquid file.

Deep Dive 2

Recurring Gift Stops Processing — The Dunning Problem for Charities

When a donor's card declines on a renewal, Recharge and Bold Subscriptions begin a dunning sequence. They retry the card over several days (typically 1–3 retries) and send automated reminder emails asking the donor to update their payment method. After the dunning period ends without a successful charge, the subscription is cancelled.

For commercial subscriptions, aggressive dunning is standard practice. For nonprofits, the same dunning emails read as transactional billing reminders — which can feel impersonal and confusing to donors who think of their relationship as charitable giving, not a subscription service. Many donors do not recognize the "Your subscription has been cancelled" email from Recharge as a notification that their monthly gift to a charity has stopped.

Recharge dashboard → Customers → [donor email] → Subscriptions Check: Status (Active / Cancelled), Last charge date, Cancellation reason If cancelled: Contact donor to re-establish gift → donor must re-subscribe

The best mitigation: configure a proactive dunning email that says "Your monthly gift to [Charity Name] could not be processed. Please update your payment method to keep your giving active." Sending from your nonprofit's domain with charity-specific language — rather than Recharge's generic subscription language — dramatically improves donor response rates before the subscription reaches the cancellation point.

Deep Dive 3

Tax Receipt Not Sent — The Compliance Gap

Shopify's order confirmation email is not a charitable tax receipt. For US nonprofits, a valid receipt for donations above $250 must include the organization's name, the donation date and amount, and the statement that no goods or services were received in exchange for the donation. Shopify's standard confirmation email includes none of these legal requirements.

The tax receipt must be configured as a separate email template inside the donation app or billing app. Donation Bear, Recharge, and most donation-specific apps have a dedicated receipt email template — but it must be set up by the merchant. If the template is blank or uses default text, donors receive only the Shopify order confirmation, which does not meet IRS or CRA requirements for charitable giving.

Required in tax receipt email: 1. Organization legal name + EIN / charity registration number 2. Donation date and amount 3. "No goods or services were provided in exchange for this contribution" 4. Contact info for donor questions (For gifts under $250, the donor's bank statement can suffice — but a receipt is best practice)

Guest checkout creates a separate tax receipt gap: a donor who checks out as a guest has no Shopify customer account. Some donation apps send the receipt to the order email regardless — but others only send tax receipts to customers with an account. If your donation flow allows guest checkout, test specifically whether the tax receipt email is sent to a guest order. If it is not, require account creation during checkout or display a warning to guest donors that their receipt will be sent via a follow-up email once they create an account.

FAQ

Shopify Recurring Donations — Common Questions

Why did the donation widget disappear from my Shopify store?
Shopify has no native donation product type — every donation widget is rendered by a third-party app. Two common causes of widget disappearance: (1) the donation app's own Shopify billing lapsed, suspending the app and stopping all its code; check Shopify Admin → Apps for Active status; (2) a theme update overwrote the app's liquid snippet; check your theme's cart.liquid or product.liquid for the app's snippet reference and re-add it if missing. Neither cause shows any error in your storefront or Shopify Admin.
Why did a donor's recurring monthly gift stop processing on Shopify?
Recurring gifts on Shopify are subscription records in Recharge or Bold Subscriptions. When a card declines, the billing app begins a dunning sequence (retries + email reminders). After the dunning period ends, the subscription is cancelled. Check the donor's subscription record in Recharge or Bold for the cancellation reason. If cancelled by dunning, the donor must re-establish their gift — the billing app cannot restart a cancelled subscription automatically. Contact the donor using nonprofit-specific language, not generic "subscription" language.
Why is the tax receipt email not being sent after a donation on Shopify?
Shopify's order confirmation is not a charitable tax receipt. The tax receipt must be configured as a separate email template in your donation app or billing app. Check the app's email settings for a "receipt" or "donation confirmation" template and verify it includes your organization's name, EIN/registration number, donation date and amount, and the no goods/services statement. Also check if the receipt is being sent for guest orders — some apps only send receipts to customers with a Shopify account.
Can a donor modify the donation amount in the Shopify cart before checkout?
Yes. Donation apps that add a variable-price line item to the Shopify cart rely on the cart to hold the donation amount — but Shopify's cart does not lock line item prices server-side. A technically knowledgeable donor could modify the amount via the Storefront API or browser tools. To prevent this: use a donation app that validates the amount server-side via a Shopify Function or checkout webhook, or use Checkout Extensibility (Shopify Plus) to verify the donation line item amount before the order is created.
What is the difference between a recurring donation and a subscription on Shopify?
From Shopify's perspective, there is no difference — both are recurring billing records in Recharge or Bold. The differences are business-layer: donations may qualify for charitable tax deductions (requiring a compliant receipt email), donors have different expectations around dunning and cancellation, and refunds on donation orders need careful handling to avoid inadvertently cancelling the recurring gift. Shopify and the billing app treat both the same — the nonprofit must configure all the charitable-specific logic in the app settings and email templates.
