Shopify Gift Card Delivery Failure

Shopify Digital Gift Card Email Not Received — Gift Card Not Delivered After Purchase

Shopify digital gift cards use two separate delivery systems. Six failure modes — spam filters, wrong recipient email, scheduled delivery gaps, resend loops, multi-currency conflicts, and DMARC misconfigurations — leave buyers certain the recipient got the gift card when they never did.

Updated June 2026 Affects: Native gift cards, Govalo, GiftKart, Rise.ai, Swym
The Silent Part: When a Shopify gift card email goes to spam or bounces, the merchant's Shopify Admin shows the gift card as Active and the order as Fulfilled. There is no delivery failure notification. The buyer assumes the recipient got it. The recipient assumes the buyer forgot. The merchant finds out when someone complains days later — or not at all.
Architecture

Two Distinct Gift Card Delivery Systems — Different Failure Modes

Shopify's native gift card system and third-party gift card apps are completely separate delivery pipelines. Which one you're using determines where to diagnose.

System 1 — Native
Shopify Native Gift Cards
Gift card product type built into Shopify. Email sent from noreply@shopify.com. Code generated in Admin → Products → Gift Cards. No scheduling — delivery is immediate at purchase. Resend available from gift card detail page.
System 2 — Third Party
App-Delivered Gift Cards
Govalo, GiftKart, Rise.ai Gift Cards, Swym. Email sent from the app's own sending infrastructure. Supports scheduling, custom design, PDF attachment, recipient video messages. Resend from inside the app — not Shopify Admin.
System 3 — Hybrid
Rise.ai Store Credit + Gift
Rise.ai manages both store credit and gift cards. Gift card delivery uses Rise.ai email. Store credit is redeemed differently than a gift card code. Customers sometimes confuse the two — "store credit not working" and "gift card not received" may be the same issue.
Recipient Experience
What the Recipient Gets
A unique alphanumeric code they enter at checkout. The code has a balance. At checkout, applying the code deducts from the balance. If the balance covers the total, no payment is needed. If partial, the remainder is charged to a payment method.

Deep Dive

The Spam Filter Problem: Why Shopify Gift Card Emails Disappear

Most "gift card not received" reports are email delivery failures — the gift card code was generated correctly, but the email never reached the recipient's inbox.

Native Shopify Gift Cards

noreply@shopify.com — High Technical Reputation, Mixed Inbox Placement

Shopify's native gift card email comes from noreply@shopify.com using Shopify's shared sending infrastructure. Shopify maintains strong SPF, DKIM, and DMARC records — the technical email authentication is correct. For personal Gmail, Outlook.com, and Yahoo accounts, inbox placement is reliable.

The problem is corporate email accounts. Microsoft 365 tenants with Exchange Online Protection, Google Workspace with Advanced Protection, and corporate email gateways (Proofpoint, Mimecast, Barracuda) use behavioral reputation scoring in addition to SPF/DKIM. High-volume transactional email from a shared platform like Shopify can be filtered as "bulk commercial email" even with perfect authentication. A gift card email sent to a recipient at a large company often ends up in the Junk or Clutter folder.

The merchant has no visibility into this. Shopify's order notification shows the gift card as Active. The order shows as Fulfilled. There is no bounce or spam notification that reaches the merchant.

To recover a native gift card whose email was filtered: 1. Admin → Products → Gift Cards 2. Filter by date or search by order number 3. Find the gift card → note the code 4. Share the code with the recipient directly (text, WhatsApp, etc.) 5. Tell them: "Your gift card code is [CODE]. Enter it in the 'Gift card or discount code' field at checkout." To verify the code is still valid: Admin → Products → Gift Cards → click the code → check Status = Active and Balance remaining
App-Delivered Gift Cards

Govalo, GiftKart, Rise.ai — Custom Sending Domains and Deliverability Gaps

Third-party gift card apps send emails from their own domains (e.g., gifts.govalo.com, mail.giftkart.app) using shared sending infrastructure (SendGrid, Postmark, Mailgun). If the app's sending IP pool has mixed-reputation tenants, or if the app's domain does not have correctly configured SPF, DKIM, and DMARC records, deliverability is significantly worse than Shopify's native system.

The fix most gift card apps offer is custom sending domain — you configure DNS records to allow the app to send email from your own domain (e.g., gifts@yourstore.com). Email received from your own domain passes your own SPF/DKIM checks, which dramatically improves inbox placement for recipients who have ever bought from your store before.

To check if your gift card app supports custom sending domain: Govalo: Dashboard → Settings → Email → Custom Sending Domain GiftKart: Settings → Email Configuration → Custom Domain Rise.ai: Settings → Emails → Sender Email If the option exists, complete the DNS verification steps. If it does not exist, contact the app's support — most have this on their roadmap or can enable it manually.

If custom sending domain is not available and corporate email filtering is causing repeated failures, the workaround is to manually retrieve the gift card code from the app's dashboard and share it with the recipient through a channel other than email.

Scheduled Delivery Failures: The Birthday Gift That Never Arrived

Scheduled gift card delivery is not a Shopify native feature. Every scheduled delivery requires a third-party app. When the schedule fails, the buyer discovers it after the occasion has passed.

The Problem

Four Reasons Scheduled Gift Card Delivery Silently Fails

1. App subscription lapse. If the merchant's monthly billing for the gift card app fails (expired card, dispute, plan downgrade), the app subscription may expire. The app's scheduled delivery queue pauses or stops entirely. Gift cards set to deliver on future dates never fire. The merchant's Shopify Admin shows no indication that anything is wrong — the gift card product still exists and orders still process.

2. App uninstall and reinstall. Merchants sometimes uninstall and reinstall apps during troubleshooting or when changing plans. Most gift card apps do not persist the scheduled delivery queue across reinstalls. Pending scheduled sends are lost when the app is uninstalled.

3. Recipient email permanent bounce. If the recipient email address has previously bounced for any sender using the same email infrastructure (SendGrid, Postmark), the email provider may have a global suppression list for that address. The scheduled delivery attempt is blocked before it reaches the recipient's mail server. The app's dashboard may show the send as "Delivered" even though it was suppressed.

4. App-side scheduling bug. During high-volume periods (Valentine's Day, Mother's Day, Christmas), gift card apps process large volumes of scheduled sends. Processing delays or bugs in the scheduler have been reported across multiple apps. The send fires late — sometimes hours or days after the scheduled date — or not at all.

Diagnosis

How to Check and Recover a Failed Scheduled Delivery

Step 1: Log into the gift card app dashboard Step 2: Find the gift card by order number, recipient email, or delivery date Step 3: Check status field: - "Scheduled" with a past date → delivery never fired - "Failed" or "Bounced" → delivery attempted but rejected - "Sent" → app believes delivery was successful (check spam) Step 4: If status shows Scheduled with a past date: - Check your app subscription status (billing) - Manually trigger a resend from the app dashboard - If no resend option, retrieve the gift card code and share manually Step 5: If status shows Failed or Bounced: - Check the error reason (usually shown in the app dashboard) - Verify the recipient email address is correct - Try resending to a different email address if the original bounced permanently

Wrong Recipient Email: The Typo That Can't Be Undone

When a buyer types the wrong recipient email address, there is no recovery path that sends the gift card to the intended recipient. You must manually intervene.

The Problem

Shopify Does Not Validate Recipient Email Addresses

At checkout for a digital gift card product, the buyer enters the recipient's email address in a text field. Shopify validates only that the input looks like an email address (contains @ and a domain). Shopify does not verify that the address exists, that the domain is reachable, or that the inbox accepts delivery.

A buyer who types "jane.smith@gamil.com" instead of "jane.smith@gmail.com" sends the gift card to a nonexistent address. The email bounces. Shopify Admin shows the gift card as Active and the order as Fulfilled. There is no bounce notification. The merchant has no way to know unless the buyer contacts support.

This is the most common cause of "I bought a gift card but my friend never got it" complaints in Shopify support forums.

The Fix

Manual Recovery: Refund the Original, Issue a New One

Recovery path for wrong recipient email: 1. Admin → Products → Gift Cards → find the gift card 2. Note the code, balance, and expiry date 3. Manually deactivate or expire the original gift card: Admin → Gift Cards → click the card → click "Deactivate" (This prevents the wrong recipient from finding and using the code if the email somehow bounced to an existing inbox) 4. Create a new gift card for the same amount: Admin → Products → Gift Cards → Create gift card → set amount → set correct email 5. Shopify will send the new gift card email to the correct address Alternative: if deactivation is too disruptive, retrieve the code and share it directly with the buyer so they can forward it to the intended recipient. Do NOT resend the original — it goes to the wrong address again.

For third-party apps (Govalo, GiftKart): most app dashboards allow editing the recipient email and resending after the original send. Log into the app, find the gift card, edit the email field, and trigger a resend. This is simpler than the Shopify Admin approach.

Prevention: some third-party gift card apps offer email confirmation validation ("Are you sure you want to send to this email?") or real-time email validation via an API. If wrong-recipient reports are a recurring issue in your store, evaluate whether your gift card app supports these features.

Step-by-Step Diagnosis

Digital Gift Card Delivery Failure — 5-Step Diagnosis

Work through these steps in order. Most gift card delivery failures are identified at step 1 or 2.

  • 1
    Confirm which delivery system you're on Are you using Shopify's native gift card product type, or a third-party app (Govalo, GiftKart, Rise.ai)? If you're unsure: Admin → Apps — look for a gift card app. If none is installed, you're on native. This determines where the code and delivery status live. Native: Admin → Products → Gift Cards. App: inside the app dashboard.
  • 2
    Verify the gift card code was generated Find the gift card in Admin → Products → Gift Cards (native) or your app dashboard. If it shows as Active with a balance, the code was generated correctly — the problem is delivery. If no code exists, there was a code generation failure (rare for native, less rare for apps during outages).
  • 3
    Check the recipient email address for typos View the gift card detail to see the email address it was sent to. Compare it to what the buyer intended. Look for common typos: gmail vs gamil, .co vs .com, missing letters. If there is a typo, do NOT resend — it goes to the wrong address again. Follow the manual recovery steps (deactivate original, issue new gift card to correct email).
  • 4
    Ask the recipient to check spam / junk / promotions For native Shopify: the email comes from noreply@shopify.com — ask the recipient to search their inbox for "Shopify" or "gift card." For apps: the email comes from the app's domain — tell the recipient the app name so they know what to search for. Corporate inboxes: ask them to check the Junk folder, Focused Inbox (Outlook), and Clutter folder. If found in spam, they can mark it as "Not Spam" and the code is in the email body.
  • 5
    Share the gift card code directly if email recovery fails If the email cannot be found and resend is not viable (wrong address or corporate block), retrieve the code directly: Admin → Products → Gift Cards → click the card → copy the code. Share it with the buyer via text, WhatsApp, or DM. The recipient enters it at checkout in the "Gift card or discount code" field. The code works regardless of whether the email was received.

FAQ

Shopify Digital Gift Card Delivery — Frequently Asked Questions

Why did the Shopify gift card email not arrive after purchase?
There are four causes. First, spam filtering: Shopify's native email from noreply@shopify.com often ends up in corporate junk folders (Office 365, Google Workspace). The code was delivered — the email was not. Second, typo in recipient email: the buyer typed the wrong address and Shopify delivered to a nonexistent inbox with no error. Third, email delivery infrastructure failure (rare): the code exists in Admin → Products → Gift Cards but the email never fired. Fourth, app delivery failure: if you use Govalo, GiftKart, or Rise.ai, the delivery uses the app's email infrastructure — not Shopify's — and the app's system may have had a failure independent of Shopify. Check the app dashboard for delivery status.
How do I find and resend a Shopify gift card code?
For native Shopify gift cards: Admin → Products → Gift Cards → find the card → the code is visible on the detail page. Click "Send email" or "Resend." Warning: resend goes to the same email address — if the original bounced or went to spam, the resend has the same outcome. For spam cases, share the code directly via text or DM. For typo cases, deactivate the original, then create a new gift card to the correct email. For third-party app gift cards: do NOT try to resend from Shopify Admin. The resend must come from inside the gift card app dashboard (Govalo, GiftKart, Rise.ai settings).
Why did my scheduled Shopify gift card delivery not send on time?
Scheduled delivery is a third-party app feature — Shopify native does not support it. Failures occur when the app subscription lapses (billing failure), when the app is uninstalled and reinstalled (clears the queue), when the recipient email has a permanent bounce flag in the app's email infrastructure, or when the app experiences a scheduler failure during high-volume periods. Check the app dashboard for the delivery status. If the status shows "Scheduled" with a past date, the send never fired — manually trigger a resend from the dashboard or retrieve the code and share it directly.
Can a Shopify gift card be used in a different currency than it was issued?
Shopify native gift cards are issued in the store's primary currency and store their balance in that currency. On stores using Shopify Markets, gift card redemption behavior varies by configuration. In some configurations, the gift card amount is applied in the issuing currency, which may not match the checkout currency — leading to confusing checkout amounts. Third-party apps like Govalo and Rise.ai handle multi-currency differently, some with flexible conversion at redemption time. If your store operates across multiple currency markets and sells gift cards, test gift card redemption in each currency market before enabling multi-currency selling.
How do I stop Shopify gift card emails from going to spam?
For native Shopify: you cannot change the sending domain (noreply@shopify.com). The only options are to tell buyers to inform recipients to whitelist Shopify, or to use a third-party gift card app with custom sending domain support. For apps (Govalo, GiftKart, Rise.ai): check whether the app supports custom sending domain. If it does, configure your own domain's DNS records (SPF, DKIM) so the app sends from your domain instead of the app's shared domain. This significantly improves inbox placement for your established customers. If the app does not support custom sending domain, contact their support — most have this on their roadmap.
