Shopify Membership Checkout Failures

Shopify Doesn't Have a Native Membership Product Type — And That's Why Member Pricing Breaks

Shopify has no built-in membership system. Member pricing, content gating, and member-only access are all handled by third-party apps — Bold Memberships, Locksmith, Conjured Memberships, or Recharge. When any of these apps fail silently, members see full prices at checkout, hit access walls on products they paid to unlock, or lose their member status after a billing event. Shopify Admin shows nothing unusual throughout.

Updated June 2026 Covers: Bold Memberships · Locksmith · Conjured Memberships · Recharge · Memberstack
The core problem: Shopify tracks membership status through customer tags. Every membership app — Bold, Locksmith, Conjured, Recharge — reads the customer tag to decide whether to show member pricing or unlock gated content. When the tag is missing, wrong, or removed by a billing event, access and pricing break instantly. Shopify Admin shows the customer account as normal throughout. The failure is completely invisible from the merchant side unless someone reports it.
Architecture

How Shopify Memberships Actually Work

Shopify's product types are Physical, Digital, Gift Card, and Service. There is no Membership type. Merchants build membership programs by stacking multiple third-party apps, each responsible for a different layer of the membership stack.

Layer 1 — Identity
Shopify Customer Account + Tag
The membership status lives as a customer tag in Shopify (e.g. "member", "gold-member", "vip"). Every other app reads this tag. If the tag is wrong, nothing works.
Layer 2 — Billing
Recharge / Conjured / Bold
Handles the recurring payment for membership. On successful payment, adds the customer tag. On failed payment, removes or revokes the tag after a dunning period.
Layer 3 — Pricing
Bold Custom Pricing / Customer-Specific Pricing
Reads the customer tag and applies a member price to the product before it is added to the cart. Shopify Plus merchants can use Price Lists natively. Non-Plus requires an app.
Layer 4 — Access
Locksmith / Conjured Access
Hides or restricts products, collections, and pages at the storefront level. Reads the customer tag to decide whether to show or hide content. Does not gate the Shopify checkout itself.
Layer 5 — Checkout
Shopify Checkout (standard)
Shopify's checkout has no membership awareness. It processes any cart regardless of customer tag. Membership validation at checkout requires Shopify Plus (Checkout Extensibility) or a post-order webhook.
Layer 6 — Fulfillment
Shopify Order + App Webhook
After checkout, the membership app may receive an order webhook to record the purchase, grant access to digital member content, or update the customer's membership period. Webhook failure = no access granted.

Diagnosis Checklist

5-Step Membership Checkout Diagnosis

  • 1
    Verify the customer tag in Shopify Admin Go to Shopify Admin → Customers → find the affected member → check the Tags field. The tag must match exactly what your membership app expects — case-sensitive, no extra spaces. If the tag is missing, the entire membership stack is broken for this customer regardless of which app you check next.
  • 2
    Check your membership app's billing status A lapsed app subscription (Bold Memberships, Conjured, Recharge) causes the app to stop processing requests. Go to Shopify Admin → Apps and verify the membership app is active and its billing is current. A billing lapse often triggers no visible error in the storefront — member pricing silently disappears.
  • 3
    Use Locksmith's built-in access test In the Locksmith app dashboard, navigate to the locked resource (product, collection, or page), click "Test access," and enter the member's email address. Locksmith will show you exactly which condition is passing or failing for that specific customer — this takes the guesswork out of tag-condition debugging.
  • 4
    Check for a failed renewal in the membership app dashboard In Conjured Memberships, Recharge, or Bold Memberships, check the customer's subscription record for a failed payment or dunning event. A failed renewal typically revokes the customer tag after 3–7 days of dunning. The customer loses pricing and access the moment the tag is removed, even if they are mid-session.
  • 5
    Reproduce in incognito to isolate the session Have the affected member log out completely, clear cookies, and re-log into their Shopify account in an incognito window. Then browse to the member-only product or attempt checkout. This eliminates stale session cache and confirms whether the failure is persistent or session-bound. If the issue disappears after re-login, the member's session token was stale — no fix needed beyond re-login.
Deep Dive

Three Failure Modes in Detail

Deep Dive 1

Member Pricing Not Applying — The Customer Tag Chain

Member pricing on non-Plus Shopify stores works through a chain: the membership app sets a customer tag on signup → Bold Custom Pricing (or equivalent) reads that tag at page load → applies a price override to the product variant before it is added to the cart. Every link in this chain must work for the member to see their price.

The most common break point is the customer tag. Bold Custom Pricing does not create the tag — that is the membership app's job. If a member signed up but the membership app failed to write the tag (due to a webhook timeout, an app outage during signup, or a plan limit), Bold never sees an active membership and serves the retail price.

Shopify Admin → Customers → [member email] → Tags field Expected: "member" (or your app's configured tag) If empty or wrong: pricing and access both fail for this customer

For Shopify Plus merchants, Customer-Specific Pricing (Shopify's native price lists) is a more reliable alternative because it does not depend on an app intermediary. Prices are set directly on the customer record via the Admin API and are evaluated server-side at checkout — not client-side by a third-party script. If you are on Plus and experiencing pricing failures, check whether the customer is assigned to the correct price list in Shopify Admin → Customers → [member] → Price list.

Deep Dive 2

Locksmith Blocking a Legitimate Member — The Checkout Gap

Locksmith is the most widely used Shopify access-control app. It works by injecting JavaScript into the storefront that evaluates the logged-in customer's tags against the lock conditions. If the condition passes, the content is shown. If it fails, the content is hidden or replaced with an access message.

Three things consistently break Locksmith gates for legitimate members. First, tag case sensitivity: if the lock says "Member" and the customer tag says "member", Locksmith denies access. Second, session timing: a customer who was anonymous when they loaded the product page, then logged in without reloading, will still be evaluated as anonymous — Locksmith evaluated the session at page load, not at login. Third, the checkout gap: Locksmith gates the storefront only. If a non-member obtains a direct product URL or a cart permalink, they can add the product to their cart and complete checkout without any Locksmith intervention.

Locksmith dashboard → [locked resource] → "Test access" → enter member email Shows: which specific condition is passing or failing Also check: Settings → "Key conditions" → verify tag case matches Shopify Admin exactly

To close the checkout gap on Shopify Plus, use Checkout Extensibility to add a checkout validation function that verifies the customer's tag before allowing the order to proceed. For non-Plus stores, the only server-side option is a post-purchase webhook that checks the order, detects unauthorized purchases, and cancels or refunds them automatically — a reactive rather than preventive approach.

Deep Dive 3

Renewal Failure Revokes Access Instantly — The Dunning Cliff

When a Recharge or Conjured Memberships subscription renewal fails, the app begins a dunning sequence — it retries the payment over several days and sends reminder emails. At the end of the dunning period (typically 3–7 days), if the payment has not been recovered, the app removes the customer tag from the Shopify customer record.

The moment the tag is removed, the member loses access to everything that depends on the tag: Locksmith gates lock immediately, Bold Custom Pricing reverts to retail prices, and any member-only product or collection becomes inaccessible. The member may have been in the middle of a browsing or checkout session when this happens — their cart still shows the member price (because the cart was priced before the tag was removed), but if they refresh or start a new session, the member price is gone.

This is one of the most disorienting member experiences on Shopify. The member believes their membership is active (they paid for another month just days ago), but their access disappears without explanation. They see no error — just a sudden change to retail pricing and locked content.

Recharge / Conjured dashboard → Customers → [member] → Subscription tab Check: Last billing attempt, Dunning status, Active/Inactive status Fix: Re-activate subscription → app re-adds customer tag → access restored within minutes

To reduce dunning-cliff damage: configure your membership app to send the member a proactive warning 3 days before dunning ends. Most apps support this. The goal is to get the member to update their payment method before access is revoked, not after. Also consider a grace period setting — some apps allow you to delay tag removal by an extra 2–3 days, giving the member time to respond without losing access.

FAQ

Shopify Membership Checkout — Common Questions

Why is member pricing not showing at Shopify checkout?
Member pricing on Shopify is applied by a third-party app — most commonly Bold Custom Pricing or a customer-tag-based price rule. The four common causes: (1) the customer's Shopify account is missing the correct member tag — check Admin → Customers → Tags; (2) the customer is browsing as a guest without logging in; (3) the Bold Memberships or pricing app has a billing lapse; (4) a stale browser session is serving an old price — ask the member to clear cache or use incognito. Member pricing works by modifying the price before the product enters the cart — if the app is not running, the full retail price is always shown throughout checkout.
Why is Locksmith blocking a member who should have access?
Locksmith gates are tag-based and case-sensitive. Check that the lock condition tag matches the customer's tag in Shopify Admin exactly — "Member" and "member" are treated as different tags. If the tag matches and the member is still blocked, check whether they loaded the page before logging in — Locksmith evaluates the session at page load, so members who logged in after loading the page must refresh to trigger a re-evaluation. Use Locksmith's built-in "Test access" tool: enter the customer's email and the locked URL to see the exact failing condition.
Why did a member lose access to member-only products after a renewal?
Most membership apps (Conjured, Recharge, Bold) represent active membership as a Shopify customer tag. When a renewal fails, the app starts a dunning sequence — after the dunning period (3–7 days), it removes the customer tag. Without the tag, Locksmith gates lock and Bold pricing reverts to retail immediately. The member must complete the failed payment in their account portal to restore the tag. Consider enabling proactive dunning warning emails so members update their card before access is revoked, not after.
Can a Shopify member-only product be purchased by a guest using checkout?
Yes. Locksmith gates the storefront only — it hides or restricts the product page and add-to-cart button. But Locksmith does not gate the Shopify checkout itself. A non-member who obtains the direct product URL or a cart permalink can complete checkout without Locksmith intervening. To prevent this on Shopify Plus, deploy a checkout validation function via Checkout Extensibility that checks the customer tag before allowing the order. For non-Plus stores, the only server-side option is a post-purchase order webhook that detects and refunds unauthorized purchases.
Why does a member discount code not work when member pricing is already applied?
Shopify enforces a one-discount-per-line-item rule. Bold Custom Pricing applies member discounts by modifying the product price directly — it does not use Shopify's discount engine. This means both the member price and a discount code can technically coexist for non-Plus merchants using Bold. However, if your membership app uses Shopify's native discount system (draft orders, automatic discounts), Shopify will treat both as discounts and refuse to stack them. The most reliable fix for non-Plus stores: avoid issuing discount codes to members and build all savings into the member price. For Plus, Shopify Scripts can enable stacking explicitly.
