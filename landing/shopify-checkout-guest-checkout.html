Shopify Guest Checkout: How to Enable It and Fix When It Redirects to Login Anyway

Requiring account creation before purchase drops first-purchase conversion by 15–35%. Most merchants don't know guest checkout is a single toggle — or why buyers still hit a login wall when the toggle says it's on.

Updated June 2026 All Shopify plans New & legacy accounts covered
The Conversion Math

What Forcing Account Creation Actually Costs You

Baymard Institute's large-scale checkout usability research — covering over 5,000 US and EU shoppers — consistently finds that requiring account creation before purchase is a top-3 reason customers abandon checkout. 26% of shoppers have abandoned a purchase specifically because a site required account creation.

For Shopify merchants, the translation is direct: if your store has "Accounts are required" enabled, you are losing roughly one in four first-time buyers before they complete payment. The segment most affected is cold traffic — buyers from paid ads, organic search, social, and influencer referrals who have no prior relationship with your brand and are not yet willing to exchange personal information beyond what a purchase requires.

High-Impact Setting

Requiring account creation before purchase is one of the highest-ROI checkout settings to fix. Unlike A/B testing checkout elements (which requires Plus) or adding trust badges (which affects a subset of buyers), guest checkout is a binary switch that affects every first-time buyer on every device. The fix takes 60 seconds.

The Post-Purchase Invitation Strategy

The correct approach — and what Shopify recommends — is to allow guest checkout and then invite account creation on the order confirmation page. Merchants who implement this typically see:

  • Account creation rate of 30–50% from post-purchase invitations (buyers who just had a positive purchase experience are highly motivated to save their details for next time)
  • Full first-purchase conversion rate recovery (no forced abandonment)
  • Higher account quality (buyers who opt in after purchase have demonstrated intent)

Shopify's order confirmation page automatically includes an account creation prompt for guest buyers. You don't need to build this — it works by default when guest checkout is enabled.

Setup Guide

How to Enable Guest Checkout on Shopify

The exact steps depend on whether your store uses legacy customer accounts or new customer accounts (introduced by Shopify in 2024). The setting is in the same place — Admin → Settings → Customer accounts — but the interface looks different.

Legacy Customer Accounts (pre-2024 interface)

01
Go to Shopify Admin → Settings → Customer accounts
02
Under "Customer accounts," find the radio button group with three options
03
Select "Accounts are optional" — this allows guest checkout. ("Accounts are disabled" also allows guest checkout but removes the account system entirely. "Accounts are required" forces login.)
04
Click Save. The change takes effect immediately — no theme republishing required.

New Customer Accounts (2024+ interface)

01
Go to Shopify Admin → Settings → Customer accounts
02
Under "New customer accounts," you'll see the new accounts system is either active or you can enable it
03
Find the "Require login before checkout" toggle — make sure it is OFF (disabled) to allow guest checkout
04
Click Save. Test immediately by opening an incognito/private browser window and adding a product to cart
Plan Note

Guest checkout is available on every Shopify plan — Basic ($39/mo), Shopify ($105/mo), Advanced ($399/mo), and Plus ($2,300/mo). There is no plan restriction on guest checkout. If you were told you need a higher plan to enable guest checkout, that was incorrect.

Troubleshooting

Why Guest Checkout Still Redirects to Login (Even When Enabled)

The most common support question about Shopify guest checkout: "I set accounts to optional / turned off 'require login' — but customers are still being sent to a login page." Here are the six causes, in order of frequency.

1
Loyalty or Rewards App Overriding Checkout Flow
Most common cause

Loyalty programs (LoyaltyLion, Smile.io, Yotpo Loyalty, Okendo Loyalty) require an account to track points. Some loyalty app configurations add a pre-checkout login step or redirect customers to login before checkout even when Shopify's native setting allows guest. To diagnose: disable the loyalty app temporarily and test checkout in an incognito window. If guest checkout works, the app is the cause. Fix: contact the app's support team — most offer a "guest checkout with loyalty points after login" flow that doesn't force pre-purchase login.

2
Subscription App Requiring Login
Affects stores with subscription products

Subscription apps (ReCharge, Seal Subscriptions, Bold Subscriptions, Loop Subscriptions) typically require an account to manage subscription billing. Some configurations redirect subscription-product checkouts to a login page. To diagnose: add a non-subscription product to cart and test guest checkout — if it works for non-subscription items but not subscription items, the subscription app is the cause. Fix: most subscription apps support "guest" subscription checkout that creates an account automatically and emails an account activation link post-purchase.

3
"Continue as Guest" Link Is Hidden or Below the Fold
UX issue, not a bug

When accounts are set to optional, Shopify's checkout shows a contact information form — but may display a login prompt above it with a small "Continue without account" link below. On mobile, this link can be below the fold and customers exit thinking they're being forced to log in. To diagnose: open checkout on mobile (not desktop) in incognito mode and scroll down past the login fields to see if a guest link appears. Fix: on Shopify Plus, use checkout extensibility to reorder these elements. On standard plans, ensure your theme's cart page makes it clear that guest checkout is available at the next step.

4
Wrong Account System Active / Settings Not Saved
Common after the 2024 new accounts migration

If your store recently migrated from legacy to new customer accounts (or vice versa), you may have changed the setting in one interface but the other system is still active. The setting for legacy accounts (radio button: "Accounts optional") and the setting for new accounts ("Require login" toggle) are independent. Check which system is currently active, verify the guest-checkout setting in that system's interface, and save. Always test in an incognito browser window after saving — the admin UI confirms a save but doesn't show you the customer experience.

5
B2B / Wholesale Customer Segment Forced Login
Affects Shopify Plus stores with B2B features

Shopify Plus B2B features (custom pricing by customer, net terms, company accounts) require login because pricing is account-specific. B2B customer segments always require login regardless of the general guest checkout setting. This affects Plus merchants who have B2B enabled — if you're seeing forced login for some customers but not others, check whether those customers are in a B2B company account. Non-B2B customers on the same Plus store can still checkout as guests if the general setting allows it.

6
Custom Theme or App Injecting Login Redirect
Rare but hard to debug

Some custom themes or apps add JavaScript that detects unauthenticated customers and redirects them to /account/login before reaching checkout. To diagnose: disable all non-essential apps, switch to a default Shopify theme (Dawn), and test guest checkout. If guest checkout works on Dawn but not your custom theme, the issue is in the theme code — search the theme's JavaScript files for "account/login" redirects triggered on cart or checkout page load.

Account System Deep Dive

New Customer Accounts vs. Legacy: What Changed for Guest Checkout

In 2024, Shopify rolled out new customer accounts — a passwordless authentication system where customers log in via a one-time 6-digit code sent to their email instead of a stored password. The rollout changed where guest checkout is configured and how the checkout login step appears to customers.

Legacy Customer Accounts (still available, being sunsetted)

  • Login method: Email + password. Password reset via email link.
  • Guest checkout setting: Radio button — "Accounts disabled," "Accounts optional" (allows guest), or "Accounts required."
  • Checkout experience: Customers see an email/password form with a "Continue as guest" option if accounts are optional.
  • Status: Shopify has signaled intent to sunset legacy accounts, but no hard end date as of mid-2026. All stores can still use legacy accounts.

New Customer Accounts (Shopify's current direction)

  • Login method: Email only. Customer receives a 6-digit one-time code. No password required or stored.
  • Guest checkout setting: "Require login before checkout" toggle — turn OFF for guest checkout.
  • Checkout experience: Customers see a contact information email field. If they've shopped before, they're prompted with the 6-digit code option. If not, they proceed directly. The experience feels less like a "login wall" than legacy accounts.
  • Key benefit for conversion: Even when customers opt to log in with new accounts, the passwordless flow has lower friction than email + password, reducing abandonment from the login step itself.
Recommendation

If your store is still on legacy customer accounts and isn't dependent on apps that require the legacy system, migrating to new customer accounts is worth evaluating. The passwordless login has lower friction for returning customers, and the guest checkout path is more clearly separated from the login flow. Verify your loyalty, subscription, and account-dependent apps support new accounts before migrating.

Optimization

Post-Purchase Account Creation: The Right Way to Build Your Customer List

The goal isn't to skip account creation — it's to collect accounts from buyers who have already proven intent by completing a purchase. Here's how the post-purchase account strategy works on Shopify.

What Shopify Does Automatically

When a customer completes a guest checkout, Shopify sends an order confirmation email that includes an "Activate your account" link. This creates an account with the same email the customer used at checkout, pre-fills their name and address, and lets them track orders without re-entering details. No setup required — this is the default behavior when guest checkout is enabled.

What You Can Customize

  • Order confirmation page: The post-checkout confirmation page shows an account creation prompt. On Shopify Plus with checkout extensibility, you can customize the message and incentive (e.g., "Create an account to save 10% on your next order").
  • Order confirmation email: The "Activate account" email can be customized in Admin → Settings → Notifications → Customer account invite. Add brand-specific copy, highlight the account benefits (order tracking, faster checkout, loyalty points), and make the CTA prominent.
  • Post-purchase upsell apps: If you use a post-purchase upsell app (ReConvert, Zipify OCU), you can add an account creation prompt within the upsell flow — buyers who are already in a positive post-purchase state convert well here.

The post-purchase approach typically delivers more total accounts than forced pre-purchase registration, because the pool of completed buyers is larger than the pool who would have converted despite the login friction.

Prerequisite

Optimizing Guest Checkout Only Works If Checkout Is Reliable

Guest checkout optimization — whether enabling it for the first time, removing forced login from an app, or testing post-purchase account prompts — requires that checkout itself works consistently. A guest checkout path that errors 3% of the time will underperform a forced-login path that never errors, because technical failures at the payment step are not recoverable the way a friction point is.

Silent checkout failures are especially common in guest checkout flows because guest buyers are less likely to retry than logged-in account holders. A guest buyer who hits a payment error at step 3 is gone — they don't have the account-based investment to try again, and they typically won't file a support ticket.

FAQ

Common Questions About Shopify Guest Checkout

How do I enable guest checkout on Shopify?

Go to Admin → Settings → Customer accounts. On legacy accounts: select "Accounts are optional." On new customer accounts: make sure "Require login before checkout" is turned OFF. The change is immediate — no republishing needed. Test in an incognito window after saving.

Why is my Shopify guest checkout redirecting to login even though I enabled it?

The most common cause is a loyalty app, subscription app, or custom theme code overriding Shopify's checkout flow with a login redirect. Diagnose by disabling all non-essential apps and switching to a default theme (Dawn) temporarily — if guest checkout works, re-enable apps one at a time to identify the culprit.

Less common causes: wrong account system active after a migration (check both legacy and new accounts settings), or a B2B customer segment on Plus that always requires login.

Does Shopify guest checkout work on all plans?

Yes. Guest checkout (allowing purchase without an account) is available on every Shopify plan — Basic ($39/mo), Shopify ($105/mo), Advanced ($399/mo), and Plus ($2,300+/mo). There is no plan restriction. The only Plus-exclusive items are customizing the visual layout of the checkout login step and segmenting login requirements by customer type (B2B vs. retail).

What is the difference between "Accounts are optional" and "Accounts are disabled"?

"Accounts are optional" allows guest checkout but also allows customers to log in with an existing account for faster checkout. The checkout shows login fields that customers can skip. "Accounts are disabled" removes the account system entirely — no login option appears at checkout or on your store. Both allow guest checkout. "Accounts are disabled" gives a slightly cleaner checkout flow but removes order history and account management for returning customers. Most merchants should use "Accounts are optional" (or the equivalent "Require login" OFF in new accounts) to allow both paths.

Can I require login for some products but not others on Shopify?

Not natively on standard plans. The guest checkout setting is store-wide. On Shopify Plus, you can configure B2B customer segments with required login — so wholesale/B2B buyers must log in while retail buyers can check out as guests. Some apps claim to gate specific products behind login, but these typically work by redirecting from the product page or cart rather than from within the Shopify checkout itself (which is locked down). If you need login gating for specific product types, verify exactly where the login wall appears before committing to an app solution.
