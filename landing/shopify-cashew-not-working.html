Shopify Cashew Not Working — Not Showing, Declined, KYC & Verification Issues

Updated June 2026 · 7 failure modes · UAE, Saudi Arabia, and Kuwait merchants

Cashew is a UAE-founded BNPL platform operating across UAE, Saudi Arabia, and Kuwait with a Pay-in-3 model and a built-in cashback rewards program — making it the only MENA BNPL with native loyalty features at checkout. Its Shopify integration fails in ways tied to its three-market footprint, its two-step Shopify activation that merchants frequently miss, and identity verification requirements for UAE, KSA, and Kuwait customers. This guide covers all 7 Cashew failure modes with exact diagnostic steps for merchants in these markets.

How Cashew Differs From Tabby, Tamara, and Spotii

Cashew covers UAE, Saudi Arabia, and Kuwait — broader than Spotii (UAE+KSA only) but narrower than Tabby (UAE, KSA, Egypt, Kuwait, Qatar). Cashew and Tamara have nearly the same geographic footprint, but Tamara covers Bahrain while Cashew does not, and Cashew covers Kuwait while both do. Merchants targeting Egyptian customers specifically must add Tabby — Cashew does not operate in Egypt.

Cashew is the only MENA BNPL with a native cashback and rewards program. Customers earn Cashew rewards points on qualifying purchases, redeemable on future orders across Cashew's merchant network. This loyalty layer differentiates Cashew from Tabby, Tamara, and Spotii — but it also introduces a unique customer confusion vector: customers who see a rewards balance in the Cashew app sometimes expect to apply it as a discount at checkout, and are confused when the rewards redemption flow differs from a simple coupon code. Merchants should be aware that Cashew customer queries are sometimes about rewards redemption, not about integration failures.

Cashew uses a Pay-in-3 model — three equal interest-free installments, typically at 0, 30, and 60 days. This is the same installment count as Tamara's Pay-in-3, but distinct from Tabby's Pay-in-4 monthly model and Spotii's Pay-in-4 bi-weekly model. Three installments means fewer payment events per plan and less payment scheduling complexity for customers — but the total repayment window of ~60 days is shorter than Tabby's ~90-day window for Pay-in-4 monthly.

Cashew's spending limit is not visible to customers. Like Tabby and Spotii, Cashew does not show customers their available spending limit — they discover it only at the moment of decline. Tamara is the exception among MENA BNPL providers that shows customers their available credit in-app. This opacity means Cashew declines generate merchant support tickets in which customers assume the merchant's Cashew integration is broken.

1

Cashew Not Showing at Shopify Checkout

Merchant-side

Cashew is a third-party alternative payment provider on Shopify, which means its visibility at checkout requires two separate actions that many merchants conflate. Installing the Cashew app from the Shopify App Store is step one — but Cashew will not appear at checkout until it is also manually enabled as an alternative payment provider in Shopify Admin → Settings → Payments → Alternative payment providers. Merchants frequently install the app, see it in their app list, and assume the checkout activation is complete. It is not. This two-step requirement is the same for all MENA BNPL providers (Tabby, Tamara, Spotii, Cashew) and remains the most common suppression cause.

Currency and geography are the hard gates for Cashew. Cashew operates in UAE (AED), Saudi Arabia (SAR), and Kuwait (KWD). If your store's checkout currency for UAE customers is USD, EUR, or any non-AED currency — even if AED is displayed as a runtime conversion from USD — Cashew will not appear. Multi-currency stores that use Shopify Markets with USD as the base currency and runtime conversion to AED will suppress Cashew for all UAE customers regardless of configuration. The same applies to SAR for Saudi Arabia and KWD for Kuwait. To fix this, set the correct fixed checkout currency for each respective market in Shopify Admin → Settings → Markets.

Regional scope matters. Cashew only appears for checkouts shipping to UAE, Saudi Arabia, and Kuwait. Customers shipping to Egypt, Bahrain, Qatar, or Oman will not see Cashew — not because of a configuration error, but because Cashew does not operate in those markets. If Egyptian, Bahraini, or Qatari customers report Cashew is missing, the correct resolution is to add Tabby (covers Egypt and Qatar) or Tamara (covers Bahrain) rather than debugging the Cashew integration.

Additional suppression causes: test/sandbox API credentials configured instead of live production keys; the cart contains only digital products or gift cards (Cashew focuses on physical goods); the Cashew merchant account is under review (KYC pending — see failure mode 7); or the Shopify Cashew app version is outdated.

How to Diagnose

  1. Check two-step activation: Shopify Admin → Apps → confirm the Cashew app is listed as installed; then Shopify Admin → Settings → Payments → Alternative payment providers → confirm Cashew is listed and the toggle is enabled. Both must be true simultaneously.
  2. Check checkout currency per market: Shopify Admin → Settings → Markets → UAE → confirm AED is set as the fixed checkout currency, not a runtime conversion. Repeat for Saudi Arabia (SAR) and Kuwait (KWD). Currency conversion from USD suppresses Cashew for all customers in those markets.
  3. Verify the customer's shipping destination: Cashew only appears when the checkout shipping address is in UAE, Saudi Arabia, or Kuwait. Checkouts to other countries will not show Cashew regardless of configuration — this is expected behavior.
  4. Check API credential mode: In the Cashew app settings in Shopify, confirm live/production API credentials are configured — not sandbox or test credentials. Test credentials suppress Cashew for real customers while allowing test checkouts to proceed.
  5. Verify merchant account status: Log into the Cashew merchant portal and confirm your account shows active/live status. Accounts with pending KYC, document review, or risk assessment will suppress Cashew at checkout silently (see failure mode 7).
  6. Test with a physical product above minimum: Add physical products totalling above 100 AED (UAE) or 100 SAR (Saudi Arabia) to cart and checkout with a UAE or Saudi Arabia shipping address. Cashew should appear for valid carts.
Three-market scope note: Cashew appears only for UAE (AED), Saudi Arabia (SAR), and Kuwait (KWD) checkouts. If customers from Egypt, Bahrain, or Qatar report Cashew is missing — it is not a configuration error. Cashew simply does not operate in those markets. Consider adding Tabby (covers Egypt, Kuwait, Qatar) or Tamara (covers Bahrain) for broader GCC coverage.
2

Per-Transaction Risk Declined — "Not Available for This Order"

Customer-side

Like all MENA BNPL providers, Cashew runs an independent risk assessment on every transaction — past approval does not guarantee future approval. Each checkout triggers a real-time decision against Cashew's risk model, which evaluates the customer's payment history across all Cashew merchants, the number of currently open Cashew plans, the customer's total outstanding Cashew balance relative to the requested cart value, the customer's phone verification status, and contextual signals from the specific transaction (merchant category, cart size, session pattern).

First-time Cashew customers face higher decline rates. The Cashew risk model starts conservatively for new users with no payment history on the platform. First purchases are scored against a limited data set, which can result in a decline or approval for a smaller amount than expected. Customers who have used Cashew consistently over several months with on-time payments generally see improving approval rates and higher available spending capacity over time.

Cashew's cashback rewards balance is separate from spending capacity. A customer may have accumulated Cashew rewards points from previous purchases — a visible balance in the app — while simultaneously being declined at checkout because their spending limit is exhausted. The rewards balance and the spending limit are independent. Customers who see rewards in the app and are then declined often report this as a system error ("I have money in Cashew but it won't let me pay"), when in fact the rewards balance does not offset the spending limit used by their open plans.

Merchants cannot override Cashew risk decisions, access the decline reason, or appeal on behalf of customers. The most effective merchant response is to ensure that alternative payment methods (Tabby, Tamara, credit card, Apple Pay) are visibly available at checkout so customers have a clear fallback when Cashew declines.

Fix Steps for Customers

  1. Try a smaller cart value: Cashew may approve a smaller purchase even if a larger one was declined. If your cart is close to your available Cashew spending capacity, reducing the total may fit within your remaining limit — you can complete the remainder in a separate order later.
  2. Wait for existing Cashew plans to progress: Pay-in-3 means each plan has two future payments. Making an early payment on an open plan reduces your outstanding balance and frees spending capacity. After a payment on an existing plan, retry the declined purchase.
  3. Verify your Cashew account is fully set up: Open the Cashew app and confirm your identity (Emirates ID / National ID / Civil ID) is verified and your phone number is confirmed. An incomplete account profile is a silent decline trigger.
  4. Note that rewards balance ≠ spending capacity: Your Cashew cashback rewards balance does not increase your spending limit. Rewards are redeemed on qualifying future purchases — they do not help if your current spending limit is exhausted from open plans.
  5. Contact Cashew customer support: Cashew's in-app support can provide more context on a specific decline — they can indicate whether it is a limit, verification, or risk issue.
  6. Use Tabby, Tamara, or a credit card as an alternative: If Cashew declines on a specific purchase, other BNPL options or card payments provide a parallel path at the same merchant.
Merchant note: Sporadic Cashew declines affecting specific customers are not integration errors — they are customer-level risk decisions. If all customers are being declined simultaneously, that is a merchant account or configuration issue (check failure mode 7). If only some customers see declines, your Cashew integration is working correctly and the issue is on the customer side.
3

Spending Limit Reached (Opaque — Customers Cannot See It)

Customer-side

Cashew assigns each customer a maximum outstanding balance — the total they can carry across all open Cashew plans simultaneously. When a new purchase would take their outstanding balance above this limit, the transaction is declined. Unlike Tamara — which shows customers their available credit limit clearly in the app — Cashew does not make this limit visible. Customers never know their Cashew spending limit until they hit it, and when they do, they receive only a generic "not available for this order" message.

The Pay-in-3 model creates a specific limit exhaustion pattern. With Pay-in-3, a Cashew plan opened today has payments due at day 0 (immediate), day 30, and day 60. A customer who opens three Cashew plans at the same time — perhaps during a major sale event — commits to paying three different merchants at each 30-day interval for the next 60 days. Each open plan holds a portion of their available Cashew limit until the plan is fully paid. During Ramadan, White Friday, or National Day sales periods in the UAE and Saudi Arabia, customers routinely open multiple Cashew plans in quick succession, exhausting their limit by the end of the sale event.

Customers confuse rewards balance with spending capacity. This is a unique confusion point for Cashew that does not apply to Tabby, Tamara, or Spotii. A customer with a visible Cashew cashback balance may assume they have "credit" to spend — but the rewards balance is a loyalty currency redeemable on qualifying future orders, not a spending limit increase. Customers who report "I have money in Cashew but it won't let me checkout" are typically experiencing a spending limit exhaustion, not a technical failure. The Cashew app's rewards balance display can inadvertently create this expectation mismatch.

Fix Steps for Customers

  1. Check your open Cashew plans: Open the Cashew app → My Plans or Orders → review how many open plans you have and what their remaining balances are. Total outstanding balance across all plans is what counts against your limit.
  2. Make an early payment on an existing plan: Cashew app → select an open plan → Make a payment. An early payment on any plan reduces your outstanding balance and frees spending capacity — you do not need to close a plan entirely.
  3. Note that rewards balance does not help: Your Cashew cashback rewards balance does not expand your spending limit. Rewards are redeemable on qualifying future orders but do not offset the limit consumed by open plans.
  4. Try a lower cart value: If your available Cashew capacity is partially consumed, a smaller cart value may fit within your remaining limit. Split a larger order into two separate purchases if possible.
  5. Wait for your next scheduled payment: Cashew's Pay-in-3 installments occur at 30-day intervals. After your next scheduled payment on any open plan clears, your outstanding balance decreases and available capacity increases — retry the declined purchase after the payment posts.
  6. Contact Cashew support for a limit review: If you have a consistent history of on-time Cashew payments, contact Cashew support to request a spending limit review. Customers with 6–12 months of clean payment history are more likely to receive a limit increase.
Merchant note: Because Cashew's limit is opaque and customers can have a visible rewards balance while being limit-declined, Cashew generates a distinctive type of support ticket: "I have rewards in Cashew but it won't work at checkout." The most useful merchant response is to clarify in your FAQ that Cashew cashback rewards are a separate loyalty currency from BNPL spending capacity, and to ensure alternative payment methods are visible and accessible.
4

Emirates ID, National ID, or Civil ID Verification Failure

Customer-side

Cashew requires identity verification as part of account setup and risk management. UAE customers must verify with their Emirates ID; Saudi Arabia customers must verify with their Saudi National ID (Huwiyya for nationals) or Iqama (residence permit for expats); Kuwait customers must verify with their Kuwaiti Civil ID. This verification happens at the Cashew account level — not at every checkout — but unverified or pending accounts are blocked from completing purchases, which surfaces at checkout as a decline with no explanation about the underlying verification status.

Emirates ID verification is the most common identity failure for UAE customers. Verification fails when: the ID number entered does not match records; the Emirates ID is expired (the UAE requires periodic Emirates ID renewal, and an expired ID is not accepted for verification); the name on the Emirates ID does not exactly match the name on the Cashew account; or the customer registered Cashew before a recent Emirates ID renewal and the Cashew account still holds outdated ID data. Customers who renewed their Emirates ID but have not updated it in the Cashew app will face verification failures at checkout without knowing why.

Kuwaiti Civil ID verification is Cashew's third-market identity requirement — unique among MENA BNPL providers on Shopify. Cashew is one of the few BNPL providers that explicitly supports Kuwait, and the Kuwaiti Civil ID verification requirement is distinct from UAE or Saudi identity flows. Kuwaiti residents who have recently received or renewed their Civil ID may experience a 24–48 hour propagation lag before the updated ID is accepted by Cashew's verification system. Expatriates in Kuwait with Civil ID must also pass Cashew's residency permit check, which introduces additional verification steps compared to Kuwaiti nationals.

Saudi Arabia expat verification is the same friction point found in Tabby and Tamara: recently renewed Iqama numbers, Iqama transliteration differences between Arabic and English, or a recently issued Iqama for new Saudi arrivals can all block verification. Customers who recently completed Iqama renewal should wait 48–72 hours and reattempt verification as government databases propagate the renewal.

Fix Steps for Customers

  1. Open the Cashew app and check your verification status: Cashew app → Profile or Account → check if your identity document (Emirates ID / National ID / Iqama / Civil ID) shows as verified. A pending or rejected status blocks all purchases.
  2. Update your ID if recently renewed: If you recently renewed your Emirates ID, Iqama, or Civil ID, your Cashew account may still reference your old document data. Cashew app → Account → Update ID → submit your new document details and a scan of both sides.
  3. Ensure name consistency: The name on your identity document must match the name on your Cashew account. For expatriates in UAE and Saudi Arabia, Arabic name transliteration to English can differ between the document and the Cashew account — contact Cashew support to manually align the name if they differ.
  4. Submit clear document images: If Cashew's automated verification rejected your document scan, resubmit with a higher quality image — well-lit, flat surface, all four corners of the ID visible, no glare across the chip or barcode.
  5. Wait after ID renewal: Government identity databases (UAE ICA, Saudi Absher, Kuwait PACI) typically propagate ID renewals within 24–72 hours. If your new ID was just issued, wait before retrying Cashew verification — the system may not yet recognize the renewed document number.
  6. Contact Cashew support directly: If automated verification has failed more than twice, manual review by Cashew's compliance team is the next step. Contact Cashew customer support with your account details and specify which verification step failed.
Kuwait-specific note: Cashew's Civil ID verification for Kuwait is a distinct flow that not all Shopify merchants with Cashew installed are aware of. If Cashew is working for UAE and Saudi Arabia customers but Kuwait customers consistently see declines at the verification stage, the most likely cause is Civil ID verification issues rather than a Shopify integration problem. Route Kuwait customer support tickets toward Cashew account re-verification rather than a merchant-side fix.
5

Phone Number Verification / OTP Failure

Customer-side

Cashew uses SMS OTP (one-time password) verification during account setup and at checkout confirmation for certain transaction flows. The OTP is sent to the mobile number registered on the customer's Cashew account. OTP delivery fails in several specific scenarios that are common in the UAE, Saudi Arabia, and Kuwait due to regional SIM and telecom patterns.

Non-local SIM cards are the primary OTP failure cause. Cashew's OTP delivery is optimized for UAE (+971), Saudi Arabia (+966), and Kuwait (+965) mobile numbers. Customers who registered Cashew with a local number but are now using a foreign SIM (roaming or a SIM swap) may not receive OTPs reliably — international roaming can delay or drop SMS messages from Gulf-based telecom gateways. Customers who relocated from UAE or Saudi Arabia but still have a Cashew account registered to their old local number will face OTP failure on every checkout until they update their phone number in the Cashew app.

Dual-SIM phones are a frequent OTP confusion source in the Gulf region. Many UAE, Saudi, and Kuwait residents carry dual-SIM phones — often one local SIM and one home-country SIM. OTPs intended for the local Gulf number sometimes arrive on the second SIM (if the local number is assigned to SIM slot 2), or the customer checks the wrong SIM for the OTP. Customers who report the OTP "didn't arrive" often find it arrived on the SIM they were not actively monitoring.

SMS filtering by telecom carriers in the Gulf can also block Cashew OTPs. Short-code SMS from fintech providers is more aggressively filtered in some carrier networks than standard SMS. Customers who consistently fail to receive Cashew OTPs should check their carrier's SMS filtering settings or spam folder before assuming a Cashew system problem.

Fix Steps for Customers

  1. Confirm which number your Cashew account is registered to: Cashew app → Account or Profile → Phone Number — verify this is your current, active UAE (+971), Saudi (+966), or Kuwait (+965) number.
  2. Check both SIMs on a dual-SIM phone: If you use a dual-SIM device, check the SMS inbox on both SIMs — the OTP may have been delivered to the SIM you are not currently monitoring.
  3. Check SMS spam or filtered folders: Some Gulf carriers route fintech short-code SMS to filtered or promotional folders. Check your messages app for a filtered or spam folder if the OTP did not arrive in your main inbox.
  4. Request a new OTP: OTPs expire after a short window (typically 3–5 minutes). If you took too long entering it, request a new one rather than retrying the expired code.
  5. Update your phone number if it has changed: Cashew app → Account → Update Phone Number → enter your current active local Gulf number and complete re-verification before attempting another checkout.
  6. Try over a strong mobile connection: OTP SMS delivery can be affected by weak signal or WiFi-only connections. Switch to a strong 4G/5G mobile data connection and request a fresh OTP.

Order Value Below Cashew Minimum

Merchant + Customer

Cashew has a minimum order value requirement — typically 100 AED in the UAE and 100 SAR in Saudi Arabia. Carts below these minimums will not display Cashew at checkout, regardless of the customer's account status or the merchant's configuration. This threshold is applied before Cashew's risk assessment runs — so a below-minimum cart never reaches the point of showing Cashew as an option, and no error is displayed.

The minimum is applied to the post-discount checkout transaction value, not catalog prices. Discount codes, automatic promotions, or order-level offers that reduce the checkout total below the Cashew minimum will suppress Cashew — even if the cart contained items priced above the minimum before discounts. A customer who applies a large discount code that brings the order total below 100 AED will no longer see Cashew at checkout, even if Cashew was visible earlier in the flow.

How Cashew's minimum compares to other MENA BNPL providers. Tabby's minimum is near-zero (approximately 1 AED / 10 SAR), making it the most accessible option for low-value carts. Cashew's 100 AED / 100 SAR minimum is the same as Tamara's minimum for UAE. Spotii's minimum is 50 AED (UAE) / 200 SAR (Saudi Arabia) — lower than Cashew in UAE and higher in Saudi Arabia. For merchants with average order values below 100 AED — cosmetics, accessories, small digital goods with physical packing — Cashew will suppress on a higher percentage of carts than Tabby, and Tabby should be considered as the primary low-AOV BNPL option.

Fix Steps

  1. Verify cart value after discounts: Add up items in the cart after all promotions and discount codes are applied — if the total is below 100 AED (UAE) or 100 SAR (Saudi Arabia), Cashew not showing is expected behavior, not a bug.
  2. Check if a discount code is bringing the cart below minimum: Test the checkout flow with and without the discount code to confirm whether the promotion is pushing the order total below the Cashew threshold.
  3. Compare Tabby and Cashew minimum thresholds: If Cashew suppression due to below-minimum carts is affecting a significant portion of orders, Tabby's near-zero minimum makes it a better fit for low-value average-order-value products.
  4. Consider minimum order incentives: If Cashew is a primary BNPL option for your customers, adding an incentive (free shipping threshold, bundle pricing) that naturally pushes orders above 100 AED may both increase Cashew eligibility and improve your average order value.

Merchant KYC Pending or Account Not Fully Onboarded

Merchant-side

Cashew requires merchants to complete a Know Your Customer (KYC) and business verification process before going live at checkout. This process typically involves submitting commercial registration documents, trade license (UAE), or equivalent business registration documents for Saudi Arabia and Kuwait, along with bank account verification and a business review period. Merchants who complete app installation and Shopify payment provider activation but have not completed the KYC process will see Cashew configured in Shopify — but no customers will be able to use it, as Cashew blocks live transactions until merchant verification is complete.

Pending KYC is a silent failure mode. Unlike a disabled Shopify payment toggle or a currency mismatch — which can be caught by checking Shopify Admin — merchant KYC status is held in the Cashew merchant portal, not in Shopify. A merchant can have Cashew fully enabled in Shopify settings, with correct currency configuration and live API credentials, and still have zero Cashew transactions completing because their merchant account is pending review. The Shopify checkout shows no error; customers simply see Cashew unavailable, and the merchant has no indication from Shopify that the blockage is happening at the Cashew account level.

KYC review timelines vary. Standard Cashew merchant KYC for UAE-licensed businesses typically completes within 3–7 business days after all required documents are submitted. Saudi Arabia and Kuwait merchants may face additional review steps due to localized business registration requirements. Merchants who submitted documents during UAE or KSA public holiday periods, or who submitted incomplete documents, may face longer review periods without receiving proactive status updates. Checking the Cashew merchant portal directly is the only reliable way to confirm KYC status.

API credential mode matters during onboarding. During the KYC period, Cashew merchants are given test/sandbox API credentials to configure the Shopify integration and run test checkouts. These test credentials allow checkout testing in a sandbox environment — but they suppress Cashew for all real customers. A common merchant error is to go live (complete KYC) but not update the Shopify integration from test credentials to the live production API credentials issued after KYC approval. Cashew will appear in test mode but will be blocked for real customers until live credentials are applied.

How to Diagnose and Fix

  1. Check Cashew merchant portal for KYC status: Log into the Cashew merchant portal and navigate to your account or onboarding status page. Confirm your account shows "Active," "Live," or equivalent status. If the portal shows "Pending Review," "Documents Required," or similar, KYC is incomplete and Cashew will be blocked for real customers until review completes.
  2. Submit any missing KYC documents: If the Cashew merchant portal indicates documents are missing or rejected, resubmit the correct documents immediately. Required documents typically include: UAE Trade License or equivalent business registration; bank account verification letter or voided cheque; authorized signatory Emirates ID or passport. The specific requirements vary by country; check the Cashew merchant onboarding checklist in the portal.
  3. Switch from test to live API credentials: After KYC approval, Cashew issues live/production API credentials. In Shopify Admin → Apps → Cashew → Configuration, replace test/sandbox API keys with the live production keys from the Cashew merchant portal. Test credentials will always suppress Cashew for real customers even after KYC is complete.
  4. Run a live test transaction: After switching to live API credentials, use a real customer account (not a Cashew test account) to complete a small purchase end-to-end. Confirm the order appears in both your Shopify order list and the Cashew merchant portal.
  5. Contact Cashew merchant support if KYC is stalled: If your KYC submission has been pending for more than 7 business days without a status update, contact Cashew's merchant support team directly with your application reference number. Manual escalation often resolves review delays caused by document processing backlogs.
  6. Check for annual re-verification: Some Cashew merchant agreements include periodic re-verification requirements — typically when a UAE Trade License or business registration document expires and needs to be resubmitted. If Cashew was working previously and stopped without any merchant-side changes, check whether any registered business documents have recently expired.
Critical diagnostic question: Is Cashew configured in Shopify but not appearing at checkout for any customer? If yes and you recently went through Cashew onboarding, check merchant KYC status in the Cashew portal before debugging currency, region, or app settings. KYC pending is the most common reason for Cashew showing as configured but producing zero transactions during the first weeks after installation.
