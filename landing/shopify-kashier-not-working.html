Shopify Kashier Not Working — Not Appearing, Account Not Approved & Order Stuck Pending

Egypt · Updated June 2026 · 7 failure modes

Kashier is Egypt's second major online payment gateway with a Shopify app, but it fails in specific ways that differ from international gateways. This guide covers the 7 root causes — from test mode left active to KYC approval delays to Meeza card issues — so you can identify which failure you're facing and fix it immediately.

What Is Kashier?

Kashier is an Egyptian payment gateway founded in Cairo in 2019. It provides Egyptian e-commerce merchants with a Shopify app and API integration, supporting Visa, Mastercard, Meeza (Egypt's national debit card), Fawry, Vodafone Cash, Etisalat Cash, and bank transfers. Kashier is positioned as an alternative to Paymob (Accept) for Egypt merchants, with comparable payment method coverage.

Kashier requires Egyptian business registration (commercial register + tax card) and manual KYC approval before live payments can be accepted. Until the account is approved, only sandbox/test mode works. Kashier is not available to international merchants without an Egyptian business entity.

The most common Shopify integration uses the Kashier Shopify App from the Shopify App Store. Alternatively, advanced merchants use Kashier's JavaScript SDK or direct API. Both require a Merchant ID and API secret from the Kashier dashboard.

7 Failure Modes

  1. Kashier not appearing at checkout — test mode active or app misconfigured
  2. Merchant account pending KYC approval — cannot accept live payments
  3. 3D Secure authentication failing — bank compatibility or popup blocker
  4. Meeza card declined — BIN support not enabled on plan
  5. Order stuck pending after payment — webhook URL not configured
  6. Fawry or wallet sub-method not showing — not enabled on plan tier
  7. Currency mismatch — store set to USD instead of EGP
1

Kashier Not Appearing at Checkout — Test Mode Active or App Misconfigured

Merchant Setup

The most common reason Kashier does not appear at Shopify checkout is that the Kashier app is installed but still in sandbox or test mode. In test mode, Kashier is visible only to admin users viewing the store — regular customers see no Kashier option at checkout. This is the intended behavior during development, but it catches many merchants off guard when they believe the integration is complete.

The second cause is a missing or incorrect Merchant ID. The Kashier Shopify app requires you to enter your live Merchant ID and API secret from the Kashier dashboard. If these fields are blank or contain test credentials, Kashier will not appear for live customers. Your live Merchant ID is found in the Kashier merchant portal under Account → Integration Details.

The third cause is a store domain mismatch. Kashier's Shopify integration validates that the request comes from a registered store domain. If your Shopify store URL in the Kashier dashboard does not match your actual Shopify store domain (including whether it uses a custom domain or the .myshopify.com domain), the checkout integration is blocked.

Finally, the Kashier app must be explicitly set as an active payment provider in Shopify: Settings → Payments → Third-party providers → Manage. Installing the app alone is not enough — you must activate it as a payment provider.

Fix Steps

  1. Open the Kashier app in your Shopify admin and verify Mode is set to Live, not Test/Sandbox.
  2. Confirm your live Merchant ID and API secret are entered (not test credentials).
  3. In Shopify: Settings → Payments → Third-party providers — verify Kashier is listed and Active.
  4. In your Kashier merchant dashboard: Account → Allowed Domains — verify your Shopify store domain is listed.
  5. Test by visiting your store checkout as a guest (not as admin) in an incognito browser window.
2

Merchant Account Pending KYC Approval — Cannot Accept Live Payments

Merchant Setup

Kashier requires manual KYC (Know Your Customer) review of every new merchant account before live payments can be processed. This is a Central Bank of Egypt compliance requirement for all licensed payment service providers in Egypt, not a Kashier-specific policy.

The review process typically takes 2–5 business days after all required documents are submitted. Required documents include: commercial registration certificate (السجل التجاري), tax card (البطاقة الضريبية), bank account details at an Egyptian licensed bank, and business activity proof. Individual (non-company) sellers may have different documentation requirements — check the Kashier merchant portal for your account type.

The most common cause of delays beyond 5 business days is an unanswered document request. Kashier's compliance team may email requesting additional documents — if this email goes to spam or is missed, the application stalls indefinitely. Check your registration email inbox and spam folder for any requests from Kashier compliance.

During the KYC review period, you can use Kashier's sandbox mode with test card numbers. No live customer charges are possible until approval is granted.

Fix Steps

  1. Log in to the Kashier merchant portal and check your account status — pending, under review, or approved.
  2. Check your registration email inbox and spam folder for document requests from Kashier.
  3. Ensure all required documents are uploaded: commercial register, tax card, bank account details.
  4. If more than 5 business days have passed with no update, contact Kashier support via their merchant portal chat or email.
  5. While awaiting approval, use sandbox mode with Kashier's published test card numbers to verify your Shopify integration is ready.
Kashier vs Paymob onboarding: Paymob's onboarding process for Egyptian merchants is similar but may differ in required documentation. If your business entity is already registered with another Egyptian gateway, you still need to resubmit documents to Kashier — approvals do not transfer between gateways.
3

3D Secure Authentication Failing — Bank Compatibility or Popup Blocker

Customer Side

Kashier uses 3D Secure (3DS) authentication for card payments as required by the Central Bank of Egypt. When a customer enters their card details, Kashier redirects them to their issuing bank's 3DS page for OTP verification. If this step fails, the payment is declined even though the card is valid and funded.

The most common 3DS failure cause is a browser popup blocker. Some banks' 3DS flows open in a popup window. If the customer's browser blocks popups, the 3DS step silently fails and the customer sees a generic "payment failed" message. Ask the customer to allow popups from the Kashier checkout domain and retry.

A second cause is the customer's bank not having enrolled their card for 3DS. This is particularly common with older Egyptian bank cards and some Meeza card variants. The customer needs to contact their issuing bank to enroll in 3DS (also called Verified by Visa or Mastercard SecureCode). Some banks allow enrollment via their mobile banking app; others require a branch visit.

The third cause is a bank-side 3DS outage. Egyptian bank 3DS systems occasionally have downtime, especially on payment-heavy dates (end of month, Eid). If multiple customers are reporting 3DS failures simultaneously with the same issuing bank, this is likely a bank-side issue and will resolve on its own.

Fix Steps

  1. Ask the customer to disable popup blockers in their browser and retry the payment.
  2. Ask if their card is enrolled in 3D Secure — if not, they should contact their bank to activate it.
  3. Ask the customer to try a different browser (Chrome vs Safari) as 3DS compatibility varies.
  4. Check if multiple customers with the same issuing bank are failing — if so, it may be a bank outage; retry later.
  5. For persistent failures, the customer can try an alternative payment method: Fawry (if enabled), Vodafone Cash, or a different card.
4

Meeza Card Declined — BIN Support Not Enabled on Plan

Merchant + Customer

Meeza (مييزة) is Egypt's national debit card scheme, launched by the Central Bank of Egypt and issued by Egyptian banks. Meeza cards use a distinct card BIN range (639204–639204 and related ranges) that differs from international Visa and Mastercard ranges, and they require specific integration with Egypt's national card switch.

Kashier supports Meeza, but Meeza acceptance is plan-dependent. Not all Kashier plan tiers have Meeza BIN processing enabled by default. If your plan does not include Meeza, customers will see a decline that looks identical to an "invalid card" error — both the customer and the merchant may assume the card itself is the problem, when actually Meeza simply isn't enabled on the gateway side.

Additionally, some early-issue Meeza cards (issued before full 3DS integration was complete at certain Egyptian banks) may have issues with 3DS enrollment. Customers with these cards need to visit their bank to upgrade their card's digital enrollment.

Meeza declines are indistinguishable from card errors from the customer's perspective. If a customer reports a decline and you've confirmed their card is funded and valid, contact Kashier support to verify whether your plan includes Meeza and request activation if needed.

Fix Steps

  1. Ask the customer to confirm their card type — specifically whether it's a Meeza card (look for the Meeza logo on the card).
  2. Log in to your Kashier merchant dashboard → Payment Methods and check if Meeza is listed and enabled.
  3. If Meeza is not enabled, contact Kashier support to request Meeza activation on your account.
  4. While Meeza is being activated, direct affected customers to pay via Fawry or Vodafone Cash as alternatives (if enabled).
  5. Verify your Kashier test environment includes a Meeza test card number to test Meeza acceptance after activation.
5

Order Stuck Pending After Payment — Webhook URL Not Configured

Merchant Setup

This is the most dangerous Kashier failure mode: the customer's card or wallet is successfully charged, but the Shopify order remains in Pending status indefinitely. The customer paid; you have no record of it in Shopify.

Kashier is an asynchronous payment gateway. After a customer completes payment (including 3DS), Kashier sends a POST request to a webhook (also called IPN — Instant Payment Notification) URL to notify Shopify that the payment succeeded. If this webhook URL is missing, incorrect, or points to a non-production endpoint, Kashier's notification cannot be delivered and Shopify never updates the order status.

Common misconfiguration causes: the webhook URL was set during development using a localhost address or ngrok tunnel that no longer exists; the merchant migrated their Shopify store to a new domain without updating the Kashier webhook URL; or the webhook URL was never set because the developer assumed the Shopify app integration handles it automatically (it does not for all integration paths).

The Kashier merchant dashboard maintains a transaction log that shows every payment and its status independently of Shopify. Compare pending Shopify orders against Kashier's successful transactions to identify customers who paid but whose orders weren't confirmed in Shopify.

Fix Steps

  1. Log in to Kashier merchant dashboard → Settings → Webhook / IPN.
  2. Verify the webhook URL is set to your live Shopify store's payment callback endpoint (not localhost or a test tunnel).
  3. If using the Kashier Shopify App, confirm the app has populated the webhook URL automatically in the Kashier dashboard — verify it matches your live store domain.
  4. In the Kashier dashboard, check Transaction History for successful payments that correspond to pending Shopify orders.
  5. For affected customers, manually mark the Shopify order as paid in Shopify admin after confirming payment in the Kashier dashboard, and fulfill the order.
Critical: Customer funds deducted but Shopify order pending means the money is in your gateway — you must fulfill those orders or issue refunds. Do not assume pending orders were failed payments before checking the Kashier transaction log.
6

Fawry or Wallet Sub-Method Not Showing — Not Enabled on Plan Tier

Merchant Setup

Kashier aggregates multiple Egyptian payment methods under a single gateway integration: Visa, Mastercard, Meeza, Fawry, Vodafone Cash, Etisalat Cash, and bank transfer. However, not all of these are automatically enabled on every merchant plan. Local wallet and Fawry methods typically require a separate plan upgrade or feature activation request.

If customers report that they cannot see a Fawry option or Vodafone Cash option at Kashier checkout, verify which payment methods are active on your plan in the Kashier merchant portal under Payment Methods. Methods not listed as active simply do not appear at checkout — there is no error message, the option is just absent.

Fawry-specific timing issue: When a customer selects Fawry payment via Kashier, Kashier generates a Fawry reference code with a 30-minute expiry window. If the customer takes the reference code but pays at a Fawry kiosk more than 30 minutes later, or if their browser session expires, the reference code is invalid and the payment fails. Kashier must generate a new reference code for them to retry.

Wallet methods (Vodafone Cash, Etisalat Cash) via Kashier require the customer's registered mobile number to match the wallet exactly — similar to direct wallet integrations. An OTP is sent to that number for verification.

Fix Steps

  1. In the Kashier merchant portal, navigate to Payment Methods and review which methods are listed as Active vs Inactive.
  2. To enable Fawry, Vodafone Cash, or other local methods, contact Kashier support to request activation — this may require a plan upgrade.
  3. For Fawry payment issues, check whether the customer's Fawry reference code has expired (30-minute window) — they need to restart checkout to get a new code.
  4. For wallet OTP issues, confirm the customer is entering the same mobile number registered with their wallet (not just any mobile number).
7

Currency Mismatch — Store Set to USD Instead of EGP

Merchant Setup

Kashier processes and settles in Egyptian Pounds (EGP). The gateway is licensed by the Central Bank of Egypt and operates within Egypt's regulated payment infrastructure, which requires EGP as the transaction currency. If your Shopify store displays prices in USD or another foreign currency, Kashier will either reject the transaction outright or produce unpredictable results with incorrect amounts.

This issue arises most commonly when Egyptian merchants set up their Shopify store for international sales (with USD pricing) and later add Kashier for local Egyptian customers. The store currency and the gateway settlement currency must match.

If you need to serve both international (USD) and local Egyptian (EGP) customers, the standard approach is to use Shopify Markets to present EGP pricing to Egyptian visitors while displaying USD for others. With Shopify Markets configured, you can route Egyptian customers to Kashier checkout with EGP amounts. This requires Shopify's multi-currency features and careful integration testing.

The symptom of a currency mismatch is usually an outright Kashier payment rejection with an error like "currency not supported" or the payment succeeding for an incorrect amount (the EGP numerical value matching the USD numerical value — e.g., a $50 USD order being charged 50 EGP instead of ~2,500 EGP).

Fix Steps

  1. In Shopify admin: Settings → Store details → Store currency — verify the store currency is set to EGP if you are exclusively targeting Egyptian customers.
  2. If serving both local and international customers, set up Shopify Markets with EGP for Egypt and configure Kashier as the payment method for the Egypt market only.
  3. Test a complete checkout flow in EGP to verify the amount charged by Kashier matches the Shopify order total in EGP.
  4. Review any recent Kashier transactions for incorrect amounts — if EGP amounts appear unrealistically low (e.g., 50 EGP for a 50 USD order), the currency mismatch has been active and affected orders need investigation.
Kashier EGP settlement: Even if the customer pays with Visa/Mastercard (which can technically process in multiple currencies), Kashier settles to your Egyptian bank account in EGP. Confirm with Kashier that your integration does not inadvertently trigger any cross-currency conversion that changes the amount.
