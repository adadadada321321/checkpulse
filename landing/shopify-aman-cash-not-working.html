Shopify Aman Cash Not Working — Reference Code Not Received, Kiosk Rejection & Order Not Confirmed

Egypt · Updated June 2026 · 7 failure modes · Aman Cash kiosk network · 50,000+ agent points

Aman Cash (أمان كاش) is Egypt's physical cash payment network — customers complete checkout online, receive a reference code, then pay cash at any of 50,000+ Aman agent locations nationwide. When Aman Cash stops working on Shopify, the cause is almost always one of seven things: no gateway integration, reference code not delivered, code expired at the kiosk, wrong digits at the counter, order stuck pending after payment, an amount mismatch, or the store priced in a foreign currency. This guide covers all seven.

Aman Cash vs. Aman Wallet — Two Completely Different Products

Aman Cash is not Aman Wallet. Aman Cash is a cash-at-kiosk payment channel: the customer gets a reference code after checkout, walks to any Aman agent (convenience store, pharmacy, post office, corner shop), hands over cash, and the agent logs the payment. No smartphone required. No digital wallet balance. Just cash and a reference number.

Aman Wallet is a separate product — a digital mobile wallet where customers store money, receive OTPs, and can apply for Aman installment financing. Failures with Aman Wallet (inactive wallet, insufficient balance, OTP mismatch, BNPL declined) are entirely different problems from Aman Cash failures.

Aman Financial Services (AFS), a subsidiary of Raya Holding for Financial Investments, operates both products. Raya Holding is one of Egypt's largest technology conglomerates. The Aman Cash kiosk network is the same physical agent network used for Aman Wallet top-ups, Fawry bill payments, and other services — so you may see the same shop accepting multiple payment types. What matters is that the service code and reference number are for the correct service at checkout.

7 Failure Modes Covered

  1. Aman Cash not appearing at checkout — no gateway integration
  2. Reference code not sent to customer after checkout
  3. Reference code expired before kiosk visit
  4. Kiosk rejected the reference number — wrong digits or wrong service
  5. Order stuck pending in Shopify after kiosk payment — webhook failure
  6. Amount mismatch between Shopify order and kiosk screen
  7. EGP-only limitation — foreign currency stores cannot use Aman Cash
1

Aman Cash Not Appearing at Checkout — No Gateway Integration

Merchant Issue

The most fundamental reason Aman Cash does not appear at Shopify checkout is that there is no official Aman Cash app in the Shopify App Store. Aman Financial Services does not maintain a direct Shopify integration — unlike Paymob or Tabby, there is no plug-and-play Aman Cash app to install. Merchants must set up Aman Cash as a payment channel through an Egyptian payment gateway that supports it, most commonly Paymob.

If you use Paymob as your Shopify payment gateway, you can enable Aman Cash (alongside Fawry and Masary) within the Paymob Accept dashboard under Payment Methods → Cash/Offline Channels. After enabling it and saving, the option becomes available at your Shopify checkout for Egyptian customers. Without this configuration step, Aman Cash will never appear regardless of what the customer does.

Some Egyptian merchants try to offer Aman Cash manually — posting their Aman merchant code and asking customers to pay directly at agents — but this approach has no integration with Shopify, so orders must be fulfilled and confirmed manually by the merchant. This workaround is fragile and cannot scale. The correct solution is a gateway integration that automatically generates the reference code, delivers it to the customer, and confirms the order when the kiosk payment is received.

If Aman Cash is enabled in your gateway but still not showing at Shopify checkout, check whether your Shopify payment settings are configured to display cash payment options, and verify that Aman Cash is enabled for the specific Shopify payment profile (some gateways allow different methods per product group or region).

How to Enable — Merchant

  1. Log in to your Paymob Accept dashboard (or whichever Egyptian gateway you use).
  2. Navigate to Payment Methods or Channels → look for Aman Cash, Cash Payments, or Offline Channels.
  3. Enable Aman Cash — you may also see Fawry and Masary as sibling options. Enable whichever you want to offer.
  4. In your Shopify admin, go to Settings → Payments → ensure your gateway's Shopify app is connected and configured.
  5. Do a test checkout using a test product to confirm Aman Cash appears as a payment option.
  6. If Aman Cash is not available in your current gateway plan, contact your gateway's support — some gateways tier access to cash channels by merchant plan level.
No Paymob account yet? Paymob is the most widely used payment gateway for Egyptian Shopify merchants and supports Aman Cash, Fawry, Masary, Meeza, InstaPay, Vodafone Cash, and card payments under one integration. Kashier is the main alternative. Both require merchant KYC verification (business documents, national ID) which typically takes 2-5 business days.

Reference Code Not Sent to Customer After Checkout

Config Issue

When a customer selects Aman Cash at Shopify checkout and completes the order, the payment gateway generates a unique reference number — also called a payment reference or collection code — that the customer must take to an Aman agent. This code should be delivered immediately via two channels: displayed on the Shopify order confirmation screen, and sent by SMS and/or email to the customer's contact information. If the customer completes checkout but never receives a code, they have no way to pay.

The most common cause of missing reference codes is a gateway notification configuration failure. In your Paymob dashboard (or equivalent), there are settings controlling whether payment references are sent via SMS, email, and whether the code appears on the checkout completion page. If these notification settings are disabled, or if the SMS provider used by the gateway has a delivery issue, the customer completes checkout but is left waiting for a code that will never arrive.

A second cause is that the Shopify order confirmation email template was not updated to include the cash payment reference. Shopify's default order confirmation email is not aware of payment references generated by external gateways. Merchants need to customize their email template (or use the gateway's hosted confirmation page) to display the Aman Cash reference code prominently so the customer has it in writing.

A third cause is a phone number mismatch: the customer's mobile number at checkout is different from the format the gateway's SMS provider expects, causing delivery failure. Egyptian mobile numbers must be in the correct format (11 digits starting with 01).

Fix Steps — Merchant

  1. Log in to your gateway dashboard → find the specific order → check if a reference code was generated for it.
  2. If a code exists, manually send it to the customer via email or WhatsApp immediately.
  3. In your gateway's notification settings, verify that SMS and email notifications are enabled for cash payment orders.
  4. Update your Shopify order confirmation email template to display the payment reference code — your gateway provider should have instructions for this.
  5. Test the flow end-to-end with a small test order to confirm the customer receives the reference code automatically.
  6. If SMS notifications consistently fail, contact your gateway's support to check SMS provider status for Egypt.

Fix Steps — Customer

  1. Check your email (including spam/junk) for a message from the merchant or their payment gateway with a payment reference.
  2. Check the order confirmation page — if you closed it, check your email for the confirmation link.
  3. Contact the merchant directly and ask them to retrieve your Aman Cash reference code from their gateway dashboard and send it to you.
  4. Provide the merchant with your order number, the amount, and your mobile number so they can look up the transaction.
3

Reference Code Expired Before Kiosk Visit — 24–72 Hour Window

Customer Side

Aman Cash reference codes have a time-limited validity window — typically 24 to 72 hours from the moment they are generated at checkout, depending on how the payment gateway has configured the expiry. When the window closes, the Aman kiosk system rejects the code with an "expired" or "not found" error, even if the digits are entered correctly.

This is the most common cause of the customer reporting "the kiosk won't accept my code." The customer received the reference, held onto it, went to the agent a few days later, and the code has since expired. The kiosk error is sometimes displayed as a generic "invalid reference" — indistinguishable from a wrong-number error — which causes confusion about whether the code was ever valid.

Once a reference code expires, the payment cannot be made against it. The underlying Shopify order remains in pending payment status indefinitely — it is not cancelled automatically, but it cannot be fulfilled because payment never arrived. The customer must contact the merchant to cancel the order and re-place it, generating a new reference code with a fresh validity window.

Merchants can sometimes adjust the expiry window in their gateway dashboard — extending it from 24 to 72 hours or even longer. This reduces expired-code support tickets, especially for customers who receive the reference on Friday and cannot reach an Aman agent until Sunday. Check your gateway's cash payment settings for an expiry configuration option.

Fix Steps — Customer

  1. Check when you received the Aman Cash reference code — if it was more than 48-72 hours ago, it has likely expired.
  2. Contact the merchant and explain that your reference code expired before you could pay.
  3. Ask the merchant to cancel the original order and help you place a new one — this generates a fresh reference code with a new validity window.
  4. Once you have the new code, pay at an Aman agent within 24 hours to avoid the same issue.

Fix Steps — Merchant

  1. In your gateway dashboard, find the expiry setting for cash payment references and extend it to 72 hours or longer where supported.
  2. When customers contact you about expired codes, cancel the original Shopify order and help them re-check out to generate a new reference.
  3. Add a note in your Aman Cash payment instructions: "Reference codes expire in 48 hours — please visit an Aman agent within 48 hours of placing your order."
Important: Shopify orders in "pending payment" status from expired Aman Cash codes do not expire automatically. Periodically audit your gateway dashboard for unclaimed cash payment references and cancel the corresponding Shopify orders to keep your order management clean.
4

Kiosk Rejected the Reference Number — Wrong Digits or Wrong Service

Customer Side

Even with a valid, unexpired reference code, kiosk rejection happens when the agent inputs the wrong details. Aman Cash reference numbers are typically long numeric strings — 10 to 18 digits — and a single transposed digit causes the system to return "reference not found." Unlike OTPs which are short and entered by the customer themselves, cash payment references are often typed by the agent from a handwritten or verbally dictated number, making transcription errors common.

A second and often missed cause: Aman agents handle multiple payment services on the same terminal — Aman Cash, Aman Wallet top-up, Fawry, Masary, utility bill payments, and more. Each service has a different entry flow and service code in the agent's POS system. If the agent selects the wrong service (e.g., Aman Wallet top-up instead of Aman Cash e-commerce), the reference number will not be found even if it is entered correctly, because the system is looking in the wrong service's database.

To avoid this, the customer should clearly tell the agent: "مدفوعات إلكترونية — Aman Cash — مرجع دفع" (electronic payment — Aman Cash — payment reference). If the merchant's confirmation message specifies a service name or code, share it with the agent verbatim. Some merchants receive a reference that includes a biller code prefix — a short numeric code that identifies the merchant — separate from the customer reference itself. Both must be entered in the correct fields.

If the kiosk consistently returns errors for a valid reference, the customer can try a different Aman agent location — POS software versions vary between agents, and occasionally a specific agent's terminal has not synced the latest reference database. A different location a few streets away may accept the same code without issue.

Fix Steps — Customer

  1. Show the agent your reference code in writing (SMS or email) — do not dictate it verbally to avoid transcription errors.
  2. Confirm with the agent they are in the correct service: Aman Cash e-commerce (مدفوعات الإنترنت), not Aman Wallet top-up or a bill payment service.
  3. If there is a biller code or merchant code in your reference message, provide it to the agent as a separate number — it goes in a different field from the customer reference.
  4. If one Aman agent cannot process it, try a different Aman agent location nearby — terminal software versions differ.
  5. If no agent can process the code, contact the merchant — the reference may need to be regenerated if it was corrupted at creation.
Merchant tip: In your order confirmation and payment instructions, clearly label which number is the "biller/merchant code" and which is the "customer reference." Many kiosk rejections happen because both numbers exist in the confirmation message but the customer presents them to the agent as one combined number.
5

Order Stuck Pending in Shopify After Kiosk Payment — Webhook Failure

Config Issue

The most frustrating Aman Cash failure for merchants is when the customer successfully pays at a kiosk — they have a printed receipt confirming the transaction — but the Shopify order remains in "Payment Pending" status indefinitely and is never fulfilled. This is a webhook/callback failure in the payment confirmation chain.

Here is how the confirmation chain works: (1) Customer pays cash at Aman agent. (2) Aman notifies the payment gateway (Paymob) that the cash collection was successful. (3) The gateway calls the webhook URL configured in your Shopify app settings to notify Shopify that the payment is confirmed. (4) Shopify updates the order to "Paid" and triggers fulfillment. If any step in this chain breaks — Aman's notification is delayed, the gateway's webhook call fails, the Shopify webhook URL is wrong, or Shopify returns an error — the order stays pending even though the customer's cash is collected.

The most common breakage point is step 3: the gateway webhook URL. If you recently reinstalled the Shopify payment app, changed your Shopify domain, migrated stores, or updated the integration, the webhook URL stored in the gateway dashboard may be outdated and returning 404 errors. The gateway tries to notify Shopify, fails, and the order stays pending. Check your gateway dashboard for webhook delivery logs — failed deliveries will be visible there.

A secondary cause: Shopify has a webhook timeout. If the gateway's callback call takes more than a few seconds to reach Shopify, Shopify times it out and the payment confirmation is lost. This can happen under high traffic or if the gateway's servers are slow. The gateway may retry failed webhooks — check whether retries are enabled and whether they eventually succeed.

Fix Steps — Merchant

  1. Log in to your gateway dashboard (Paymob) → find the specific transaction → confirm the payment status is "Collected" or "Success" on the gateway side.
  2. If the gateway shows payment collected, check your webhook delivery logs in the gateway dashboard for failed delivery attempts to Shopify.
  3. Verify that the webhook URL configured in your gateway matches your current Shopify store's webhook endpoint — reinstalling the Shopify app regenerates this URL.
  4. If the gateway shows payment collected and the webhook is correctly configured, manually mark the Shopify order as paid in your Shopify admin to unblock fulfillment.
  5. After manual resolution, contact gateway support to investigate why automatic webhook delivery failed.
  6. For the customer: confirm receipt of their kiosk payment receipt details, then fulfill their order manually while the technical issue is resolved.

Fix Steps — Customer

  1. Keep your kiosk receipt — it is proof of payment. Photograph it immediately.
  2. Contact the merchant with your order number, the kiosk receipt details (amount, date/time, transaction ID on receipt), and your payment reference number.
  3. The merchant can verify the payment in their gateway dashboard and manually confirm your Shopify order for fulfillment.
  4. If the merchant does not respond within 24 hours, contact the payment gateway's customer support directly and reference your transaction.
Important for merchants: Customer cash is collected the moment they pay at the kiosk. If your Shopify order stays pending indefinitely, you have received payment and owe the customer their order. Never make the customer re-pay. Always resolve the webhook issue and fulfill the original order.

Amount Mismatch Between Shopify Order and Kiosk Screen

Config Issue

When the customer arrives at the Aman agent to pay, the kiosk system displays the expected payment amount. If this amount does not match what the customer saw at Shopify checkout, they may refuse to pay (suspecting an error or fraud) or the agent may alert them to the discrepancy. This mismatch has several causes, each pointing to a different configuration problem.

The most common cause is currency conversion rounding. If your Shopify store is priced in USD and your Egyptian gateway converts to EGP at checkout for the Aman Cash reference, the EGP amount on the kiosk depends on the exchange rate at the moment the reference was created. If the rate changed between when the customer saw the checkout total and when you queried the kiosk data, the displayed amounts differ slightly. This is not an error — it is the expected behavior of live currency conversion — but customers unfamiliar with it may be confused.

A second cause is gateway service fees added at checkout. Some Egyptian payment gateways add a convenience fee to cash payment transactions (typically 1-2% or a flat fee). If this fee is not displayed clearly during the Shopify checkout flow, the kiosk amount surprises the customer. The fee is the gateway's charge for facilitating the cash collection network — it is separate from Shopify's transaction fees.

A third cause is a double-entry error: the gateway recorded the order amount differently from Shopify. This can happen if the Shopify webhook that notifies the gateway of the order amount misfired, and the gateway created a reference for an incorrect amount. Check both your Shopify order total and the gateway transaction amount to confirm they match.

Fix Steps — Merchant

  1. In your Shopify admin, check the exact total of the customer's order including any discounts, taxes, and shipping.
  2. In your gateway dashboard, check the amount recorded for the Aman Cash reference — confirm it matches the Shopify order total in EGP.
  3. If your store is in USD, verify how your gateway converts to EGP and display the EGP equivalent at checkout so customers know exactly what they will pay at the kiosk.
  4. If your gateway charges a cash payment convenience fee, display this fee line-item clearly during checkout so the kiosk amount is not a surprise.
  5. If there is a genuine mismatch error, the reference must be cancelled and recreated with the correct amount — contact your gateway support to reset the transaction.

Fix Steps — Customer

  1. Before refusing to pay at the kiosk, check if the difference is small (under 5 EGP) — this may be a rounding or exchange rate difference, not an error.
  2. If the difference is significant, do not pay and contact the merchant to clarify the amount before proceeding.
  3. Ask the merchant to confirm the expected EGP amount at the kiosk and get it in writing before your next kiosk visit.

EGP-Only Limitation — Foreign Currency Stores Cannot Use Aman Cash

Network Limit

Aman Cash exclusively processes payments in Egyptian Pounds (EGP). The entire network — from kiosk terminals to backend settlement — operates in EGP only. This creates a fundamental incompatibility with Shopify stores that are priced in USD, EUR, GBP, or any foreign currency without a conversion layer.

If your Shopify store presents prices in USD to Egyptian customers and you rely on Shopify's built-in currency conversion without an Egyptian payment gateway in the middle, Aman Cash cannot be offered at checkout. The gateway cannot generate an EGP reference code from a USD checkout if there is no EGP-to-USD conversion step built into the payment flow.

The solution for merchants who want to serve Egyptian customers with Aman Cash is to use a gateway like Paymob, which presents a separate EGP checkout layer for Egyptian customers. The customer sees prices in EGP (converted from your store currency at the current rate), selects Aman Cash, receives an EGP-denominated reference, and pays at the kiosk in EGP. The gateway then settles with you in EGP (or, for some gateway plans, converts to USD for payout). This EGP checkout layer is sometimes called a "hosted payment page" or "Egypt-localized checkout."

Merchants selling digital products or small physical goods to Egyptian customers often find that pricing a specific product in EGP (using Shopify Markets or manual price overrides for Egypt) is simpler than relying on real-time conversion at checkout — it avoids both the mismatch issues from mode 6 and the EGP conversion dependency of Aman Cash.

Fix Steps — Merchant

  1. Confirm your gateway (Paymob) is configured to present an EGP checkout for Egyptian customers — check gateway settings for localized pricing or Egypt-specific payment page.
  2. If your store is USD-priced, verify that the currency conversion step happens at the gateway level, not just in Shopify's display layer — the reference must be generated in EGP.
  3. Consider using Shopify Markets to present EGP prices specifically to Egyptian visitors — this eliminates conversion ambiguity and allows Aman Cash to work cleanly.
  4. Contact Paymob support to confirm which Aman Cash integration mode they support for your store's currency setup.

Fix Steps — Customer

  1. If Aman Cash is not appearing at a store's checkout, the store may not offer an EGP checkout option at all.
  2. Contact the merchant and ask if they offer EGP pricing or an Egyptian payment gateway option.
  3. If no EGP option is available, consider using a prepaid Visa or Mastercard that can be loaded from your Aman Wallet or at an Aman agent — this allows you to pay on foreign-currency stores without needing Aman Cash.
CBE note: Aman Cash kiosk payments do not involve foreign currency exchange and are not subject to the CBE individual forex purchase caps that apply to international card transactions. Egyptian customers with exhausted annual forex allowances can use Aman Cash (for EGP-priced stores) as an alternative to international cards without touching their forex limit.
