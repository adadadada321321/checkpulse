Payment Acceptance

Shopify Checkout Not Accepting Payment

The form appears. The customer fills it in. Payment still won't go through. Here are 10 specific causes — and exact fix steps for each.

This is different from: "No payment methods showing" (the form is blank) or "payment failed" (bank declined after submission). This page covers the in-between state: the payment form loads, but the transaction won't complete or process.

Root Causes

10 Causes — Payment Form Loads but Won't Process

1
Store still in test mode
Most common cause after launch — real cards always fail in test mode

Shopify Payments test mode is enabled by default when you first set up the payment provider. In test mode, only Shopify's official test card numbers are accepted — every real customer card will be rejected at the gateway level with a generic "unable to process payment" error.

Many merchants build their store in test mode, run successful test transactions with Shopify's test card numbers (4242 4242 4242 4242), then launch without disabling test mode. Real customers find they cannot pay at all.

How to confirm: Go to Admin → Settings → Payments → Shopify Payments → Manage. If you see a "Test mode enabled" toggle or banner, this is your issue.

Fix Steps
  1. Admin → Settings → Payments → Shopify Payments → Manage
  2. Scroll to the Test mode section
  3. Toggle test mode off
  4. Click Save
  5. Place a real $1 test order using a real card in an incognito window to confirm live payments work
  6. Immediately refund the test order after confirming
2
3D Secure redirect didn't complete
Customer authenticated with bank but payment still failed

3D Secure (3DS) authentication sends the customer to their bank's verification page. After they authenticate, the bank redirects them back to Shopify. If this return redirect breaks — due to Safari's cross-site cookie policy, a network interruption, or a misconfigured redirect URL in the gateway — the payment authorization expires and the order never completes.

The customer sees the checkout page again as if nothing happened. They may have been charged by their bank (a temporary authorization) but no Shopify order was created.

How to identify: Customer reports entering their bank OTP/authentication but returning to a blank checkout. Check Shopify Admin → Orders — no order created. If using Stripe or another gateway, check their dashboard for abandoned payment intents.

Fix Steps
  1. Ask the affected customer which browser and device they used
  2. Safari on iPhone: Safari's Intelligent Tracking Prevention (ITP) blocks cross-site cookies that some 3DS implementations rely on — switch the customer to Chrome on iPhone and retry
  3. Third-party gateway: Check the gateway's dashboard for the failed payment intent. The error code there explains why the return redirect failed
  4. If using Shopify Payments: contact Shopify Support with the customer's email and approximate order time — they can trace the failed 3DS session
  5. Long-term fix: Switch to Shopify Payments which uses Shopify's native 3DS handling and avoids third-party redirect dependencies
3
Shopify Payments on hold or restricted
Payments processing fails for all customers simultaneously

Shopify Payments can place accounts on hold for several reasons: unusual transaction volume, high chargeback ratio, products that violate Shopify's acceptable use policy, incomplete KYC (identity verification), or suspicious activity flagged by Shopify's fraud detection.

When on hold, payouts are paused and in some cases new charges are also blocked — all customers see payment failures simultaneously. This is the cause when payments were working yesterday and stopped suddenly with no changes made.

How to identify: Shopify Admin → Settings → Payments. Look for a red or yellow banner about account restrictions, holds, or required actions. Also check the email associated with your Shopify account — Shopify sends hold notifications by email.

Fix Steps
  1. Check Admin → Settings → Payments for any hold or restriction banners
  2. Check your store owner email for Shopify Payments notification emails
  3. If identity verification is pending: Admin → Settings → Payments → Shopify Payments → Complete verification — upload the requested documents
  4. If a policy violation: review Shopify's acceptable use policy, remove any flagged products, then contact Shopify Support
  5. Immediate workaround: Add PayPal as a payment option (Admin → Settings → Payments → Add payment methods → PayPal) — customers can still pay while Shopify Payments hold is resolved
  6. Contact Shopify Support via live chat (fastest resolution for holds)
4
Card type not enabled (Amex, Discover, debit)
Only specific card types fail — Visa/Mastercard work fine

Shopify Payments doesn't accept all card types by default. American Express, Discover, Diners Club, JCB, and some debit card networks require separate activation. The payment form will accept the card number format and show no error until submission — then decline with a payment processing error.

American Express acceptance requires American Express to approve your merchant account and may not be available in all countries. Discover has similar regional availability constraints.

How to identify: A specific card type always fails while others succeed. Customers report "my Amex won't work" or "my debit card is declined" while credit cards from the same bank work.

Fix Steps
  1. Admin → Settings → Payments → Shopify Payments → Manage
  2. Find the Accepted card types or Payment methods section
  3. Enable any card types that are currently disabled (Amex, Discover, etc.)
  4. For American Express: enabling it initiates an approval process with Amex — takes 2–5 business days in some regions
  5. For debit cards: most major debit networks (Visa Debit, Mastercard Debit) are enabled automatically — but regional debit networks (Interac in Canada, EFTPOS in Australia) may need separate activation
  6. If Amex isn't available in your region: offer PayPal as an alternative — PayPal accepts Amex from customer accounts even when the merchant's gateway doesn't
5
PayPal checkout loop — payment approved but order not created
Customer authorizes payment in PayPal but is returned to empty checkout

PayPal Express Checkout redirects customers to PayPal to log in and approve the payment, then returns them to Shopify to complete the order. If the return URL is misconfigured — or if PayPal's "Auto Return" setting overrides Shopify's return endpoint — the payment is authorized in PayPal but the order is never created in Shopify.

This often starts happening after: a store domain change, a PayPal account reconnect, a PayPal API update (particularly the 2024 PayPal Checkout SDK migration), or enabling Auto Return in PayPal's Business settings.

How to identify: Customer reports completing PayPal login and approving payment, but landing back on checkout with no order confirmation. Check PayPal Business dashboard — you may see completed payments with no corresponding Shopify orders.

Fix Steps
  1. Admin → Settings → Payments → PayPal → Disconnect
  2. Wait 60 seconds, then click Connect and re-authorize the PayPal account — this resets the return URL registered with PayPal
  3. In PayPal Business: Account Settings → Website Payments → Website Preferences → Auto Return → set to Off
  4. In PayPal Business: check Instant Payment Notification (IPN) — if enabled with a custom URL, this may interfere with Shopify's order confirmation webhook — disable or set to Shopify's notification URL
  5. Ensure the PayPal app in Shopify is on the latest version: Admin → Apps → PayPal Express Checkout
6
Test API keys in production (third-party gateway)
Custom gateway configured with sandbox credentials

When using a third-party payment gateway (Stripe, Braintree, Authorize.net, Square, etc.) instead of Shopify Payments, the gateway is configured with API keys. If test/sandbox API keys were entered instead of live production keys — or if live keys expired and were replaced with test keys during debugging — all production payments will fail at the gateway level.

Test API keys typically start with distinguishable prefixes: Stripe test keys start with sk_test_ instead of sk_live_. Authorizing a real card against a sandbox API returns a "card declined" or "invalid API key" error.

How to identify: Admin → Settings → Payments → third-party provider → view the configured API key. If it contains "test", "sandbox", or "dev" — or the gateway's prefix pattern indicates a test key — this is your cause.

Fix Steps
  1. Log in to your payment gateway dashboard (Stripe, Braintree, etc.)
  2. Go to Developers → API keys and copy the live publishable and secret keys
  3. Admin → Settings → Payments → third-party provider → Manage
  4. Replace the current API keys with the live production keys
  5. Save and test with a real card in incognito mode
  6. In your gateway dashboard: ensure the account is in live mode (not sandbox/test mode) — some gateways have a separate mode toggle in addition to key type
7
International cards declined — country or currency restriction
Domestic payments work; international customers always fail

Shopify Payments accepts cards issued in a configurable list of countries. By default, high-risk issuing countries may be blocked. Additionally, if your store sells in multiple currencies but the gateway isn't configured for multi-currency processing, international cards may be declined at the currency authorization step.

A separate but related issue: some international cards require dynamic currency conversion (DCC) to be enabled, or they use card networks (UnionPay, RuPay, JCB) that need separate activation.

How to identify: Customers from specific countries or regions always fail. Domestic customers with the same card type succeed. Check the gateway dashboard for decline reason codes — "card_not_supported", "do_not_honor", or "currency_not_supported" point to this issue.

Fix Steps
  1. Admin → Settings → Payments → Shopify Payments → Manage → Fraud prevention or Country restrictions
  2. Review the blocked country list and remove restrictions for legitimate markets you want to serve
  3. For multi-currency: Admin → Settings → Payments → Shopify Payments → Manage → Countries/regions — add the currencies you want to accept
  4. For UnionPay/JCB/RuPay: these may not be supported by Shopify Payments in your region — consider adding a gateway that supports these networks (Stripe, 2Checkout, Adyen) as an alternative
  5. As a short-term fix: enable PayPal — PayPal accepts most international payment methods and card types, acting as a catch-all for international customers
8
App or checkout.liquid script blocking payment submission
Payment button clicks do nothing — no error message, no redirect

Shopify Plus stores with checkout.liquid customizations, or stores with third-party checkout extension apps, can have JavaScript conflicts that prevent the payment form submission event from reaching Shopify's payment handler. The customer clicks "Pay Now" and nothing happens — no loading indicator, no error, no redirect.

Apps that add scripts to checkout (loyalty points, post-purchase upsells, review requests, custom validation) can accidentally intercept the payment submit event. This happens more often after Shopify updates to checkout extensibility — app scripts written for the old checkout UI break in the new architecture.

How to identify: Open the browser console (F12 → Console) during a checkout attempt. Look for JavaScript errors. Common indicators: "uncaught TypeError", "cannot read properties of null", or errors referencing a specific app's script filename.

Fix Steps
  1. Admin → Apps — temporarily disable all checkout-related apps one by one and test payment after each disable
  2. If Shopify Plus: review checkout.liquid for any JavaScript that attaches event listeners to the payment submit button — these can block or prevent form submission
  3. Open checkout in browser developer tools (F12) → Console tab → attempt payment → note any JavaScript errors immediately after clicking Pay
  4. Check the Network tab for any failed XHR/fetch requests during payment submission — a failed AJAX call from an app can block the payment flow
  5. If a specific app is identified: contact the app's support team with the console error — most have quick fixes for Shopify checkout extensibility compatibility
9
Only manual payment methods active (bank transfer, cash on delivery)
Customers see payment instructions instead of a card form

If Shopify Payments was disconnected or never set up, and no other online payment provider is active, the only payment options customers see are manual methods: bank transfer, money order, cash on delivery, or custom payment instructions. Most customers don't know how to complete a manual payment and abandon the checkout.

This can happen after: Shopify Payments suspension (Shopify removes the gateway from checkout), a store migration where payment settings weren't reconfigured, or a deliberate removal where the replacement gateway wasn't properly connected.

How to identify: Go through your own store's checkout as a customer. If you see "Bank transfer", "Pay by check", or any instructions instead of a card input form, you have manual-only payments.

Fix Steps
  1. Admin → Settings → Payments → check the Payment providers and Manual payment methods sections
  2. If Shopify Payments is eligible for your country: set it up under Shopify Payments → Complete setup
  3. If Shopify Payments isn't available in your country: add a supported third-party provider (Stripe, PayPal, Braintree, Square) under Third-party providers → Choose third-party provider
  4. After adding an online payment method, go through checkout as a customer to confirm the card form appears
  5. Disable manual payment methods (or restrict them to wholesale/B2B customers only) to avoid confusion for retail customers
10
Checkout session expired or customer account required
Payment step reached, but checkout can't complete due to session state

Shopify checkout sessions expire after 24 hours. If a customer starts checkout, leaves, and returns to the same checkout URL the next day, the session is expired — payments will fail because the checkout token is no longer valid. The customer sees a payment processing error even though their card is fine.

Separately: if your store is configured to require customer accounts before checkout, customers without accounts may reach the payment step but be blocked from completing payment when the account requirement check triggers on submission. This is a settings issue, not a payment gateway issue.

How to identify: For session expiry — check the checkout URL. If it contains a token that was created more than 24 hours ago, it's expired. Ask the customer to start a fresh cart. For account requirement — Admin → Settings → Checkout → Customer accounts → check if "Accounts are required" is selected.

Fix Steps
  1. For expired sessions: ask the customer to clear their cart and add products again — this creates a new checkout session with a fresh token
  2. For required accounts: Admin → Settings → Checkout → Customer accounts → change to "Accounts are optional" or "Accounts are disabled" to allow guest checkout
  3. To prevent session expiry issues: if your abandonment recovery emails contain checkout links, add a note that links expire after 24 hours and direct customers to restart checkout if the link is old
  4. For persistent cart issues: Admin → Online Store → Themes → Theme actions → Edit code → check if any theme code is persisting expired checkout tokens in localStorage

Common Questions

Shopify Checkout Payment FAQ

What's the difference between "payment not accepted" and "payment failed"?
"Payment not accepted" means the transaction never reached the bank — it was blocked at the merchant or gateway level (test mode, card type restriction, gateway misconfiguration). "Payment failed" means the transaction reached the bank and was declined there (insufficient funds, fraud flag, card restriction set by the bank). The fix is different: not-accepted failures are merchant-side configuration issues; payment-failed is usually a customer-side card issue. If your customers are seeing a "your payment could not be processed" error immediately on submission, it's almost always merchant-side. If they see a specific decline reason (declined, insufficient funds, do not honor), it's bank-side.
Why do payments work for some customers but not others?
Selective payment failures — where some customers pay successfully and others fail — usually indicate a card type restriction, country restriction, or browser/device-specific issue rather than a gateway configuration problem. If only Amex fails → card type not enabled. If only international customers fail → country or currency restriction. If only mobile customers fail → see the mobile checkout guide (Safari ITP, 3DS on iPhone). If it's seemingly random → check for checkout app JS errors that may only trigger in certain browsers. A complete gateway failure (test mode, Shopify Payments on hold) fails for everyone simultaneously.
How do I test if payments are working without charging a real customer?
The safest way: enable Shopify Payments test mode temporarily, use Shopify's test card (4242 4242 4242 4242 with any future expiry and any CVV), run a test transaction, then immediately disable test mode before going live. Alternatively: place a real order with a $0.50–$1 product using your own card in an incognito window, then immediately refund it. This confirms the full payment flow works without a real customer being charged. Never test using a customer's card or a colleague's without their explicit consent.
Can I accept payments if Shopify Payments is on hold?
Yes — you can add an alternative payment provider while Shopify Payments is on hold. Go to Admin → Settings → Payments → Add payment methods and add PayPal, Stripe, or another third-party gateway. This allows customers to continue purchasing while you resolve the Shopify Payments hold. Note that using a third-party gateway while Shopify Payments is available (even on hold) may incur additional transaction fees depending on your Shopify plan. Shopify Plus merchants have negotiated rates that may eliminate this fee — check your plan terms.
My checkout was working and suddenly stopped accepting payments — what's most likely?
Sudden checkout payment failure (was working, now broken, no changes made) has three most common causes in order of frequency: (1) Shopify Payments placed on hold — check Admin → Settings → Payments for any banners, and check your email for Shopify notifications. (2) A third-party app or theme update broke checkout JavaScript — apps auto-update silently. Check Admin → Apps for any apps updated in the last 24 hours and temporarily disable checkout-related apps. (3) A Shopify platform incident affecting payment methods — check Shopify's status page at status.shopify.com. If you're not receiving payment and cannot find the cause, use CheckPulse's free check to confirm whether the payment methods endpoint is returning correctly, which narrows down gateway vs. app vs. platform issue.
