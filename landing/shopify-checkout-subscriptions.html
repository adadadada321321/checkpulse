Subscription Checkout Errors

Shopify Subscription Checkout Not Working — ReCharge, Bold & Shopify Subscriptions Errors

Subscription checkout fails differently than regular checkout. Apps like ReCharge and Bold Subscriptions intercept the checkout flow — when that interception breaks, customers can't subscribe, and you won't know until they complain.

Root Cause

Why Subscription Checkout Breaks Differently

A standard Shopify checkout has two layers: Shopify's cart/checkout pipeline and your payment gateway. Subscription checkout adds a third layer — the subscription app — that sits between the customer's cart and the Shopify checkout. This app must intercept the "Add to Cart" or "Buy Now" action, replace or modify the checkout URL, set up a recurring billing agreement with the payment processor, and return the customer to a confirmation state.

When any part of this three-layer stack is updated — a theme update, an app update, a Shopify platform change, or a payment gateway update — the interception can break without any error appearing on Shopify's own checkout page. The checkout URL returns HTTP 200. Standard uptime monitors show green. But customers can't complete a subscription purchase.

Why standard monitoring misses subscription checkout failures

Uptime monitors check that a URL loads. Subscription checkout failures happen inside the user interaction — the app's script fails to initialize, the recurring billing setup call fails, or the redirect loop occurs after checkout is already "open." These failures require end-to-end simulation, not just HTTP status checks.

Platform Reference

Subscription Apps: Checkout Architecture & Common Failure Points

ReCharge
Largest third-party subscription platform
Uses its own hosted checkout portal (checkout.rechargeapps.com). Failures often appear as redirect loops or blank checkout pages. The Shopify–ReCharge connection can lapse after plan changes or permission updates.
Shopify Subscriptions (Native)
Built-in since 2021 — Shopify Plus & Advanced
Uses Shopify's own checkout — the most stable option. Requires Shopify Payments; cannot use third-party gateways for subscription renewal billing. Selling plan setup errors are common.
Bold Subscriptions
V2: API-based; V1: legacy (deprecated)
Bold V2 uses Shopify Checkout natively. V1 used a separate hosted checkout — many merchants still on V1 face deprecation-related failures. Verify which version you're running in your Bold dashboard.
Appstle Subscriptions
Growing mid-market option
Widget-based, injects into product pages. Theme updates frequently break the widget injection — the subscribe option disappears from product pages silently after a theme change.
Loop Subscriptions
Direct-to-consumer focus
Uses Shopify Checkout with a custom portal for management. Issues most often appear in the customer portal (subscription management), not the initial checkout.
Diagnosis

7 Subscription Checkout Failure Modes

CAUSE 1 Subscription app script removed by theme update
When you update or switch Shopify themes, the code snippets injected by your subscription app (the widget that shows "Subscribe & Save" on product pages) can be removed. The subscription option silently disappears. Customers see only the one-time purchase price.
Fix: In your subscription app admin, look for a "Reinstall widget" or "Re-inject snippet" option. For ReCharge: Admin → Integrations → Shopify → Verify widget installation. For Appstle: Admin → Embed settings → Reinstall. After a theme update, always verify the subscription widget appears on a product page.
CAUSE 2 ReCharge checkout redirect loop or blank portal
ReCharge routes customers through its hosted checkout portal (checkout.rechargeapps.com). If the Shopify–ReCharge OAuth connection lapses, or if there's a session conflict, customers get stuck in a redirect loop between your store and the ReCharge portal, or the portal loads blank.
Fix: ReCharge Admin → Settings → Integrations → Shopify → check the connection status. If it shows "Reconnect needed" or any warning, re-authorize. Also check status.rechargepayments.com for platform-level issues. Test in a clean browser window without extensions.
CAUSE 3 Shopify Subscriptions payment gateway incompatibility
Shopify's native Subscriptions API requires Shopify Payments for recurring billing. If you've recently changed your payment gateway, added a secondary gateway, or have a Shopify Payments account restriction, subscription checkout will fail at the payment step — often with a generic "payment error" message that doesn't explain the root cause.
Fix: Admin → Settings → Payments → verify Shopify Payments is active, verified, and set as the primary gateway. If your account has any pending verification, complete it — even a partially restricted account can block subscription initial charges. Non-Shopify-Payments gateways cannot process Shopify Subscriptions renewals.
CAUSE 4 Conflicting checkout app interfering with subscription flow
Checkout customization apps, post-purchase upsell apps, and order tracking apps inject scripts into the checkout. Some of these conflict with subscription apps — particularly apps that modify the cart's line_item properties, which is also how subscription apps store subscription metadata. The result: the subscription purchase is processed as a one-time order, or the checkout fails entirely.
Fix: Temporarily disable checkout-modifying apps one at a time and test subscription checkout after each. Common culprits: Zipify OCU, ReConvert, CartHook, and checkout.liquid customizations. If a subscription purchase goes through as one-time after an upsell app is active, that app is the conflict source.
CAUSE 5 Selling plan configuration error (Shopify Subscriptions)
Shopify's native Subscriptions require a "selling plan" to be correctly configured and associated with the product. Common mistakes: selling plan was created but not assigned to the product, selling plan has an incorrect billing interval, or the plan was duplicated without updating the product associations.
Fix: Admin → Products → [product] → Subscriptions → verify the selling plan is assigned and the billing frequency is correct. Also check Admin → Settings → Subscription selling plans — make sure the plan status is "Active" (not draft). If you recently duplicated a product, the subscription assignment doesn't copy automatically.
CAUSE 6 Bold Subscriptions V1 deprecation failures
Bold Subscriptions V1 used a separate hosted checkout. Bold has been deprecating V1 for several years. Stores still on V1 increasingly encounter checkout failures as the legacy infrastructure ages. These failures don't always produce clear error messages — sometimes the checkout just doesn't complete.
Fix: Log in to your Bold Subscriptions dashboard → check if you're on V1 or V2. If V1, Bold's support team can assist with migration to V2, which uses Shopify's native checkout and is significantly more stable. This migration requires careful handling of existing subscriber data.
CAUSE 7 Customer portal broken (subscription management fails)
Even if the initial subscription checkout works, a broken customer portal — where subscribers can't update payment methods, skip deliveries, or cancel — causes a surge in support tickets and forced chargebacks. Portal failures often stem from theme changes that break the customer account portal integration.
Fix: Log in to your store as a test subscriber (create a test subscription → log in as that customer). Navigate to the customer portal and verify all management actions work: update payment method, skip order, cancel. If the portal link doesn't appear in the customer account page, your subscription app's customer portal integration needs to be reinstalled after the theme update.

Testing Protocol

Subscription Checkout Test Checklist

Run this after any theme update, app update, payment gateway change, or Shopify plan change.

  • Subscribe option visible on product page with correct pricing and frequency options
  • Subscribe & Save discount applied correctly at checkout (not showing one-time price)
  • Checkout completes with a real payment method (not just test cards)
  • Order confirmation email received by customer — includes subscription details
  • Subscription appears in subscription app admin as "Active"
  • Next billing date is set correctly (not blank or past date)
  • Customer portal accessible from account page — shows active subscription
  • Customer can update payment method in portal without error
  • Subscription option available in all target markets if using Shopify Markets
  • Mobile checkout (iOS Safari): subscribe option visible and checkout completes

FAQ

Subscription Checkout Questions

Why is my Shopify subscription checkout not working?
The most common causes are: subscription app script removed by a theme update (subscribe option disappears), ReCharge OAuth connection lapsed (redirect loop), Shopify Payments restricted (payment error for native subscriptions), or a conflicting app overwriting cart data. Start by checking your subscription app's admin for any connection warnings, then test on a clean browser window.
Why does ReCharge checkout redirect loop or fail to load?
ReCharge redirect loops usually mean the Shopify–ReCharge OAuth connection has lapsed (go to ReCharge Admin → Settings → Integrations → Shopify and re-authorize) or your ReCharge account is suspended. Also check status.rechargepayments.com for platform outages and test in a clean incognito window to rule out browser extension interference.
Why can't customers purchase a subscription — they only see one-time purchase?
The subscription widget is missing from the product page. This happens after theme updates (the app snippet was removed), when the product wasn't configured as a subscription product in the app, or when the app's widget script has a JavaScript error. Check the browser console (F12) on the product page for errors from your subscription app's domain.
Shopify Subscriptions (native) checkout gives a payment error — why?
Shopify's native Subscriptions API requires Shopify Payments — it can't use third-party gateways for recurring billing. Check Admin → Settings → Payments → Shopify Payments for any verification warnings. If your Shopify Payments account is pending verification or has a restriction, subscription charges will fail even if regular purchases work.
How do I test Shopify subscription checkout?
Use a real payment method (not just test cards) — create a $1/month test subscription and verify it creates an active subscription in your app admin with the correct next billing date. Test the full flow: product page widget → checkout → confirmation email → customer portal. After any theme or app update, rerun this test before assuming it still works.
