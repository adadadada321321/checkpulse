Shopify Google Pay Not Working — Button Not Showing at Checkout (2026)

Google Pay button not appearing at checkout, missing for some customers but not others, or disappeared after working before — here are all 8 failure modes for Google Pay breaking on Shopify, with exact diagnostic steps and fixes for each.

Updated June 2026 8 failure modes Shopify Payments · Wrong Browser · iOS Restriction · Empty Wallet · Unsupported Country · Payments Suspended · Extension Conflict · Card Not Eligible

Google Pay at Shopify checkout uses the browser's Payment Request API — the same W3C standard that powers Apple Pay on Safari. Before the button renders, Shopify calls canMakePayment() and the browser responds with either true or false. If the answer is false for any reason — wrong browser, no card saved, Shopify Payments inactive, or a platform restriction — the button silently doesn't appear. There is no error message in the checkout, no alert in the admin, and no notification to you as the merchant.

Google Pay has a secondary complexity that Apple Pay does not: platform availability is asymmetric. Apple controls which browsers can show Apple Pay (Safari only, but universally on Apple devices). Google controls Google Pay for Android and Chrome, but Apple's platform restrictions on iOS prevent Google Pay from appearing on any iPhone or iPad regardless of browser. This means Google Pay is fundamentally a desktop-Chrome and Android-Chrome button, not a universal wallet like buyers might expect.

Quick Check: Is Shopify Payments Active?
Before diagnosing anything else, verify Shopify Payments is active: go to Admin → Settings → Payments. Shopify Payments must show as "Active" — not "Suspended", "Pending review", or absent. Google Pay is exclusive to Shopify Payments. If Shopify Payments is not your primary gateway, Google Pay will never appear regardless of any other setting. This accounts for the majority of "Google Pay not working" reports.

Failure Mode 1: Shopify Payments Is Not Active as the Primary Gateway

Google Pay on Shopify is not a standalone integration — it is a feature of Shopify Payments. Shopify uses its own checkout JavaScript to call the Payment Request API and identify the merchant's payment processor. If the processor is not Shopify Payments, the express checkout section (which hosts Google Pay, Apple Pay, and Shop Pay buttons) either doesn't appear or only shows Shop Pay (which has its own separate flow).

This is the most commonly missed fact about Google Pay on Shopify: merchants who use Stripe, PayPal, Authorize.net, Braintree, or any other third-party gateway as their primary payment provider will not see Google Pay in their express checkout section. The gateway relationship is exclusive — you cannot mix-and-match Stripe as your primary gateway and still get Google Pay from Shopify's infrastructure.

Why third-party gateways don't enable Google Pay

  • Shopify's express checkout buttons are rendered by Shopify's checkout JavaScript and tied to Shopify's merchant identifier in the Payment Request API
  • The merchant identifier must match an active Shopify Payments account in a supported country — a third-party gateway has no Shopify merchant ID in this system
  • Third-party gateways can implement Google Pay independently through their own apps, SDKs, or checkout flows, but these appear differently and are outside Shopify's native express checkout section
  • Shopify's "Additional payment methods" section (which is where third-party gateways live) does not support Google Pay natively

How to activate Shopify Payments

  1. Go to Shopify Admin → Settings → Payments
  2. If a third-party gateway is active, you will see an option to switch to or activate Shopify Payments
  3. Shopify Payments is available in: United States, Canada, United Kingdom, Australia, Ireland, New Zealand, Singapore, Hong Kong, Austria, Belgium, Czech Republic, Denmark, Finland, France, Germany, Italy, Japan, Netherlands, New Zealand, Portugal, Romania, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland, and others — check Shopify's current country list as availability expands
  4. Once Shopify Payments is active, run a test checkout in Chrome (desktop or Android) with a Google account that has a card saved in Google Pay — the button should appear in the express section above the contact form
Switching Gateways Has Revenue Implications
Switching from a third-party gateway to Shopify Payments requires re-entering your banking details and re-verifying your identity. Payout schedules may change. If you use Stripe directly for subscriptions or recurring billing outside of Shopify, those will need to be migrated. Do not switch payment gateways without planning for continuity of existing subscription customers and recurring charges.

Failure Mode 2: Wrong Browser — Google Pay Only Works in Chrome and Select Chromium Browsers

Google Pay uses the Payment Request API, which is implemented differently across browser engines. Google Pay specifically requires a Chromium-based browser with Google Pay enabled as a payment method. This means Safari, Firefox, and most non-Chromium browsers cannot display the Google Pay button at Shopify checkout — not because of a Shopify limitation, but because those browsers do not expose Google Pay as an available payment method through the API.

Most Shopify Merchants Test in the Wrong Browser
The most common Google Pay testing mistake: testing on a Mac in Chrome expecting to see Google Pay, then checking only with a Google account that has no cards saved. Or testing on an iPhone at all. Always test Google Pay in Chrome on Windows/macOS/Android with a Google account that has an active card at pay.google.com. If you're testing with a colleague's iPhone, you will never see Google Pay regardless of settings.

Failure Mode 3: iOS Platform Restriction — Google Pay Cannot Appear on Any iPhone or iPad

This is the most misunderstood aspect of Google Pay on Shopify. On iPhones and iPads, Apple requires all browsers — Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Brave, Opera, and any other iOS browser — to use Apple's WebKit rendering engine. As part of this restriction, Apple allows only Safari to access the Payment Request API with Apple Pay. All other payment methods via the Payment Request API, including Google Pay, are blocked at the platform level on iOS.

The practical result: on any iPhone or any iPad, no browser can show the Google Pay button at Shopify checkout. A customer using Chrome on their iPhone sees the same thing as a customer using Safari on their iPhone — Apple Pay if they have Apple Wallet cards set up, nothing if they don't, and Google Pay never under any circumstances.

What this means for your customer base

  • If your customers are primarily on iPhone (common for US, UK, Australian, Canadian, Japanese merchants where iPhone market share is 50–60%+), Google Pay will naturally have low adoption — not because it's misconfigured, but because those customers are physically on a platform that cannot show it
  • Google Pay is primarily an Android and desktop button — merchants selling to markets with high Android share (South America, parts of Europe, Southeast Asia) will see more Google Pay impressions
  • You cannot override this — no Shopify setting, no app, no code change can make Google Pay appear on iOS. It is an Apple platform policy, not a Shopify or Google limitation
  • Recommendation: ensure Apple Pay is working for iOS customers (it's the natural alternative for the same one-tap checkout experience on Apple devices) and rely on Google Pay for Android/desktop coverage
Google Pay (GPay UPI) in India vs Google Pay on Shopify
The "Google Pay" or "GPay" app widely used in India for UPI payments is a completely different product from Google Pay via the web Payment Request API. GPay India processes UPI transfers between Indian bank accounts — it does not integrate with Shopify's express checkout section. Shopify's Google Pay button processes card payments tokenized through Google's network, which requires Shopify Payments to be active in a supported country. India is not currently in Shopify Payments' supported countries, so the international Google Pay button is not available for India-based merchants through Shopify's native integration.

Failure Mode 4: No Card Saved in Google Pay or Not Signed Into Google Account in Chrome

Even when all platform and gateway conditions are met, Google Pay's canMakePayment() call returns false if the customer does not have a card saved in their Google Pay wallet or is not signed into their Google account in Chrome. This is a customer-side condition, not something you as a merchant can control — and it means Google Pay may appear for some customers (those with cards set up) but not others (those who haven't set up Google Pay on their device).

Wallet requirements by platform

  • Chrome on Android: The customer must have the Google Pay app installed and at least one card added to it. Opening the Google Pay app → tap "Add card" to set up. Google Pay on Android also requires Google Play Services to be active (see Failure Mode 5).
  • Chrome on desktop (Windows or macOS): The customer must be signed into their Google account in Chrome and have at least one card saved at pay.google.com. The card does not need to be on their phone — desktop Google Pay draws from the cards saved to the Google account, not a physical device wallet.
  • Samsung Internet on Android: Similar to Chrome on Android; customer must have Google Pay set up. Samsung Internet can also use Samsung Pay cards, which may expand eligibility beyond pure Google Pay users.
How to Test If Your Customer's Wallet Is the Issue
To verify Google Pay is working from your side and the missing button is customer-wallet specific: sign into Chrome on your own computer with a Google account that has a card saved at pay.google.com. Visit your store's checkout page. If the Google Pay button appears for you, Shopify Payments and your store's configuration are correct — customers not seeing it simply haven't set up Google Pay in their account. Nothing needs to be fixed on your end.

Failure Mode 5: Google Play Services Not Active on Android

On Android devices, Google Pay requires Google Play Services to be running and up to date. Google Play Services is the background service Google uses to manage device identity, security, and payment token provisioning. If Google Play Services is disabled, outdated, or the device is a heavily modified Android variant without Google services (common in China and on some budget devices sold in markets where Google services are restricted), Google Pay will not work.

Devices where Google Pay fails due to Play Services issues

  • Rooted Android devices: Google Pay typically refuses to work on devices where SafetyNet (or its successor, Play Integrity API) fails. Google considers rooted devices insecure for payment tokenization. Buyers on rooted phones cannot use Google Pay at all.
  • Huawei devices without Google services: Huawei devices sold after mid-2019 (following US trade restrictions) do not include Google Play Services on new models sold in markets outside Europe. These devices cannot use Google Pay via Payment Request API.
  • Budget Android devices with outdated Play Services: Very old versions of Google Play Services may not support the payment token APIs that Google Pay requires. The device will receive a Play Services update prompt, but customers who dismiss it will not be able to use Google Pay.
  • Android tablets with limited Google integration: Some Android tablets, particularly those sold without Google services certification, cannot support Google Pay regardless of the Chrome browser version installed.

What to do if Google Play Services is the suspected cause

  1. Ask the customer to open the Google Play Store app → tap their profile icon → "Manage apps & device" → "Updates available" → check if Google Play Services has a pending update
  2. The customer can also go to Settings → Apps → Google Play Services → check the version number and compare with the latest available
  3. If on a Huawei device without Google services, Google Pay is not available on that device regardless of fixes — suggest the customer use an alternative payment method
  4. If SafetyNet or Play Integrity is failing (rooted device), Google Pay will not work by design — this is a security feature, not a bug

Failure Mode 6: Store Country Not Supported for Google Pay via Shopify Payments

Shopify Payments is available in more countries than Google Pay via Shopify Payments. Even if you have Shopify Payments active, your store's billing country must be on Google's list of supported merchant countries for Google Pay. If your country is supported by Shopify Payments but not yet by Google Pay (or vice versa), the Google Pay button will not appear.

Additionally, even if your store's country is supported, your customers' bank-issued cards may not be supported for Google Pay in their country. Google Pay requires banks to participate in its tokenization program — not all banks in all countries have enrolled. A customer with a card from an unsupported bank will not see the Google Pay button even if all other conditions are met.

How to verify country support

  1. Confirm your store's billing country (Admin → Settings → General → Store currency and billing country)
  2. Cross-reference against Shopify's current documentation on which countries support Google Pay via Shopify Payments — the list is updated periodically as Google expands its network
  3. If your store country is supported, test with a card from a major international bank (Visa or Mastercard issued by a large bank) to rule out card-specific eligibility issues
  4. If you are in a supported country but a specific customer reports Google Pay not appearing, the most likely cause is their card's bank not participating in Google Pay tokenization in their country
Google Pay Supported Countries (via Shopify Payments)
Google Pay via Shopify Payments is supported in the United States, Canada, United Kingdom, Australia, Ireland, New Zealand, Singapore, Hong Kong, and most Western European countries where Shopify Payments is active. Support is expanding — check Shopify's official help documentation for the current list. If your country is not on the list, there is no workaround through Shopify's native integration.

Failure Mode 7: Shopify Payments Suspended or Under Account Review

When Shopify Payments is suspended — either temporarily for document verification, for a fraud review, because of elevated dispute rates, or for payout account issues — all Shopify Payments features are paused simultaneously. This includes not just transaction processing but also the display of all express checkout buttons: Google Pay, Apple Pay, and Shop Pay all disappear from the checkout during a suspension.

Critically, this disappearance is not announced to customers. They simply see no express checkout section. Merchants may not notice immediately because the suspension notification goes to the Shopify admin email, which is easy to miss. If Google Pay (and Apple Pay and Shop Pay) all disappeared at the same time across all devices, Shopify Payments suspension is almost certainly the cause.

How to diagnose a Shopify Payments suspension

  1. Go to Shopify Admin → Settings → Payments → check the status banner for Shopify Payments
  2. Check your Shopify admin email (the address in Settings → General) for any recent emails from Shopify about payment account reviews, required documents, or payout holds
  3. Go to Shopify Admin → Finances → Payouts — if payouts are paused or show a "under review" message, Shopify Payments is restricted
  4. If a suspension is confirmed, follow Shopify's instructions in the email or in the admin banner — typically you need to submit identity documents or respond to a compliance request
  5. Once the suspension is resolved and Shopify Payments returns to "Active" status, all express buttons will reappear automatically without any additional configuration
All Three Express Buttons Disappear Together During Suspension
If Google Pay, Apple Pay, and Shop Pay all disappeared at the same time, this is almost certainly a Shopify Payments suspension. Individual button failures (just Google Pay missing) are caused by browser or wallet conditions. Simultaneous disappearance of all express buttons is a payment processor status issue. Do not attempt to fix individual button settings until you have confirmed Shopify Payments is active.

Failure Mode 8: Checkout Extension or UI Customization Hiding the Express Section

Shopify's checkout extensibility framework allows apps to add, modify, or hide sections of the checkout. If a checkout UI extension is incorrectly targeting the express payment section — or if a checkout customization has hidden or removed the express payment container — Google Pay, Apple Pay, and Shop Pay may all stop appearing even though Shopify Payments is active and the customer's device is compatible.

This failure mode is more common after app updates, theme updates on Shopify Plus custom checkouts, or after publishing a new checkout customization. If the timing of the Google Pay disappearance coincides with an app update or a checkout customization change, this is the likely cause.

How to isolate a checkout extension conflict

  1. Go to Shopify Admin → Settings → Checkout → Customize (for Online Store 2.0 with checkout extensibility)
  2. In the checkout editor, look for any sections that may be hiding or overriding the express payment section — check "Express checkout" in the left panel
  3. Go to Shopify Admin → Apps → look for any apps that interact with checkout customization (upsell apps, loyalty apps, post-purchase apps, checkout UI extension apps)
  4. Temporarily disable checkout-modifying apps one at a time and test after each disable — when Google Pay reappears, the last disabled app is the conflict source
  5. If using Shopify Plus with custom checkout.liquid, check for any JavaScript that modifies or removes the express payment container
  6. Go to Admin → Settings → Checkout → Checkout Functions — review any active functions to see if they're modifying payment method display
App Updates Can Break Express Checkout Without Warning
Checkout apps update frequently and independently. An update to a checkout upsell app or loyalty program app last week may have introduced a bug that hides or conflicts with the express payment section. Merchants rarely receive notifications when third-party app updates break native Shopify features. Subscribe to changelog notifications for all checkout-critical apps and monitor your checkout after any app updates.

Google Pay Configuration Best Practices for Shopify Merchants

Because Google Pay is largely a pass-through feature of Shopify Payments (there is no manual configuration required beyond activating Shopify Payments), the main practices are around testing, monitoring, and setting correct expectations for your customer support team.

  • Test across the correct device matrix: Test Google Pay in Chrome on Android and Chrome on desktop Windows/macOS. Do not test on iPhone or in Safari. If you see Google Pay in those tests, your setup is correct for all eligible customers.
  • Configure Apple Pay in parallel: Because iOS customers (who may be 50%+ of your traffic) cannot use Google Pay, ensure Apple Pay is working in Safari on iPhone. Apple Pay and Google Pay together cover the one-tap checkout experience across both major mobile platforms.
  • Set customer support expectations: When customers report "Google Pay not working", the most common explanation is they're on iPhone (where it can never appear) or they haven't set up Google Pay in their Google account. Train your support team on the browser and device requirements so they can resolve these efficiently without escalating as a technical issue.
  • Monitor Shopify Payments status: Subscribe to Shopify status updates and monitor your Shopify admin emails so you catch any Shopify Payments suspension quickly. Every hour of suspension means Google Pay (and all express buttons) are down for all customers.
  • Check after any checkout app update: Verify express checkout buttons are still working in Chrome after updating any checkout customization app. Checkout app updates are the primary cause of silent Google Pay regressions.
Monitor Google Pay Availability Proactively
Google Pay failures — whether from Shopify Payments suspension, browser configuration changes, or app conflicts — happen silently. There is no merchant-facing alert when the button stops appearing. Customers on Android and desktop Chrome who expect one-tap checkout see nothing and may abandon. A checkout monitoring tool that tests your full express checkout flow from eligible browser/device combinations can catch these failures before they affect revenue.
