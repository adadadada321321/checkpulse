Shopify Shahry Not Working — Not Showing, Declined, National ID & Credit Issues

Updated June 2026 · 7 failure modes · Egypt merchants (EGP) · VC-backed BNPL

Shahry is a VC-backed Egyptian BNPL fintech that uses machine learning credit scoring to serve Egyptian customers with installment plans of 3 to 12 months — entirely in Egyptian Pounds. Unlike valU's institutional EFG Hermes model, Shahry's tech-native approach is designed to approve customers with thin credit files who lack formal bank history, making it particularly valuable for merchants targeting Egypt's large underbanked consumer segment. Its Shopify integration fails in seven distinct ways tied to its EGP-only currency gate, ML credit model, and Egyptian National ID requirements. This guide covers every failure mode with exact diagnostic steps.

How Shahry Differs From valU and Tabby on Shopify in Egypt

Shahry is Egypt-exclusive — it operates only in Egyptian Pounds (EGP) and only for Egyptian customers. Its closest Egypt-only competitor is valU. The key structural difference: Shahry is a VC-backed fintech (Algebra Ventures, 4DX Ventures, Beltone Financial) using a proprietary machine learning credit model, while valU is backed by EFG Hermes, one of Egypt's largest financial institutions, with a more formal institutional underwriting process. This makes their failure modes diverge even though both require Egyptian National ID and EGP currency.

Shahry's ML scoring targets underbanked Egyptians. Egypt has a large population without formal credit history, bank accounts, or credit cards. Shahry's ML model is designed to approve customers who would fail a traditional credit check — using alternative signals like mobile wallet activity, purchase history, and behavioral data rather than relying on I-Score (Egypt's credit bureau). This means Shahry can approve customers that valU's more conservative institutional model might decline. However, first-time Shahry users still receive conservative initial credit limits that increase over time with repayment history.

Shahry offers installment plans of 3, 6, 9, and 12 months — shorter than valU's 36-month maximum but longer than Tabby's typical Pay-in-4 monthly structure. Shahry's 0% installment plans (for qualifying merchants and plans) make it competitive with Tabby for Egypt. Unlike valU's longer-term plans, which carry financing fees, Shahry's plans are typically 0% for merchant-subsidized short terms. Shahry's minimum order (~200 EGP) is also lower than valU's (~500 EGP), making it viable for smaller-basket merchants in fashion, beauty, and lifestyle.

Shahry does not show customers their credit limit — unlike valU, which displays an available credit limit in the app. This means when a Shahry customer is declined, they cannot self-diagnose whether it is a credit limit issue or a scoring issue. Merchants should direct declined Shahry customers to Shahry's customer support rather than troubleshooting independently.

Shahry Not Showing at Checkout

Merchant-Side

Shahry not appearing at checkout is the most common issue Egypt merchants report. Unlike payment failures that surface during the transaction, this failure is invisible — the customer simply never sees Shahry as an option, causing silent drop-off for customers expecting to pay in installments.

The primary cause is a missed activation step. Merchants install the Shahry app from the Shopify App Store — which adds the integration — but never complete the mandatory second step: enabling Shahry as an alternative payment provider inside Shopify's payments settings. Installing the app alone is not sufficient. This two-step activation requirement is the same for every BNPL provider on Shopify (Tabby, valU, Postpay, Tamara, Cashew) and is consistently the most skipped step during merchant onboarding.

The second most common cause is currency. Shahry is EGP-only — it will not render at checkout if the cart currency is USD, EUR, AED, SAR, or any currency other than Egyptian Pounds. Shopify stores that serve multiple regions and display prices in the customer's local currency need to verify that Egyptian customers see EGP at checkout, not a converted amount in another currency.

Shahry is also Egypt-shipping-only. It will not appear for customers shipping to UAE, Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, or any non-Egypt address — even if the customer is Egyptian and browsing from Egypt. The shipping address country at checkout determines whether Shahry renders.

How to Fix

  1. Go to Shopify Admin → Settings → Payments
  2. Scroll to Alternative payment providers → Confirm Shahry is listed and set to Active (not just installed)
  3. Verify your store currency for Egyptian customers is EGP (Egyptian Pound) — check under Settings → General → Store currency and Shopify Markets for Egypt
  4. In your Shahry merchant dashboard, confirm your account status is Live / Approved — not Pending or Test mode
  5. Check that Shahry's app is using live production credentials, not sandbox/test API keys
  6. Test checkout with a shipping address in Egypt — Shahry will not appear for non-Egypt shipping addresses
Note: Shahry's Shopify app may have a separate configuration step inside the app itself (not just the Shopify payments toggle). Check the Shahry merchant portal for any "complete setup" prompts after installation.
2

Credit Assessment Declined — ML Scoring Rejection

Customer-Side

When Shahry is visible at checkout but the customer's application is declined, the cause is Shahry's machine learning credit assessment. Unlike valU's institutional EFG Hermes underwriting or Tabby's per-transaction risk model, Shahry uses a proprietary ML system that evaluates customers based on a combination of identity verification signals, behavioral patterns, repayment history within Shahry, and alternative data sources — without necessarily requiring formal bank account or I-Score credit bureau history.

First-time Shahry users are the most commonly declined segment. Shahry's ML model starts new customers at a conservative credit limit, which increases with successful repayment history. A customer with no Shahry history attempting a high-value first purchase is likely to be declined or offered a smaller installment plan than requested.

Customers who were recently declined and re-apply too quickly face an automatic temporary block. Shahry enforces a waiting period between applications (typically 30 days) before re-evaluating a declined customer. Advising customers to re-apply the same day they were declined will result in another immediate decline regardless of what they change.

Unlike valU — which shows customers their available credit limit in the app — Shahry does not display this information. Customers cannot determine themselves whether they were declined due to an insufficient limit or a scoring issue. Direct declined customers to Shahry's in-app support or their official support channels to understand the specific reason.

What to Tell Your Customer

  1. First-time Shahry user: Start with a smaller cart value — Shahry assigns conservative initial limits that increase with repayment history
  2. Recent decline: Wait at least 30 days before re-applying — re-applying immediately will result in another automatic decline
  3. Existing Shahry user: Check that no previous Shahry installment payments are overdue — late payments reduce available credit
  4. Open the Shahry app → check repayment status for any existing open plans that may be consuming available credit
  5. Contact Shahry customer support through the app for the specific decline reason — only Shahry can provide the exact scoring explanation
Merchant note: Shahry credit declines are customer-side decisions by Shahry's credit model — merchants cannot override or appeal them. If Shahry declines a customer, offer Tabby or Postpay as alternatives that serve Egypt. Both have different risk models and may approve customers Shahry declined.

Credit Limit Insufficient for Cart Value

Customer-Side

Even a long-standing Shahry customer with a good repayment history may be declined at checkout if their available credit limit is insufficient for the cart value. This differs from a scoring decline — the customer is approved in principle, but their remaining credit after existing open installment plans is below the cart total.

Unlike valU, which shows customers their available credit limit in the app, Shahry does not display this figure prominently. A customer may not know their effective available limit before attempting checkout. When Shahry is installed at a merchant's store, there is no mechanism for customers to check their limit before adding items to cart.

A customer with multiple active Shahry plans — for purchases at different merchants — has a total credit limit split across all of them. The more open plans, the less remaining credit for a new purchase. This is especially relevant for returning customers who use Shahry frequently across multiple merchants.

How to Address This

  1. Tell the customer to open the Shahry app and review all active open installment plans
  2. Suggest completing payments on existing plans to free up credit before the next purchase
  3. Suggest trying a smaller cart value — Shahry may approve a lower amount even when declining the full cart
  4. Offer Tabby or Postpay as alternative Egypt BNPL options — each has its own independent credit line
  5. Contact Shahry support to confirm current available credit limit
Key difference from valU: valU customers can see their available credit limit in the valU app before checkout. Shahry customers cannot — making a "credit limit insufficient" decline more confusing and harder to self-diagnose without contacting Shahry support.

Egyptian National ID Verification Failure

Customer-Side

Shahry requires a valid Egyptian National ID (رقم القومي) for identity verification. This is an absolute requirement — no alternative document type (passport, residence permit, driving license) is accepted. Customers who do not hold an Egyptian National ID cannot use Shahry, regardless of their nationality or residence status in Egypt.

Shahry's minimum age requirement is 21 years old. Customers under 21 — even with a valid Egyptian National ID — are ineligible for Shahry's BNPL product. This is a stricter age requirement than many BNPL providers, which typically require only 18+. Merchants in categories popular with younger Egyptian consumers (fashion, gaming, electronics accessories) will see a meaningful share of Shahry declines attributable to the 21+ age threshold.

Shahry cross-references the National ID number against Egypt's civil registration database (operated by the Ministry of Interior). Recently issued or newly renewed Egyptian National IDs may not yet appear in this database — there is typically a propagation lag of 7 to 14 days for new or renewed IDs to be queryable. Customers with recently renewed IDs who see verification failures should wait and retry after 1–2 weeks.

A name mismatch between the name provided at checkout and the name on the National ID will also cause verification failure. Egyptian names with alternative transliterations (Mohamed vs. Mohammed, Ahmedein vs. Ahmad, etc.) must exactly match the official National ID transliteration used during Shahry registration.

How to Diagnose

  1. Confirm the customer is 21 years of age or older — under-21 customers are ineligible for Shahry regardless of other criteria
  2. Confirm the customer holds a valid Egyptian National ID (not a passport, residence permit, or other document)
  3. Ask if the ID was recently issued or renewed — if within the past 2 weeks, advise waiting 7–14 days for civil registry propagation
  4. Verify the name on the ID matches exactly what the customer registered with in the Shahry app — including transliteration
  5. Direct the customer to Shahry's in-app identity verification flow to re-submit their ID with a clear photo
Age threshold note: Shahry's 21+ minimum is stricter than Tabby's 18+ and Postpay's 18+ requirements. For Egyptian merchants serving college students or customers aged 18–20, Tabby is the appropriate BNPL alternative — Shahry is not an option for this demographic.
5

Phone Number Verification / OTP Failure

Customer-Side

Shahry sends a one-time password (OTP) to the customer's registered Egyptian mobile number (+20) to authenticate each checkout session. If the OTP is not received, the transaction cannot be completed. This failure is distinct from identity verification — it affects customers who are already fully verified with Shahry but cannot receive the OTP at the moment of checkout.

Shahry exclusively uses Egyptian mobile numbers — numbers beginning with Egypt's country code (+20). International SIM cards (UAE +971, Saudi +966, UK +44, US +1) will not receive Shahry OTPs regardless of whether the customer is physically present in Egypt. This affects Egyptian expats and frequent travelers who may carry a non-Egyptian SIM while shopping at Egyptian Shopify merchants.

Egyptian law requires all SIM cards to be registered with the cardholder's National ID. An unregistered or improperly registered SIM may cause OTP delivery issues. Egypt's telecom operators (Vodafone Egypt, Orange Egypt, Etisalat Egypt/e&, WE) maintain registration records. If a customer recently changed numbers or ported their number between operators, there may be a brief period of OTP instability.

Network delivery delays — especially during high-traffic periods, late at night, or in areas with limited coverage — can cause OTP delays. Shahry's OTP has an expiry window. If the customer waits too long before entering the OTP, it expires and must be re-requested.

How to Fix OTP Issues

  1. Confirm the customer is using an Egyptian mobile number (+20) — non-Egyptian SIMs will not receive Shahry OTPs
  2. Verify the phone number on the customer's Shahry account is current and correct — numbers are updated in the Shahry app under account settings
  3. Request a new OTP — the previous one may have expired
  4. Check SMS spam / blocked messages folders on the customer's phone
  5. If the customer recently changed SIM or ported their number, wait 24–48 hours for operator record propagation
  6. If issues persist, contact Shahry support to update the registered mobile number

Order Below Shahry's Minimum Value (~200 EGP)

Merchant & Customer

Shahry enforces a minimum order value for BNPL eligibility. The current minimum is approximately 200 EGP — lower than valU's minimum of approximately 500 EGP, and lower than Postpay's minimum. This makes Shahry accessible for smaller purchases in categories like fashion accessories, beauty products, and lifestyle goods.

Even though Shahry's minimum is relatively low for Egypt's BNPL market, merchants in very low-AOV categories (under 200 EGP average order value) will see Shahry unavailable for a significant portion of orders. In these cases, Shahry will either not appear at checkout or will display as unavailable for the specific cart value.

The minimum may also be affected by merchant-specific agreements. Shahry occasionally applies different minimum thresholds for specific merchant partnerships or promotional campaigns. Merchants should verify their specific minimum in the Shahry merchant portal, as the 200 EGP figure represents the general market baseline.

How to Handle This

  1. Check the Shahry merchant dashboard to confirm your specific minimum order threshold
  2. If your AOV is below the minimum, bundle products or set a minimum cart value for Shahry eligibility in your store
  3. Display Shahry's minimum in your product pages and cart to set customer expectations before checkout
  4. For low-value orders, consider Tabby as an alternative — Tabby's minimum for Egypt may differ
Shahry vs valU minimum comparison: Shahry (~200 EGP) has a significantly lower minimum than valU (~500 EGP). For Egypt merchants selling mid-range products in the 200–500 EGP range, Shahry may serve customers that valU cannot. Offering both broadens the eligible installment basket range.

Merchant Onboarding Not Complete or Pending Approval

Merchant-Side

Even when the Shahry app is correctly installed and enabled as an alternative payment provider in Shopify, it will not appear at checkout if the merchant's Shahry account has not been fully approved. Shahry conducts a merchant onboarding review before activating a merchant's integration — this is not just an API connection but a business verification process.

Shahry's merchant onboarding requires: Egyptian commercial registration (سجل تجاري), Egyptian Tax Card (بطاقة ضريبية), bank account verification (Egyptian bank preferred), and business category review. Shahry reviews the merchant's business type — some categories may be restricted or require additional documentation. This process typically takes 3 to 10 business days but can extend longer for new applicants or for businesses in categories requiring additional review.

Shahry's Shopify integration will often show as "installed" in the Shopify App Store before the merchant's back-end account is approved. Merchants frequently assume installation equals activation. The Shahry merchant portal will show the account as "Pending" or "Under Review" during this period — Shahry does not always proactively notify merchants of the pending status via email.

Shahry occasionally places existing approved merchants on temporary hold for periodic compliance reviews, especially if the merchant's business registration or tax card has expired. Merchants should periodically verify their Shahry merchant account status even if onboarding was previously completed.

How to Resolve Onboarding Issues

  1. Log into the Shahry merchant portal and check the account status under Settings or Dashboard
  2. If status is Pending Review, wait for Shahry's approval notification (3–10 business days) or contact Shahry merchant support to expedite
  3. Ensure your Egyptian commercial registration and Tax Card are current and uploaded to the Shahry merchant portal
  4. Verify your business category — Shahry may restrict certain merchant types or require additional documentation
  5. If you were previously approved, check whether your commercial registration has expired — expiry can trigger a compliance hold
  6. Contact Shahry's merchant onboarding team directly for status updates — do not rely solely on email notifications
Common confusion: Installing the Shahry Shopify app and enabling it in Shopify's payment settings does NOT activate Shahry for your customers. Your Shahry merchant account must also be in Live / Approved status in the Shahry merchant portal. Both must be true simultaneously for Shahry to appear at checkout.

Frequently Asked

Common Questions About Shahry on Shopify

Can I use Shahry and valU together on the same Shopify store?

Yes — Shopify allows multiple alternative payment providers to be active simultaneously. Merchants can offer both Shahry and valU for Egyptian customers, giving customers a choice between Shahry's faster-approval ML model and valU's longer repayment terms. Each provider has an independent credit assessment, so a customer declined by one may be approved by the other. Displaying both options at checkout increases the probability of checkout completion for Egypt-based shoppers.

Why did Shahry stop working after it was working before?

A previously working Shahry integration can stop functioning for several reasons: the merchant's commercial registration or Tax Card expired, triggering a compliance hold; Shahry pushed an API update that requires app reinstallation; the Shopify app's payment provider activation was accidentally disabled; or Shahry placed the merchant account on hold for a periodic compliance review. Check the Shahry merchant portal for account status first. If the account is Active, try reinstalling the Shahry Shopify app and re-enabling it as an alternative payment provider.

Does Shahry work with Shopify's one-page checkout?

Shahry's compatibility with Shopify's one-page checkout (introduced in Shopify's 2023–2024 checkout updates) depends on the Shopify app version and whether Shahry has updated its integration to support the new checkout architecture. Merchants on one-page checkout who do not see Shahry should first verify their Shahry Shopify app is on the latest version. If the latest version still does not appear on one-page checkout, contact Shahry merchant support — one-page checkout compatibility may require Shahry's team to update the integration on their side.
