Shopify Local Payment Methods Not Showing — iDEAL, OXXO, Bancontact, PIX, Konbini, COD Silent Failures

Enabling Shopify Markets does not automatically activate local payment methods. iDEAL, Bancontact, OXXO, PIX, Klarna Pay Later, BLIK, and Cash on Delivery each require a separate toggle in Settings → Payments — and the correct currency. When they silently fail to appear, there is no error in Shopify Admin. This guide covers the full activation stack, eight silent failure modes, and country-by-country diagnosis.

Updated June 2026 Shopify Markets · Local Payments · International Checkout
Silent failure pattern: A merchant enables the Netherlands market, Shopify Payments is active, the store is live — but Dutch customers don't see iDEAL at checkout. There is no error anywhere in Shopify Admin. The toggle for iDEAL in Settings → Payments → Shopify Payments → Manage → Local payment methods is off by default. It must be explicitly activated. This pattern repeats for every local payment method in every market.
Architecture

The 4-Layer Local Payment Method Stack

Local payment methods sit at the intersection of four independent configuration layers. All four must be correct simultaneously. A failure at any one layer silently prevents the payment method from appearing — with no error surfaced to the merchant or customer.

Layer 1
Shopify Markets
The target country must have an active Shopify Market with the correct currency configured. Market = country + currency + language + domain. If the customer is being routed to the wrong market (e.g., UK market instead of Belgium), Bancontact won't appear even if it's enabled for Belgium.
Layer 2
Shopify Payments
Shopify Payments must be the active gateway — not Stripe direct, Adyen, Mollie, Authorize.net, or any third-party gateway. Local payment methods (iDEAL, Bancontact, OXXO, PIX, BLIK) are exclusively available through Shopify Payments. Switching to any other gateway removes all local methods instantly.
Layer 3
Per-Method Toggle
Each local payment method has its own activation toggle in Admin → Settings → Payments → Shopify Payments → Manage → Local payment methods. These are OFF by default. A new market with a new country does not inherit any local payment method settings. Every method must be manually enabled.
Layer 4
Currency Match
Each local method requires a specific currency: iDEAL → EUR, OXXO → MXN, PIX/Boleto → BRL, BLIK → PLN, Bancontact → EUR, SEPA → EUR. If the market currency is set to USD or a non-matching currency, the payment method will not appear regardless of the toggle state.

Diagnosis

5-Step Local Payment Method Diagnosis

Work through this checklist in order when a local payment method is missing from checkout. Each step eliminates one layer of the activation stack.

  • 1
    Verify market assignment. Confirm the customer is being routed to the correct Shopify Market. Go to Admin → Analytics → Reports → Sessions by location and compare actual customer country vs. market assignment. If a Dutch customer is landing in your primary US market, their checkout won't offer iDEAL even if iDEAL is enabled for the Netherlands market. Fix: Admin → Settings → Markets → Preferences → configure geolocation settings.
  • 2
    Confirm Shopify Payments is the active gateway. Admin → Settings → Payments. Shopify Payments must be listed as the active payment provider — not deactivated, not replaced by a third-party gateway. Check for recently installed payment apps that may have added an alternative gateway and changed the default. Any third-party gateway as the primary processor disables all local methods.
  • 3
    Check the per-method toggle. Admin → Settings → Payments → Shopify Payments → Manage → scroll to "Local payment methods." Locate the specific method (iDEAL, Bancontact, OXXO, PIX, BLIK) and verify its toggle is Active. If this section is empty or the method is not listed, it may not be available in your Shopify Payments country — verify your store's Shopify Payments registration country matches an eligible region.
  • 4
    Verify market currency matches method requirement. Admin → Settings → Markets → [Target market] → Currency. Confirm the currency is: EUR for iDEAL/Bancontact/SEPA, MXN for OXXO, BRL for PIX/Boleto, PLN for BLIK. A Netherlands market in USD will not show iDEAL. A Mexico market in USD will not show OXXO. Currency mismatch is the second most common cause after the missing toggle.
  • 5
    For Japan (konbini) and Brazil (CPF): check gateway compatibility. If your target method is Japanese konbini, it requires a separate Japanese gateway app — it is not available in Shopify Payments. Install Komoju or GMO-PG and add it under Settings → Payments → Alternative payment methods. If your issue is Brazilian CPF/CNPJ validation failures, this requires either Checkout Extensibility (Shopify Plus) to add a custom tax ID field, or a Brazilian payment gateway that collects the field independently.
Deep Dive

Country-Specific Failure Guides

Deep Dive A — Netherlands

iDEAL Not Showing: The Complete Setup Walkthrough

iDEAL is used in over 60% of online payments in the Netherlands. If Dutch customers don't see iDEAL at checkout, you are losing the majority of Netherlands checkout completions — they will not complete the order with an unfamiliar credit card form.

Required steps (all must be complete):

  • Shopify Payments active as your primary payment gateway
  • Netherlands market created in Admin → Settings → Markets → Add market → Netherlands
  • Netherlands market currency set to EUR (not USD)
  • iDEAL toggled on in Admin → Settings → Payments → Shopify Payments → Manage → Local payment methods → iDEAL → toggle Active
  • Shopify Payments account fully verified (no pending verification banner in Admin → Settings → Payments)

The most common miss: The iDEAL toggle. Merchants set up the Netherlands market and assume local methods activate automatically. They don't. The toggle is in a sub-menu three levels deep in Shopify Payments settings. It is off by default after market creation and after any gateway change.

Testing: After completing all steps, use a VPN set to a Netherlands IP and browse to your store. Shopify uses geolocation to assign the market. Add a product to cart and proceed to checkout — iDEAL should appear in the payment method list. If it doesn't appear: check market assignment (the VPN IP should trigger the Netherlands market) and re-verify the toggle state.

Bank redirect failures: iDEAL is a synchronous bank redirect — the customer is taken to their bank's website to authorize the payment and then returned to your checkout. If the redirect back fails (network issue, browser back button during bank auth, bank session timeout), the order is created with "Payment pending" status. Shopify does not automatically cancel or retry these orders. Monitor "Payment pending" orders daily and contact customers with failed iDEAL redirects to offer alternative payment.

Deep Dive B — Brazil

PIX, Boleto, and the CPF/CNPJ Problem

Brazil has unique payment requirements that Shopify's default checkout does not fully support. The two most common Brazilian payment methods — PIX (instant bank transfer) and Boleto (bank slip) — require the customer's CPF (individual tax ID) or CNPJ (company tax ID) in the transaction data. Shopify's native checkout form does not have a CPF/CNPJ field.

What happens without CPF/CNPJ: If you are using Shopify Payments for Brazil with PIX or card payments, Brazilian payment processors may reject the transaction server-side. The customer sees a generic "your payment could not be processed" message. You see "Payment failed" in the order timeline with no indication that tax ID is the missing element. This creates a false appearance of payment gateway instability when the actual issue is a missing required field.

For Shopify Plus merchants — Checkout Extensibility: Build or install a checkout UI extension that adds a CPF/CNPJ input to the checkout form. The extension validates the tax ID format (11 digits for CPF, 14 for CNPJ) and stores the value as order metadata. Your payment processing app or gateway can then retrieve this value via the Order API and include it in the payment request. Several Shopify App Store partners offer pre-built extensions for this.

For non-Plus merchants: Use a Brazilian-focused payment gateway app (Pagar.me, Mercado Pago, Boa Compra) that handles CPF/CNPJ collection in its own checkout overlay, separate from Shopify's checkout form. These apps add themselves as alternative payment methods in your Settings → Payments → Alternative payment methods. The customer completes the card/PIX/Boleto flow through the gateway's interface. This works but creates a checkout experience that differs from Shopify's native flow.

PIX currency requirement: PIX requires BRL. If your Brazil market is in USD, PIX will not appear regardless of gateway configuration. Set the Brazil market currency to BRL in Admin → Settings → Markets → Brazil → Currency.

Boleto pending order management: Boleto is a bank slip payment with a 3–5 day payment window. Like OXXO, if the customer does not pay within the window, the order is not automatically cancelled in Shopify — its payment status remains "Pending." Monitor and cancel unpaid Boleto orders after the payment window closes to restore inventory.

Deep Dive C — Japan

Konbini: Why You Need a Separate Gateway

Konbini payment (convenience store cash payment at 7-Eleven, Lawson, FamilyMart, Ministop, Daily Yamazaki) is the second most common payment method in Japan after credit cards. A significant portion of Japanese online shoppers do not have credit cards or prefer not to use them online. If you are selling in Japan without konbini payment, you are effectively excluding a large share of the market.

The key constraint: Konbini is NOT available through Shopify Payments. It will never appear in your Settings → Payments → Shopify Payments → Local payment methods list, regardless of how your Japan market is configured. Shopify Payments does not have a konbini integration. This is not a toggle issue or a currency issue — it is a fundamental gateway limitation.

How to add konbini:

  • Komoju — the most Shopify-compatible Japanese payment gateway. Install the Komoju app from the Shopify App Store, complete KYC (Japanese bank account required or Komoju can assist international merchants), and add Komoju as an alternative payment method in Settings → Payments → Alternative payment methods. Komoju supports konbini, credit cards, bank transfer (Furikomi), PayPay, and Paidy.
  • Paidy — a buy-now-pay-later service popular in Japan that is also widely used as a no-card-required payment option. Add via the Paidy app or Komoju which includes Paidy.
  • GMO Payment Gateway — used by larger merchants, requires a Japanese entity or partnership with a local agent.

How konbini checkout works: The customer selects konbini at checkout, chooses their preferred chain, and receives a payment barcode or code. They have 3–7 days to visit a konbini and pay cash. After payment is confirmed (usually within 30 minutes via the konbini network), Shopify receives a payment capture webhook and marks the order as paid. The merchant should not fulfill orders with konbini payment until the "paid" webhook arrives — the order status is "Pending" until then.

JPY currency requirement: All Japanese local payment methods (konbini, Paidy, bank transfer) require the Japan market currency to be JPY. Configure this in Admin → Settings → Markets → Japan → Currency → JPY.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is iDEAL not showing at Shopify checkout for my Dutch customers?
Four simultaneous conditions must be true: (1) Shopify Payments is your active gateway, (2) your Netherlands market is configured with EUR as the currency, (3) iDEAL is toggled on in Settings → Payments → Shopify Payments → Manage → Local payment methods, and (4) your Shopify Payments account is fully verified. The most common cause is #3 — iDEAL is off by default and must be explicitly activated. There is no error message when it's off — iDEAL simply doesn't appear at checkout.
Does Shopify Payments support OXXO for Mexico?
Yes, but with conditions. Your store's Shopify Payments registration must be eligible for Mexico, the Mexico market currency must be MXN (not USD), and OXXO must be toggled on in Settings → Payments → Local payment methods. OXXO gives the customer a 3-day payment window. If they don't pay within that window, the order is silently cancelled — there is no automatic Shopify notification to you when this happens.
How do I add a CPF or CNPJ field to Shopify checkout for Brazil?
Shopify does not collect CPF/CNPJ natively. For Shopify Plus merchants, use Checkout Extensibility to build or install a UI extension that adds a validated tax ID field to checkout — the value is stored as order metadata and passed to your gateway. For non-Plus merchants, use a Brazilian payment gateway app (Pagar.me, Mercado Pago) that collects CPF/CNPJ in its own payment overlay. Without CPF/CNPJ, Brazilian card transactions may fail server-side with a generic decline error — the gateway error code will reference "CPF" or "buyer document" in most cases.
Why did SOFORT payment stop working for German customers?
SOFORT was deprecated in 2024 and is no longer a functional payment method. The legacy SOFORT toggle in Shopify Payments settings does not activate a working payment flow. The replacement is Klarna Bank Transfer, which uses the Klarna network that absorbed the SOFORT infrastructure. Enable "Klarna — Pay Now" in your Germany/EU market's local payment methods settings. Remove any legacy SOFORT configuration.
Can I offer Cash on Delivery only for specific countries on Shopify?
Shopify's native Cash on Delivery (COD) manual payment method supports market-level restrictions but not per-country restrictions within a multi-country market. If your Middle East market includes UAE, Kuwait, and Saudi Arabia and you want COD only for UAE, you need to either split UAE into its own single-country market, or use a checkout app that applies payment method rules at a more granular geographic level. "All markets" COD will appear everywhere, which is usually not what international merchants intend.
