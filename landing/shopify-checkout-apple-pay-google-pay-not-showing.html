Shopify Checkout Troubleshooting

Shopify Apple Pay / Google Pay Not Showing — Express Checkout Buttons Missing

Shopify's wallet buttons use the browser's Payment Request API. When eligibility fails — wrong provider, wrong browser, unsupported country, or empty wallet — the button simply disappears. No error. No message. Here's what's actually happening.

How Shopify's Express Checkout Buttons Work

Apple Pay, Google Pay, and Shop Pay buttons appear in a dedicated "Express checkout" section above the contact field at Shopify checkout. They're not rendered by your theme — they're injected by Shopify's checkout JavaScript using the browser's Payment Request API (W3C standard) and, for Apple Pay, the Apple Pay JS API.

Before rendering any button, Shopify calls the API's canMakePayment() method. If the browser returns false — because the device has no saved card, the browser doesn't support the method, or the payment provider can't process this origin — the button is silently omitted. There is no error in the console, no fallback message, and no admin notification. The express section simply doesn't render.

Key insight: The express checkout section is entirely controlled by Shopify and the browser — not your theme, not your apps. If it's missing, the problem is almost always one of six eligibility failures explained below.
Step 1

Browser calls canMakePayment()

Shopify JS asks the browser if it can complete a payment for this merchant. Returns true/false silently.

Step 2

Shopify checks provider eligibility

Shopify Payments must be active. Third-party gateways cannot trigger express buttons regardless of browser support.

Step 3

Button renders (or doesn't)

If both checks pass, the button appears. If either fails, the express section is hidden — no error shown.

Six Root Causes

Cause 1 — Most Common

Shopify Payments not active

Apple Pay and Google Pay are exclusive to Shopify Payments. Third-party gateways don't qualify. No exceptions.

Cause 2

Wrong browser or device

Apple Pay: Safari only (Apple devices). Google Pay: Chrome only (Android + desktop). iOS blocks all non-Safari Payment Request API calls.

Cause 3

Country not supported

Shopify Payments is available in more countries than Apple Pay or Google Pay. Your store's country may have Shopify Payments but lack wallet payment support.

Cause 4

Customer wallet is empty

The button appears only if the customer has a card in Apple Wallet / Google Pay. No card = no button. This is customer-side, not merchant-side.

Cause 5

Shopify Payments suspended

During a payout hold or account review, Shopify Payments is paused. All express buttons disappear silently until the suspension is resolved.

Cause 6

Checkout customization conflict

A checkout UI extension or checkout.liquid edit (Plus stores) has hidden or broken the express payment section.

Apple Pay Not Showing — Specific Requirements

Apple Pay at Shopify checkout requires all five of these conditions simultaneously:

Shopify Payments is active as the primary gateway

Go to Settings → Payments in your Shopify admin. Shopify Payments must show as "Active" — not "Pending", "Suspended", or replaced by a third-party provider. If you see a third-party gateway listed as the primary provider, Apple Pay will never appear.

Customer is using Safari on an Apple device

Apple Pay uses Apple's proprietary API which is only exposed in Safari (iOS, iPadOS, macOS). Chrome, Firefox, Edge, and Brave on macOS do not expose Apple Pay, even on Apple hardware. There is no workaround — this is an Apple platform constraint, not a Shopify limitation.

Store is in an Apple Pay-supported country

Apple Pay via Shopify Payments is supported in: United States, Canada, United Kingdom, Australia, Ireland, New Zealand, Singapore, Hong Kong, and a growing list of others. Verify your store's country is on Apple's merchant payment list — Shopify Payments availability alone is not sufficient.

Customer has at least one card in Apple Wallet

On iOS: Settings → Wallet & Apple Pay must show at least one payment card. On macOS: Safari Preferences → Privacy → Apple Pay must be enabled and a card must be present. On Mac with Touch ID, the card can be on a paired iPhone. On a Mac without Touch ID, Apple Pay requires a paired iPhone or Apple Watch. No card = no button.

The checkout is served over HTTPS

The Payment Request API and Apple Pay JS refuse to initialize on HTTP origins. Shopify's standard checkout URL (checkout.shopify.com) is always HTTPS. Custom Plus domains require a valid SSL certificate. If your custom domain has an SSL error or mixed-content warning, Apple Pay will not appear.

Common testing mistake: Merchants test on a desktop Mac in Chrome and wonder why Apple Pay doesn't appear. Chrome on Mac does not support Apple Pay. Always test in Safari, on a real Apple device with cards in Wallet.

Apple Pay domain verification

Shopify automatically handles Apple Pay domain verification for checkout.shopify.com and Plus custom checkout domains when you activate Shopify Payments. You do not need to manually serve a .well-known/apple-developer-merchantid-domain-association file — Shopify does this. If you've migrated to a new custom domain on Plus and Apple Pay stopped working, it may take 24–48 hours for Shopify to complete domain re-verification.

Google Pay Not Showing — Specific Requirements

Google Pay at Shopify checkout uses the Payment Request API with Google's payment method. Requirements differ from Apple Pay:

Supported browsers

  • Chrome on Android (primary)
  • Chrome on Windows desktop
  • Chrome on macOS desktop
  • Microsoft Edge (Chromium) on some configurations
  • Samsung Internet on Android

Not supported

  • Safari on iOS (Apple restricts Payment Request API to Apple Pay only)
  • Safari on macOS (same restriction)
  • Firefox on any platform
  • Chrome on iOS (iOS forces all browsers to use WebKit, which blocks non-Apple Pay Payment Request)
  • Any browser on iOS, regardless of name
iOS note: On iPhones and iPads, Apple does not allow browsers other than Safari to use the Payment Request API. So even if a customer on iPhone uses Chrome, they cannot see Google Pay — only Safari can show Apple Pay. This is an Apple platform policy, not a Google or Shopify decision.

Google Pay eligibility check

For Google Pay to show on desktop Chrome, the customer must be signed into a Google account in Chrome and have at least one card saved in Google Pay (pay.google.com). On Android, Google Pay requires Google Play Services. In some markets, Google Pay has bank participation requirements — not all banks support the tap-to-pay tokens that Google Pay uses, so even customers with Google accounts may not have eligible cards.

Important: Google Pay disappearing after previously working is often caused by the customer signing out of their Google account in Chrome, or removing their saved card from Google Pay. It's a customer wallet issue — not a Shopify configuration regression.

Diagnostic Checklist

Work through this in order. Each step eliminates a class of failure:

Verify Shopify Payments is active

Shopify Admin → Settings → Payments. Shopify Payments must show as "Active". If it shows "Suspended", "Pending review", or is absent entirely, no express buttons will appear. This is the fix 60%+ of the time.

Test in the correct browser/device combination

For Apple Pay: use Safari on iPhone, iPad, or Mac. For Google Pay: use Chrome on Android or Chrome desktop. Using the wrong browser is the most common reason merchants think the feature is broken when it's actually working correctly for eligible customers.

Confirm your country is supported

Check Shopify's help docs for current Apple Pay and Google Pay country lists (these update periodically). If your store's billing country is not on the list, you cannot enable these payment methods regardless of other settings.

Check the customer's wallet

On iPhone: Settings → Wallet & Apple Pay → confirm at least one card is present. On Android: open the Google Pay app or visit pay.google.com → confirm at least one payment method is saved. If no card exists, the button won't appear for that customer.

Check for checkout customization conflicts

Shopify Admin → Settings → Checkout → Customize (for Online Store 2.0) or via Checkout Extensibility. Look for any hidden sections or UI extensions that may be suppressing the express checkout section. Temporarily disable customizations to test if buttons reappear.

Check SSL on custom domains (Plus)

If you have a custom checkout domain on Shopify Plus, verify the SSL certificate is valid. Go to your custom domain in a browser and look for any certificate warnings. An expired or invalid cert will prevent Apple Pay domain verification from completing.

If buttons disappeared after working fine: The most likely culprit is Shopify Payments being temporarily suspended or placed under review. Check your Shopify admin email for any payment account alerts, and verify your Shopify Payments status in Settings → Payments immediately.
