Shopify Checkout Inventory Reservation Not Working — Ghost OOS, Hold Not Releasing & Overselling

Updated June 2026 Shopify Basic · Shopify · Advanced · Plus 3 failure modes · 7-step diagnostic

Shopify reserves inventory the moment a checkout session is created — not when the order is paid. Ghost out-of-stock errors happen when abandoned checkouts hold inventory before the session expires. Overselling happens when inventory tracking is bypassed by a product setting or an app. Race conditions at flash sales let multiple customers reach checkout for the same unit simultaneously, with only one succeeding.

How Shopify Inventory Reservation Actually Works

Most merchants assume Shopify deducts inventory when an order is paid. That's not accurate. Shopify reserves inventory at checkout session creation — the moment a customer clicks "Checkout" from the cart. The hold prevents two customers from completing payment for the same last unit.

Step 1 — Cart

No inventory hold at cart

Adding to cart doesn't reserve anything. Multiple customers can have the same item in their carts simultaneously. Cart-level availability is based on the raw inventory count with no reservation applied.

Step 2 — Checkout Created

Inventory hold begins here

When the customer clicks Checkout, Shopify creates a checkout session and immediately places a hold on the inventory. The available quantity for other customers is reduced at this point — even though no payment has occurred.

Step 3 — Payment Completed

Hold converts to order deduction

When payment succeeds, the checkout session converts to an order and the inventory hold is converted to a permanent deduction. No gap exists between hold and deduction — the transition is atomic.

Step 4 — Checkout Abandoned

Hold must be explicitly released

If the customer abandons checkout, the hold persists until Shopify's session cleanup detects inactivity. This cleanup is not instant — it runs on a schedule, typically within 10–30 minutes of session inactivity. During this window, the item appears sold out.

The critical architectural insight: Shopify's inventory reservation is designed around a checkout session lifecycle, not a payment lifecycle. A single abandoned checkout session for the last unit creates a period where your item appears sold out to every other customer — even if you have zero completed orders.

Hold Duration: What Shopify Actually Does

There is no single fixed hold duration that applies to all stores. The hold lifetime depends on plan and configuration:

  • Standard plans (Basic, Shopify, Advanced): Hold duration is controlled entirely by Shopify. An active checkout session is kept alive while the browser is open and the customer is interacting. After the browser closes or the customer navigates away, Shopify's background jobs detect inactivity and release the hold — typically within 10–30 minutes, though this is not guaranteed or documented as a hard SLA.
  • Shopify Plus: The Inventory Reservation API lets Plus merchants explicitly set how long inventory is held at checkout. This can be set from a minimum of 10 minutes up to multiple hours, giving merchants control over the ghost OOS window.
  • Draft orders (all plans): Draft orders do not create checkout sessions and do not trigger the inventory reservation system at creation. Inventory is only deducted when the draft order is paid.

Ghost OOS: Holds Not Releasing / Item Stuck as Out of Stock

Ghost OOS is the most common inventory reservation complaint: customers report items are sold out, but you have no corresponding orders. The item is being held by active or recently-abandoned checkout sessions.

How Ghost OOS Happens

Scenario: You have 1 unit left of a popular product. Customer A reaches checkout — Shopify reserves the unit. Customer A decides not to buy and closes the tab. Shopify needs time to detect the abandonment. During that window — which can be 10–30 minutes — Customer B visits the product page and sees "Out of Stock." Customer B never comes back. The unit is released, but Customer B is gone.

At scale during a product launch or flash sale, this compounds: 50 customers simultaneously reach checkout for 10 available units. Shopify successfully reserves all 10. The other 40 checkouts fail to reserve. But even after those 50 customers abandon, the 10 holds persist for the cleanup window, blocking the next wave of customers from seeing availability.

Diagnosing Ghost OOS

1
Check inventory levels vs. orders

In Shopify admin → Products → [Product] → Inventory, check the available quantity. Then check Orders to count actual purchased units. If available + ordered does not equal your total stock, you have active holds consuming the difference.

2
Check abandoned checkouts

Shopify admin → Orders → Abandoned checkouts. Active abandoned checkouts that contain the out-of-stock item are holding inventory. Shopify Plus merchants can also query active inventory reservations via the Inventory Reservation API.

3
Wait for automatic release

If you've confirmed the holds exist and are from truly abandoned sessions, they will release automatically. Standard plan merchants cannot force-release checkout-level inventory holds from the admin UI — only session expiry releases them.

4
Shopify Plus: use Inventory Reservation API to reduce hold duration

If you're on Plus, reduce the locationActivationThreshold or configure a shorter reservation window via the Inventory Reservation API. A 10-minute hold window dramatically reduces ghost OOS impact versus a 60-minute window.

You cannot manually delete inventory holds from the Shopify admin on standard plans. Adjusting inventory quantities manually (adding stock) will show items as available again, but this creates data integrity issues — you're telling Shopify you have more stock than you actually do. Only use manual quantity adjustments if you're certain the holds are truly abandoned and won't convert to orders.

Ghost OOS Prevention

  • Shopify Plus — shorten the reservation window: Use the Inventory Reservation API to set a shorter hold duration (10–15 minutes). Most genuine customers complete checkout within 10 minutes; longer holds primarily benefit bots and indecisive browsers.
  • Add a "notify when available" option: If ghost OOS is persistent, adding a back-in-stock notification app means customers who see OOS can be captured and notified when the hold releases — recovering demand that would otherwise be lost.
  • Increase stock buffers before launches: For high-demand product launches, having a small inventory buffer (e.g., not selling 100% of available units via Shopify) reduces ghost OOS sensitivity at zero-remaining thresholds.

Inventory Not Being Held — Overselling at Checkout

The opposite problem: customers successfully complete payment for items that should be out of stock. Shopify failed to hold or enforce inventory limits. Four causes account for virtually all overselling cases.

Cause 1 — Most Common

"Continue selling when out of stock" is enabled

In the product editor, the "Continue selling when out of stock" checkbox disables inventory enforcement entirely. When checked, Shopify allows checkout to proceed regardless of inventory levels. Check every product and variant individually — this setting is per-variant, not per-product.

Cause 2 — App Orders

Apps creating orders via API bypass checkout

Apps that create orders directly via the Shopify Orders API (subscription apps like Recharge, wholesale apps, POS-adjacent tools) bypass the standard checkout reservation system. The Orders API does not check or reserve inventory by default — it deducts after the fact, which can allow overselling.

Cause 3 — Draft Orders

Draft orders skip inventory reservation

Draft orders created in Shopify admin or via the Draft Orders API do not trigger checkout-level inventory holds. If a draft order is sent to a customer and multiple customers receive draft order payment links, all of them can complete payment even if inventory is insufficient.

Cause 4 — Multi-Location Gap

Inventory not tracked at all locations

If a product is available at some locations but not tracked at others, Shopify's reservation may reference the wrong location's inventory. Items marked "Not tracked" for inventory bypass all hold logic at checkout for those locations.

Checking "Continue Selling When Out of Stock" Per-Variant

This is the most overlooked setting because it appears to be a product-level option but is actually stored per-variant. When you set up a product, the setting is applied globally, but if variants are edited individually, they can drift. The only reliable way to audit is:

1
Shopify admin → Products → [Product]

Click on the product, scroll to the Inventory section. If the product has variants, each variant has its own inventory settings page.

2
Click each variant → check "Continue selling when out of stock"

This checkbox must be unchecked for inventory limits to be enforced at checkout. The checkbox appears under the Inventory section of each variant's detail page.

3
Audit via Shopify admin bulk editor (for many products)

Products → [select all] → Edit products → add the "Continue selling" column to the bulk editor. This lets you see and correct the setting across all products without opening each individually.

Subscription apps and the Orders API. If you use a subscription billing app (Recharge, Seal Subscriptions, Bold Subscriptions), renewal orders are created via Shopify's Orders API, not via the standard checkout. These orders bypass inventory reservation. If a subscriber's renewal creates an order when inventory is 0, the order succeeds and you'll need to fulfill it manually, backorder it, or cancel it. This is expected behavior — you must configure inventory management within your subscription app settings, not in Shopify's checkout inventory settings.

Race Conditions During Flash Sales and High Traffic

Race conditions occur when the time between "customer sees item as available" and "checkout reservation fires" is longer than expected — allowing multiple customers to proceed toward payment for the same unit.

The Anatomy of a Flash Sale Race Condition

At normal traffic, Shopify's checkout reservation resolves race conditions cleanly: two customers click Checkout simultaneously, one gets the hold, the other sees an error at payment. At very high traffic (thousands of simultaneous requests during a product drop), the system can show stale availability information before the reservation layer catches up.

The key vulnerability: Shopify's product page and cart page read inventory from a CDN-cached layer. This cache can lag by a few seconds. During those seconds, customers who load the product page may see "1 in stock" when in reality all units are already held at checkout by concurrent sessions. All those customers add to cart and proceed to checkout — only one will successfully complete payment, but all of them reach the payment step before receiving the "no longer available" error.

The "no longer available" error at payment step is intentional behavior. Shopify's inventory system resolves conflicts at the checkout reservation layer. Customers who reach the payment form and then see this error are not experiencing a bug — they lost the inventory race at the reservation stage. The alternative (allowing the error earlier at checkout creation) would cause more friction for customers who reach checkout first-come-first-served.

High-Traffic Flash Sale Inventory Strategies

Strategy 1

Use Shopify's built-in queue (Plus)

Shopify Plus includes a waiting room / virtual queue feature for high-traffic launches. This serializes access to the product page, preventing thousands of simultaneous checkout creations that create both ghost OOS and degraded performance.

Strategy 2

Pre-launch password page with timed reveal

For product drops, keeping the store password-protected and revealing at a precise time with a countdown creates a more controlled first-wave. The first wave is large but not infinite — reducing simultaneous checkout creation spikes.

Strategy 3

Reduce cart-to-checkout friction

Flash sale drop-off between cart and checkout is high. Reducing steps (using Shopify's accelerated checkout buttons — Shop Pay, PayPal, Google Pay) means customers who reach checkout convert faster, keeping holds shorter and releasing sooner for the next customer.

Strategy 4

Stagger product availability

Release inventory in waves rather than all at once. Instead of releasing 100 units simultaneously, release 25 every 15 minutes. Smaller inventory releases create smaller race windows and reduce simultaneous checkout spikes.

When "Item No Longer Available" Errors Are the Race Condition Signal

If your checkout monitoring (or customers) reports "This item is no longer available" errors at the payment step during a sale, this is the race condition manifesting. It means customers are reaching payment only to discover the inventory was reserved by a concurrent session. Multi-Location Inventory Hold Issues

Stores with multiple inventory locations have an additional layer of complexity: inventory reservations are tied to specific locations, and the checkout's fulfillment location assignment determines which location's inventory is held.

How Shopify Assigns Fulfillment Location at Checkout

When a checkout session is created, Shopify evaluates the customer's shipping address against your fulfillment settings (shipping zones, local delivery settings, and the Locations settings in Shopify admin). The checkout assigns an inventory hold at the location that will fulfill the order.

This creates a specific failure pattern: a product has 0 units at Location A (the assigned fulfillment location) and 50 units at Location B. The checkout reserves from Location A, finds 0 inventory, and the item shows as sold out — even though the store has 50 units in total. The customer sees OOS because the checkout's location assignment doesn't match where the stock actually is.

Common trigger for this issue: Creating a new location (e.g., a warehouse or 3PL) and moving stock there without updating fulfillment priorities. The old default location has 0 stock; the new location has all the stock; checkout reserves from the old location. Fix: Shopify admin → Settings → Shipping and Delivery → Shipping origin → update the active fulfillment location. Or reassign shipping zones to include the new location.

Location-Specific Inventory Checks

  • Check inventory per location: Products → [Product] → Inventory → view the "Available" column per location. Not just the total — per location.
  • Check active fulfillment locations: Settings → Shipping and Delivery → confirm which location is marked as the default shipping origin for each shipping zone.
  • Check "Not tracked" inventory at any location: If a location has "Not tracked" set for a product, that location's inventory bypasses reservation logic entirely. This means orders can be created against that location with no inventory deduction — a potential overselling path.
  • Third-party 3PLs and fulfillment apps: If you use a 3PL app (ShipBob, Flexport, etc.), the app manages its own location's inventory. If the 3PL location is the primary fulfillment location, inventory holds reference the 3PL's reported stock counts. Sync delays between the 3PL and Shopify create windows where Shopify's view of inventory is stale.

Third-Party App Conflicts with Inventory Reservation

Several categories of apps interact with Shopify's inventory reservation system in ways that create either ghost OOS or overselling, depending on how the app is implemented.

Cart Reservation Apps (Add-to-Cart Hold)

Apps that hold inventory at the add-to-cart stage (before checkout) operate in parallel with Shopify's checkout-level reservation. The result is a double-hold: the app holds the item when it goes into the cart, and Shopify holds it again when the customer reaches checkout. When the cart session expires and the app releases its hold, Shopify's checkout hold persists — or vice versa. This creates longer-than-expected hold windows and more ghost OOS incidents than either system would produce alone.

Well-built cart reservation apps account for this by monitoring checkout session state via Shopify webhooks and coordinating their release timing. Poorly-built apps do not — they create independent hold lifecycles that compound the problem.

Pre-Order Apps

Pre-order apps typically work by either: (a) setting inventory to a high negative number to allow oversell, or (b) using "Continue selling when out of stock" to bypass inventory enforcement. Both approaches mean Shopify's inventory reservation system is effectively disabled for pre-order products. This is intentional for pre-orders, but it means you must rely entirely on the pre-order app's own quantity controls — Shopify checkout won't enforce limits for you.

Subscription Renewal Conflicts

Subscription renewal orders created by apps like Recharge use the Orders API and bypass checkout inventory reservation. If you have a product available in both subscription and one-time-purchase formats, a high-demand subscription renewal cycle can oversell inventory that you expected to protect for new checkout-based customers. This is especially common for limited-edition or seasonal subscription products.

7-Step Inventory Reservation Diagnostic

Use this flow to determine which failure mode you're experiencing — ghost OOS, overselling, or race condition — before attempting a fix.

1
Identify which direction the problem goes

Ghost OOS: item appears sold out, but you have inventory and no matching orders. Overselling: orders exist for more units than you had in stock. Race condition: "no longer available" errors at payment during high-traffic periods. Each requires a different diagnostic path.

2
Check product inventory tracking settings

Shopify admin → Products → [Product] → each variant → Inventory. Confirm: "Track quantity" is enabled (not "Don't track"). Confirm: "Continue selling when out of stock" is unchecked for overselling scenarios. These are per-variant settings.

3
Check abandoned checkouts for active holds

Shopify admin → Orders → Abandoned checkouts. Filter by date and look for abandoned checkouts containing the affected product. Active abandoned checkouts with that product are consuming inventory holds. Plus merchants: use the Inventory Reservation API to query active reservations by location and inventory item ID.

4
Verify inventory counts per location

Products → [Product] → Inventory → expand to show per-location counts. Confirm the location that's assigned as the default fulfillment location for the affected customer's shipping zone actually has the inventory. Check Settings → Shipping and Delivery for location priority.

5
Audit apps that create orders or hold carts

Check whether any installed app: creates orders via API (subscription, wholesale, B2B apps), implements cart-level reservation, or manages a pre-order flow with custom inventory logic. Each of these operates outside Shopify's standard checkout reservation system.

6
Test a live checkout with the affected product

Use an incognito browser with a fresh session. Add the affected product to cart, proceed to checkout, and observe: does the checkout create successfully? Does the available inventory count drop? Use CheckPulse's free checkout check for an automated version of this test with a full diagnostic report.

7
Check Shopify's system status for platform-wide inventory issues

During high-traffic periods, Shopify's inventory reservation system can have platform-wide degradation that affects all merchants. Check status.shopify.com. If there's an active incident, inventory holds may not be applied or released on the normal schedule — wait for Shopify's resolution rather than chasing a store-specific fix.

FAQ

How long does Shopify hold inventory during checkout?
Shopify reserves inventory at checkout session creation and holds it until the session completes or Shopify's cleanup detects abandonment. For standard plans, the release after abandonment typically happens within 10–30 minutes of inactivity — there is no publicly documented hard timer. On Shopify Plus, you can configure the hold duration via the Inventory Reservation API, with a minimum of 10 minutes. There is no way to set a custom hold duration on Basic, Shopify, or Advanced plans.
Why does Shopify show an item as out of stock when it was never actually purchased?
Ghost OOS. When a customer reaches checkout, Shopify immediately places a hold on the inventory — even before payment. If the customer abandons the checkout, the hold persists until Shopify detects inactivity and runs the cleanup job. During this window, the item appears sold out to other customers. For the last available unit, this is especially damaging because 100% of that unit's availability is consumed by a hold from someone who didn't buy. The hold releases automatically; it's not permanent.
Why is Shopify letting people buy items that are out of stock?
One of four causes: (1) "Continue selling when out of stock" is checked for the product/variant — this disables all inventory enforcement. (2) An app is creating orders via the Shopify Orders API rather than through the standard checkout flow — API orders bypass checkout-level inventory validation. (3) Draft orders don't create checkout reservations — all draft order recipients can complete payment even if inventory is insufficient. (4) Inventory isn't tracked at the location used for fulfillment — "Not tracked" status bypasses all hold logic.
What happens when two customers try to buy the last item at the same time on Shopify?
Shopify resolves this at checkout session creation. Whichever customer's checkout is created first gets the inventory hold. The second customer's checkout is also created, but when they reach payment, they receive "This item is no longer available." Carts do not reserve inventory — two customers can both have the same item in their cart. The race is only resolved when both try to reach checkout. At high traffic, many customers can see the product as available (from a CDN-cached product page) and proceed to checkout, with all but one failing at the reservation step.
Can I manually release inventory holds on Shopify?
On standard Shopify plans, there is no UI to manually release checkout-level inventory holds. The holds release automatically when the checkout session expires. You can manually adjust inventory quantities in Shopify admin (add stock to make the item appear available again), but this doesn't release the hold — it creates new "available" units on top of the held ones. On Shopify Plus, the Inventory Reservation API allows querying and managing active reservations programmatically, giving more control over hold lifecycle.
Does Shopify's inventory reservation work with multi-location inventory?
Yes, but reservations are location-specific. The checkout reserves inventory at the location assigned as the fulfillment location for the customer's order, based on shipping zones and location priority settings. If your stock is at Location B but the checkout reserves from Location A (which has 0 stock), the item shows as unavailable even if you have plenty of stock elsewhere. Check Settings → Shipping and Delivery to verify which location is the active fulfillment origin for each shipping zone, and ensure inventory is stocked at the correct location.
