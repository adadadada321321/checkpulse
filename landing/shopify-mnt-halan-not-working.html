Shopify MNT-Halan Not Working — Halan BNPL Not Showing, Application Declined & Microfinance Issues

Updated June 2026 · 7 failure modes · Egypt merchants · Halan microfinance BNPL · underbanked segment

MNT-Halan (now Halan) is Egypt's leading microfinance super-app — a BNPL and microloan product specifically built for underbanked Egyptians who may have no formal bank account, no credit bureau history, and no traditional credit card. Unlike Forsa (bank-backed CIB), valU (EFG Hermes), or Shahry (VC-backed ML scoring), Halan uses a proprietary AI credit model fed by alternative data signals: National ID verification, selfie liveness, behavioral patterns, and mobile usage data. When Halan stops working on Shopify, the failure mode matters — not showing at checkout, a customer declined by AI scoring, an ID photo rejection, and an OTP failure are four entirely different problems with four different fixes.

How MNT-Halan Works — and Why It's Different From Other Egypt BNPL

Halan targets the underbanked majority, not the banked minority. While Forsa (CIB) and valU serve customers with existing bank accounts and credit bureau records, MNT-Halan was built for the estimated 70%+ of Egyptians who are unbanked or underbanked. Halan's credit model uses alternative data — National ID, selfie biometrics, device signals, and behavioral history from Halan's own ecosystem — rather than traditional credit bureau scoring. This means customers who would be automatically declined by Forsa or valU can be approved by Halan.

Halan requires a selfie and National ID photo during the checkout flow. Unlike Tabby or Postpay where the customer provides card or bank details, Halan's checkout flow includes a KYC step where the customer uploads or captures their National ID and takes a selfie for liveness verification. This additional step is by design — it is how Halan verifies identity without requiring a bank account. Any issue with image quality, lighting, ID expiry, or liveness detection causes a hard stop in the checkout flow.

Halan credit limits are personalized and dynamically assigned. Halan does not publish a fixed credit limit table. Each customer's approved credit limit is determined by their Halan profile — first-time applicants receive lower initial limits that increase as they build a repayment track record within the Halan ecosystem. If a customer's limit is below the cart total, Halan cannot be used for that purchase and no partial financing is available.

Halan is Egypt-only and EGP-only. Halan financing is available exclusively for Egyptian customers with valid Egyptian National IDs. The payment processes in Egyptian Pounds only. Non-Egyptian customers and non-EGP orders cannot use Halan BNPL at Shopify checkout regardless of integration status.

1

Halan not appearing at checkout — merchant not onboarded or integration misconfigured

Merchant Issue

The most common reason Halan does not appear at Shopify checkout is that the merchant has not completed Halan's merchant onboarding process. MNT-Halan requires merchants to register through Halan's merchant program — either through Halan's merchant portal or via a Halan business development representative — and receive production API credentials before the BNPL option becomes available to customers. There is no self-serve instant-activation path; Halan reviews merchant applications and issues credentials after approval.

The second most common cause is an installed but unconfigured Halan integration. The Halan Shopify plugin or payment gateway integration requires your Halan merchant API key and merchant ID to be entered correctly in the payment settings. If the credentials are missing, are from a test/sandbox environment, or do not match the production credentials issued by Halan, the API authentication fails silently and the payment option is not rendered at checkout. There is no error visible to the customer — Halan simply does not appear.

A third cause specific to Halan: the payment method must be explicitly enabled in Shopify's payment settings after installation. Installing the Halan app does not automatically enable it as a payment method. Many merchants install the app but do not complete the Shopify-side activation step under Settings → Payments → Third-party payment providers.

How to fix

  1. Confirm merchant onboarding status: Check your Halan merchant account dashboard or contact your Halan business development contact to confirm your merchant account is approved and active — not just applied for. Applied status means no payment option will appear.
  2. Retrieve production credentials: Log in to the Halan merchant portal and locate your production merchant ID and API key. These are different from sandbox/test credentials. If you cannot locate them, contact Halan merchant support.
  3. Enter credentials in Shopify: In your Shopify admin, go to Settings → Payments → Third-party payment providers → Halan. Enter the production merchant ID and API key exactly as provided. Save and confirm there are no whitespace or copy-paste formatting issues.
  4. Enable the payment method: After saving credentials, ensure the Halan payment method is toggled to Active/Enabled under Shopify payment settings. Installed but not enabled means customers cannot see it.
  5. Test with a real device and small order: Open an incognito browser window, add a product to your cart, proceed to checkout, and verify Halan appears as a payment option. Do not test with a product below the minimum order amount.
2

Customer application declined — AI scoring rejected / thin behavioral profile

Customer Issue

Halan application declines at Shopify checkout are driven by Halan's proprietary AI credit scoring model. Unlike bank-backed BNPL services that rely on Egypt's i-Score credit bureau, Halan's model uses alternative and behavioral data signals — National ID metadata, selfie biometric match scores, device fingerprint, mobile number history, and repayment history within the Halan ecosystem. This is intentionally designed to serve customers outside the traditional credit system.

The most common reason for a first-time Halan applicant to be declined is insufficient profile data for the model to make a confident approval decision. Halan's AI needs enough signals to assess risk — if the customer has no prior Halan history and limited alternative data, the model defaults to a conservative decline rather than approving an unknown risk. Customers who have used Halan's ride-hailing, logistics, or micro-loan products previously build a data profile that improves BNPL approval odds.

Other decline reasons include: a previous Halan loan or BNPL installment that was missed or defaulted on; National ID that fails the verification check (expired, damaged photo, or data mismatch); a credit amount requested that exceeds what Halan's model approves for that customer's risk tier; or a device or behavioral signal that Halan's fraud detection system flags as suspicious.

What merchants can tell declined customers

  1. Build a Halan history first: Customers new to Halan can start by using Halan's app for other services (logistics, ride-hailing, or small micro-loans) to build a behavioral profile before attempting a BNPL purchase. This increases approval odds on subsequent attempts.
  2. Ensure National ID is valid: The customer's National ID must be current, not expired, and the photo must match their selfie. An expired ID is a hard decline.
  3. Try a lower cart total: If the requested amount exceeds the customer's Halan limit, declining the current purchase and trying with a smaller cart may succeed — Halan's limit is per-customer and may be below the full order amount.
  4. Check for prior defaults: Customers with a missed Halan repayment are typically locked out of new BNPL applications until the outstanding balance is settled. Direct them to the Halan app to check account status.
  5. Offer alternative payment methods: Paymob, Fawry COD, or direct card payment are fallbacks for declined Halan applicants. Ensure your store has these configured.
Note for merchants: Halan's decline rate for first-time applicants is typically higher than for returning Halan users. If your customer base is primarily new to Halan, consider also offering Shahry (which has a different AI model) and valU to capture customers who may qualify for one but not another.
3

National ID or selfie verification failed — KYC hard stop in checkout flow

Customer Issue

MNT-Halan includes a KYC (Know Your Customer) step embedded in the Shopify checkout flow that requires the customer to capture or upload their Egyptian National ID and complete a selfie liveness check. This step is unique to Halan among Egypt BNPL services — it is how Halan verifies identity without requiring a bank account or credit bureau record. When the KYC step fails, the Halan application stops and the customer cannot proceed to payment.

National ID verification failures occur when the ID photo is blurry, too dark, partially obscured, or captured at an angle that makes the text or photo unreadable by Halan's OCR and verification system. An expired National ID is a hard failure — Halan does not accept expired IDs regardless of photo quality. A National ID where the customer's face photo on the ID does not match the selfie within acceptable biometric thresholds also causes a hard rejection.

Selfie liveness check failures occur when the customer's device camera is poor quality, the environment is too dark or backlit, the customer's face is partially obscured (by a mask, glasses in some positions, or hand), or the liveness detection algorithm cannot confirm the selfie is from a live person rather than a photo of a photo. Some customers also fail the selfie because they do not follow the on-screen instructions (look left, blink, etc.) correctly during the liveness capture.

How to fix

  1. Improve lighting conditions: The customer should retry in a well-lit environment with even light on both the National ID and their face. Avoid direct backlighting from a window behind them.
  2. Clean camera lens: A dirty camera lens is a common source of blurry ID or selfie captures. Wiping the lens with a cloth resolves this.
  3. Flat surface for ID capture: Place the National ID flat on a dark, plain surface and capture directly overhead rather than holding it. This prevents angle distortion and glare.
  4. Remove face coverings: Glasses, hats, or partial face coverings may interfere with the biometric match. Retry without these if possible.
  5. Check ID expiry: An expired National ID must be renewed at a civil registry office before Halan can be used. No workaround exists for an expired ID.
  6. Follow liveness instructions precisely: During the selfie liveness check, the customer should follow every on-screen prompt exactly (head turn, blink, hold still) to pass liveness detection.
Merchant note: The KYC step adds time to the Halan checkout flow compared to card or COD payment. If your store has a significant Halan customer segment, consider adding on-page instructions near the Halan payment option explaining that customers will need their National ID and device camera ready. This reduces abandonment at the KYC step.
4

Credit limit below order amount — Halan limit insufficient for cart total

Customer Issue

MNT-Halan assigns each customer a personalized credit limit based on their Halan profile, behavioral history, and AI scoring result. This limit is not fixed or published — it varies by customer and changes over time as the customer builds a repayment history within the Halan ecosystem. First-time Halan BNPL applicants typically receive a conservative initial credit limit that may be lower than the cart total on a merchant's Shopify store.

When the customer's Halan credit limit is below the order total, the Halan payment option either does not appear for that order amount or appears but returns an error when the customer proceeds. Halan does not support partial financing or split payment — the customer cannot use their Halan limit for a portion of the order and pay the remainder by another method. The full cart total must fall within the approved limit.

Halan credit limits tend to increase as customers successfully repay previous Halan purchases or loans. A customer declined for a 2,000 EGP order today may be approved for that same amount after completing 2–3 smaller Halan purchases and repaying on time. The limit growth is automatic within Halan's system — customers do not need to manually apply for a limit increase.

How to fix

  1. Inform customers of the dynamic limit: Add messaging near the Halan payment option explaining that Halan limits are personalized and grow with repayment history. This manages expectations for customers whose limit is below the current cart value.
  2. Suggest splitting across orders: A customer with a 1,500 EGP Halan limit trying to buy a 3,000 EGP item cannot split it. But they could purchase two separate lower-priced items across two separate Halan orders over time, building limit history.
  3. Offer alternative BNPL: valU and Shahry have different limit models. A customer whose Halan limit is insufficient may qualify for a higher limit with valU or Shahry for the same order. Offering multiple BNPL options captures these customers.
  4. Direct customers to the Halan app: Customers can view their current Halan credit limit in the Halan app. This helps them understand what order value they can finance and avoids failed checkout attempts.
5

OTP not received — mobile number mismatch with Halan records

Customer Issue

Halan uses OTP (one-time password) verification via SMS as part of its checkout authentication flow. The OTP is sent to the mobile number registered in the customer's Halan account or, for first-time applicants, to the number captured during the KYC application at checkout. When this number does not match the SIM the customer is actively using, the OTP is delivered to a different device and the customer cannot complete the Halan checkout step.

This happens most often when a customer has changed their SIM card or mobile number since registering with Halan. In Egypt, number changes happen when switching carriers, traveling internationally and swapping SIMs, or when customers have multiple SIM cards and are using a different one than the one associated with their Halan account.

Network delivery delays are a separate issue — if the OTP is sent to the correct number but the SMS is delayed due to carrier congestion or roaming, the OTP expires before it arrives. Halan's OTP codes have a limited validity window (typically 2–5 minutes) after which a new OTP must be requested.

How to fix

  1. Update Halan account mobile number: The customer should open the Halan app and update their registered mobile number to their current active SIM before attempting the checkout again. The number update in the Halan app triggers its own verification step to confirm the new number.
  2. Switch to the registered SIM: If the customer has the original SIM accessible (in another device, dual-SIM slot, etc.), they can temporarily switch to it to receive the OTP and complete the purchase, then switch back.
  3. Request a new OTP: If the OTP expired due to a delay, the customer can tap "Resend OTP" to request a fresh code. On congested networks, waiting 30–60 seconds before requesting again improves delivery success.
  4. Check network signal: Poor signal or roaming can delay or block SMS delivery. The customer should ensure they have adequate signal strength before retrying.
6

Below minimum order amount — cart total too low for Halan BNPL

Merchant + Customer

Halan sets a minimum eligible order amount below which the BNPL option is not available. If a customer's cart total is under this threshold, Halan will not appear at checkout or will appear but return an error when the customer attempts to use it. The minimum is designed to ensure that the Halan installment plan (which involves administrative fees and a multi-step KYC process) is economically sensible for both the customer and Halan — very small purchases are not viable for installment financing.

As of 2026, Halan's minimum is generally in the range of 200 to 500 Egyptian Pounds per transaction, though the exact threshold may vary based on the merchant agreement. Halan's minimum is intentionally lower than Forsa (~500–1,000 EGP) and valU (~500 EGP) to serve lower-ticket purchases accessible to its underbanked customer segment. Shahry also has a lower minimum (~200 EGP) that competes in a similar range.

How to fix

  1. Display minimum order amount at checkout: Add messaging near the Halan payment option stating the minimum order amount (e.g., "Available for orders over 300 EGP"). This prevents customers from starting the Halan flow on a small cart and hitting an error.
  2. Conditionally show Halan: Use Shopify's payment visibility rules or a checkout extension to show the Halan option only when the cart total meets the minimum. This removes the confusing situation of Halan appearing but then failing.
  3. Confirm your minimum with Halan: Contact Halan merchant support to confirm the exact minimum order amount in your merchant agreement, as it may differ from the default Halan minimum.
7

Product category not eligible — Halan financing restricted for this product type

Merchant Issue

Halan's financing is not available for all product categories. Certain types of products are excluded from Halan BNPL under Halan's policy and are also commonly excluded under merchant agreement terms. Categories typically restricted include digital goods (software, codes, digital subscriptions), purely consumable items with no physical collateral, adult content, gambling-related products, and certain financial products.

Physical goods like electronics, appliances, furniture, clothing, and accessories are generally eligible for Halan financing. However, specific categories may be restricted based on Halan's risk assessment or regulatory requirements in Egypt. A merchant whose primary products fall in a restricted category will find that Halan never appears at checkout despite a correctly configured integration — because Halan's backend silently suppresses the option for ineligible product types.

How to fix

  1. Review your merchant agreement: Your Halan merchant agreement specifies which product categories are approved for financing under your contract. Review this document to confirm your products are in scope.
  2. Contact Halan merchant support: If you are unsure whether your product category is eligible, contact Halan directly with your product category details. Halan's merchant support can confirm eligibility and, in some cases, request an exception for specific product types.
  3. Separate digital and physical SKUs: If your store sells both digital and physical products, consider whether the Halan option can be conditionally displayed only for physical product carts. Shopify's checkout extensions support this level of payment method logic.
Important: If Halan never appears despite a fully configured integration and passing the merchant onboarding check, product category restriction is the most likely explanation. Do not assume it is a technical error until you have verified your category eligibility with Halan.

Frequently Asked

Common Questions About MNT-Halan on Shopify

Should I offer Halan alongside Shahry and valU on the same Shopify store?

Yes — for Egyptian merchant stores, offering MNT-Halan, Shahry, and valU together maximizes BNPL coverage across the full Egyptian buyer spectrum. Halan specifically targets the underbanked segment that both Shahry and valU may decline. Shahry fills the gap between Halan (underbanked) and valU (middle market) with its own ML model. valU serves the broader middle class. Together, the three products cover the widest range of Egyptian BNPL applicants with minimal overlap in the customers they each uniquely approve. Adding Forsa (CIB) as a fourth option extends coverage to formally banked, higher-income customers — making all four together the most comprehensive Egypt BNPL stack a Shopify merchant can offer.

How does Halan pay out merchants after a successful BNPL purchase?

After a customer completes a Halan BNPL purchase at your Shopify checkout, Halan pays the merchant the full order amount (minus any agreed merchant discount rate or administrative fee) according to the settlement schedule in your merchant agreement. Halan then manages the installment collection from the customer directly — you as the merchant are not involved in the customer repayment process. The settlement timeline is typically 1 to 5 business days after the transaction, though the exact cycle is specified in your merchant contract. Unlike some BNPL services that batch weekly, Halan aims for faster merchant settlement given its super-app payment infrastructure.

What happens if a customer defaults on their Halan BNPL installments after buying from my store?

As a merchant, you are not exposed to Halan customer defaults. Once Halan approves the customer's application and processes the payment at your Shopify checkout, the merchant receives the full settlement amount from Halan — the repayment risk is fully transferred to Halan. If the customer misses installments or defaults, Halan's collections process handles it; you are not liable and your settlement is not reversed. This is a standard feature of third-party BNPL services: the provider bears the customer credit risk in exchange for the merchant discount rate charged at each transaction. Chargebacks for fraud or disputes follow a separate process governed by your merchant agreement with Halan.
