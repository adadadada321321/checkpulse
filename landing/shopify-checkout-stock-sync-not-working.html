Shopify Inventory Not Updating After Checkout — Stock Sync Not Working, Overselling, Count Not Decreasing

Shopify does not reserve stock at add-to-cart — only at checkout entry, for 10 minutes. Draft orders never hold inventory. Third-party sync apps can reset counts back to pre-sale levels. Here are all 8 failure modes.

📅 Updated June 2026 ⏱ 10 min read 💡 Applies to: All Shopify plans
Adding to Cart Does NOT Reserve Inventory in Shopify
This is the most widely misunderstood Shopify inventory behavior. When a customer adds an item to their cart, Shopify does not hold or reduce that stock. Multiple customers can have the same last unit in their carts simultaneously. A temporary hold is only created when a customer enters the checkout flow — and that hold expires in approximately 10 minutes.

How Shopify Inventory Reservation Actually Works

Shopify uses a two-stage inventory system that surprises most merchants:

Customer Action Inventory Impact Duration
Add to cart None — no hold N/A
Enter checkout (click Check Out) Temporary hold created ~10 minutes
Complete payment Inventory deducted permanently Permanent
Abandon checkout (hold expires) Hold released, stock restored After ~10 min
Draft order created (unpaid) None — no hold N/A
Draft order paid Inventory deducted permanently Permanent

The practical consequence: two customers can both have the same last unit in their carts and both see "In Stock." Only the one who completes payment first actually gets it. The other will get a checkout error at the payment step.

Failure Mode 1 — Add-to-Cart Does Not Reserve Stock (Race Condition)

When a customer adds an item to their Shopify cart, the inventory count you see in Admin is unchanged. There is no hold, no decrement, no lock. Other customers browsing the same product page at the same moment see the exact same available quantity.

A temporary inventory hold is only created when the customer clicks "Check Out" and enters the checkout flow. That hold is approximately 10 minutes. If the customer does not complete purchase within 10 minutes, the hold is automatically released.

When This Causes Overselling
During flash sales, limited drops, or any high-demand moment: multiple customers add the same last unit to their carts. All see "In Stock." All click Check Out within a few seconds of each other. Shopify processes checkouts sequentially — the first payment to clear wins. The others get an inventory error at the payment step. From the merchant's side, this looks like "sold the same item twice" but only one order actually completed.

How to mitigate add-to-cart race conditions

  • Ensure Continue selling when out of stock is OFF for all affected variants (Admin → Products → variant → Inventory). This blocks Shopify from completing payment on a zero-stock item and is the most important setting.
  • Install an inventory hold app that reserves stock at add-to-cart time (Pre-Order Now, Amai, Cart Reserve, or similar). These create a hold in the cart session before checkout starts.
  • For very high-demand drops, use a virtual queue (Queue-it, Locksmith queue mode) to limit simultaneous product page access.
  • Show a live stock counter on the product page so customers see competition is real and are incentivized to complete purchase quickly.

Failure Mode 2 — 10-Minute Checkout Hold Expires During Flash Sales

The temporary inventory hold Shopify creates at checkout entry lasts approximately 10 minutes. This is normally more than enough time for a customer to complete a purchase. But during high-demand events, several scenarios cause the hold to expire before the customer can pay:

  • Customer gets distracted during checkout and comes back 15 minutes later — hold is gone, item may now be taken
  • Payment processing takes longer than 10 minutes due to 3DS authentication or slow bank response
  • Customer on a slow mobile connection takes longer to load payment screen
  • Shop Pay installments or BNPL approval flow adds time
Diagnosing Expired Holds
If customers report reaching the payment step and getting an "item is no longer available" error during a sale, even though they started checkout when stock was available, the 10-minute hold expiration is the most likely cause. The item was genuinely available when they entered checkout — it became unavailable because the hold expired and another buyer completed payment first.

Failure Mode 3 — Third-Party Inventory Apps Resetting Stock Counts

This is the most common cause of "inventory went back up after a sale." An external inventory management tool — Linnworks, Brightpearl, Cin7, Veeqo, SKUVault, Inventory Planner, or a multichannel listing tool — runs a scheduled sync on a 15–60 minute cycle. It pushes its known stock count to Shopify. If the external source has not yet received the sale data from Shopify, it pushes the old (higher) count, undoing Shopify's deduction.

Why This Happens
The sync cycle is: sale occurs in Shopify → Shopify deducts inventory → 20 minutes later, external tool queries its own database → database still shows pre-sale count (hasn't been updated yet) → external tool pushes that old count to Shopify → inventory appears to reset. The sync lag is the root cause.

How to diagnose

Check Shopify's inventory history for the specific variant:

  1. Admin → Products → click the product → click the specific variant → scroll to the Inventory section → click "View history"
  2. Look for upward inventory adjustments labeled with the name of an app or "Third-party app"
  3. Note the timestamp — if an upward adjustment fires roughly on a 15, 30, or 60-minute interval after your sales, it is your sync app overwriting counts
  4. Also check: Admin → Apps → your inventory app → look for a "sync log" or "activity log" showing what values it pushed to Shopify and when

How to fix

  • Configure the sync to pull from Shopify as the source of truth for online inventory rather than pushing from the external system
  • Increase the sync frequency so the external system learns of sales faster (some tools support near-real-time webhook-based sync instead of polling)
  • During sales events, temporarily pause or disable the scheduled sync and monitor inventory manually
  • If your tool supports it, configure it to adjust inventory by delta (sold quantity) rather than set an absolute count — this prevents overwrites

Failure Mode 4 — Multi-Channel Sync Delay (Amazon, eBay, Etsy)

When you sell on multiple channels through Shopify integrations (Amazon, eBay, Etsy, Google Shopping, TikTok Shop), inventory is synchronized across channels on a scheduled basis — not in real time. A sale on one channel can take 15–60 minutes to reduce inventory in Shopify, and then another 15–60 minutes for the updated count to propagate to other channels.

During this sync window, the same item is still showing as available on all other channels. Cross-channel overselling is a near-inevitable outcome when stock drops to 1–2 units across active multichannel listings.

Standard mitigation strategies

Strategy How it works Trade-off
Safety stock buffer Configure each channel to show 0 when actual stock hits N (e.g., reserve last 2 units) Prevents last-unit oversell; may lose some sales if items remain unsold
Webhook-based sync Tool subscribes to Shopify inventory_levels/update webhook for near-instant updates Reduces delay to seconds; webhook can fail silently if endpoint is down
Single-channel exclusivity List last 1-2 units on one channel only, rotate as stock depletes Manual overhead; requires active management
Increase sync frequency Switch to 5-minute or real-time sync if tool supports it Higher API usage; may approach Shopify rate limits

Failure Mode 5 — Draft Orders Do Not Reserve Inventory

Creating a draft order in Shopify Admin (to quote a B2B customer, process a phone order, or reserve units for a VIP) does not hold or reduce inventory in any way. The items remain fully available to all other customers on the storefront and all other channels.

Inventory is only deducted when the draft order is paid and converts to a fulfilled order. If you create a draft order for 3 units on Tuesday and the items sell through the storefront on Wednesday before the draft is paid, you will have oversold.

Workarounds for draft order inventory reservation

  • Manually reduce inventory — after creating a draft, go to the variant and subtract the quantity yourself. When the draft is paid, inventory deducts again (double-deduction) so you need to add it back by 1x when the draft is cancelled or add the adjustment when paid. Messy but functional for small volumes.
  • Set the variant to Out of Stock — if the draft is for the last available units, manually set inventory to 0 to block online sales, then restore if the draft falls through.
  • Use the draft's "reserve stock" feature (if available in your plan) — some Shopify Plus apps and B2B wholesale apps provide true inventory quoting that holds stock against a draft or quote.
  • Draft ordering time window — set a clear internal policy that drafts must be paid within X hours or they expire, and train staff to cancel unpaid drafts promptly so stock is mentally "unblocked."

Failure Mode 6 — Bundle and Kit Apps Deducting the Wrong SKU

Bundle and kit apps (Bundler, Fast Bundle, Rebundle, Infinite Options virtual bundles) create a logical bundle product that is sold as a single unit but fulfills from component SKUs. The inventory deduction path depends entirely on how the app is configured:

  • Deduct from bundle SKU only — some apps track inventory on the bundle variant and do not touch component variants. If you expect component inventory to decrease, it will not.
  • Deduct from component SKUs only — other apps deduct components but do not track the bundle itself. If you have a separate bundle SKU with its own stock count, it will never go down.
  • Double-deduction — if a bundle app deducts components AND your external inventory tool also deducts the same components, you get double the reduction per sale.
  • App only deducts at order creation — some apps use the orders/create webhook to trigger deduction. If the webhook fails (5-second timeout, app downtime), no deduction fires for that order.
How to Diagnose Bundle Deduction Issues
Place a test bundle order. Then check the inventory history (Admin → Products → component product → variant → View history) for the timestamp of your test order. You should see a deduction fired by the bundle app. If you see nothing, the app did not fire. If you see two deductions, it fired twice. Check the app's own order log and webhook delivery log to trace what happened.

Failure Mode 7 — POS Inventory Sync Delay

Shopify POS and the online store share inventory at the location level, and sync is near-real-time — but not instantaneous. Under normal conditions, a POS sale reflects in online inventory within 1–3 minutes. Under degraded conditions (poor network at the POS device, Shopify platform issues, high transaction volume), this delay can extend.

Common POS inventory sync failures

  • Different locations — the POS location and the online store are assigned to different inventory locations. They share product catalog but not inventory counts. Sales in one location do not reduce stock in the other. Fix: assign both POS and online orders to the same location, or configure "Inventory from multiple locations" correctly.
  • Offline mode POS — when a POS device loses internet connectivity and processes sales in offline mode, those sales are queued locally and synced to Shopify when connectivity returns. During the offline period, online inventory is not updated. Fix: check if "Inventory will be sold from this location even when you're out of stock" is enabled in POS settings — if not, offline sales can create negative inventory.
  • Allow selling when out of stock enabled on POS — if this setting is on, POS will complete a sale even when online inventory is at zero, creating a negative inventory count that then looks like a sync anomaly.

Verifying POS location assignment

Admin → Settings → Locations
→ check which location is assigned to online sales channel
→ check which location your POS devices use
→ they must match (or inventory management must bridge between them)

Failure Mode 8 — Headless Storefronts: Storefront API Does Not Enforce Inventory at Add-to-Cart

Headless Shopify implementations (Hydrogen, custom React/Next.js storefronts using the Storefront API) do not get automatic inventory enforcement at the cart stage. The key behaviors:

  • cartCreate and cartLinesAdd mutations do NOT validate whether the requested quantity is in stock. They add lines to the cart regardless of inventory state.
  • The cart can contain out-of-stock items and the API will not return an error at add-to-cart time.
  • Inventory is only enforced at payment time — the checkoutCompleteWith* mutations and the hosted checkout flow.
  • This means customers in headless storefronts can reach the payment screen, enter their card details, and only then learn that the item is out of stock — a much worse UX than catching it earlier.

Best practices for headless inventory handling

// Check availability BEFORE allowing add-to-cart
query ProductAvailability($id: ID!) {
  product(id: $id) {
    variants(first: 10) {
      nodes {
        id
        availableForSale
        quantityAvailable
        currentlyNotInStock
      }
    }
  }
}

// Also check after cartLinesAdd — the cart response includes availability
mutation AddToCart($cartId: ID!, $lines: [CartLineInput!]!) {
  cartLinesAdd(cartId: $cartId, lines: $lines) {
    cart {
      lines(first: 10) {
        nodes {
          merchandise {
            ... on ProductVariant {
              availableForSale
              quantityAvailable
            }
          }
        }
      }
    }
    userErrors {
      field
      message
    }
  }
}
Keep Frontend Inventory State Fresh
The Storefront API returns inventory at query time, not in real-time. For high-demand products, poll quantityAvailable every 30–60 seconds and display a "Only X left" count. Subscribe to the inventory_levels/update Admin API webhook server-side to push updates to your frontend in near-real-time.

Checking Inventory History in Shopify Admin

Shopify keeps a full audit log of every inventory change for each variant. This is the most reliable way to diagnose why stock changed unexpectedly:

  1. Admin → Products → click the product
  2. Scroll to the Variants section → click the specific variant
  3. Scroll to the Inventory section → look for "View history" or "Inventory history" link
  4. Each row shows: date/time, quantity change, new quantity, and the source (order number, app name, manual adjustment, or "Third-party app")
  5. Sort by most recent — look for unexpected upward adjustments (positive quantity changes that are not manual restocks or returns)
API Alternative for Bulk Diagnosis
For merchants with many SKUs or needing to audit across products: 
GET /admin/api/2024-01/events.json?filter=InventoryLevel&verb=adjusted&limit=250
This returns the recent inventory adjustment events across all products, including the source app or user. Filter by a date range around the incident time.

API Reference for Inventory Webhooks

If you are building custom integrations, subscribe to these webhooks to keep inventory state accurate:

Webhook Topic When it fires Use for
inventory_levels/update Any inventory change at any location Sync to external systems, push to frontend
inventory_levels/connect Inventory item connected to a location New location setup detection
inventory_levels/disconnect Inventory item removed from a location Location decommission tracking
inventory_items/update Inventory item metadata change (cost, tracking, etc.) Cost/tracking configuration sync
orders/create Order placed (inventory already deducted by this point) Post-sale audit; not for inventory updates
Webhook Reliability Note
Shopify automatically disables webhooks after 19 consecutive delivery failures. If your inventory_levels/update consumer endpoint was down, webhooks may have been silently paused. Check Admin → Settings → Notifications → Webhooks for disabled subscriptions. Re-enable and check for gaps in inventory sync during the disabled window.
