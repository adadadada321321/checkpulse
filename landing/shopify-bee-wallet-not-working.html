Shopify Bee Wallet (Banque Misr) Not Working — Wallet Not Appearing, OTP Not Received & Order Stuck Pending

Updated June 2026 · 7 failure modes · Egypt merchants · Banque Misr · CBE regulated · EGP only · Bank-issued wallet (any carrier)

Bee Wallet is Egypt's leading bank-issued mobile wallet, operated by Banque Misr — one of Egypt's two largest state banks. Unlike carrier wallets (Vodafone Cash, Orange Cash, WE Cash), Bee is not tied to a specific telecom SIM: any Egyptian mobile number can register. When Bee Wallet stops working on Shopify, the failure falls into one of seven patterns — the most distinct being the gap between having a Banque Misr bank account and having an active Bee wallet, which are two separate products. Identifying which failure applies is the only path to fixing it.

How Bee Wallet Works — and Why It Fails Differently From Carrier Wallets

Bee Wallet is a bank-issued mobile wallet operated by Banque Misr (Egypt's second-largest state bank, founded 1920) — it is not tied to any mobile carrier SIM card. Unlike Vodafone Cash (010 only), Orange Cash (012 only), e& Cash (011 only), or WE Cash (015 only), Bee Wallet works with any Egyptian mobile number regardless of the customer's carrier. Customers register their wallet via the Bee app (Android and iOS) using their national ID and a mobile number, then pre-fund the Bee wallet through the app, at Banque Misr branches, or through bank transfers. At checkout, the customer selects Bee Wallet, enters the mobile number linked to their Bee account, receives an OTP, and the purchase amount is deducted from the Bee wallet balance.

The most common Bee Wallet mistake: customers assume their Banque Misr bank account automatically enables Bee Wallet payments. It does not. A customer who has held a Banque Misr savings account, current account, or debit card for years but never downloaded the Bee app and activated their wallet will fail at checkout with a "wallet not found" error. The Bee wallet is a separate financial layer — it is not a direct charge to the Banque Misr bank account unless the customer has explicitly set up a bank-to-wallet top-up. This is the most misunderstood aspect of Bee Wallet for both merchants and customers, and it is the failure mode that generates the most confused support inquiries.

Bee Wallet is an asynchronous payment with the same callback dependency as carrier wallets. The customer approves via OTP, the Bee wallet is debited, and the gateway sends a confirmation to Shopify via a webhook callback. If the callback URL is misconfigured, the order stays stuck in pending even though the customer's Bee wallet has been charged. This is the most dangerous failure mode — a debited customer with an unfulfilled order and no automated reconciliation path.

Bee Wallet is EGP only and requires a compatible Egyptian payment gateway. Stripe and PayPal do not support Bee Wallet. Merchants must use an Egypt-focused gateway with explicit Banque Misr Bee Wallet API integration. Confirm with Paymob whether your plan tier includes Bee Wallet and enable it as a distinct payment method separate from carrier wallets and card payments.

7 Failure Modes Covered

  1. Bee Wallet option not appearing at checkout — gateway does not support or has not enabled it
  2. Customer has Banque Misr account but has never activated the Bee Wallet app
  3. OTP not received or times out before customer enters it
  4. Insufficient wallet balance — Bee wallet must be pre-funded separately from bank account
  5. CBE daily or monthly transaction limit exceeded
  6. Gateway callback not configured — order stuck in pending after customer pays
  7. Mobile number mismatch — customer enters a number not registered in the Bee app
1

Bee Wallet option not appearing at checkout — gateway does not support or has not enabled it

Merchant Issue

The most immediate reason Bee Wallet does not appear at Shopify checkout is that the merchant's payment gateway either does not support Bee Wallet at all, or supports it but has not enabled it. Bee Wallet requires an explicit API integration with Banque Misr's payment system — this is a separate integration from Vodafone Cash, Orange Cash, or card payment integrations. A merchant who enabled carrier wallets on Paymob cannot assume Bee Wallet is automatically included.

Stripe and PayPal do not support Bee Wallet. International gateways without Egyptian bank wallet integrations cannot offer it. Paymob is the primary Egyptian payment gateway with Bee Wallet integration, but availability and access depend on the merchant's Paymob plan tier. Some Paymob plan tiers include Bee Wallet; others require a plan upgrade or a separate activation request. Merchants who set up Paymob before Bee Wallet was added to their plan may not see it in the dashboard without contacting Paymob support.

A secondary issue affects merchants using older Paymob integrations. Legacy payment widget configurations may not automatically surface new payment methods that become available on the account — the merchant must log in to the Paymob dashboard and explicitly enable Bee Wallet as an active method rather than expecting it to appear on its own. Even if Bee Wallet is listed in the account as available, it needs to be toggled on in the payment methods configuration.

Merchants targeting the Egyptian market who have enabled carrier wallets should treat Bee Wallet as a distinct integration requiring its own confirmation and activation. Given that Bee Wallet is available to customers on any carrier — unlike carrier-specific wallets — enabling it opens access to a broad segment of Egyptian customers who prefer bank-issued wallets over carrier wallets.

How to fix

  1. Log in to your gateway dashboard and search for Bee Wallet as a payment method: In Paymob's merchant dashboard, navigate to the payment methods or integrations section and look for Bee Wallet (Banque Misr). It may be labeled as "Bee" or "Banque Misr Wallet." If it does not appear, contact Paymob support to confirm whether Bee Wallet is available on your current plan and what activation steps are required.
  2. Enable Bee Wallet as a distinct payment method: If Bee Wallet is listed as available in your dashboard, toggle it on in the payment methods settings. Save the configuration. The payment widget at Shopify checkout should update to include a Bee Wallet option alongside other Egyptian payment methods.
  3. Test the checkout to confirm Bee Wallet appears: After enabling, visit your Shopify checkout from a browser where you are not logged in as the store owner. Confirm that a Bee Wallet option appears in the payment methods list. If it does not appear despite enabling it in the gateway dashboard, check whether a payment widget update or re-embed is needed on the Shopify side.
  4. Confirm plan tier access if Bee Wallet is not in the dashboard: Contact Paymob support directly with your merchant account ID and ask: "Is Bee Wallet (Banque Misr) available on my current plan? If not, what plan tier includes it, and what is the activation process?" Paymob representatives can confirm integration availability and initiate activation if needed.
  5. Offer alternative Egyptian payment methods while resolving Bee Wallet access: While confirming Bee Wallet gateway availability, ensure customers have alternative Egyptian options at checkout — Vodafone Cash, Fawry, Meeza card, or bank transfer. This prevents checkout abandonment from customers who prefer bank wallet payments while the integration is being activated.
2

Customer has Banque Misr account but has never activated the Bee Wallet app

Customer Issue

This is the most distinctively Bee-specific failure mode — and the one that generates the most confused customer support inquiries. A customer who has a Banque Misr savings account, current account, or debit card for years may attempt to pay with Bee Wallet at checkout, enter their mobile number, and receive a "wallet not found" or "account not registered" error. They are certain they are a Banque Misr customer and do not understand why Bee Wallet fails. The reason is that the Bee digital wallet is a separate product from the Banque Misr bank account — having a bank account does not create a Bee wallet.

To use Bee Wallet, the customer must: download the Bee app (available for Android and iOS); register using their national ID, mobile number, and Banque Misr account details (or register as a standalone wallet user without a bank account); set a wallet PIN; and activate the wallet. Once registered, the wallet starts with a zero balance and must be loaded separately before purchases can be made. None of this happens automatically from holding a Banque Misr bank account.

When a gateway sends a wallet lookup for an unregistered mobile number to Banque Misr's Bee payment system, it receives a "wallet not found" response. This is displayed to the customer at checkout as a generic payment failure — "payment could not be completed," "account not recognized," or an error code — without clearly explaining that the issue is a missing wallet registration rather than a technical checkout error. Customers frequently contact the merchant's support team believing the store's checkout has a technical problem when the actual issue is that they have not activated their Bee wallet.

A related confusion occurs with customers who use the Banque Misr BM Online app for internet banking. BM Online is a separate product from the Bee app — a customer who actively uses BM Online for bank transfers and bill payments has not automatically activated a Bee wallet. The two apps are distinct, though Bee wallet may be linked from within the BM Online interface in some configurations.

How to fix

  1. Add activation guidance near the Bee Wallet checkout option: Display a clear note: "First time using Bee Wallet? Download the Bee app by Banque Misr (Android or iOS), register with your national ID, and activate your wallet before checkout. Having a Banque Misr bank account does not automatically create a Bee Wallet." This preempts the most common customer confusion before the payment attempt fails.
  2. Set up a cart abandonment email for Bee Wallet failures: If a customer abandons checkout after a Bee Wallet error, send a follow-up message explaining that Bee Wallet requires a one-time activation through the Bee app — it is separate from any existing Banque Misr bank account — and that they can return to complete their order once the wallet is activated and funded.
  3. Distinguish "wallet not registered" error codes from other failure codes in gateway logs: Your gateway returns specific error codes for "wallet not registered" versus "insufficient balance" versus "OTP failed." Identify the code for unregistered wallets in your gateway documentation. This ensures customer service routes unregistered wallet contacts to registration guidance — not to retry instructions or technical support escalations.
  4. Offer clear alternative payment methods for first-time Bee users: A customer who attempts Bee Wallet but is not yet registered should be able to complete their purchase immediately via another method. Display Fawry offline payment, Vodafone Cash, or bank transfer prominently on the payment error page so that the registration issue does not result in a lost sale.
  5. Point customers to the Bee app as the primary registration channel: The Bee app digital registration (without visiting a branch) is the fastest path for most customers. Include a link to the Bee app store page in any Bee Wallet guidance on your store's FAQ or checkout help text.
Merchant note: This failure mode is unique to Bee Wallet among Egyptian payment methods — carrier wallets like Vodafone Cash are more widely understood as requiring a separate wallet registration from a SIM card. Bee's association with Banque Misr — one of Egypt's most established banks — creates an expectation that it "just works" for anyone who banks with Banque Misr. Clear pre-checkout guidance about the separate activation step significantly reduces support volume for this issue.
3

OTP not received or times out before customer enters it

Merchant + Customer

Bee Wallet payments require OTP confirmation — after the customer enters their registered mobile number at checkout, Banque Misr's payment system sends a one-time password to that number. The customer must enter the OTP within the payment session window to complete the transaction. If the OTP does not arrive or expires before the customer enters it, the payment fails and the order is not placed.

Unlike carrier wallets where OTP delivery depends on a single carrier's SMS network, Bee Wallet OTPs are sent to the customer's registered mobile number regardless of carrier. However, OTP delivery can still fail: if the customer's phone has no signal, if SMS delivery is delayed during network congestion, if the customer's phone blocks unknown senders, or if the payment session timeout in the gateway configuration is too short. The default payment session timeout for Egyptian mobile wallet integrations is often set to 60–90 seconds — extending this to at least 3 minutes (180 seconds) significantly reduces OTP-related failures.

A Bee-specific OTP issue occurs when the customer's registered Bee number is a number they no longer actively use as their primary SIM. Because Bee Wallet is not tied to an active carrier SIM at the moment of payment — unlike Vodafone Cash which requires an active Vodafone SIM — a customer can register Bee with one number but later acquire a new primary number. If the registered Bee number is in an old phone, inactive, or has changed carriers, the OTP will not arrive on the device the customer is using to shop. The customer must update their registered number in the Bee app before retrying the payment.

On the merchant side, the OTP session timeout for Bee Wallet is typically configurable in the payment gateway's integration settings. Increasing this from the default to at least 3 minutes is the most effective merchant-side action to reduce OTP-related Bee Wallet failures.

How to fix

  1. Increase the payment session timeout in gateway settings: Log in to your gateway dashboard and find the session expiry or OTP timeout setting for the Bee Wallet integration. Increase it from the default (60–90 seconds) to at least 3 minutes (180 seconds). This gives customers more time to receive and enter the OTP, especially when SMS delivery is delayed.
  2. Advise customers to enter the number registered in the Bee app — not their current SIM number: Add a note near the Bee Wallet checkout option: "Enter the mobile number registered in your Bee app — this may differ from your current primary number. The OTP will be sent to your registered Bee number." This prevents customers from entering a different number expecting OTP delivery.
  3. Guide customers on how to update their Bee registered number: If a customer's registered Bee number is no longer their active number, they can update it in the Bee app settings or at a Banque Misr branch with national ID. Include this guidance in payment failure messaging so customers know what corrective action is available.
  4. Distinguish OTP timeout from OTP non-delivery in gateway logs: Session expired and OTP not delivered are different error codes. Timeout errors indicate the session duration needs to be extended. Non-delivery errors indicate a customer-side issue (no signal, inactive number, number mismatch). Knowing which occurred guides the correct resolution path when customers contact support.
  5. Offer a fallback payment method after OTP failure: If the OTP step fails, ensure customers can immediately switch to Fawry, Vodafone Cash, Meeza card, or bank transfer without restarting the entire order. Display alternatives clearly on the payment error screen.
4

Insufficient wallet balance — Bee wallet must be pre-funded separately from bank account

Customer Issue

Bee Wallet is a pre-funded mobile wallet — the customer must load funds into the Bee wallet before it can be used for purchases. If the Bee wallet balance is less than the order total, the payment is declined at Banque Misr's payment system level before the OTP step completes. The customer receives an "insufficient funds" or "payment declined" error that is frequently mistaken for a technical checkout failure.

This is the most confusing failure mode for Banque Misr customers who are not yet familiar with how Bee Wallet works. A customer with EGP 5,000 in their Banque Misr savings account, who also has a Bee wallet with a zero balance, will receive an "insufficient funds" error when attempting to pay via Bee — even though their bank account has more than enough funds. The Bee wallet and the Banque Misr bank account are separate balances. The payment at checkout draws from the Bee wallet balance only, not from any linked bank account, unless the customer has set up automatic or manual wallet top-ups from their bank account through the Bee app.

Customers can load their Bee wallet through: the Bee app using a bank card or bank transfer from their Banque Misr or other Egyptian bank account; at Banque Misr branches and ATMs; or through instant transfer from the Bee app if linked to a bank account. The Bee app bank-to-wallet transfer is typically the fastest method for existing Banque Misr customers — it does not require visiting a branch. However, the transfer must be completed before the checkout attempt, not during it.

For merchants, insufficient balance declines appear in gateway logs as a balance-related decline code originating from Banque Misr's system. There is no merchant-side action to resolve a specific customer's balance shortfall — the customer must top up the Bee wallet and retry. Merchants can reduce this friction by making the pre-funding requirement and the order total visible before the customer enters their Bee number at checkout.

How to fix

  1. Display the order total and a pre-funding reminder before the Bee Wallet OTP step: Show the required amount and a message: "This amount will be charged to your Bee wallet balance — make sure your Bee wallet is topped up to this amount before selecting this option." Confirming balance requirements before the OTP sequence prevents wasted payment attempts that fail due to insufficient Bee wallet balance.
  2. Clarify that Bee wallet balance is separate from Banque Misr bank account balance on the payment failure screen: If Bee Wallet is declined due to insufficient funds, display: "Your Bee wallet balance was insufficient. Note: Bee wallet funds are separate from your Banque Misr bank account — top up your Bee wallet via the Bee app (transfer from bank account), at a Banque Misr branch or ATM, then retry." This prevents customers from being confused about why they have "no funds" when their bank account is healthy.
  3. Offer alternative payment methods for high-value orders: If your average order value is high relative to typical mobile wallet pre-funded balances, ensure bank transfer is prominently available at checkout. Bank transfer draws directly from the customer's account and does not require pre-funding a separate wallet.
  4. Add a balance check note for higher-priced products: For products priced above EGP 1,000–2,000, add a note near the buy button: "Paying with Bee Wallet? Check your Bee app balance equals or exceeds the order total before checkout." This sets expectations before the payment attempt and prevents balance failures at the final step.
Merchant note: The bank-account/wallet balance gap is uniquely prominent for Bee Wallet because customers naturally associate Banque Misr with their bank funds. Consider a short explanation in your store FAQ: "Bee Wallet requires a separately loaded balance — it is not a direct charge to your Banque Misr bank account." This one sentence prevents a large share of Bee Wallet balance-related support contacts.
5

CBE daily or monthly transaction limit exceeded

Customer Issue

The Central Bank of Egypt sets regulatory transaction limits for all mobile wallets operating in Egypt, including Bee Wallet. These limits apply to all Bee users regardless of their wallet balance and are enforced at Banque Misr's payment system level. When a customer's daily or monthly transaction limit has been reached, their payment is declined even if their Bee wallet has sufficient funds.

The CBE's tiered limit structure for Bee Wallet is based on the customer's verification level. Unverified wallets (basic registration) have lower daily and monthly transaction limits and a lower maximum balance ceiling. Fully verified wallets — requiring extended KYC completed through the Bee app or at a Banque Misr branch — have significantly higher limits. A Bee customer with an unverified wallet who has made multiple purchases earlier in the day may find that a subsequent order total exceeds the remaining daily limit even if the wallet balance is sufficient.

From the merchant's perspective, a CBE limit exceeded error and a balance error can appear similar in the gateway response — both are declined at the Banque Misr system level before the transaction completes. The distinction matters for customer guidance: a balance error requires topping up the wallet, while a limit error requires either waiting for the daily limit to reset (midnight reset) or completing wallet verification to access a higher tier. Providing the wrong guidance wastes the customer's effort and delays their return to purchase.

Unlike carrier wallets where limit errors are less commonly encountered for typical e-commerce orders, Bee Wallet users who use the wallet actively for daily purchases — Banque Misr customers who route multiple transactions through Bee — may hit daily limits more frequently. For merchants selling higher-value items, bank transfer is the most practical alternative since it carries no CBE mobile wallet tier restrictions.

How to fix

  1. Advise customers on Bee wallet tier upgrades: Customers who encounter CBE limit errors should be advised to upgrade their Bee wallet to the verified tier. Full verification can typically be completed through the Bee app (biometric or document upload) or at a Banque Misr branch with a national ID. A verified wallet has substantially higher daily and monthly transaction limits.
  2. Display limit guidance for high-value cart totals: If your store has products above EGP 1,500–2,000, add a note near the Bee Wallet payment option for high carts: "Note: Bee Wallet transaction limits apply based on your wallet verification tier. For higher-value orders, use bank transfer or complete Bee wallet verification first." This sets expectations before the payment attempt fails.
  3. Offer bank transfer as the primary alternative for large orders: Egyptian bank transfers carry no CBE mobile wallet tier limits and accommodate any order size. For order values where CBE limit errors are frequent with unverified wallets, make bank transfer prominently available with clear instructions.
  4. Distinguish limit errors from balance errors in customer service responses: When a customer contacts you after a declined Bee Wallet payment, the gateway error code tells you whether it is a balance shortfall or a regulatory limit. A balance error requires topping up. A limit error requires waiting for the daily reset or upgrading the wallet verification tier. Providing the correct guidance avoids sending customers on a circular troubleshooting loop.
Merchant note: CBE mobile wallet limits are reviewed periodically and may increase as Egypt's digital payments ecosystem expands. The tier structure described here reflects 2026 CBE guidelines. Monitor CBE announcements and check with Paymob for any limit updates that may affect your Bee Wallet checkout configuration.
6

Gateway callback not configured — order stuck in pending after customer pays

Merchant Issue

A Shopify order stuck in pending status after a Bee Wallet payment is almost always caused by a missing or broken webhook callback URL. Bee Wallet is an asynchronous payment method — the payment flow is: (1) customer selects Bee Wallet and enters their registered mobile number; (2) Banque Misr sends the customer an OTP; (3) customer approves the payment via OTP; (4) Banque Misr's payment system notifies the payment gateway that the payment was approved; (5) the gateway notifies Shopify via a webhook callback URL; (6) Shopify marks the order as paid. If step 5 fails because the callback URL is misconfigured or unreachable, Shopify never receives the payment confirmation and the order stays in pending indefinitely.

This is the most dangerous Bee Wallet failure mode because it results in the customer having paid — their Bee wallet is debited, they may receive a Banque Misr payment confirmation — while the merchant's Shopify system shows no completed payment. Without a process to cross-check pending orders against gateway transaction records, the merchant may never fulfill the order, leading to customer disputes and potential chargebacks from customers who were charged but received no goods.

The most common cause of callback failures is a localhost or development URL remaining in the production gateway configuration. During development and testing, merchants configure callback URLs pointing to local addresses (localhost, 127.0.0.1, ngrok tunnels). When the store goes live, if the callback URL is not updated to the production store's HTTPS address, all payment confirmations from Banque Misr's system go to an unreachable address and fail silently. No error appears in the Shopify admin — only the gateway's transaction logs show repeated failed callback attempts.

Callback failures also occur when the Shopify store domain changes after the initial gateway setup — a custom domain is added, HTTPS is enforced, or the store migrates to a new URL. If the callback URL in the gateway dashboard still points to the old domain or an HTTP address, callbacks fail silently for all Bee Wallet payments indefinitely until the URL is updated.

How to fix

  1. Check and update the callback URL in your gateway dashboard immediately: Log in to your Paymob (or gateway) dashboard and find the webhook or callback URL configuration for the Bee Wallet integration. The callback URL must be the production Shopify payment confirmation endpoint — a publicly accessible HTTPS URL matching your current live store domain. It must not be a localhost address, development tunnel, or HTTP URL.
  2. Verify the callback URL is reachable from the public internet: Use curl or a webhook testing service to confirm that a POST request to the configured callback URL returns a 200 response. A non-200 response (404, redirect, or timeout) means payment confirmations cannot reach Shopify. Update the URL and retest until a 200 is confirmed.
  3. Manually reconcile currently stuck pending orders: Log in to your payment gateway's merchant dashboard and review completed Bee Wallet transactions for the past 7 days. Match each completed transaction against pending Shopify orders. For each matched order, manually mark it as paid in Shopify admin and fulfill it. Contact gateway support if a settlement reconciliation is needed for any order where payment was debited but not confirmed.
  4. Set up a monitoring alert for orders stuck in pending: Configure a notification (via a Shopify app or scheduled check) to alert you when any order has been in pending status for more than 30 minutes. Pending orders that do not resolve quickly indicate a callback failure requiring immediate attention before they become customer disputes.
  5. Complete a real end-to-end test after configuration: After updating the callback URL, complete a real Bee Wallet payment for a small test amount through your live Shopify checkout. Confirm: OTP is received and entered, payment is approved, the Shopify order updates from pending to paid within 60 seconds, and the transaction appears as settled in gateway logs. Only a successful real-money test confirms the callback is working correctly.
Critical: Do not ignore Shopify orders stuck in pending after Bee Wallet payments. These represent real money deducted from the customer's Bee wallet with no fulfillment. Check your gateway transaction logs daily and cross-reference against pending Shopify orders. Every unmatched Bee Wallet payment is a potential dispute requiring manual reconciliation.
7

Mobile number mismatch — customer enters a number not registered in the Bee app

Customer Issue

Because Bee Wallet is not tied to a specific carrier SIM, customers can register their Bee wallet with any mobile number — and they may later acquire additional phone numbers or switch their primary number without updating the Bee app registration. At checkout, when the customer enters a mobile number that does not match the number registered in their Bee wallet, Banque Misr's payment system returns a "wallet not found" or "account not registered" error, even if the customer has an active Bee wallet with sufficient balance on a different number.

This failure mode is unique to Bee Wallet compared to carrier wallets. Vodafone Cash users enter the number of their active Vodafone SIM — there is a direct link between the SIM and the wallet. Bee Wallet users can have their registered number in a different phone, on a different carrier, or even in a number they now rarely use as primary. A customer who shops online primarily from their 011 number (e& SIM) but registered Bee with their 010 number (Vodafone SIM) will receive a wallet error when they enter 011 at checkout — they must enter 010 to reach their Bee wallet.

A related issue occurs when the customer has recently changed their registered mobile number in the Bee app. If the number update is still processing, or if the customer is trying to use the old number force of habit, the lookup will fail. Banque Misr requires a grace period or verification step when changing the registered number in Bee to prevent fraud.

From the merchant's perspective, a number mismatch error and a "wallet not registered" error for a genuinely unregistered customer can appear similar. The distinction matters: a genuinely unregistered customer needs to activate Bee Wallet from scratch. A customer with a number mismatch already has a Bee wallet — they just need to enter the correct number or update their Bee app registration.

How to fix

  1. Advise customers to check which number is registered in their Bee app before checkout: Add a note near the Bee Wallet payment option: "Enter the mobile number registered in your Bee app — open the Bee app and check your profile to confirm which number is linked to your wallet. This may differ from your current primary SIM." This preempts the most common number mismatch before the payment attempt fails.
  2. Include number verification guidance in payment failure messaging: If a customer receives a "wallet not found" error after entering their number, display: "Number not recognized: Make sure you're entering the mobile number registered in your Bee app (check under your Bee app profile). This may be a different number from your current SIM." This helps customers self-diagnose instead of assuming the checkout has a technical error.
  3. Direct customers to update their Bee registered number if needed: Customers who have changed their primary phone number can update their Bee registered number through the Bee app settings or at a Banque Misr branch. The update requires national ID verification. If a customer needs to update their Bee number, advise them to complete the update and then return to retry the purchase — and offer an alternative payment method for immediate checkout.
  4. Distinguish number mismatch from genuinely unregistered wallet in customer service: A customer who has an active Bee wallet but entered the wrong number is a different support case from a customer who has never activated Bee. The resolution for a mismatch is entering the correct registered number — not going through the full Bee activation process. Correct routing of this support inquiry prevents unnecessary escalation and improves customer experience.
  5. Consider adding a Bee wallet help link near the payment option: Link to a short help article or FAQ entry explaining how to find the number registered in the Bee app. Even a single sentence — "Not sure which number to enter? Open Bee app → Profile → Your registered mobile number" — directly resolves the most common source of this failure.

Bee Wallet Shopify Integration Diagnostic Checklist

Gateway confirmed to support Bee Wallet on your plan — verified with Paymob that Bee Wallet (Banque Misr) is available on your plan tier and has been enabled as a distinct payment method separate from carrier wallets and card payments.

Bee Wallet enabled in gateway dashboard — Bee Wallet is toggled on as an active payment method in the merchant's gateway settings, confirmed with a test that the option appears at checkout for real customers.

Production callback/webhook URL configured and reachable — the callback URL in the gateway dashboard is the production Shopify store URL (HTTPS, current domain), not a localhost or development address, and returns a 200 response to a test POST request.

OTP session timeout set to at least 3 minutes — payment session expiry for Bee Wallet OTP is configured to 180 seconds or more to accommodate SMS delivery delays and customer response time.

Checkout guidance explains that Banque Misr bank account ≠ Bee wallet — a note near the Bee Wallet payment option clarifies that a separate Bee app registration and wallet top-up are required, even for existing Banque Misr bank customers.

Checkout currency is EGP — store is priced in EGP, or Shopify Markets is configured to present EGP to Egyptian customers. Bee Wallet processes EGP only and cannot be used for non-EGP orders.

Number registration guidance available to customers — a help note near the Bee Wallet option explains that customers must enter the mobile number registered in their Bee app, which may differ from their current primary SIM number.

Pre-funding requirement communicated clearly — customers are informed before the OTP step that Bee wallet balance is separate from any Banque Misr bank account and must be topped up via the Bee app, at a branch, or through ATM before checkout.
