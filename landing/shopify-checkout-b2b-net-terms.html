Shopify B2B Checkout Troubleshooting

Shopify B2B Net Terms Not Working — Net 30/60/90 Not Showing, Invoice Not Generated, Payment Reminder Failures

Shopify Plus B2B net payment terms (Net 30, Net 60, Net 90) have four hard dependencies that must all be true simultaneously. When even one is missing — wrong gateway, unconfigured company account, overdue balance — the net terms option silently disappears at checkout with no error message anywhere in Shopify Admin.

Updated June 2026 Affects: Shopify Plus B2B stores, wholesale merchants, enterprise buyers
Net terms are a Shopify Plus–only feature and require Shopify Payments. Merchants on lower plans or using third-party gateways (Stripe, Authorize.net, PayPal) cannot enable net terms for B2B buyers regardless of how the company account is configured. This single constraint silently blocks net terms for the majority of merchants who try to set them up.
Architecture

How Shopify B2B Net Terms Work — The Full Invoice Lifecycle

Net terms on Shopify are a deferred payment flow. Unlike standard checkout where payment is captured at order placement, net terms defer capture to a future due date. Each stage of the lifecycle can break independently.

Stage 1 — Prerequisites
Four Hard Dependencies
Shopify Plus plan + Shopify Payments as active gateway + company account with net terms configured + zero overdue invoices. All four must be true. Missing any one silently removes the net terms option at checkout.
Stage 2 — Checkout
Net Terms Appears at Payment Step
Authenticated B2B buyer selects their net terms option (Net 30, Net 60, Net 90) at the payment step. Order is placed with deferred payment — no card is charged. An invoice due date is calculated and assigned to the order.
Stage 3 — Invoice
PDF Invoice Generated and Emailed
Shopify generates a PDF invoice and emails it to the company's billing contact. The invoice shows the order total, due date, and payment instructions. This step commonly fails if the billing contact email is incorrect or the invoice goes to spam.
Stage 4 — Reminders
Automated Payment Reminders
Shopify sends automated reminder emails as the due date approaches (typically 7 days before and on the due date). Buyers with IT email filters often miss these. There is no built-in Shopify Admin dashboard showing which reminders were sent.
Stage 5 — Collection
Payment Captured on Due Date
On the due date, Shopify attempts to capture payment via Shopify Payments. If the buyer pays manually (bank transfer, check), the merchant marks the invoice paid in Admin. Overdue invoices block the buyer from placing new orders.
Stage 6 — Block
Overdue Invoice Blocks Future Orders
When an invoice passes its due date without payment, Shopify automatically blocks the company from placing new net-terms orders at checkout. The block is immediate, silent to the merchant, and only lifted when the overdue invoice is marked paid.

Diagnosis Checklist

5-Step B2B Net Terms Diagnosis

  • 1
    Verify all four prerequisites in Shopify Admin Go to Admin → Settings → Plan: confirm Shopify Plus is active. Go to Admin → Settings → Payments: confirm Shopify Payments is the active gateway and no third-party processor is primary. Go to Admin → Customers → Companies → [Company] → Payment Terms: confirm net terms (Net 30, Net 60, or Net 90) are configured and not blank. Go to Admin → Customers → Companies → [Company] → Orders: check for any orders with a past-due date and an unpaid status. Any one of these failing silently removes net terms from checkout.
  • 2
    Check the company's billing contact email Go to Admin → Customers → Companies → [Company] → Contacts. Verify at least one contact has the Billing or Ordering permission and that the email address is current. Shopify sends the invoice PDF and payment reminders to the billing contact, not necessarily the email used during the last checkout session. If the billing contact email is outdated or incorrect, all invoice and reminder emails are silently lost.
  • 3
    Verify the order's payment section shows net terms with a due date Go to Admin → Orders → [Order] → scroll to the Payment section. A correctly applied net-terms order shows "Net 30" (or your configured terms) with a specific due date listed. If the payment section shows "Pending" without a due date, the net terms were not applied during checkout — the buyer may have started checkout before the company account was configured. The buyer must restart checkout for the terms to apply. You cannot backdate net terms onto a completed order.
  • 4
    Check the buyer's spam folder for invoice and reminder emails Shopify sends B2B invoice and reminder emails from its own email infrastructure (typically a shopify.com or myshopify.com sending domain). Corporate IT environments with strict email filters frequently quarantine these. Ask the buyer to check spam, junk, and quarantine folders. If emails are consistently filtered, the buyer's IT team needs to allowlist Shopify's sending IP range or the shopify.com domain. Shopify Admin has no delivery log for these emails — you cannot confirm delivery from Admin.
  • 5
    Test the net terms flow from the buyer's account in a separate browser session Log into your store using the B2B buyer's company account credentials (or create a test buyer account under the company). Add products to cart, proceed to checkout, and verify the net terms option appears at the payment step. If it does not appear, one of the four prerequisites is failing — go back to step 1. If net terms appear in the test but the real buyer cannot see them, the issue is session-specific: the buyer may have an existing checkout session from before net terms were configured. Ask them to clear their cart, log out, log back in, and start a fresh checkout.
Deep Dive

Three Failure Modes in Detail

Deep Dive 1

The Shopify Payments Lock-In — Why Third-Party Gateways Break Net Terms

This is the most surprising constraint in Shopify B2B net terms: the feature is only available if Shopify Payments is your active payment gateway. Merchants using Stripe (direct integration), Authorize.net, PayPal, Braintree, Adyen, or any other third-party gateway cannot enable net terms — the option simply does not appear at checkout regardless of how the company account is configured.

The reason is architectural. Shopify's net terms flow relies on Shopify's internal payment infrastructure to handle deferred capture, invoice generation, and the payment collection workflow when the invoice comes due. These capabilities are tightly coupled to Shopify Payments and are not exposed to third-party gateway integrations through Shopify's payment APIs.

Admin → Settings → Payments Verify: Shopify Payments = Active (green checkmark) If third-party gateway is primary → net terms are silently unavailable Fix: Migrate B2B payment processing to Shopify Payments Alternative: Manage net terms outside Shopify (QuickBooks, NetSuite, Xero)

Merchants who switch gateways during a store migration — even briefly — discover that all previously configured net terms options silently stop appearing at checkout. If your B2B buyers recently lost access to net terms without any other change, verify the payment gateway first. The company account configuration is unchanged but the gateway dependency is gone.

Deep Dive 2

Invoice Not Generated — The Draft Order Gap

Shopify automatically generates a PDF invoice for net-terms orders placed through the native B2B checkout flow. However, many B2B merchants use draft orders — created manually by a sales rep or via the Admin — for large or custom orders. Draft orders follow a different code path and do not automatically generate invoices even when net terms are selected.

For a draft order with net terms, the merchant must manually send the invoice from the order detail page. Go to Admin → Orders → [Draft Order] → click "Send invoice." This manually triggers the invoice email with the payment due date. If this step is skipped, the buyer receives only the draft order email (which does not include the invoice PDF or the payment terms language) and may not know a payment is due or when.

Native B2B checkout flow → invoice auto-generated and emailed ✓ Draft order + net terms → must manually click "Send invoice" in Admin Check: Admin → Orders → [Order] → Timeline — look for "Invoice sent" event If Timeline shows no invoice event → buyer has not received an invoice

A second invoice generation gap: if the billing contact's email address is wrong, Shopify records the invoice as sent (the event appears in the order Timeline) but the email bounces silently. Shopify Admin does not show bounced emails. Always verify the billing contact email directly in the company account before concluding an invoice was successfully delivered.

Deep Dive 3

The Overdue Block — How One Late Invoice Freezes a Buyer's Account

Shopify's overdue invoice block is a cascade risk: a single invoice that goes unpaid past its due date blocks the entire company from placing any new net-terms orders. The block applies company-wide, not just to the individual who placed the overdue order. If a company has five active buyers, all five are blocked until the overdue invoice is resolved.

The block is immediate and silent. The merchant receives no notification in Shopify Admin that a company has been blocked. The buyer sees a checkout error — but the error message does not specify that overdue invoices are the cause. From the buyer's perspective, their account appears broken; from the merchant's perspective, Admin looks normal.

Admin → Customers → Companies → [Company] → Orders Filter by: Payment Status = Overdue Find orders with due date in the past and status not Paid Resolve: collect payment → mark invoice paid → buyer access restored immediately

To prevent the cascade: set up a proactive dunning workflow. Shopify's built-in reminders fire at 7 days before and on the due date — but corporate AR departments often need 14–30 days notice to process a payment. Consider sending a manual email reminder 30 days before the due date for large invoices, in addition to relying on Shopify's automated reminders. Some merchants use Shopify Flow to trigger custom reminder sequences based on invoice age and amount.

FAQ

Shopify B2B Net Terms — Common Questions

Why are Shopify B2B net payment terms not showing at checkout?
Four prerequisites must all be true simultaneously: (1) Shopify Plus plan active; (2) Shopify Payments as the active payment gateway; (3) the buyer's company account has net terms configured in Admin → Customers → Companies → [Company] → Payment Terms; (4) the company has no overdue invoices. Missing any one silently removes the net terms option from checkout with no error message. The most common cause is Shopify Payments not being the active gateway — any third-party processor blocks net terms entirely.
Why is Shopify B2B not generating an invoice after a net terms order?
Shopify auto-generates invoices only for orders placed through the native B2B checkout with net terms applied. Draft orders require manually clicking "Send invoice" in Admin. If net terms were configured after a buyer started their checkout session, the terms may not have applied — verify the order's Payment section shows "Net 30" with a specific due date. If billing contact email is wrong, the invoice is sent but bounces silently (Shopify records it as sent; no bounce notification appears in Admin).
Why are Shopify B2B payment due reminders not being sent?
Reminders are sent to the billing contact on the company account, not necessarily the checkout email. Check Admin → Customers → Companies → [Company] → Contacts for a contact with Billing permission and a current email. Corporate IT email filters frequently quarantine Shopify reminder emails. Shopify Admin has no delivery log — you cannot confirm reminders were received from Admin. Ask buyers to check spam and have their IT team allowlist Shopify's sending domain.
Why is a Shopify B2B company blocked from placing new orders?
Two causes: (1) An overdue invoice — one unpaid invoice past its due date blocks all new net-terms orders company-wide immediately. Go to Admin → Customers → Companies → [Company] → Orders, filter for Overdue status, collect payment or mark paid to unblock. (2) Credit limit exceeded — the company's outstanding net-terms balance (including all current, not-yet-due invoices) has reached or exceeded their credit limit. Adjust the limit in Admin → Customers → Companies → [Company] → Payment Terms → Credit Limit.
Can Shopify B2B net terms be used without Shopify Payments?
No. This is a hard platform constraint. Shopify net terms require Shopify Payments as the active gateway — third-party processors (Stripe direct, Authorize.net, PayPal, Braintree, Adyen) cannot be used with Shopify net terms. Merchants who need net terms without Shopify Payments must handle deferred payment outside Shopify using draft orders plus external invoicing (QuickBooks, Xero, NetSuite) or B2B middleware that bridges Shopify orders to an external AR system.
