Shopify Splitit Not Working — Not Showing, Hold Declined, Order Not Created

Updated June 2026 · 8 failure modes · Merchants & customers

Splitit is fundamentally different from every other BNPL on Shopify — it uses your customer's existing Visa or Mastercard credit card with no new credit check. That unique model creates failure modes that don't exist with Afterpay, Klarna, or Affirm: authorization holds being blocked by banks, debit cards silently ineligible, and holds expiring during long installment plans. This guide covers all 8 failure modes with exact fixes.

Why Splitit Fails Differently From Every Other BNPL

Splitit doesn't issue new credit. It places an authorization hold on the customer's existing Visa or Mastercard for the full purchase amount, then captures each installment monthly. No credit check, no application — but the customer must have the full order total available as unused credit on their card.

This creates unique failure modes: debit cards and prepaid cards are completely ineligible (holds don't work), the customer's bank can block an unfamiliar large-amount hold, the hold can expire before the plan ends, and a card replacement mid-plan breaks all future installments.

As a merchant, you're paid at checkout (or shortly after) — Splitit absorbs the installment risk. Failed later installments don't affect your revenue, but they can trigger plan terminations that look like order cancellations in your dashboard.

8 Failure Modes Covered

  1. Splitit not showing at checkout
  2. Authorization hold declined by bank
  3. Card not eligible — debit, prepaid, or insufficient credit
  4. Cart amount changed after hold was initiated
  5. Order not created in Shopify after approval
  6. Monthly installment charge failed
  7. 3DS / SCA authentication failure during plan setup
  8. Refund or cancellation not releasing the hold
1

Splitit Not Showing at Shopify Checkout

Merchant-side

Splitit is a third-party app — it is not built into Shopify Payments. This means it can disappear silently from checkout for configuration reasons that Shopify's built-in payment options never experience.

The most common reason Splitit vanishes: the app is installed but sandbox/test mode is still active. Splitit's integration requires switching from test API credentials to live API credentials before going live — if the merchant never completed this step, Splitit hides itself at checkout to avoid customers attempting real purchases against a test environment.

Other common causes: the Splitit app was not installed from the Shopify App Store at all (a previous developer may have removed it during a theme migration); the cart total falls below Splitit's minimum order threshold (typically $36); the customer's country is not a supported Splitit market; or a theme update broke the Splitit payment widget's rendering logic.

How to Diagnose

  1. Check app installation: Shopify Admin → Apps → confirm Splitit appears and is active
  2. Check API mode: In the Splitit app settings, confirm Live API credentials are entered (not sandbox/test credentials)
  3. Check payment settings: Settings → Payments → Alternative payment providers → confirm Splitit shows as active
  4. Test cart value: Add a product over $50 to the cart and test checkout — rules out minimum order threshold
  5. Test in incognito: Open a private browser window and go through checkout — rules out browser extension conflicts
  6. Check country: Splitit is primarily available in US, UK, Canada, Australia, and select EU markets — test with a supported country address
  7. Review app logs: In the Splitit merchant dashboard, check for any configuration errors or warnings
2

Authorization Hold Declined by Bank

Customer-side

When a customer selects Splitit at checkout, Splitit immediately attempts to place an authorization hold on their credit card for the full purchase amount — not the first installment. This is how Splitit secures all future payments without requiring a new credit check.

Banks can decline this hold even when the customer has sufficient available credit. The most common reason: the bank's fraud system sees an unusually large authorization from an unfamiliar payment processor (Splitit's processor, not the merchant), flags it as suspicious, and declines. This is especially common for first-time Splitit users and for large order amounts.

A secondary reason: the customer's available credit is lower than the purchase amount even though their credit limit is high. Existing balances, pending authorizations from other purchases, or previous Splitit plans with active holds can reduce available credit below the threshold needed for the new hold.

Fix Steps for Customers

  1. Check available credit: Log into your credit card's mobile app and confirm the available credit (not the credit limit) exceeds the order total
  2. Contact your bank: Call the number on the back of your card and ask them to approve a Splitit authorization — describe it as an installment payment service
  3. Try a different card: If you have a second Visa or Mastercard credit card, try it — some banks are more Splitit-friendly than others
  4. Reduce hold conflicts: If you have an existing Splitit plan on the same card, that plan's hold reduces available credit — contact Splitit to check your existing hold amount
Merchant note: You cannot override bank hold declines. If customers report Splitit failures after selecting it, offer Afterpay or Klarna as alternatives — they don't require a full-amount hold and use their own credit assessment.
3

Card Not Eligible — Debit, Prepaid, or Insufficient Credit

Customer-side

Splitit only works with Visa and Mastercard credit cards. It does not support debit cards, prepaid cards, virtual cards, or gift cards — because authorization holds on these card types either don't work reliably for multi-month durations or aren't supported at all.

This causes a significant support issue: many customers have a Visa or Mastercard that says "Debit" on the front. These look identical to credit cards but are fundamentally different products. A Visa Debit card will be declined at Splitit's card eligibility check before any hold attempt. The error message customers see is often generic ("card not accepted") and doesn't explain why.

American Express cards are supported in some Splitit markets but not all. Depending on the merchant's Splitit configuration and the customer's country, Amex may or may not appear as an option.

Insufficient available credit is a related issue: even if the card is eligible (a true Visa credit card), Splitit checks that the customer has at least the full purchase amount available as unused credit. A card with a $2,000 limit but a $1,800 balance cannot support a $500 Splitit purchase because only $200 is available.

How to Identify the Card Type

  1. Look at the card front: Check for the word "DEBIT" or "CREDIT" printed on the card — this determines eligibility
  2. Check with your bank: Call or check online banking — some banks issue "Visa Debit" cards that look like credit cards but are debit cards
  3. Check available credit: Log into your card's app and confirm the available credit (not just the credit limit) is higher than the order total
  4. Use a qualifying card: Only Visa or Mastercard credit cards with sufficient available credit will work with Splitit
4

Cart Amount Changed After Hold Was Initiated

Merchant + Customer

When a customer enters the Splitit checkout flow, Splitit locks in the cart total and begins the process of setting up the authorization hold for that specific amount. If the cart total changes between when the customer initiated Splitit and when the hold is confirmed — for any reason — the plan setup fails.

Common causes of mid-checkout amount changes: shipping rates that recalculate after the customer enters their address; discount codes applied after Splitit was selected; tax adjustments based on the shipping address; or a cart-level discount app that modifies prices during checkout.

This failure is more common on Shopify stores with complex discount logic, carrier-calculated shipping, or apps that modify cart prices dynamically. The solution is to ensure all prices are finalized before the customer reaches the payment selection step.

Fix for Merchants

  1. Review discount apps: Audit any Shopify apps that modify cart prices — check if they apply changes during the payment step rather than at cart view
  2. Shipping rate sequence: Ensure carrier-calculated shipping rates are presented before the customer reaches payment selection — not after
  3. Test checkout flow: Run a test order using Splitit with a cart that has a discount code and carrier shipping — observe whether the amount stays consistent
  4. Customer instructions: For customers who hit this, instruct them to refresh checkout, confirm the final total, then reselect Splitit
5

Order Not Created in Shopify After Splitit Approval

Merchant-side

This is the most financially risky failure mode. The customer's card has an active authorization hold (placed by Splitit) but no order exists in Shopify Admin. The customer believes the purchase completed; the merchant sees nothing.

This happens when the webhook or redirect from Splitit back to Shopify fails after the hold is placed. The hold is real — the customer's credit card statement will show the authorization — but Shopify never received the signal to create the order. Causes include: network timeout during the webhook delivery, a misconfigured webhook endpoint in the Splitit app settings, or a Shopify platform hiccup that caused the webhook to be dropped.

How to Investigate

  1. Check Splitit merchant dashboard: Log in and look for the transaction — if it shows "approved" or "hold placed" on their end, the failure is in the Shopify webhook delivery
  2. Check Shopify webhooks: Admin → Settings → Notifications → Webhooks — look for recent delivery failures on the Splitit endpoint URL
  3. Contact Splitit merchant support: Provide the customer's email and approximate checkout time — they can look up the transaction and void the hold if no order should be created
  4. Do not ask customer to retry: A second attempt will place a second authorization hold on the customer's card — confirm whether the first hold exists before any retry
Critical: Never instruct the customer to try again without first checking whether Splitit recorded the transaction. A second checkout creates a second hold, potentially tying up twice the purchase amount on the customer's card for days.
6

Monthly Installment Charge Failed

Customer-side

Splitit captures installments monthly by charging against the original authorization hold. The most common reason a later installment fails: the customer's card expired. If the card used at checkout has an expiration date that falls during the installment plan (e.g., a 6-month plan started on a card expiring in month 3), Splitit loses the ability to capture future installments.

A related failure: the customer replaced their card due to loss or fraud. A new card number invalidates the existing authorization hold — Splitit cannot charge a different card number than the one that approved the original hold.

Less commonly: the customer's bank reduced their credit limit, making the existing hold now exceed their available credit, causing future captures to be declined. Or the bank placed a block on the account due to suspected fraud on other transactions.

As a merchant, this typically does not affect you — Splitit pays merchants upfront or in guaranteed installments, and absorbs the customer-side collection risk. However, you may receive a notification from Splitit if they need to terminate a plan, which can look like an order reversal in your dashboard.

Fix Steps for Customers

  1. Log into your Splitit account: Check the plan details and whether a payment failure notification was sent
  2. Update your card in Splitit: If your card expired or was replaced, contact Splitit support to update the payment method on the existing plan
  3. Contact your bank: If the card is valid but the charge was declined, call your bank to understand why and unblock the charge
  4. Act quickly: Splitit typically allows a grace period to resolve payment failures — delayed action can result in plan cancellation
7

3DS / SCA Authentication Failure During Plan Setup

Customer-side

When Splitit initiates the authorization hold, the customer's card-issuing bank may require 3D Secure (3DS) authentication — particularly for European cards under PSD2/SCA regulations, or for any bank that has enabled 3DS for large-amount transactions.

The 3DS prompt appears as an interstitial step asking the customer to confirm via their bank's app, an SMS code, or a biometric check. This flow can fail if: the customer dismisses the prompt; the SMS OTP never arrives due to a phone carrier issue; the bank's authentication app is outdated; or the 3DS timeout is too short and the customer doesn't complete it in time.

When 3DS fails, the authorization hold is rejected and Splitit cannot complete the plan setup. The failure is indistinguishable to the customer from a card decline, but the root cause is authentication, not credit availability.

Fix Steps for Customers

  1. Have your phone ready: When retrying Splitit, keep your phone nearby for the bank's SMS or app notification
  2. Update your bank's app: If 3DS uses your bank's mobile app, ensure it's up to date and notifications are enabled
  3. Complete the challenge quickly: 3DS prompts expire — don't leave the browser tab during the Splitit checkout
  4. Contact your bank: Ask whether they require 3DS for Splitit transactions and whether there is a way to pre-authorize the charge
  5. Try a different card: If one card consistently fails 3DS for Splitit, try a Visa or Mastercard from a different bank
8

Refund or Cancellation Not Releasing the Hold

Merchant + Customer

When a Splitit order is refunded or canceled through Shopify Admin, Splitit should automatically terminate the installment plan and release the authorization hold on the customer's card. The hold release typically takes 3–10 business days to appear on the customer's statement depending on the bank.

Problems occur in two scenarios: (1) the refund is processed in Shopify but not communicated to Splitit — this happens when the webhook from Shopify to Splitit fails, leaving the plan active and the hold in place even though the merchant issued the refund; (2) the hold was placed for the full purchase amount but only a partial refund was issued — Splitit will adjust the plan to reflect the partial refund, but the remaining hold stays active for the outstanding balance.

Customers often contact merchants confused about why the hold remains on their card after a refund confirmation. The hold and the refund are separate actions — the refund returns money already captured (first installment), while the hold release frees up remaining credit that was reserved but never charged.

How to Handle Hold Release Issues

  1. Initiate refund through Shopify Admin only: Never refund directly in Splitit's merchant portal for Shopify orders — it breaks the sync
  2. Confirm webhook delivery: After issuing the refund in Shopify, check Splitit's merchant dashboard to confirm the plan shows as terminated/refunded
  3. Wait 5–10 business days: Hold releases require bank processing time — advise customers that the hold will disappear from their statement within this window
  4. Escalate to Splitit support: If the plan still shows active in Splitit's dashboard after a Shopify refund, contact Splitit merchant support to force-terminate the plan
  5. For partial refunds: Confirm with Splitit support how the partial refund affects the remaining hold — the hold should be reduced to cover only the remaining plan balance

Merchant Diagnostic Checklist

Splitit Troubleshooting — 8-Step Merchant Checklist

Run through this before contacting Splitit support

App installed and active: Shopify Admin → Apps → confirm Splitit is installed and shows as active (not paused)

Live API credentials entered: In Splitit app settings, confirm Live mode is selected and live API keys are configured — not sandbox/test credentials

Cart minimum confirmed: Test with a cart value above $50 to rule out Splitit's minimum order threshold hiding the option

Supported country verified: Confirm the shipping address country is a Splitit-supported market (US, UK, CA, AU, select EU)

Webhook endpoint active: Settings → Notifications → Webhooks — confirm Splitit's webhook URL is registered and receiving without errors

Splitit merchant dashboard checked: Log into Splitit's portal to verify transaction status — always do this before asking a customer to retry

No duplicate hold risk: Before any retry, confirm Splitit did not already place an authorization hold for the failed checkout

Alternative BNPL offered: When Splitit fails due to card ineligibility or hold decline, offer Afterpay or Klarna — these don't require a full-amount hold and may succeed where Splitit didn't
