Shopify Laybuy Not Working — Not Showing, Payment Declined, Weekly Payment Failed

Updated June 2026 · 7 failure modes · NZ, AU, and UK merchants & customers

Laybuy splits purchases into 6 equal weekly payments — the first at checkout, the remaining 5 charged automatically each week. That ongoing payment schedule creates failure modes that card-based BNPL services never face: weekly payment card declines, account restrictions from missed installments, and refund complexity mid-plan. Add strict country and currency requirements for NZ, AU, and UK, and Laybuy has more ways to silently break than it first appears. This guide covers all 7 failure modes with exact fixes.

Why Laybuy Fails Differently From Other BNPL

Laybuy's 6-week model creates an ongoing payment relationship. Unlike Afterpay (4 payments over 6 weeks) or Klarna (pay later in 30 days), Laybuy collects 5 additional weekly automatic charges after the checkout payment. Each charge is a fresh card transaction that can independently fail — the customer's financial situation at week 3 or week 5 determines whether the plan succeeds, not just their situation at checkout.

This creates post-checkout failure modes: a customer who checks out successfully on Monday may have their week-3 payment declined due to insufficient funds, a card replacement, or a bank block. Laybuy will retry and notify the customer — but if retries fail, the customer's account gets restricted across all merchants until the debt is cleared.

Geography is also strict: Laybuy only operates in New Zealand, Australia, and the UK. It requires the matching regional currency (NZD, AUD, or GBP) and a shipping address in a supported country. Stores selling globally in USD or EUR cannot offer Laybuy, and multi-currency stores must configure Laybuy per market — not globally.

1

Laybuy Not Showing at Shopify Checkout

Merchant-side

Laybuy is a third-party payment app — it is not built into Shopify Payments. Like any third-party payment method, it can silently disappear from checkout for configuration or eligibility reasons that built-in methods never experience.

The most common reason Laybuy vanishes: the store currency is not NZD, AUD, or GBP. Laybuy only operates in New Zealand, Australia, and the United Kingdom and will not appear at checkout for any other currency. Multi-currency Shopify stores using Shopify Markets must ensure each of the NZ, AU, and UK market storefronts has the correct regional currency set as the mandatory checkout currency — not a conversion from USD.

Other common causes: the Laybuy app was not installed from the Shopify App Store, or was removed during a theme, platform, or headless migration; the Laybuy app is installed but the merchant's Laybuy account has not completed the approval process (Laybuy requires a merchant account setup and approval before going live); the app is still using test/sandbox credentials which do not allow live checkout display; the order amount falls below Laybuy's minimum eligible purchase threshold; or Laybuy is not enabled in Shopify's alternative payment providers settings after installation.

How to Diagnose

  1. Check store currency: Shopify Admin → Settings → Store details → Currency — must be NZD, AUD, or GBP for Laybuy to display
  2. Check app installation: Shopify Admin → Apps → confirm Laybuy is installed and active (not paused or uninstalled)
  3. Check merchant account status: Log into the Laybuy merchant portal and confirm your account is approved and active — pending accounts cannot process live orders
  4. Check API credentials mode: In the Laybuy app settings, confirm Live credentials are configured — test/sandbox credentials suppress Laybuy at live checkout
  5. Check payment settings: Shopify Admin → Settings → Payments → Alternative payment providers — confirm Laybuy is listed and enabled
  6. Test with regional address: In checkout, enter a New Zealand, Australian, or UK shipping address and confirm Laybuy appears — rules out country filtering
  7. Check order minimum: Ensure the cart total meets Laybuy's minimum eligible amount for the region (typically NZ$20 / AU$20 / £10)
  8. Test in incognito: Open a private browser window and complete checkout — rules out browser extension or session cache conflicts
2

Eligibility Check Declined at First Use

Customer-side

Before approving a customer's first Laybuy payment plan, Laybuy performs a soft credit check — a background eligibility assessment that does not affect the customer's credit score but does evaluate their creditworthiness against Laybuy's risk model. If the assessment returns a decline, the customer cannot use Laybuy for that transaction and is shown a decline message.

Common reasons for a first-use eligibility decline: the customer is under 18 years old (Laybuy requires all customers to be 18 or over); thin credit file with insufficient credit history for the risk model to assess; previous defaults or adverse entries visible on the customer's credit file; or the customer's address, identity details, or card details could not be verified against credit bureau records. Laybuy does not disclose the specific decline reason to customers.

Returning Laybuy customers with a good payment history rarely face eligibility declines — the ongoing assessment is primarily for new customers. Customers who are declined should wait at least 30 days before attempting again, as repeated application attempts within a short window can create an unfavorable signal. During this time, alternative payment methods (credit card, PayPal, Afterpay) should be offered at checkout.

Fix Steps for Customers

  1. Verify age requirement: Laybuy requires customers to be 18 or older — customers under 18 cannot use Laybuy
  2. Check address accuracy: Ensure the billing address entered at checkout exactly matches the address on your credit file and bank records
  3. Wait 30 days: If declined, avoid reapplying immediately — wait at least 30 days to avoid signaling repeated failed applications
  4. Use alternative payment: Credit card, PayPal, or Afterpay as a checkout alternative while building credit history
  5. Contact Laybuy: Laybuy's customer support can provide limited information about account status — they cannot reverse the credit model's decision but can clarify account-level issues
Merchant note: Eligibility declines are entirely between the customer and Laybuy's risk model. You cannot override or appeal a decline on a customer's behalf. Ensure your checkout offers multiple BNPL options (Afterpay, Zip, or Klarna) so customers who cannot use Laybuy have alternatives rather than abandoning the cart.
3

Weekly Installment Payment Card Failed

Customer-side

Laybuy's most unique failure mode — and the one most likely to cause customer service contacts to a merchant — is a failed weekly installment payment that occurs after the customer has already received the goods. The checkout payment (1 of 6) succeeds, the order is fulfilled, and then weeks 2 through 6 fail one by one.

The most common cause: insufficient funds on the scheduled payment date. Laybuy charges each weekly installment automatically on the same card used at checkout. If the card balance is low on the scheduled day, the charge fails. Laybuy immediately notifies the customer by email and SMS and retries the charge over the following days.

A second common cause is card replacement. Customers whose card was lost, stolen, or expired between checkout and a later installment will have weekly payments fail because the card number changed. The customer must log into the Laybuy app and update their payment card before the overdue amount accumulates — but many customers don't realize their Laybuy plan is on the old card until a payment fails.

Bank-side blocks are another cause: some banks treat Laybuy's recurring weekly charges as unusual and decline them after the first transaction. The customer may need to call their bank and authorize Laybuy as a recurring payment source, or switch to a different card in the Laybuy app.

Fix Steps for Customers

  1. Check Laybuy app immediately: Open the Laybuy app — failed payments show on the home screen with an "Update payment" prompt
  2. Update card if replaced: If your card was renewed, lost, or replaced since checkout, add the new card in Laybuy → Account → Payment Methods before the next retry attempt
  3. Ensure sufficient funds: Check your card balance on the scheduled payment day — Laybuy charges are made early in the morning in your local time zone
  4. Contact your bank: If Laybuy charges are being blocked by your bank, call the bank's customer service and authorize Laybuy as a trusted recurring payment
  5. Switch payment card: In the Laybuy app, add an alternative card and set it as the default for the current payment plan to avoid further failures
  6. Pay the overdue installment manually: If a payment failed, Laybuy allows manual payment of the overdue amount in the app — doing this promptly prevents account restriction
Merchant note: Once an order is fulfilled and the first payment cleared, weekly payment failures after delivery are between the customer and Laybuy. You are paid by Laybuy at the time of order — Laybuy absorbs the collection risk and merchant payment is not affected by whether the customer pays their installments. However, customers may contact you about payment failures, not realizing the issue is with Laybuy. Direct them to the Laybuy app or Laybuy customer support.
4

Customer Account Restricted — Overdue Installments

Customer-side

When a customer's weekly installment payment fails and all retry attempts are exhausted, Laybuy restricts the customer's account. A restricted account cannot be used for new Laybuy purchases at any merchant until the overdue balance is paid in full. This is the most common reason a returning Laybuy customer suddenly cannot use Laybuy at a checkout they've used before.

The restriction lifecycle: Laybuy charges the scheduled installment → charge fails → Laybuy retries over several days → all retries fail → Laybuy sends overdue notices by email and SMS → account is restricted. The customer receives multiple notifications before restriction, but if they miss the emails, the restriction comes as a surprise at their next checkout attempt.

To clear a restriction, the customer must pay the total overdue balance (all missed installments plus any late fees) using a different payment method — typically a debit or credit card that is not the failed card. Payment is made directly through the Laybuy app or website. After the payment processes, Laybuy typically reinstates the account within hours. For customers with persistent non-payment, Laybuy may refer the debt to a collections agency and add an adverse entry to the customer's credit file.

Fix Steps for Customers

  1. Open the Laybuy app: Check the dashboard — overdue balances and account restrictions are prominently displayed with a "Pay now" option
  2. Pay the overdue balance immediately: Use a different card than the one that failed — add a new card in Laybuy → Account → Payment Methods, then pay the outstanding amount
  3. Check for late fees: Laybuy may charge a late payment fee for missed installments — the total due in the app includes all fees
  4. Wait for account reinstatement: After payment confirms, Laybuy typically reinstates account access within a few hours — if it doesn't clear within 24 hours, contact Laybuy customer support
  5. Update the card on file: After paying the overdue amount, update your primary payment card in the Laybuy app to prevent future failures
  6. Set up calendar reminders: Laybuy sends payment schedule emails at checkout — add each payment date to your calendar as a reminder to ensure sufficient funds
Merchant note: A restricted Laybuy account appears as a generic decline at checkout — you will not see the reason. If a customer says Laybuy worked before but is now failing, an overdue installment restriction is the most likely cause. Advise them to check the Laybuy app directly. You cannot clear account restrictions on behalf of customers, and your revenue from previous orders is not affected by the customer's repayment status.
5

Order Below Minimum or Above Customer Maximum

Merchant + Customer

Laybuy enforces both a minimum order amount and a per-customer maximum. Orders outside these bounds will either not show Laybuy as a payment option or will fail at the eligibility check step.

Minimum order: Laybuy has a minimum eligible purchase amount per region — typically NZ$20, AU$20, or £10. Carts below this threshold will not show Laybuy at checkout at all. This silently excludes low-value transactions and can confuse merchants who see Laybuy working for some orders but not others.

Per-customer maximum: Each Laybuy customer has a maximum order value based on their creditworthiness assessment. New customers receive a conservative initial limit that increases over time with a good payment history. The maximum is not displayed to customers before they attempt checkout — they only discover it when Laybuy declines the order at the eligibility step. This creates a poor checkout experience for new high-value orders: the customer selects Laybuy, enters their details, and is declined without understanding why.

Unlike a credit card limit, there is no way to request a temporary limit increase for a specific purchase. The limit is set by Laybuy's algorithm and updates periodically as the customer builds history.

How to Diagnose and Fix

  1. Check order minimum: Verify the cart value meets Laybuy's minimum for the store's region — NZ$20, AU$20, or £10 as of 2026; check Laybuy's merchant documentation for current thresholds
  2. Inform customers of limits: Add a note at checkout (via Shopify's checkout customization or an app) that Laybuy is available for orders above the minimum — this prevents confusion for small purchases
  3. For maximum exceeded: If a customer is declined for exceeding their limit, offer alternative payment methods (credit card, PayPal) and suggest they build Laybuy history with smaller purchases first
  4. New customer guidance: For high-AOV stores, add checkout messaging that new Laybuy customers may face lower initial limits — this sets expectations before the decline screen
  5. Advise customers to build history: Customers who want higher Laybuy limits should make smaller Laybuy purchases and pay all installments on time — limits increase with a positive payment track record
6

Unsupported Country or Currency

Merchant-side

Laybuy will not display at checkout unless two conditions are simultaneously met: the active checkout currency is NZD, AUD, or GBP, and the customer's shipping address is in a supported country (New Zealand, Australia, or the United Kingdom). Both conditions must be true — a NZD store with a US shipping address will not show Laybuy, and a NZ shipping address with an AUD currency mismatch will also suppress it.

This creates issues for international Shopify stores. A store that sells globally and uses Shopify Payments' automatic currency conversion presents the correct currency to regional customers — but if the currency detection misfires (a VPN user appearing as NZ-based while shipping internationally, or a customer who manually overrides the currency selector), Laybuy will silently hide itself.

For Shopify Markets setups, Laybuy must be configured and enabled per market — not as a global payment method. Enabling Laybuy globally on a store that sells in USD and converts to other currencies will cause Laybuy to appear inconsistently: working for NZ/AU/UK customers but failing for all others. Additionally, some Shopify themes incorrectly display Laybuy to all visitors in the payment options preview but only render it at the actual checkout step for eligible customers — this can create merchant confusion about whether Laybuy is working.

Fix for Merchants

  1. Set regional markets to fixed currency: Shopify Admin → Markets → New Zealand / Australia / United Kingdom → ensure each market has its regional currency fixed as the checkout currency, not converted from a base currency
  2. Enable Laybuy per market: In Laybuy app settings and Shopify payment settings, confirm Laybuy is enabled specifically for NZ, AU, and UK markets — not as a global alternative payment
  3. Test end-to-end per region: Add a product to cart, enter a New Zealand address with NZD currency → confirm Laybuy appears; repeat for AU address + AUD; repeat for UK address + GBP
  4. Test with VPN consideration: Customers using VPNs with NZ/AU/UK IP addresses but shipping internationally may see Laybuy in the payment list but be declined at eligibility — shipping address is the determinative factor, not IP
  5. Add Laybuy to checkout messaging: State on product or checkout pages that Laybuy is available for NZ, AU, and UK orders only — this reduces confusion for international customers who see it and expect it to work

Refund Timing Issues During Active Payment Plan

Merchant + Customer

Refunds for Laybuy orders are more complex than card-based refunds because the customer's payment plan may still be partially active at the time the merchant issues the refund. Laybuy must cancel future installments, calculate what has already been collected, and determine whether a refund payment is needed to the customer or whether the remaining uncollected installments simply get cancelled.

Full refund: When a merchant issues a full refund in Shopify Admin, Laybuy immediately cancels all remaining weekly installments in the customer's payment plan and refunds any installments already collected back to the customer's original card. The refund typically takes 3–7 business days to appear on the customer's card statement. Customers often call merchants expecting an immediate refund — the bank processing time is not controllable by Laybuy or the merchant.

Partial refund: Partial refunds are more complex. Laybuy adjusts the remaining payment schedule proportionally — future installment amounts are reduced to reflect the partial refund. If the refund amount is less than the installments already collected, the customer receives a card refund for the overpayment difference. If more installments were collected than the partial refund amount, only the schedule is adjusted with no refund payment issued. Customers may not understand why they receive a smaller-than-expected refund.

Webhook failures between Shopify and Laybuy can cause refund processing delays. If the Shopify refund event webhook does not reach Laybuy, the payment plan is not cancelled or adjusted — the customer may continue to be charged weekly installments for an order the merchant already refunded. Always confirm refund processing on the Laybuy merchant portal, not just in Shopify Admin.

How to Handle Refund Issues

  1. Always initiate refunds through Shopify Admin: Use Shopify's refund flow — not the Laybuy merchant portal directly — to ensure the webhook is triggered and records stay synchronized
  2. Confirm in Laybuy merchant portal: After issuing the refund in Shopify, log into the Laybuy merchant portal and confirm the order shows as refunded and the customer's payment plan is cancelled or adjusted
  3. Explain the bank timeline: Tell customers that Laybuy refunds take 3–7 business days to appear on their card — this is bank processing time, not a delay by Laybuy or the merchant
  4. For partial refunds, set expectations: Explain to customers that Laybuy's partial refund process adjusts the remaining schedule — they may not receive cash back if future installments cover the refund amount
  5. Monitor for ongoing charges: If a customer reports being charged after a refund was issued, check the Laybuy merchant portal for webhook delivery failures and escalate to Laybuy merchant support with the order and transaction ID
  6. Escalate webhook failures: If refunds are systematically not triggering Laybuy plan cancellations, check the Shopify webhook delivery log (Admin → Settings → Notifications → Webhooks) for failed Laybuy endpoint calls
Customer tip: If you returned an item and expected a Laybuy refund, check your card statement for a refund from Laybuy within 5–7 business days. If future weekly payments are still being charged after a full return, contact the merchant to confirm the refund was processed in Shopify and then escalate to Laybuy support directly with your order number.
