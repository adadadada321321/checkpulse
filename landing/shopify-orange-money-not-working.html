Shopify Orange Money (Orange Cash / Orange Egypt) Not Working — Wallet Not Appearing, OTP Not Received & Order Stuck Pending

Updated June 2026 · 7 failure modes · Egypt merchants · Orange Egypt · CBE regulated · EGP only · 012 prefix

Orange Money (operated by Orange Egypt, formerly Mobinil, 012 prefix) is Egypt's second-largest carrier wallet. Despite Orange Egypt's strong market position, Orange Money is not automatically enabled on every Egyptian payment gateway — merchants must explicitly confirm and activate it separately from Vodafone Cash. When Orange Money stops working on Shopify, the failure is one of seven distinct problems: limited gateway support, OTP delivery issues, asynchronous callback failures, or checkouts that only accept Vodafone Cash 010 numbers and reject 012 entries. Identifying which failure you have is the only path to fixing it.

How Orange Money Works — and Why It Fails at Shopify Checkout

Orange Money is a mobile wallet tied to the customer's Orange Egypt SIM card (012 prefix), not a card scheme and not interchangeable with Vodafone Cash, e& Cash, or WE Cash. Customers with an Orange Egypt SIM register their national ID via the Orange Money app or at an Orange Egypt store to create a wallet linked to their 012 number. Funds are loaded via Orange Egypt stores, ATMs, or bank transfers. At checkout, the customer selects Orange Money, enters their 012 mobile number, receives an OTP confirmation, and the purchase amount is deducted from the wallet balance.

Orange Egypt is Egypt's second-largest mobile carrier (formerly Mobinil), giving Orange Money a large potential user base — but gateway support still requires explicit activation. Despite Orange Egypt's market position, Orange Money integration in payment gateways is not universal. Merchants cannot assume that because Vodafone Cash is enabled, Orange Money is also available. Orange Money uses a separate carrier API with distinct registration with Orange Egypt's payment system. Paymob and similar Egyptian gateways may support Orange Money on select plan tiers, but merchants must confirm availability and enable it separately. International gateways (Stripe, PayPal) do not support Orange Money.

Orange Money is an asynchronous mobile wallet payment with the same callback dependency as all Egyptian mobile wallets. The customer approves via OTP, the wallet is debited, and the gateway sends a confirmation to Shopify via a webhook callback. If the callback URL is misconfigured, the order stays stuck in pending even though the customer's wallet has been charged. This is the most dangerous failure mode — a debited customer with an unfulfilled order and no automated reconciliation path.

Orange Money is EGP only and requires a compatible gateway. Merchants must use an Egypt-focused gateway with explicit Orange Egypt mobile wallet API integration. Confirm with Paymob whether your plan tier includes Orange Money and enable it as a distinct payment method separate from Vodafone Cash, e& Cash, and card payments.

7 Failure Modes Covered

  1. Orange Money option not appearing at checkout — gateway does not support or has not enabled 012 wallet
  2. Customer has Orange SIM but has not registered for an Orange Money wallet
  3. OTP not received or times out before customer enters it
  4. Insufficient wallet balance — wallet pre-loading required
  5. CBE daily or monthly transaction limit exceeded
  6. Gateway callback not configured — order stuck in pending after customer pays
  7. 012 number rejected — checkout or gateway configured only for Vodafone Cash 010 numbers
1

Orange Money option not appearing at checkout — gateway does not support or has not enabled 012 wallet

Merchant Issue

The most common reason Orange Egypt customers do not see an Orange Money payment option at Shopify checkout is that the merchant's payment gateway either does not support Orange Money at all, or supports it on some plans but has not confirmed availability or activated it. Orange Money requires an explicit API integration with Orange Egypt's mobile wallet system — this is a separate integration from Vodafone Cash, e& Cash, and WE Cash, each of which uses a different carrier API.

Stripe and PayPal do not support Orange Money. International gateways without Egyptian mobile wallet integrations cannot offer it. For Egyptian-focused gateways, Paymob is the primary provider — but Orange Money availability depends on the merchant's plan tier and requires explicit activation. A merchant who enabled Vodafone Cash on Paymob cannot assume Orange Money is also live. Orange Money requires a separate toggle in the gateway dashboard and may not be available on all plan tiers.

A common error pattern: a merchant integrates Paymob, enables Vodafone Cash, and considers Egyptian mobile wallets covered. Orange Egypt customers (012 prefix) attempt to pay, see no Orange Money option, and abandon checkout. The merchant, testing from a Vodafone number, sees the payment working and never identifies that Orange Money is simply not enabled. Only testing from an Orange Egypt 012 SIM or auditing gateway settings reveals the gap.

Legacy integrations can also cause this issue. Merchants who set up Paymob before Orange Money was added to their plan's available methods may not see it in the dashboard automatically — they must actively check for the option and enable it rather than waiting for it to appear. A gateway account created years ago may not reflect all currently available payment methods without a proactive review of the gateway settings.

How to fix

  1. Contact your gateway to confirm Orange Money availability on your plan: Log in to your Paymob (or gateway) dashboard and look for Orange Money as a distinct payment method option, separate from Vodafone Cash and e& Cash. If it does not appear, contact gateway support to confirm whether Orange Money (Orange Egypt, 012 prefix) is available on your plan tier and what steps are needed to activate it. Orange Money may require a plan upgrade or separate configuration.
  2. Enable Orange Money in the gateway dashboard if available: If Orange Money is listed as an available payment method, navigate to payment method settings and toggle it to enabled. Save the configuration. The payment widget at Shopify checkout should update to include an Orange Money option alongside other Egyptian payment methods.
  3. Test from an Orange Egypt SIM explicitly: After enabling, verify the checkout from a device with an Orange Egypt 012 SIM or ask a colleague with an Orange Egypt number to test. A test with a Vodafone or e& number confirms only those wallets are visible — it does not confirm Orange Money is visible. Only a genuine 012 payment attempt shows whether the Orange Money option appears correctly at checkout.
  4. Consider offering Fawry or bank transfer as a fallback while resolving Orange Money availability: While confirming gateway support for Orange Money, ensure Orange Egypt customers have an alternative payment path. Fawry is accessible across all Egyptian carriers without a registered wallet. Bank transfer is carrier-agnostic and accommodates any order value.
  5. Audit gateway settings periodically for new payment method options: Egyptian payment gateways regularly add new wallet integrations and update plan-level availability. Log in to your gateway dashboard quarterly to check whether new payment methods are available that you have not yet enabled — including Orange Money if you initially set up before it was added to your plan.
2

Customer has Orange SIM but has not registered for an Orange Money wallet

Customer Issue

Owning an Orange Egypt SIM card and having a 012 mobile number does not mean the customer has an Orange Money wallet. Orange Money is a separate financial service requiring explicit registration — the customer must create a wallet account linked to their 012 number before any payment can be made. An Orange Egypt subscriber who has never registered for Orange Money will receive a "wallet not found" or "account not registered" error when they enter their number at checkout, even if the merchant's gateway is correctly configured and Orange Money is shown as a payment option.

When the gateway sends a wallet lookup for an unregistered 012 number to Orange Egypt's payment system, it receives a "wallet not found" response. This is typically displayed to the customer as a generic payment error — "payment could not be completed," "account not recognized," or an error code without explanation. Customers frequently assume the merchant's checkout has a technical problem rather than recognizing they need to register a separate wallet service with their carrier.

Orange Money wallet registration can be completed through: the Orange Money app (available for Android and iOS), at Orange Egypt stores or authorized dealers with a valid national ID, or online through the Orange Egypt website. Orange Egypt has an extensive retail presence across Egypt due to its long history as Mobinil — registration touchpoints are broadly available. Registration typically completes immediately upon national ID verification.

Long-standing Orange Egypt mobile subscribers who have used the carrier for years may have never needed a mobile wallet before and may not know that Orange Money is a separate product requiring its own registration. This is common among customers who use Orange primarily for voice and data, have not adopted digital payments, and encounter Orange Money for the first time when attempting to pay at a Shopify store.

How to fix

  1. Add wallet registration guidance near the Orange Money option at checkout: Display a brief note: "First time using Orange Money? Register your wallet via the Orange Money app or at any Orange Egypt store (bring national ID) before selecting this payment option." This guides unregistered customers to the correct action instead of leaving them with a confusing payment error.
  2. Include Orange Money registration guidance in cart abandonment follow-up: If a customer abandons checkout after attempting Orange Money, send a follow-up email explaining that Orange Money requires a one-time wallet registration via the Orange Money app or at an Orange Egypt store, and that they can return to complete their order once registered.
  3. Offer alternative payment methods for unregistered customers: Customers who want to pay with Orange Money but have not yet registered should be able to complete their purchase immediately. Display Fawry offline payment, Meeza card, or bank transfer as clear alternatives so that an Orange Money registration issue does not result in order abandonment.
  4. Distinguish registration errors from other failure codes in gateway logs: Your gateway returns specific error codes for "wallet not registered" versus "OTP failed" versus "insufficient balance." Identify the error code for unregistered wallets in your gateway documentation. This ensures that customer service correctly routes unregistered-wallet inquiries to registration guidance rather than payment retry instructions.
Merchant note: Orange Egypt's retail network is extensive due to its Mobinil history, which means registration touchpoints are broadly available across Egyptian cities and towns. Point customers to the Orange Money app as the fastest registration channel — it can be completed without a store visit if national ID verification is handled digitally through the app.
3

OTP not received or times out before customer enters it

Merchant + Customer

Orange Money payments require OTP confirmation — after the customer enters their 012 number at checkout, Orange Egypt's payment system sends a one-time password to the registered number. The customer must enter this OTP within the payment session window to complete the transaction. If the OTP does not arrive or expires before the customer enters it, the payment fails and the order is not placed.

If the gateway's session timeout is set to the default 60–90 seconds, OTP failures are more likely. Orange Egypt's network is well-established across Egypt due to its long presence as Mobinil, but SMS delivery delays still occur — particularly during peak usage hours, in areas with congested cell towers, or when the customer is switching between Wi-Fi and mobile data. Increasing the session timeout to 180 seconds significantly reduces OTP-related failures for all Egyptian mobile wallet payments including Orange Money.

Other OTP delivery failures include: the customer has a dual-SIM phone where the Orange SIM is not the active SIM at the time of the OTP; the customer's 012 number registered to the Orange Money wallet differs from the number entered at checkout (for example, if the customer registered with a different Orange SIM than the one they currently use); the customer's Orange SIM is a data-only SIM without SMS service enabled; or the customer confuses an in-app push notification with an SMS OTP — the delivery channel depends on the customer's wallet notification settings in the Orange Money app.

On the merchant side, the OTP session timeout for Orange Money is typically configurable in the payment gateway's integration settings. Increasing this timeout from the default is the single most effective action a merchant can take to reduce OTP-related Orange Money failures. This configuration change benefits all Egyptian mobile wallet integrations simultaneously, not just Orange Money.

How to fix

  1. Increase the payment session timeout in gateway settings: Log in to your gateway dashboard and find the session expiry or OTP timeout setting for the Orange Money integration. Increase it from the default (60–90 seconds) to at least 3 minutes (180 seconds). This applies to all Egyptian mobile wallet methods and reduces OTP timeout failures across Vodafone Cash, Orange Money, e& Cash, and WE Cash simultaneously.
  2. Advise customers to have their Orange phone ready with signal before checkout: Add a note to the Orange Money payment option: "You will receive an OTP on your registered Orange number — make sure your Orange Egypt SIM has signal and is the active SIM on dual-SIM phones before selecting this option." Customers who understand the OTP flow are less likely to be caught unprepared.
  3. Remind customers to check the Orange Money app for OTP notifications: In some configurations, the Orange Money confirmation arrives as an in-app notification rather than by SMS. If a customer reports not receiving an SMS OTP, advise them to check the Orange Money app on their phone for a payment authorization prompt.
  4. Distinguish OTP timeout from OTP non-delivery in gateway logs: Session expired and OTP not delivered are different error codes in the gateway response. Timeout errors point to a session duration fix. Non-delivery errors point to customer-side issues (wrong SIM active, number mismatch, data-only SIM). Knowing which occurred guides the correct resolution path.
  5. Provide a fallback payment option after OTP failure: If the OTP step fails, the customer should be able to switch to another payment method — Fawry, Meeza, or bank transfer — without restarting the entire order. Display alternatives clearly on the payment error screen.
4

Insufficient wallet balance — wallet pre-loading required

Customer Issue

Orange Money is a pre-funded wallet — the customer must load funds into their wallet in advance before it can be used for purchases. If the Orange Money wallet balance is less than the order total, the payment is declined at the Orange Egypt payment system level before the OTP step completes. The customer receives an "insufficient funds" or "payment declined" error that is often mistaken for a technical checkout failure rather than a balance shortfall.

The pre-funded nature of Orange Money is fundamentally different from a debit card or direct bank transfer. Customers accustomed to debit card payments may not realize their Orange Money wallet is a separate account requiring explicit top-up that is independent of their bank account balance. A customer with sufficient bank funds may be confused by an "insufficient funds" Orange Money error because they assume the wallet draws from their bank — it does not unless the customer has set up a bank-to-wallet auto-load through the Orange Money app.

Customers can load their Orange Money wallet through: the Orange Money app using a linked bank card or bank transfer; at Orange Egypt stores or authorized dealers (cash deposit); and at ATMs of compatible Egyptian banks. Orange Egypt's dealer network is extensive across Egypt, making in-person loading broadly accessible. The Orange Money app provides the most convenient loading channel for customers who prefer not to visit a store.

For merchants, insufficient balance declines appear in the gateway logs as a balance-related decline code. There is no technical action the merchant can take to resolve a specific customer's balance issue — the customer must load funds and retry. Merchants can reduce this friction by displaying the order amount clearly before the customer enters their 012 number, and by providing explicit top-up guidance on the payment failure screen.

How to fix

  1. Display the order total prominently before the Orange Money OTP step: Show the required amount and a "this will be charged to your Orange Money wallet" message before the customer enters their 012 number. This allows customers to confirm they have sufficient balance before initiating the OTP sequence, reducing wasted payment attempts that fail due to insufficient funds.
  2. Provide wallet top-up guidance on the payment failure page: If Orange Money is declined with an insufficient balance error, show: "To top up your Orange Money wallet: use the Orange Money app, visit an Orange Egypt store, or transfer from your bank app. Then return and retry your order." Link to the Orange Money app download where possible.
  3. Offer alternative payment methods for high-value orders: If your average order value is high relative to typical mobile wallet balances, ensure bank transfer and Fawry are prominently available at checkout. These options do not require pre-funding and can accommodate any order value.
  4. Add balance-awareness guidance for higher-priced products: For items priced above EGP 1,000–2,000, add a note near the buy button: "Paying with Orange Money? Make sure your wallet is topped up to the full order amount before checkout." This sets expectations early and prevents balance failures at the final payment step.
5

CBE daily or monthly transaction limit exceeded

Customer Issue

The Central Bank of Egypt sets regulatory transaction limits for all mobile wallets operating in Egypt, including Orange Money. These limits apply to all Orange Money users regardless of their wallet balance and are enforced at the Orange Egypt payment system level. When a customer's daily or monthly transaction limit has been reached, their payment is declined even if their wallet has sufficient funds.

The CBE's tiered limit structure is based on the customer's verification level. Unverified wallets — registered with basic national ID — have lower daily and monthly transaction limits and a lower maximum balance ceiling (approximately EGP 10,000). Fully verified wallets require extended KYC, typically face verification or in-person confirmation, and have significantly higher limits. An Orange Money customer with an unverified wallet attempting a high-value purchase may find the order total exceeds their daily limit even if the wallet balance is sufficient.

From the merchant's perspective, a CBE limit exceeded error and a balance error look similar in the gateway response — both are declined at the Orange Egypt system level before the transaction completes. The distinction matters for customer guidance: a balance error requires topping up the wallet, while a limit error requires either waiting for the limit to reset (daily limits typically reset at midnight) or completing wallet verification to access a higher tier.

Merchants selling higher-value items — electronics, furniture, premium software — will encounter CBE limit errors more frequently among customers with unverified Orange Money wallets. The practical solution for high-value orders is to offer bank transfer as an alternative, since Egyptian bank transfers do not carry CBE mobile wallet tier limits.

How to fix

  1. Advise customers on wallet tier upgrades: Customers who encounter CBE limit errors should be advised to upgrade their Orange Money wallet to the verified tier. Verification can typically be completed through the Orange Money app or at an Orange Egypt store with a national ID. A verified wallet has substantially higher daily and monthly transaction limits.
  2. Display limit guidance for high-value carts: For products priced above EGP 1,500–2,000, add a note near the Orange Money payment option: "Note: Orange Money transaction limits apply based on your wallet verification tier. For higher-value orders, choose bank transfer or complete Orange Money wallet verification first." This sets expectations before the payment attempt fails.
  3. Offer bank transfer as the primary alternative for large orders: Egyptian bank transfers carry no CBE mobile wallet limits and can accommodate any order size. For order values where CBE limit errors are frequent with unverified mobile wallets, make bank transfer prominently available with clear instructions.
  4. Distinguish limit errors from balance errors in customer service: When a customer contacts you after a declined Orange Money payment, the gateway error code indicates whether it is a balance issue or a regulatory limit issue. A balance error requires topping up. A limit error requires waiting for the daily reset or upgrading verification tier. Providing the correct guidance prevents customers from topping up when the real problem is a regulatory limit.
Merchant note: CBE mobile wallet limits are reviewed periodically. The tier structure here reflects 2026 CBE guidelines for Egyptian mobile wallets. Monitor CBE announcements annually — limits may increase as Egypt's digital payments adoption grows.
6

Gateway callback not configured — order stuck in pending after customer pays

Merchant Issue

A Shopify order stuck in pending status after an Orange Money payment is almost always caused by a missing or broken webhook callback URL. Orange Money is an asynchronous payment method — the payment flow is: (1) customer selects Orange Money and enters their 012 number; (2) Orange Egypt sends the customer an OTP; (3) customer approves the payment via OTP; (4) Orange Egypt's payment system notifies the payment gateway that the payment was approved; (5) the gateway notifies Shopify via a webhook callback; (6) Shopify marks the order as paid. If step 5 fails because the callback URL is misconfigured or unreachable, the payment never reaches Shopify, and the order stays in pending indefinitely.

This is the most dangerous Orange Money failure mode because it results in the customer having paid — their 012 wallet is debited and they have received a payment confirmation from Orange Egypt — while the merchant's Shopify system shows no completed payment. Without a process to cross-check pending orders against gateway transaction records, the merchant may never fulfill the order, leading to customer disputes and potential chargebacks.

The most common cause of callback failures is a localhost or development URL left in the production gateway configuration. During development and testing, merchants configure callback URLs pointing to local addresses (localhost, 127.0.0.1, ngrok tunnels). When the merchant goes live, if the callback URL is not updated to the production store address, all payment confirmations from Orange Egypt's system are sent to an unreachable address and silently fail. No error appears in the Shopify admin — only the gateway's logs show repeated failed callback attempts.

A related issue occurs when the Shopify store domain changes after the initial gateway setup: a custom domain is added, HTTPS is enforced, or the store migrates to a new URL. If the callback URL in the gateway dashboard still points to the old domain or an HTTP address, callbacks fail silently and all Orange Money payments result in stuck pending orders.

How to fix

  1. Check and update the callback URL in your gateway dashboard immediately: Log in to your Paymob (or gateway) dashboard and find the webhook or callback URL configuration for the Orange Money integration. The callback URL must be the production Shopify payment confirmation endpoint — a publicly accessible HTTPS URL matching your current live store domain. It must not be a localhost address, development tunnel, or HTTP URL.
  2. Verify the callback URL is reachable from the public internet: Use curl or a webhook testing service to confirm that a POST request to the configured callback URL returns a 200 response. If the URL returns 404, a redirect, or times out, update it and retest. A non-200 response means payment confirmations cannot reach Shopify.
  3. Manually reconcile currently stuck pending orders: Log in to your payment gateway's merchant dashboard and review completed Orange Money transactions for the past 7 days. Match them against pending Shopify orders. For each matched order, manually mark it as paid in Shopify admin and fulfill it. Contact your gateway support if a settlement reconciliation is needed for orders where payment was debited but not confirmed.
  4. Set up a monitoring alert for orders stuck in pending: Configure a Shopify alert to notify you when any order has been in pending status for more than 30 minutes. Pending orders that do not resolve quickly indicate a callback failure requiring immediate attention before they become customer disputes.
  5. Test the complete payment and callback flow after configuration: After updating the callback URL, complete a real Orange Money payment for a small test amount through your live Shopify checkout. Confirm that: the OTP is received, the payment is approved, the order in Shopify updates from pending to paid within 60 seconds, and the transaction appears as settled in gateway logs. Only a real end-to-end test confirms the callback is working correctly.
Critical: Do not ignore Shopify orders stuck in pending after Orange Money payments. These represent real money deducted from the customer's wallet with no fulfillment. Check your gateway transaction logs daily and cross-reference against pending Shopify orders. Every unmatched Orange Money payment is a potential dispute requiring manual reconciliation.
7

012 number rejected — checkout or gateway configured only for Vodafone Cash 010 numbers

Merchant Issue

A common but subtle failure occurs when the merchant's checkout or gateway is configured to accept Egyptian mobile wallet payments but only recognizes Vodafone Cash 010 numbers. When an Orange Egypt customer (012 prefix) enters their number, the payment widget rejects it with an "invalid mobile number," "unsupported carrier," or "account not found" error — even if the customer's Orange Money wallet is registered, funded, and fully active.

This failure occurs in two ways. First, the gateway may have only Vodafone Cash enabled — the payment option at checkout is labeled for Vodafone Cash, the Orange customer attempts to enter their 012 number, and the gateway rejects the non-Vodafone number at the API lookup stage. Second, some custom checkout implementations include client-side validation that checks for a 010 prefix and flags all other numbers as invalid before the payment even reaches the gateway. A merchant who built or customized the mobile wallet input may have hard-coded a 010 prefix check without accounting for 011, 012, or 015 prefixes.

Egyptian carrier prefixes: Vodafone Egypt uses 010, e& Egypt (formerly Etisalat) uses 011, Orange Egypt uses 012, and Telecom Egypt (WE) uses 015. A checkout that validates "must start with 010" inadvertently locks out all non-Vodafone mobile wallet users. Orange Egypt is Egypt's second-largest carrier — rejecting 012 numbers excludes a substantial portion of Egypt's mobile subscribers from wallet payments. Given Orange Egypt's significant market share, the 012 prefix rejection represents a measurable revenue loss for merchants targeting Egyptian customers.

The resolution requires either enabling Orange Money as a separate payment method in the gateway dashboard (which creates a checkout input specifically for 012 numbers), or updating client-side validation to accept 012 entries and route them through the Orange Money API. Both require a gateway that actually supports Orange Money — if the gateway only supports Vodafone Cash, 012 numbers cannot be accepted for wallet payments regardless of validation changes.

How to fix

  1. Enable Orange Money as a separate payment method in your gateway dashboard: Do not attempt to route Orange Money through the Vodafone Cash integration — they use different carrier APIs. Log in to your gateway dashboard and enable Orange Money as a distinct payment option with its own 012 number input, separate from Vodafone Cash (010), e& Cash (011), and WE Cash (015).
  2. Remove or update client-side validation that enforces 010-only prefix: If your checkout form has JavaScript validation accepting only numbers starting with 010, update it to also accept 011 (e&), 012 (Orange), and 015 (WE) numbers, routing each prefix to its respective wallet API. A carrier-agnostic number input with server-side routing is cleaner than multiple separate labeled inputs.
  3. Test the checkout from a 012 number explicitly: After enabling Orange Money and updating any validation, test by entering a 012 number in the mobile wallet field. Confirm it is accepted (not flagged as invalid), that an OTP is received, and that a payment completes. Testing with only a 010 number confirms Vodafone Cash works — it does not confirm Orange Money works.
  4. Add carrier-specific helper text to mobile wallet inputs: If you offer multiple Egyptian carrier wallets as separate checkout options, label each clearly: "Vodafone Cash (010)," "e& Cash / Etisalat (011)," "Orange Money (012)," and "WE Cash / Telecom Egypt (015)." Clear labeling prevents customers from entering their 012 number in the Vodafone Cash field and receiving a confusing rejection.
  5. Confirm your gateway plan covers Orange Money API access before troubleshooting validation: If you cannot enable Orange Money in the dashboard despite following the steps above, contact your gateway provider to confirm whether Orange Money API access is included in your plan. Orange Money may require a plan upgrade or separate activation depending on your gateway's integration tiers.
Merchant note: Orange Egypt (formerly Mobinil) has one of the largest subscriber bases in Egypt. Merchants selling to a broad Egyptian audience who have not explicitly confirmed Orange Money (012) support are likely missing a significant segment of potential customers. Auditing the mobile wallet checkout for all four Egyptian prefixes (010, 011, 012, 015) is a recommended quarterly step for any Egypt-focused Shopify store.

Orange Money Shopify Integration Diagnostic Checklist

Gateway confirmed to support Orange Money on your plan — verified with gateway provider (Paymob) that Orange Money (Orange Egypt, 012 prefix) is available on your plan tier and has been enabled as a distinct payment method.

Orange Money enabled in gateway dashboard — Orange Money is toggled on as an active payment method in the merchant's gateway settings, separate from Vodafone Cash, e& Cash, and card payment activations.

Production callback/webhook URL configured and reachable — the callback URL in the gateway dashboard is the production Shopify store URL (HTTPS, current domain), not a localhost or development address, and returns a 200 response to a test POST request.

Checkout currency is EGP — store is priced in EGP, or Shopify Markets is configured to present EGP to Egyptian customers. Orange Money processes EGP only.

Wallet registration guidance available to customers — a help note or FAQ near the Orange Money option explains that a separate wallet registration is required (Orange Money app or Orange Egypt store with national ID) for first-time users.

End-to-end test with a real Orange Money wallet completed — a test payment was made through the live checkout with a real registered Orange Money 012 wallet, OTP was received and entered, and the Shopify order updated from pending to paid within 60 seconds.

Fallback payment methods available — customers who cannot use Orange Money (wallet not registered, OTP failure, insufficient balance, gateway not supporting 012) have clear alternatives: Fawry offline code, Meeza card, or bank transfer.
