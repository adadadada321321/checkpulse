Shopify OPay Not Working — Not Showing at Checkout, Merchant Account Pending & Order Stuck

Egypt · Updated June 2026 · 7 failure modes

OPay is one of Africa's largest fintech platforms with a growing Egypt presence, but its Shopify integration differs significantly from plug-and-play gateways like Kashier or Paymob. This guide covers the 7 root causes of OPay not working on Shopify — from missing API integration to wallet payment failures to pending order issues — so you can identify exactly what's wrong and fix it.

What Is OPay for Shopify in Egypt?

OPay is a fintech platform founded in 2018, originally in Nigeria, that expanded to Egypt and became one of the country's significant digital wallet providers. OPay Egypt is licensed by the Central Bank of Egypt and operates a consumer wallet app (OPay Wallet) and a merchant payment gateway (OPay Business) for e-commerce integration.

Unlike Kashier or Paymob, OPay does not have an official app in the Shopify App Store for Egypt merchants. Integration requires using OPay Business's payment API directly or via a third-party plugin that supports OPay's payment gateway. This means the setup is more technical and failure points are different from gateway apps that install in one click.

OPay accepts payments from OPay wallet users, Visa, Mastercard, and in some plans Meeza cards. All transactions settle in Egyptian Pounds (EGP). OPay is not available to merchants without an Egyptian business registration and CBE KYC approval. It is distinct from the OPay consumer app — merchants must register for OPay Business separately.

7 Failure Modes

  1. OPay not appearing at checkout — no official Shopify app; API integration required
  2. OPay Business merchant account pending KYC — cannot accept live payments
  3. OPay wallet payment failing — insufficient balance, wrong number, or OTP not delivered
  4. Order stuck pending after OPay payment — callback URL not configured
  5. OPay Business API credentials expired or revoked — silent authentication failure
  6. OPay consumer app confused with OPay Business — wrong product registered
  7. Currency mismatch — store set to USD instead of EGP
1

OPay Not Appearing at Checkout — No Official Shopify App; API Integration Required

Merchant Setup

The most fundamental OPay-on-Shopify issue is that OPay does not have an official app in the Shopify App Store for Egyptian merchants, unlike Kashier or Paymob (Accept) which have dedicated Shopify apps. OPay integration on Shopify requires either a custom API integration built by a developer using OPay Business's payment gateway API, or a third-party Shopify payment plugin that has been configured to use OPay as the backend processor.

If OPay simply does not appear at checkout, the integration was either never completed, is still in development/test mode, or the payment plugin was misconfigured. There is no default OPay option that Shopify enables automatically — a developer must explicitly wire it up.

Merchants who hired a developer to "add OPay" need to verify that the developer: (1) registered for OPay Business (not just the consumer OPay app), (2) obtained API keys from the OPay Business merchant portal, (3) integrated those credentials into the Shopify store, and (4) switched the integration from sandbox to live mode. All four steps must be complete before OPay appears for customers.

Additionally, even when integrated, the OPay payment option must be activated in Shopify's Payments settings under Settings → Payments → Third-party providers. If the payment gateway appears in the code but is not activated as a provider in Shopify's admin, it will not render at checkout.

Fix Steps

  1. Confirm you have an OPay Business account (not OPay consumer app) — register at OPay's merchant portal if not.
  2. Obtain live API credentials from the OPay Business dashboard: API key, API secret, and merchant ID.
  3. Verify your developer has integrated these credentials into Shopify using OPay Business payment gateway documentation — this is a custom integration, not an app install.
  4. In Shopify admin: Settings → Payments → Third-party providers — confirm OPay is listed as an active provider.
  5. Confirm the integration is set to live mode, not sandbox — test in incognito as a guest customer.
No-code alternative: If a full API integration is too complex, some Egypt-focused Shopify agencies offer pre-built OPay connector modules. Search for developers on Shopify Experts or Egyptian developer communities (e.g., on LinkedIn or Facebook Groups for Egyptian Shopify developers) who have built OPay integrations before.

OPay Business Merchant Account Pending KYC — Cannot Accept Live Payments

Merchant Setup

OPay Business requires KYC (Know Your Customer) approval from the Central Bank of Egypt before a merchant account can process live payments. This is a CBE regulatory requirement for all licensed payment service providers in Egypt — not an OPay-specific policy — and applies to any gateway including OPay, Kashier, and Paymob.

The OPay Business KYC review typically takes 3–7 business days after all required documents are submitted. Required documentation includes: commercial registration certificate (السجل التجاري), tax card (البطاقة الضريبية), a valid business bank account at an Egyptian licensed bank, and proof of business activity appropriate to your registered business type. Individual freelancers and sole proprietors may have different documentation requirements — check the OPay Business merchant portal for your specific account type.

The most common reason applications stall beyond 7 business days is an unanswered document request. OPay's compliance team may send email requesting additional verification — if this email arrives in spam or is missed, the application pauses indefinitely. Check both your inbox and spam folder regularly during the review period.

Until KYC approval is granted, you can use OPay Business's sandbox environment with test credentials to build and test the Shopify integration, but no real customer payments can be processed.

Fix Steps

  1. Log in to the OPay Business merchant portal and check your account status (pending / under review / approved).
  2. Check your registration email inbox and spam folder for any document requests from OPay compliance.
  3. Ensure all required documents are uploaded: commercial register, tax card, and Egyptian bank account details.
  4. If more than 7 business days have passed without a status update, contact OPay Business support via the merchant portal or their business support email.
  5. While awaiting approval, use OPay sandbox credentials to test and finalize your Shopify integration — this way you are ready to switch to live immediately upon approval.
OPay vs Kashier vs Paymob KYC timeline: All three Egypt gateways require CBE-mandated KYC. OPay Business typically takes 3–7 days, Kashier 2–5 days, and Paymob varies. If you need faster activation, Kashier's review process is often cited as slightly faster for established Egyptian businesses. Having all documents prepared in advance is the single biggest factor in reducing review time.
3

OPay Wallet Payment Failing — Insufficient Balance, Wrong Number, or OTP Not Delivered

Customer Side

When a customer selects OPay Wallet at Shopify checkout, the payment flow requires them to enter their OPay-registered mobile number and confirm via OTP. This flow has four distinct failure points that produce the same "payment failed" message to the customer, making diagnosis difficult.

The first and most common failure is insufficient wallet balance. The OPay wallet is pre-funded — customers must top up before spending. Unlike credit cards, there is no overdraft. If the wallet balance is less than the purchase amount, the payment is declined immediately. The customer needs to top up via their OPay app, bank transfer, or Fawry point before retrying.

The second failure is a mobile number mismatch. OPay wallets are tied to a specific Egyptian mobile number. The customer must enter the exact number registered with their OPay account — including the correct carrier prefix (010, 011, 012, 015). A single digit error causes the transaction to fail because OPay cannot locate the wallet.

The third failure is OTP delivery failure. OPay sends a one-time password via SMS to the registered number. If the customer's phone has no signal, is in Do Not Disturb mode, or the carrier is experiencing an SMS delay, the OTP does not arrive. The payment window typically expires after 5 minutes. Ask the customer to ensure they have a stable network connection and retry.

A fourth, less obvious failure is the customer attempting to pay using OPay promotional credits or cashback. OPay rewards and cashback balances shown in the consumer app are often not eligible for merchant payments on external platforms — only the primary funded wallet balance counts. If the customer's "balance" is mostly promotional credits, the effective available amount for merchant payments may be lower than displayed.

Fix Steps

  1. Ask the customer to open the OPay app and check their wallet balance — confirm it's the primary funded balance, not promotional credits only.
  2. If balance is insufficient, ask them to top up via bank transfer, Fawry kiosk, or OPay app before retrying.
  3. Confirm the customer is entering their OPay-registered mobile number exactly — same number they used when creating their OPay account.
  4. If OTP is not arriving, ask the customer to check their mobile signal and verify their number is active for SMS reception.
  5. If the OTP window has expired, ask the customer to restart the checkout process to trigger a new OTP.
  6. Offer an alternative payment method (card via OPay gateway, Fawry if enabled, or a different gateway) if OPay wallet payment continues to fail.

Order Stuck Pending After OPay Payment — Callback URL Not Configured

Merchant Setup

This is the most dangerous OPay failure mode: the customer completes payment successfully in the OPay Express checkout flow, but the Shopify order remains in Pending status. The customer has been charged; the merchant has no record of the payment in Shopify.

OPay Business uses an asynchronous payment notification model. When a customer completes a payment in OPay Express (OPay's hosted payment page), OPay sends a POST callback to a merchant-configured URL to notify the store that the payment succeeded. Shopify only marks the order as paid when this callback is received and validated. If the callback URL is missing, incorrect, or unreachable, OPay cannot deliver the notification and the order stays pending.

Common misconfiguration causes: the callback URL was set during development pointing to localhost or an ngrok tunnel that no longer exists; the Shopify store was migrated to a new domain without updating the OPay Business dashboard; or the developer configured the success redirect URL (the page the customer sees) but not the backend callback URL (the server-to-server notification) — these are two separate settings in OPay Business.

OPay Business maintains a transaction log in the merchant portal showing every payment and its status independently of Shopify. Cross-reference pending Shopify orders against OPay's successful transaction log to identify customers who paid but whose orders were not confirmed.

Fix Steps

  1. Log in to the OPay Business merchant portal → Integration Settings and locate the Callback / Notification URL field.
  2. Verify the callback URL is your live Shopify store's payment notification endpoint — not localhost, ngrok, or a development URL.
  3. Confirm the callback URL is distinct from the success redirect URL — server-to-server callback and customer redirect URL are different fields in OPay Business settings.
  4. In OPay Business: check the Transaction History for successful payments that do not have corresponding paid orders in Shopify — these are affected customers.
  5. For confirmed-paid customers with pending Shopify orders: manually mark the order as paid in Shopify admin after verifying payment in the OPay transaction log, then fulfill the order.
Critical: A customer charged but Shopify order pending means funds were deducted from their OPay wallet or card. You must reconcile all pending orders against OPay transaction logs before assuming they were failed payments. Unfulfilled paid orders must be fulfilled or refunded.
5

OPay Business API Credentials Expired or Revoked — Silent Authentication Failure

Merchant Setup

OPay Business API credentials (API key and API secret) can expire, be revoked after a security event, or be rotated when a merchant resets their merchant portal password. When credentials become invalid, the OPay payment integration silently fails — the payment option may still appear at checkout, but when the customer attempts to pay, the transaction is rejected with a generic error. There is no obvious indication to the customer or merchant that the issue is an authentication failure on the gateway side.

This failure mode typically appears weeks or months after initial setup, when everything was working and then suddenly stops without any obvious change. Triggers include: the merchant reset their OPay Business account password (which can trigger credential rotation in some gateway configurations); OPay performed a security review and flagged the account; or the API key was manually regenerated in the merchant portal by a developer who then forgot to update the Shopify integration with the new key.

The symptom is payments consistently failing at the OPay step with a cryptic error, while everything else in the checkout works. Unlike merchant account suspension, the OPay payment option still shows — it just cannot complete transactions.

The fix is straightforward: generate new API credentials in the OPay Business merchant portal and update them in your Shopify integration. However, after updating credentials, test immediately to confirm the new keys work before assuming the issue is resolved, as some credential rotation flows require additional confirmation steps.

Fix Steps

  1. Log in to the OPay Business merchant portal → API Settings or Integration and check the status of your API credentials.
  2. If credentials show as expired or revoked, generate new API key and secret from the merchant portal.
  3. Update the new credentials in your Shopify integration code or payment plugin configuration — do not leave old credentials in any environment.
  4. Make a test payment in sandbox mode with the new credentials to confirm they are working before switching to live.
  5. After confirming sandbox works, update live credentials and do a small live test transaction to verify end-to-end.
  6. Check your Shopify server logs or OPay dashboard error logs for "401 Unauthorized" or "403 Forbidden" errors — these confirm the root cause was authentication failure.

OPay Consumer App Confused with OPay Business — Wrong Product Registered

Merchant Setup

A significant source of confusion for Egyptian Shopify merchants is that OPay operates two entirely separate products under the OPay brand: the OPay consumer wallet app (what customers use to hold money, pay bills, and receive cashback) and OPay Business (the merchant payment gateway for e-commerce integration). Registering for one does not give access to the other.

Merchants who download the OPay app from the App Store or Play Store, create a consumer account, and then search for "how to accept OPay payments on Shopify" often go in circles — the consumer OPay app has no merchant API, no payment gateway credentials, and no Shopify integration tools. The consumer app is only for personal spending and wallet management.

To accept OPay payments on Shopify, you must register for OPay Business through OPay's merchant onboarding portal, which is a separate registration flow requiring business documentation. The OPay Business dashboard is different from the consumer OPay app interface. Once registered for OPay Business, you receive an API key and secret that can be used to integrate OPay as a payment gateway.

If you have successfully integrated OPay Business but customers who want to pay with their OPay wallet are having trouble, verify that OPay Wallet is listed as an enabled payment method in your OPay Business dashboard — wallet acceptance by business merchants requires activation on your plan, separate from card payment acceptance.

Fix Steps

  1. Confirm you have registered for OPay Business (merchant gateway), not just the OPay consumer wallet app.
  2. Log in to the OPay Business merchant portal — if your login credentials are the same as your personal OPay app, you may have a consumer account only.
  3. If you only have a consumer account, complete the OPay Business registration with your commercial documentation.
  4. In OPay Business, navigate to Payment Methods and confirm that OPay Wallet acceptance is enabled on your plan (not just card payments).
  5. After confirming OPay Business is active, obtain the merchant API credentials from the Business dashboard and update your Shopify integration.
Key distinction: OPay consumer app (green icon, for personal use) ≠ OPay Business (merchant portal, for accepting payments). Both exist under the OPay brand but are completely separate systems with separate registrations, dashboards, and use cases.

Currency Mismatch — Store Set to USD Instead of EGP

Merchant Setup

OPay Business Egypt processes and settles exclusively in Egyptian Pounds (EGP). It is licensed and operates within Egypt's regulated financial infrastructure under the Central Bank of Egypt, which requires EGP as the settlement currency for domestic transactions. If your Shopify store displays prices in USD or another foreign currency, OPay will either reject the transaction or process an incorrect amount.

This mismatch is most common when an Egyptian merchant configured their Shopify store for international sales (USD pricing) and added OPay specifically for local Egyptian customers. The symptom is either a clear currency rejection error from OPay, or a payment succeeding for a dramatically wrong amount — for example, a $100 USD order being charged 100 EGP instead of approximately 5,000 EGP at current exchange rates. The latter is particularly dangerous because the customer and merchant both see a "success" but the settlement amount is wrong.

If you serve both Egyptian (EGP) and international (USD) customers, use Shopify Markets to present different currencies to different visitors. With Shopify Markets, Egyptian customers see EGP pricing and are routed to OPay, while international customers see USD pricing and are routed to a different payment gateway (such as Stripe or PayPal). This requires careful configuration and testing to ensure currency routing is correct.

Even if your store operates in EGP, verify that the amount passed to OPay's API in your integration code is in EGP piastres or EGP units as OPay's API expects — some integrations accidentally pass amounts in the wrong unit (e.g., piastres when EGP is expected, causing 100x overcharging).

Fix Steps

  1. In Shopify admin: Settings → Store details → Store currency — confirm the store currency is EGP if exclusively targeting Egyptian customers.
  2. If you serve both local and international customers, configure Shopify Markets with EGP for Egypt and a separate payment gateway for non-Egyptian markets.
  3. Review your OPay Business integration code to confirm the transaction amount is passed in the correct unit (EGP units vs piastres) as documented in OPay Business's API.
  4. Run a test transaction for a small, specific amount (e.g., 10 EGP) and verify the amount that appears in the OPay dashboard matches exactly — confirm no conversion occurred.
  5. Review recent OPay transaction history for any suspiciously low amounts (e.g., 10 EGP for an order that should be 1,000 EGP) which would indicate a currency unit error in the integration.
OPay EGP settlement: OPay Business settles to your Egyptian bank account in EGP, typically on a T+1 or T+2 schedule depending on your agreement. Confirm the settlement schedule with OPay Business to understand when funds become available after a transaction.
