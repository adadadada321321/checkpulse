Shopify Affirm Not Working — Shopify Installments Not Showing, Declined, Order Not Created (2026)

Affirm on Shopify (sold as Shopify Installments) has 8 distinct failure categories: not appearing at checkout, application declined by Affirm's credit system, orders lost after Affirm approval, US-only restrictions, and refund failures — each requires a different fix.

Updated June 2026 8 failure modes Shopify Installments · Cart Limits · Eligibility · Affirm Credit · Webhook Failures · US-Only · Shopify Payments · Refunds

Affirm on Shopify is sold as Shopify Installments — the buy-now-pay-later option powered by Affirm behind the scenes. When merchants say "Affirm not working on Shopify," they almost always mean Shopify Installments, though some stores also install a separate Affirm app from the Shopify App Store which creates its own integration with its own failure modes.

Affirm failures fall into two categories: not appearing (the installments option is invisible at checkout) and not completing (the option shows, the customer starts the application, but something fails before or after Affirm approval). Both categories look the same to the customer — "pay in installments isn't working" — but the diagnosis paths are completely different.

Affirm = Shopify Installments — Key Eligibility Check First
Before diagnosing anything else: confirm your store meets all five baseline requirements. (1) Store country: United States only. (2) Payment provider: Shopify Payments must be active — not a third-party gateway. (3) Cart total: between $50 and $17,500. (4) Product types: no gift cards, digital products, or subscriptions in cart. (5) Feature enabled: Admin → Settings → Payments → Shopify Payments → Manage → Installments toggle must be on. If any of these five are missing, Affirm will not appear regardless of any other configuration.

Failure Mode 1: Shopify Installments Not Appearing at Checkout

The most common Affirm issue: the pay-in-installments option is simply not visible at checkout. Customers don't see it, merchants can't find it. This is almost always an eligibility failure — one or more of Shopify Installments' strict requirements are not met.

Unlike payment methods such as credit cards or PayPal that show broadly, Shopify Installments has a narrow eligibility window. The option is hidden automatically when any eligibility condition fails, with no error message explaining why.

Requirement Detail Status

How to enable Shopify Installments

  1. Go to Shopify Admin → Settings → Payments
  2. Under Shopify Payments, click Manage
  3. Scroll to find the Installments section
  4. Toggle it on if available. If you don't see the Installments option, your store or merchant account may not yet be approved — contact Shopify Support
  5. Save settings and test with a cart between $50 and $17,500 containing only physical, non-subscription products
Merchant Approval Is Separate From Enabling Shopify Payments
Having Shopify Payments active does not automatically qualify you for Shopify Installments. Shopify reviews merchant accounts separately for Installments eligibility. If you don't see the Installments toggle in your Shopify Payments settings, your account hasn't been approved yet. Contact Shopify Support through your admin to request Installments access. Approval typically takes 1–5 business days.

Failure Mode 2: Cart Total Outside the Eligible Range ($50–$17,500)

Shopify Installments has a hard minimum of $50 and a hard maximum of $17,500. Outside these bounds, the pay-in-installments option is completely hidden — no message, no explanation, just absent.

The $50 minimum is the most commonly hit limit. If your average order value is below $50, your customers will never see Affirm as a payment option. The $17,500 maximum is rarely reached in most stores but can affect high-ticket merchants selling furniture, electronics, or custom products.

Cart total edge cases that cause invisible Affirm

  • Discount codes applied — a discount code that drops the cart below $50 will hide Affirm mid-checkout. Customer adds product, sees Affirm, applies 60% off coupon, Affirm disappears
  • Cart with mixed eligible/ineligible items — if an ineligible product (gift card, digital item) is in cart alongside eligible products, the entire cart becomes ineligible for Affirm
  • Tip or custom fields that affect total — some stores use checkout extensions that add tips or fees. These affect the cart total calculation for Affirm eligibility
  • Currency display — Shopify Installments applies to USD only. If you display prices in multiple currencies, the USD amount must fall within the range
Setting a Minimum for Your Store to Make Affirm Useful
If most of your products are below $50, consider whether Affirm is the right BNPL for your catalog. Alternatively, use Shopify's minimum order requirements feature to set a cart minimum for your store — this ensures customers reaching checkout with Affirm visible are already above the threshold. Merchants with average order values of $100–$500 see the highest Affirm uptake because the monthly payment amounts are compelling.

Failure Mode 3: Ineligible Product Types in Cart

Certain product types are categorically excluded from Shopify Installments, regardless of cart total or any other factor. If any of these product types exist in the cart, Affirm is hidden for the entire checkout — not just for those specific items.

Product types that block Affirm

Product Type Why Excluded Alternative
Shopify Gift Cards Regulatory — BNPL financing gift cards creates compound debt risk Standard payment methods only
Digital products No physical collateral; immediate delivery creates chargeback risk Standard payment methods only
Subscription products Recurring billing conflicts with installment loan structure Klarna, Afterpay for subscriptions (limited support)
Pre-orders (some cases) Delayed fulfillment can conflict with Affirm's loan servicing timeline Depends on your Shopify Payments account status

The most common merchant mistake: adding a single Shopify gift card option to the cart during testing — Affirm disappears and the merchant concludes Affirm is broken, when actually the test cart contained a gift card.

Failure Mode 4: Affirm Application Declined by Affirm's Credit System

When a customer selects Shopify Installments at checkout, they are redirected to Affirm's application modal. Affirm evaluates the customer and issues an approval or decline decision — entirely on Affirm's side, independently of Shopify. If Affirm declines the customer, the customer is returned to Shopify checkout and must select a different payment method.

This failure mode is often misidentified by merchants as a "store problem" because the customer reports "Affirm didn't work." In reality, the store is functioning correctly — Affirm's underwriting rejected that specific customer.

Why Affirm declines customers

  • Credit history — Affirm performs a soft credit pull. Thin credit file, recent delinquencies, or poor credit score increases decline probability
  • No valid US SSN or ITIN — Affirm requires a US Social Security Number or Individual Taxpayer Identification Number for identity and credit verification. No SSN = automatic decline
  • Existing Affirm loan balance — customers with multiple active Affirm loans are scored as higher risk and are more likely to be declined for additional loans
  • Income and repayment capacity — Affirm assesses ability to repay the specific loan amount at the installment cadence chosen
  • Purchase amount relative to credit profile — a $3,000 purchase may be declined for a customer who was approved for a $500 purchase from the same store
  • Phone number or address verification failures — Affirm's fraud checks require verifiable US contact information
Merchants Cannot Override Affirm Credit Decisions
Affirm's approval/decline decision is entirely Affirm's. There is no merchant-side setting to increase approvals, no way to approve specific customers, and no way to see why a specific customer was declined. Directing a declined customer to Affirm's customer support (1-855-423-3729 or affirm.com/help) is the only path forward for that customer. Do not ask declined customers to retry immediately — the same decision will be issued again.

What to tell declined customers

Declined customers often return to merchants frustrated. A clear response: "Affirm's lending decision was made by Affirm, not by us — we don't have access to the reasons or the ability to change the outcome. Affirm's customer support at 1-855-423-3729 can provide more information. In the meantime, we can complete your order with a credit card, debit card, PayPal, or any other payment method we accept."

Failure Mode 5: Order Not Created in Shopify After Affirm Approval

This is the highest-stakes Affirm failure mode: the customer completes the Affirm application, gets approved, sees a confirmation from Affirm, but returns to the Shopify store and no order appears. No order ID, no confirmation email, nothing in Shopify admin — yet Affirm may have already created the loan.

This happens because the Affirm approval flow works as a redirect: Shopify → Affirm modal → back to Shopify. The return redirect must complete successfully for Shopify to receive the payment confirmation and create the order. If the redirect fails mid-flight, Shopify never gets the signal.

What breaks the Affirm → Shopify redirect

  • Browser popup blocker — some browsers block the redirect if Affirm's modal was treated as a popup
  • Network interruption — mobile data dropping during the redirect, especially common on 4G/5G during handoffs
  • Customer closes the tab or browser — closing the Affirm modal before the redirect completes prevents order creation
  • Session timeout — long Affirm applications (customer takes 10+ minutes to fill out details) can cause the Shopify checkout session to expire
  • Third-party redirect interference — some checkout apps or fraud prevention tools intercept the return redirect from Affirm
  • Custom domain or headless checkout — mismatched domain configurations can cause redirect failures where Affirm sends the customer to the wrong URL
Do Not Ask Customers to Retry Without Checking Affirm First
If a customer reports an Affirm approval with no Shopify order, their Affirm loan may already be active. Asking them to retry checkout could create a second Affirm loan on top of the first. Before any retry: contact Shopify Support with the incident details, and have the customer log into affirm.com to check if a loan is showing. If a loan exists with no Shopify order, Shopify Support needs to reconcile this with Affirm — do not attempt to handle it yourself.

Investigating and preventing redirect failures

  1. Check Shopify Admin → Orders for any draft orders or incomplete orders around the timestamp
  2. Check Shopify Admin → Analytics → Reports → Abandoned checkouts to find the specific checkout attempt
  3. Ask the customer to check affirm.com → Account to confirm whether a loan was created
  4. Contact Shopify Support with: order timestamp, customer email, and any Affirm loan ID the customer can provide
  5. For prevention: audit checkout apps that run JavaScript during the checkout return flow — any app that intercepts or modifies the post-payment redirect is a candidate for causing this

Failure Mode 6: Affirm Not Available for International Customers

Affirm via Shopify Installments is exclusively available to customers in the United States. This is a hard restriction from Affirm's operating license and credit infrastructure — Affirm requires a US Social Security Number or ITIN for identity verification and credit assessment, which international customers don't have.

Shopify's checkout automatically hides Shopify Installments for customers who appear to be located outside the US, based on billing address country or IP geolocation. International customers shopping at US Shopify stores simply don't see the installments option — which is correct behavior, not a bug.

BNPL coverage by region

Region Affirm / Shopify Installments Alternative BNPL Options
United States Available Klarna, Afterpay, Sezzle
Canada Not available Afterpay, Klarna (limited)
United Kingdom Not available Klarna, Clearpay (Afterpay)
European Union Not available Klarna (most EU countries)
Australia Not available Afterpay, Zip (native)
Other regions Not available Regional BNPL providers vary

If your Shopify store serves significant international traffic and you want BNPL available to those customers, enable Klarna or Afterpay separately — both have broader geographic coverage than Affirm.

Failure Mode 7: Shopify Payments Not Active or Under Review

Shopify Installments (Affirm) is architecturally dependent on Shopify Payments. This is not a soft dependency — if Shopify Payments is inactive, suspended, under compliance review, or replaced with a third-party gateway, Shopify Installments stops working immediately and completely.

Merchants who switch payment processors or who have their Shopify Payments account flagged for review often discover this dependency for the first time when customers report that the installments option has disappeared.

Shopify Payments status effects on Affirm

Shopify Payments Status Effect on Affirm Customer Experience
Active, healthy Affirm available Installments option appears normally
Under compliance review Affirm disabled Installments option disappears silently
Suspended Affirm disabled Installments option absent; standard card payments also affected
Payouts paused May still process Affirm may still appear but Shopify Payments is restricted
Replaced by third-party gateway Affirm permanently disabled No installments option; must re-enable Shopify Payments to restore

To check Shopify Payments status: Admin → Settings → Payments → look for warning banners or action-required notices. Also check Admin → Finance → Payouts — if payouts are paused or suspended, the account is under review and Affirm is disabled.

Failure Mode 8: Refunds Not Processing Back Through Affirm

When you refund an order paid through Shopify Installments (Affirm), the refund needs to flow back through Affirm to update the customer's loan balance. Shopify handles this automatically when you process a standard refund through the Shopify admin, but the Affirm-side processing has its own timeline and its own potential failure points.

How Affirm refunds work

  • Full refund — the customer's entire Affirm loan is cancelled. Any payments already made by the customer to Affirm are refunded to the payment method the customer used with Affirm (bank account or card)
  • Partial refund — the remaining loan balance is reduced by the refund amount. Future Affirm installment payments are recalculated and reduced accordingly
  • Refund for orders fully paid — if the customer has already paid off their entire Affirm loan before you issue the refund, Affirm refunds directly to the customer's bank account on file with Affirm — not via original payment method

Common refund problems and fixes

Problem Cause Fix
Refund processed in Shopify but Affirm loan not updated Affirm processing lag (up to 10 business days) Wait 10 business days; then contact Shopify Support with order ID
Customer still being charged installments after full refund Affirm didn't receive the refund signal Contact Shopify Support with refund timestamp and amount
Partial refund not reducing installment amount Affirm loan recalculation takes 24–48 hours Wait 48 hours; confirm with Affirm customer support if not updated
Refund failed in Shopify admin Shopify Payments gateway error or account restriction Check Shopify Payments status; contact Shopify Support
Refund Timeline Expectation for Customers
When a customer asks about their Affirm refund, the standard timeline is: Shopify processes the refund immediately, Affirm receives and processes within 1–5 business days, the customer's Affirm account reflects the change within 5–10 business days. If the customer has already paid off the loan, their bank account refund from Affirm can take an additional 3–5 business days after Affirm processes. Set this expectation upfront to prevent customer service escalations.

8-Step Affirm / Shopify Installments Diagnostic Checklist

  1. Confirm store location — Shopify admin: Settings → General → Store address. Must be United States
  2. Confirm Shopify Payments is active — Admin → Settings → Payments. No warning banners. Shopify Payments must be the active provider, not a third-party gateway
  3. Check Installments toggle — Admin → Settings → Payments → Shopify Payments → Manage → Installments. Must be enabled. If not visible, contact Shopify Support for approval
  4. Test with an eligible cart — physical product only, no gift cards or digital items, cart total between $50 and $17,500, no discount code applied that drops total below $50
  5. Check Shopify Payments for flags — Admin → Finance → Payouts. Any paused payouts or compliance review indicators mean Affirm is disabled
  6. Test from a US-located browser — if testing from outside the US (VPN, international office), Affirm won't appear. Test from a US IP address
  7. Check for conflicting checkout apps — if you have a third-party Affirm app installed alongside Shopify Installments, they can conflict. Disable one and test
  8. Check browser console for JavaScript errors — open browser dev tools during checkout and look for JS errors that might suppress the payment methods render

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does Affirm show for some customers but not others on my Shopify store?

Affirm appearing for some customers but not others on the same store is almost always a customer eligibility issue rather than a store problem. Most common patterns: Affirm shows for US customers but not international customers (expected — Affirm is US-only); Affirm shows when cart is over $50 but disappears after a discount code is applied (discount drops cart below minimum); Affirm doesn't show for carts with gift cards or digital items mixed with physical products. If Affirm is consistent for all US customers with eligible carts, your store configuration is correct.

Can I use Affirm with a third-party payment gateway on Shopify?

No. Shopify Installments (Affirm's native Shopify integration) requires Shopify Payments as your active payment provider. If you use a third-party gateway such as Stripe, PayPal, or Authorize.net as your primary processor, Shopify Installments is unavailable. Some merchants install a separate Affirm app from the Shopify App Store as an alternative — this app-based integration has different requirements and uses a different checkout flow than native Shopify Installments. If you need Affirm with a third-party gateway, check Affirm's app listing in the Shopify App Store.

Why did Affirm work before but suddenly stopped for all customers?

A sudden Affirm outage for all customers simultaneously points to a store-level change rather than individual customer issues. Most likely causes: Shopify Payments was suspended or placed under compliance review (check Admin → Finance → Payouts for warnings); Shopify Payments was switched to a third-party gateway (Affirm requires Shopify Payments); the Installments toggle was accidentally disabled during a settings change; or Shopify issued an update that changed how payment methods are rendered in your theme. Check Admin → Settings → Payments first and look for any warning banners or status changes.

Do I get paid immediately for Affirm orders?

Yes. When a customer completes an Affirm purchase on your Shopify store, you receive the full purchase amount from Affirm immediately — typically within 1–3 business days, the same as other Shopify Payments transactions. Affirm takes on the credit risk and collects installment payments from the customer over time. The customer's payment schedule with Affirm (monthly installments over 3–36 months) is between Affirm and the customer — it does not affect when or how much you receive. Affirm charges a merchant fee (percentage of transaction) similar to a credit card processing fee; this is deducted before payout.
