Shopify Checkout Login Not Working — 8 Causes Explained

OTP email not arriving? Guest checkout disappeared? Shop Pay logging customers into the wrong account? B2B customers seeing retail prices? Here are all 8 root causes for Shopify login failures at checkout — for both merchants and customers.

Updated June 2026 8 failure modes New Customer Accounts · OTP · Shop Pay · B2B Login · SSO · Multipass · Headless API

Shopify's customer account and login system changed fundamentally with New Customer Accounts (NCA), introduced in 2023 and becoming the default path for new stores in 2024. Passwords are gone — replaced by 6-digit OTP codes sent via email. The classic username/password login page at /account/login still exists for stores on Classic Accounts, but NCA stores use a completely different authentication flow built on Shopify's Shop ecosystem.

The result: eight distinct failure modes that all look like "login not working" but have completely different root causes. Most are not bugs — they are behaviors that changed with NCA that merchants and customers haven't encountered before.

Classic Accounts vs New Customer Accounts (NCA) — Key Differences
Classic Accounts (Settings → Customer accounts → Classic): password-based login at /account/login, "Continue as guest" available, third-party SSO apps work, Multipass supported.

New Customer Accounts (NCA) (Settings → Customer accounts → New): passwordless 6-digit OTP via email, login powered by Shop account ecosystem (shop.app), third-party login apps not supported, social login (Google/Facebook) not natively supported, guest checkout controlled by "Accounts are optional / required" toggle.

Check which system your store uses: Admin → Settings → Customer accounts. The setting controls which login experience customers see at checkout.

Failure Mode 1: "Continue as Guest" Disappeared — New Customer Accounts + Required Accounts

The most common login complaint after enabling New Customer Accounts: customers report they "can't check out without creating an account" or that "the guest checkout option is gone." Both are correct — but for a specific reason.

What controls whether guest checkout is available

Account System Setting Guest Checkout Available?
Classic Accounts Accounts are optional Yes — customers see "Continue as guest" and "Log in"
Classic Accounts Accounts are required No — customers must log in or create a password account
New Customer Accounts (NCA) Accounts are optional Yes — customers see "Continue as guest" or log in via OTP
New Customer Accounts (NCA) Accounts are required No — customers must authenticate via OTP; no guest path exists in NCA required mode

Why merchants accidentally lose guest checkout

Shopify prompts merchants to try New Customer Accounts via an Admin banner. Merchants who click through and enable NCA without changing the account requirement setting inherit whatever setting was active on Classic Accounts. If the classic setting was "Accounts are required," NCA is also "required" — and since NCA has no password recovery flow, customers who never set up an OTP-linked Shop account cannot authenticate and cannot check out.

  1. Go to Admin → Settings → Customer accounts
  2. Under "Login experience," confirm which account system is active (Classic or New)
  3. Check the account requirement setting: "Accounts are optional" vs "Accounts are required"
  4. If you want guest checkout: set to "Accounts are optional" — this restores the guest path regardless of which account system is active
  5. If you need required accounts for a specific reason (age verification, members-only store): use Locksmith or a Checkout UI Extension (Plus) to enforce requirements more granularly, and keep the checkout account setting as "optional" to preserve the guest flow for non-restricted products
Impact of Losing Guest Checkout
Research consistently shows that requiring account creation at checkout increases abandonment by 20-35% for first-time buyers. If you switched to NCA required accounts and your conversion rate dropped shortly after, this is the likely cause. Use Admin → Analytics → Reports → Checkout funnel to check if the "Reached account/login step" to "Completed checkout" ratio changed after the account setting change.

Failure Mode 2: OTP / Magic Link Email Not Arriving

New Customer Accounts replaces passwords with a 6-digit one-time code sent to the customer's email address. The OTP expires after 10 minutes. If the email doesn't arrive within that window, the customer cannot log in — there is no password fallback.

Why OTP emails fail to arrive

Cause Frequency Fix
Email landed in spam / promotions folder Very common Customer checks spam; merchant can reduce spam risk by configuring SPF/DKIM for their domain in Admin → Settings → Notifications → Sender email
Customer typed a different email than their registered account Very common OTP goes to the typed email — customer should check if they registered with a different address; merchant can look up the customer in Admin → Customers
OTP expired (more than 10 minutes elapsed) Common Customer requests a new code; mobile browser tab switching during OTP wait can cause this — customer should keep checkout tab open
Corporate / enterprise email filtering Common Microsoft 365 / Google Workspace with aggressive filtering blocks Shopify OTP emails; customer must whitelist mail.shopify.com or use a personal email address
Email address not associated with any customer account Common NCA creates an account on first OTP use — the email is valid but shows no order history; customer's prior orders were under a different email
Email provider delayed delivery (peak load) Less common Gmail/Outlook occasionally delays transactional emails by 3-8 minutes during peak periods; wait up to 10 minutes before requesting a new code
Customer Self-Help for OTP Not Received
In order: (1) check spam/promotions, (2) check that the email shown on the login page is correct — it shows a partially masked version like "c****r@example.com", (3) if wrong email, click "Use a different email" on the OTP entry screen, (4) wait up to 5 minutes before requesting a new code, (5) try a different device or browser if the current one is blocking popup notifications. If none of these work, contact the merchant — the merchant can look up the customer's registered email in Admin → Customers and share which email address is on file.

Failure Mode 3: Shop Pay Logs Customer Into Wrong Account — Mismatched Email Identity

Shopify's New Customer Accounts is built on the Shop account ecosystem (shop.app). When a customer authenticates via Shop Pay at checkout, they authenticate using their Shop account — which may be registered to a different email address than the one they used for prior purchases on the store.

How the mismatch happens

Customer made previous orders on the store using alice@company.com. They have a Shop account at alice@personal.com. When they tap "Continue with Shop Pay" at checkout, they authenticate as alice@personal.com — which is a separate customer record with no order history on the merchant's store. The customer sees a "Welcome" screen and an empty order history, and believes their account was reset or their orders were lost.

What actually happened to their orders

  • The orders still exist — they're attached to the alice@company.com customer record in Admin → Customers
  • The customer just authenticated as a different identity (alice@personal.com)
  • Shopify does not automatically merge customer records when the email addresses differ
  • The customer can see their old orders by logging in with alice@company.com — but NCA may show them the Shop Pay option first, and they may not realize they can use a different email

How to resolve a duplicate customer identity

  1. Merchant goes to Admin → Customers and searches for both email addresses
  2. Confirm which record has the order history (the original purchase email)
  3. Use the "Merge customers" option (available on Shopify plans with customer merge enabled) to combine both records — the order history, tags, and notes from both are merged into one record
  4. If merge is not available for your plan, manually update the customer's Shop account email to match their purchase email at shop.app — or ask the customer to update their Shop account email and log in again
  5. Customer should then authenticate with the Shop account that matches the purchase email
This Affects Loyalty Points and Subscription Renewals
If a customer's loyalty app (Smile.io, Yotpo) or subscription app (Recharge, Bold) tracks identity by email, the mismatched Shop Pay login creates a second profile with zero points and no subscription history. The customer's points and subscription are still on the original email record. Merchants must manually merge or transfer the loyalty/subscription data after resolving the identity mismatch — this is not automatic.

Failure Mode 4: B2B Customers Can't Access B2B Pricing After Login

Shopify B2B (Shopify Plus) uses an entirely separate authentication and authorization system from B2C customer accounts. A B2B customer who logs in through the standard storefront login page (/account/login or the NCA OTP flow) will see retail pricing — not their assigned B2B price list — even if their email is associated with a B2B company in Shopify Admin.

Why standard login doesn't grant B2B access

B2B authentication on Shopify validates three things simultaneously: (1) the customer's identity, (2) their company association, and (3) which company location and price list applies to this session. This context is embedded in the B2B access URL or invitation link that the merchant sends. Standard storefront login authenticates identity only — company context is never established, so B2B pricing rules don't activate.

Login Method Gets B2B Pricing? Sees Order History? Can Place B2B Orders?
Standard storefront /account/login No — retail pricing Personal orders only (not company orders) No
B2B invitation link (from email) Yes Company purchase history Yes
B2B portal URL (direct link to company page) Yes Company purchase history Yes
Shop Pay at storefront checkout No — B2B pricing not applied via standard checkout Personal account only No

Setting up B2B login correctly

  1. Admin → Customers → Companies — create the company record and assign the customer as a contact
  2. Assign a price list, payment terms (net 30/60/90 or immediate), and default shipping address to the company
  3. Send the B2B invitation from the company record — Shopify sends an email with a unique access link
  4. Alternatively, share the direct B2B portal URL with the customer — it's the company's storefront URL with company context embedded
  5. B2B customers should bookmark the portal URL and use it for every session — not navigate to the main storefront
B2B is Shopify Plus Only
Native B2B with price lists, net payment terms, and company accounts requires Shopify Plus. Non-Plus merchants who need B2B functionality must use third-party apps (Wholesale Club, BSS Commerce B2B, Wholesale Gorilla) — these apps implement their own login gates and pricing rules, which work on Classic Accounts but may conflict with New Customer Accounts. If you're on a non-Plus plan and your B2B app stopped working after enabling NCA, check the app's compatibility with NCA in its documentation or support channel.

Failure Mode 5: Third-Party SSO and Social Login Apps Break After Switching to NCA

Classic Accounts support an extensive ecosystem of third-party login apps: Google OAuth, Facebook Login, Apple Sign-In, enterprise SSO (SAML/OIDC), and social login aggregators. All of these stop working after a merchant switches to New Customer Accounts.

Why SSO apps break on NCA

Third-party login apps for Shopify Classic Accounts work by intercepting the /account/login page, redirecting customers to the OAuth provider, and then creating or authenticating a Shopify customer record using the Classic Accounts customer API. New Customer Accounts replaces this entire flow with the Shop ecosystem's own authentication — the /account/login page now shows the NCA OTP interface, and the Classic Accounts customer API endpoints that SSO apps rely on are not available for NCA stores.

SSO Type Works on Classic Accounts? Works on NCA? Path Forward
Google / Facebook OAuth (via Shopify app) Yes No Stay on Classic Accounts, or build custom Multipass integration (Plus only)
Apple Sign-In (via Shopify app) Yes No Same as above
Enterprise SAML / OIDC SSO Yes (via Multipass) Partial Multipass still works on NCA stores for redirecting authenticated sessions — but requires Plus and custom implementation
Custom OAuth (Multipass, Plus) Yes Yes Multipass is supported on NCA stores — it pre-authenticates and redirects; no conflict
Do Not Switch to NCA If You Depend on Social Login Apps
If your store uses a social login app (OmniLogin, One Click Social Login, Easy Social Login, etc.) and you switch to NCA, those apps will immediately stop functioning. The app will show a broken login page or silently do nothing. Before switching, check: (1) is social login a meaningful driver of account creation in your store? (2) does the social login app vendor have an NCA-compatible version? Most do not as of 2026. If social login is important, stay on Classic Accounts.

Failure Mode 6: Login Redirect Loop After Checkout Session Expires

A customer starts checkout, gets to the login step, and is redirected to /account/login?checkout_url=https://checkout.shopify.com/.... If they take more than 10-15 minutes at this step (or close and reopen the tab), the checkout session expires. After logging in, Shopify redirects them back to the now-expired checkout URL — which redirects them back to login. The result is an infinite redirect loop.

The loop mechanism

  • Checkout session timeout: 10-15 minutes of inactivity kills the session
  • The checkout_url parameter in the login redirect contains a session-specific token that is now invalid
  • After logging in, Shopify follows the redirect to the expired checkout URL
  • The expired checkout redirects to /cart or back to /account/login — creating the loop
  • Clearing cookies and starting a fresh checkout resolves it, but customers don't know to do this

When this is most likely to happen

  • Mobile: customer switches to another app for OTP, iOS evicts the browser tab from memory, session context is lost on return
  • Customer is using a slow connection and takes extra time at the login step
  • Customer opens the login email link in a different browser than where checkout started
  • Customer bookmarks the login URL and returns to it later — the embedded checkout_url is stale

How to diagnose and fix

  1. Customer: clear all cookies for the store domain, go back to the product page, add to cart, and start a fresh checkout
  2. Customer: if on mobile, keep the checkout tab open and don't switch apps during OTP entry — tap directly in the tab and type the OTP manually without switching to the Messages or email app
  3. Merchant: ensure your checkout pages load fast (under 3 seconds) to minimize the time customers spend on the login step before session expires — CheckPulse monitors this
  4. Merchant (Plus): consider using Shop Pay as the primary express checkout button — authenticated Shop Pay customers skip the login step entirely
Reducing Login Step Drop-off With Shop Pay
Returning customers with a Shop account and a saved payment method can bypass the login step entirely using Shop Pay one-tap checkout. This eliminates the OTP friction for customers who have previously completed a Shop Pay order. Enable Shop Pay in Admin → Settings → Payments → Accelerated checkouts. Note: Shop Pay one-tap has its own failure modes — see the One-Click Purchase Not Working guide.

Failure Mode 7: Multipass Token Expired — Headless and External System Login Failures

Merchants on Shopify Plus who authenticate customers from an external system (CRM, membership platform, ERP, custom auth server) use Multipass — a server-side token mechanism that pre-authenticates a customer and redirects them into a Shopify session. Multipass is the correct approach for enterprise SSO on Shopify. But it has the tightest token expiry window of any common SSO system: tokens are valid for only 90 seconds.

Common Multipass failure modes

Failure Symptom Fix
Token generated too early (server processing delay) Customer clicks link, reaches Shopify, gets "Invalid token" error or is logged out Generate the Multipass token as close to the redirect as possible — in the HTTP request handler that serves the login redirect, not in a background job
Clock skew between auth server and Shopify Token appears valid on server but Shopify rejects it as expired Ensure the auth server's system time is synchronized via NTP — even 2-minute clock skew can cause Multipass failures; verify with date on the server
Incorrect store URL format in token Redirect goes to wrong store or returns 404 The return_to field must be a full URL on the store domain — e.g., https://store.myshopify.com/ not just /
Multipass secret used in wrong encoding Token is generated but Shopify rejects it as invalid signature Multipass uses AES-128-CBC + SHA256 HMAC with specific key derivation — use an officially maintained library or the exact Shopify documentation implementation; do not improvise the crypto
Multipass token logged or cached Replaying the same token URL fails — "This link has already been used" Multipass tokens are single-use — each login attempt must generate a new token; never cache or reuse tokens

Generating a Multipass token (server-side only)

// Node.js example — server-side ONLY, never expose the Multipass secret client-side
const crypto = require('crypto');

function generateMultipassToken(customerData, multipassSecret) {
  // Derive two 16-byte keys from the secret using SHA256
  const keyMaterial = crypto.createHash('sha256').update(multipassSecret).digest();
  const encryptionKey = keyMaterial.subarray(0, 16);
  const signingKey = keyMaterial.subarray(16, 32);

  // Serialize customer data to JSON with current timestamp
  const customerJson = JSON.stringify({
    ...customerData,
    created_at: new Date().toISOString(), // must be fresh — 90 second window
  });

  // Encrypt with AES-128-CBC and a random IV
  const iv = crypto.randomBytes(16);
  const cipher = crypto.createCipheriv('aes-128-cbc', encryptionKey, iv);
  const encrypted = Buffer.concat([iv, cipher.update(customerJson, 'utf8'), cipher.final()]);

  // Sign with HMAC-SHA256
  const signature = crypto.createHmac('sha256', signingKey).update(encrypted).digest();

  // Base64 URL-encode
  return Buffer.concat([encrypted, signature])
    .toString('base64')
    .replace(/\+/g, '-').replace(/\//g, '_');
}

// Usage: redirect customer to this URL immediately after generating
const token = generateMultipassToken({ email: 'customer@example.com' }, process.env.SHOPIFY_MULTIPASS_SECRET);
const loginUrl = `https://yourstore.myshopify.com/account/login/multipass/${token}`;
Multipass is Shopify Plus Only — and the Secret Must Never Be Client-Side
Multipass is only available to Shopify Plus merchants. The Multipass secret is found in Admin → Settings → Customer accounts → Multipass (visible only on Plus). This secret must never be exposed in client-side JavaScript, browser requests, or public endpoints — if the secret leaks, anyone can generate tokens and authenticate as any customer email on your store. Generate tokens server-side only, behind authentication, and rotate the secret immediately if it is ever exposed.

Failure Mode 8: Headless Storefront — Customer Account API Auth Flow Not Implemented Correctly

Headless Shopify storefronts (Hydrogen, custom Next.js, or any build using the Storefront API) must implement customer authentication manually. Shopify provides the Customer Account API — a separate OAuth 2.0 PKCE-based API distinct from the classic REST Customer API. Missing or incorrectly implemented steps in this flow are the most common cause of headless login failures.

The Customer Account API OAuth 2.0 PKCE flow

Step What Must Happen Common Mistake
1. Generate PKCE code verifier Generate a cryptographically random 43-128 character string; derive code_challenge using SHA-256 Not storing the code verifier in session storage between the redirect steps — leads to "invalid_grant" error on token exchange
2. Build authorization URL Redirect customer to https://shopify.com/authentication/{shop-id}/oauth/authorize with client_id, scope, redirect_uri, code_challenge, state Using the old REST Storefront API customer endpoint instead of the new Customer Account API URL; not registering the redirect_uri in Partner Dashboard first
3. Customer authenticates (OTP) Shopify handles the OTP flow — customer does not see your UI during this step Trying to implement custom OTP UI — this step is Shopify-hosted; you redirect to Shopify and get the code back
4. Receive authorization code Shopify redirects to your redirect_uri with ?code=... and state=... Not validating the state parameter — CSRF vulnerability; customer lands on redirect page but state mismatch triggers error
5. Exchange code for tokens POST to token endpoint with code + code_verifier; receive access_token + refresh_token Not persisting the refresh_token — access tokens expire in 5 minutes; without refresh_token, customer is logged out on next API call
6. Make authenticated requests Pass access_token in Authorization header to Customer Account API GraphQL endpoint Using the Storefront API endpoint instead of the Customer Account API endpoint — these are different URLs with different schemas
7. Refresh expired tokens Use refresh_token to get a new access_token when the current one expires Not implementing token refresh — customers appear to be logged out every 5 minutes; login loop results

Customer Account API vs Storefront API — key differences

Aspect Customer Account API Storefront API
Authentication OAuth 2.0 PKCE — customer-specific tokens API key — store-level access
Customer data access Full: orders, addresses, profile, B2B context Limited: only what the customer shared during checkout
Endpoint https://shopify.com/authentication/{shop-id}/... https://{shop}.myshopify.com/api/...
Token expiry Access: 5 min / Refresh: 7 days API key doesn't expire
Availability All Shopify plans All Shopify plans
NCA compatibility Full Classic customer mutations only (deprecated for NCA)
Enable Customer Account API in Admin
The Customer Account API must be explicitly enabled before your headless store can use it. Go to Admin → Settings → Customer accounts → Customer Account API → Enable. You also need to register your redirect URIs in the Shopify Partner Dashboard under your app's configuration. Missing this step results in "redirect_uri mismatch" errors during OAuth flow.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I switch back from New Customer Accounts to Classic Accounts?

Yes, as of 2026, merchants can switch back to Classic Accounts in Admin → Settings → Customer accounts. Customer data is preserved in both directions — switching does not delete customer records. However, any customers who created accounts exclusively via NCA OTP will need to set a password when they first log in after the switch to Classic, since they have no password in the system. Shopify sends a "set your password" email flow when this scenario is detected.

Why does the Shopify login page look different on my store vs another store?

Stores on New Customer Accounts show a passwordless OTP login page hosted on Shopify's infrastructure (accounts.shopify.com or the Shop ecosystem). Stores on Classic Accounts show a customizable login page within the storefront theme. The visual appearance and UX of the login page is determined by which account system is active — not by theme settings alone. On NCA stores, the login page cannot be customized with Liquid or theme CSS; only Checkout UI Extensions (Plus) can add custom elements near the account login entry point.

Why can't my customer log in on mobile but they can on desktop?

Mobile-specific login failures usually have three causes: (1) the OTP email arrives in the customer's email app while the Shopify tab is backgrounded — switching to the email app on iOS causes Safari to evict the checkout tab from memory, so the OTP session is lost on return; (2) the customer's mobile email client opens the OTP notification in a separate browser and the session context doesn't transfer; (3) the mobile browser's cookie settings are stricter than desktop (Safari ITP, Firefox Enhanced Tracking Protection) and block the session cookies that Shopify uses to maintain the login state between the OTP entry and the checkout continuation.

What happens to a customer's saved addresses and payment methods when the store switches to NCA?

Saved addresses from Classic Accounts are preserved and accessible via the new account portal. However, saved payment methods (stored via Shop Pay or Shopify's vault) are managed by the Shop Pay ecosystem, not by the storefront directly — they transfer with the customer's Shop account, not the Classic Account. Customers who did not use Shop Pay may find that their previously saved payment methods are not present in the new NCA account portal. They will need to add payment methods again via the Shop Pay interface.
