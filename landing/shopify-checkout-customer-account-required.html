Shopify Checkout — Customer Accounts

Shopify Checkout Requires Customer Account — Guest Checkout Blocked, "Continue as Guest" Missing, New Customer Accounts Login Prompt

When Shopify checkout shows "Please log in to continue" or removes the "Continue as guest" option, five root causes account for almost all cases: the Accounts Required setting, Shopify's New Customer Accounts migration confusing existing customers, a third-party app enforcing login independently of Shopify's setting, Shopify Plus B2B Company accounts conflicting with storefront accounts, or a Shop Pay auth state conflict. This guide covers all five, seven silent failure modes, and exact fix paths.

Account Mode Overview

Three Account Settings, Two Account Systems

Shopify's customer account behavior is controlled by two independent variables: the account setting (Disabled / Optional / Required) and the account system type (Legacy Customer Accounts or New Customer Accounts). Most checkout login issues come from misunderstanding how these interact.

Setting: Disabled
No Accounts — Pure Guest
No login prompt appears. No account creation offered. Customers check out as guest only. All checkout contact info is entered fresh each time. Marketing consent collected at checkout but not linked to a customer record.
Setting: Optional (Recommended)
Guest + Login Both Available
Guest checkout available. Login or account creation offered but not required. Repeat customers can log in for faster checkout. Most stores should use this setting — it maximizes conversion while supporting customer loyalty.
Setting: Required
Login Mandatory — No Guest
No "Continue as guest" button. Customer must log in or create an account before placing an order. Appropriate for B2B wholesale, membership-only stores, or age-gated purchases. Causes significant friction for first-time buyers.
System Type A
Legacy Customer Accounts
Login URL: /account/login. Authentication: email + password. Password reset via email link. Customer portal at /account/orders. Broad app compatibility (most third-party loyalty, membership, and review apps built for this). Being phased out by Shopify.
System Type B
New Customer Accounts
Login URL: /a/account. Authentication: passwordless — one-time 6-digit code sent via email, expires in 15 minutes. No password field. Customer portal at /a/account/orders. Rolled out 2023–2024. Some third-party apps not yet compatible. Existing customer passwords are NOT migrated.

Root Causes

Five Root Causes of Customer Account Issues at Checkout

Each cause has a distinct fix path. Identify which applies before making changes — some are a single setting toggle, others require app-level investigation.

Root Cause 1 — Most Common
"Accounts are required" Setting Enabled
Admin → Settings → Checkout → Customer accounts = "Required." This is the most common cause. No guest checkout is possible in this mode. Fix: change to "Optional." Takes effect immediately for new sessions.
Root Cause 2
New Customer Accounts Migration — No Password Field
Store was migrated to New Customer Accounts (passwordless). Existing customers can't find the password field. Legacy passwords are not transferred — customers must use one-time code. Fix: communicate the change clearly via announcement bar or email.
Root Cause 3
Third-Party App Enforcing Login Independently
Locksmith, Wholesale Club, Bold Customer Groups, or a B2B portal app is enforcing authentication at the product, collection, or cart level — independent of Shopify's account setting. Fix: disable apps one at a time to isolate; adjust the app's login requirement settings.
Root Cause 4 — Plus Only
B2B Company Accounts vs Storefront Accounts Confusion
Shopify Plus B2B uses "Company accounts" — a separate system from storefront customer accounts. A B2B buyer logged into the storefront is NOT automatically logged into a company account. Fix: use the dedicated B2B login URL (/a/account) and Company accounts flow for wholesale customers.
Root Cause 5
Shop Pay Auth State Conflict
Shop Pay uses Shopify's identity infrastructure but has its own session. When accounts are required, Shop Pay customers may bypass the requirement OR get stuck in a re-authentication loop if their Shop Pay session conflicts with the store's account requirement. Fix: test with Shop Pay disabled to isolate; contact Shopify Support if the conflict persists.
Deep Dive A

New Customer Accounts vs Legacy Accounts — What Changed and What Breaks

Account System Migration

What Shopify Changed (2023–2024)

Shopify rolled out New Customer Accounts as the replacement for Legacy Customer Accounts starting in 2023. The core change: Legacy accounts used email + password. New Customer Accounts use passwordless authentication — customers enter their email address and receive a one-time 6-digit code (valid for 15 minutes) every time they log in. There is no password field. There is no password reset flow. The customer portal URL changed from /account to /a/account.

Shopify's stated reasons for the change: improved security (no password to steal or reuse), better mobile conversion (customers don't need to remember a password), and alignment with modern passwordless authentication patterns.

What was NOT migrated: Customer passwords are permanently discarded when a store moves to New Customer Accounts. The customer's identity (name, order history, addresses) is preserved, but the authentication credential is gone. The first time an existing customer tries to log in, they receive a one-time code — many interpret this as evidence that their account was deleted.

Customer Portal URLs

URL Changes That Break Bookmarks and App Integrations

Legacy Customer Accounts used /account as the base URL:

/account/login → Login page (email + password) /account/register → Registration page /account/orders → Order history /account/addresses → Saved addresses /account/forgot_password → Password reset

New Customer Accounts use /a/account as the base URL:

/a/account → Customer portal home (passwordless) /a/account/orders → Order history /a/account/profile → Profile and address management (no /register or /forgot_password — code-based auth replaces both)

Shopify does redirect /account/login to the new portal, but any deep links (bookmarked order pages, loyalty app links, email templates pointing to /account/orders) may break or redirect incorrectly. Third-party apps that hardcode /account URLs will need updates.

Deep Dive B

The "Accounts Required" Setting — When to Use It and When Not To

Setting Location and Impact

Where the Setting Lives and What It Controls

Location: Shopify Admin → Settings → Checkout → Customer contact method (scroll down) → Customer accounts section. The section header says "Customer accounts" and shows a radio button group with three options.

Accounts are disabled: No login UI appears anywhere in checkout. No account creation is offered. The customer enters their contact info (email) fresh every time. Best for stores that don't want any account friction and don't need repeat purchase benefits. Downside: no order history for customers, no saved addresses, harder to run loyalty programs.

Accounts are optional (recommended for most stores): Guest checkout is available. At the email step, customers can choose to log in or continue as guest. After purchase, they may be invited to create an account. This setting maximizes checkout conversion while still supporting loyalty and repeat purchase features.

Accounts are required: No guest path exists. A customer who is not logged in will see a login/create account screen and cannot proceed until they authenticate. Use cases: wholesale stores where pricing is customer-specific, membership-only stores, age-verification stores that use account data to confirm eligibility, and subscription stores where every purchase creates a customer record. Do not use this setting for general retail — it reduces first-time conversion significantly (studies consistently show 25–35% of cart abandonments are caused by forced account creation).

Changing the Setting

How to Change the Account Setting and What to Expect

Changing from Required to Optional or Disabled takes effect immediately for new checkout sessions. Customers already in a checkout session at the moment of change may still see the old behavior until their session expires (typically 24 hours). No data is lost when changing the setting.

Changing from Legacy to New Customer Accounts (the system type, not just the setting) is more significant: go to Admin → Settings → Customer accounts → "Upgrade to new customer accounts." This migration is irreversible on non-Plus stores without contacting Shopify Support. On Shopify Plus, you can switch back by contacting Support. Before migrating: verify that all third-party apps you use (loyalty, membership, wholesale, reviews) have a compatibility update for New Customer Accounts.

If you need accounts required but want to minimize friction: Enable Shop Pay — Shop Pay customers bypass the account creation step because Shop Pay has its own identity system. Shop Pay users can check out faster than a guest checkout flow. This is the recommended approach for stores that want required accounts but don't want the friction to hurt conversion.

Deep Dive C

Third-Party App Gating — How to Diagnose Which App Is Blocking Checkout

App-Level Login Enforcement

How Apps Block Checkout Independently of Shopify's Setting

Shopify's built-in account setting controls what happens after the customer reaches the checkout URL. But third-party apps can intercept customers much earlier — at the product page, collection page, add-to-cart action, or cart page — before Shopify's own checkout even loads. This means you can have accounts set to "Optional" in Shopify Admin and still have customers blocked behind a login wall, because an app is enforcing login before the checkout URL is ever reached.

Common apps that enforce login:

  • Locksmith — locks pages, products, collections behind login or passcode conditions. Very common for wholesale and membership stores.
  • Wholesale Club / Wholesale Helper — shows wholesale pricing only to logged-in customers tagged as wholesale. Guest visitors see retail prices or can't add to cart.
  • Bold Customer Groups — segments customers into pricing tiers; requires login to display the correct tier pricing at checkout.
  • B2B Portal apps (Handshake, B2B Ninja, etc.) — require login to the B2B portal before any wholesale order can be placed.
  • Age Verification apps — some require account creation to record verified age and store consent records.
Diagnosis Steps

How to Isolate Which App Is Causing the Block

Step 1 — Confirm Shopify's own setting is not the cause: Go to Admin → Settings → Checkout → Customer accounts. If it already says "Optional" or "Disabled," the issue is not Shopify's built-in setting.

Step 2 — Test in a private/incognito window: Open your store in a private browser window (no cookies, no existing session). Try to add a product to cart and proceed to checkout. If you see a login prompt before the checkout URL, an app is intercepting.

Step 3 — Disable apps one by one: Go to Admin → Apps → find apps that handle access control, wholesale pricing, or membership. Use "Pause" or the app's own disable option (not uninstall — you don't want to lose settings). After disabling each app, test the checkout again in a fresh private window. The app that resolves the issue when disabled is the one enforcing login.

Step 4 — Adjust the app's settings: Once you've identified the app, go into its configuration and look for settings like "Require login to view prices," "Require login to add to cart," "Restrict checkout to authenticated customers." Adjust these to match your intended behavior. Re-enable the app and test again.

Note: If you want some customers (wholesale/members) to require login while others (retail/guests) do not, most access control apps support customer tag-based conditions — e.g., "require login only for customers without the 'wholesale' tag." This is the correct approach rather than using Shopify's global "Accounts required" setting, which applies to everyone.

Fix Checklist

6-Step Customer Account Fix Path

  • 1
    Check the account setting in Shopify Admin Admin → Settings → Checkout → Customer accounts. If set to "Required" and you want guest checkout, change to "Optional." Applies immediately to new sessions.
  • 2
    Identify your account system type (Legacy or New) In Admin → Settings → Customer accounts, check whether you see "Legacy customer accounts" or "New customer accounts." If New: customers use one-time code login — no password field. If you recently migrated and customers are confused, add a notice via announcement bar.
  • 3
    Test checkout in a fresh private/incognito window Open your store in an incognito window (no cookies, no session). Add a product to cart and attempt checkout. If you see a login prompt before the checkout URL (/checkouts/...), an app is intercepting before Shopify's checkout.
  • 4
    Disable access-control apps one by one to isolate the cause Temporarily disable Locksmith, Wholesale Club, Bold Customer Groups, or other gating apps. After each disable, test in a fresh private window. When the login prompt disappears, you've found the app. Adjust that app's settings — don't uninstall it.
  • 5
    Verify one-time code email deliverability (New Customer Accounts) If on New Customer Accounts with "Accounts required," customers who never receive the login code are completely blocked. Check Admin → Settings → Notifications → Sender email and verify SPF/DKIM is configured for your sending domain. Test the code email flow yourself from an unrelated email address.
  • 6
    Check third-party app compatibility with New Customer Accounts If loyalty points are missing or membership access is lost after login, the app may not yet be compatible with New Customer Accounts. Check the app's documentation or changelog for a 2024+ compatibility update. If none exists, contact the app developer or consider reverting to Legacy Customer Accounts (contact Shopify Support).
FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I remove the customer account requirement from Shopify checkout?
Go to Shopify Admin → Settings → Checkout → Customer accounts section. Change the setting from "Accounts are required" to "Accounts are optional." This takes effect immediately for new checkout sessions — no redeployment needed. If a third-party app (Locksmith, Wholesale Club, Bold Customer Groups) is also enforcing login, you will need to adjust that app's settings separately, because the app enforcement is independent of Shopify's built-in account setting.
Why can't my customers find the password field at Shopify checkout?
Your store was likely migrated to New Customer Accounts, which is Shopify's passwordless authentication system. Customers enter their email address and receive a one-time 6-digit code (valid for 15 minutes) instead of entering a password. Existing customer passwords from Legacy accounts are permanently discarded — they are not migrated to the new system. Customers who had passwords will receive a one-time code the first time they try to log in on the new system, which many interpret as their account being deleted. The fix: add an announcement or help article explaining that Shopify has moved to passwordless login and that customers should check their email for a code when they try to log in.
Why do some customers see a login prompt at Shopify checkout even though accounts are optional?
A third-party app is most likely enforcing login before the checkout even loads. Apps like Locksmith, Wholesale Club, and Bold Customer Groups can require authentication at the product, collection, or cart level — independent of Shopify's built-in account setting. To diagnose: test your store in a fresh private/incognito window. If you see a login prompt before the checkout URL (/checkouts/...), disable apps one at a time until the prompt disappears. Adjust that app's login-requirement settings rather than changing Shopify's global account setting.
How do I enable guest checkout on Shopify?
Go to Admin → Settings → Checkout → Customer accounts and select either "Accounts are disabled" (no login option at all, pure guest) or "Accounts are optional" (guest checkout available, login also offered). "Accounts are optional" is recommended for most stores because it lets repeat customers log in for faster checkout while still allowing new customers to purchase without creating an account. If you are on Shopify Plus with B2B wholesale customers, the guest checkout setting applies to your retail storefront — B2B Company accounts have separate access controls configured in Admin → Sales channels → Wholesale.
Why do loyalty points not show up after Shopify's New Customer Accounts migration?
Most loyalty apps (Smile.io, LoyaltyLion, Yotpo Loyalty, Rise.ai) were built to integrate with Legacy Customer Accounts and use the legacy session format and /account URL to look up points balances. After migrating to New Customer Accounts, the session token format and portal URL changed, and some apps could not match the new session to the existing customer record — showing 0 points or not recognizing the logged-in customer. Fix: check the loyalty app's documentation or changelog for a "New Customer Accounts compatibility" update (most major apps released these in 2024-2025). Install the update and follow any migration steps. If no update is available, contact the app developer. As a last resort, contact Shopify Support about reverting to Legacy Customer Accounts — this is possible on Plus stores and on some non-Plus stores with Support assistance.
