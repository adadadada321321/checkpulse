Shopify Virtual Card Not Working — Bank VCN, Privacy.com, Corporate Card Declined (2026)

Virtual card declined on Shopify? Virtual cards fail in ways that physical cards don't — because they carry constraints like single-use limits, merchant locks, amount caps, and unique billing addresses that trip Shopify's payment flow. 8 failure modes: one-time use exhausted, merchant lock mismatch, spending limit exceeded, billing address AVS failure, corporate MCC restriction, expired VCN, pre-auth hold overage, and card paused or closed.

Updated June 2026 8 failure modes Bank VCN · Privacy.com · Corporate Cards · One-Time Use · AVS · MCC Restrictions

Virtual cards — bank-generated virtual account numbers (VCNs), Privacy.com cards, Revolut disposable cards, Apple Cash virtual Visa, and corporate virtual cards from Brex, Ramp, or Divvy — are increasingly common. They all look like regular Visa or Mastercard numbers at checkout, but they carry constraints that standard physical cards don't have. When those constraints collide with Shopify's payment flow, the result is a decline with an error message that rarely explains the actual cause.

The critical difference between virtual card failures and regular card failures: most virtual card failures are caused by settings the cardholder controls, not by Shopify configuration. Merchants almost never need to change their Shopify settings. The fix is almost always in the virtual card app, not in the store admin. Understanding this saves both customers and merchants hours of troubleshooting in the wrong place.

First Check: Open the Virtual Card App
Before anything else — open the app that issued the virtual card (Privacy.com, your bank app, your corporate card platform). Check: (1) Is the card active or paused? (2) Is the spending limit high enough for the order total? (3) Is the card locked to a specific merchant? (4) Has the card expired? These four checks resolve the majority of virtual card failures in under two minutes, without touching Shopify at all.

Why Virtual Cards Fail on Shopify Differently from Physical Cards

Physical cards have one set of constraints: the card network rules and your bank's fraud settings. Virtual cards add a second layer: the rules set by whoever generated the virtual number — and those rules are invisible to the merchant and to Shopify's payment processor.

When a virtual card is declined, Shopify receives a generic decline code from the card network — the same codes used for physical card declines. Shopify cannot see that the card is virtual, that it's single-use, or that it's merchant-locked. The decline message shown to the customer ("Your card was declined" or "Payment could not be processed") gives no indication that the problem is a virtual card constraint. This is why customers and merchants often spend time investigating Shopify configuration when the real issue is in the virtual card settings.

Failure Mode 1 — One-Time Use Card Exhausted by Authorization

This is the most technically subtle virtual card failure on Shopify. When you place an order, Shopify does not charge the card immediately. It first sends an authorization — a temporary hold that verifies the card is valid and has sufficient funds. Then, when the order is confirmed and fulfilled, Shopify sends a capture — the actual charge. These are two separate network transactions.

Single-use virtual cards are designed to accept exactly one transaction. The authorization counts as that one transaction. When Shopify later sends the capture, the virtual card network sees a second transaction against an already-exhausted card and declines it. From the customer's perspective, the order seemed to go through (no error at checkout), but the merchant receives a "capture failed" error and the payment is never collected. The order is created but unpaid.

Silent Failure: Order Created But Payment Not Collected
When a single-use VCN is exhausted at the capture stage (not the authorization stage), Shopify may show the customer a successful order confirmation. The customer believes they paid. The merchant receives a payment failure notification but the customer does not. This creates a fulfillment dispute — the customer expects delivery, the merchant has no payment. Always use multi-use or per-merchant virtual cards for any Shopify purchase, never single-use cards.

Affects: Privacy.com single-use cards, Revolut disposable cards, some bank VCNs when set to "one purchase" at creation.

Fix: Do not use single-use virtual cards for Shopify purchases. Use Privacy.com per-merchant cards, or a bank VCN configured for the expected total amount with multiple-use allowed. If an order was already created in this state, the merchant should contact the customer to provide an alternate payment method — the order is in a "payment pending" or "payment failed" state in Shopify admin.

Failure Mode 2 — Merchant-Locked VCN Used at the Wrong Store

Citi Virtual Account Numbers, Capital One Eno virtual cards, and some bank-issued VCNs can be locked to a specific merchant during creation. This lock is a security feature — the virtual card number only works at the one merchant it was created for, reducing fraud risk. The lock is set by the cardholder when generating the VCN, often by starting the purchase flow on that specific merchant's site.

The failure happens in two scenarios. First: the customer generated the VCN on one Shopify store and tries to use it on a different Shopify store. Both stores are Shopify, but the merchant lock is at the merchant name level — the card fails at any store other than the locked one. Second: the customer's Shopify store processes payments through a different legal entity or payment processor than expected — some headless Shopify stores or marketplace stores route through a third-party name that doesn't match the merchant the VCN was locked to.

Affects: Citi Virtual Account Numbers, Capital One Eno virtual cards, any bank VCN with merchant locking enabled.

Fix (customer): Generate a new VCN without merchant locking, or generate it while on the exact checkout page of the target store. For Capital One Eno, the VCN is locked to the site where the browser extension is active — ensure the extension is active on the correct store. For Citi, leave the "Lock to merchant" option unchecked when creating the VCN.

Failure Mode 3 — Spending Limit Set Below Order Total

Privacy.com, Revolut, corporate virtual cards, and bank VCNs all allow the cardholder to set a maximum spending amount per transaction, per month, or per year. When the Shopify order total — including shipping, taxes, and any pre-authorization hold — exceeds that limit, the card is declined.

The tricky part: the Shopify pre-authorization hold is sometimes slightly higher than the displayed order total. Some stores pre-authorize 100–110% of the order amount to cover potential shipping adjustments or tax recalculations. If a customer sets their Privacy.com card to exactly $50 and the order total is $49.99, a 1% pre-auth hold ($49.99 × 1.01 = $50.49) can push the total over the card limit and cause a decline.

Set the Card Limit 10–15% Above the Order Total
When creating a virtual card for a specific Shopify purchase, set the spending limit 10–15% higher than the displayed order total. This accounts for pre-authorization holds, last-minute tax calculations, and any shipping rate adjustments. A $50 order needs a $55–$58 limit to be safe. After the order completes, you can lower the limit or close the card.

Affects: All virtual cards with configurable spending limits — Privacy.com, Revolut, corporate virtual cards (Brex, Ramp, Divvy), some bank VCNs.

Fix: Open the virtual card app and increase the spending limit to at least 15% above the Shopify order total. Then retry the payment. If the card has already been declined multiple times, ensure the card has not been flagged or locked by the issuer after repeated declined attempts before retrying.

Failure Mode 4 — Billing Address AVS Mismatch

Shopify's checkout asks for a billing address and passes it to the payment processor for Address Verification System (AVS) matching. Physical cards match against the address on file at the card's issuing bank. Virtual cards match against the address registered with the virtual card issuer — which is often a different address entirely.

This is the most common virtual card mistake: entering your home address at checkout when the virtual card is registered to a different address.

Shopify merchant note: If you're seeing many virtual card declines for AVS mismatch in your Shopify Payments fraud analysis, do not change your AVS settings to ignore mismatches. Instead, add a note at checkout — "Using a virtual card? Enter the billing address registered with your virtual card service, not your home address." This reduces abandonment without loosening fraud protection.

Failure Mode 5 — Corporate Card Merchant Category Restriction

Corporate virtual cards from Brex, Ramp, Divvy, Stripe Issuing, and similar platforms let finance teams restrict which types of merchants employees can charge to the card. These restrictions use Merchant Category Codes (MCCs) — four-digit codes assigned by card networks to classify merchants by type.

Shopify stores are typically assigned one of these MCCs:

  • 5999 — Miscellaneous and Specialty Retail Stores
  • 5961 — Catalog and Mail-Order Houses (common for online-only Shopify stores)
  • 5045 — Computers, Peripherals, and Software (for tech product stores)
  • 5912 — Drug Stores and Pharmacies (health/beauty stores)
  • 5411 — Grocery Stores (food-adjacent Shopify stores)

Corporate card policies commonly block MCCs 5999 and 5961 by default — these are considered non-business purchases. If an employee's Brex or Ramp card has a policy restricting general retail spending, any Shopify store categorized in those MCCs will decline regardless of the purchase content.

Fix (employee): Contact your finance/admin team. Ask them to create a virtual card with the specific MCC of the target Shopify merchant allowed, or to temporarily expand the MCC allowlist for your card. You can find a Shopify store's MCC by asking the merchant or checking your first declined attempt in your corporate card dashboard — the MCC of the attempted transaction is usually shown.

Fix (merchant): You cannot control your MCC assignment — it's set by your acquiring bank (Shopify Payments). But you can note in your FAQ or at checkout: "Purchasing with a company card? Some corporate expense cards block retail MCC codes. Contact your finance team to enable MCC 5999 / 5961 for this purchase."

Failure Mode 6 — Expired Virtual Card Number

Bank-generated VCNs are time-limited. Unlike physical cards that expire every 3–5 years, bank VCNs are often set to expire within 1–12 months at the time of creation. Customers who save a VCN in their browser's autofill or in Shopify's saved payment methods may not notice the VCN has expired until they return to the store and the payment declines.

Privacy.com per-merchant card No expiry (until closed) No — stays open until paused/closed
Revolut disposable card Single use (closes after first charge) N/A — single use by design

Fix: Generate a new VCN and update the saved payment method in Shopify's customer account. When creating a VCN for a recurring purchase or a store you'll return to, use a longer expiry window or use a service without VCN expiry (Privacy.com per-merchant cards, Capital One Eno which inherits the physical card's date).

Failure Mode 7 — Pre-Authorization Hold Exceeds Card Limit

When Shopify initiates a payment, it sends a pre-authorization request to the card network for an amount slightly higher than the displayed order total. This is standard practice — the pre-auth hold reserves funds for the expected charge plus a buffer for potential adjustments (shipping rate changes, tax recalculations).

For virtual cards with tight spending limits, this buffer causes the authorization to fail. A $100 order with a $100 virtual card limit declines when Shopify pre-authorizes $102–$105 as a buffer. The customer sees a decline even though their card limit appears sufficient for the displayed order total.

This failure mode is particularly common with:

  • Privacy.com cards set to an exact match of the order total
  • Corporate expense cards with per-transaction limits set by finance teams
  • Bank VCNs created for the exact expected purchase amount
  • Gift card-funded virtual cards with the exact remaining balance
15% Buffer Rule for Virtual Card Limits
When setting a spending limit on a virtual card for a Shopify purchase, add at least 15% to the displayed order total. $100 order → $115 limit. This covers the pre-auth buffer, any shipping adjustments, and any tax recalculations that happen between authorization and capture. After the order ships and the final charge posts, you can reduce the limit or close the card.

Failure Mode 8 — Card Paused or Closed

Privacy.com, Revolut, and similar virtual card services let users pause or close cards with a single tap. A paused card declines every transaction with no explanation — the decline code looks identical to a regular card decline. Customers who paused a card weeks or months ago may not remember doing so, especially if they have multiple virtual cards.

This also affects merchants who saved a customer's virtual card for subscriptions or saved payment methods. If the customer pauses or closes the virtual card after the initial purchase, every subsequent charge fails silently. The subscription appears to churn (payment failure) when the actual cause is a paused card.

Fix (customer): Open the virtual card app. Locate the card used for the Shopify purchase. Check its status — "active," "paused," or "closed." Unpause the card (or create a new one if closed) before retrying. Privacy.com cards show their status on the main cards list; a paused card is shown with a pause icon.

Fix (merchant): When a subscription payment fails on a virtual card, the dunning email (Shopify's failed payment email) should include clear instructions to check whether the payment card is active in the card's app. Generic "please update your payment method" dunning messaging often fails because the customer thinks their card is fine — they don't know it's paused. Specific messaging ("If you're using a virtual card — Privacy.com, Revolut, or a bank virtual number — confirm the card is active in the app") recovers more failed payments.

Virtual Card Type Quick-Diagnosis Guide

Use this guide to match your virtual card type to the most likely failure cause.

If you have a Privacy.com card

  1. Open Privacy.com app → check card is Active (not Paused or Closed)
  2. Check the spending limit — is it at least 15% above the Shopify order total?
  3. Check the billing address: Privacy.com app → Settings → Card billing address. Enter that address at Shopify checkout, not your home address.
  4. If card type is "Single-use" — do not use for Shopify. Create a "Per merchant" card instead.
  5. If the card is locked to a specific merchant — create a new card for this Shopify store.

If you have a bank-issued VCN (Citi, BofA, Capital One Eno)

  1. Check the VCN expiry date — is it still valid?
  2. Is the VCN locked to a specific merchant? If yes, generate a new VCN without merchant lock.
  3. Is the VCN's credit limit high enough for the order total plus 15% buffer?
  4. Enter the billing address associated with your main card account at checkout (not your current residence if different).
  5. For Capital One Eno: ensure the browser extension is active on the Shopify store where you're purchasing.

If you have a corporate virtual card (Brex, Ramp, Divvy)

  1. Check the card's per-transaction limit — is it above the order total plus 15%?
  2. Check your company's MCC policy — is the Shopify store's merchant category (5999, 5961) allowed?
  3. Enter your company's registered billing address at checkout, not your personal address.
  4. If the card has a budget period that expired — ask finance to extend or issue a new card.
  5. If restricted by vendor policy — ask finance to create a card explicitly allowed for this merchant or MCC.

If you have a Revolut virtual or disposable card

  1. If it's a disposable card — do not use for Shopify purchases. Shopify's auth + capture can exhaust it. Use a Revolut standard virtual card instead.
  2. Check the card is not frozen in the Revolut app.
  3. Check your Revolut account has sufficient balance (Revolut virtual cards draw from your Revolut balance).
  4. Enter your Revolut account address at checkout.
  5. If you've enabled online payment controls in Revolut's Security settings — confirm e-commerce is allowed.

Can Merchants Fix Virtual Card Failures in Shopify Admin?

Most virtual card failures cannot be fixed in Shopify admin — the constraints are on the card side, not the store side. However, there are two Shopify settings that can cause unnecessary virtual card failures that merchants should review:

AVS Settings (Shopify Payments → Fraud Prevention)

Shopify Payments lets merchants choose what happens when the billing address doesn't match the card's registered address (AVS mismatch). The options are: "Cancel the order," "Flag the order for review," or "No action." Virtual cards with unique billing addresses (Privacy.com, Revolut, corporate cards) will frequently trigger AVS mismatches. Setting AVS mismatch to "Cancel the order" auto-cancels every virtual card order with a billing address issue. Consider changing this to "Flag for review" and manually reviewing the flagged orders — virtual card AVS mismatches are not fraud indicators by themselves.

CVV Settings (Shopify Payments → Fraud Prevention)

Virtual cards all have CVV codes — this is rarely the issue. But if CVV verification is set to "Cancel the order" on mismatch, any customer who enters the wrong CVV for their virtual card (especially if they're used to their physical card's CVV) will be declined. The virtual card CVV is always different from the physical card's CVV. Setting CVV mismatch to "Flag for review" rather than auto-cancel catches fewer false positives.

Virtual Card Failure Diagnostic Checklist

Before contacting Shopify support or assuming a store is broken, work through this checklist:

  • Is the virtual card Active (not Paused, Closed, or Frozen)?
  • Is the spending limit at least 15% above the Shopify order total?
  • Is the card expired?
  • Is the card locked to a specific merchant — and is this the right merchant?
  • Is the billing address at checkout the virtual card's billing address (not your home address)?
  • Is the card a single-use card? (If yes: replace with a per-merchant or multi-use card)
  • Is the card a corporate card with MCC restrictions? (If yes: confirm retail MCCs are allowed)
  • Have there been multiple failed retry attempts? (Multiple declines can lock the virtual card)

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is my virtual card not working on Shopify?

A virtual card not working on Shopify is most commonly caused by: (1) the card is paused or closed in the issuing app, (2) the spending limit is set too low for the order total including the pre-auth hold, (3) the billing address entered at checkout doesn't match the address registered with the virtual card service, or (4) the card is a single-use card that was consumed by Shopify's authorization step. Open the virtual card app first and check these four things before investigating Shopify configuration.

Why does Privacy.com card fail on Shopify?

Privacy.com cards fail on Shopify most commonly because of billing address mismatch. Privacy.com cards use Privacy.com's billing address for AVS verification — not your home address. Log into Privacy.com, go to Settings, and find the "Card billing address" field. Enter that exact address at Shopify checkout. Also ensure the card is Active and the spending limit is at least 15% above the order total.

Do bank virtual card numbers work on Shopify?

Yes, bank VCNs (Citi, BofA ShopSafe, Capital One Eno) work on Shopify when configured correctly. Key requirements: the VCN must not be merchant-locked to a different store, it must not be expired, the credit limit must cover the order total plus a 15% buffer, and the billing address entered must match the address on the underlying credit card account.

Can I use a virtual card for Shopify subscriptions?

Not recommended. Single-use VCNs fail after the first billing. Bank VCNs expire and cause subscription renewals to fail. Privacy.com per-merchant cards work for subscriptions as long as the card is never paused and the spending limit covers each recurring charge. For reliable subscriptions, use a physical credit or debit card or a PayPal account instead of virtual cards.

Why does my corporate virtual card get declined on Shopify?

Corporate virtual cards (Brex, Ramp, Divvy) most often fail on Shopify because the Shopify merchant's MCC code (typically 5999 or 5961 for retail/direct marketing) is blocked by your company's card policy. Contact your finance team and ask them to create a virtual card with retail MCCs enabled, or to temporarily allow the specific merchant. Also ensure the billing address entered is your company's registered business address, not your personal address.
