Shopify Paidy Not Working — Not Showing, Verification Failed, Limit Exceeded

Updated June 2026 · 7 failure modes · Japan-market merchants & customers

Paidy is Japan's dominant BNPL — but it works completely differently from Afterpay, Klarna, or Splitit. No credit card needed: customers enter their phone number, verify via SMS, and receive a consolidated bill on the 27th of the following month. That unique model creates failure modes specific to Japan: currency mismatches, SMS OTP issues, dynamic spending limits, and monthly bill restrictions. This guide covers all 7 failure modes with exact fixes.

Why Paidy Fails Differently From Every Other BNPL

Paidy doesn't use a credit card at all. Customers identify themselves with a Japanese mobile phone number and email address, verify via SMS OTP, and Paidy pays the merchant upfront. The customer receives a consolidated bill on the 27th of the following month covering all their Paidy purchases — paid at convenience stores, via bank transfer, or auto-debit.

This creates Japan-specific failure modes: the store must be in JPY with Japanese shipping addresses for Paidy to even appear; SMS verification fails for non-Japanese phone numbers; Paidy assigns dynamic spending limits that can reject large orders; and a past-due monthly bill locks the customer's account completely.

As a merchant, you're paid by Paidy at checkout — you don't wait for the customer to pay their bill. Paidy absorbs the collection risk. But configuration errors, currency mismatches, and Japan-address requirements make Paidy more setup-sensitive than card-based BNPL.

7 Failure Modes Covered

  1. Paidy not showing at checkout
  2. SMS OTP verification failure
  3. Spending limit too low for the order
  4. Paidy Plus installment plan not available
  5. Customer account restricted — overdue bill
  6. Currency or address mismatch (non-Japan)
  7. Refund or credit not appearing on customer's bill
1

Paidy Not Showing at Shopify Checkout

Merchant-side

Paidy is a third-party payment app — it is not built into Shopify Payments. Like any third-party app, it can vanish silently from checkout for configuration or eligibility reasons that built-in payment options never experience.

The most common reason Paidy disappears: the store currency is not JPY. Paidy is Japan-exclusive and only activates when the checkout currency is Japanese yen. If your Shopify store sells in USD, EUR, or any other currency, Paidy will not appear regardless of how it is configured. Multi-currency stores using Shopify Markets must ensure the Japan-market storefront is set to JPY.

Other common causes: the Paidy app was not installed from the Shopify App Store, or was removed during a theme or platform migration; the Paidy app is installed but merchant verification with Paidy has not been completed (Paidy requires a merchant account approval process); the app is in test/sandbox mode and sandbox credentials are not valid for live checkout display; or the Paidy payment option was not enabled in Shopify's payment settings after the app was installed.

How to Diagnose

  1. Check store currency: Shopify Admin → Settings → Store details → Currency — must be JPY for Paidy to show
  2. Check app installation: Shopify Admin → Apps → confirm Paidy appears and shows as active
  3. Check merchant account status: Log into the Paidy merchant portal and confirm your merchant account is verified and approved
  4. Check API mode: In the Paidy app settings, confirm Live API credentials are configured — not test/sandbox credentials
  5. Check payment settings: Shopify Admin → Settings → Payments → Alternative payment providers — confirm Paidy is listed and enabled
  6. Test with Japan address: In checkout, enter a Japanese shipping address (e.g., Tokyo prefecture) and confirm Paidy appears — rules out address-filtering
  7. Test in incognito: Open a private browser window and go through checkout — rules out browser extension or cache conflicts
2

SMS OTP Verification Failure

Customer-side

Paidy's entire identity model is built on phone number verification — there is no credit card to validate, no login to create, just a Japanese mobile number and an email address. At checkout, Paidy sends a 4-digit OTP via SMS to the customer's phone. If this OTP step fails, the customer cannot proceed with Paidy regardless of their account standing.

The most frequent cause: the phone number is not a Japanese mobile number. Paidy requires a Japanese phone number in the +81 country code format. International customers, Japanese customers using foreign SIM cards, or customers who recently changed carriers may encounter OTP delivery failures. The Paidy app on checkout typically shows a phone input pre-formatted for Japanese numbers — entering a number in a different format causes a silent failure.

Carrier-side filtering is a secondary cause: Japanese mobile carriers (docomo, au, SoftBank) occasionally filter OTP messages from certain senders. Customers on MVNO (virtual carrier) networks sometimes face higher filter rates. The OTP is valid for approximately 3–5 minutes — customers who switch browser tabs or take too long will receive an expired OTP error and need to request a new one.

A third cause: the phone number is already registered to a Paidy account with a different email address. Paidy links a phone number to exactly one account. Entering a different email for the same phone number creates a verification conflict.

Fix Steps for Customers

  1. Confirm phone number format: Enter your number in Japanese format without the leading zero — e.g., 090-XXXX-XXXX becomes 90-XXXX-XXXX in international format
  2. Wait for SMS: OTP delivery can take up to 60 seconds on some carriers — wait before requesting a new code
  3. Check SMS filtering: Some Japanese carriers allow SMS filtering in their settings — check your carrier's web portal and disable filters for short-code messages
  4. Complete OTP quickly: Enter the OTP within 3 minutes of receiving it — expired OTPs require requesting a new code
  5. Use consistent email: If you have an existing Paidy account, use the same email address associated with your registered phone number
  6. Contact Paidy support: If OTPs consistently fail to arrive, contact Paidy's customer support — they can check whether your number is registered and troubleshoot delivery issues
Merchant note: SMS verification failures are entirely customer-side and carrier-side. You cannot resolve these on behalf of the customer. Offer alternative payment methods (credit card, convenience store payment via other providers) for customers who cannot complete Paidy verification.
3

Spending Limit Too Low for the Order

Customer-side

Paidy assigns each customer a dynamic spending limit — similar in concept to a credit limit, but without a traditional credit check. The limit is set algorithmically based on the customer's Paidy payment history, account age, and internal risk factors. New Paidy users start with a conservative limit that grows over time as they make purchases and pay their monthly bills on time.

When the order total exceeds the customer's current Paidy limit, the transaction is declined at the limit check step. The error message shown to customers is typically generic ("your Paidy limit is insufficient for this purchase") and doesn't reveal what the actual limit is. This is intentional — Paidy does not publicly display spending limits to prevent gaming.

Common scenarios: a new Paidy user trying to make a high-value first purchase; a customer who recently made several large Paidy purchases and is near their limit; or a customer whose limit was temporarily reduced after a late payment. The limit is recalculated monthly and increases automatically with a good payment history — but there is no instant limit increase available for a specific purchase.

Fix Steps for Customers

  1. Check Paidy app: Open the Paidy app — it shows your current available limit under account settings
  2. Pay outstanding bills on time: The single biggest factor in limit increases is consistent on-time monthly bill payment
  3. Wait for monthly recalculation: Paidy limits update on a monthly cycle — if you recently paid a large bill, your limit should increase next month
  4. Split the purchase: If the store allows it, purchase a portion now within your limit and purchase the remainder in a subsequent transaction or next month
  5. Use alternative payment: For high-value items where the limit is insufficient, use a credit card or other payment method for this transaction while building Paidy history for future purchases
Merchant note: You cannot override or increase a customer's Paidy spending limit. This is entirely between the customer and Paidy. For high-AOV stores, this is a structural limitation — Paidy works best for mid-range purchases where most customers' limits will be sufficient.
4

Paidy Plus Installment Plan Not Available

Merchant + Customer

Standard Paidy defers payment to the 27th of the following month — the entire purchase amount, in one bill, all purchases from the past month consolidated. Paidy Plus is a separate product that allows customers to split that deferred amount into 3 or 6 monthly installments (with a fee per installment). These are two distinct Paidy products with separate eligibility and enrollment requirements.

Paidy Plus failures at checkout occur when the installment option is shown as greyed out or unavailable. This can happen for several reasons: the customer has not enrolled in Paidy Plus through the Paidy app (Paidy Plus requires a separate in-app application); the merchant's Paidy integration does not include the Plus installment option (not all Paidy merchant contracts include Paidy Plus); or the specific cart amount does not qualify for installment splitting (Paidy Plus typically requires a minimum purchase amount).

A subtler issue: the customer's Paidy Plus limit may differ from their standard Paidy limit. A customer may have sufficient standard Paidy limit for a purchase but their Paidy Plus limit may be lower, causing the installment option to fail while the standard deferred payment would succeed.

How to Diagnose

  1. Check merchant contract: Confirm with Paidy whether your merchant agreement includes Paidy Plus — contact your Paidy account manager
  2. Check integration configuration: In your Paidy app settings, verify whether the installment option is enabled in the merchant dashboard
  3. Instruct customers to check enrollment: Customers must open the Paidy app → Account → Paidy Plus and verify Plus is enrolled and active on their account
  4. Confirm minimum order amount: Paidy Plus typically requires a minimum purchase amount — check Paidy's current merchant guidelines for the threshold
  5. Fallback option: If Plus fails, standard Paidy (defer to next month) may still work — ensure this is presented as an alternative at checkout
5

Customer Account Restricted — Overdue Monthly Bill

Customer-side

Paidy's billing model is fundamentally different from BNPL services that charge the card at checkout. Paidy pays the merchant immediately, then collects from the customer on the 27th of the following month. If a customer misses their monthly payment deadline, Paidy restricts their account immediately — no new purchases can be made until the overdue bill is paid.

This is the most common reason a returning Paidy customer suddenly cannot use Paidy at a store they've successfully used before. The restriction is not merchant-specific — the customer is blocked from all Paidy transactions across all merchants until the overdue amount is cleared. The restriction typically lifts within minutes to hours of payment confirmation, depending on the payment method used (convenience store payments may have a processing delay).

Payment methods for the monthly bill include convenience store payment (konbini — at FamilyMart, Lawson, 7-Eleven, etc.), bank transfer (furikomi), and automatic direct debit (if enrolled). Customers who set up auto-debit rarely miss payments, but auto-debit failures (insufficient bank balance) will also trigger a restriction.

Fix Steps for Customers

  1. Open the Paidy app: Check the Home screen — overdue bills show prominently with a "Pay now" button
  2. Pay the overdue bill immediately: Use the Paidy app to generate a convenience store payment barcode or bank transfer details
  3. Pay at a convenience store: FamilyMart, Lawson, and 7-Eleven all accept Paidy bill payments — bring the barcode or reference number from the app
  4. Wait for restriction lift: Convenience store payments confirm within 30–60 minutes; bank transfers may take until the next business day
  5. Set up auto-debit: After paying the overdue bill, set up auto-debit in the Paidy app to prevent future restrictions
Merchant note: You cannot see the reason for a Paidy decline — it appears as a generic payment failure. If a customer reports that Paidy worked before but is now failing, an overdue bill is the most likely cause. Advise them to check the Paidy app directly. You cannot resolve or override account restrictions on behalf of customers.
6

Currency or Address Mismatch — Non-Japan Configuration

Merchant-side

Paidy will not display at checkout unless two conditions are simultaneously true: the active checkout currency is JPY, and the shipping address is in Japan. This is not a toggle or setting — it is hard-coded into Paidy's checkout integration logic. Both conditions must be met or Paidy silently hides itself.

This creates issues for international Shopify stores with multi-currency setups. A store that sells globally and uses Shopify Payments' automatic currency conversion will present JPY to Japanese customers — but if the checkout currency detection misfires (e.g., a VPN user, a Japanese expat with a non-Japan shipping address, or a currency selector overridden by the customer), Paidy will disappear.

The address requirement is equally strict: Paidy will not show for shipping addresses outside Japan, even when the currency is JPY. A Japanese customer purchasing while abroad and shipping to a non-Japan address cannot use Paidy — Paidy's risk model is built around Japan-domestic delivery data.

For Shopify Markets setups targeting Japan, ensure the Japan market is configured with JPY as the mandatory currency and that Paidy is specifically enabled for the Japan market context — not globally. Enabling Paidy globally but selling in multiple currencies will cause Paidy to behave inconsistently across markets.

Fix for Merchants

  1. Set Japan market to JPY only: Shopify Admin → Markets → Japan → ensure the currency is JPY and not multi-currency for the Japan storefront
  2. Enable Paidy per-market: In Paidy app settings, confirm Paidy is configured for the Japan market specifically, not as a global payment method
  3. Test end-to-end: Add a product to cart, proceed to checkout, enter a Japanese address (e.g., 1-1 Chiyoda, Chiyoda-ku, Tokyo 100-0001) and confirm Paidy appears and the currency shows as JPY
  4. Check VPN/proxy behavior: International customers using VPNs to appear as Japan-based may get inconsistent results — Paidy eligibility is based on the shipping address, not IP location
  5. Document for customers: Add a note on checkout or product pages that Paidy is available for Japan shipping addresses only — this reduces confusion for international customers
7

Refund or Credit Not Appearing on Customer's Bill

Merchant + Customer

Paidy refunds work differently from card-based refund flows because Paidy may not have collected payment from the customer yet at the time the merchant issues the refund. The timing relative to the customer's billing cycle determines what happens to the refund.

Pre-bill refund (most common): If the merchant issues a refund before the customer's monthly bill is finalized (typically before the 20th of the month), Paidy removes the purchase from the upcoming bill entirely. The customer never pays for it, and no credit is issued — the amount simply doesn't appear on the bill. Customers expecting to see a "refund" line item on their bill will not see one; the charge just won't be there.

Post-payment refund: If the customer already paid the monthly bill and the merchant issues a refund afterward, Paidy issues a credit. This credit either reduces the customer's next monthly bill or is refunded to the bank account linked to the customer's Paidy direct debit — typically within 3–10 business days after Paidy processes the refund from the merchant.

Webhook failures between Shopify and Paidy can cause refund processing delays. If the Shopify refund webhook doesn't reach Paidy successfully, the customer's bill adjustment doesn't happen, creating confusion where the merchant shows a refund but Paidy still bills the customer for the full amount.

How to Handle Refund Issues

  1. Always initiate refunds through Shopify Admin: Use Shopify's refund flow — not the Paidy merchant portal directly — to keep records in sync
  2. Explain the bill timing to customers: Tell customers that if their monthly bill hasn't been issued yet, the refund will simply remove the charge from the upcoming bill (no refund line item will appear)
  3. Confirm webhook delivery: After issuing the refund in Shopify, check Paidy's merchant portal to confirm the transaction shows as refunded on their end
  4. Allow processing time: For post-payment refunds, allow 3–10 business days for the credit to appear on the customer's account or next bill
  5. Escalate to Paidy support: If the refund shows in Shopify but the customer's Paidy bill is not adjusted after 10 business days, contact Paidy merchant support with the transaction ID
Customer tip: If you returned an item and expect a Paidy refund, check your Paidy app first — refunds before the bill is issued won't appear as credits, the original charge will simply be gone from your upcoming bill. Only contact the merchant if the charge still appears on your bill after the merchant confirmed the refund.

Paidy Troubleshooting — 7-Step Merchant Checklist

Run through this before contacting Paidy merchant support

Store currency is JPY: Shopify Admin → Settings → Store details → confirm the active currency for Japan-market checkout is JPY — Paidy does not appear in any other currency
