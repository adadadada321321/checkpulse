Shopify PayPal Not Working at Checkout — 8 Failure Modes Explained

PayPal button not showing? Popup closes immediately? Order shows paid in PayPal but not in Shopify? Works on desktop but breaks on mobile or in Instagram? Here are all 8 root causes for Shopify PayPal checkout failures — with exact diagnostic steps and fixes for each.

Updated June 2026 8 failure modes PayPal Express · IPN Webhook · Currency Mismatch · Account Holds · Pay Later · In-App Browser

PayPal appears at Shopify checkout in two distinct places: as "PayPal" in the express checkout row above the contact form (alongside Shop Pay, Apple Pay, Google Pay) and as a payment method in the payment method list below the billing form. Each placement has different activation requirements, and a misconfiguration in one does not affect the other. Most PayPal failures are silent from the merchant side — no alert in Admin, no error log — which makes checkout monitoring the only reliable way to detect them before customers report the problem.

The most deceptive PayPal failure is when a customer successfully completes payment in the PayPal flow but returns to Shopify to find the order unpaid or missing. This is an IPN (Instant Payment Notification) webhook failure — PayPal processed the payment but Shopify never received the confirmation. The customer's card was charged. The merchant never got the order. This failure affects approximately 1-2% of PayPal transactions on stores with misconfigured IPN settings and is entirely invisible without order reconciliation monitoring.

Two PayPal Integrations in Shopify — Know Which You Have
PayPal Express Checkout (via Shopify Payments): Activated inside Shopify Payments settings under Wallets. This is the recommended integration. PayPal appears as a wallet button in the express checkout row. Shopify manages the connection.

PayPal as an Alternative Payment Provider: Set up separately under Admin → Settings → Payments → Alternative payment methods → PayPal. Uses older API. Still required for stores that cannot access Shopify Payments (certain countries). Has separate IPN configuration.

Most PayPal failures in 2026 involve the Shopify Payments integration. If you recently migrated from the Alternative Provider integration to Shopify Payments, verify both are not active simultaneously — duplicate PayPal connections cause checkout errors.

Failure Mode 1: PayPal Button Not Appearing at Checkout

PayPal not showing as a payment option at all — neither in the express checkout row nor in the payment methods list — is caused by one of four issues: PayPal is not activated, the connection is broken, the market doesn't include PayPal, or the cart total disqualifies the order.

Check PayPal activation status

  1. Admin → Settings → Payments
  2. Under "Shopify Payments" → click "Manage"
  3. Scroll to "Wallets" section → verify PayPal toggle is ON
  4. If PayPal shows as disconnected, click "Connect PayPal" and authorize with your PayPal Business account credentials
  5. Also check under "Alternative payment methods" — if PayPal appears there AND in Shopify Payments, you have a duplicate connection. Remove the Alternative Provider version.

PayPal wallet toggle off Admin → Payments → Manage Shopify Payments → Wallets Toggle on
PayPal connection expired/disconnected Same page — shows "Reconnect PayPal" link Reconnect with PayPal Business credentials
Market doesn't include PayPal Admin → Markets → [market] → Payment methods Add PayPal to market's payment methods

Shopify Payments not available in your country Admin → Settings → Payments → "Shopify Payments unavailable" Use PayPal as Alternative Payment Provider instead
PayPal Requires a PayPal Business Account
Shopify's PayPal integration requires a PayPal Business account, not a Personal account. Personal accounts can receive payments but cannot be connected to the Shopify Payments wallet integration. If you connected a Personal account, the PayPal button may appear but transactions will fail silently or the connect flow will show an error. Upgrade the account at paypal.com/upgrade or create a new Business account and connect it.

Failure Mode 2: PayPal Popup Closes Immediately — Currency Mismatch

A PayPal checkout popup that opens and immediately closes — or shows a white screen for 1-2 seconds then disappears — is almost always caused by a currency mismatch. PayPal accepts only ~25 currencies for transactions, while Shopify supports 133+. When a buyer has selected an unsupported currency, the PayPal SDK initialization fails silently and the popup closes without showing an error to the buyer or the merchant.

PayPal accepted currencies (2026)

Currency Code Currency PayPal Supported?
USD, EUR, GBP, CAD, AUD Major Western currencies Supported
JPY, CNY, HKD, SGD, MYR Major Asian currencies Supported
BRL, MXN, ARS Latin American currencies Partial — BRL/MXN yes, ARS limited

How to diagnose currency-related PayPal failures

  1. Open checkout in browser DevTools (F12) → Console tab
  2. Click the PayPal button and watch the console output as the popup attempts to open
  3. Look for errors containing "currency" or "INVALID_CURRENCY_CODE" or "This transaction cannot be completed"
  4. Alternatively: switch the browser to a different currency via your storefront's currency selector and test PayPal in USD or EUR — if it works in USD but not in the buyer's local currency, it's a currency mismatch
  5. Fix: either remove unsupported currencies from your store's currency list, or present prices in a PayPal-supported currency for buyers in unsupported-currency countries
Currency Mismatch Produces No Merchant Alert
When PayPal rejects a currency, there is no alert in your Shopify Admin, no email notification, and no error in the checkout UI that tells the customer what went wrong. The customer sees the PayPal popup appear and disappear. They may try multiple times, assume their PayPal account is broken, and abandon the checkout. This is one of the highest-impact silent checkout failures: globally-selling stores with many active currencies can have PayPal silently unavailable for a significant portion of their customers.

Failure Mode 3: Order Paid in PayPal But Not in Shopify — IPN Webhook Failure

The most financially damaging PayPal failure: the customer completes payment in PayPal's flow, their card or bank account is charged, and they receive a PayPal receipt — but when they return to your Shopify store, the order shows as "Payment pending" or doesn't exist at all. This is caused by a breakdown in the Instant Payment Notification (IPN) handshake between PayPal and Shopify.

How the PayPal IPN flow works

  1. Customer completes payment in PayPal popup or redirect
  2. PayPal charges the customer and sends an IPN POST request to Shopify's payment confirmation endpoint
  3. Shopify validates the IPN, marks the order as paid, fulfills inventory reservations, and sends the order confirmation email
  4. PayPal receives Shopify's acknowledgment and considers the IPN delivered
  5. If step 3 fails: PayPal retries every few minutes for up to 5 days. Shopify never marks the order as paid. Customer has no order confirmation. Money is in PayPal limbo.

Root causes of IPN failure

  • IPN URL changed in PayPal settings: If anyone manually edited the IPN URL in your PayPal Business account settings to anything other than Shopify's endpoint, PayPal sends confirmations to the wrong URL and Shopify never receives them. Check: PayPal Business → Account Settings → Notifications → Instant Payment Notifications → see the URL. It should be set to "Shopify's default" (do not override it).
  • PayPal Sandbox mode active on production store: If your store was set up with PayPal sandbox credentials (for testing) and someone forgot to switch to live credentials, sandbox transactions never send real IPN notifications. Check Admin → Payments → Alternative Payment Methods → PayPal → see if "Test mode" is enabled.
  • PayPal account email mismatch: The email address in your Shopify PayPal configuration must exactly match the primary email of your PayPal Business account. A typo, different capitalization, or a pending email change in PayPal routes transactions to a different account silently.
  • Shopify endpoint temporarily unavailable: If Shopify had a brief outage when PayPal attempted the IPN, PayPal will retry. Most retried IPNs eventually succeed within hours. Check your PayPal account's IPN history for "Resend" status on recent transactions.
# To check IPN delivery status in PayPal:
# PayPal Business dashboard → Activity → All Transactions → click a transaction
# → Transaction Details → scroll to "IPN"
# Status will show: "Delivered", "Retrying", or "Failed"
# For "Failed" — Shopify's endpoint rejected the notification.
# For "Retrying" — IPN is pending, order may self-resolve within 24-48 hours.

# To manually resend an IPN (for a specific transaction):
# PayPal Business → Tools → Business Tools → Instant Payment Notifications
# → IPN History → find the transaction → Resend IPN
Immediate Action for IPN Failure
If a customer contacts you about a PayPal charge with no Shopify order: (1) Verify the payment in your PayPal account, (2) Create a manual order in Shopify Admin → Orders → Create order for the same items, (3) Mark it as paid using "Mark as paid" (do not re-charge the customer), (4) Then investigate and fix the IPN configuration to prevent future occurrences.

Failure Mode 4: PayPal Not Available for Certain Customers or Countries

PayPal is not available in all countries, and Shopify's market configuration can further restrict which payment methods appear for buyers in specific regions. PayPal also applies its own geographic restrictions based on the buyer's IP address and account location.

PayPal geographic restrictions

Restriction Type Who Applies It How to Check
PayPal not available in buyer's country PayPal (applies to ~60 countries including Iran, Cuba, North Korea, and many others) Visit PayPal's "Countries and regions" page; test with VPN to buyer's country
PayPal excluded from market's payment methods Shopify merchant (Admin → Markets → [market] → Payment methods) Check each market — PayPal must be explicitly enabled per market
PayPal not shown for Business accounts buying B2B PayPal (B2B restrictions on some account types) PayPal's business-to-business rules; test with a personal PayPal account
Cart amount outside PayPal limits PayPal ($0 minimum, $10,000 maximum per transaction for most accounts) Test with cart values at both extremes of your typical order range
Testing PayPal Availability by Country
Use a reputable VPN to simulate a buyer's location before assuming PayPal should be available. Shopify shows different payment methods based on the buyer's market (determined by shipping address or browser locale). Even if you have PayPal enabled globally, a buyer in a PayPal-unsupported country will not see the PayPal button — this is correct behavior, not a bug.

Failure Mode 5: "Trouble Processing Your Payment Through PayPal" Error

The error message "We're having trouble processing your payment through PayPal" shown during checkout — usually after the customer is redirected back from the PayPal flow — indicates a transaction-level decline by PayPal rather than a Shopify configuration problem. This error comes from PayPal's risk and compliance systems.

Common causes of PayPal processing errors

  • New PayPal Business account review period: PayPal places new Business accounts under a 30-day review with transaction limits (typically $500–$2,000 total before review clears). Transactions that would exceed the limit are declined. Contact PayPal business support to expedite the review if you're in this period.
  • PayPal's fraud risk score blocking the transaction: High-value orders, new customer PayPal accounts, mismatched billing/shipping addresses, or multiple failed attempts from the same IP address can trigger PayPal's fraud filters. This affects only specific transactions, not all PayPal transactions. The customer should try a different payment method or contact their PayPal account support.
  • Product category restriction: PayPal's Acceptable Use Policy prohibits certain product categories (supplements with health claims, certain digital goods, firearms accessories, adult content). If your products fall into a restricted category, PayPal may allow some transactions but block others based on product descriptions in the order data Shopify sends. Review PayPal's Acceptable Use Policy and ensure your product descriptions don't trigger category flags.
  • Transaction velocity limit exceeded: Multiple large orders from the same customer or same IP in a short window can trigger PayPal's velocity controls. This is rare but occurs during flash sales where the same buyer attempts multiple purchases.
PayPal Processing Errors Are Not Shopify Errors
When PayPal declines a transaction, the error is generated and returned by PayPal's API — Shopify displays it as-is. No change to your Shopify configuration will resolve a PayPal-side decline. The buyer must resolve the issue with PayPal directly (verify their account, remove limitations, use a different funding source). For persistent processing errors affecting all transactions, contact PayPal Business support — your account may have a hold or restriction that isn't visible in your dashboard.

Failure Mode 6: "Sorry, We Can't Complete Your Purchase at This Time"

This specific PayPal error message — shown inside the PayPal popup or on the PayPal redirect page — is different from the Shopify-side processing error in Failure Mode 5. This error comes from PayPal's session validation and usually means PayPal cannot route the payment to the merchant's account as configured.

What causes this specific error

  • PayPal email mismatch between Shopify and PayPal: The PayPal Business account email configured in Shopify Admin → Payments does not match the primary email of the PayPal account receiving funds. Even a period difference ("john.smith" vs "johnsmith") can cause this. Go to PayPal → Account Settings → Account info → verify the primary email exactly matches what's in Shopify.
  • PayPal account suspended or limited: Your PayPal Business account has a limitation or suspension that prevents receiving payments. Log in to your PayPal Business account and check the notification banner. PayPal often places accounts under review without emailing the merchant — the account looks normal but cannot process transactions until the review is resolved.
  • PayPal API credentials expired: If you're using the Alternative Payment Provider integration (not Shopify Payments), the API credentials (API username, password, signature) may have expired. PayPal rotates API credentials periodically for security. Generate new credentials from PayPal → Account Settings → Security → API access → Classic API credentials.
  • Duplicate PayPal connection: Both "Shopify Payments + PayPal wallet" and "PayPal Alternative Provider" are active simultaneously, creating conflicting session tokens. Disable one — keep the Shopify Payments integration if available in your country, remove the Alternative Provider connection.
  1. Log in to your PayPal Business account at business.paypal.com
  2. Check the top of the page for any limitation, resolution request, or suspension notices
  3. If there's a banner about account review, click through and provide requested documents
  4. If the account shows no issues, go to Admin → Settings → Payments → disconnect PayPal
  5. Reconnect PayPal fresh — this regenerates the OAuth token and fixes stale connection issues

Failure Mode 7: PayPal Pay Later (Pay in 4) Not Appearing

PayPal Pay Later — including "Pay in 4" (four interest-free installments) and PayPal Credit (revolving credit line) — has strict eligibility requirements that are separate from standard PayPal availability. Merchants frequently enable PayPal expecting Pay Later to appear automatically, but it doesn't.

Pay Later eligibility requirements

Requirement Pay in 4 PayPal Credit
Eligible currencies USD, GBP, AUD, EUR, FRA (French stores) USD only
Minimum cart total $30 USD (or equivalent) No minimum
Maximum cart total $1,500 USD (or equivalent) Up to approved credit limit
Merchant country requirement US, UK, Australia, Germany, France, Italy, Spain US only
PayPal account type PayPal Business account required PayPal Business account required
Pay Later opt-in Must be enabled in PayPal Business settings Must be enabled in PayPal Business settings

Enabling Pay Later in PayPal

  1. Log in to your PayPal Business account at business.paypal.com
  2. Go to Account Settings → Website Payments → Pay Later
  3. Toggle "Accept Pay Later payments" to On
  4. Also enable "Show Pay Later messaging" to display the Pay Later option prominently on product pages
  5. In Shopify: reconnect your PayPal account after changing the PayPal settings to sync the updated configuration
Pay Later Requires Pay Later Messaging for Maximum Visibility
Even when Pay Later is enabled and the buyer is eligible, the "Pay in 4" option may not be prominently visible at checkout because the PayPal checkout button doesn't display the installment option until the buyer opens the PayPal flow. Consider adding PayPal's Pay Later messaging banner to your product pages and cart page (via Shopify's PayPal messaging app or the PayPal JavaScript SDK) so buyers know the installment option is available before they begin checkout. This alone can increase PayPal conversion by 15-20% according to PayPal's own data.

Failure Mode 8: PayPal Works on Desktop But Fails on Mobile or In-App Browsers

Mobile-specific PayPal failures are among the hardest to diagnose because they depend on the browser environment the customer is using — which varies by device, OS version, and how the customer arrived at your store. Three specific environments cause the most mobile PayPal failures.

Meta (Facebook/Instagram) in-app browser

When a customer taps your product link from an Instagram story ad, a Facebook post, or a DM, they see your store inside Meta's in-app browser (IAB) — not their device's default browser (Safari or Chrome). Meta's IAB blocks third-party OAuth redirects to prevent fraud. When the customer taps "Pay with PayPal", the PayPal authorization redirect either fails silently, shows a white screen, or returns the customer to the start of checkout with no payment applied.

  • Symptom: PayPal abandonment rate is significantly higher for traffic from paid social ads than from direct or organic traffic
  • Fix option 1: Ensure your Instagram ads use the "Open in external browser" link parameter — this forces the link to open in Safari/Chrome instead of the IAB. In Meta Ads Manager, use the "Destination" setting to choose "External browser"
  • Fix option 2: Add a prominent "Tap to open in Safari" banner at the top of your store when the in-app browser is detected, using navigator.userAgent detection for "FBIOS" or "FB_IAB"

iOS Safari popup blocker

iOS Safari blocks popups unless they are triggered directly by a user tap event with no JavaScript intermediary. If your theme or a checkout extension runs JavaScript when the PayPal button is clicked (for analytics tracking, upsell modal checking, or cart validation), iOS Safari may classify the subsequent PayPal window as a "popup not triggered by user action" and block it.

  • Symptom: PayPal fails specifically on iPhone Safari; works on Android Chrome
  • Diagnostic: Open Safari on iPhone → Settings → scroll to the site → disable "Block Pop-ups" temporarily → test PayPal
  • Fix: Remove any JavaScript click interceptors on the PayPal button. If you need to run tracking code, use the PayPal JavaScript SDK's onApprove callback after the payment completes rather than intercepting the button click

Samsung Internet browser third-party cookie restrictions

Samsung Internet browser (the default browser on Samsung devices, which represent ~20% of Android users) applies strict third-party cookie restrictions in recent versions. PayPal's checkout flow sets cross-domain cookies to maintain the session between your Shopify store domain and paypal.com. If these cookies are blocked, the PayPal session cannot be maintained and the buyer is returned to checkout without completing payment.

  • Symptom: PayPal abandonment higher on Samsung Android devices vs other Android devices
  • Fix: This is a browser-level behavior that cannot be resolved at the store level. Ensure your checkout has good fallback payment options (Apple Pay, Google Pay, credit card) that do not depend on cross-domain session cookies
