Shopify Tabby Not Working — Not Showing, Declined, Limit Reached

Updated June 2026 · 7 failure modes · UAE, Saudi Arabia, Egypt, Kuwait & Qatar merchants

Tabby is the dominant BNPL platform across the GCC — but it fails in ways that no Western BNPL product does. Its per-transaction risk assessment can decline customers who used Tabby successfully the day before. Its spending limit is opaque: customers don't know how much they have left, so declines arrive without warning. And its phone-first identity model means international tourists and expats with foreign SIM cards are systematically excluded at checkout. This guide covers all 7 Tabby failure modes with exact diagnostic steps for MENA Shopify merchants.

Why Tabby Fails Differently From Western BNPL

Tabby runs a per-transaction risk assessment, not just a per-account check. Unlike Afterpay or Klarna, where a customer account is approved and then used across purchases, Tabby evaluates every single checkout attempt independently. A customer with a perfect Tabby history can be declined for a new purchase if their available limit is exhausted across other merchants, if their risk score has changed, or if the specific order pattern flags for review. This is by design — Tabby manages credit risk at the transaction level, not the account level. Merchants cannot predict or override Tabby's per-transaction decisions.

Tabby spending limits are intentionally opaque. Tabby does not show customers their credit limit or remaining available amount. Customers learn their limit has been reached only when a purchase is declined — which is often during a high-value checkout after a busy shopping period. The limit grows over time as customers build payment history, but the growth rate and ceiling are not communicated. This design creates a specific support burden: customers contact merchants to report Tabby "not working" when the real cause is a Tabby limit constraint the merchant cannot see or resolve.

Tabby is phone-first in a market where phone number trust varies sharply. In the GCC, where 30–40% of the population is expatriate, phone numbers span dozens of country codes. Tabby's OTP verification is calibrated for local SIM cards (UAE 05x, Saudi 05x, Egyptian 01x, etc.) — foreign numbers trigger heightened friction or outright failure. A UAE-based customer shopping on a UK +44 number will not be able to complete Tabby checkout. This exclusion is rarely documented and causes significant confusion for expatriate shoppers who have lived and worked in the region for years.

1

Tabby Not Showing at Shopify Checkout

Merchant-side

Tabby is a third-party alternative payment provider — it is not built into Shopify Payments and has a distinct two-step activation process that many merchants miss. Installing the Tabby app from the Shopify App Store is necessary but not sufficient — Tabby must also be manually enabled as an alternative payment provider in Shopify Admin → Settings → Payments. The two steps are frequently confused: merchants see the app installed and assume checkout activation is automatic.

Currency is the other primary suppressor. Tabby only appears at checkout when the transaction currency is AED (UAE), SAR (Saudi Arabia), EGP (Egypt), KWD (Kuwait), or QAR (Qatar). Stores with USD, EUR, or any non-MENA base currency will not display Tabby regardless of how the app is configured. Multi-currency stores that display AED as a runtime conversion from USD — rather than setting AED as the fixed checkout currency for the UAE market — will suppress Tabby for all UAE customers.

Other common causes: the merchant's Tabby onboarding is incomplete (Tabby requires business verification and approval before going live); the Tabby app is using test/sandbox API credentials instead of live production keys; or there is a product category restriction (Tabby does not support digital goods, gift cards, or subscription products — stores whose entire catalog falls into these categories will not see Tabby regardless of configuration).

The order minimum suppresses Tabby silently. If the cart value is below Tabby's minimum purchase threshold for the market (typically 100 AED, 100 SAR, 100 EGP, 5 KWD, or 100 QAR), Tabby does not appear — with no error message to the customer. This is indistinguishable from an outage to the customer and is the second most common cause of "Tabby has disappeared from my checkout" reports.

How to Diagnose

  1. Check the two-step activation: Shopify Admin → Apps → confirm Tabby app is installed; then Shopify Admin → Settings → Payments → Alternative payment providers → confirm Tabby is listed and enabled (installed ≠ enabled)
  2. Check store currency per market: Shopify Admin → Settings → Markets → UAE → confirm AED is the fixed checkout currency, not a runtime conversion from USD; repeat for KSA (SAR), Egypt (EGP), Kuwait (KWD), Qatar (QAR)
  3. Check Tabby merchant account status: Log into the Tabby merchant portal and confirm your account is fully approved and live — accounts pending document review cannot process transactions
  4. Check API credential mode: In the Tabby app settings, confirm Live API public and secret keys are configured — test/sandbox credentials suppress Tabby at live checkout
  5. Test cart value above minimum: Add products totalling above 100 AED (or local equivalent) and test checkout with a MENA shipping address — Tabby should appear for cart values above the minimum threshold
  6. Check product category: If your store sells only digital products, gift cards, or subscriptions, Tabby will not appear — Tabby supports physical goods only
  7. Test in incognito: Open a private browser window, add to cart, enter a UAE/KSA/etc. shipping address, and proceed to checkout — rules out browser extension or session cache conflicts
Onboarding tip: Tabby merchant approval typically takes 2–5 business days after submitting business documentation. If you installed the app but Tabby never appeared, log into the Tabby merchant portal to check your onboarding status — a common oversight is assuming approval happens automatically after app installation.
2

Per-Transaction Risk Assessment Declined — "Not Available for This Purchase"

Customer-side

Tabby's most distinctive and frequently misunderstood failure mode is the per-transaction risk assessment decline. Unlike most Western BNPL products where approval happens at the account level and customers shop freely up to their limit, Tabby evaluates every single purchase independently. Each checkout attempt triggers a real-time decision against Tabby's risk model — and that model can decline a customer who has successfully used Tabby at the same merchant the previous week.

The decision factors are not disclosed. Tabby's risk model is proprietary and not explained to customers or merchants. Known contributing factors include: the customer's Tabby payment history (late payments, missed payments, and defaults reduce approval probability for future transactions); the number and value of currently open Tabby plans across all merchants; the pattern of the specific purchase (unusual merchant category, unusually high value relative to customer history, high-frequency purchasing); the customer's phone number trust score; and regional macroeconomic signals that Tabby incorporates into its model.

Returning customers are not immune. This is the most confusing aspect for both merchants and customers — a regular Tabby shopper who has never missed a payment can receive a decline. The most common cause is that their available Tabby limit (the gap between their total limit and their currently open plan balances) is insufficient for the new purchase, even though they believe they have "never had a problem with Tabby before." Because Tabby's limit is not disclosed, customers do not know they are approaching it until a decline occurs.

Merchants cannot appeal Tabby declines on behalf of customers. The decline decision is between Tabby and the customer. Merchants should direct declined customers to the Tabby app or Tabby customer support, and offer alternative payment methods at checkout.

Fix Steps for Customers

  1. Check open Tabby plans: Open the Tabby app → My Plans → review all currently active installment plans across all merchants — the total outstanding balance may be consuming your available Tabby limit
  2. Make ahead payments on existing plans: Pay off one or more existing Tabby plan installments early — this frees up available limit for new purchases. Tabby app → My Plans → select a plan → Pay Now
  3. Try a lower cart value: If your cart is close to your estimated Tabby limit, reducing the cart value below that threshold may allow the transaction to proceed
  4. Wait and retry after existing plans close: When an existing Tabby plan completes and the balance clears, your available limit increases — the next checkout attempt is more likely to succeed
  5. Contact Tabby customer support: Ask Tabby if there is a specific reason for the decline and whether there is an action you can take — Tabby support can sometimes provide more context, though the exact risk score is not disclosed
  6. Use an alternative payment method: If Tabby is unavailable for this purchase, use Tamara, a credit card, or Apple Pay — not all declines can be resolved immediately
Merchant note: "Tabby not available" messages at checkout are almost always customer-level risk decisions, not a problem with your Tabby integration. If only some customers see this message and others can complete Tabby checkout successfully, your integration is working correctly. If all customers see it, check your merchant account status and API credentials.
3

Customer Spending Limit Reached

Customer-side

Tabby assigns each customer a spending limit — a maximum total outstanding balance they can carry across all merchants simultaneously. This limit is dynamic: it starts conservatively for new customers, grows as payment history builds, and can be temporarily constrained during risk events. Critically, Tabby does not show customers their limit or their remaining available amount anywhere in the app. Customers discover the limit only when a purchase is declined.

The hidden limit creates predictable seasonal problems. During Ramadan, White Friday (Black Friday), and other shopping events, GCC consumers use Tabby heavily across multiple merchants simultaneously. By late in the shopping season, many customers have open plans from three or four merchants — and their available Tabby limit, which they have never been told, has been consumed. The next purchase attempt fails with a generic decline that offers no explanation. These customers flood merchant support channels asking why Tabby is "broken" when their individual integration is fine.

Tabby limits grow organically over time. Customers who complete plans on time, build a history of on-time payments, and avoid late fees see their limits increase without any action required. The growth rate is not disclosed, but customers who have used Tabby for 12+ months consistently and on time generally report higher available amounts than new customers. Limits also reset progressively as active plans close — a customer with AED 5,000 in open plans who sees one plan close will find their available limit increases by approximately that plan's remaining balance.

Fix Steps for Customers

  1. Open the Tabby app and review all active plans: Tabby app → My Plans — see all currently open installment plans across all merchants and the remaining balance on each
  2. Pay down one or more plans early: Tabby app → My Plans → select a plan → Pay Now — making early payments reduces your outstanding balance and increases your available limit immediately
  3. Close your smallest plan first: Paying off the plan with the smallest remaining balance clears it completely from your outstanding total — this typically frees up the most limit relative to the payment amount
  4. Retry the purchase after making early payments: After reducing your outstanding balance via early payments, attempt the checkout again — Tabby's real-time assessment will reflect the updated balance
  5. Build history by completing plans on time: Each plan completed on time increases your Tabby limit over time — this is the only way to increase your limit without paying down existing plans
  6. Split the purchase if possible: If the store allows partial payment or you can make a smaller order, a lower cart value may fall within your remaining Tabby limit even when a full cart does not
Merchant note: During peak shopping periods (Ramadan, White Friday, DSF), expect a higher-than-usual rate of Tabby declines from otherwise reliable customers. This is a Tabby limit phenomenon, not a Shopify integration issue. Consider displaying alternative MENA BNPL options (Tamara, Postpay) at checkout so customers who hit a Tabby limit have a frictionless alternative path.

Phone Number Verification / OTP Failure

Customer-side

Tabby's identity model is phone-first: the mobile number is the primary customer identifier and the channel through which all verification codes are sent. Tabby's OTP system is calibrated for local MENA SIM cards — UAE numbers starting 05x, Saudi numbers starting 05x, Egyptian numbers starting 01x, Kuwaiti numbers starting 05x or 06x, and Qatari numbers starting 03x or 07x. When a customer provides a number from outside these ranges, Tabby's verification process encounters friction that is rarely explained clearly to the customer.

International tourists and long-term expatriates are the most affected group. The GCC has a resident expatriate population of 30–40% — many of whom have lived in the UAE or Saudi Arabia for years but still use a home-country mobile number (UK +44, Indian +91, Pakistani +92, etc.) as their primary number. Tabby does not support these numbers for the same regional verification process as local SIM cards. These customers cannot complete new Tabby account creation — or Tabby checkout if the OTP delivery fails — regardless of how long they have been in the country or their creditworthiness.

Newly activated local SIM cards can also fail. Tabby's carrier data partnership means that a new SIM card (activated within the last 30–90 days) may have a lower trust score than an established number. Customers who recently switched operators, replaced a lost SIM, or bought a new number to use with Tabby may encounter OTP delivery failure or heightened verification friction during this initial period.

Additional OTP failure causes: SMS delivery delays or failures during high-traffic periods; spam filtering by the customer's carrier blocking Tabby's OTP messages; the customer using a VoIP or business number (Tabby requires a registered personal mobile number — business lines and virtual numbers are not accepted); or the OTP expiring before the customer enters it (Tabby OTPs are short-lived).

Fix Steps for Customers

  1. Use a local MENA mobile number: Tabby requires a UAE, Saudi, Egyptian, Kuwaiti, or Qatari mobile number for the respective market — a local SIM is required for OTP delivery and account creation in that market
  2. Check that your number is not a VoIP or virtual number: Tabby does not accept WhatsApp Business numbers, VoIP numbers, or business landlines for OTP — use a personal registered mobile SIM
  3. Enter the number exactly as registered: Include the correct country code and format — UAE: +971 5x xxx xxxx, Saudi: +966 5x xxx xxxx, Egypt: +20 1x xxxx xxxx, Kuwait: +965 xxxx xxxx, Qatar: +974 xxxx xxxx
  4. Request a new OTP if it expires: Tabby OTP codes expire quickly — if you did not receive it or it expired, request a new code via the "Resend OTP" option before the session times out
  5. Check carrier spam filtering: Ask your carrier if they block SMS from international senders — Tabby OTP messages may be classified as promotional and filtered by some network-level spam controls
  6. Contact Tabby customer support: If your local number consistently fails OTP, contact Tabby support — they can manually verify your account through an alternative identity verification path in some cases
  7. Use Tamara or Postpay as an alternative: If Tabby OTP cannot be resolved at checkout, Tamara offers a similar MENA BNPL product with similar market coverage — offer it as an alternative at your checkout
Expat customer note: Tabby's phone verification is designed for locally-registered SIM cards in each MENA market. Expatriates using their home-country numbers will be unable to use Tabby for new account creation or may face persistent OTP issues. Acquiring a local SIM is the only reliable fix — this is a structural constraint of Tabby's identity model, not a temporary technical issue.

Order Value Below Tabby Minimum

Merchant + Customer

Tabby enforces minimum purchase thresholds in each market — a cart below the minimum will not display Tabby at checkout, with no error message explaining why. The minimum is designed to ensure the installment amounts across four payments are economically meaningful — splitting a AED 40 purchase into four AED 10 payments is not a practical use case for either Tabby or the customer.

Market-specific minimums: UAE (AED 100), Saudi Arabia (SAR 100), Egypt (EGP 100–200 range), Kuwait (KWD 5), Qatar (QAR 100). These thresholds apply to the cart subtotal — if the cart total (before shipping) is below the minimum, Tabby is suppressed silently. Shipping costs are not included in the calculation in most configurations.

Merchants whose stores include low-price accessories, add-ons, or starter products frequently see Tabby absent for those small carts and present for larger carts — which confuses customers and support teams who believe Tabby has "stopped working" for specific product types. The minimum is a Tabby platform constraint and cannot be adjusted by the merchant.

Unlike Afterpay's AU$10 minimum (rarely triggered for retail), Tabby's AED 100 minimum is regularly reached by MENA stores selling lower-cost accessories, beauty consumables, or subscription boxes. Merchants should consider this threshold when deciding which products to promote with Tabby instalment widgets on product pages — showing Tabby BNPL messaging for products that will never qualify creates false customer expectations.

Fix Steps for Merchants

  1. Check your cart total at checkout: If Tabby disappears for small orders, test with cart values above the market minimum (AED 100, SAR 100, EGP 100+, KWD 5, QAR 100) — confirm Tabby reappears above the threshold
  2. Remove Tabby widgets from low-price product pages: Do not display "Pay in 4 with Tabby" widgets on product pages where the standalone product price is below the market minimum — customers who see the widget expect Tabby to work at checkout
  3. Add cart value guidance for Tabby: Consider adding a message in the cart like "Add AED X more to unlock Tabby pay-in-4" — reduces abandonment when customers are just below the threshold and might add an accessory to qualify
  4. Audit your product catalog against Tabby minimums: Review your product price distribution against the market minimum — identify which products can realistically never offer Tabby and remove Tabby promotional materials from those pages
  5. Consider bundling for threshold-adjacent products: For products priced at AED 80–99, consider bundling with a small complementary accessory — or a gift-wrapping option — to bring the cart above the minimum while adding value
Shopify widget tip: Tabby's official Shopify widget has configuration options to set a minimum display amount — you can configure the widget to not appear on product pages where the product price is below the market minimum. This prevents the false expectation of Tabby availability for products that will never qualify at checkout.

Identity Verification Failure — New Tabby Account

Customer-side

First-time Tabby users must complete an identity verification step before their first purchase. The verification requirements and process vary by market — UAE and Saudi Arabia have stricter KYC requirements than Egypt in some scenarios — but the core process compares the customer's name, national ID or residency document number, and mobile number against identity records. Existing verified Tabby customers who have completed this process previously do not re-verify at checkout.

Name mismatches are the leading cause of verification failure. In MENA markets where Arabic names have multiple transliteration conventions (Mohamed vs Muhammad vs Mohammed; Abdullah vs Abdallah), the name entered at checkout may not match the name on the national ID or the name registered with the telecom carrier. Customers who use an informal or shortened version of their name — or who provide a name in one script while records are in another — fail verification. The name entered must match identity documents exactly as they appear in the issuing authority's system.

Residency permit (Iqama) mismatches affect expatriate residents in Saudi Arabia and Kuwait. Saudi Arabia's Iqama number is required for expatriate residents — the Iqama number, name, and sponsoring employer data must be current and match the ABSHER or Nafath digital identity system. Recently-renewed Iqamas with data that hasn't propagated to Tabby's verification partner create temporary mismatches that resolve after 24–72 hours. Customers who renewed their Iqama within the last week may encounter verification failures that self-resolve.

UAE customers with Emirates ID cards must have an active, non-expired Emirates ID — a valid passport alone is often insufficient for full Tabby verification in UAE. Customers who recently moved to the UAE and have not yet received their Emirates ID cannot complete full Tabby verification and will be limited to smaller purchase amounts or declined entirely.

Fix Steps for Customers

  1. Use your legal name exactly as on your ID: Enter your full name precisely as it appears on your national ID, Emirates ID, Iqama, or passport — no nicknames, initials, or abbreviated forms
  2. Check transliteration consistency: If your name can be transliterated multiple ways (Mohamed / Muhammad / Mohammed), use the exact spelling shown on your Emirates ID or national ID — and ensure the same spelling is used across all fields
  3. Have your Emirates ID or Iqama ready: For UAE customers, Emirates ID number is required during Tabby verification; for Saudi expatriates, your Iqama number is required — have the physical card available to copy the number accurately
  4. Wait 72 hours after document renewals: If you recently renewed your Emirates ID, Iqama, or national ID, wait 2–3 business days for the updated data to propagate to Tabby's verification partner before retrying
  5. Contact Tabby support for manual verification: Tabby offers a manual document verification pathway for customers who cannot pass automated verification — contact Tabby support with your ID document and request manual review
  6. Use an alternative payment method while awaiting verification: Complete your purchase by credit card, Apple Pay, or Google Pay — then complete Tabby verification via the app for future purchases
Merchant note: Identity verification failures are entirely between the customer and Tabby. You cannot verify customers on their behalf or access their identity data. If new customers consistently report Tabby account creation failures, consider adding a note in the checkout or pre-checkout experience directing first-time Tabby users to create and verify their account at tabby.ai before checkout — reducing mid-checkout friction for a first-time KYC flow.

Store Currency or Shopify Markets Configuration Mismatch

Merchant-side

Tabby operates exclusively in AED (UAE), SAR (Saudi Arabia), EGP (Egypt), KWD (Kuwait), and QAR (Qatar). It will not appear at checkout unless the active transaction currency is one of these five, and the customer's shipping address is in the corresponding country. Both conditions must be true simultaneously — a UAE shipping address with a USD checkout currency will suppress Tabby, and an AED store that ships globally will suppress Tabby for non-UAE addresses.

Shopify Markets currency configuration is the most common source of this failure. Many MENA-based Shopify stores began as local stores with a single currency and later added international shipping using Shopify Markets. During international expansion, merchants often set USD as the base currency with other currencies presented as runtime conversions for display purposes. This satisfies international customers but breaks Tabby for local MENA customers — Tabby requires the actual transaction currency to be AED/SAR/etc., not a display conversion of USD.

The Shopify Markets fix is to configure per-market fixed currencies: UAE market → fixed AED, Saudi Arabia market → fixed SAR, Egypt market → fixed EGP, Kuwait market → fixed KWD, Qatar market → fixed QAR. This is different from currency conversion, which displays prices in local currency but processes the transaction in the store's base currency. Only fixed checkout currencies trigger Tabby's availability check correctly.

Merchants who recently migrated their store from a single-country Shopify plan to a multi-market plan using Shopify Markets should specifically audit their Tabby checkout behaviour after migration — international expansion is the most reliable trigger for domestic alternative payment suppression.

Fix for Merchants

  1. Set per-market fixed currencies in Shopify Markets: Shopify Admin → Settings → Markets → UAE → set AED as the fixed checkout currency (not a conversion from USD); repeat for KSA (SAR), Egypt (EGP), Kuwait (KWD), Qatar (QAR)
  2. Scope Tabby per market: In Tabby app settings and Shopify Payments settings, confirm Tabby is enabled for each MENA market specifically — not as a catch-all global alternative payment method
  3. Test each market independently: Add a cart, enter a UAE shipping address with AED fixed currency → confirm Tabby appears; repeat for KSA with SAR, Egypt with EGP, Kuwait with KWD, Qatar with QAR
  4. Audit currency conversion vs fixed currency settings: Shopify Admin → Settings → Markets → check each MENA market's currency setting — "fixed" means Tabby works; "auto-convert" or "display only" means Tabby will be suppressed
  5. Check Shopify Markets currency locking per market: For each MENA market, confirm "customers can only pay in [local currency]" is enabled if possible — prevents multi-currency display from interfering with Tabby's currency check
  6. Re-test after any Shopify Markets changes: Whenever you add a new market, change base currency, or update Shopify Markets currency settings, re-test Tabby for all five MENA markets — currency configuration changes can suppress MENA alternative payment methods without a visible error
USD-priced stores: Some MENA-targeting stores price in USD for global consistency. These stores cannot offer Tabby — Tabby's merchant agreement and payment infrastructure requires local currency transactions. If your store sells in USD, offer credit card, Apple Pay, or PayPal for MENA customers. Tabby is only available when the checkout currency is AED, SAR, EGP, KWD, or QAR.
