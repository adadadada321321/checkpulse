Shopify Vodafone Cash Not Working — Wallet Not Appearing, OTP Not Received & Order Stuck Pending

Updated June 2026 · 7 failure modes · Egypt merchants · Vodafone Egypt mobile wallet · CBE regulated · EGP only

Vodafone Cash (فودافون كاش) is Egypt's largest mobile wallet service, operated by Vodafone Egypt and regulated by the Central Bank of Egypt. When Vodafone Cash stops working on Shopify, the failure is one of seven completely separate problems — the wallet option not appearing at checkout is a gateway configuration issue, the OTP not arriving is a customer or network issue, and the order stuck in pending after payment is a webhook callback failure that leaves the customer's wallet debited with no order confirmation. Identifying which of the seven failures you have is the only way to fix it quickly.

Vodafone Cash not appearing or order stuck in pending? A 4-step checkout diagnostic confirms whether your gateway is configured for Vodafone Cash payments and whether your callback URL is correctly set up.

How Vodafone Cash Works — and Why It Fails Differently From Card Payments

Vodafone Cash is a mobile wallet tied to the customer's Vodafone Egypt SIM card, not a card scheme. Customers register their national ID at a Vodafone store or via the Vodafone Cash app to create a wallet linked to their 010xxxxxxx mobile number. They load funds into the wallet via Vodafone dealers, ATMs, bank transfers, or the Vodafone Cash app. At checkout, the customer selects Vodafone Cash, enters their mobile number, receives an OTP to confirm the payment, and the purchase amount is deducted from their wallet balance.

Vodafone Cash is an asynchronous mobile wallet payment — the OTP confirmation step happens outside the browser session. After the customer enters their mobile number at checkout, the browser waits while the customer receives the OTP (by SMS or in-app) and approves the payment. The gateway then receives a callback from Vodafone Egypt confirming the payment. If the callback URL is misconfigured, this confirmation never reaches the gateway — the customer has paid, but the merchant's Shopify store still shows the order as pending. This callback dependency is the most critical difference from card payments and the most common cause of serious issues.

Vodafone Cash wallets have CBE-mandated tier limits that apply at the transaction level. The Central Bank of Egypt sets balance and daily transaction limits for mobile wallets based on the customer's verification tier. An unverified wallet (basic registration) has lower limits than a fully verified wallet. A customer who has reached their daily transaction limit will see their Vodafone Cash payment declined even if they have sufficient wallet balance — and the error message from the gateway may not clearly indicate that a regulatory limit, not a technical failure, is the cause.

Vodafone Cash requires a compatible payment gateway — Stripe and PayPal do not support it. Vodafone Cash payments must be processed through a gateway with direct Vodafone Egypt API integration. Paymob is the most widely used option and offers Vodafone Cash as an enableable payment method. Kashier and PayTabs Egypt also support it. Having a compatible gateway is necessary but not sufficient — the merchant must also enable Vodafone Cash specifically within the gateway dashboard, which is a separate step from enabling card payments.

7 Failure Modes Covered

  1. Vodafone Cash option not appearing at checkout — gateway not configured
  2. Customer has a Vodafone SIM but has not registered for a Vodafone Cash wallet
  3. OTP not received or times out before customer enters it
  4. Insufficient wallet balance — wallet pre-loading required
  5. CBE daily or monthly transaction limit exceeded
  6. Gateway callback not configured — order stuck in pending after customer pays
  7. Phone number format mismatch — account not found at checkout
1

Vodafone Cash option not appearing at checkout — gateway not configured

Merchant Issue

The most common reason Egyptian customers do not see a Vodafone Cash payment option at Shopify checkout is that either the merchant is using a payment gateway that does not support Vodafone Cash at all, or they are using a compatible gateway but have not enabled Vodafone Cash as a payment method within the gateway's merchant dashboard. Vodafone Cash is not a card scheme — it is a mobile wallet payment method that requires explicit API integration with Vodafone Egypt's payment system. Gateways that have not built this integration simply cannot offer it.

Stripe and PayPal, the two most commonly integrated payment gateways on Shopify globally, do not support Vodafone Cash. Egyptian merchants using only Stripe or PayPal will never show a Vodafone Cash option to their customers regardless of any Shopify settings — the capability does not exist at the gateway level. The merchant must use an Egypt-focused gateway: Paymob (most widely used), Kashier (Egypt-founded), or PayTabs Egypt.

For merchants already using Paymob, the Vodafone Cash option will not appear unless it is specifically enabled. After integrating Paymob with Shopify, the merchant must log in to the Paymob merchant dashboard, navigate to payment methods or integration settings, and toggle Vodafone Cash to enabled. The payment widget at checkout then adds Vodafone Cash as an option alongside card payments and other enabled methods. Enabling Paymob for card payments does not automatically enable Vodafone Cash — these are separate toggles within the same integration.

A related issue occurs when the Paymob integration is configured in sandbox (test) mode: Vodafone Cash sandbox testing requires test credentials provided by Paymob, and some merchants configure card testing in sandbox but never test or enable Vodafone Cash in sandbox before switching to production. When switching to production, Vodafone Cash may not be included in the production configuration if it was never enabled in the test environment.

How to fix

  1. Confirm your gateway supports Vodafone Cash: If you are using Stripe, PayPal, or another international gateway, Vodafone Cash support is not available. You need to integrate Paymob, Kashier, or PayTabs Egypt as an additional or replacement payment provider for Egyptian customers.
  2. Log in to your Paymob dashboard and enable Vodafone Cash: Navigate to Payment Methods or Integrations within the Paymob merchant dashboard. Look for the Vodafone Cash payment method and toggle it to enabled. Save the configuration. The payment widget on your Shopify checkout should update within minutes to show the Vodafone Cash option.
  3. Verify the integration ID is linked to the correct Shopify store: If you have multiple Shopify stores or multiple Paymob integrations, confirm that the integration ID configured in your Shopify payment provider settings matches the Paymob integration where you enabled Vodafone Cash. A mismatch will result in the enabled methods from one integration not appearing in another.
  4. Test in an incognito window: After enabling Vodafone Cash in the gateway dashboard, open your Shopify store in an incognito browser session and add a product to cart. Proceed to checkout and confirm the Vodafone Cash option now appears as a payment method. If it does not appear, clear your Shopify theme cache or check whether the payment widget is set to display all enabled methods.
  5. Contact gateway support if the option still does not appear: If Vodafone Cash is enabled in the dashboard but not showing at checkout after 30 minutes, contact your gateway's technical support with your integration ID, your Shopify store URL, and a screenshot of the enabled payment methods in your dashboard. There may be an account-level approval requirement for Vodafone Cash that has not been completed.
2

Customer has a Vodafone SIM but has not registered for a Vodafone Cash wallet

Customer Issue

Having a Vodafone Egypt SIM card and mobile number is not the same as having a registered Vodafone Cash wallet. Vodafone Cash is a separate financial service that requires explicit registration — the customer must create a wallet account before they can use it for payments. A customer with a Vodafone number who has never registered for Vodafone Cash cannot pay with it, regardless of whether the merchant's gateway and checkout are perfectly configured.

When an unregistered Vodafone number is entered at checkout, the gateway sends a wallet lookup request to Vodafone Egypt's system and receives a "wallet not found" or "account not registered" response. This is returned to the customer as a generic payment error — "payment could not be completed," "invalid account," or simply an error code — with no clear indication that the issue is wallet registration rather than a technical failure. The customer typically assumes the merchant's checkout has a problem.

Wallet registration can be completed through several channels: at any Vodafone Egypt store or authorized dealer with a valid national ID; via the USSD code *9*6# on the Vodafone number; through the Vodafone Cash app available on Android and iOS; or, for some customers, through their Vodafone Egypt online account. Registration is typically immediate — the wallet is active within minutes of completing registration with national ID verification.

The registration distinction is especially important for Vodafone Egypt prepaid SIM customers and for customers who received a Vodafone SIM recently. New SIM holders often do not know that Vodafone Cash is a separate registration step. Merchants who serve Egyptian customers benefit from adding a brief note near the Vodafone Cash payment option: "First time? Register your Vodafone Cash wallet at any Vodafone store or via the Vodafone Cash app before using this option."

How to fix

  1. Guide customers to register before checkout: Add a help note near the Vodafone Cash payment option at checkout explaining that a Vodafone Cash wallet registration is required. Include the registration options: Vodafone store (bring national ID), USSD code *9*6#, or Vodafone Cash app.
  2. Include Vodafone Cash registration guidance in your cart abandonment email: If a customer abandons checkout after selecting Vodafone Cash, follow up with a brief explanation that Vodafone Cash requires a wallet registration and that they can register via the app or at a Vodafone store in minutes before completing their purchase.
  3. Add alternative payment methods for unregistered customers: Customers who want to pay with Vodafone Cash but have not yet registered need an alternative to complete their purchase immediately. Offer Fawry offline payment, Meeza card, or bank transfer as fallback options so they can purchase now while registering for Vodafone Cash in the background.
  4. Distinguish "not registered" errors from other failures in your gateway logs: Review your gateway's payment logs for the specific error code returned when a number is not registered. Paymob and other gateways return different codes for "wallet not found" versus "OTP not received" versus "insufficient balance." Knowing which error is occurring helps you provide accurate guidance to customers who contact you after a failed payment.
Merchant note: Vodafone Cash registration errors are most common when a store launches a new Vodafone Cash payment option for the first time. Existing customers who have never used mobile wallet payments may have Vodafone numbers but no wallets. Plan for a wave of registration-related failures in the first weeks after enabling Vodafone Cash and prepare customer service guidance accordingly.
3

OTP not received or times out before customer enters it

Merchant + Customer

Vodafone Cash payments require OTP confirmation — after the customer enters their Vodafone mobile number at checkout, Vodafone Egypt's system sends a one-time password to the registered number or via the Vodafone Cash app. The customer must enter this OTP within the payment session window to complete the transaction. If the OTP does not arrive or expires before the customer enters it, the payment fails and the order is not completed.

The most common reason an OTP does not arrive is network delivery delay. During peak times or when Vodafone Egypt's SMS system is congested, OTP delivery can be delayed by seconds to minutes. If the payment session timeout is short — which is typical for hosted payment widgets with a 60–90 second OTP window — the OTP may arrive after the session has already expired. The customer then sees a "payment session expired" error and must start the checkout process again.

Other OTP delivery failures include: the customer's phone is out of Vodafone network coverage (no signal, traveling internationally, or in a coverage dead zone); the phone number entered at checkout differs from the number registered to the Vodafone Cash wallet (even a single digit error causes the OTP to go to the wrong number or not be generated at all); the customer's phone is in airplane mode or has SMS blocked; or the customer's SIM has been replaced and the new SIM has not been linked to the existing Vodafone Cash wallet.

On the merchant side, an OTP timeout can sometimes be adjusted in the gateway configuration. Paymob's integration settings include a session expiry parameter — increasing this from the default (often 60 or 90 seconds) to 3–5 minutes gives customers more time to receive and enter the OTP without the session expiring. This configuration change reduces OTP timeout failures without any cost or risk to the merchant.

How to fix

  1. Increase the payment session timeout in gateway settings: Log in to your Paymob or gateway dashboard and find the session expiry or OTP timeout setting for the Vodafone Cash integration. Increase it from the default (60–90 seconds) to at least 3 minutes (180 seconds). This gives customers time to receive the OTP, unlock their phone, and enter it without the session expiring. A longer timeout does not increase risk — incomplete sessions expire normally.
  2. Advise customers to have their phone ready before entering checkout: Add a note to the Vodafone Cash payment option at checkout: "You will receive an OTP on your registered Vodafone number — make sure your phone is nearby with network coverage before proceeding." Customers who understand the flow are less likely to be surprised by the OTP step and more likely to complete it quickly.
  3. Distinguish OTP timeout from OTP not received in logs: Your gateway will return different error codes for an expired session (timeout) versus an OTP that was never delivered (unregistered number, network failure). OTP timeout errors suggest you need to increase the session timeout. OTP not delivered errors suggest a customer-side issue (wrong number entered, no signal).
  4. Guide customers with persistent OTP failures: If a customer contacts you about not receiving their Vodafone Cash OTP, ask them to: (a) confirm their Vodafone number is active and has network signal; (b) check that they entered their number correctly at checkout (full 11-digit format: 010xxxxxxxx); (c) check whether the OTP arrived in the Vodafone Cash app notification rather than by SMS; (d) try USSD *9*6# to confirm their wallet is active.
  5. Provide a fallback payment option at checkout: If the OTP step fails, the customer should be able to immediately try another payment method without having to restart their entire order. Ensure your checkout shows Fawry or card payment options alongside Vodafone Cash so a failed OTP step does not result in cart abandonment.
4

Insufficient wallet balance — wallet pre-loading required

Customer Issue

Vodafone Cash is a pre-funded wallet — the customer must load funds into their wallet in advance before they can use it for purchases. This is fundamentally different from a debit card or bank transfer, where funds are drawn directly from a bank account. If the Vodafone Cash wallet balance is insufficient for the order total, the payment is declined at the Vodafone system level before the OTP step is even completed.

The error returned to the customer is typically displayed as "insufficient funds" or "payment declined" — but customers who are accustomed to debit card payments may not immediately understand that their Vodafone Cash wallet is a separate account from their bank and has its own balance that must be maintained. A customer whose bank account has ample funds may be confused by a Vodafone Cash "insufficient funds" error because they assume the wallet draws from their bank account directly. It does not — unless the customer has set up an auto-load or bank-to-wallet transfer, the Vodafone Cash balance is independent.

Customers can load their Vodafone Cash wallet through multiple channels: at any Vodafone Egypt store or dealer by depositing cash; at ATMs of banks that support Vodafone Cash loading (Banque Misr, NBE, CIB, and others); via the mobile banking apps of compatible banks that support wallet top-up; or through the Vodafone Cash app using a bank transfer or card. Loading is typically instant at Vodafone stores and via bank app top-up; ATM loading may have a short processing delay.

For merchants, insufficient balance declines appear in the gateway logs as a specific decline code related to wallet balance. There is no technical action the merchant can take to resolve this for a specific transaction — the customer must load funds and retry. However, merchants can reduce this friction by displaying the Vodafone Cash balance requirement clearly at checkout and providing guidance on how to load the wallet quickly if the balance is low.

How to fix

  1. Display the order total prominently before the Vodafone Cash OTP step: Make the order amount and the "this will be charged to your Vodafone Cash wallet" message visible before the customer enters their phone number. Customers should confirm they have sufficient balance before initiating the OTP sequence, reducing wasted OTP sessions that fail due to insufficient funds.
  2. Provide wallet top-up guidance on the payment failure page: If a Vodafone Cash payment fails with an insufficient balance error, display a clear message with instructions for how to load the wallet: "To top up your Vodafone Cash wallet: visit any Vodafone store, use the Vodafone Cash app, or transfer from your bank app. Then retry your order." Link to the Vodafone Cash app download or the Vodafone Egypt top-up page.
  3. Offer installment or smaller-order options for high-value purchases: If your average order value is high relative to typical Vodafone Cash wallet balances, consider offering order splitting or installment options (via Aman Wallet or other BNPL providers) as alternative payment paths for customers who want to use mobile wallet payments but do not have the full amount loaded.
  4. Set wallet minimum balance alerts in customer communications: If you have returning customers who use Vodafone Cash regularly, include a reminder in your order confirmation or marketing emails that Vodafone Cash requires sufficient wallet balance — "check your Vodafone Cash balance before your next order." This reduces failed repeat purchase attempts.
5

CBE daily or monthly transaction limit exceeded

Customer Issue

The Central Bank of Egypt sets regulatory limits on mobile wallet transactions that apply to all Vodafone Cash users regardless of their wallet balance. These limits exist at two levels: the maximum wallet balance a customer can hold, and the maximum amount the customer can transact per day or per month. When a customer's daily or monthly transaction limit has been reached, subsequent payments are declined by Vodafone's system even if the wallet has sufficient funds for the purchase.

The CBE's tiered limit structure for mobile wallets applies based on the customer's wallet verification level. Unverified wallets — registered with basic national ID — have lower daily and monthly transaction limits and a lower maximum balance ceiling (EGP 10,000). Fully verified wallets, which require extended identity verification including facial recognition or in-person bank verification, have significantly higher limits. A customer attempting to pay with an unverified wallet for a high-value order may find the order total exceeds their daily limit even if it does not exceed their balance.

From the merchant's perspective, a CBE limit exceeded error looks similar to an insufficient balance error in the gateway response — both are declined at the Vodafone system level with a generic decline code. The distinction matters for customer communication: a balance error requires topping up the wallet, while a limit error cannot be resolved by adding funds and requires either waiting until the next day (for daily limit resets) or upgrading the wallet verification tier (for limit increases).

Merchants selling high-value items in Egypt — electronics, furniture, jewelry, or bundled service packages priced above the typical Vodafone Cash daily transaction limit — will encounter CBE limit failures frequently among customers with unverified wallets. The practical solution is to offer Aman Wallet (which offers BNPL financing and thus smaller per-transaction amounts) or bank transfer as alternatives for high-value orders.

How to fix

  1. Advise customers on wallet tier upgrades: Customers who encounter limit errors should be advised to upgrade their Vodafone Cash wallet to the verified tier to increase their transaction limits. Verification can be completed at any Vodafone Egypt store with national ID and may be available through the Vodafone Cash app. A verified wallet has substantially higher daily and monthly limits and a higher maximum balance.
  2. Display limit guidance for high-value orders: If your store has products priced above common CBE wallet tier limits, add a note near the Vodafone Cash option at checkout for high-value carts: "Note: Vodafone Cash transaction limits apply. For orders above [amount], you may need a verified Vodafone Cash wallet or choose an alternative payment method." This sets expectations before the payment attempt fails.
  3. Offer bank transfer or Paymob card payment for high-value orders: For orders where Vodafone Cash limit errors are frequent, ensure bank transfer is prominently available at checkout. Bank transfers from Egyptian banks do not carry the CBE mobile wallet tier limits and can handle any order value.
  4. Distinguish limit errors from balance errors in customer service: When a customer contacts you about a declined Vodafone Cash payment, the gateway error code tells you whether it is a balance issue or a limit issue. A balance error requires topping up. A limit error requires either waiting until the next day (if the daily limit reset time has not passed) or upgrading to a verified wallet. Providing the correct guidance prevents customers from incorrectly topping up their wallet when the issue is a transaction limit.
Merchant note: CBE mobile wallet limits are updated periodically by the Central Bank of Egypt as Egypt's digital payments regulations evolve. Check the CBE's published guidelines for current Vodafone Cash tier limits annually — the limits in place in 2025 may differ from those in 2026 and beyond as Egypt's financial inclusion targets are revised.

Gateway callback not configured — order stuck in pending after customer pays

Merchant Issue

A Shopify order stuck in pending status after a Vodafone Cash payment is almost always caused by a missing or broken callback (webhook) URL. Vodafone Cash is an asynchronous payment method — the payment flow is: (1) customer selects Vodafone Cash and enters their number; (2) Vodafone sends the customer an OTP; (3) customer approves the payment; (4) Vodafone's system notifies the payment gateway that the payment was approved; (5) the gateway notifies Shopify via a webhook callback; (6) Shopify marks the order as paid. If step 5 fails because the callback URL is misconfigured or unreachable, the payment never reaches Shopify, and the order stays in pending indefinitely.

This is the most dangerous Vodafone Cash failure mode because it results in the customer having paid (their wallet is debited, and they have a payment confirmation from Vodafone) while the merchant's system shows no completed payment. Without an automated process to check for pending Vodafone Cash orders, the merchant may never fulfill the order, and the customer faces a serious dispute requiring manual reconciliation and a refund from the merchant's payment gateway.

The most common cause of callback failures is a localhost or development URL left in the production gateway configuration. During development and testing, merchants often set the callback URL to a local address (localhost:3000, 127.0.0.1, or a tunneling service URL like ngrok). When deploying to production, if the callback URL is not updated to the production Shopify store URL, all payment confirmations from Vodafone will be sent to an unreachable address and silently fail. Orders remain in pending with no error visible to the merchant — only the payment gateway's logs show the failed callback attempts.

A related issue occurs when the Shopify store's domain changes (custom domain added, store migrated, HTTPS enforcement added) and the callback URL configured in the gateway dashboard is not updated to match. The gateway still sends callbacks to the old URL, which now returns a 404 or redirects to a non-Shopify page, and payment confirmation never reaches the store.

How to fix

  1. Check and update the callback URL in your gateway dashboard immediately: Log in to your Paymob (or gateway) dashboard and find the webhook or callback URL configuration for your Vodafone Cash integration. The callback URL must be the production Shopify payment confirmation endpoint provided by your gateway's Shopify integration — typically in the format your gateway specified during setup. It must be a publicly accessible HTTPS URL, not localhost or a development tunnel.
  2. Verify the callback URL is reachable from the public internet: Use a tool like curl or a webhook testing service to confirm that a POST request to your configured callback URL returns a 200 response. If the URL returns a 404, redirect, or timeout, payments cannot be confirmed. Update the URL and retest.
  3. Manually reconcile stuck pending orders: For any orders currently stuck in pending after a Vodafone Cash payment, log in to your payment gateway's merchant dashboard and check the transaction list. Find completed Vodafone Cash transactions that correspond to pending Shopify orders. Manually mark the corresponding Shopify orders as paid (use manual payment capture in Shopify admin) and fulfill them. Contact your gateway support to confirm the refund or reconciliation process if the amount was captured but not forwarded.
  4. Set up a monitoring alert for orders stuck in pending: Configure a Shopify webhook or alert (via a Shopify app or custom script) to notify you when an order has been in pending status for more than 30 minutes. Pending orders that are not resolved quickly indicate a callback failure and require immediate attention before customers dispute the charge.
  5. Test the full payment and callback flow in production with a real transaction: After updating the callback URL, complete a real Vodafone Cash payment for a small amount (EGP 1 if your gateway supports it, or a minimum real transaction) through your live Shopify checkout. Confirm that: the OTP is received, the payment is approved, the order in Shopify updates from pending to paid within 60 seconds, and the payment appears in your gateway transaction list as settled. Only then is the callback correctly configured.
Critical: Do not ignore Shopify orders stuck in pending after Vodafone Cash payments. These represent real money deducted from the customer's wallet with no fulfillment. Check your gateway dashboard daily for completed Vodafone Cash transactions that do not match paid Shopify orders. Every unmatched payment is a pending dispute or chargeback risk.
7

Phone number format mismatch — account not found at checkout

Merchant + Customer

Vodafone Cash wallets are identified by the customer's Vodafone Egypt mobile number. When a customer enters their number at checkout, the gateway submits it to Vodafone Egypt's system to look up the associated wallet. If the number format does not match the format that the Vodafone system expects, the lookup fails and the customer receives an "account not found" or "invalid mobile number" error even if their wallet is active and fully funded.

Egyptian mobile numbers are 11 digits starting with a carrier prefix: Vodafone uses 010, Orange uses 012, Etisalat/e& uses 011, and WE (Telecom Egypt) uses 015. The full 11-digit format (e.g., 01012345678) is the standard for Vodafone Cash entry. Common format errors include: entering the number with the country code (201012345678 or +201012345678 instead of 01012345678); omitting the leading 0 and entering only 10 digits (1012345678); adding spaces between digits; or entering a non-Vodafone number (012, 011, 015 prefix) in the Vodafone Cash field.

On the merchant side, the phone number input field at checkout may not have clear formatting guidance, leading customers to enter their number in various formats. If the gateway's API requires exactly 11 digits without country code and the checkout form does not normalize the input before submitting, formatting errors will cause regular failures for a subset of customers who enter their number differently. Some gateways handle format normalization automatically (stripping the country code prefix if present); others require the exact expected format.

A non-obvious variant of this problem occurs when a customer enters a valid Vodafone number that belongs to someone else — a family member's number, a remembered number, or a mistyped number that happens to be an active Vodafone number registered to a different wallet. The OTP goes to the number entered, not the customer's own wallet, and the customer cannot complete the payment. From the gateway's perspective, the transaction is technically valid until the OTP step fails.

How to fix

  1. Add phone number format guidance to the Vodafone Cash input field: Display placeholder text or a helper note in the mobile number input field showing the expected format: "Enter your 11-digit Vodafone number (e.g., 010XXXXXXXX)." This prevents most accidental format errors by setting the customer's expectation before they type.
  2. Implement input normalization in the checkout form or gateway widget: If you control the mobile number input (via a custom checkout extension or theme modification), add JavaScript to normalize the entered number before submission: strip leading +20 or 20 country code prefix, strip spaces, trim to 11 digits starting with 010. Paymob's hosted payment page typically handles this normalization — custom implementations should do the same.
  3. Validate carrier prefix before submission: Add client-side validation that confirms the entered number starts with 010 (Vodafone) before allowing checkout to proceed. If the customer enters a number starting with 012 (Orange), 011 (e&), or 015 (WE), display an inline error: "Please enter a Vodafone number starting with 010. For other carriers, use [alternative payment method]." This immediately clarifies the carrier restriction without requiring a payment attempt.
  4. Confirm with gateway whether format normalization is automatic: Ask your gateway provider (Paymob, Kashier) whether their Vodafone Cash integration normalizes phone number format automatically before submitting to Vodafone's API. If it does, the format issue only occurs when customers enter a non-Vodafone number. If it does not, you should implement normalization at the checkout form level before the number is submitted to the widget.
  5. Review gateway logs for format error codes: Your gateway returns different error codes for "number not found" (which could be format error or unregistered wallet) versus "invalid number format." If format errors are frequent in your logs, the checkout form needs normalization. If "account not found" errors dominate, the issue is more likely customers entering the wrong number or using a non-Vodafone SIM.

Vodafone Cash Shopify Integration Diagnostic Checklist

Compatible payment gateway in use — gateway is Paymob, Kashier, PayTabs Egypt, or another provider with explicit Vodafone Cash API integration. Stripe and PayPal do not support Vodafone Cash.

Vodafone Cash enabled in gateway dashboard — Vodafone Cash is toggled on as an active payment method in the merchant's gateway settings, separate from card payment method activation.

Production callback/webhook URL configured and reachable — the callback URL in the gateway dashboard is the production Shopify store URL, not a localhost or development address, and returns a 200 response to a test POST request.

OTP session timeout set to at least 3 minutes — payment session expiry for Vodafone Cash OTP is configured to 180 seconds or more to accommodate SMS delivery delays.

Phone number format guidance shown at checkout — the Vodafone Cash mobile number field includes a placeholder or label showing the expected 11-digit format (010XXXXXXXX), preventing format entry errors.

Checkout currency is EGP — store is priced in EGP, or Shopify Markets is configured to present EGP to Egyptian customers. Vodafone Cash processes EGP only.
