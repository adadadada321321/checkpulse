Shopify One-Click Purchase Not Working — 8 Causes Explained

Shop Pay one-tap not showing for returning customers? OTP not arriving? Saved card failing after authentication? Here are all 8 root causes — for both customers and merchants debugging accelerated checkout.

Updated June 2026 8 failure modes Shop Pay one-tap · OTP issues · Expired saved card · Mobile session loss · Subscription conflict · Headless integration

Shopify's "one-click" accelerated checkout is called Shop Pay. When it works correctly for returning customers, they see a pre-filled checkout with their saved address and payment method and can complete the purchase with a single tap — no re-entering card details, no address typing. This is the closest Shopify gets to Amazon-style one-click purchasing.

When it doesn't work, the failure is almost always invisible. Shop Pay silently falls back to its standard OTP flow, the OTP doesn't arrive, or the saved payment fails after authentication. Merchants see abandoned checkouts; customers are confused. There are 8 distinct root causes, each with a different diagnostic path.

Terminology: One-Tap vs Accelerated vs Express vs Buy Now
These four terms are often used interchangeably but they describe different things. One-tap = Shop Pay completing checkout with a single button tap using saved credentials (no OTP required). Accelerated checkout = Shop Pay, Apple Pay, or Google Pay appearing above the standard checkout form. Express checkout = a lane that skips address/payment entry by using saved wallet data (Shop Pay, Apple Pay, Google Pay, PayPal Express). Buy Now button = the product-page shortcut that bypasses the cart and jumps directly to checkout. Buy Now is covered in the Express Checkout Not Working guide. This guide covers Shop Pay's saved-credential / one-tap flow specifically.

Failure Mode 1: Shop Pay One-Tap Eligibility — Four Conditions Must All Be True

Shop Pay one-tap is not a feature merchants enable. It activates automatically — or doesn't — based on whether the customer meets all four requirements at the moment of checkout. Missing any single condition causes a silent fallback to the standard OTP flow.

The four eligibility conditions

Condition What Must Be True What Fails If Missing
Email match The email entered at checkout must exactly match an existing Shop Pay account No Shop Pay at all — customer must create an account or use a different payment method
Device recognized The customer's browser or app must be a recognized device (Shop Pay cookie or stored token) Falls back to OTP verification on an unrecognized device
Saved delivery address At least one delivery address must be saved in the Shop Pay account Shop Pay prompts for address entry — not one-tap
Valid saved payment method A non-expired card or payment method must be on file One-tap silently fails at payment step — customer sees error after OTP

Merchants have zero visibility into which condition is missing for a specific customer. There is no admin log, no failed attempt record, and no Shopify notification. The customer's perspective is that Shop Pay "doesn't work" or "doesn't show up" — the only way to diagnose is to walk through their Shop Pay account state at shop.app.

One-Tap Is Not the Same as Shop Pay Showing
Shop Pay can appear in the accelerated checkout row (showing the Shop Pay button) without one-tap eligibility. When Shop Pay shows but isn't one-tap, the customer must complete an OTP step. One-tap bypasses the OTP entirely because the device is already recognized. Customers who previously used Shop Pay on any Shopify store from the same device are more likely to have device recognition active.

Failure Mode 2: OTP Not Received — Wrong Phone Number in Shop Pay Account

Shop Pay sends the 6-digit OTP to the phone number stored in the customer's Shop Pay account — not the phone number entered at checkout. This is the most misunderstood aspect of the OTP flow.

Why the OTP goes to the wrong number

  • Customer changed mobile providers and has a new phone number
  • Customer is traveling internationally on a different SIM
  • Customer created their Shop Pay account with a number they no longer own
  • International roaming plan blocks short-code SMS messages (common in some EU and Asian carriers)
  • Customer uses a VoIP number that doesn't accept short-code messages

How to fix: update the Shop Pay phone number

  1. Go to shop.app or open the Shop mobile app
  2. Sign in with the Shop Pay email address
  3. Navigate to Account → Contact information → Phone number
  4. Enter the new phone number and verify with OTP on the new number
  5. Future Shop Pay sessions will send OTP to the updated number
10-Minute OTP Expiry
The Shop Pay OTP expires in 10 minutes. If the customer spent time reading their email, switching apps, or looking for the SMS, the code may have expired by the time they enter it. Shop Pay shows a "code expired" error in this case. The customer must request a new code — Shop Pay limits how many times a code can be re-sent within a short window to prevent SMS abuse.

Failure Mode 3: Saved Card Expired — Payment Fails After Successful OTP

The most confusing Shop Pay failure: the customer completes the OTP successfully and sees their saved address and payment method — then hits a payment error. The cause is almost always an expired card in the Shop Pay wallet.

Shop Pay charges the card stored on the account, not any card the customer types during checkout. If that stored card expired one month ago, the authorization attempt fails at the bank level after OTP authentication succeeds at the Shop Pay level. The customer authenticated themselves correctly but has no valid payment method for the charge.

Diagnosing an expired card vs other payment failures

Symptom Likely Cause Resolution
OTP succeeds, payment step immediately errors Expired card or invalid saved payment method Update card at shop.app → Payment methods
OTP succeeds, payment pending, then declines Bank declined authorization (fraud, insufficient funds, AVS mismatch) Customer contacts their bank or uses a different card
OTP succeeds, payment captured, then order auto-cancelled Shopify post-capture fraud score elevated the risk Merchant reviews and manually approves or sends invoice
OTP requested but amount charged is different Currency conversion by customer's bank (FX fee added) Expected behavior — bank converts at its own rate

How to update a card in Shop Pay

  1. Open shop.app or the Shop mobile app
  2. Sign in with the Shop Pay email address
  3. Go to Payment methods
  4. Find the expired card (shown with red expiry date)
  5. Select Update or Add new card
  6. Enter updated card details — the saved card is immediately available for next checkout

Failure Mode 4: Mobile Browser Tab Eviction During OTP Switch

The second most common cause of Shop Pay one-tap failure on mobile: the customer receives the OTP via SMS, switches from the browser to the Messages app to copy the code, then returns to the browser to find the tab has reloaded — or is blank.

Why this happens on iOS and Android

iOS and Android both use aggressive memory management that evicts browser tabs from RAM when the user switches to another app. On iOS, Safari evicts tabs that are not the frontmost tab when the device is under memory pressure. On Android, Chrome background tabs are evicted after a variable time based on device RAM. When the customer switches to Messages to read the OTP and then returns to the browser:

  • The Shopify checkout tab reloads from scratch — all session state gone
  • The Shop Pay OTP entry screen is replaced by the cart or product page
  • The 10-minute OTP timer has been running — if loading was slow, it may have expired
  • The authorization hold placed when the customer first entered payment may already exist on their card

Workarounds for merchants

  • Add visible instructional text near the Shop Pay button: "Keep this page open while checking your text messages"
  • Consider a pop-up or sticky message that appears when Shop Pay loads: "Complete authentication in this window"
  • For high-value products where abandonment is costly, consider advertising Shop Pay's App Clip flow — completing checkout inside the Shop app avoids browser tab eviction entirely
Two Authorization Holds After Retry
If the customer's session expires and they restart checkout, a new authorization hold is placed on their card. Combined with the hold from the first failed attempt, customers may see two pending holds — two identical amounts in their banking app. Both will release in 1–7 business days, but the customer experience is alarming. Merchants may receive support tickets about "being charged twice."

Failure Mode 5: Subscription Product in Cart Suppresses Shop Pay One-Tap

When any subscription product is in the cart — a product sold through ReCharge, Shopify Subscriptions, Bold Subscriptions, Loop Subscriptions, or any other subscription app — Shopify hides all express payment buttons simultaneously: Shop Pay, Apple Pay, Google Pay, and PayPal Express all disappear from the checkout.

This is not a bug. Subscription products require the customer to explicitly agree to recurring billing terms. Express checkout flows like Shop Pay one-tap cannot display the recurring billing consent screen that Shopify requires for subscription agreements. Rather than complete a purchase without consent, Shopify removes all accelerated options and forces the customer through the full standard checkout where the consent screen can appear.

What this means for merchants selling subscriptions

  • Customers who previously purchased non-subscription items via Shop Pay one-tap will notice it's gone when they add a subscription product
  • Mixed carts (subscription + one-time products) also lose the express buttons — the subscription item dominates
  • There is no workaround — this is a Shopify platform restriction, not a configuration error
  • The only way to enable a faster flow for subscription checkouts is Shopify's native Subscriptions product on Plus, which has a managed consent flow — but even this doesn't restore one-tap
  • Document this for your customer support team: "Why can't I use Shop Pay for my subscription order" is a frequent contact reason

Failure Mode 6: Shop Pay Not Showing — Store Eligibility and Status Issues

Before diagnosing one-tap specifically, confirm that Shop Pay shows at all. When Shop Pay disappears from the checkout entirely (not just falling back to OTP), the cause is almost always a store-level eligibility issue.

Store-level reasons Shop Pay is hidden

Condition Why Shop Pay Is Hidden How to Fix
Store password-protected Shop Pay is disabled on access-controlled storefronts Remove password or test in a browser that has the store password entered; Shop Pay should appear once the password gate is passed
Shopify Payments Pending or Suspended Shop Pay is built on Shopify Payments — all native payment buttons disappear when Payments is not Active Complete Shopify Payments identity verification or resolve the suspension before Shop Pay returns
Third-party payment gateway active Switching to Stripe, Braintree, PayPal, or other gateways removes Shop Pay, Apple Pay, and Google Pay simultaneously Restore Shopify Payments as the primary gateway to re-enable Shop Pay; third-party gateways can coexist as additional methods without removing the primary
Shop Pay not available in country Shop Pay is available in US, Canada, UK, Australia, Ireland, New Zealand and expanding — unavailable in other regions Check the Shopify Help Center for the current Shop Pay availability list; use local payment methods for unsupported countries
Development / preview theme Some theme preview modes don't load checkout correctly, hiding payment buttons Test with the live published theme, not a preview URL

Failure Mode 7: Headless — Shop Pay Button Renders But Doesn't Work

In headless Shopify storefronts (Hydrogen, custom React/Next.js frontends, or any build that uses the Storefront API instead of Shopify's hosted checkout theme), rendering the Shop Pay button component is only the first step. The button does nothing useful without an active payment request session created via the Storefront API.

What headless developers must implement

  1. Call the Storefront API shopPayPaymentRequestSessionCreate mutation to create a session — this returns a token required by the button
  2. Pass the token to the <shop-pay-button> web component as the store-url and variants attributes (or via the equivalent SDK method)
  3. Listen for the shopPaySessionEstablished custom event fired by the button after the customer authenticates
  4. Use the session ID from the event to complete order creation via checkoutCompleteWithTokenizedPaymentV3 or the equivalent mutation
  5. Handle the post-checkout redirect back to your custom domain's order confirmation page (Shopify will redirect to checkout.shopify.com by default — override via completionBehavior in the Storefront API config)
Common Headless Mistake: Button With No Session
The most common headless Shop Pay implementation error is rendering the button UI without calling shopPayPaymentRequestSessionCreate first. The button appears and is clickable. When tapped, it either shows a generic error screen, opens a blank modal, or silently fails. The developer assumes Shop Pay is broken on their store when the issue is a missing API call.

CORS and domain registration

The shopPayPaymentRequestSessionCreate mutation must be called from a domain that is registered as an allowed storefront origin. If your headless frontend is on a different domain than the Shopify store, you must register the frontend domain in the Shopify Partner dashboard or via the storefrontAllowedOrigins setting. Without this, the browser will block the Storefront API request with a CORS error before the mutation even fires.

Storefront API version requirement

The shopPayPaymentRequestSessionCreate mutation requires Storefront API version 2023-01 or later. If your headless storefront was built before 2023 and hasn't been updated, the mutation may not exist in your pinned API version. Update the X-Shopify-Storefront-Access-Token header requests to use a current API version.

Failure Mode 8: Shop Pay Installments Selected — Order Doesn't Qualify

Shop Pay Installments is the buy-now-pay-later (BNPL) feature built into Shop Pay. When a customer has previously used Installments on another Shopify store, their Shop Pay account may default to the Installments payment method. If the current order doesn't qualify for Installments, the one-tap attempt fails — the customer sees a payment error without a clear explanation.

Installments eligibility requirements (all must be true)

  • Geography: US only — Installments is not available in Canada, UK, Australia, or other countries even if Shop Pay is
  • Order total: $50 minimum, $17,500 maximum per transaction
  • Product type: Physical products only — digital products, gift cards, and subscriptions are ineligible
  • Merchant toggle: The merchant must have Installments enabled separately at Admin → Settings → Payments → Shop Pay Installments — it does not activate automatically with Shop Pay
  • Customer eligibility: Affirm (which powers Installments) performs a soft credit check at the time of purchase — some customers are not approved

Why customers see Installments failures

A customer whose Shop Pay account is configured to default to Installments will attempt an Installments payment even on a store where Installments is not enabled or the order doesn't qualify. Shop Pay cannot pre-screen the cart against Installments eligibility before the customer commits. The payment attempt fires and is rejected, showing an error that reads as a generic "payment failed."

How to Recover: Switch to Pay-in-Full
When Installments fails, the customer must switch to pay-in-full within the Shop Pay flow. On the payment error screen, look for "Change payment method" or "Pay in full" option. If this option is not visible, the customer must abandon the Shop Pay flow and complete checkout using the standard payment form — entering their card details directly. Merchants can reduce confusion by adding clear messaging about which payment methods are accepted on the product page or checkout.

Work through these in order before escalating to Shopify support:

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I enable Shop Pay one-tap for specific customers?

No. Merchants cannot configure, enable, or force Shop Pay one-tap for individual customers. One-tap activates automatically based on the customer's Shop Pay account state. The merchant has no control or visibility into which customers receive the one-tap vs OTP experience. The best a merchant can do is encourage customers to keep their Shop Pay account updated (phone number, payment card, address) and to complete prior purchases using Shop Pay to build device recognition.

Does Shop Pay work on development stores?

Shop Pay appears on development stores in test mode. However, one-tap and OTP flows behave differently in test mode — real OTPs are not sent, and the payment flow uses Shopify's test payment gateway. You cannot accurately test the real-world one-tap experience in a development store. Use a live store with Shopify Payments active and test with a real customer account on a recognized device.

My customer used Shop Pay successfully before but it's not working now. Why?

The most common reasons for a regression: (1) the customer's saved card has since expired, (2) the customer changed their phone number and the OTP goes to the old number, (3) the customer cleared their browser cookies or switched devices — device recognition is lost and OTP is required again, (4) a subscription product was added to the cart that wasn't there on prior purchases, (5) the store added a third-party payment gateway that replaced Shopify Payments, removing Shop Pay. Check shop.app for the customer's account state as the first diagnostic step.

Why does the Shop Pay button appear but nothing happens when I tap it?

On a standard Shopify hosted checkout, this is usually a JavaScript conflict — a third-party app script is interfering with the Shop Pay button's event handlers. Disable apps one by one (or use a checkout without apps installed) to identify the conflict. On a headless storefront, this is almost always a missing shopPayPaymentRequestSessionCreate session — the button renders without a valid payment token and cannot initiate the flow.
