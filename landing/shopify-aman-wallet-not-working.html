Shopify Aman Wallet Not Working — Aman Payment Not Showing, Declined & Digital Wallet Issues

Updated June 2026 · 7 failure modes · Egypt merchants · Aman digital wallet + BNPL · Raya Holding-backed

Aman (أمان) is one of Egypt's leading digital wallets and installment financing platforms, backed by Raya Holding for Financial Investments — one of Egypt's largest technology and outsourcing conglomerates. Aman operates a dual model: customers can pay directly from their Aman wallet balance (stored-value), or apply for Aman installment financing (BNPL) at merchant checkout. When Aman stops working on Shopify, the failure mode determines the fix — not showing at checkout (merchant enrollment issue), a wallet payment declined (balance or verification issue), an installment application rejected (scoring issue), and an OTP failure are four entirely different problems requiring four different solutions.

How Aman Works — and Why It Fails Differently From Other Egypt Payment Options

Aman operates as both a digital wallet and an installment financing provider. This dual nature means failure modes differ depending on which Aman product the customer is using at your Shopify checkout. A customer paying from their Aman wallet balance fails differently from a customer applying for Aman installment financing — the diagnostic steps and fixes are completely different for each.

Aman is backed by Raya Holding — a large Egyptian conglomerate, not a VC startup. This gives Aman institutional stability and a wide physical agent network across Egypt (over 100,000 points of service as of 2026), allowing customers to top up their Aman wallets at convenience stores, pharmacies, and service points. Unlike newer BNPL startups, Aman's integration may be available via direct merchant enrollment or via payment aggregators like Paymob, which bundles Aman wallet acceptance alongside other payment methods.

Aman wallet requires mobile number registration and, for higher-value transactions, National ID verification. Unverified Aman wallets have a lower transaction limit than fully KYC-verified wallets. If a customer is trying to pay an amount that exceeds their unverified wallet limit, the transaction will be blocked even if they have sufficient balance. This is a Central Bank of Egypt regulatory requirement for mobile wallet tiers, not an Aman-specific policy.

Aman is Egypt-only and EGP-only. Aman wallet and installment financing operate exclusively in Egyptian Pounds for Egyptian customers. Non-Egyptian customers and non-EGP transactions cannot use Aman at Shopify checkout regardless of integration status.

1

Aman not appearing at checkout — merchant not enrolled or integration misconfigured

Merchant Issue

The most common reason Aman wallet does not appear at Shopify checkout is that the merchant has not completed enrollment in Aman's merchant acceptance program. To accept Aman wallet payments or offer Aman installment financing, merchants must register directly with Aman through their merchant portal or via a commercial contact at Raya Holding's payment division, or alternatively accept Aman as a wallet option through a payment aggregator like Paymob that includes Aman wallet in its supported payment methods.

Direct Aman merchant enrollment requires submitting business documentation, receiving approval, and being issued production merchant credentials — a merchant ID and API key specific to the Aman payment gateway. Without completed enrollment and production credentials, the Aman payment option cannot appear at checkout. Pending or rejected enrollment means the option is simply absent at checkout with no error visible to customers.

A second common cause is a correctly enrolled merchant whose Aman integration in Shopify is installed but not configured with credentials. The Aman payment gateway plugin or custom integration requires the merchant's production credentials to be entered correctly under Shopify's payment settings. Sandbox or test credentials from the Aman developer environment do not work in production. Additionally, the payment method must be explicitly activated in Shopify after credential entry — installation alone does not enable it.

Merchants using Paymob as their payment aggregator should note that Aman wallet acceptance via Paymob requires Aman to be enabled as a specific payment method within the Paymob dashboard, separate from card and Fawry configuration. If Aman is not toggled on within Paymob's wallet payment methods, it will not appear at Shopify checkout even if Paymob itself is correctly configured.

How to fix

  1. Confirm merchant enrollment status: Check your Aman merchant account dashboard or contact Aman merchant support / your Raya Holding commercial contact to confirm your account is approved and active — not just applied for. Active status is required for the payment option to appear.
  2. Retrieve production credentials: Log in to the Aman merchant portal and locate your production merchant ID and API key. These differ from sandbox credentials used during development testing. Contact Aman merchant support if you cannot locate them.
  3. Enter credentials in Shopify: Navigate to your Shopify admin → Settings → Payments → Third-party payment providers → Aman. Enter the production merchant ID and API key exactly as issued, checking for extra spaces or copy-paste formatting errors. Save the configuration.
  4. Activate the payment method: After saving credentials, ensure Aman is toggled to Active/Enabled under Shopify payment settings. Installing the Aman plugin does not automatically enable it as a visible payment method.
  5. If using Paymob: Log in to your Paymob dashboard and navigate to payment integrations. Confirm that the Aman wallet payment method is enabled within your Paymob configuration in addition to your existing card and Fawry methods.
  6. Test in incognito: Open an incognito browser window, add a product to cart, proceed to checkout, and verify Aman appears as a payment option. Use a product above the minimum order amount for the test.
2

Aman wallet inactive or unverified — CBE mobile wallet tier limit exceeded

Customer Issue

Egypt's Central Bank of Egypt (CBE) mandates a tiered KYC verification system for all licensed mobile wallets, including Aman. An unverified Aman wallet (registered with only a mobile number, without National ID verification) has a significantly lower monthly transaction limit — typically capped at a few thousand Egyptian Pounds per month. A customer who has spent to their unverified limit or whose cart total exceeds the unverified wallet ceiling will have their payment blocked even if they have a non-zero balance available.

An Aman wallet that was created but never activated through the customer's first successful payment or top-up may also be in a dormant or pending state. Some Aman wallet registrations initiated via SMS or agent registration require a secondary activation step — completing this step in the Aman app — before the wallet can make merchant payments at Shopify checkout.

Additionally, Aman may suspend or restrict a wallet if suspicious activity is detected, if the customer's registered mobile number has become inactive, or if the customer has flagged compliance issues. A suspended wallet returns a payment failure at checkout without a clear customer-facing error message, which can be confused with a merchant-side integration problem.

How to fix

  1. Complete National ID verification: The customer should open the Aman app and complete the full KYC verification process by uploading their National ID. Full verification raises the wallet tier and significantly increases monthly transaction limits, enabling payments that the unverified tier blocked.
  2. Activate the wallet: If the customer registered via SMS or agent but has not yet used the Aman app, they should download the app, log in, and complete the wallet activation flow before attempting to pay at Shopify checkout.
  3. Check wallet status in the app: The customer should open the Aman app and check their wallet status and monthly remaining limit. If the limit is exhausted for the current month, the wallet cannot be used for purchases until the limit resets (typically on the 1st of the following month) or until KYC verification upgrades the tier.
  4. Contact Aman support: For a wallet that appears active but keeps failing, the customer should contact Aman's customer support (via the app or Aman's customer service line) to check for any restrictions or compliance flags on the account.
Merchant note: CBE mobile wallet tier limits are a regulatory constraint affecting all Egyptian mobile wallets — Aman, Fawry wallet, Vodafone Cash, Meeza, and others. If you see a pattern of Aman wallet payment failures for a particular order value range, wallet tier limits are likely the cause. Consider adding messaging near the Aman payment option noting that National ID verification in the Aman app is required for transactions above a certain amount.
3

Wallet balance insufficient for order total — Aman balance below cart value

Customer Issue

When a customer chooses to pay directly from their Aman wallet balance (rather than using Aman installment financing), the payment fails if the wallet balance is lower than the order total. Aman does not allow partial wallet payments — if the wallet balance covers only part of the order, the customer cannot use Aman for that transaction unless they first top up their wallet to cover the full amount.

Unlike credit or debit cards where the issuer displays a "card declined" message, Aman wallet balance failures may appear as a generic payment error at Shopify checkout, making it unclear to the customer that the issue is simply insufficient funds rather than a technical integration problem. This can cause confusion and unnecessary support contacts to the merchant.

Aman wallet top-ups can be done through Aman's extensive physical agent network (convenience stores, pharmacies, Raya service points), through the Aman app via bank card, or through mobile banking in some cases. The top-up is typically credited to the wallet within minutes at physical agents and nearly instantly via card or bank transfer through the app.

How to fix

  1. Direct customer to top up their Aman wallet: The customer can top up at any of Aman's physical agents (pharmacies, convenience stores, Raya service points across Egypt) or directly through the Aman app using a bank card. Top-ups at physical agents are typically credited within 1–5 minutes.
  2. Show wallet balance before payment: If your Shopify checkout integration supports it, display the Aman wallet balance to the customer after they select Aman as a payment method. This lets them see immediately whether their balance covers the order total, reducing failed payment attempts.
  3. Offer Aman installment as an alternative: If the customer's Aman wallet balance is insufficient but they qualify for Aman installment financing, they may be able to complete the purchase via the installment option without needing to top up their wallet first. Ensure both Aman wallet and Aman installment (if separately configurable) are available as payment options.
  4. Provide alternative payment fallbacks: Fawry cash payment, Paymob card, COD, or other available Egypt payment methods should be visible as alternatives for customers whose Aman wallet balance is insufficient for the current order.
4

Installment application declined — eligibility criteria not met

Customer Issue

When a customer applies for Aman installment financing (rather than paying from wallet balance) at Shopify checkout, a decline means their application did not meet Aman's financing eligibility criteria. Aman's installment product is not simply an extension of the wallet — it is a separate financing facility with its own approval process governed by Aman's credit policies and any applicable Egyptian consumer finance regulations.

Common reasons for Aman installment declines include: the customer is below the minimum age requirement for credit products in Egypt; the customer's Egyptian National ID failed verification (expired, damaged, or data mismatch); the customer has a prior Aman installment that is overdue or in default; the customer has not completed full KYC verification on their Aman wallet; the requested financing amount exceeds the customer's approved installment limit; or the product being purchased falls in a category restricted from Aman installment financing.

Unlike bank-based BNPL (Forsa via CIB), Aman's installment product does not rely primarily on Egypt's i-Score credit bureau for all approvals — Aman uses its own customer data from the wallet ecosystem alongside any available credit data. However, customers with flagged repayment history within Aman's system are typically blocked from new installment approvals until the overdue amounts are resolved.

What merchants can tell declined customers

  1. Verify KYC completion: The customer should ensure their Aman wallet is fully verified — National ID uploaded and confirmed — in the Aman app. Unverified wallets are typically ineligible for installment financing.
  2. Check for outstanding balances: Customers with a previous overdue Aman installment should settle the outstanding balance through the Aman app or at an Aman agent before attempting a new installment application.
  3. Try a lower order amount: If the requested financing amount exceeds the customer's Aman installment limit, a smaller cart may be approved. Encourage the customer to check their available Aman installment credit in the Aman app.
  4. Offer alternative BNPL: Shahry, valU, and MNT-Halan each have different scoring models. A customer declined for Aman installments may qualify with Shahry or valU for the same order. Offering multiple BNPL options in parallel captures declined customers.
  5. Suggest wallet balance top-up: If the customer does not qualify for Aman installments but has or can build sufficient Aman wallet balance, a direct wallet payment may still be possible. Guide them to top up via agents or the Aman app.
Merchant note: Aman installment decline rates vary by customer segment. If your store primarily serves first-time Aman installment applicants with no Aman wallet history, expect higher decline rates than for established Aman wallet users with a track record. Offering Paymob card payments, Fawry, and multiple BNPL options alongside Aman reduces lost conversions from declined installment applications.
5

OTP or wallet PIN failure — authentication blocked at payment step

Customer Issue

Aman wallet payments at Shopify checkout require the customer to authenticate via OTP (one-time password sent via SMS) or wallet PIN before the transaction is authorized. This authentication step exists on all Egyptian CBE-licensed mobile wallets as a security requirement. When this step fails, the Aman payment cannot be completed regardless of wallet balance or eligibility status.

OTP failures occur most commonly when the mobile number the customer uses at checkout does not match the number registered to their Aman wallet. If the customer changed SIM cards, is using a dual-SIM device on a different SIM, or is roaming internationally, the OTP is delivered to a number they cannot access. Network delivery delays on Egypt's mobile networks can also cause OTPs to arrive after the expiry window (typically 2–5 minutes), requiring the customer to request a new code.

PIN failures occur when the customer enters their Aman wallet PIN incorrectly. After a certain number of failed PIN attempts (typically 3–5), Aman temporarily locks the wallet as a fraud prevention measure. A locked wallet cannot authorize any transactions until the lockout period expires or the customer resets their PIN through Aman's account recovery process in the app.

How to fix

  1. Confirm active SIM matches Aman registration: The customer should verify they are using the SIM associated with their Aman wallet account. On a dual-SIM device, confirm the primary/active SIM is the registered one before retrying OTP delivery.
  2. Update registered mobile number: If the customer has changed their mobile number since registering with Aman, they should update it in the Aman app before attempting checkout. The number update process in the Aman app requires its own verification step.
  3. Request a fresh OTP: If the OTP expired due to network delay, tap "Resend OTP" and wait 30–60 seconds before trying again. Avoid requesting multiple OTPs in rapid succession — carriers may throttle SMS delivery if too many codes are requested in a short window.
  4. Reset wallet PIN: If the customer has forgotten their PIN or triggered a lockout, direct them to the Aman app to reset their PIN via the account recovery flow. PIN reset typically requires OTP verification to the registered mobile number, so mobile number access is needed for this step as well.
  5. Check network signal: Poor mobile signal or active international roaming can delay or block SMS delivery. The customer should ensure adequate signal before retrying the OTP step.
6

Below minimum transaction amount — cart total too low for Aman installment

Merchant + Customer

Aman installment financing has a minimum eligible transaction amount below which the installment option is not offered. If a customer's cart total falls below this threshold, the Aman installment option may not appear at checkout or will be shown but unavailable. The minimum is in place because the administrative overhead of installment plan setup (KYC, scoring, disbursement, collection) is not economically viable for very small order values.

As of 2026, Aman's installment minimum is generally in the range of 300 to 500 Egyptian Pounds per transaction, though the exact amount depends on the merchant's agreement and the specific Aman installment product active on the account. Aman direct wallet balance payments (non-installment) are not subject to this minimum — a customer can pay any amount from their Aman wallet balance as long as it does not exceed their wallet tier limit and the balance is sufficient.

A common merchant mistake is assuming the minimum applies to wallet balance payments when it only applies to installments — or vice versa. If Aman appears at checkout but fails for small orders, confirm with Aman whether the minimum applies to your specific merchant agreement and which payment type (wallet vs. installment) it affects.

How to fix

  1. Display installment minimum at checkout: Add messaging near the Aman installment payment option stating the minimum eligible order amount (for example, "Installment plans available for orders over 350 EGP"). This prevents customers from selecting installments for a small cart only to receive an error.
  2. Conditionally show installment option: Use Shopify's payment visibility logic or a checkout extension to show the Aman installment option only when the cart total meets the minimum. The direct wallet payment option can remain visible for all order sizes if separately configurable.
  3. Confirm your minimum with Aman: Contact Aman merchant support to confirm the exact minimum order amount specified in your merchant agreement, as it may differ from Aman's default published minimum.
7

Product category not eligible — Aman installment restricted for this product type

Merchant Issue

Aman installment financing is not available for all product categories. Certain product types are excluded from installment financing under Aman's policies, Egyptian consumer finance regulations, or the specific terms of the merchant agreement. Categories commonly excluded include digital goods (software licenses, download codes, digital subscriptions), purely consumable items, adult content, gambling-related products, and some financial or insurance products.

Physical goods — electronics, appliances, furniture, clothing, accessories, and household items — are generally eligible for Aman installment financing. However, the specific eligibility depends on the merchant's product category as registered with Aman during merchant enrollment. A merchant whose primary product type was not included in the approved category during enrollment may find that Aman installments never appear despite a fully configured integration.

Direct Aman wallet balance payments are generally not subject to product category restrictions — if the customer has sufficient balance and the wallet is verified, direct wallet payment can proceed for most product types regardless of installment eligibility.

How to fix

  1. Review your merchant agreement: Your Aman merchant agreement specifies which product categories are approved for installment financing under your contract. Confirm your primary products fall within the approved scope.
  2. Contact Aman merchant support: If uncertain about your product category eligibility, contact Aman with your product details. Aman's merchant support can confirm eligibility and may be able to expand your approved categories if your products qualify.
  3. Separate digital and physical SKUs: If your store sells both digital and physical products, use Shopify checkout extensions to show the Aman installment option only for physical product carts where installment financing is eligible.
  4. Offer direct wallet payment for restricted categories: If your product category is excluded from installment financing but qualifies for direct Aman wallet payment, ensure both payment options are clearly differentiated at checkout so customers understand they can still pay with their Aman wallet balance even when installments are unavailable.
Important: If Aman installment never appears despite a fully configured integration and confirmed merchant enrollment, product category restriction is the most likely cause. Verify category eligibility with Aman before concluding there is a technical integration error.

Frequently Asked

Common Questions About Aman Wallet on Shopify

Should I integrate Aman directly or use Paymob to accept Aman wallet on Shopify?

For most Shopify merchants targeting Egypt, integrating Aman wallet via Paymob is more practical than a direct Aman integration. Paymob is an authorized payment aggregator that supports Aman wallet alongside Visa/Mastercard, Fawry, Meeza, Vodafone Cash, and BNPL options within a single Shopify integration. This means one integration covers multiple Egypt payment methods rather than managing separate credentials and plugins per wallet provider. Direct Aman integration makes sense if your store's primary customer base is concentrated in Aman's specific wallet ecosystem (Raya Holding customers, Aman loyalty program participants) or if Aman has offered you negotiated merchant discount rates that are better than what Paymob passes through. For new Egypt market entrants, Paymob is the recommended starting point.

How does Aman installment compare to MNT-Halan and Shahry for Egypt merchants?

Aman installment financing, MNT-Halan, and Shahry all serve Egypt's BNPL market but with different customer bases and scoring approaches. Aman's installment product leverages its wallet ecosystem and Raya Holding's institutional infrastructure — it is best suited for customers already within the Aman wallet ecosystem. MNT-Halan (backed by Halan super-app) specifically targets underbanked Egyptians with no traditional banking relationship, using a proprietary AI model fed by behavioral and biometric signals. Shahry uses a standalone ML credit model and is accessible to a wider underbanked demographic without requiring prior engagement with a specific super-app. For maximum Egypt BNPL coverage, offering Aman installment, Shahry, valU, and Halan in combination covers the full spectrum from deeply underbanked to middle-class banked Egyptian buyers — each product uniquely approves customers the others may decline.

What happens if a customer's Aman installment payment to a merchant defaults?

As a merchant accepting Aman installment financing, you receive the full settlement amount from Aman after the transaction is approved — the customer repayment risk is transferred to Aman. If the customer subsequently defaults on their Aman installment repayments, you as the merchant are not liable and your settlement is not reversed or clawed back. Aman's collections process handles overdue installments directly with the customer. This is the standard structure for third-party BNPL and installment financing: the provider bears customer credit risk in exchange for the merchant discount rate (MDR) charged at each transaction. Chargebacks for unauthorized transactions or disputes follow a separate process governed by your merchant agreement with Aman.
