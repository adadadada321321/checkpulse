Shopify Fawry Plus Not Working — Installment Not Appearing, Credit Rejected & Order Stuck Pending

Updated June 2026 · 7 failure modes · Egypt merchants · Fawry BNPL · Credit product · EGP only · Separate from Fawry reference code

Fawry Plus is Egypt's leading merchant-facing BNPL (Buy Now Pay Later) product operated by Fawry Digital Banking & Payment Solutions. It allows customers to split purchases into 3, 6, 12, or 24 monthly installments. Fawry Plus is a credit product — completely separate from Fawry's reference code outlet payments and the Fawry mobile wallet. When Fawry Plus stops working on Shopify, the failure almost always traces to one of seven patterns, the most critical being the product confusion: a customer who uses Fawry for bill payments or outlet checkout has not qualified for Fawry Plus installments, which requires a separate application and credit approval.

How Fawry Plus Works — and Why It Fails Differently From Fawry Reference Code Payments

Fawry Plus is a BNPL (Buy Now Pay Later) credit product — it is not a payment method for customers who already use Fawry for outlet payments, bill payments, or mobile wallet transactions. Fawry (reference code) lets customers generate a code and pay cash at a Fawry outlet; no bank account, no credit approval, no application required. Fawry Plus is fundamentally different: the customer applies for a Fawry Plus credit account, undergoes an identity and credit check, receives an approved credit limit in EGP, and can then split qualifying purchases into monthly installments of 3, 6, 12, or 24 months. Monthly payments are deducted automatically from the customer's linked payment source.

The most common Fawry Plus mistake: customers and merchants assume any existing Fawry relationship (app, wallet, reference code usage) carries over to Fawry Plus. It does not. A customer who pays Fawry reference codes at outlets every week, uses the Fawry app for bill payments, or has a Fawry mobile wallet balance cannot use Fawry Plus until they have specifically applied for and been approved for a Fawry Plus credit account. This is the failure mode that generates the most confused support inquiries: "the customer has Fawry — why is Fawry Plus declining?" The answer: different product, separate approval, no crossover.

Fawry Plus requires a separate merchant agreement and gateway integration from regular Fawry. Merchants who added Fawry reference code payments to their gateway have not added Fawry Plus. The two products have separate integration IDs, separate callback URLs, and separate activation timelines. Fawry's merchant onboarding for Fawry Plus typically takes 5–10 business days after the agreement is signed — during this window, the gateway may show the option but every transaction will fail until Fawry completes their internal merchant activation.

Fawry Plus is EGP only and has a minimum transaction threshold. Orders below the minimum (typically 500 EGP, varies by agreement) will not show Fawry Plus or will reject at submission. Stripe and PayPal do not support Fawry Plus. Egyptian merchants must use a gateway with explicit Fawry Plus BNPL integration — Paymob is the primary option.

7 Failure Modes Covered

  1. Fawry Plus option not appearing — gateway plan does not include BNPL or Fawry Plus not enabled
  2. Customer has Fawry app or wallet but no Fawry Plus credit account
  3. Customer's Fawry Plus credit limit is insufficient for the order total
  4. Order total is below Fawry Plus minimum transaction threshold
  5. Installment tenors not configured — option appears but plan selector is empty or fails
  6. Order stuck in pending after Fawry Plus installment approval
  7. Fawry-side merchant activation not yet complete — gateway shows option but all transactions fail
Failure Mode 1
Fawry Plus option not appearing at checkout
Merchant configuration


When Fawry Plus does not appear at checkout, the cause is almost always that the gateway plan does not include BNPL products, or that Fawry Plus has not been enabled as a separate payment method in the gateway dashboard. Fawry Plus is a distinct integration from Fawry reference code payments. Merchants who turned on Fawry for outlet payments have not turned on Fawry Plus — they are different products with separate gateway integration IDs, separate API credentials, and separate enablement toggles.

On Paymob, BNPL products including Fawry Plus are typically available only on specific plan tiers or require a separate product request. A merchant on a standard Paymob plan that includes Fawry reference codes may need to upgrade or request Fawry Plus separately. The option will simply not render at checkout until both the gateway plan includes it and the payment method is actively enabled in the merchant's gateway settings.

Steps to fix

  1. Contact your gateway (e.g., Paymob) and confirm whether your plan tier includes Fawry Plus BNPL as a separate product — not whether it includes Fawry reference code payments.
  2. If included: go to your gateway dashboard → Payment Methods → locate Fawry Plus (may be listed as "Fawry Installments" or "Fawry BNPL") → enable it and confirm it is active.
  3. If not included: request a plan upgrade or a Fawry Plus add-on from your gateway account manager.
  4. Check Shopify payment settings: ensure the Fawry Plus gateway is listed and active under Shopify Admin → Settings → Payments → Third-party providers.
  5. Test with a real device at your live checkout URL to confirm the option renders after enabling.
Note: International gateways like Stripe and PayPal do not support Fawry Plus. Fawry Plus requires an Egyptian gateway with an explicit BNPL integration and an active Fawry Plus merchant agreement.
Failure Mode 2
Customer has Fawry app or wallet but no Fawry Plus credit account
Customer setup


This is the most common Fawry Plus failure and the one that generates the most confused support tickets. Customers who use Fawry for reference code bill payments, outlet checkout, or mobile wallet transfers assume that their existing Fawry relationship automatically qualifies them for Fawry Plus installments. It does not. Fawry Plus is a credit product that requires a completely separate application, identity verification, income assessment, and credit approval. There is no automatic upgrade or crossover from any other Fawry product to Fawry Plus.

At checkout, when a customer without an approved Fawry Plus account attempts to use Fawry Plus, they will receive an error such as "account not found," "customer not registered," or "credit account does not exist" — depending on how the gateway displays Fawry Plus errors. The customer may interpret this as a technical failure with your store, when in reality they simply do not have a Fawry Plus account. Adding a visible explanation at checkout that Fawry Plus is a separate credit product requiring prior approval can eliminate a significant portion of these failed payments and confused support inquiries.

Steps to fix

  1. Add a checkout note near the Fawry Plus payment option explaining that it requires a separate Fawry Plus credit account — not the standard Fawry app, Fawry wallet, or outlet payment account.
  2. Include instructions for customers to apply: "Apply for Fawry Plus at the Fawry Plus app or any Fawry branch. Credit approval is required before use."
  3. Provide an alternative payment method at checkout (Fawry reference code, Meeza card, Vodafone Cash) so customers who lack a Fawry Plus account can still complete the purchase.
  4. Verify with a test customer who has an active Fawry Plus account that the installment flow completes end-to-end before pointing customers to the option.
Warning: Do not tell customers to "reconnect their Fawry account" or "reinstall the Fawry app." These actions will not create a Fawry Plus credit account. The customer needs to apply for Fawry Plus as a new credit product.
Failure Mode 3
Customer's Fawry Plus credit limit is insufficient for the order total
Customer account


Fawry Plus is a revolving credit product. Each approved customer has a maximum credit limit set during the application process, and an available credit balance that decreases as they use installment plans. If a customer's available Fawry Plus credit is less than the order total, the transaction will be declined — regardless of the customer's bank balance, Fawry wallet balance, or any other financial account. The Fawry Plus available credit and the customer's bank account are entirely separate.

For example, a customer with a Fawry Plus credit limit of 5,000 EGP who has 3,000 EGP already in use across prior installment plans has 2,000 EGP available. An order for 2,500 EGP will be declined, not because of any merchant or gateway misconfiguration, but because the customer does not have enough available Fawry Plus credit. The gateway will return a credit-related rejection code. The fix is on the customer side: they need to pay down existing Fawry Plus installments to free up credit, or contact Fawry to request a credit limit increase.

Steps to fix

  1. When a customer reports a Fawry Plus decline: check the gateway transaction log for the specific error code — credit limit errors are distinct from authentication errors or configuration errors.
  2. Inform the customer that the decline is due to insufficient Fawry Plus available credit, not a store problem.
  3. Advise the customer to log into their Fawry Plus account to check available credit and either pay down existing installments or request a limit increase from Fawry Plus.
  4. Offer an alternative payment method so the customer can complete the purchase with a different method while they resolve their Fawry Plus credit.
Note: Merchants cannot see a customer's Fawry Plus credit limit. The gateway will only return a decline code. You cannot resolve this issue from the merchant side — it requires the customer to manage their Fawry Plus account.
Failure Mode 4
Order total is below Fawry Plus minimum transaction threshold
Merchant configuration


Fawry Plus has a minimum transaction amount — typically around 500 EGP, though the exact threshold depends on the merchant agreement and gateway plan configuration. Orders below this threshold will either not show Fawry Plus as a payment option at checkout, or will display it but reject the transaction with a minimum amount error when the customer attempts to proceed. The customer sees a failure that appears to be a technical problem, when the actual cause is that their order value is too low for the installment product.

This creates a confusing user experience: a customer sees Fawry Plus at checkout, selects it, selects their installment plan, and then hits an error at the payment confirmation step. The error message from the gateway may not be customer-friendly. Adding a visual indicator to the Fawry Plus payment option (for example, "Available for orders above 500 EGP") prevents the customer from attempting a payment that will fail. Hiding the Fawry Plus option programmatically when the cart is below the threshold is the cleanest solution.

Steps to fix

  1. Confirm your minimum threshold with your gateway or Fawry Plus merchant agreement — it is typically between 500 EGP and 1,000 EGP.
  2. Add a conditional display note near Fawry Plus at checkout indicating the minimum order requirement (e.g., "Available for orders 500 EGP and above").
  3. If your gateway supports it: configure a cart minimum condition so Fawry Plus only appears when the cart value meets the threshold.
  4. Review gateway transaction logs for minimum amount rejection codes to identify how frequently this is occurring and whether you need a more prominent display solution.
Failure Mode 5
Installment tenors not configured — option appears but plan selector is empty or fails
Merchant configuration


Fawry Plus offers installment plans of 3, 6, 12, and 24 months, but these tenors must be explicitly configured in the merchant's gateway settings and Fawry Plus merchant agreement. If no tenors are activated, the Fawry Plus option appears at checkout but the installment plan selection step either shows an empty plan picker, an error such as "no plans available," or a spinner that never resolves. The customer selects Fawry Plus, cannot select a plan, and abandons the checkout.

This is a silent failure: the payment option is visible and the customer expects it to work, but the configuration step was never completed. Each installment duration (3 months, 6 months, 12 months, 24 months) may need to be individually enabled in the gateway dashboard, and some durations may require a separate approval from Fawry based on the merchant's business category or agreement tier. For example, a merchant might have 3-month and 6-month tenors active but not 12-month or 24-month. Always confirm which tenors are active before launching Fawry Plus to customers.

Steps to fix

  1. Log into your gateway dashboard and navigate to the Fawry Plus configuration section — look for "Installment Plans," "Tenors," or "Fawry Plus Settings."
  2. Verify which tenors are enabled: 3 months, 6 months, 12 months, 24 months. Enable all that are approved in your merchant agreement.
  3. If no tenors are available: contact your Fawry Plus account manager — some tenors require explicit approval based on your merchant category and agreement level.
  4. Test the plan selector with a real Fawry Plus account (your own or a test customer's) to confirm the available tenors render correctly and that a plan can be selected and confirmed end-to-end.
  5. Match displayed tenors to your agreement: avoid displaying tenors that are not activated — the customer will hit a failure after selecting a plan that the integration cannot process.
Failure Mode 6
Order stuck in pending after Fawry Plus installment approval
Merchant configuration


When a Shopify order remains in pending payment status after the customer has completed the Fawry Plus installment plan selection and authentication, the cause is a misconfigured or unreachable callback (webhook) URL in the gateway settings. Fawry Plus approval is asynchronous: the customer selects their installment plan and authenticates via the Fawry Plus app or OTP, Fawry approves the credit transaction server-to-server, and the gateway sends a payment confirmation to a callback URL that updates the Shopify order status from pending to paid. If the callback URL is wrong, the customer is enrolled in an active installment plan — monthly deductions will begin — but Shopify never receives the confirmation and the order remains pending.

This is the most dangerous Fawry Plus failure mode. The customer is financially committed to monthly payments on an installment plan, but the merchant has not received a paid order in Shopify and may not fulfill the purchase. The customer paid; the merchant thinks they did not. Manually reconciling gateway transaction records against pending Shopify orders is the only way to identify and resolve these cases. The fix for future orders is updating the callback URL to the correct, live, HTTPS production URL immediately.

Steps to fix

  1. Log into your gateway dashboard → Fawry Plus settings → callback/webhook URL configuration.
  2. Verify the callback URL is your production Shopify store URL with HTTPS — not localhost, not a development tunnel, not an outdated domain.
  3. Test the URL is publicly reachable and returns 200 to a POST request. Use a tool like curl or Requestbin to verify.
  4. Cross-reference: pull recent Fawry Plus approved transactions from your gateway dashboard and compare against pending Shopify orders. For each order that has a gateway approval but is pending in Shopify, manually mark the Shopify order as paid and fulfill.
  5. Contact the customer for any orders where the customer is enrolled in installments but has not received their purchase — explain the issue, fulfill promptly, and retain records for any Fawry Plus dispute process.
Critical: Do not cancel Shopify orders that are pending but have a Fawry Plus approval in the gateway. The customer is already enrolled in an installment plan. Cancelling the Shopify order without handling the Fawry Plus side can leave the customer making monthly payments for a purchase they never received.
Failure Mode 7
Fawry-side merchant activation not yet complete
Merchant onboarding


Activating Fawry Plus requires two separate processes: enabling it in the gateway dashboard (the merchant side) and completing Fawry's internal merchant activation process (the Fawry side). Signing the Fawry Plus merchant agreement and enabling the integration in the gateway does not mean Fawry has completed their internal configuration. Fawry's merchant activation for Fawry Plus typically takes 5–10 business days after all documentation is submitted. During this window, the Fawry Plus payment option may appear at checkout — because the gateway integration is active — but every transaction will fail with a cryptic error such as "merchant not found," "integration not active," or "service unavailable" because Fawry's back-end has not yet activated the merchant's installment agreement.

This failure is particularly frustrating because the gateway dashboard shows everything as configured correctly, and from the gateway's perspective, the integration is set up. The failure is entirely on Fawry's side. Merchants who receive consistent failures immediately after setting up Fawry Plus — before any customer reports — should check the activation status directly with their Fawry Plus account manager before attempting to debug gateway or callback configurations that are not the cause of the problem.

Steps to fix

  1. Check with your Fawry Plus account manager whether your merchant activation is complete on Fawry's side — confirm you have received explicit written confirmation that your Fawry Plus merchant ID is fully active.
  2. Request a test transaction from your Fawry Plus account manager using Fawry's test/staging environment to confirm the integration is working before testing in production.
  3. Do not enable Fawry Plus for customers until you have confirmed end-to-end functionality with a real Fawry Plus account completing a full transaction flow.
  4. If activation is delayed beyond 10 business days: escalate with your gateway account manager, who can often expedite the Fawry side activation.
  5. Hide Fawry Plus at checkout during the activation window to prevent customers from attempting transactions that will fail — re-enable once activation is confirmed.
Note: A correctly configured gateway integration that consistently fails for all customers (not just some) with opaque "service" or "merchant" errors is almost always a Fawry-side activation issue, not a gateway configuration problem. Do not spend time debugging callback URLs or integration IDs when this is the cause.

Fawry Plus Shopify Integration Diagnostic Checklist

Gateway plan confirmed to include Fawry Plus BNPL — verified with gateway that your plan tier includes Fawry Plus installments as a separate product from Fawry reference code payments.

Fawry Plus enabled as a separate payment method in gateway dashboard — Fawry Plus is toggled on in the merchant's gateway settings independently from regular Fawry, with a distinct integration ID.

Fawry-side merchant activation confirmed in writing — received explicit confirmation from Fawry Plus account manager that the merchant ID is fully active on Fawry's back-end, not just enabled in the gateway dashboard.

Installment tenors configured and active — at least one installment tenor (3, 6, 12, or 24 months) is enabled in the gateway dashboard and confirmed as active in the Fawry Plus merchant agreement.

Production callback/webhook URL configured and reachable — the callback URL in the gateway settings is the live production Shopify store URL (HTTPS, current domain), not localhost or a development address.

Minimum transaction amount communicated at checkout — a note near the Fawry Plus option informs customers of the minimum order amount (e.g., 500 EGP) so they are not surprised when small orders cannot use the installment option.

Checkout guidance clarifies Fawry Plus requires a separate account — a visible note near the Fawry Plus option explains that a separate Fawry Plus credit account is required — distinct from the Fawry app, Fawry wallet, and Fawry reference code payments.

Checkout currency is EGP — store is priced in EGP, or Shopify Markets is configured to present EGP to Egyptian customers. Fawry Plus processes EGP only.

End-to-end test with a real Fawry Plus account completed — a full test transaction was made through the live checkout with a real registered Fawry Plus customer, installment plan was selected, authentication completed, and the Shopify order updated from pending to paid.

Fallback payment methods available at checkout — customers who cannot use Fawry Plus have clear alternatives (Fawry reference code, Meeza card, Vodafone Cash, or bank transfer) to prevent cart abandonment when Fawry Plus fails for any reason.
